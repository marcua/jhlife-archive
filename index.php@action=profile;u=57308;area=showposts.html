Neighborhood Chat / Violent Teen Mob--Please Read
« on: April 09, 2015, 09:54:15 PM »
I hate that this has to be my first post to this site.

Tonight, around 9PM, I was walking past the big Rite Aide on 37th. I came across a group of approximately 15-20 teenage boys, many of them on bikes, all of them wearing casual/athletic clothes.  My guess is that they were 14-16 years of age.  They were yelling at another young man, around their age, and appeared ready to attack him.  I stood between the victim and the attackers, who seemed unwilling to draw away from the victim.  They repeatedly threatened him with violence, often calling him "fat ass n-----."  Another adult appeared, and we were able to get the victim and his friend to start walking away from the group up 37th.  As we started, one of the attackers threw a piece of lumber (approx. 2 feet long) in our direction.  Thankfully, it didn't hit anyone.  As we walked up 37th, about three-four of the attackers followed us by bike.  They repeatedly made threats against the victim, and again, referred to him by the n-word.  At one point, one of them yelled "You're gonna die  n-----"  The group headed towards 34th avenue, and I waited with the victim, his friend, and the other adult at the corner of 85th and 37th. I asked what had happened, and if victim went to school with the attackers (as I was worried about his future safety).  The victim stated that he didn't go to school with attackers, and that the group played basketball (I don't know if they were a team, or not).  The other adult said that he was the father or uncle (I can't remember) of the victim's friend. He called the police, who arrived shortly thereafter.  I told the police what I witnessed and, as a lawyer with experience in law enforcement, that they should take this incident very seriously.  I told the police the direction in which the group headed, and the victim said that the group of attackers might be headed to a park (I'm not sure how he knew, or which park it was).  The victim, his friends, and the police then left.

As you might imagine, this was a very upsetting incident to witness.  If I were to guess, the group of attackers were otherwise normal teens who would never act so heinously without the encouragement of a mob of their friends.  If you think that one of the attackers is your kid, or you know someone who might be their parent, you seriously need to intervene.
