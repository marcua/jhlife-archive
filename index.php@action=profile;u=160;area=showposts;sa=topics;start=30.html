Neighborhood Chat / Job opportunity: Immigrant Integration Community Fellow
« on: August 26, 2014, 03:33:05 AM »
This just came over the transom... could be a great fit for someone from our neighborhood.

Mayor’s Office of Immigrant Affairs
Job Posting, Immigrant Integration Community Fellow
 
The Mayor’s Office of Immigrant Affairs seeks an individual to join our team for one year as an Immigrant Integration Community Fellow, a new position funded by the JM Kaplan Fund, a New York City-based foundation that focuses on immigrant integration.
 
The Mayor’s Office of Immigrant Affairs (MOIA) promotes the well-being of immigrant communities by recommending policies and programs that facilitate the successful integration of immigrant New Yorkers into the civic, economic, and cultural life of the City. Current initiatives include the launch of New York City’s municipal identification card, increasing language access across City government, and expanding immigrant access to justice as a tool for fighting poverty and inequality. New York City is also at the forefront of the national conversation on immigrant integration and immigration reform, with MOIA seeking to advance progressive immigrant integration and immigration policies at the city, state, and federal levels.
 
The Community Fellow will work with the Director of Community Outreach and senior staff on the agency’s policy initiatives. Responsibilities include:
 
·         Assist in the development of a landscape analysis of immigrant community organizations by borough and community/language, including nascent and informal groups, formal non-profit organizations, and citywide federations.
·         Advise on a staffing structure to ensure that MOIA staff can serve as dedicated liaisons to specific sectors of New York City’s immigrant community.
·         Participate in outreach initiatives and work with senior staff to improve engagement strategy to better understand the needs and concerns of immigrant communities, and share resources.
·         Collaborate with sister agencies to finalize a technological infrastructure to better manage and track community interactions and relationships.
·         Utilize this infrastructure to assist in the design of a comprehensive outreach strategy for municipal ID, legal services and the citywide immigrant inclusion assessment.
 
The Immigrant Integration Community Fellow will report to the Director of Community Outreach of MOIA.
 
Essential Skills / Qualifications:
 
Required skills and abilities include:
·         Excellent writing, research, analytic and oral communication skills
·         Track record of effectively handling multiple priorities and assignments on a tight timeline
·         Ability to work with a wide range of people, inside and outside the government
·         Ability to think creatively, work independently and exercise a high degree of initiative to accomplish tasks and solve problems
·         Familiarity with issues impacting immigrant New Yorkers
·         A demonstrated commitment to public service, preferably through experience in a not-for-profit or government context
·         Proficiency in a second language is a plus.
 
Salary is $50,000 for the one-year fellowship, with excellent benefits. The City of New York is an Equal Opportunity Employer. To apply, submit cover letter, resume, writing sample, and three references by August 30, 2014 to Marie-Fatima Hyacinthe (mfhyacinthe@cityhall.nyc.gov) with the subject line: “Immigrant Integration Community Fellow Application – MOIA.”

Restaurants & Food / Kesari dal: a cautionary tale
« on: April 23, 2013, 12:52:02 PM »
A week ago I popped into one of our local South Asian grocery stores for a few bananas and ended up also buying a bag of yummy-looking golden lentils called Kesari Dal. Last night, before dumping them in a pot with a bunch of curried vegetables and chicken, I did a quick Google search to make sure I wasn't missing any special cooking tips. Here is what I found:

From The Times of India:

“Historically, the link between Kesari dal (Lathyrus Sativus) and the incidence of lathyrism, a form of paralysis that affects the lower limbs, has been well documented. This has been attributed to a particular neurotoxin ODAP, which is a form of an amino acid. The research currently being undertaken is in the form of animal trials to find examine this link and to verify it.”

From the FDA Poisonous Foods Database:

“Lathyrism is a major health problem in India, especially during drought-related famines. The severely crippling and potentially lethal disease results from eating large quantities of so-called "poison peas"… known in India as kesari dal.”

Apparently cultivation of Kesari Dal is still banned in India, though farmers are lobbying to have it lifted. The Times of India article notes that scientists are trying to develop a non-toxic variety of the dal, though they haven't been successful yet.

So…  although I probably wasn’t going to paralyze my family by feeding them a single serving of Kesari dal, I was still surprised that this banned pea is openly sold in our neighborhood.  I’m curious if anyone else has any experience with this form of dal—is there any way to prep it so that it’s not poisonous?

And note for adventurous eaters: maybe best to Google that new food before putting it on the dinner table.
