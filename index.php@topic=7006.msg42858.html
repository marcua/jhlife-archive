First, I'm of Eastern European ancestry on my mother's side (emigrated from Austria-Hungary at turn of century), so I know pastries!

JH is very lucky to have Cannelle in the neighborhood. Beautiful pastries, good baguette, nice people, authentic Francophone; I go all the time.

But, if you're serious about bakeries, you must go to Tanto Dulce Baking Co. at the Elmhurst/Maspeth border, 74-08 Grand Ave., 718-779-4703. The owner knows dough; everything is barely sweet. She knows how to use fruit too. She creates her own versions of the classics. They do have flan but I've never bought it. The family is Venezuelan but the owner (I call her Kattie) was trained in NYC and worked top restaurants before opening her own business with a micro-loan. Now the whole family works for her. It's a true and happy immigrant success story. The coffee's good too. I think it's Illy, not sure, but they really know how to prepare it.

Does anyone know if Homestead in Rego Park (can't remember name of street, used to be on Austin St.) still sells the homemade cherry strudel?

Jeanette
