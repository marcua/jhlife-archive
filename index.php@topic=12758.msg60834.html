News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I totally agree. The cognitive dissonance (chains vs. Trader Joe's, neighborhood character vs. more American restaurants, bars vs. no bars, YMCA vs. pool, more restaurants vs. we already have lots of restaurants) got in the way for me a bit, but at the very least, they got to hear the kinds of businesses the community wants to have there, and that would be successful additions. People don't need to be experts in real estate to know what they miss in their neighborhood. And if people weren't speaking as one voice there, that's totally fine.

It might have been a more productive discussion if we had more detail on the specific needs and goals from the property owner. He's not going to talk about any of that, but people like the gentleman who mentioned that small businesses can only be established if there is at least one big business in the building (he mentioned Starbucks/Shake Shack) had a good point. I missed hearing more insight into the limitations of commercial real estate in JH that could trigger more realistic/better ideas for what we could have there. It could also attract the right businesses and entrepreneurs.

If there are any commercial real estate agents who can give insight on these challenges, I'd be really curious to hear.

(And I think Shake Shack is a really cool idea that might cover the need for more American restaurants
