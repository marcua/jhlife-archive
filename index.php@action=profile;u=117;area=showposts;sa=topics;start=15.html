Neighborhood Chat / Fighting Parking Tickets
« on: June 02, 2010, 06:24:05 PM »
I have a sure fire way to reduce any parking ticket you get just by contesting it by mail.  When you do this you will always be offered a reduced fine settlement usually somewhere between a 25% to 33% reduction.  If you don't accept the reduced fine a judge will review your case and the evidence and rule on the merits and you forfeit your right to the reduced fine.  So if you get a parking ticket contest it and you will get more time to pay and a reduction in the fine.

Neighborhood Chat / When is your first sign of Spring?
« on: January 21, 2009, 10:02:45 PM »
For me the hardest part of winter is when the holidays pass and it is bitterly cold and dark.  My first sign of Spring is when the days start to get longer.  We are now gaining about 8-9 minutes of daylight every week in the evening (the morning daylight change is slower this time of year).  I think I hate the darkness of winter more than anything else.  I actually like snow while it is falling but hate it afterwards because of the parking headaches it causes and the sloppiness of melting dirty snow.

Do you realize that the sun sets tomorrow at 5:00 PM which is 32 minutes later than when it set at 4:28 PM on December 14th and that on February 28th the sun will set at 5:45!  Spring is coming!  To me the glass is always half full!

Restaurants & Food / Uncle Jacks Bayside
« on: October 22, 2008, 08:46:45 PM »
I recently had the pleasure of eating at Uncle Jacks on Bell Blvd in Bayside.  It is a first rate steakhouse.  The prices are high but comparable to other steakhouses like Christos in Astoria.  The cheapest steaks on the menu are the T-Bone and NY Strip.  They are both about $45.  I was lucky though that Shelby from this site had mailed me a gift certificate to offset part of the cost.  That was very nice especially since we don't know each other and have never met.  Entrees and salads are extra but we only split an order of steak fries because the steaks were large.  I think they were 24 ounces each.  Definitely worth a trip if you are a steak eater.  Bell Boulevard is also a good place to stroll and eat if you feel like a change a pace from JH.
