Restaurants & Food / ka-chori --can I find this in JH?
« on: May 14, 2011, 02:40:53 PM »
I've found the appetizer ka-chori at two different indian restaurants in different cities, and both times, the product description is "too difficult to put into words but highly recommended". 

It's a vegetarian appetizer, sort of like a chaat, with some type of fried patties, covered with chick peas and various sauces.  I like to dump mango chutney on top of it all.  I believe it's considered Northern Indian.

I know where to find it in NYC (Baluchi's), but what about in JH?  I haven't seen it on a menu here yet, but perhaps I'm missing it.  While it's great that I can order it for delivery to work, I do prefer to stuff myself with indian food in private:).

Restaurants & Food / Re: Fresh Direct in JH and idling
« on: April 02, 2011, 08:45:41 AM »
Maybe the refrigeration in the trucks only stays on if the truck is running?  I can certainly understand why they're not driving around the block 10 times and nearest available legal parking spot is likely very far away.

I used to get FreshDirect when I lived in Astoria.  I didn't have a car at the time, and my landlord told me not to drink the water from the tap.  Gallons of water were too heavy to carry home (more than 1 at a time), so I would have several delivered, along with my groceries from the week.  Groceries are heavy, and especially in JH, the supermarkets are far from most of the residential areas.  If someone is older, has children with them, or injured or disabled, it can be difficult to carry what they need home without making several trips over the course of a week.

FreshDirect has been sending me promotions lately... 15% off my entire purchase to come back, and the emails come once or twice a week.  I consider it, but I hate that you have to pick a "window" of time for delivery and then wait around for them.  If I didn't have a car, I certainly would, though.

« on: January 27, 2011, 12:34:26 PM »
I received a card in the mail for RCN Cable and Internet Service for $59.99 per month.  I went to the website and see that a 12-month contract is required and equipment is not included.  To get a TIVO/digital cable box, it would be an extra $19.95 per month.  They recommend you buy your own cable modem for the internet, and I see I can purchase one for around $50 on Amazon.  So, let's say this would make the monthly cost of a 1 year contract approximately $85.00.

My 2-year contract with Time Warner Cable ended a few months ago, and the cost of my cable/internet and DVR is now $135 a month.  When I called about the price increase (previously the bill was about $115 per month), they did not seem too concerned about it and didn't offer any kind of discount or try to get me to enter into a new contract.  Generally, I'm happy with my TWC cable and internet, and I love the DVR.  No HD, and I don't want it.  In actuality, I have way more channels than I need, as all I watch are the basic 1-11 channels, E!, Bravo, VH1 and MTV.  I'd have no problem if my channels only went up to channel 25, but I do like the channel guide, and I do love my DVR.

I'm considering this switch to lower my monthy cost, but I just wanted to check to see if others had an opinion on RCN.  Are there hidden fees I am not aware of?  Are there installation costs (i really just did a quick look at the website)?  Does the internet/ cable frequently go out?  Is TIVO as good as a DVR?

OR, has anyone had any luck getting TWC to significantly lower their prices if I'm considering switching?  I am not aware of any package that will give me only the channels I want, but if there is a way and you have negotiated it, please let me know.  I'm willing to enter into another 2-year contract if I need to.  I don't want their digital phone, and I'm annoyed that you can't call up TWC without them offering it!

Now, just waiting for my Verizon cell phone contract to run out so that I can find a way to stop paying them $70 a month...
