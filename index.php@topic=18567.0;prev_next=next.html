How small is your building?

Have you considered having your current company take on some more responsibilities?

The reason I'm asking about the number of units and your current company is that luring a full time management company is very difficult for small co-cops. The cost can be prohibitive and, on the management end, there may not be enough money in it for them to take on the building.

The board needs to assess its needs and set a clear list of what they want the management company to tackle -- and what the expectations are. I have lived in several buildings where the board and the residents haven't thought this through. They hire a management company and expect the manager to be at their beck-and-call and they don't realize that for the company to keep afloat as a business they have to have a lot of buildings under management. So you have to be realistic about the service you expect. At a large management company, a building manager might have a half dozen or more buildings to run. At a small one they might have more.

If your building has a few big projects coming up perhaps it would be best to hire your current manager to take those on for a specific fee rather than hire a full time manager. We've done this and it has worked out well.
