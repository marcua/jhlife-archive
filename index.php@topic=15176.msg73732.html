This is a huge problem in Jackson Heights, and in NYC in general. I'm in my early twenties, female, and have suffered from street harassment the entire time I've lived in this neighborhood, on a constant basis (more often on Roosevelt and side streets). I've gotten the simple stuff, like wolf whistles and catcalls and disgusting comments and stares.

There have been worse situations, as well. Multiple times, men have tried to follow me home, asking me where I live. I've been followed down Roosevelt Avenue with men trying to grab me. Usually they back off when I respond with extreme anger, but I can't always do that because I never know if that response is going to get me hurt or worse. I've actually asked my boyfriend to walk behind me down the street for ten minutes, pretending he doesn't know me, so he can see how many guys give me lewd stares or make inappropriate comments. I've even had to shoo away a disgusting man who followed a young girl (probably around 14) off the 7 train trying to touch her and talk to her.

I don't think it's a cultural thing, although almost 100% of the men who have done this to me in JH have been Hispanic/Latino (often speaking Spanish and thinking I don't understand -- I am fluent). I think it's a matter of education; men with education, especially on social issues, seem to be less likely to be street harassers. Obviously, anyone can be a street harasser or a rapist, but it's definitely not a racial thing as some people on this thread have implied. My boyfriend is Latino and neither he nor any of his loved ones/friends were raised to think like this -- he agrees that it is perpetuated by ignorance.

Men...it is important to educate yourselves on the issues facing women today, like this. With understanding and support I hope this kind of nonsense won't be happening on the same scale when I eventually have kids someday. If you see a man harassing a woman, CALL HIM OUT!
