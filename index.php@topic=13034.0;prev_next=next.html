Hi QueensGal

First congratulations! Since in a cash closing, there is no HUD1 closing statement, proper estimate has to come from your attorney’s office. If you plan on transferring funds into one account to be able to cut checks, the amount has to be enough to cover the difference between the purchase price and the down payment you’ve already given to the sponsor and funds you need to close, which would typically be your attorney’s fee, any messenger fees, one month’s maintenance, ACRIS recording fees.

Another variable is how your sponsor deal is structured. If you agreed to pay the transfer taxes and/or sponsor’s legal fees, you would likely add that amount to the total as well.

My advice would be to first call your attorney’s office and get a safe ballpark figure of what you should have in the account before closing and make the transfers you need. Then once the numbers get confirmed based on your closing date, you can cut the checks as appropriate. Some attorneys lump them up and disburse themselves, others like you to cut your own. Most of them will likely need to be certified.

Most attorneys ask that you also bring your check book for any incidentals. Don’t forget your photo ID

Happy closing!
