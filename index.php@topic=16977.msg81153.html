Sorry, but, I gotta make an example of this. Don't be afraid to walk around and find your spot. There's no shortage of nail salons around. And a bad manicure is of very little consequence.

I don't get manicures but, when I moved here I left my barber of 15 years because I wanted to go to a local guy. I walked around and selected someone after surveying the scene inside. Now we are pretty friendly when I go in. And that is essentially what makes a neighborhood feel like one. You walk around and get familiar. Spend time and money in the local businesses. Based on recent comments, it seems like there's a concern that the friendly and village like quality in JH is at stake as new folks move in. That's pretty normal for NY today and probably always has been. But, when your new, it's important to try to integrate into a place rather than bend it to ones tastes and interests. Not trying to pick on you. Just more of general thing in response to other threads I've seen on here recently....
