Cans & Bottles - collecting and scavenging
« on: July 23, 2012, 08:58:06 PM »
I was wondering what is the best way to handle can and bottle collectors that roam into my backyard. I recently moved back from LA and I don't recall this happening as much 10 years ago.

Not only does this happen everyday but it can happen numerous times a day. Like most JH homeowners I have a shared driveway that is gated. My next door neighbor is the owner as well. We have our driveway gates closed at all times. We don't want to lock the gates since it can be an inconvenience.

I asked the same handful of ladies to leave many times but they always find their way back. I understand that they are just looking for cans but the fact that a stranger walks into my yard can get a bit annoying.

Do any homeowners also have this problem? If so, what attempts were made to prevent this from happening?

The only other thing I can think of is something I saw on Astoria Blvd when I rented a parking space in an outdoor lot for a week when I went away.  The lot had a gate that only looked locked.  That is, it had a big chain with a heavy looking lock but in fact that chain was just draped over the gate.  Short-term renters (like me) only had to just lift the chain off and swing the gate open.  We didn't have to have a key or a combination.  

Perhaps if you put a real lock on for 2 weeks and then switched it to something that only looks locked, that would be enough to deter them.

Good luck.
