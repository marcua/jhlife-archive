Restaurants & Food / Re: Organic Milk
« on: January 18, 2010, 07:41:03 PM »
Quote from: Chuckster on January 17, 2010, 11:26:43 PM
A considerable amount of time has gone by, and I've experimented with organic milk.  Unfortunately, ECG is right.  The lactose in organic milk still has a negative effect on me, so I'm choosing to avoid it.   I really don't like the taste of Lactaid milk because I find it so artificially sweet.  Until I explore almond and rice milks, soy seems to be my best option for now.  Again, I don't down glasses of the stuff.  I just like to pour some into my coffee and over cereal on occasion.  Anyone have any other suggestions?

I use lactaid pills for dairy - except for milk because I find it easier to buy lactose-free milk.  I'm sure it would be cheaper to use lactaid pills (especially since I get the pills at a good price at Costco - generics are also much cheaper than the branded pills).  I agree that lactose-free milk tastes sweeter - bothers me more in non-fat than in 1% or 2%.  I haven't done a side-by-side taste test, but I think Organic Valley Lactose Free tastes better than Lactaid brand (even the organic Lactaid brand).  Stop and Shop has a store brand that I also prefer to Lactaid (I think).

Re: the pills not working well.  I definitely find my body reacts differently even to the same type and amount of dairy at different times.  And while I know some people find yogurt with active cultures and sheep or goat dairy not as much of a problem, because they have less lactose, I always need to take a lactaid or two.  But, if you're taking a pill or two at the first bite and still having lots of problems, it might be time to talk to your doctor because something else might be going on.  If I have a big bowl of ice cream, I'll always have some discomfort, but I'm usually not in massive trouble as long as I take the pills.

Oh, and I don't like soy milk, but have tried unsweetened almond milk recently and find that pretty palatable.

ETA: The health food store on 37th at 83rd has Organic Valley lactose-free milk most of the time.  I never can remember which supermarkets carry it, but I'm pretty sure Compare Foods on Northern does not and the Met at 76th does.  Not entirely sure, though.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Commuting on the 7 train
« on: November 08, 2009, 01:55:38 PM »
Hi Shelby - timing definitely makes a difference.  I find that at 8, if I have walked all the way to the end of the platform, I can usually get a seat at 82nd st or, if not, sit once people leave at 74th st, or, at the least, it isn't that horribly crowded.  By 8:15, even the ends of the trains are sometimes uncomfortably crowded often and occasionally horribly so.  I wouldn't swear to it, but I think it thins out again by 8:45 (and is a breeze after 9 - I often am on the train after 9, but rarely do I get on at 8:45 so I'm not sure).  The 7 runs at such full capacity that it doesn't take much for it to get overloaded and then trains start skipping 82nd st.  It doesn't happen that frequently, but it is frustrating when it does happen.  On the other hand, the 7 runs so frequently that I rarely have to wait long for a train that is stopping.

It definitely takes longer to get to Manhattan at rush hour on the 7 then it does at other times, just because the train is stopped longer in the stations.  I usually don't switch to an express b/c those are usually crowded.  Hope that is helpful!

Restaurants & Food / Re: Butchers in JH
« on: February 10, 2009, 03:29:57 PM »
I'm a big fan of Don Francisco's on 37th between 85th and 86th.  Unfortunately, he's only open Fri-Sun for the past year or so, but great meat and the butcher is very knowledgeable and helpful.  Not a massive selection - go for the beef or pork - but good stuff.  He has no labels or price tags (I think there is a price list on the wall), but fair prices, especially for the quality.  Plus, you can't get thick pork chops at supermarkets anymore, nor big racks of ribs!  Pretty good sausages as well.
