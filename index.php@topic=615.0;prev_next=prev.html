Have any particularly nice displays of holiday lights caught your attention?

The Daily News has a feature on the Greystones' annual tradition...

With their lights all aglow Jackson Heights co-op displays help to make season bright
BY ROBERT GEARTY
DAILY NEWS STAFF WRITER
Sunday, December 21st 2008, 4:00 AM

A COLORFUL holiday display in the Jackson Heights historic district has been making spirits bright for decades.
The festive outdoor decorations are put up by residents of the Greystones, a complex of 14 Gothic-style co-op buildings on 80th St. between 37th and 35th Aves.

Above each entrance vestibule, a 6-foot high Christmas tree with traditional colored lights is festooned. A large wreath hangs from each building's facade to complete the holiday arrangement.

"It's very charming," said Jackson Heights historian Daniel Karatzas, author of "Jackson Heights, A Garden in the City." "It certainly brightens up the block."

The trees are lit each day at 4 p.m. and stay on until midnight. On Christmas and New Year's Day the trees remain lit all day. The display will be up until Jan. 10. Each building kicks in $95 for the tree and wreath, which are purchased from a local florist.

"When we moved in we used to have tree-lighting ceremonies," said Bruno Scauri, 78, a Greystone resident since 1961. "We went about getting the Police Department to cordon off the street. We would sing carols and what have you."

Scauri said the tradition goes back to the years just after World War II and has remained a source of pride on the block.

Moira Breen, a Greystone resident for more than 45 years, also remembered those tree-lighting festivities. They featured bagpipers from County Cork in Ireland, she said, who would march up and down the block after the trees were lit.

"Then we would invite everyone up for refreshments and Irish coffee," said Breen. "My husband made a great Irish coffee."

For years Breen has helped organize the holiday display, sending out flyers notifying Greystone residents when the Christmas trees will go up.

Breen said passersby stop to take photos of the holiday display all the time.

"It's very picturesque and it's a tradition that shouldn't go away," she said.

Another big fan of the Greystones holiday display is the Jackson Heights Beautification Group.

"It's spectacular," said Edwin Westley, the nonprofit's secretary.

The Greystones are not the only buildings in the neighborhood keeping the holiday season merry and bright.
Elm Court, an eight-building complex on 79th and 80th Sts. between 35th and 34th Ave., also has a colorful display similar to the Greystones.

"It's been going on for 50 years," said Elm Court resident Martin Maier, 82. "It's a tradition or a custom, whatever you want to call it."

Other co-op buildings that decorate with seasonal displays - some of them quite elaborate - include: Dunolly Gardens, 78th and 79th Sts. between 35th and 34th Aves; Hawthorne Court, 76th and 77th Sts. between 37th and 35th Aves.; the Jackson, 35-50 78th St.; the Manchester 77-11 35th Ave.; the Griswold 86-10 34th Ave., and the Saxony, 86-11 34th Ave.

In addition, the beautification group held a Christmas tree-lighting ceremony in front of the Post Office on 37th Ave. last week and today a Menorah lighting will be held at the historic building.
rgearty@nydailynews.com
