Title: Re: recommendation for contact lenses
Post by: Shelby2 on June 20, 2017, 11:14:01 PM
I usually buy my contacts online. I was using VisionDirect, but they were purchased by Walgreens. What I used to do was mark my calendar to know exactly what date my prescription was expiring, and then if my eyesight seemed ok just before that date, I would buy another 6 months or a year's worth of contacts prior to the prescription expiring in order to avoid having to go in for another exam right away. That won't help in this case since yours is already expired, but if you end up going for an exam, perhaps you could use that method to help you extend the time next time around.

I just joined Costco so will probably be getting my exam and purchasing my contacts there next time.

I use the same exact strategy  ;D

I go to Optics & Beyond in Flushing - only a couple blocks away from Main St subway station - and get a standalone contact lens exam, which costs $50 (it may slightly higher now). The doctor only comes in on certain days, so I would call to check which ones. They will provide you with a written prescription after the exam. Then, I would purchase my contacts from VisionDirect, which was recently taken over by Walgreens. I just visited the Walgreens website and they still carry the lens in my prescription - rarer toric ones - at a similar price, so I will probably continue with them in the future. Finally, just before the 1-year expiration date, I'd order another year's worth of lens.
