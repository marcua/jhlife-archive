If this is a "hello" thread- I will say hello too.

I am Shelby2.  I chose my username after the small town of Shelby, Montana, where my dad lived once upon a time when he was growing up.  Even though I'm originally from Massachusetts and my dad is from the west, my mother's family is from right here in Queens. My mother was born in Flushing; my grandmother graduated from Hunter College High in 1918, and I had a great aunt living in Elmhurst and another one living in Whitestone.

I've been in Jackson Heights for almost two years and Brooklyn before that.  I'm in the arts and am finding more and more that JH is a great neighborhood for people in this field.

I'm also on the Astorians forum so I guess we'll see each other here and there.

Restaurants & Food / New Trader Joes in Queens
« on: November 11, 2007, 03:34:23 PM »
Has anyone been yet?  I just went this morning at 11 am.  The parking lot was quite full but I still got a space.  On a Sunday morning it was a breeze to drive there from JH.  I drove on 76th St. to 45th Ave to Queens Blvd to Woodhaven to Metropolitan.  I came back on Metropolitan to 69th St. all the way to JH.

It's a nice store - very wide aisles - guess they did their homework before building the store.  There's a wide range of products there and it's 10 times more pleasant than shopping in the Manhattan one.

No wine, though - you'll have to go the Manhattan TJ's special wine store for that.

Restaurants & Food / Re: The Obligatory JH Starbucks Thread
« on: October 29, 2007, 09:45:31 AM »
I'm looking forward to it - I check for progress every time I walk by. . . but unfortunately I'm not seeing any!

And perhaps this should be a different thread, but everyone always talks about how the rents on 37th Ave are prohibitively expensive.  I still don't understand how all those 99 cent stores stay alive.  Do they really sell that many items?  How about that absolutely giant health and beauty items store on 37th between 83rd and 84th that always seems to be empty?  I heard the rent is $17,000 a month.  That's a lot of shampoo.

So back to the point - why is it so hard to attract better businesses to the district when these other stores are managing to stay alive?  At one point when people thought Starbucks was not coming, I heard this tossed around as a potential reason - that they demand lower rent spaces as they tend to be anchor stores for a neighborhood and rents on 37th Ave were too high.
