My guess is that many of the businesses along Roosevelt are already successful -- at least by the number of customers you see patronizing various stores/ restaurants, particularly  on weekends.  It is not really clear to me that these already profitable businesses would gain that much by implementing a BID, in terms of increased foot traffic, cash flow etc.   

Also, not really sure how much a BID can realistically accomplish in terms of general aesthetics. I think Roosevelt will always be a bit noisy/gritty as long as the 7 remains an elevated line -- which is likely to be for a very long time to come.   (Although its a ways before my time, I understand that 3rd Avenue in Manhattan was very gritty up until the 1950s, when the El line was finally demolished.)   

And while I know this is anathema to many people on this board, Roosevelt has its own urban charms.  For many people, the image of an elevated line rattling over a somewhat grimy but very bustling street of bars, stores and eateries is quintessentially New York. Why not just embrace that? 

Particularly when the rest of Jackson Heights is filled with such architecturally distinguished buildings, tree-lined and tranquil streets, lovely gardens etc. -- and is overall a very pleasant place to live.
