Neighborhood Chat / Re: Numbers is gone
« on: August 29, 2013, 04:22:13 PM »
Quote from: jeanette on August 16, 2013, 04:32:50 PM
I Live Here Too, I find it incredulous that all these pharmacies are fronts for "something" (illegal activities, presumably). Perhaps these stores belong to something bigger, a cartel-like association. You know that Big Pharma supports the chains; wouldn't they put out for a big buyer made up of little guys? Jeanette

Regarding the pharmacy situation - the truth is somewhere in the middle.  What they're doing isn't something as insidious as dealing cocaine or something, but it does involve um...creative uses of medicare/medicaid/govt health plans that allows them to pay the rent.  The customer is also happy because they get to go to a pharmacist that usually speaks their language and will throw in an extra month or two, free samples, etc. as a favor.

A lot of these types of pharmacies thrive in ethnic neighborhoods w/ a decent percentage of elderly medicare recipients across the city, so it's not a problem unique to JH.  I am familiar with the situation in Chinatown, where you'll find tons of similar independent pharmacies.  They are guaranteed a customer base that trusts them since it's not a sterile CVS setting with an English-speaking pharmacist behind the counter.  The fraudish practices usually looks like this: an elderly patient with typical problems like say, hypertension and arthritis on medicare will get prescribed by their doctors (also within the community) a bunch of bells and whistles - multiple prescriptions, canes, etc. that they know the pharmacies would get reimbursed  for.  The patients, who tend to trust people within their own community, don't usually question these things and accept the freebies like canes or orthopedic shoes.  These practices run the gamut - I'd like to believe that most of the time it's just lil extras like getting extra gear that they probably need anyway, but other times it's wide-scale reimbursement fraud.  There are busts on a semi-routine basis (notice both are in ethnic neighborhoods as well): http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/bronx/bronx-pharmacist-sentenced-medicaid-fraud-rap-article-1.1420443
http://www.nydailynews.com/news/trio-brooklyn-pharmacists-reimburse-state-400-000-false-medicaid-bill-scam-article-1.1032545

It's a shame because fraud aside, I do think there's a natural need for their translation services and it's very understandable why an elderly immigrant would trust a Spanish, Bengali, or Chinese speaking pharmacist over the guy at Duane Reade.  It's one of those moral gray area type things - the way the proprietors see it, they found an interesting sorta-legal niche (or not legal, but they keep getting approved for reimbursement anyway), they profit from it, and the community they serve is happy, so....

Just some of the joys of having our wacky decentralized medical system!
