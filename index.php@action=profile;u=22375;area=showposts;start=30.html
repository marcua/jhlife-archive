Neighborhood Chat / Re: Time for a limit on all these flea markets
« on: November 10, 2016, 10:26:29 PM »
Sorry, I've been away. Thanks to all those who've added to a nicely civil discussion. Yes, it's every week (or virtually every week). To address the "what's the problem?" point of view, first, as I said, there's the blocking of traffic, the blocking of sidewalks, the trash left behind, and the wrecked aesthetics of that particular part of the neighborhood.

But there's a larger issue, which is, why do we even have zoning? To those who don't think we should uphold the zoning in cases like this, the logical consequence is that we'd have people selling stuff on blankets everywhere and anywhere (something close to reality in midtown, it sometimes seems). I really don't think this would improve the quality of life in our city, and I wonder why legitimate businesses would persist in paying high rents (covering crucial property taxes), salaries, payroll taxes and so forth if anyone can set up anywhere and sell anything. How about if we look the other way while the Methodist church erects a skyscraper on its property? Where does it end? I agree with whoever made the comment that a flea market a couple times a year would be fine. But it would also be enough.

Neighborhood Chat / Time for a limit on all these flea markets
« on: October 18, 2016, 01:07:49 AM »
At the risk of raining on somebody's parade, I want to register a note of dissent on all our never-ending neighborhood flea markets. The Methodist church at the corner of 81st and 35th Ave. has a vast flea market every single weekend that takes over the entire block, wraps around the corners, blocks traffic with double-parked cars, and leaves trash all over the place in the aftermath. These institutions are not zoned for retail; they are houses of worship. Nobody objects to an occasional flea market, although I'm not sure who benefits beyond the vendors. But churches and schools in residential neighborhoods should not be turned into open air bazaars on a continual basis, at least not without a change in zoning (which I can't imagine will be forthcoming). These aren't retail establishments, nor should they be. The one at the Methodist church in particular has become a reliable weekend blight. Enough already.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Espresso 77 is expanding and we need your help!
« on: September 13, 2015, 10:41:48 AM »
While I'm delighted Espresso 77 is expanding, I'm not willing to donate for this purpose, and in my view they should offer either a share of the profits or a commensurate amount of food/drink. I'm happy to donate for feeding the hungry, for fighting disease, for supporting nonprofit cultural institutions. But I have a small business of sorts, and it's hard to see why I should donate to theirs when they aren't donating to mine. They produce positive externalities, but so do I. And this isn't about rescuing a beloved business that is failing; on the contrary, they have a second outlet, in Park Slope no less, and by expanding they'll have a chance to increase their profits in JH. I wish them every success at this, and I look forward to spending money in their expanded digs. But the case for donating seems to me extremely weak.
