This was happening in DC when I lived there (moved back to NYC in 2001).  Quite a few new condo buildings were completely smoke free, including the inside of apartments. 

The thing is, these were almost all new construction, so it was easier to enforce - people that smoked wouldn't even try to buy in the building since they knew they would be busted and fined.  I don't see how it would work in an already-established condo/coop.  I have no issue with smoke-free buildings and it would actually be a plus to me if I were looking to buy.

I think we are just going to see a lot more of these types of creative smoke-free initiatives as people continue to become less tolerant of cigarette smoke.

Hi Eddie,

You're right that it is definitely difficult to decide where to draw the line in regulating behavior that harms others.  However, smoking is clearly on its way out as an acceptable activity at all in this city (and country more broadly).  Places of business used to have spittoons, and they went away.  Then ashtrays and indoor smoking outside of private residences went away.  My place of work is currently trying to stop smoking within several feet of the front of our buildings.

We have emissions regulations for vehicles that will almost certainly get more stringent in the years to come (already many cars sold in NY state must adhered to "partial zero emissions" standards, taxis will all be hybrids or similar soon, and many of the MTA buses are natural gas).  My coop has rules against offensive cooking odors, although I don't know that they are ever enforced (and non-toxic food odors in the hallway don't offend me so much, but my household is probably an offender).  Many coops require rugs to cover so much % of floor space to reduce noise.  All of these regulations seem reasonable to me to protect health and enjoyment of people's private property.  And coops are voluntary, i.e. nobody is forced to buy in to them, so they are allowed broad leeway in restricting activities within the building.

The flushing rule is obviously over the line!
