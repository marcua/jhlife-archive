Re: Forest Hills Barnes & Noble closing
« Reply #14 on: September 02, 2015, 07:38:55 PM »
I worked full-time at a small independent bookstore chain in the mid-nineties.  It was a great place to work.  I loved books, and I didn't drink coffee.  Then a B&N moved in about 100 yds down the road.  They sold cheap books, and coffee.  My manager swore we would never sell coffee, but within 6 months we had a small self-serve coffee kiosk and a growing clearance section.  Within another 6 months the entire bookstore chain went out of business.

Since then I've purchased many books at B&N, simply because there was little real choice available.  Over time I actually came to enjoy browsing there the same I would enjoy at other book shops.  So when I hear about a bookstore closing I always feel a bit sad, even if it is a B&N.  The people who work there love books, and so do the people who shop there.  And all those people are losing in this situation.

But still there is a part of me that is sadistically happy to see this happen to B&N... truly, what goes around comes around.  It somehow feels like just a little bit of justice.

(oh yeah, I still don't drink coffee.)
