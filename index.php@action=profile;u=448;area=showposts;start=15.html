Neighborhood Chat / Art classes for children, young adults and adults in Jackson Heights!
« on: January 06, 2014, 12:06:51 PM »
Good afternoon to all of you and happy 2014 !!!!
 
Have you considered registering your child to art classes? If so, why not do it NOW? ART FOR A START Spring semester is starting this week!!! Attached is the class schedule.  Feel free to contact me with any questions or inquiries you may have. 
 
Yours,
Deborah
www.artforastartnyc.com
 
 
 
I recently read an article in the NYTimes entitled: Art Makes You Smart http://www.nytimes.com/2013/11/24/opinion/sunday/art-makes-you-smart.html?_r=0) and I could not agree more.  Working as an art educator for over fifteen years, I  witnessed how exposure to art has contributed to the overall well being of my students and to their mental, verbal and creative development. Having tutored art students for high schools and universities, it's evident that the development of drawing capabilities and design tools from an early age is invaluable.  My arts program is Inquiry-based, no crafty and cookie-cutter projects, no rigid emphasis on techniques. The curriculum emphasizes  explorations, skill building, creativity and experimentation. My life's mission is to share my passion for the arts through my artwork and teaching.
 
 
ART FOR A START 2014 SPRING SEMESTER
 
*Spring Semester starts on January 7th and will end on June 25th.
 
All regular classes are one hour long and the fee is $15.00 per class, including materials. We create a thematic and well-planned curriculum and send weekly emails informing parents about our plan for the week. Subjects to explore throughout the year:
 
-       Techniques in drawings
-       Techniques in painting
-       Collage techniques
-       Sculpture and 3-d
-       Watercolors
 
All classes are taught by artist and educator Deborah Wasserman. Our program is considered by many as exceptional, and we have students who are now registering for their third year.

 
Regular weekly classes
Art for a Start offers four sessions per week at P.S. 69
(37th Avenue between 77th and 78th Street):
 
•                Tuesdays 4:00PM-5:00PM and 5:00PM-6:00PM
•                Wednesdays 4:00PM-5:00PM and 5:00PM-6:00PM.
 
The 4:00PM classes are geared towards younger students (ages 5-7) and the 5:00PM classes are geared towards older students (ages 7-9). If your child's schedule cannot accommodate these particular age groups, we suggest that you still take one or two trial classes, as many students end up thriving with younger/older peers.
 
- Students can take up to two trial classes before you make a full commitment for one semester. Trial classes are paid classes.
 
- Fees: We require a first month and last month payment for each semester when registering plus a $10.00 registration fee. The last month payment covers the fee for the month on which the semester ends.  If you choose to leave the program before it ends, you will lose your last month payment. We are open to make exceptions to this rule in the case of an emergency or any unplanned circumstances.
 
- Enrollment is capped at 10 students per class. Keeping our classes small allows us to give more personal attention to each student.
 
- Make up classes are available. Tuesdays and Wednesdays classes are very similar in age and content.  You are welcome to bring your child to any of these classes if he or she missed their regular class.
 
Saturday Advanced classes - A walk from Sunnyside!
Saturday classes take place at Mondo Studios in Woodside
(33-55 55th Street between 37th Ave. and Broadway, one block from the M/R Northern Blvd subway station).
 
•                10:00AM-11:00PM
•                11:00:00-12:00 - Private class, not available
•                12:00 to 1:00PM
 
Saturday Advanced classes are one hour long, specialized lessons with no more than five students per class. Featuring an in-depth exploration of techniques and the use of professional materials. Students work progressively on elaborate projects over a period of weeks.
 
-The fee for advanced classes is $25.00 per session.
- Enrollment is capped at 5 students per class.
 
 
PRIVATE CLASSES: We are available to schedule private classes throughout the year.  Please contact us if interested in arranging a meeting to discuss the possibility of a private art class.   We offer portfolio preparation class for teenagers and young adults who are applying for art schools and are seeking to build a strong presentation. We also offer private classes for young students who are interested in more focused, artistic training.
 
 
REGISTRATION:
 
Tuesdays:
 4:00PM-5:00PM, Ages 5-7 Location: P.S. 69. Still open for registration.
 5:00PM-6:00PM, Ages 7-9 Location: P.S. 69. Still open for  registration.
 
Wednesdays:
 4:00PM-5:00PM, Ages 5-7 Location: P.S. 69. Still open for registration.

Hello folks,

ART for a START, the visual arts program in Jackson Heights, is  now registering for the fall semester. We serve kids, young adults and adults, ages 3-20. 
 
Last year we had more than 45 students attending our program on a weekly basis. We offer high quality art classes while encouraging: Creativity, expression, imagination and skill building. Classes are divided into age groups and take place at P.S. 69 and in student's homes.  We also offer private classes on weekends, at Mondo Mundo Studios in Woodside.   

Check out some of our student's work from last year's Queensboro Bridge Art Contest (http://www.licartsopen.org/?page_id=262).  We have one child winning or receiving an honorable mention in each and every age group. 
More images of student's work can be found on our web site.

Please take a minute to read the info below, or check out our web site
for the fall schedule at: www.artforastartny.com.

If you choose to register, contact us directly at: debwass@earthlink.net
or send us an email via ART for a START web site.

Looking forward to another successful year!

About ART for a START:


With school budgets being drastically cut, one of the first subjects to be eliminated is the art class. Children are magnificently creative.  They want to make art, explore, touch and experiment with materials. They need to make art as much as they need to learn how to read, write, do math exercises etc... To fill this critical void, we will be conducting art classes for children to explore the exciting and enjoyable process of art making.  Working out of a centrally located space (P.S. 69) in Jackson Heights, this convenient location allows parents to catch up with some local errands or shopping while your child is in class.

 
Philosophy and curriculum:

 
All classes will be conducted under the supervision of Deborah Wasserman.  Deborah has been teaching art to children and adults for over 15 years.  She has worked in the Art Education Departments of many major museums in NYC (the Guggenheim Museum, The New Museum of contemporary Art, The Bronx Museum, the Jewish Museum, Rotunda Gallery, The College of New Jersey) and has developed an â€˜inquiry basedâ€™ approach to teaching art. An open-ended dialogue replaces â€˜Lecturingâ€™ and the students will be encouraged to create their own vocabulary and set of tools to perceive, understand and make art. The main emphasis of these classes is on the process of art making and the wondrous exploration of the studentâ€™s unique vision. None of the projects assigned to the students is a â€˜Cookie cutterâ€™.  The projects are designed in such a way that students will employ their imagination, make choices, learn techniques, discover new materials and get creative! If time permits,  Deborah will lead monthly museum tours for families and children as an extension of these classes.

Deborah Wasserman's biography:

Born in Sao Paulo, Brazil and raised in Israel, Deborah Wasserman is currently living and working in Queens, NY.  She is a graduate of the California Institute of the Arts and the Whitney Museum Independent Study Program with a Masters and a Post Masters degree.  Deborah Wasserman has received grants from the Experimental Television Center, Skowhegan School of Painting and Sculpture, Aljira Center for the Arts and the America-Israel cultural foundation. Her work has been exhibited in the USA, Brazil, the Netherlands, Germany and Israel, in such venues as The Bronx Museum of the Arts, The Tel Aviv Museum of the Arts, The World Wide Video Festival, White Columns, Gale Gates Gallery, GenArt and A.I.R gallery.
