Neighborhood Chat / Petition to Keep Travers Park Car Free & Safe - Please sign!
« on: April 15, 2019, 11:57:40 PM »
Please sign this petition to keep Travers Park car free!   https://www.change.org/p/koeppel-madza-keep-travers-park-safe-and-car-free?recruiter=360972628&utm_source=share_petition&utm_medium=facebook&utm_campaign=psf_combo_share_initial&utm_term=psf_combo_share_abi&recruited_by_id=87abf0c0-421f-11e5-b92a-f99d3786bc83&utm_content=fht-14952941-en-us%3Av5

As the $6 million renovation of Travers Park enters its final phase, the De Blasio Administration is attempting to quietly break their commitment to close 78th Street completely to cars as a result of pressure from Koeppel Mazda, and use public money that was earmarked for the last stage of park renovations to build a dangerous, private driveway for Koeppel instead.
 
This breaks a commitment from the NYC Department of Transportation, is an egregious misuse of tax dollars, and creates a dangerous situation in which cars and park-goers will be in open conflict.

Koeppel Mazda’s new service entrance falls within the redesigned park’s boundaries and will endanger this vitally important community plan to expand open space, combat climate change, and protect the park-goers from conflicts with dangerous motor vehicles.

Jackson Heights suffers from having the least amount of open space per capita in New York City as well as a high rate of pedestrian fatalities, in particular on Northern Boulevard. For the past ten years, the community been working closely with Council Member Dromm to permanently close 78th Street between Northern Blvd and 34th Avenue to all vehicular traffic to facilitate expanding Travers Park.

Restaurants & Food / Sign Up For Farm Spot Summer 2016!!
« on: April 24, 2016, 10:19:39 AM »
Interested in eating local?  Want to eat organic?  Want to support sustainable farming?

You can do all three by joining Farm Spot, Jackson Heights’ Community Supported Agriculture program!

During the harvest season, beginning May 26th ending on November 17th 2016, Farm Spot members come to St. Mark’s Church (33-50 82nd Street) on Thursdays anytime from 5:30-8:15 p.m. to pick up a share of produce each week.

Since Farm Spot is a volunteer-run endeavor, each member is asked to give a minimum of 4 hours of their time during the year to keep the CSA running. Volunteer responsibilities primarily involve working at weekly distribution, but can also be fulfilled by participating in the low income task force, data entry, special events and vendor recruitment. Please visit the volunteering page for more information.

Membership includes a weekly newsletter from the farm with recipes and information, and CSA events include cooking demonstrations and farm trips.

Click here to sign up!! http://csa.farmigo.com/join/farmspot/summer2016

when and where can I pick up my share?

On Thursdays between the hours of 5:30 and 8:15 p.m. at St. Mark’s Church at 33-50 82nd Street. Enter through the 82nd Street gate, and come through the backyard. In good weather, we’ll be outside; in the rain, snow or cold, we’ll be indoors on the first floor.

how much produce will I get each week?

There are between 6 and 10 items in the share each week. During the summer and autumn months, the shares are much fuller than during the first few weeks in the spring. If you are a large family eating a primarily vegetarian diet, you might consider ordering more than one full share. See harvest schedule for more information.

vegetable share

$587.08 includes $548 paid to the farmer, + $11 administrative and operating expenses + $25 subsidized share fees + $3 Just Food Network Fee

This is the standard 26-week membership share.This share contains 6 to 10 items each week, depending on the season and availability. Shares may include items such as: In spring: Arugula, Lettuce, Radishes, Strawberries, Sugar Snap Peas, Swiss Chard, Beets. In summer: Watermelon, Potatoes, Garlic, Red Tomatoes, Basil, Green Beans, Eggplant, Zucchini In fall: Fennel, Potatoes, Lettuce, Broccoli, Sweet Potatoes, Carrots, Toscano Kale, Winter Squash, Celeriac. The amount is enough to feed a family of three to four for a week. Many Farm Spot members split one share between two households. Please note that members organize this on their own and Farm Spot does not coordinate half shares.
26 weeks / $587.08 26 weeks (May 26th and ending on November 17th)

fruit share

$102.90 includes $96 paid to the farmer + $6 administrative and operating expenses
Golden Earthworm Farm is teaming up with Briermere Farms to offer a non-organic, yet responsibly grown, minimally treated fruit share. The Fruit Share will run for 15 weeks from July through October. It will consist of tree fruit only – apples, peaches and pears.  3-4 lb bag of fruit per week.  One portion per week.

subsidized vegetable share
Farm Spot remains committed to making our CSA financially accessible to our neighbors, and we are once again able to offer 15 subsidized shares for the upcoming season. The subsidies are funded by your fellow Farm Spot members. This year we’ve made some changes to the qualifications to increase access for people needing more financial assistance, so please read the following carefully before submitting your registration.

Subsidized shares are now available at two levels, depending on your income:

Share A, at a cost of $401.44 is for people who fit into the guidelines adapted from the Self-Sufficiency Standard for the City of New York: One Adult: $27,919• One Adult, One child: $47,854• One Adult, Two Children: $62,420• Two Adults, Two Children: $68,175. A minimum of a $75 deposit is required upon registration, and you may pay the balance in full or by monthly installments during the six months of the growing season at $50 per month.

Share B, at a cost of $239.72 is for people who fit into the guidelines based on the WIC program by the size of each household: One person: $21,775 • Two people: $29,471 • Three people: $37,167 • Four people: $44,863 (for families of five and up, add +7,696 per person). A minimum of a $50 deposit is required upon registration, and you may pay the balance in full or by monthly installments during the six months of the growing season at $50.00 per month.

Income verification is on the honor system — we won’t ask for proof of your income. If you have substantial resources beyond earned income, please be mindful of those who don’t and purchase a full-priced share instead. We cannot offer discounts on any share other than the vegetable share.

Click here to sign up!! http://csa.farmigo.com/join/farmspot/summer2016

For more information e-mail us at farmspot@gmail.com

Neighborhood Chat / Come Meet The Farm Spot CSA Farmers This Thursday!
« on: April 19, 2016, 10:33:53 PM »
Farmer Presentation at Farm Spot 
Thursday, April 21st, 5:30-7:00pm
St. Mark’s Church 33-50 82nd St. Jackson Heights

Ever wondered what Farm Spot is and how it works?  Want to learn more about Community Supported Agriculture? 

Come meet Maggie Wood and Matt Kurek, the farmers from Golden Earthworm Organic Farm, the farm that provides all the vegetables for Farm Spot! Come see what CSA is all about! Meet some of your Jackson Heights neighbors!
Raffle of a Golden Earthworm t-shirt and tote bag!

Farm Spot is the Jackson Heights CSA and has been running for 10 years. At the end of May, we will begin our 11th year! CSA (Community Supported Agriculture) is a model where neighborhood groups partner with a local farm. Each member of the group pre-pays for a share of the farm’s bounty. This model is beneficial to everyone! It gives the farmers a guaranteed income, which they receive upfront, when they need it most; it gives each member very fresh, affordable, organic produce that was grown locally (in our case, the farm is 70 miles away), and also a chance to get to know our farm and our farmers).

Maggie and Matt will talk about the history of the farm, which is in Jamesport, Long Island. She will also discuss how a CSA works, the many different types of vegetables grown on the farm, the challenges of running a certified organic farm, who works on the farm, and much more!

There will be a raffle of a Golden Earthworm t-shirt and tote bag which will be announced at 6:45pm!

Stop by with your questions and curiosity about CSA. Farm Spot is now taking new members for the 2016 season, which starts at the end of May.

All are welcome!!

farmspot@gmail.com
