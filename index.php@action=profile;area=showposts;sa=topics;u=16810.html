Neighborhood Chat / Attracting new business to the neighborhood
« on: May 08, 2013, 10:33:54 AM »
I really wanted hummus one night and didn't know of any place in JH with a good hummus. I stopped by a hole-in-the wall Mediterranean/Asian/Indian place (I know, weird combo) and decided to give them a try. It turns out that the place was new and the owner was very chatty so I started talking with him. I told him that I came from JH and it would be nice to have some more food options in the area. I asked if he had considered JH when he was looking for locations for his restaurant.  He said that he was very familiar with JH as he has family members that live there.  He decided against JH because he didn't think the neighborhood could support his restaurant.  His take was that JH has more families and working class people who don't have the discretionary income/won't go out as often. He felt that Astoria has more young professionals who are interested in eating out, so he had a greater chance for success in that neighborhood. 

I was kinda disheartened by that. I wonder how many other people decide not to open here because they feel the same way.  It appears that he might be right, just going by observation, but I haven't looked at the census info or anything to know for sure. Has anybody had a similar experience? Is there any way to change the perception.  Going by this forum, it seems like there are lots of people here who are interested in a wider variety of food options.

I apologize in advance if this topic has been covered to death and I missed it.

Neighborhood Chat / Doors at 74th Street- Roosevelt train station
« on: April 04, 2012, 05:34:01 PM »
I apologize if this is a duplicate post. I recently got my finger smashed between two doors at the Roosevelt-74th street train station. The doors are on the side facing Roosevelt--- across the street from Dunkin Donuts. Upon further investigation, I noticed that there's a high probability for injury with those doors. The handicap-access door and the door immediately to the right of it open onto each other and have heavy metal handles. If you are trying to be nice, holding the door for someone else could be very dangerous there. I'm not sure why those doors don't open away from each other instead of onto each other, but they should.

I reported it to the MTA station manager, but I doubt anything will be done until someone sustains a more serious injury.  Has anybody else had any experiences with those doors?
