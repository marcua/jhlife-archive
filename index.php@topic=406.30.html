Real Clear Politics is a "poll of polls," that aggregates polls from lots of sources to (hopefully) arrive at a result that is more robust than any individual poll.  Although it is a politically conservative site, I think its methodology is broadly respected, since I see it cited frequently on progressive websites. 

That said, I prefer a different site, because they take pains to be as statistically valid as possible, and also have really cool looking graphics:
http://www.fivethirtyeight.com/

They run 10,000 scenarios daily, based on the probabilities of each candidate running each state.  According to their scenarios, Obama currently has a 53% chance of winning the election.  Definitely still in "toss-up" territory!

The next few weeks should be very interesting!  Anybody planning to watch the elections?
