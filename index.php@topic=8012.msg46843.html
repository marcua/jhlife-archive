Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

I call the southeast corner of 91st St. and 34th Ave. "Poop Corner" because of - well, I'm sure you can figure out why. I wonder what I look like to people across the street or looking out the window when I'm doing a hop, skip and jump to try to avoid it.

I often see "presents" left right in front of someone's gate or walk. Honestly, I can think of very few things that are ruder than that. Pretty much the fecal equivalent of slapping someone in the face. I can not, for the life of me, imagine the mentality behind someone who would do that. Very similar to littering, but much more disgusting.

I try not to do "what I would change about Jackson Heights" posts, especially since I've lived here less than two years, but yes, I'd love to see an end, or at least a reduction, of this kind of behavior.

And before any one mentions it, most dog owners I know are very vigilant about this, so this message is aimed at the "1%."

my ex and i owned a house on 75th street -- one of the "spanish" ones -- back in the late '90s. to compound matters, we had lush hedges AND a fire hydrant in front of the house. i was stunned that dog owners would walk away from a mess with one of us sitting right on the porch.

several times, i confronted people for the offense, and they always treated me like i was nuts. my favorite was the girl who said, 'i don't have any paper.' i handed her a met food circular from the porch, she cursed at me in spanish, and i cursed back in the same language, which surprised her to no end. i feel fortunate that i now have an in crediblyconscientious  super-porter combo, though i feel sorry for them for this task.

p.s. one other trick i pulled a couple times was to scoop the poop myself, and follow the offender -- usually on the other side of the street -- and deposit the package on THEIR doorstep.
