News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Well, my kid goes to school in Astoria and I work in Manhattan, so ...

But I understand that's not what you mean. Here's what immediately comes to mind
* Higher quality "white bourgie people" brand groceries at Fairway, also olives and cheese <-- this much more than anything else
* Gluten-free burgers or GF American comfort food at Bareburger or Schnipper's
* Cocktail bars
* Parks, most specifically Sunnyside and Central

Except for the last one, though, I understand that I am part of a tiny minority in a neighborhood that consists mostly of lower-income Latino and South Asian people, and so there are not enough people like me here to support those kinds of businesses. And that's OK! Because one of the top reasons I moved to JH was the absolutely excellent subway access. I can bring a grocery bag home from the parts of town where everybody looks like me.
