Neighborhood Chat / Re: Target coming to jackson Heights
« on: May 03, 2017, 04:14:10 PM »
MrPlaza is why I come to this board. Smart, insightful comments about our (evolving) neighborhood.  Thank you.

I would also like to throw this into the mix: a store like Target will hire more employees than a typical store does.  Many of the family-owned stores in JH understandably hire family first.   More hires also come in part because of another benefit: longer hours.  Check out the hours for a comparable store in Forest Hills: Mon-Fri 7am to 10pm. Sat 8am-11pm. Sunday 8am to 10pm.  The working class, the working poor, and let's face it, the average New Yorker, works LONG HOURS and will benefit from having a store open those hours.

The reality is that theater space sat empty, boarded and blighted for years at this point and having another economic engine chugging in our neighborhood will lift retailers of all stripes.

There is NO such thing as "sanctuary district" regardless of the bizarre statement from our state senator.   It’s a legally meaningless statement on all accounts and it’s insulting to the public to make such claims.  This is so clearly an attempt by Senator Peralta to deflect attention from his embrace of Trump and the Republican Party in Albany.   Why would he do that? It’s a move designed to give Peralta more power in Albany, however at the same time it also empowers the Republicans to further its agenda.   

Who is Peralta supporting? Men like New York Republican chair Ed Cox who posted this message on Twitter over the weekend mocking Governor Cuomo’s efforts to fight Trump’s attack on cities like New York that protect immigrants from deportation sweeps. "It's all about illegal immigrants not him or his" he wrote. ( see that here: https://twitter.com/ChairmanEdCox/status/825817860626870272 )  That's the man Perlata has cozied up to.

What’s even sadder than that?  The move will reportedly pay Peralta an additional $27,500 stipend on top of his $79,500 per year base pay. ( more on that here: http://nypost.com/2017/01/29/state-senator-switched-parties-because-he-needed-a-pay-raise/ )

Let Senator Peralta know how you feel about that at Peralta's town hall meeting on Friday, February 3, 6-7:30 PM at the Jewish Center of Jackson Heights.
