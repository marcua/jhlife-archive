Neighborhood Chat / Re: Proposed Stadiums, Mall, Garages at Flushing Meadows Park
« on: August 23, 2012, 09:34:39 PM »
A couple of pictures i shot today at the area in question which illustrate my reservations about the plan. The US Open has not even begun, but already fields are being used as parking lots.



This is a field just past the indoor pool, which is normally used for picnicking, not parking cars. From what I could tell, the excess amount of cars were due to CBS staff getting ready for the US Open. Can't wait to see how much space will be required once thousands of fans show up.

As for the "scummy green industrial 'pond,'" which I've read others say is filled with "sewage water," it didn't seem so bad to me. The water seemed no more scummy than any other body of water in New York City parks, and definitely did not smell like sewage. This family of geese seemed to think it was swell.



But we all know geese hate soccer.

At the time I shot these pictures, I did not realize my camera was in black and white mode. You're just going to have to believe me that is was not ooze-green and the geese weren't dying.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: New bike trail planned for Western Queens
« on: May 29, 2012, 10:57:14 PM »
I decided to take a look at the DOT's website for data on bike lanes, and was surprised by how dramatically the creation of new bike lanes has decreased. (link to data) The peak seems to have been 2008, when 81.6 miles of lanes were created. Last year, 15.4 miles were added, and 10.8 miles are scheduled to be added this year (seen here). Only half a mile of bike lanes will be added in Queens.

For those with hopes of more bike lanes in Queens, this is not good news.
