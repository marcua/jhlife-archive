Hi JHALUM

Good observation, there are few different answers. First floor units don’t really need to worry about sound transfer, two of them have soundproof sub flooring , couple had area rugs which we rolled up for shoot , but in general I find carpeting rule gets activated in the event of noise complaints. Buildings where sound insulation between floors is weak, tend to enforce it more vigorously. In most cases area rugs are sufficient. Vacant units usually have carpets removed as well.

Important to understand, that buyers still want to know the condition of floors below carpets, regardless if they want to carpet them wall to wall or use area rugs or other alternative materials. If possible to display floor for sale, it is good practice to do so.
