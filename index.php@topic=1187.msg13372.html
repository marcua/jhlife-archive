I guess I could google this, but I'm wondering if flax seed must be freshly ground before you eat it?  Does this mean you buy the flax seeds whole and then have a designated grinder for them?  (wouldn't want to use the same grinder that my coffee beans go in.)

I like to grind my flax seeds as I use them.  I've found that the ready ground flax seed that is available at many markets tends to go rancid rather quickly...not tasty at all!  From what I've heard, it's best to grind flax seeds because the beneficial oils are released this way.  And I do have a dedicated grinder for the seeds.  It's a small Krups coffee bean grinder that I picked up at Bed, Bath & Beyond for about $20, and I only use it for the seeds.

During the week I, much like Really4Rob, live on DD coffee and horrible bagels, though I only get bagels prepared in ways I would never get at a real bagel shop- like with strawberry cream cheese. Why disappoint myself with a bad everything bagel?

On weekends I'm really big on cooking breakfast at least one day. Most weekends, one day I'll walk up to Bagel House on 90th and get bagels and coffee there or from Espresso 77 if I'm not in the mood to walk so far. I like the coffee better at 77, but the bagels better at Bagel House, so really, it's a draw.

In general I make a lot of eggs, sometimes using up leftovers, sometimes just scrambled with cheese, sometimes fried. I've made very good home fries the past few weeks with some red potatoes from the farmer's market. When fruit is in season, I really like berry pancakes. Pancakes from scratch are some of the easiest and delicious things you can make. Once I realized it was basically as easy as a mix it's all I do. I love throwing crazy things in there too- like pumpkin puree, sour cream or ricotta cheese. Yum!

On Saturday and Sunday I go to the Colombian bakery on 37th between 80th and 81st, south side of the street near Jahn's.  It has the best coffee in the neighborhood and really good bread that is still warm if you go in there around 7:30 or 8:00 am.  Get the brown bread with the raisins or the lighter bread with guava (I think) jelly inside and some white sugar on top.  Great inexpensive food and very friendly ladies behind the counter even if you don't speak Spanish, like me.
