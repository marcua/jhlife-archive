One of the frustrations about street harassment of women is the tendency for some men to play down how big of a problem it really is for women. Street harassment of women is nothing more than letting women know they are not welcome to occupy public space without it being made clear to them they are not truly safe in that space.

That so many men tend to discount it I think is often rooted in the incorrect belief that because because those guys don't see it happen much, it's not a real issue, ignoring the fact that the reason they don't see it much is because the harassers don't do it to women accompanied by men. They also don't understand why something as seemingly innocuous as strange men telling women to "smile" on the street is a statement that women exist for the sole pleasure of men, don't have a right to exist in public without performing for their benefit, nor have a right to an interior life that has nothing to do with men.

Consider getting some pepper spray. They are a little bulky, but you can get ones that you can carry on your key chain (and importantly, can easily be removed and left at home when you're going some place with a security check).

I find if I'm in a public place with others lots of other people around, loud confrontation from a distance does well. A lot of these guys really seem to expect a woman to just quietly take this kind of harassment and often seem bewildered when she speaks out loudly and aggressively in public. Pulling out your phone and letting them know you're calling the police also communicates clearly there could be very negative consequences to their actions. It might not be a tactic for late at night or on a side street when no one else is around, but along a busy commercial area that provides store fronts to pop in to and with enough distance, I think it works well. Women should be encouraged to use their voices loudly and aggressively when someone makes them feel unsafe in a public space.
