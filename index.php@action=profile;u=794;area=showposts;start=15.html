Neighborhood Chat / Re: Education: Common Core
« on: September 21, 2016, 10:04:14 AM »
I'm a mathematician committed to working with math teachers for years (currently at CCNY, previously at Rutgers, and professional development organized by the DOE, Math for America, Museum of Modern Art, other universities, and several schools and teachers' organizations in India).

Most of the teachers that I work with find the Common Core to be an improvement over previous standards, and the best teachers I work with say that it allows them to teach at a  deeper level. Yes, it is asking more of them and their students, but they are committed teachers, and they welcome that.

And everyone hates the testing -- which is not the same thing as the Common Core.

But unfortunately, we are trapped in a discourse that conflates the two, and we can all see the writing on the wall. If we do manage to defeat the constant testing of basic skills, we may lose the Common Core along with it, with nothing better to replace it.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: 7 train service + Q49
« on: November 28, 2015, 09:51:09 PM »
The Q49 is quite erratic, but the MTA Bus Time app (or webpage) is quite accurate if you use it to find out where the buses are at any point in time. On a rainy day I look at it before I leave my apartment so that I don't have to wait long.

BTW, if you want the E train, you could also try the Q32/Q33 which stop at 82nd and 35th on their way to the subway hub. (They go up 81st and 83rd respectively on their way back.)

The Q49 buses seem to disappear from the app when they get to the subway hub. So it's not great for finding out if you will be able to catch a bus to come back home. But the Q32 buses do not. I never take the Q33, so I don't know if those show up or not.
