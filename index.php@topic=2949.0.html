Even when I *do* give a recommendation about something, I usually encourage the person I am giving it to to do their own research by reading yelp reviews, etc.   Just this week I gave someone the name of a mover I had used that I had a good experience with, but I gave it with the caveat that my friend should additionally check yelp and the moving scam site to see if other people had made any complaints.

The other thing I wanted to mention about recommendations on forums is that even when you see what looks like a recommendation from a happy customer and not just a business ad like the one I posted, you should still do more research especially because many companies are using viral marketing where people sign up for online forums and act like satisfied customers in order to bring more business in.

I have seen this happening on many sites.  Usually it's easy to spot by looking at the type of language used and at how many prior posts the member has on the site.  If it's their first post and it gives a glowing review of a business, this is a red flag.  Buyer beware.
