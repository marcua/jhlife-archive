Shalom Chavarim!

There are Jews in Jackson Heights!?  YES! And some of have been talking for awhile about starting a chavurah right here!  Soooo-- LET'S DO IT!

Quote
A chavurah or havurah (Hebrew: "fellowship", plural chavuroth) is a small group of like-minded Jews who assemble for the purposes of facilitating Shabbat and holiday prayer services, sharing communal experiences such as lifecycle events, and Jewish learning. Chavuroth usually provide autonomous alternatives to established Jewish institutions and Jewish denominations.

Most chavurot place an emphasis on egalitarianism in the broad sense (of which gender egalitarianism is one piece), depending on participation by (and welcoming of) the entire community rather than top-down direction by clergy.

The above is a definition from Wikipedia--except for my addition of "and welcoming of". MY definition of "community" includes ALL Jews, Interfaith families, Gay and Lesbian families, singles, all ages raging from pre-natal to most mature.

The first meeting of The New Jackson Heights Chavurah will be:

Friday, January 4, 2008 @ 7PM

The place of our meeting will depend on the numbers of definite RSVPs I receive by December 1, 2007.

We should have a committee involved in planning the service--so, anyone interested in doing this--let me know.  It would be nice to have a mixture of experienced and novice individuals to provide a learning forum for those interested in expanding their knowledge of our liturgy.

So--Please RSVP whether you are coming to the January Chavurah and if you want to be on the service planning committee--to adrucker@spamarrest.com.

B'Shalom-

Abby Drucker
