Community Groups, Activities & Events / Rally Tonight
« on: June 26, 2014, 06:17:40 PM »
East Elmhurst, NY:
The East Elmhurst community will gather with the local advocacy group Make Queens Safer on Thursday 6/26/14 at 6:30pm  at Northern Playground, Northern Blvd and 93rd Street,  to demand assignment of a traffic enforcement officer and other pedestrian safety improvements to protect children crossing Northern Blvd. on their way to this school.  This is in response to a crash in which a kindergarten student and her mother, reported by eye witnesses as crossing in the crosswalk with the light, were victims of a crash on Tuesday 6/24/14 at 8:30 am, on their way to school. They remain hospitalized, the girl with reportedly serious injuries according to school contacts.
 
Community Board 3 voted in favor of DOT safety enhancements on Northern Blvd last week, including 9 new pedestrian safety islands between 61st Street and 102nd Street, acknowledging the necessity of protecting children and all pedestrians, as three children were killed in crashes on Northern,  two of  them on their way to school, since December, 2013.
 
On June 23rd, 2014, Mayor DeBlasio appeared at PS 152, a school on Northern Blvd where an eight-year-old boy was killed on his way to school earlier this year, to sign eleven New York City Vision Zero bills, placing New York City firmly on the path to Vision Zero's ultimate goal of ending all preventable deaths of pedestrians.
 
Make Queens Safer is grateful that the City and our local elected officials recognize, and are acting on, the critical safety hazards, especially to vulnerable populations such as children on their way to school, on Northern Blvd, an arterial thoroughfare which passes through much of Queens. Residents are pleased that the speed limit on Northern Blvd was reduced to 25 mph on the stretch between 40th Road and 114th Street.
 
Make Queens Safer and the PS 228 school community know that these legislative steps are not enough to protect students on their way to school. The rally this evening will call the for the NYPD to assign a traffic safety enforcement officer to protect students on their way to PS 228, all of whom are second graders or younger.
 
Quotations:
 
"While other graduating kindergartners are with their families celebrating a joyous and momentous day, this little girl lies in a hospital bed battling serious injuries after she and her mother were hit by a car as they crossed Northern Blvd on their way to PS 228, a school whose oldest students are second graders. We are relieved that this crash did not end in another Northern Blvd child death, as we continue to grieve the deaths of Miguel Torres, Noshat Nahian, and Jahir Figueroa, all killed crossing Northern Blvd. As a parent, I must find a way to answer my nine-year-old's question: 'I thought we have Vision Zero and 25 mph now, Why are kids still getting hit?' Our community demands safety enforcement officers on Northern Blvd at the crossing for PS 228."  Laura Newman, Make Queens Safer
