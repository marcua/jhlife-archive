Title: Re: Emoji Burger coming to 37th Ave near 81st
Post by: rabucat on December 19, 2015, 06:31:51 PM
My boyfriend and I just went to try out Emoji Burger, and WOW, what a success!  The space is cute inside--a little cramped given the small size, but well built-out, with nice lighting and ambiance for a little neigborhood burger joint.  As for the burgers themselves.  As a devotee of, and regular visitor to, Shake Shack, I can confidently say that that these burgers are just as good, if not better, than Shake Shack's.  Pat LaFrieda beef, Martin's potato buns, the whole nine yards.  Their default doneness is medium, which is just right--the burgers develop that lacy, crispy edge, while still being moist and tender and juicy on the inside.  I had the "Ori Emo," (which is their name for the regular cheeseburger), and my boyfriend had the "Da Wink," which is the regular cheeseburger with applewood bacon.  Both were fantastic.  We also split the Emo Fries, which are fries with bacon bits, grated parmesan, scallion, and the house aioli.  These were also good, though I actually think I might have preferred just plain fries, as the fries themselves were fried to perfection.  One quibble--they bring out packets of ketchup right now, and I think that squeeze bottles at the table might be better (at least for those of us who like generous amounts of ketchup on burgers and fries). 

Short take:  this place, only two days in, is poised to be a neighborhood fixture, if not a destination spot for people in nearby neighborhoods.  SERIOUSLY good burgers! 
Title: Re: Emoji Burger coming to 37th Ave near 81st
Post by: deja on December 19, 2015, 06:51:25 PM
I stopped in this afternoon and got an outgoing order.  It's pretty good.  It's not Minetta Tavern or Spotted Pig good, but hey, it's a fraction of the price and you don't have to hike into Manhattan.  If you feel like a burger and don't feel like leaving the neighborhood it's going to be the place to go.  Nice to see a lot of families, that's good for the neighborhood as is the large number of jobs created for such a small storefront. 

The patties are indeed on the small side.  Since the buns are full size, choice of potato or sesame, the patties are very thin in order to fill the bun.  That unfortunately leaves the single patty burger on the wee bit too little side and the double on the way too much side, at least for me.  The shredded iceberg lettuce did make me unhappy.  The fries are forgettable.  They're just frozen, very thin cut fries.  Not terrible or anything, but meh.

It would have been nice if they had followed through on the concept they had for the meat with the rest of the ingredients.  I.e., fresh cut potatoes, romaine lettuce, etc.  To cut them some slack, I'd bet a lot of that is due to trying to keep to a price point they feel would be viable in this neighborhood.
