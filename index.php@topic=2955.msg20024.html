I bought a couple of nice Persian rugs from a rug dealer who is on the Upper East Side and advertises on craigslist.  So if you are looking for Persian, go to the furniture section of craigslist and type in Persian UES in the search box (don't use quotes).  http://newyork.craigslist.org/search/fua?query=persian+ues&minAsk=min&maxAsk=max

The other place I looked at online was http://www.rugman.com/ but I found the prices for Persians were at least double the prices that the UES dealer was asking.

Other major stores like Ikea that have rugs that you could probably look at online are West Elm, Crate and Barrel, and Pottery Barn.

HomeDecorators.com is excellent.  I have been furnishing my new co-op from scratch & somewhat on a budget, & HomeDecorators (owned by Home Depot) has a vast selection of quality furnishings at reasonable prices.  Moreover, their customer service is excellent.  Items come pretty quickly usually by Fed Ex (MUCH better than UPS), and if anything has even minor damage, they will almost automatically give you a significant discount on future purchases, if you choose (of course, you can return or get a replacement).  Many of their rugs come from a large importer on Long Island & are delivered within a week.

I had very good luck buying "semi-antique" Persian rugs on eBay - all clean, in good condition, and were shipped very quickly.
I bought two huge (10x13) rugs and two long (5x10) rugs, for under $800 combined.

http://www.flickr.com/photos/marcuswoollen/tags/carpet/
I bought from http://shop.ebay.com/ecgeurope/m.html and http://shop.ebay.com/www.ecarpetgallery.com/m.html

For smaller rugs, prices go as low as 25-40 bucks.

We love Persian rugs, and we have animals - so for us - the solution was to get Persian rugs that were nice, but not so nice that we'd cry when the inevitable happens to one of them. PS - so far, the cats love to sharpen their claws on one of the runners, but it's so thick and well-made that it's handling the abuse far better than the cheap Target Store Brand rug we used to have, where we always had to vacuum up pile every few days.

overstock.com is by far the best place I know to buy rugs.  They have a huge selection, great quality, great prices and the shipping costs can't be beat ($2.95 and it will arrive in about 3 days). 

You'd be surprised how many high-end designers (including my best friend who works out in the Hamptons) buy rugs from there.  I have 5 rugs, all from overstock, and I am thrilled with all of them. I get compliments all the time. 

Their furniture is mostly crap, lighting is hit or miss (lots of tacky lamps but a few gems if you search well), and the rugs are outstanding.  I usually shop at much pricier places, but they will always get my business for rugs.
