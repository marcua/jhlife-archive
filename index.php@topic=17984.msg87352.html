deja:  I moved here for the same reason to escape paying a high rent.  I was renting a 700 SF 1 BR Condo in Murray Hill.  The rent in 2003 went from $2000 to $2400.  I had a friend living in JH's so I was familiar with the neighborhood.  I purchased a 900 SF 1 BR with a beautiful garden view for $155,000 in December 2003.  The loan + maintenance was $1190 a monthly a saving of $1210 and an additional 200 SF.  I work from home so I didn't have to live in Manhattan.  The former apartment is now renting for $3500 and I'm currently paying $1410.

Queens has always been at least half the price of Manhattan for twice the space. And the commute can be the same or shorter depending on where one lives. I worked in the West Village for a time and getting to the UES for a meeting could take an hour.

I grew up in Queens and have lived all over the borough...never anywhere else. JH has the best housing stock by far. I was fortunate to have purchased my house 20 years ago this month (wow) and can't imagine staying in NYC and living anywhere else. 

I think Archie Bunker and other TV and film stereotypes (the drug problems in the 1980s didn't help either) hurt the borough's image while Brooklyn had less stigma. I can't remember the last time I heard the term "bridge and tunnel crowd".  That moniker can now apply to residents of Manhattan.

We lived in Gramercy Park for 23 years in a shoebox of an apartment, but an under market rent. When the building got sold they raised the rent and after searching in Manhattan and eventually Long Island City; the latter is where we landed for 3+ years. We got so much more for our money there, a city view, doorman, gym. Alas, it was time to buy and after looking at several places in the Long Island City area and Astoria, where we placed a back up offer on a 1 bed new construction condo, which fell through, then told that they'd accept it after the 1st offer fell through, we said no, after reconsidering price versus size. In general, in both LIC and Astoria, apartments were very expensive for what you didn't get, closet space and square footage. Enter Jackson Heights. We ended up buying a 1000 sq ft co op a year ago; with a walk in closet and a second large closet in the master bedroom, plus a second bedroom, for much less then where we had been looking. The transportation options are great, also a consideration for us. I knew little about Jackson Heights before looking to buy here, but I love the diversity, markets and restaurants. I also enjoy walking the neighborhood and those surrounding us to see what I can see.
