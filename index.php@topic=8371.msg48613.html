New Fruit Vendor at 82nd St & 37th Avenue
« on: May 22, 2013, 02:49:46 PM »
The warm weather has brought with it a new fruit and vegetable vendor at the corner of 82nd Street and 37th Avenue in front of the CapitalOne bank.  I already spoke with the 82nd Street Partnership who said it has contacted the police about this unwanted and unwelcome eyesore.  We have had a food truck parked on the same corner for years now, and I guess it will never leave.  While we all might complain about the quality of our merchants, they are least paying rent and taxes and not crowding our already crowded commercial streets.

It is unfortunate that residents and passers-by patronize these vendors.  Only recently a new grocery opened up just down the block, by 81st Street.

Please make your voices heard, requesting the removal of this vendor from our streets.  Remember, this is likely the first of many to come....  please email or call to complain:

City Councilwoman Julissa Ferraras: 718 651 1917
City Councilman Daniel Dromm:  718 803 6373
115th Precinct: 718 533 2002
Community Board #3: 718 458 2707   communityboard3@nyc.rr.com

In case anyone is wondering, this fruit cart does have their 2013 NYC vendor permit prominently displayed as they should. They have every right to operate their business. Most food-cart vendors are hardworking immigrants and I think their sense of entrepreneurship should be celebrated and encouraged. I also think these vendors add a sense of vibrancy to our streets.

Here is a nice article and video about a Jackson Heights resident who operates a fruit cart in lower Manhattan:

Making Money: The Fruit Vendor
http://www.newyorker.com/online/blogs/newsdesk/2013/05/making-money-the-fruit-vendor.html

Also, answers to some questions:

How Clean Are Those Things? Where Does the Meat Come From?
And other essential street-cart questions answered.
http://nymag.com/restaurants/features/33530/

We are fortunate to have such an abundance of options to purchase healthy fruit and vegetables. Many communities are not so lucky which is why the city initiated the Green Carts program a few years ago:

http://www.nyc.gov/html/doh/html/diseases/green-carts.shtml

Some of jhlifer's concerns are expressed in this article about the Green Carts program:

Customers Prove There’s a Market for Fresh Produce
http://www.nytimes.com/2009/06/11/nyregion/11carts.html?_r=0

I've never heard of a shop going out of business because of competition from a food-cart. Unless someone has some evidence to indicate otherwise, I don't think we should be concerned. It looked like the vendor on 82nd had a nice selection and was doing a brisk business.
