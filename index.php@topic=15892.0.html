Hi everybody,

I can contribute something to this discussion.

First I think it’s important to understand that a 250k one bedrooms (however rare), are hardly considered cheap by a buyer making around 60k annually. As a matter of fact, 30% of their GROSS income (which means literally half of what they actually net after taxes) is applied towards the monthly payment. And that is before they paid to turn on electricity, cable and cooking gas. So “cheap” or “expensive” are subjective terms, and based on an individual’s earnings and resource may differ in opinion, regardless of the price point.

Now, the next question here is, what is the MEDIAN price for a 1 bedroom in Jackson Heights. What we have seen is that most 1 bed units trade between 350k to 400k, some above 400k. The annual income that would keep the payment at 30% ratio (which means literally half of what they actually net after taxes) in these cases would be 90-100k.

There are and always will be a cluster of buildings, that may offer a buying opportunity in lower price points, but it is important to understand that they are few and hardly influence the actual price of the higher price units in other buildings. They rather benefit incrementally from the rise in the prices overall. In direct comparison, buyers likely see different value in one over the other. If the properties were identical, I hardly doubt that a buyer would prefer to pay more for one over the other. Would you agree?

So are 1 bedrooms in Jackson Heights Cheap? Expensive? Medium? Just right? - Yes to all  

Every property has its rightful price, and the buyer who agrees with owner’s perceived value is the one who will likely own it. Hence the term “Fair Market Value”

One of the things that seems to get overlooked *a lot* on this board is that there is a different price point between pre-war buildings and post-war building. And there are a decent number of post-war buildings in JH in the historic district. Just a few years ago you could find a post-war 1 BR that needed work but was livable in the $160-185K range. You can't find that today. You're looking at $250K+ for an apartment that needs work.
