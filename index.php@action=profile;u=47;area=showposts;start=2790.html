Neighborhood Chat / Re: Honorary Street Names
« on: December 01, 2007, 11:01:22 AM »
I scrounged up this article on Cuban born, Diario La Prensa journalist and anti-drug trade crusader Manuel de Dios Unanue.  As many long-time Jackson Heights residents may remember, Unanue was shot to death due to his active involvement in reporting the truth on the Colombian drug trade back in the 80s/early 90s.  If I remember correctly, he was killed inside the restaurant Meson Asturiano (name?) here in Jackson Heights, right off Roosevelt near 83rd/84th Street.  The small square with benches has been dedicated in his honor.

http://backissues.cjrarchives.org/year/93/2/dead.asp

Unfortunately, it seems that Jackson Heights doesn't have any place for dogs to socialize yet.  What some folks opt to do is gather at Travers Park in the evenings (cemented areas) with their dogs.  There have been issues in the past due to parents complaining of unleashed dogs in the children's areas during the summer months, but most dog owners go later in the evenings when there are no children in the playground.  Cleanup issues have also arisen in the past.
