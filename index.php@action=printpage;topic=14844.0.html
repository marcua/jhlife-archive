Hi all! Happy to contribute to this discussion.

Historically coops have held their value well, even during the downturn in the market, since most coop units are owner occupied and owners are 20 % or more (not to mention emotionally) invested in the properties. Quote
Can anyone predict if real estate prices for coops in the historic Jackson Heights neighborhood will go down over the next 1-2 years? I am shocked at how
much prices have increased over the last 3 years (some places have almost doubled in price). Do you suggest waiting for prices to drop or buying now?

The honest answer to the question is no, no-one can make such prediction. Any projections can only state opinion, nothing more. That said, the increase in Jackson Heights prices dates back farther thatn three years. The price climb started as far back as 2011, when I think Jackson Heights prices bottomed out after the 2008 market crash. Since then prices have seen a steady 7-10% annual increase which in a way had a compounding effect. In simple dollars and cents 1 bedrooms increased 15-20k annually where 2 bedrooms as much as 20-30k annually, ultimately reaching today’s pricing levels. In day to day experience we see this is as a steady climb, not an immediate massive trajectory. With this knowledge in hand, it stands to reason that in the event of a market shift, any price declines will be just as slow, rather than a massive drop.

Now, would anyone be advised to wait until prices begin to drop to make a purchase… It would NOT be simply advisable to buy into a market that you believe is dropping. No-one wants to be catching a falling knife. But think about this - when evaluating any investment you evaluate your long term risks and tax implications. When buying company stock for instance, you often go in with the understanding that the value of your portfolio may drop over time, but you believe in its long term growth. Real estate is no exception. You buy it long term for value, tax benefits and hold it as a long term investment.

I hope this helps in your planning. I’d recommend that you speak with your accountant to establish what your tax benefits of ownership may be, it may help with your decision to purchase and manage your long term risk.
