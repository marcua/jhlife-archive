I'm with AmazingJason. 

JH is a small, quiet community on a daily basis.  It's also very diverse and that's what makes it special.  I understand that horns are incredibly annoying and being kept awake is the worst. I also wish horns weren't needlessly used by impatient drivers on a daily basis but there is nothing I can do about that except to move to Long Island or something equally horrible. However, when the horns are used in celebration after a football match, a revered event all around the world, I have to give a pass. Further, the horn beeping only happens after large national games during big tournaments since matches are played each weekend in different leagues and there are no horn celebrations. When you posted, our neighbors were celebrating the Copa America.  In fairness, let our neighbors celebrate a bit. It's not a daily or weekly or even monthly occurrence.

Not sure if you've done this already but next time there's a game, why don't you hit a bar and share in the festivities?  It's a different culture and I wonder if you would have the same complaint over loud fireworks celebrations for Chinese New Year? Food for thought.

I am a long time owner and resident and I worry that JH will become a gentrified 'hood where the "Park Slope" contingency gets louder and washes out all of the quirky specialness out of area. Including the minorities. It's bad enough mom and pop shops are being kicked out for The Gap and such.

Here's wishing you peaceful evenings and I'm sure that duane reade has some ear plugs you can pop in to get some rest on these special occasions.   

Cheers neighbor!



The drunk crying person on your stoop? That happens any day in NYC.  Not only on certain days.  It's the beauty of living here.
