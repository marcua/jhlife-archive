“Elliman Report: Manhattan, Brooklyn & Queens Rentals”

We have just released the “Elliman Report: Manhattan, Brooklyn & Queens Rentals” for January 2016, the leading resource on the state of the rental market.  As always, our market reports are produced in conjunction with Miller Samuel to provide you and your clients with the most comprehensive and neutral market insight available.
 
Although Manhattan rents were higher than last year, they are not rising as quickly.  Landlords are offering more concessions than they have in a number of years and rental price trends tended to be weaker at the top of the market.
 
Market-wide Brooklyn rents were up only slightly after a few months with weaker trends. The new development boom has shifted offerings to larger apartments and inventory has increased.
 
Rental prices in Northwest Queens showed mixed results this month as the luxury market remained weaker than the overall market.  While marketing time declined, negotiability increased.

“Elliman Report: Manhattan, Brooklyn & Queens Rentals”

We have just released the “Elliman Report: Manhattan, Brooklyn & Queens Rentals” for March 2016, the leading resource on the state of the rental market. Our Brooklyn rental report coverage area was recently expanded to include the entire borough. As always, our market reports are produced in conjunction with Miller Samuel to provide you and your clients with the most comprehensive and neutral market insight available.
 
Overall Manhattan rents softened for the first time in two years. Weaker conditions were seenacross apartment sizes as vacancy rates and landlord concessions were than higher than last year’s levels.
 
In contrast, Brooklyn rents moved higher this month after showing weakness in the fall. Like the other boroughs, luxury rents remained the weakest segment of the marketwhile rents for smaller apartments remained strong.
 
The Queens rental market continued to see new units enter the market, accounting for about a quarter of activity. Rental prices slipped with the weaker trends found in the luxury market.
