$75 for the super seems reasonable but if you call on him regularly then maybe more. The super is essential to tip. Title: Re: Tipping Building Staff on Christmas
Post by: am315 on December 10, 2018, 03:46:40 PM
If there's a big staff the other rule of thumb would be to only give to those you really know and have interacted with or those who have assisted you in some particular way.

Most supers live rent free and get a salary. You still ought to tip them before anyobne as they're your go-to when problems arise.

I can't tell you how many times I've heard folks say: `I called my super five times about this issue and I can't get him to come.' Next question I ask: `Did you tip him at Christmas and do you ever tip him when he stops by to do something?' The answer is usually no.

I know that sounds harsh but the reality is the super is a service employee and no one gets rich doing the job. It's often dirty and thankless and not that all well paying. You're expected to be on call 24/7 and deal with all manner of people.

Giving the guy $75 or whatever is reasonable.

Title: Re: Tipping Building Staff on Christmas
Post by: CaptainFlannel on January 01, 2019, 09:38:39 AM
Quote
Tipping is like giving a year end bonus at any other place of employ. Many places do and some places don't. I know it makes a difference to me even if I already have a "good comp package." I don't see how it would be different for staff at a co-op.

I think the difference is that any other place of employment that gives a year end bonus ties the bonus to two things: 1.) your individual performance; and 2.) how the company performed. So it's rather like comparing apples to oranges since coops aren't profit making businesses.

Basically, I and others draw a line between tipping management and tipping non-management. I tip my server at a restaurant, but I don't tip the manager; I tip my hairdresser, but if the owner of the hair salon is doing my hair, I don't tip him/her. I get it if folks want to tip their full-time building super (or do so with a gift of a nice bottle of wine) out of appreciation for hard work and excellence, but I don't get it as a shareholder if the intent of the tip to the super is to ensure the super actually performs the job the individual is hired and paid fairly to do. If the only way to get your super to return your phone call is to give a holiday tip, the super is derelict in his/her basic job description.
