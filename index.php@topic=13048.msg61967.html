We have a Queens film festival and part of it takes place in PS 69.
There are 13 films screening on March 20, Friday night; 6:30, 8:15, 10:00
There are 11 films starting at 12:30 on Saturday March 21; 12:30, 2:30, 4:30
Please support the Queens World Film Festival at PS 69. It is a great opportunity
to see professional grade independently made films you cannot see anywhere else.
QWFF will be showing films outdoors during the summer in Jackson Heights also!
LET'S KEEP THEM HERE IN JACKSON HEIGHTS!

This is fairly weird. This morning I was composing the below email which I sent a bit ago to the JHFamilies list. Afterward I said to myself - why don't I post it on jacksonheightslife as well. P.S. Hi Patti 

Here's my letter from JHFamalies
-----------------------
Neighbors,

The Queens World Film Festival http://queensworldfilmfestival.com is in full swing with 177 films being shown this week in Astoria, Long Island City and here in Jackson Heights.

Overall the festival is doing quite well with the films in Astoria and LIC attracting decent crowds - 150 attended a film at the Museum of the Moving Image Wednesday, and 75% of the seats were filled at the Big Secret Theater in LIC. Here in JH it's being held at P.S. 69.

I attended the 8 PM block at P.S. 69 on Wednesday and saw a great film, Coming Back To The Hoop - stream it. The director, Jane Pittman, who'd flown in from Virginia, took questions.

As a neighborhood booster there was one downer, I was was surprised to see how few neighbors were in attendance. If we don't support the festival it will move to a more arts friendly neighborhood and we'll bemoan the fact that we don't have one here. [By the way, on Wednesday morning, at P.S. 69, a by-product of the festival took place. The 5th grade class showed 4 films made by students, with the assistance of QWFF mentors. And, for the first time ever, one of the films was edited by a
student. Yup, a 5th grader edited a film.]

Let's keep the festival here. Attend a film tonight. They start at 6:30 and go past midnight. There will be 3 "blocks" of films: starting at: 6:30 2 films, 8:15 5 films, 10:00 6 films. And there are films all day tomorrow - noon to 6 pm

Best,

Tom Lowenhaupt
