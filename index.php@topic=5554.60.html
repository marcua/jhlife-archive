Umm.. let's see.. large apt. building with several more people putting that much more demand on already strained city services: streets, roads, sewers, schools, water, sanitation, buses & transportation etc. and the increased demand on electric supply from a very old and VERY outdated system. Hello everyone. Go to Google Maps and enter the address of the Garden City School. Look at the entire block in satellite view and those adjacent to it... Travers Park and the Joseph Pulitzer School. If you really want to expand the neighborhood's park and green space for our kids and families, how about making an offer to the owner(s) of the commercial properties on Northern Boulevard? (Hell, forget making them a fair offer and claim eminent domain!) If you got rid of the Toyota dealership, Blockbuster (soon to be out-of-business anyway) and Mandees, you could expand Travers Park to the entire block, move the children's playground across the street and even include the intermediate school's running track as part of a three block park-and-recreation space. Yes, this proposal would be far more expensive than the Garden School alternative and require a lot more planning, but it would also go much, much further towards making a truly useful green space for the community.
