definitely staying away from:
-food.
-toiletries.
-anything electrical.
-kitchen and other household tools.
(the couple times I violated this rule in the last 15 years:
-tried a razor scraper in a home improvement emergency that didn't give me enough time to go to the proper hardware store: fell apart in the first pass trying to scrape silicone off of glass.
-tried a potato masher: broke on the first couple mashes, ended up using a fork.)
-the cleaning products always seem to have an brutally intense fragrance of carcinogens
-even the stuff that is intended to be single use like sponges, latex gloves, paper towels tend to break-up during the first use...having said that, the pack of four latex gloves did get me through the aforementioned silicone sealing project...first two broke while trying to get them on.
-toys unless you're doing an experiment requiring high concentrations of lead and BPAs

not sure what that leaves except for cheap ass fire-hazard decorations and party supplies?

I may try the clothes pins if I ever get an outdoor clothesline...
