Neighborhood Chat / Re: New vertical mall coming to Flushing
« on: October 13, 2009, 06:56:49 AM »
Vertical malls, while a popular suburban concept in some areas, and most popular in Asian countries, kind of ruin the urban experience. Take a look at Flushing Mall. It's dead nearly all the time. The Manhattan Mall near 34th Street is only slightly better. In New York City, people are more accustomed to the pedestrian outdoor shopping experience. 2nd story retail typically only survives if it has a ground floor presence. 3rd Story retail rarely, if ever, works. However, this project might be more successful because of it's convenient location to public transportation and heavy Asian population that is more accustomed to this scenario.

As for the parking, this is New York City and there happens to be an extremely convenient subway that goes directly from JH to Flushing. I realize that cars will never be eliminated, but the answer is not to add more parking or widen streets. The answer is to encourage public transportation and pedestrian experience and remove vehicles from the street.

We run into this complaint about traffic all the time when doing large scale projects in the city. And unfortunately when you do traffic studies, the finding is more often than not that nothing can be done to mitigate traffic congestion unless people change their habits. Typically the traffic problems get worse if you add more parking because that encourages more people to drive thus heightening the already terrible congestion. Unfortunately people tend to get so single minded about their parking or traffic congestion problem they find it impossible to believe that adding zero new parking spaces will actually solve the problem.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Wine recommendations -- anyone?
« on: October 08, 2009, 06:54:01 PM »
My two favorites (and they are very cheap) are:

Montepulciano D'Abruzzo
Readily available at nearly all wine/liquor stores. I've paid as little as $5/bottle before. But certain vineyards can be near $20/bottle.

Lambrusco (bubbly red)
This is more difficult to find. I've found it at bottlerocket on 19th and 5th Avenue in the Flatiron District. They have two types; a red and a rose. You can also find it at Via Amelia, on 21st? between Park and Broadway. It's a restaurant, but they sell wine as well and they have a larger selection. Lambrusco is particular to the Amelia Romagna region of Italy (northern Italy, Parma region). This is usually priced in the mid-teens. There are cheaper ones (by Riunite, ick...), but I don't recommend them.
