It should be noted that the proponents of putting a soccer stadium and concert venue in the heart of Flushing Meadows Corona Park frequently cite the need for repairs to the community soccer fields as a rationale for development. Can't we find a better location for this stadium?

Taggie -

I agree that the soccer stadium has its merits. I have heard numerous people propose what I think is a sensible solution: Move the Soccer Stadium to the CitiField parking lot (which is where the Mall is proposed for). If the Mets feel like they don't need their parking lot anymore, then that is the ideal place for a soccer stadium. It would be closer to mass transit. It could share the remaining parking with the Mets and USTA. And most importantly, it would keep the professional sports complexes, which are car dependent, in a wing of the park. This way cars are not driving on parkland and there wouldn't be a need to displace thousands of soccer players by removing the 4 community soccer fields.

There is some empirical evidence that this could work. Shea Stadium and CitiField co-existed for a long stretch of time while CitiField was being built. We know there can be two stadiums there. Plus, the footprint of a soccer stadium would be far smaller than the proposed footprint of the mall.

Also, in several other cities, you see this set-up where one sports stadium is located next to another sports stadium.

This all comes down to the simple fact that the community should have a voice and input in the design of these projects. Community members could have told any developer that asked that there are better sites for the soccer stadium and that we don't need another mall - there are already 7 malls within a 3 mile radius!

Also, deeply troubling is the concept that the Bloomberg Administration would be giving away parkland for FREE. None of the three proposals has any mention of paying for the land or purchasing the land. And because it is parkland -- they wouldn't even have to pay property taxes. That is part of this proposal which is mind-boggling. We are looking at a budget crisis and considering laying off teachers but Mayor Bloomberg won't ask multi-billion dollar corporations to pay for land!
