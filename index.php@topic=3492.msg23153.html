I posted this early this morning on another message board:

I know of an incident where a very large rat entered an apartment on 77th Street about 6 weeks ago.  It was caught by the building staff using a Rat Zapper Electric Trap.  The rat easily escaped the glue traps but not the Rat Zapper.  It measured 16".  I'm told that this size rat is rare and most in the area are small and live in burrows under Ivy and rarely enter apartment buildings.  I would recommend Rat Zapper in every basement building as a precaution.  They can be ordered for $29.95 at www.ratzapperonline.com.  You won't find them in hardware stores.  If anyone is concerned rather than depending on merchants or city agencies purchase one for your home.  It kills without poision.  No touching, no mess, no blood and it kills quickly.  They operate on 4 AA batteries.  You install the batteries, add poison pellets (MET Supermakret has it for 89 cents) in the rear of the rat zapper turn the switch ON.  When a rat/mouse is killed a red light on the Rat Zapper will blink.  You don't even have to see the rodent.  Simply empty contents into a plastic bag.  

You don't want to kills rodents with poison.  They die inside your home and you don't know where.  If caught in the glue traps they take many hours to die.  Most superintendents are not aware of these machines and will call in an exterminator.  You now know about Rat Zapper.  Print the information and give a copy to your super, managing agent and a board member.  It would also make sense if a copy of this message was sent to the local free neighborhood newspapers for printing.   I'm not even certain if the City Agencies are aware of these machines or the Health Department that inspects restaurants.  Every restaurant/food business should have one or several.  

Don't place food such as cheese or peanut butter which will rot in the machine.  Only use the poison pellets.  Also, a cat won't keep a rat especially a large one from entering your home.  They even seek out and eat pet food.  Sorry, cat owners.
