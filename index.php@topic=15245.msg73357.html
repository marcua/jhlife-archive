Len, I certainly never said you do, and you are correct that the government cannot tell you what type of business you can or cannot open, so long as the business complies with the zoning laws and does not require the issuance of a special license.  As I said above, we may be able to prevent further liquor licenses from being issued by showing that our neighborhood has been oversaturated by business selling liquor, but there is no such equivalent for 99 cent stores, cell phone stores or pharmacies, other than what the free market can handle.

I can feel the frustration as far as what type of businesses we have, but even in the last 12 years that I've lived here, the commercial enterprises that have opened have been steadily addressing my needs.  Hell, we now have a Blink and a Planet Fitness whereas before we had one lousy gym (I went in there once - never joined).  Restaurants now have a pleasing look to them.  Our markets have been updated.  And we have some small indie shops that do good business. 

Of course, I'm still going to complain about some of our commercial neighbors, but that's NYC.  I complained when I lived in Gramercy and the East and West Village also.  We're NYers.  It's what we do.
