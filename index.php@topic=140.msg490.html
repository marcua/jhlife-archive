Re: Business District Improvement for 74th and 73rd St??
« Reply #5 on: December 04, 2007, 10:29:23 AM »
BIDs are strictly voluntary.  The city council could never pressure businesses to form a BID. 

I don't know the specifics of what has been going on here, but I have a general idea.  The litter problem is more complex than you may think.  The trash you see on the ground is mostly wind-blown litter from overflowing trash receptacles.  The businesses argue that the city should provide more trash cans and more frequent collection; the city argues that if the businesses want more service, they should pay for it.  So far, they have not been willing.  I think the Doe Fund's earlier maintenance work on 74th Street and 37th Ave. was paid for by grants secured by a city councilmember or somebody from the state assembly.  But this grant must have run out. 

I imagine this is an issue the WJHA and and JHBG have been tracking closely.  I suggest getting involved with one of these organizations if you would like to learn more about this and/or get involved in fixing the problem.  Also, please report back to us on what you learn!!
