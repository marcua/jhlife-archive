Restaurants & Food / Krispy Krunchy Chicken
« on: January 07, 2016, 02:00:39 PM »
Tried out Krispy Krunchy Chicken over on 37/82-83 yesterday. Good stuff.


The style of chicken is very similar to Popeye's - fried with some Louisiana spices. I think the chicken is better than any Popeye's I've had, and unfortunately I think KFC is terrible now (was my favorite growing up). If you want other fried chicken, I think the only great one in this area would be at Broadway Bakery over by the Elmhurst Ave station - they call it Broaster.


This is a franchised company. I don't remember seeing them before this year, but if you look on their map, they are all over the country. http://www.krispykrunchy.com/ - Many times they are located within a fast food place that already exists, and only uses the Krispy Krunch moniker on the menus or on window signs.


This is in the same vein as Kennedy Chicken, Kennedy Fried Chicken, Texas Chicken, and our local Al Araf. This cuisine is something which I have been really interested in; As I understand it, this is "American food" by Muslims. It's not just fried chicken, but a catch-all for American foods like pizza, subs (I use the word "subs" because they are Subway crap style), chicken wings, etc ---- and all Halal. It's a safe place for Muslims to get American food.


So.... Why do I support this and not Starbucks ? I guess because I love the business of fast food and there's not much competition from small business here (although this can definitely be argued). I am also very interested in immigrant businesses, adaptation, assimilation to American culture. Also, I believe that they just pay a fee for the licensing and the recipes, so it's kinda sorta locally owned (like most dunkin donuts)- ---- Which, I respect less because there's no individual character, but whatever.... guilty pleasure I guess!
