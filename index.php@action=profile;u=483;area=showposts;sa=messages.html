My tax needs are different because of the many changes in my life and future plans, most importantly, my recent marital status. "No big deal" you might say. But when yours is a gay marriage in NY, and your marriage isn't recognized by the Federal Gov, then things get a little more labor intensive.

Not only was he straight to the point and knowledgeable, but he was very polite and professional.

I recommend his services and hope others have similar experiences with him.

I went to Lima Limon and I was just not impressed with the place. Mind you, I eat at La Gata Golosa (boy do they need help with decor) but their food is TASTY so I put up with the atmosphere --or lack thereof. But LimaLimon needs to improve fast. The staff is not friendly nor are they nasty. But on to the most important stuff: their food.

I must say they have this stuffed potato which was excellent. But their tripe...(ewww...the spanish names sound better) their tripe was just not good. Sorry Lima Limon...I won't be going back. I enjoy walking to the restaurants in the 'hood, but this one is just way too far for me. I wish them the best.

New York State 13th Senate District Special Elections Forum on LGBT Equality
La comunidad LGBT alza la voz

March 10, 2010
7:00pm - 8:30pm
Location: Queens Pride House
76-11 37th Ave., Suite 206, Jackson Heights, Queens (Map)

What kind of change do you want to see for the LGBT community of Western Queens? What pressing issues affect you? Come bring these ideas to a forum with political candidates and local activists!

 In connection with the upcoming special election for the New York State Senate 13th District Seat, the LGBT Equality Coalition of Queens is organizing a forum to discuss the status of the LGBT community, particularly of Western Queens.

The forum will focus on the following four major issues: responses to hate crimes against LGBT Queens residents, police-community relations, funding for health and social services in the district, and LGBT-related legislative initiatives.
