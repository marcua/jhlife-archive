Neighborhood Chat / Re: Attracting new business to the neighborhood
« on: May 08, 2013, 01:31:55 PM »
JuJu's has good homemade hummus, and there is no shortage of places in the neighborhood where you can buy garbanzos, tahini, lemon, etc. It's really easy to make hummus. Pro tip: soak your beans overnight in water that has a tablespoon of baking soda in it. 

My only real food-related complaint about the neighborhood is lack of a good burger (though I can get excellent ground beef at the farmers market). But in terms of the availability, and variety, of restaurants and markets, if you complain about access to food in Jackson Heights you're living in the wrong place. And as someone said, it is so easy to get to Astoria, LIC, Sunnyside should you want something like Salt & Fat.

It seems to me that there are plenty of people living here with discretionary income and that perceptions of Jackson Heights will change/are changing. I don't think this means it will become the next Park Slope -- that would be horrible -- but more and more people know about the great apartments, proximity to Manhattan, and amazing food. Just take a look at how the rental rates are going up.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Piggy backing off farmers market
« on: July 31, 2012, 06:06:46 PM »
If I'm in town, I do not miss the Sunday market, no matter the time of year -- it is one of my favorite things about Jackson Heights. I'm going to echo the sentiment that of all the things to complain about in the neighborhood, and there are plenty of legitimate complaints to be made, the vendors in question here are not stealing business and should not be vilified; in fact, they are adding to the Greenmarket's vibrancy, which is a true reflection of the neighborhood. As mentioned previously, people selling tamales, flautas and elote in no way compete with or detract from the Greenmarket vendors selling produce, dairy, meats, baked goods, honey, juice, etc.

Who remembers when the Le Gamin truck used to park on 34th Avenue during the market? Now, they probably had proper permits but I don't recall anyone ever accusing Le Gamin of stealing business from the market vendors. What's up with that?     

Especially during the summer with the play street, these vendors provide food after the market has ended. I've seen farmers eat this food, as well as all the people using the park. I've never noticed an accumulation of trash on the corner where these folks set up. As is regularly discussed here, there is far more trash on 82nd Street and around the pedestrian plaza in front of all the legal businesses. 

Le Gamin is long gone but I wish other food vendors and trucks, whether from Roosevelt Avenue, Astoria or elsewhere, would park for a few hours during the Sunday market. As far as I'm concerned people selling prepared food on the periphery of the market is a mutually beneficial relationship. If you're scared of getting sick, don't eat their food. It just means there is more for the rest of us.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Are there any Brooklyn-like bars in Jackson Heights?
« on: August 29, 2011, 01:16:38 PM »
For the kind of bar some of you are looking for check out Terraza. It is the kind of place you find in the hip neighborhoods of Buenos Aires and Mexico City, and the LES. Yes, Spanish is the dominant language, but even if you have no Spanish, the young, artsy crowd speaks perfect English. It's easy to strike up conversations, enjoy the nightly music and pretty regular readings. Things really get going late, but I've had lots of fun here on the early side. I'm married, but I can also tell you that the women aren't shy if that's a factor for you.

What I love about this neighborhood is that it is definitely resistant to the kind of blanket gentrification that has gone down in Brooklyn, or even Astoria. Walk down 30th Avenue between Steinway and 31st Street and it looks like Bedford Avenue.

But I also agree that the demand is here for some of the types of places listed above, like Bare Burger or a exposed-wood wine bar. I don't want scores of these places, but one or two would do gangbusters.

Maybe we'll get something cool in the vacant corner spot on 37th at 81st (I think).
