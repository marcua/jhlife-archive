Neighborhood Chat / Improving Safety on Northern Boulevard
« on: September 19, 2018, 11:16:36 AM »
We live near the new boulevard of death in Queens. Five pedestrians were killed near our section of Northern Boulevard over the past 12 months.

Here's an important petition asking that the city to solve the problem.

https://campaigns.transalt.org/FixNorthernBlvd-The-New-Boulevard-of-Death

Please take a second to sign it, your signature is important and takes one minute.
 

Here the people killed:
Jose Gomez, 71, killed September 13, 2017 on Northern Blvd. near 85th St.
Myriam Nino, 82, struck on January 5, 2018 at Northern Blvd. and 90th Street in Jackson Heights, died on January 20th
Giovanni Ampuero, 9, killed on April 28, 2018 on 70th Street and Northern Boulevard
Carlos Gavilanes, 47, killed on May 21, 2018 on Northern Boulevard between 100th and 101st Streets
Unidentified male, 70, killed on September 9, 2018 on Northenrn Boulevard and 108th Street

Neighborhood Chat / Important meeting in Sunnyside tomorrow
« on: December 18, 2017, 11:41:18 AM »
Tomorrow there will be an important public meeting in Sunnyside to discuss the redesign of 43rd Ave and Skillman Ave.

The redesign includes measures that will make the streets safer for everyone — there have been more than 340 people injured in crashes on those two streets since 2010.

But this is especially important for Jackson Heights cyclists:
1. those streets are dangerous for cyclists (one died there earlier this year)
2. those streets are the most direct route to Queensboro bridge and to Parks in LIC and Astoria.
3. the redesign includes a protected bike lane.

More info on this video:
https://www.facebook.com/qnsbike/videos/1565037263572926/

Neighborhood Chat / Public meeting to improve bike infrastructure in our area
« on: October 23, 2017, 01:13:01 PM »
NYC DOT will hold a first meeting this Wednesday to collect community input for the Big Jump Project at the Queens Museum. This is your chance to have a say if you want more and better bike lanes and other types of cycling infrastructure in the neighborhoods of Corona, East Elmhusrt, Elmhusrt and Jackson Heights.

Big Jump Community Advisory Meeting
Wednesday, Oct. 25, 2017
6:30 PM to 8:00 PM
Queens Museum
RSVP here: https://www.eventbrite.com/e/big-jump-community-advisory-committee-meeting-tickets-38770017165?mc_cid=6400061f7a&mc_eid=a9d14892ea

What is the Big Jump?
It’s a three-year community-driven planning process that aims to improve cycling safety while strengthening bicycle infrastructure within the neighborhoods of Corona, East Elmhusrt, Elmhusrt, and Jackson Heights. This initiative will include programming to encourage residents to use alternative modes of transportation and will draw on local community input to develop on-street improvements such as general traffic calming designs, new bike facilities and traffic flow enhancements.

What's next?
Over the next year, DOT will partner with residents, Queens Community Boards 3 and 4, elected officials and civic groups to hear what neighborhood residents want in an effort to develop community-centric programming, projects and events.

Neighborhood Chat / Update on bike lanes connecting parks
« on: September 24, 2017, 07:37:49 PM »

Friends, this week we are going to hand our petitions to our neighbors at Community Board 3 to formalize our request. We ask that if you have not signed the online version yet, please do so today:

http://www.qns.bike/petition

If you're not familiar with our initiative
We are a group of more than 30 people living anywhere from Astoria to Flushing, but most in Jackson Heights. In 2015 we started  a campaign here on this forum to ask the city to build a bike lane grid connecting parks in our area, from Astoria do Flushing Meadows Corona Park.

We did nearly 20 group bike rides to promote healthy cycling and the diversity, culture, businesses and food in our area. During the process, we collected more than 3,300 signatures for a petition asking the city build the bike lane grid that we want.

Updates on the campaign
We've had overwhelmingly positive feedback, learned from our neighbors and made new friends. We also had a few great successes, like a bike lane on 31st avenue that connects Jackson Heights the water front parks in Astoria and Flushing promenade.



But there's still a lot to be done before we can get a bike network that that can get people of all ages safely to a park. Our goal is to have a grid that would allow anyone between the ages 8 and 88 to safely bike from Flushing Meadows Corona Park to Astoria, connecting the neighborhoods in between. This is even more urgent considering that Travers Park is scheduled to close for renovation soon.

Recently NYC selected our area, Community Board 3, to be one of the priority zones for the implementation of a safer bike infrastructure. Data showed that cyclists are already riding here and that streets needed to become safer. It also showed that areas like East Elmhurst and Northern Corona would benefit from another transportation alternative, since they are far from the subway. In the coming months, the city will organize workshops to make a grid that serves all here.

If you want to join us
Email qnsbike@gmail.com, we have a list serve that we use to talk and organize rides. Many of our rides are kid friendly. Including two upcoming rides in Halloween and Christmas.

We want to take the chance to thank all our neighbors for their support.
Thanks also to all the businesses that received us on our food rides and that hosted our petitions, like Table Wine, Cannelle Patisserie, Aroma Brazil, Arepa Lady and many others. Also thanks to our friends and neighbors from Jackson Heights Beautification Group,  Jackson Heights Green Alliance, East Elmhurst Historical Society, Louis Armstrong Museum, Queens Museum and Make Queens Safer. To council members Daniel Dromm, Costa Constantinides and Julissa Ferreras for their continuous support.

Finally, thanks to NYPD for helping us on our Santa Ride! There’s one more coming up on Dec. 3! Save the date, bring your kids!

Visit us on facebook.com/qnsbike

Our vision for our area is a protected bike/pedestrian loop connecting the parks in Jackson Heights, East Elmurst and Corona.



THANK YOU!

Neighborhood Chat / Update from the campaign for bike lanes connecting parks
« on: March 14, 2016, 01:16:02 PM »
Wonderful news! NYC DOT has unveiled a plan last week for a bike lane on 31st Avenue, from Socrates Park to Jackson Heights, to eventually reach Flushing Promenade. This is the very first and essential step to reach our dreamed network of bike lanes connecting parks in Queens.

See maps and plan details at www.facebook.com/qnsbike

This bike lane will increase park access and life quality to 500,000 people living in Astoria, East Elmhurst, Ditmars, Jackson Heights and Corona. It will serve as a transportation alternative in areas underserved by the subway. It will improve access to business centers in a low cost green way. It will calm traffic and increase safety. And, look at this: no lane or parking loss.

We want to say THANK YOU to our community who has been showing incredible support for this initiative. More than 2,600 neighbors have signed our petition! We'll keep collecting signatures as the weather warms up, promoting rides and events.

THANK YOU!!!
