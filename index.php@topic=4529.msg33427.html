TRY GABBANA!
« Reply #11 on: September 11, 2010, 09:41:25 AM »
I have gone twice to Gabbana. I think the service and food are excellent! Last night, my husband and I had steaks. A few sauces are offered like papaya, tamarind, or a spin on au poivre.  We had delicious mashed potatoes on the side--one lobster and the other truffle.  The avocado and watercress salad with crispy white cheese was really tasty. So are the 3-empanadas appetizer and the creamy croquetas.  The pernil was a little dry but also good. The presentation of the food is very nice. My pizco sour was excellently executed.  I SUPPORT THIS PLACE AND I URGE EVERYONE TO TRY IT OUT. BUSINESS SEEMED A LITTLE SLOW BOTH TIMES I HAVE GONE. THIS CORONA GEM DESERVES TO SURVIVE!

Quote from: earlie on September 12, 2010, 10:28:44 AM
While it looks FANTASTIC, they are going to need a lot of luck, considering their location. It is a 'big spender' type of establishment, but not a big spender nabe, and difficult to get to on public transportation.

I wouldn't be so sure about that.  Just look at Jubilee on 23rd Ave and 94th St.  They have done very well for themselves and draw huge crowds on weekend nights with a lot of 'big spender' types.  $20+ for cover for men, $200+ bottle service, etc.

My opinion is that Gabbana will do okay as a restaurant and very well as a nightclub provided they keep security tight, the music local (i.e. bachata, merengue, salsa) and have some good promoters.  There are plenty of 'big spender' types living in the 'hood that usually drop G's partying uptown at Umbrella's, Talay, etc. and would welcome not having to travel so far to have fun.
