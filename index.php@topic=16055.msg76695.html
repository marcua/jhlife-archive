Pot busts and gang activity in Travers Park
« on: February 25, 2017, 11:42:04 PM »
This afternoon I was sitting with a friend by the sprinkler area of Travers Park, watching our kids zoom around on scooters. We smelled marijuana and joked about it for a second. A few minutes later, another friend came over and said we’d missed the action. Apparently two undercover officers had busted the marijuana smokers in one of the handball courts, handcuffed them, and taken them away in a patrol car.
It’s a small thing, but it bothered me, so I decided to come here to post. First, if you have any teenagers/young adults in your life, please remind them that policing in NYC is still aggressive. They're looking for reasons to make arrests. God forbid any of the kids that were arrested today are also undocumented.
Second, I was surprised to learn there were undercover officers in the park. In all my years of going to Travers, I have never once seen a uniformed officer walk through it. This sort of policing—showing your face, talking with people—would be very different from went on today, which is more akin to waiting on the side of a highway with a radar gun.
What do y’all think? Are we any closer to seeing community policing in Jackson Heights, or is this still just something the mayor talks about on the radio?
And yes, I know I need to start going to the 112th Precinct meetings.

Okay, I looked up the letter of the law as it were... As per De Blasio's NYPD policy anyone caught with 25 grams or less provided they have ID and no outstanding warrants gets a ticket instead of being arrested.  For smoking in public the police have discretion whether to issue a warning, a summons, or arrest the person(s).

So, it's possible the people arrested didn't have ID, had outstanding warrants, had more than 25g in their possession, mouthed off at the officers or any combination thereof.  It's also possible that said officers got up on the wrong side of the world that day, or were responding to a complaint.  If the latter, discretion could well have meant having to arrest because say, some parent called about people smoking pot in the park near their toddler, or some such.

Thanks for all the replies.  I agree with Francis and other posters: I don't know exactly what happened, and it's possible that the person/s arrested were involved in something beyond just smoking pot. And yeah, smoking of any kind in a public park is obnoxious and warrants a ticket.
My point, though, is this: when have any of us ever seen a police officer just *walk* through Travers Park? When was the last time you saw a cop interact with the kids on the basketball courts or playing handball, not to arrest them or give out a ticket, but just to show face and build relationships? I've been using this park almost every day for the past seven years, and I've never seen this happen. If a uniformed officer showed up at the park, that would have a deterrent effect on petty stuff like pot smoking. But instead, we have a law enforcement structure that's focused on arrest metrics. That's a problem.
