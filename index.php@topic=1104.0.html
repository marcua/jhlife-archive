We have a thread on ice cream, another on frozen yogurt and another on refreshing drinks, but none for frozen neighborhood specialties that don't fit into any of these categories.

So here goes...

Cholados are a great place to start.  Jackson Heights seems to be their global epicenter (at least outside Colombia).  If you Google "Cholados," half of the top ten hits relate to Jackson Heights, e.g. Cholados at Queens Eats and Cholados at Eating in Translation.  There are at least half a dozen places around that sell them, including El Palacio de Cholados (83-18 Northern Blvd), Xtasis (82-12 Northern Blvd.), and AquÃ­ Colombia Antojitos (81-06 37th Ave).

This is in Elmhurst and not JH, but last night my friend and I went to Oz Tea Cafe and had a lovely red bean-coconut smoothie with tapioca pearls.  There's also a comprehensive sushi menu - I ordered a salmon avocado roll for only $3.25 that I thought was better than Tomo's.

This place is open til 11pm weeknights and 12pm Friday and Saturday.

It's 6/10 of a mile from the 82nd st subway and 7/10 of a mile from the 74th St. subway.  Of course, if you're already on the subway just get off at the Elmhurst Ave stop on the R/V and it's right around the corner.

Check out the reviews on yelp
http://www.yelp.com/biz/oz-cafe-elmhurst
