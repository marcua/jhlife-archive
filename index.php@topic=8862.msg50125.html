Re: Diversity Plaza Upgrade?
« Reply #65 on: August 28, 2013, 11:59:56 PM »
Some of us met with Danny Dromm's office today for a very productive meeting.

Here's what we got: First, the plaza is in a very early phase right now. What we see there is not the final product. DOT will be doing community outreach sometime in the fall to come up with a final vision. It is important that everyone who is available be there to make their voices and opinions heard. This will determine the eventual design of the space.

Second, whatever plan is ultimately drafted will not be implemented for at least another year.

Third, there is a chance this process might be sped up if enough community pressure is applied. This would require all of us coming together and petitioning.

Fourth and most importantly, since the final phase won't be here for a while, it is up to us the community to make this space work in the meantime. The city has its own schedule, which is going to be drawn out. For the time being, getting more immediate results will require people stepping up, getting involved, and helping out. SUKHI went out on a limb to get this plaza started. They are very small in capacity and both need and welcome help. So criticizing them will get us nowhere. Offering our assistance as a community will move this thing forward.

Those of us who met with Dromm's office today will be meeting again next week to talk about some next steps. Anyone who is interested in making this space successful is encouraged and more than welcome to join. Feel free to let know what days and times you are available to meet.

We should not compare Diversity Plaza with other city plazas because it came about in a unique way. Normally, an established local organization puts in a request to DOT for a plaza (Jackson Heights Green Alliance and the 78th Street Playstreet; Queens Econonic Development Corporation and Corona Plaza). In the case of Diversity Plaza, this space was the result of a study DOT did a couple years ago in which they recommended the closure of this street to traffic (http://a841-tfpweb.nyc.gov/jackson-heights/recommendations-new-public-space/).

As Jeff said, SUKHI is just two people who are are busy running their businesses and were pro-active enough to take on this plaza when no one else would. We should not wait around for them to broadcast appeals for assistance. And I would not necessarily assume that they are members of this forum and hearing these concerns. So, instead, let's step up ourselves, pay them a visit next Wednesday at 7:30pm, get to know them, eat, hang out, and see what we can do to to help.

If anyone has unanswered questions, pressing concerns, or productive ideas, next Wednesday will be an opportunity to get them out into the open and actually be effectual.
