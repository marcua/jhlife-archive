Thank you to all who read my response in the spirit in which it was written: not as a characterization of the homeless or immigrants as "vermin" (where did I say that?) but a recognition that poverty, whatever it's roots and causes, can cause very difficult quality of life issues for a community.  I am not a yuppie, I am not anti-immigrant but yes, my husband and I are proudly middle-class and own our apartment.  We care about garbage, crime, overcrowding, noise etc. because we have invested our life-savings in our home. (and therefore Jackson Heights.)  We are not "sainted" owners; we became owners just three years ago after years after years of renting.  We are a hard-working couple, just trying to take care of ourselves during difficult economic times.  As a young woman I am sure that I saw and expressed myself through a narrow prism of political correctness. I have "become" my parents, but I'd like to think that their ethics of hard work and reponsibility are a worthy legacy.

Whatever negative impression I may have given was born out of my personal observation that so often Jackson Heights is described as some kind of diversity Shangri-la.  It's not ALL giggles and hugs living here. Maybe we should move?  In a perfect world, we might consider it.

I shouldn't have problems with used condoms on the sidewalk on my street?  I shouldn't have problems with music blasting from the cars idling beneath my bedroom window at the light?  I shouldn't want people to follow existing laws like not littering, drinking in public or other quality iof life issues?   I guess it's part of the charm of living in shangri-la.

Did I say throw people out? Did I say that only homewoners are the only good law-abiding people? I guess people thought I did, but I did not, nor did I mean to. 

So bash me if you want, call me a racist if you want, but I guarantee you that my opinions are not mean-spirited and certainly not coming out of vacumn.
