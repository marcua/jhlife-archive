So we've finished scrubbing and painting - and I took one last round of photos before we moved in - no more pictures until we've unpacked (nothing like a pile of boxes in a room to ruin the mood, eh?)

Still, we're thrilled to have such a nice home to live in. We moved over the weekend - we're exhausted - but happy. The cats and the puppy keep going from room to room (they're not used to more than one living space!) and we can't wait to get fully settled.

We scrubbed and repainted every surface (ceiling, walls, trim) and then ripped up the old wall-to-wall brown carpet that had been installed in every room. The whole process took slightly longer than we thought, but it's finally done.

Heals & Stockings: No, I think that the big 2BR on the ground floor is still available. We bought a JR 4 on the second floor that faces 35th Ave (and the bedroom is on the back corner and also faces the alley in back where the fire escape is).

I think that the maintenance varies by floor - we're OK with it on the 2nd floor, although I believe from other listings we've seen in our research of the building that it's higher on higher floors (with city views). It's money well spent as far as we're concerned, as the building is spotlessly maintained, and the financial statements made sense to us when we reviewed as we were buying. And for other large pre-war elevator buildings, it seems in-line with others buildings in the area.

Mark, Todd and Marlene - thanks for the compliments! We have some more furniture on the way, but once we're done, I'll post some fully-furnished photos.

I found the board members that I met with to be relaxed, normal people.  I didn't feel that any of the questions were overly intrusive or invasive.
