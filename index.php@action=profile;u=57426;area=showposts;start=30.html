More on Open Table...

It was one of the early, if not the earliest, companies that offered online restaurant reservations.

Competitors have a problem with name recognition that in turn creates an issue with trust.

Yelp has entered the market and indeed has made headway. An increasing number of restaurants are using their reservations app. But while Yelp has a lot of name recognition, it involves trade offs that some restaurants, and indeed associations that some consumers,  don't like.

In my case, I intend to use a system - ResDiary - that is dominant in the U.K. and Australia, and fairly widespread in Europe. I'm going to use it because it offers more features than Open Table and Yelp, because the company is much more personal and responsive in its interactions, and because the system is less expensive than Ipen Table.

However, few of my customers will have heard if it, and the reaction of many may be "What is this?"  That is an issue, and one of the reasons why Open Table retains dominance.

Re the comments on Open Table...

Open Table actuality has very little penetration nationally outside city centers.

The few restaurants that are using it in Queens were almost certainly offered an attractive initial deal, with emphasis on the word initial.

I have been pitched very hard by Open Table, including two face to face meetings, with deals that they told me I couldn't refuse. Their "deal" was superficially attractive, but made no sense if I thought it through. I made them a counter offer. That brought the discussions to an end.

There is a reason why few Queens restaurants are using Open Table, and in my view those that are using it have been sold a bill of goods that is costing both them and their customers.
