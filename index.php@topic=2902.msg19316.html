Here are the choices for July:

Ihawan is a Filipino BBQ restuarant in woodside
Menu: http://www.bridgeandtunnelclub.com/bigmap/queens/menus/ihawan.htm
Yelp reviews: http://www.yelp.com/biz/ihawan-woodside
Review with pictures on Chowhound: http://chowhound.chow.com/topics/379792

The Arepa Lady is a Roosevelt Ave legend. If we were to select this, the day and time would have to be Saturday at 10.30pm. There are a lot of variables in this choice, but when the stars align, the results are beautiful.
Will she be out? Check her MySpace page the days before: http://chowhound.chow.com/topics/379792

Tortilleria Nixtamal is a new taqueria in Corona. It is a very special restaurant, perhaps the most remarkable being that they make their own tortillas with homemade masa. This is not your ordinary taco shop.
http://blogs.villagevoice.com/forkintheroad/archives/2009/02/the_early_word-_3.php
http://newyork.seriouseats.com/2009/01/tortilleria-nixtamal-corona-queens-nyc-fresh-masa-tamales-mexican-italian.html
http://www.yelp.com/biz/tortilleria-nixtamal-corona

Dates? July 6th,12th, 19th, 26th or the corresponding saturday. we will work it out as it gets closer, just post your vetos.

Just found (what I think) is great food - that happens to be filipino!
This little hole in the wall place on Woodside ave and 65th street called Go2 King. 65-40 is the address.
They are just 2 weeks old, have 2 tables and 8 chairs, and serve from the steam tables but also have a rotisserie oven cooking chicken. I went there yes\terday and thought the food was excellent. The owner was sweet and the prices are cheap. They used to be in Jamaica for 5 months, then they got kicked out by the landlord. I'm glad they decided to come here.
Show them some love and check it out. I hope they make it.
