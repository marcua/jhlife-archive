Neighborhood Chat / Re: Fight Airplane Noise! -- a msg from Daniel Dromm
« on: October 19, 2013, 05:52:17 PM »
Here comes Solomon.

Dssjh, of course you are right that it's unreasonable to expect all airplane noise over Jackson Heights to cease, and that it would not be equitable to force other neighborhoods to take on the entire noise burden. But I assume you do agree it's gotten a bit worse lately? It sounds like you do. And without the ability to be in every neighborhood at once, it's impossible for any of us to know whether the change is an appropriate rebalancing or an unfair burden placed on Jackson Heights. So people complain and write letters to the Port Authority, Danny Dromm, etc. I think the complaining is understandable, and the letter writing is potentially effective.

That said, I would note that this thread may itself be as big a threat to property values as the airplane noise. People who consider buying property here look at Jackson Heights Life all the time, and this thread could, I think, easily scare some of them away. That doesn't mean the thread shouldn't exist. But, as someone else said above, let's ease up on the over the top comments, try to be as realistic as possible about how bad it really is, and maybe consider writing letters to people in power as a more effective response than venting emotions on JH Life. My perspective: I'd say the noise is worse than it was, and worse than in neighborhoods that are nowhere near airports. But there are certainly neighborhoods with a lot more noise than we get, and I think there's a decent chance political action can help roll back some of the increase in plane traffic over our neighborhood.
