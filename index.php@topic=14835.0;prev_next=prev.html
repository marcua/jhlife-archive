Race is more than sematics. It's a cultural construct.

American ideas about race are different than English and European ideas about race (though I am certain European ideas about race are more complicated than I realize). Americans ideas about race are born from an idea of white superiority, which was really all about justifying slavery, as well as creating class divisions. So, the well off could point to black people, native Americans, etc. and say to the poor white people that, hey you might be poor, but at least you're superior to those people.

In some other places, race just isn't something people really focus on, because they don't think about it there like we do. They are more concerned with tribal or ethnic affiliation. And example might be the Middle East (I think you'd be hard pressed to find many Americans who think people from Northern Africa, Lebanon or Iran are white) where the interest is are you a Kurd, a Turk, a Bedouian, Armenian, etc.

I'm not clear on the European evolution on ideas about race. I don't think a "white race" was something that existed prior to the African slave trade. My understanding is that you were French or German or English. But you can see how the idea of a "white race" as being superior to Africans would be very convenient to supplying labor to colonies in the Americas. I'm not sure how it played out as  European powers expanded in to Africa and Asia.
