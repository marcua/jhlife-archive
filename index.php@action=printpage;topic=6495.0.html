I may just be very fortunate, but I've been in the Greystones for just about 4 years now and I've only had one waterbug/cockroach sighting in my apartment since moving in. (And I credit that to an upstairs neighbor who was gut renovating her kitchen at the time.) So I don't think you can make a blanket statement about the entire historic district.

As a lifelong New York apartment dweller, my experience tells me it's not just the cleanliness of the building in general, but also that of your neighbors. If you've got neighbors who leave food out, pile dirty dishes in the sink all week, or hoarders who never throw anything out living adjacent to your apartment, there's almost nothing you can do about certain pests.

The other possibility is that there's a serious and chronic water/moisture condition. I presume the 6th is the top floor? Has the roof membrane been checked? What about the parapet walls, coping stones or pointing on the brickwork? Are all the drainage pipes clear and unblocked?

Title: Re: Pest Problem? (the insect kind)
Post by: StevenGrey on August 11, 2011, 02:41:07 PM
Quote
Could you please explain how the pointing, parapet walls contribute to a "water/moisture" condition.

We just had the roof completely redone on our building. The previous generations of tenants chose to simply add another layer of tar paper over the previous layers, and at some point had a tar-like material applied to the inside of the parapet walls to waterproof them. Water and moisture becomes trapped between layers, which can rot wood and cause brick and mortar to disintegrate over time. It can generate mold, and probably creates an environment suitable for colonies of waterbugs. We had all of this old material removed.
