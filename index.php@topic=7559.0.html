One issue in JH is that there is a very, very limited supply of nice pre-war condo (or even post-war but charming) buildings. Maybe three or four? When units do come up for sale, they frequently command a 30-40% premium over similar sized co-ops and you have to factor in the monthly carrying costs + property taxes. The property taxes for some reason tend to be quite high, but I am not sure exactly why. By high I mean they are comparable to the taxes you would pay on a two family house in the same area worth 2-3x more.

Does anyone have a listing of all of the nice condo buildings in JH?

The only nice one I can think of is Colton House on 76th and 35th.  It's pre-war and not shoddy construction like most of the other ones in the hood. 

There is no way I would ever buy a condo in Plaza 75 or Sage or Bravo - they are poorly-made, overpriced, small for the area, and being right next to the subway isn't really much of a selling point around here because there are so many gorgeous co-ops within a 5-minute walk at much lower prices.  It might be easier to sublet than a coop, but I think it would be more difficult to sell because there is more desirable housing stock nearby.
