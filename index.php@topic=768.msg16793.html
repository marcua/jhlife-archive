Maybe this is just me, but besides the issue of noise, it seems really ill-advised for airplanes to take off from LaGuardia Airport's shorter runway in the direction of land. There is almost no chance for a pilot to abort a takeoff without it resulting in a potential disaster for the flight's passengers and crew, but also for dozens (if not hundreds) of civilians. If the plane does not achieve liftoff and overshoots the runway, it ends up plowing into the Grand Central Parkway, and if it does get airborne for even a few seconds and stalls, it potentially falls into one of the densely-populated neighborhoods of western Queens.

I'd really like an explanation from the LGA management as to why takeoffs over land seem to be happening on a regular basis every Sunday now.

Today the airplanes were taking off in the opposite direction over the water. LaGuardia airport has been around for 70 years. Incidents that involved fatalities at La Guardia during takeoff.

 On February 1, 1957, Northeast Airlines Flight 823 crashed on takeoff into Rikers Island. Of 101 people aboard, 21 were killed.

 On September 21, 1989, USAir Flight 5050 bound for Charlotte/Douglas International Airport in Charlotte, North Carolina, crashed after aborting takeoff and rolling off the end of the runway into the East River. The plane broke into three pieces, and three passengers died as a result.

 On March 22, 1992, USAir Flight 405 bound for Cleveland Hopkins International Airport in Cleveland, Ohio, crashed on takeoff at LaGuardia due to icing on its wings. Of 51 people aboard, 27 were killed.
