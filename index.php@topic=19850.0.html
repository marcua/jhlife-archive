We are asked to stay at home as much as possible to protect the most vulnerable among us, and tp prevent overwhelming hospitals. How are we protecting our most vulnerable if we're making it impossible for them to feed themselves?

We're ALL asked to stay home to prevent the spread of the virus. There was never an instruction that only at-risk people are supposed to stay home. In addition, even if someone is healthy, they may live with others who are more vulnerable and the healthy person can't take the risk of bringing the virus home to their household.

There is a COVID Care Neighbor Network that is set up specifically for the most vulnerable to be able to get groceries and deliveries, so people who are at-risk can use that. And the rest of us can (and should!) use the other delivery services, since we are not eligible to use services that are designated for elderly/vulnerable. If you don't know about this service, please see this post elsewhere on Jackson Heights Life: http://www.jacksonheightslife.com/community/index.php?topic=19778.msg98489#msg98489 (also pasted below)

There is help available for those who can't go out.
