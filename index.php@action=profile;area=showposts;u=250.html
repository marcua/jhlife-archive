Hi there.
I live in CT and my boyfriend lives in Jackson Heights.
I work near Danbury and when I drive down to JH on Friday afternoons I usually hit traffic on the Hutch and it takes me about 2 hours.  When I drive back to CT on Sunday nights it's about 45 minutes (to Norwalk).  It's do-able but really a question of what you can get used to.
Where in CT is the job?
The pros are that you would be going against traffic but the cons are that mileage is something you can't overcome.
A coworker of mine did the Jackson Heights to CT commute for a few months and I think it was about 1.5 hours to 2.  I can get more info from her if you'd like.
I hope this helped.

I work in marketing and the big buzz word is SEO (search engine optimization).
I'm not too savvy in this area but I do know that it's all about getting your site to show up at the top of searches.

For example, I did a search for Inspa World (which I knew was mentioned several times in a thread) and the JH result didn't show up until page 7, a spot which very few searchers would ever make it to.
When i searched for Jackson Heights Blog, this site shows up at the bottom of page 1.  Better.

Again, I don't know the exact science but it might be something worth looking in to.  (Unfortunately I do enough procrastinating as is so I can't devote too much time.)

Another suggestion would be to ask everyone who is active to link to the site on any personal pages they have (blogs, myspace, facebook, youtube etc.).
