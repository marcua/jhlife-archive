Neighborhood Chat / The new Post Office garden is being planted today.
« on: October 28, 2017, 09:16:01 AM »
The new Post Office garden is being planted today.
Please go to JHBG's Facebook page for more details: https://www.facebook.com/JHBGNY/

There we have an early planting plan attached that gives a rough idea of what it will look like. We also have a file that shows some of the shrubs we are considering. The final plan includes winterberry, cherry laurel, crape myrtle, thuja and hydrangea paniculata. We will be doing more planting - smaller shrubs and perennial flowers in the spring
The plan is designed to have what's called four season interest: flowers from spring through fall, berries etc, during winter.
It was designed for us by a Jackson Heights neighbor who is a landscape architect for NYC Parks and Rec.

Neighborhood Chat / Fight climate change in Jackson Heights - SATURDAY
« on: June 02, 2017, 01:08:12 PM »
Americans are not giving up the battle against climate change, despite the president. Local communities on up to state governors are doubling efforts to battle climate change and to temper whatever rise in temperature is already built into the system.

That's where JHBG's Tree LC program (street tree care) comes in. Our trees, especially street trees, significantly cool the sidewalks and create shade that also helps cool nearby buildings. That in turn reduces the need for air conditioning, which uses up lots of energy and releases lots of global-warming CO2 into the atmosphere.

This is not just something we are doing in JH, but a worldwide effort to cool the planet's cities, where increasingly more and more people are living.

We meet Saturday at 80th Street and 37th Avenue at 10 AM SHARP. Please see the attached poster for more details and NOTE THE NEW DATE.

And for folks who can't make Saturdays, We have a Sunday tree care event schedule for the following week, June 11. Hope to see you there.
