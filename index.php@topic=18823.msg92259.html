Pedestrian killed by bus at 75th/Roosevelt
« on: March 04, 2019, 08:40:13 AM »
https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/MTA-Bus-Kill-Pedestrian-Queens-506647581.html

An MTA bus struck and killed a pedestrian in Queens, dragging him along the road before coming to a stop early Monday, authorities say.

The man was hit and dragged by the bus near Roosevelt Avenue and 75th Street in Jackson Heights shortly before 3 a.m. He was taken to a hospital in critical condition and later died. He has not been identified.

It's not clear how far the man was dragged by the bus. Police say no criminality is suspected and it appears the driver did not realize the person had been hit.

Authorities are investigating if weather may have been a factor in the accident, which happened as snowfall rates started to ramp up overnight.

This intersection is extremely dangerous, despite the traffic department's efforts to direct traffic out of the area. Too many pedestrians take risks just to save a few seconds. This is an area that is crying out for some kind of left turn lights to go with the left turn lanes that were painted. More importantly, police need to enforce pedestrian right of way laws and jaywalking laws. The traffic lights on 74th street often cause cars to back up into the crosswalks on 75th street and frustrated drivers trying to turn on Roosevelt end up finishing their turns after the lights have turned red. And the number of cars making left turns in front of oncoming traffic with no regard for pedestrian safety is a recipe for more accidents. I try to avoid this area as much as possible, both as a pedestrian and a driver. The people who did the traffic study recently need to revisit the area to reassess what they have wrought.
