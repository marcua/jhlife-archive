I took the survey too. Had to focus on Flushing Meadows- it's such a shame that its so neglected. Also, the access points are extremely difficult to reach by foot, bike, or mass transit. A few summers ago, there was an Arts trolley that went from Louis Armstrong, Flushing and other locations into the park.

Another big gripe I've noticed recently is that there is hardly any programming there. And, it lacks a parks office, center like beautiful and totally utilized Inwood Park. (which by the way is still awaiting reconstruction of it's activity center after Hurricane Sandy)

Parks offers nature walks, nature skills classes, sports, etc at other parks- closest to us are Juniper and the Lost Battalion Hall rec center. Why not Flushing Meadows? In fact, I get the weekly Parks email and the event was to take place in the parking lot at 111th St!!!

Below is the schedule for a Tuesday- there are many events that could and should take place at FMCP. We would love an environmental center for the creek, or Astronomy in partnership with NYSCI.
Gormley Park and Moore Park are also fantastic parks- filled to the brim, but lacking programming.

As for small parks and plazas- We have a few and could have a few more. Look at Woodside- each subway stop has a beautiful plaza! Ours at 74th, 82nd and 90th are dumps. Donningham Triangle at 82nd and Baxter has just been renovated but they didn't put benches in it, seriously- for fear of homeless sleeping there.

And- all of our parks are soon to be renovated- good and bad, as they will close. Here's the schedule for that- https://www.nycgovparks.org/planning-and-building/capital-project-tracker

 missmarty, thanks for this! You are so right.

  In Jackson Heights, we get left out of so many Parks Dept programs. It would be great if we could get behind
a list of programming and activities that we are interested in having here. I am not talking about open green space
but something actually doable with what we have available. Dancing in the streets for kids, adults, too. Stargazing,
for example. A weather station up on the high point of 69th street. An organized walk along our avenues.

 I have been complaining to Parks for some time about Flushing Meadows. It's a horror in certain areas much of
the time. Am there several times a week year round. Usually nice around the stadiums, the lovely Queens museum
and other facilities. But it is the wide open grassy or muddy lawns that are awful- weedy and usually filled with
trash. The Conservancy seems to be a closed shop and full of political appointees. They could absolutely use a Parks
Office and we could use a smaller one at Travers Park for prime times. How about offering a service of small carts
rides like the zoo does for park goers and the police to get around? It feels like you are on your own there if something
were to happen.

  I was hugely supportive of the soccer stadium with local coaching and teams. It seemed inevitable that some
development would happen there. Now it's a collossal tennis stadium with unaffordable tickets and which is not a
serving a big population here. Real loss for our community and am not surprised that they are paying a pittance to
the park.

  Let's ask for more, biking, walking, programing, safety, just overall better. What's the worst that can happen?
