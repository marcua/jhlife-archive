I'm not sure on what basis you are making these claims, but if true most Europeans are not masking, please review this data showing their rates are currently increasing so that seems to be a failing strategy: https://www.ecdc.europa.eu/en/covid-19/country-overviews
Key points:
- As of 19 August 2020, the 14-day case notification rate for the EU/EEA and the UK was 37 (country range: 4–124) per 100 000 population. The rate has been increasing for 31 days.
-Seven countries (Bulgaria, Czechia, France, Netherlands, Poland, Romania and Sweden) had a weekly test positivity of 3% or higher and four countries (France, Greece, Netherlands and Slovenia) had a weekly test positivity that had increased compared to last week.

Your "only city"/"nowhere/everywhere" claims are quite Eurocentric... masking is incredibly common in many Asian cities.  See these articles for reference:
https://www.bbc.com/news/world-52015486
https://www.politico.com/news/magazine/2020/08/11/what-a-korean-teenage-fashion-trend-reveals-about-the-culture-of-mask-wearing-393204
And a scholarly article citing the rate of mask-wearing in Korea as one reason for their low rates: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7291980/

As for it being a "weird NYC cultural thing," we are learning new things about COVID transmission, infectivity, and treatments every day and personally, I plan to continue wearing a mask to protect both myself and my community until we understand the virus and have better prevention/treatment available.  Wearing a mask is such a truly minor ask, I don't see what the rush to end the practice is if it may have even a minimal benefit.
