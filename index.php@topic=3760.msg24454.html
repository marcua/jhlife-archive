December 4, 2009, Jackson Heights, New york:

In an exciting turn of events, a bizarre giant invasive species of enormous plastic flowers has landed in Jackson Heights, a district with a unique history of promoting vibrant primary colors, creative use of petroleum-based public sculpture, and an emphasis on flash and wit over the cool and downbeat. These flowers have appeared in the courtyard behind the Community Methodist Church at 81st and 35th Ave and are available for public viewing 24/7.
