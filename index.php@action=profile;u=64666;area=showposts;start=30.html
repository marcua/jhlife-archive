"Critical" violations are displayed in red.
Violation points: 40
Sanitary Violations
1) Live roaches present in facility's food and/or non-food areas.
2) Filth flies or food/refuse/sewage-associated (FRSA) flies present in facility’s food and/or non-food areas. Filth flies include house flies, little house flies, blow flies, bottle flies and flesh flies. Food/refuse/sewage-associated flies include fruit flies, drain flies and Phorid flies.
3) Food not protected from potential source of contamination during storage, preparation, transportation, display or service.

I do not live in the co-op and therefore I don’t have enough information to decide whether or not to believe that there is a rat problem in the building. I have frequented the establishments in that building numerous times over the years and never encountered any rats. However, given logical reasoning, I do know that it would be fairly counter-productive for all of those who complained to waste that much time and energy if there was no problem to begin with. To quote my previous post: “To clarify, I support one's right to vocalize garbage/rat issues and it is completely reasonable for residents to want their premises maintain hygienic standards.”

Your suggestion that adjacent Hawthorne Court complaints resulted in the eviction of the two businesses is ridiculous.
I’m a long-time resident of the Court and here are some facts:
There was a serious rat infestation in the garden complex that peaked 2 years ago. Unsanitary conditions in the alleyway behind the storefronts were an obvious source of at least part of the problem. Open garbage, spilled cooking oil and most definitely rats regularly observed scurrying about. Calls to 311 were made, inspectors came out and working with the city, the coops and the commercial tenants the situation has improved. The alleyway is cleaner and rat sightings this past summer have been negligible.
The Marshall’s notices of eviction are most likely a result of non-payment of rent or business tax default. Certainly not because the landlord decided that those 2 out of the 5 food establishments on the block had to go because of neighbor complaints.
Ludicrous.
