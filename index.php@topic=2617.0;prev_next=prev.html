Livery cab drivers on the lookout for ConEd?
« on: April 05, 2009, 11:44:51 PM »
A few days ago I noticed a livery cab parked on the corner of 35th Avenue and 78th Street.  The car had an orange construction cone on the roof.  To make a long story short, three days have gone by, and it now appears that there are shifts of cab drivers keeping vigil over a manhole cover that has been surrounded by yellow cones and caution tape.  After alerting the police and not getting a response, my husband approached the driver early this morning and asked about the cab's presence.  He was told that ConEdison pays them to keep an eye on the manhole cover and to prevent people from walking over it because it may be electrified.  Apparently, they'll keep vigil until ConEd finishes whatever emergency work they're doing to fix the problem.

Feeling a bit skeptical, my husband approached a ConEd truck that was parked near 37th Avenue this morning.  He asked for verification on the cab driver's story and found out that it is indeed true.  Does anyone know if this is this common practice for ConEd?  My guess is that it may be cheaper to pay the cab drivers than to pay overtime rates for ConEd work trucks.
