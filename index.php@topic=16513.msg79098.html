I lived in Dunnolly in the late 1990's early 2000's and I really liked it.  When I was there the gardens were user friendly with benches and picnic tables but of course there were guidelines.  It is the only building that has block long gardens encompassing even the avenues.  I found the location between 34th and 35th avenues to be perfect it was far enough from 37th avenue and Northern Boulevard to have slightly less traffic and noise than other blocks.  I also liked the  fact that there were only five apartments on each side of the building so elevators came quickly and you recognized your neighbors so that made it feel safer.  Although the apartments were spacious I didn't think the closets were that big in my apartment so that is a slight downside.The only other downside is that  now that they have turned 79th street between Northern Boulevard and 34th Avenue into a play street it is much more difficult to park if you have a car or have visitors who drive to visit you.

« Reply #9 on: September 21, 2017, 12:35:14 PM »
The garden at Dunnolly Gardens is not a playground. While the co-op is certainly child-friendly, there have been many complaints about the use of the garden for noisy play that unfairly disturbs other residents. This has become a major problem over the last several years due to real estate agents and others promoting the garden as a playground. Please do not add to this false impression. The board is currently reviewing garden rules so that all residents can enjoy the use of the gardens and their apartments.
