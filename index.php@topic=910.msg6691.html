I am a Caucasian male of French/Italian background and have lived in Jackson Heights for about 20 years. I grew up in Brooklyn and during the civil rights movement my family moved to Queens.  The one thing I love about JH is that it is a real global village where a diversity of people coexist peacefully. It's like taking a trip around the world and being able to walk home. I own a private home and work throughout Brooklyn so I'm driving throughout the day. I use public transportation on the weekends to get in and out of the city.  There have been many changes within the neighborhood, such as landmarking and  the creation of civic groups to affect positive change. Despite this, overdevelopment with a lack of sensitivity to the design of the neighborhood has caused overpopulation and the destruction of green space. City services are not commensurate to the population numbers.  An increase in noise, pollution,illegal conversions of single family homes , destruction of single family homes to create multiple dwellings have also acted to facillitate graffiti, drug activity  and a lack of services for both seniors and youth. In the presence of these difficulties,  the community remains in balance.

     Your survey is the first I'm answering on this board.  (I was actually referred by a friend from the yahoo group, only to find out that I must have joined months ago and never recalled ... that's a sign of old age!     )

First name (optional)?  Rob

Male or Female?  Male

Age (optional)?  38

What is your ethnic background?  Italian-Greek-Polish

How long have you lived in Jackson Heights?  38 years (5 apartments all within 6 blocks of each other ... I'm not really a nomad.)

Do you work in Jackson Heights?  No, I work in Floral Park.  I teach special education.

Do you drive or use public transportation?  Public transportation, though I'm a very good passenger when others drive.   

Do you own or rent accommodation?  Own, always have.

What major changes do you think have taken place in Jackson Heights (in terms of economic development, density, housing, ethnicity, transportation, etc.)?

Wow, that's a longer answer. 

     Obviously the neighborhood has gone through major changes a few times in my 38 years.
     As far as crime, I remember the street gang 70s, the wild drug 80s, the cleaner and leaner 90s, etc.  It's partially the change in the neighborhood (I recall when we first got the 115th precinct), and partially the overlying betters sense of community here.
     Housing prices, after a dip in the early 90s, have skyrocketted.  (I bought a co-op in 1993, sold it for over 200% what I paid in 2002, bought a larger co-op with those profits, and now that property is worth over twice that value.  If I were starting out today, I'd be priced out of my own neighborhood.)
     Economic development is different ... better and worse.  Rather than give any one opinion, I would point out that franchises were not here when we were growing up (we had to hike to Astoria Blvd or the mall for McDonalds!), but the smaller stores were always special as well (for example, we bought our (yes, its true) RECORDS from Numbers, not the mall).
     Ethnicity was always diverse ... Jackson Heights was unique in that aspect.  I recall when I went to high school at Bronx Science, there were kids from all over the city.  There were neighborhoods that were known as predominately Italian, or Colombian, or Irish.  Jackson Heights had no "classification" ... it was always diverse.
     Transportation was always great ... 20 minutes from Manhattan.  It's only been improved by better service overall (although the once-19B-now-49 needs more buses!!) and more train lines.
     And the density is best summed up in the education.  When I went to PS 69, kids from all over the neighborhood went there.  We then went to IS 145 (except for the few who tried the then brand new IS 227 magnet school).  It was a little crowded but we all fit.  Now there are so many more kids, there are 4 extra schools ... 69 itself only takes kids from 72nd Street to 79th or 80th.  So the population density has increased, as is reflected by that fact.

What problems in Jackson Heights cause you concern?

     The neighborhood is great, but of course it could be even better.  Security can sometimes be an issue (I'd had family assaulted outside the school while dropping children off), especially at night.  The over pricing of the real estate tends to attract a certain "trendy" element that then express dissatisfaction over the way the neighborhood is.  Some object to the noise ... me I thrive in it.
    Finally, Jackson Heights is the reason so many of us are overweight!  All this great food to eat out or order in ... why stir up the Campbells when I can get aloo gobi or pad khing or a Colombian steak?    Yes I can blame the neighborhood for the extra pounds I packed since college.

Glad I found your survey!  Its a great way to introduce myself to a board that I apparently found late night months ago (ah insomnia!).
