Neighborhood Chat / Re: What would be a successful business?
« on: March 05, 2017, 09:49:09 AM »
This question comes up every 2-3 months and always gets the same response. For those of us without kids (and disposable income that we spend outside of the neighborhood), the things we really want are food (and drink) related. And they're received with a mix of eye rolling and scorn. Alas.

- Cheese, meats, and prepared foods store similar to Bklyn Larder or R&D Foods
- Cocktail bar
- Boozy brunch spot
- Good coffee shop in the mid 80s (bonus points for a good bagel)

While an upscale gym or a movie theatre or a Fairway would amazing, they're obviously not viable options for a single store front on 37th. But there are some chain stores that wouldn't be so bad to have:

- Sephora
- Muji
- Dog daycare (probably requires extra space)
- Ice cream shop (this winter you'd have business all year round)

Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Kitchen reno by Corrigan Services (highly recommended)
« on: February 27, 2017, 08:02:49 AM »
The steam riser was split in two with the main riser being set into the wall, and a capped pipe run around the stove to the back wall in the event a future owner wants to connect a small radiator. The pipe is exposed where it comes up through the floor, so we had the ventilated door on the bottom row custom made to prevent the heat from building up inside and warping the wood.

Our dumbwaiter is still in use, alas. Otherwise we would have looked at doing something similar. Although I actually think that the shaft would be good for running the conduits for wall-mounted air conditioners—the ones with exterior compressors. But, of course, you have to sell the whole building on that plan.
