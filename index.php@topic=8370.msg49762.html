While the bistro debate has been entertaining, it will be of little use if nobody acts. A suggestion to those who are interested in a bistro: put your money where your mouth is. If you want a bistro, a pub, American cuisine, all natural or other fancier restaurant to come to JH, you should do a kickstarter.

If you don't know what is kickstater, look here:
http://www.kickstarter.com/start

This is an example showing kickstarter being used to raise money for a restaurant:
http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1448509144/forno-moto-bringing-pizza-napoletana-to-trade-stre?ref=live

One way this could work
1. people interested in a bistro create a group.
2. they approach a handful of restaurants in other neighborhoods that they think would do well in JH. Ask how much they'd need to open a place here and find a candidate.
3. the group starts a campaign to raise money via kickstarter.
4. If enough money is raised, the bistro would commit to open a JH branch.

Email me if you are interested. (Frankly, I don't think that this will take off. But I hate to just talk talk talk.)

I realize I may have ruffled a few feathers by suggesting Starbucks goers are mindless, because almost EVERYONE goes there. I just get so frustrated that Juju's lines aren't out the door and even e77s aren't overwhelmed with business. I walk past and think if there were that many people inside a mom and pop shop, how much more positive impact that would create. People go to what they know, people make safe decisions. You can get a coffee at a ton of other places, but people pay more for it at Starbucks because they know exactly what they are going to get. And no higher education, miseducation, place of birth, spouse of choice is going to get you a pass for sending your money to Seattle when you could get a coffee at Lety's.

What the demographic would support and what makes a good business decision are two different things. There are probably spaces near Alchemy which have much lower rent, I would think there are no 10k rents there.  -- I do think some place like Queens Kickshaw would do well here though. I think the demographic would support that. Also, with the popularity of the farmers market, I think there might even be space for a family oriented local market type place to eat. 

But considering how big NYC is, I would never rent out a space for such an exorbitant amount. Maybe I don't think big enough.
