Community Groups, Activities & Events / Queens Around the World Bike Ride 10/11
« on: October 03, 2009, 01:34:28 PM »
Join the Transportation Alternatives Queens Committee on our 25-mile bike ride, beginning at the War Memorial in Astoria Park on Shore Blvd and looping up to and back from Flushing. We'll pass many historical and cultural stops in Queens, like the Lemon King, Sunnyside Historical District and the birthplace of Scrabble! We'll be joined by local historian Rich Melnick of the Greater Astoria Historical Society, who will impart his wealth of knowledge of the area.

WHAT:  Queens Around the World Bike Ride
WHERE: Leaves from War Memorial in Astoria Park on Shore Blvd
WHEN: Sunday 10/11 Leaves at 10am

RSVP to Sarah at fourdozen@gmail.com, space is limited!

NOTE: This event is free. Please bring a working bike, helmet and lock.

« on: October 01, 2008, 03:55:42 PM »
Join the Transportation Alternatives Queens Committee for our 2nd annual Rockaway Ride, which will be held on Saturday, October 18.

I know, I know, it's the day before the Tour de Bronx.  Just consider the 45 mile round-trip Rockaway Ride your pre-Bronx warm-up!  Here are the details:

Saturday, October 18

Queens Meeting time: 8:30am
Queens Meeting place: Steinway Municipal Lot, Steinway Street between 31st Ave. and Broadway (next to Starbucks), Astoria

Brooklyn Meeting time: 10:15am
Brooklyn Meeting place: Grand Army Plaza, outside Prospect Park

Rockaway Meeting time: 11:30am
Rockaway Meeting place: entrance to Fort Tilden on Beach 169th Street and Murray Road

Cost: current TA members ride for free!  All others are asked to join TA at the discounted rate of $20 (all member benefits apply).

Route: We'll ride from Astoria to the Rockaways and back, traveling through quiet neighborhoods, Prospect Park, and on Shore Blvd. Parkway and the Marine Parkway Bridge.  When we arrive at the Rockaways, we'll enjoy a guided tour of some of Fort Tilden's very active community buildings, courtesy of the Rockaway Waterfront Alliance.  We'll have a snack and then turn around, perhaps stopping at Di Fara Pizza in Midwood on the way back.  45-ish miles total, relaxed pace.

RSVP: Please email emiliajocrotty(at)gmail.com to sign-up and receive a cue sheet and information in advance.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Trans Alt Queens Membership Drive
« on: October 01, 2008, 03:46:50 PM »
Hey JHL'ers I wanted to let you know about a special offer from your transit, pedestrian and cycling advocates at Transportation Alternatives.  During the whole month of October TA is offering all Queens residents a special discount on TA membership, only $20.  In addition to the TA magazine Reclaim new TA Queens Members also receive a limited edition "I bike Queens" t-shirt.  It's easy to sign up, just go to http://www.transalt.org/queens and click the banner at the top.

Those who follow the TA Queens Committee know the work we've been doing in Queens, including the first Tour de Queens and the Vernon Blvd bike lane.  By increasing membership in Queens TA increases its ability to better advocate for transit, pedestrian and cycling improvements in our fair borough.

We've got some fun events coming up in October to promote the committee.  I've added the first one, a ride out to Fort Tilden in the Rockaways, to the calendar.  More to come.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Summer Streets Feeder Rides In Queens
« on: July 30, 2008, 04:38:09 PM »
Transportation Alternatives will be leading feeder rides from a different location in Queens on each of the days of Summer Streets.  You can get more info here.

For those who don't know Summer Streets is a new program from the DoT where they will close Park Ave from the Brooklyn Bridge up to 72nd St to all car traffic and open it up to the community.  This is not a street fair, merchants along Park may be out of the street but you won't see sock vendors or funnel cake vendors (not that I have anything against socks or funnel cake).  Summer Streets takes place 8/9, 8/16 and 8/23 from 7am-1pm.

To sign up for a feeder ride send your full name and the neighborhood of the ride you wish to do to summerstreets@transalt.org or call 212.629.8080.   Riders will receive a special gift when they stop by the T.A. tables at Summer Streets.  Here is the schedule:

Saturday, August 9th
    * Astoria
      Meet up in Astoria Park in front of the World War I Memorial along Shore Boulevard
      Ride begins at 9 am

Saturday, August 16th
    * Sunnyside
      Meet up at Doughboy Park (enter at 52nd Street an 39th Drive) at 8:30 am
      Ride begins at 9 am

Saturday, August 23rd
    * Flushing
      Meet up in Flushing Meadows Park next to the Unisphere at 8 am
      Ride begins at 8:30 am

Riders should bring a a bike (duh), a helmet, water and sunscreen.

You can also check out the 30 sec PSA running on CBS here.

Even if you don't ride you should check out Summer Streets, it's a great experiment about opening up our public space for good times and if people don't come out that's what it'll remain, an experiment.  Plus if you do the feeder ride you'll get to meet me, and that is an experiment in itself.
