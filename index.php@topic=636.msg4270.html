You know, I've been running a program offering English classes to Jackson Heights residents for almost two decades.

Right now we serve almost 700 adult students per day, providing intensive instruction, but even that many slots does not come close to meeting the needs in the community  We need to select students by a highly competitive lottery... sometimes turning away three out of every four applicants.  Students who lose the lottery often beg, cry, plead, literally break down in tears.  English is the key to everything in this country, and immigrants know it.  Anyone who says otherwise, quite frankly, does not know what they are talking about.  

Learning a new language as an adult is an enormous amount of work, requiring hundreds of hours of instruction and even more practice and application.   But getting access to free or low-cost classes is sometimes even more difficult.  

I agree that if you live here, you should speak English.  But I lose patience with those who feel free to bash folks who are trying to learn, but haven't gotten there yet.

You know, I've been running a program offering English classes to Jackson Heights residents for almost two decades. 

I agree that if you live here, you should speak English.  But I lose patience with those who feel free to bash folks who are trying to learn, but haven't gotten there yet. 
I'd like to applaud you on the good work that you are doing.

That said, people who are trying to learn English don't get bashed. It's the people who have been in this country for decades who have no desire to learn English that get bashed. All the supers on my block get free rent, telephone, gas, electricity and their union salary and yet when you ask them to do something in your apartment they don't understand what you are saying. My building's super has been in this country for 35 years and still cannot understand "my toilet is overflowing" or a "pipe just ruptured in my bathroom" or "UPS said you signed for a package for me." My parents, and all their peers came to this country speaking nothing but another language and learned English without free schools, or free tutors. They learned because they had to learn in order to make a living. My parents and their peers not only learned to speak the language on their own, and read the language on their own, they embraced English without ever losing the love or the ability to speak their native tongue and made certain that their children also learn. When I was a little girl we had a Polish butcher and a German grocer. They also learned how to communicate effectively in their adopted country. They learned from their children who also had to learn English in school or be expelled. When I went to school, all classes were taught in English, even those classes which taught English. I have no patience for people who expect everyone around them to be catering to their unrealistic expectations. There are some countries who have an official second-language, we are not, yet, one of them. Learning any new language is a difficult process. But not showing any inclination to wanting to learn, out of regard for the traditions of your adopted country, is simply disrespectful.
