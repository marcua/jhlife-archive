I spoke up at the Community Board tonight against the development of this 6 story building in place of the former one story building destroyed by the fire. I was sadden to see the Community Board approve the development plan and drawings. Having heard other people speak and having seen the plans for the first time I have learned a few other disheartening things.

For instance, the owner's plans call for building all the way up to the side walk without any room for grass, plants or trees in the front of the building, like almost every other residential building in the neighborhood. There is no internal courtyard. The owner's plans call for building all the way up to the neighboring building on 84th Street and bricking-up every window on that building.  The owner's plans call for the entire eastern side of the building, the one that is right on top of the existing 1 story building, to have no windows and be entirely of brick. The term used by some is that is going to look like a 6 story jail. They are building it as mini skyscraper, meaning that it will be tall and rectangular.  There will be no shaping of the building to provide natural light and air. It will be provided entirely by a HVAC system and one window facing either 37th Ave. or 84th Street, depending of where the apartment is located.

There will be no community space or room in the building. Instead, what they mean by "community room", is that there will be a second story commercial mezzanine for most likely medical offices. That, and that the building itself, will have a community room for its own residents and not the community. The building will be a free market rental building without any rent regulated apartments, of which there will be 45. There will be only 16 parking spaces available.

These are Landmark issues which need to be addressed. This plan is completely opposite to what this neighborhood is all about. And of course these issues are all on top of the development issues in general that should provide concern for the neighborhood, such as stresses on the local parking, schools, parks, libraries, sanitation, light, air, etc.

I urge people to come to the Landmarks hearing scheduled for Tuesday morning, November 23, 2010 at 9:30am to speak up against this development.
