Title: Re: Advice Needed: Co-Op keeps rejecting sale price, renovation/contracter options?
Post by: NYC Native on August 08, 2014, 02:32:56 AM
Hi Breakdown

I'm sorry about all of the drama...1st thing to do is take it off the market and do a bit of sprucing up - keep your agent but ask him / her to pull it off MLS and Trulia etc for a couple of weeks while u get it right.  You then place it back on the market with new photos etc etc.

Send me some pictures of the unit, Ill give you some free advise if you would welcome it.  You might be able to get a lot more money if you so some simple things to the bathroom and kitchen.  Have you painted it and staged it?  Its not a lot of money if you think about your return..  Lighting is really important and so is staging!

The board is just being careful about bringing down the value of the other apartments - so while their actions are hurting your sale they are in fact helping you and the other shareholders.  40k represents about 22% of 180k so that's a big drop...I am certain that you can probably get another 20 to 25k with a 6k to 8k investment on your end.  If a bank is involved then you get another 6% on a concession so you are closer to the previous sale price and if you get a cash buyer than you can do a full concession for renovations matching the previous higher sale.  On a final note, You and your agent should have been aware of the latest sales sheet before accepting this offer - an experience Coop Broker would have advised against accepting it since the price would have red flagged the Board and management.

I have served on Boards and still serve on committees myself...including an admissions committee .   While I understand your frustration and believe me, I have been in your shoes myself, as a Board member, I couldn't justify accepting a sale that would represent a 20% decrease from a previous comparable sale.

Good Luck
