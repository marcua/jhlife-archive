I went to Valentino's on the Green (former Caffe on the green - Bayside, right off the Cross island)  - I guess it being the former home of Valentino the name is adequate.  The menu has a big black & White photo of Valentino in front.  I went during lunch time and the place had a dozen patrons or so.  Looks very similar to the old on the green with a few touches, but I don't remember the old place that well, since it has been almost a decade since I patronize the place.  They are supposed to be working on a cigar area outside with a bar.  It sounded like it would be fun to come back when it is done.  

I went there for lunch with my friend Danny and he was very hungry.  I ordered the Merlot and Dan ordered the Cabernet. We were served with great enthusiasm and professionalism - the waiter was very courteous and cracked a couple of jokes.  We shared the tomato and Mozzarella - it was OK but they would have benefited from a nicer picked tomato.  They put a couple of slivers of red onions which I didn't understand, because it was barely sufficient for even one tomato (i guess they used it as a garnish   )  In my opinion, either put a couple of healthy slices of sweet onions or don't put any.  Dan also ordered the eggplant soup which he said was great.  

The small bread basket was very nice and varied, but all of the breads were very chewy.  Dan also ordered the fillet of sea bass which I tried and I found very bland, even with the fancy sauce.  I ordered the ossobucco (hey, pattern  :2funny:).  It was served with flame grilled veggies and a polenta, but I have to tell you WOW!  It was a home run, and not just the the meat but the polenta was creamy but not runny, the veggies were crispy and smokey. When Danny tried it he paused and looked at me - he said "I ordered the wrong dish".  I will come back sometime during one evening and let you guys know about the ambiance and food in th evening.  In the old days the spot was stigmatized but the building is very pretty and would be a shame not to patronize it if the food turns out to consistently good.
