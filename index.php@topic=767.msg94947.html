I hope they don't feel the need to compete with the new guys up the avenue...I've been going to lety's since they opened, and while they don't make world class pastry, it's the quintessential New York bakery, the kind you don't see too much any more. I love the cannolis and we always get kids b-day cakes here. More than anything though, I hope they aren't planning on kicking out the old timers and neighborhood kids who park in their seats. The fact that it's a neighborhood rest-stop is the greatest service they provide, and I know conventional wisdom says that being too lenient in this way is bad business, I'd argue that it's the only reason this pace has sustained itself all these years.

Lety's Italian cheesecake is excellent and a rare find nowadays. The traditional macarons (her S-shaped cookies are gooey delicious almond paste which was the macaron before someone decided to color them, shape them by machine, and sell them in a box for a ridiculous price) are superb. Her cappuccino is only 2-something and is served in a glass mug.

I find the pretty stuff at fancier places is often too sweet for me. Sugar does not make pastries better.

Love Lety's.
