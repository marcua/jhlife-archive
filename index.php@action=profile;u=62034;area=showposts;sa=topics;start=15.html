Neighborhood Chat / If you didn't live in Jackson Heights where would you live?
« on: March 27, 2018, 09:25:03 PM »
I  already have my answer to that question since i moved to Bayside about five years ago.  For me it was a hard decision since I lived in Jackson Heights or the immediate area since the 1970's.  Why did I decide to move?  The need for two cars to get to work.  Back in the  1980's neither one of us had a car and we both worked in Manhattan or near JH  so it made the decision easy because the area has such great public transportation.  We eventually got one car in the 1990's because we wanted to do day trips on the weekend and going to the beach on public transportation was a long day  and a hassle.  In 2008 we needed a second car because  one of us  was working in Eastern Queens and the other in Western  Nassau and it was just too hard to get to work with public transportation.  After two years of battling the parking we decided to buy a house in Bayside.  If we could have rented garages for both cars (even at a high cost) we would have stayed in JH but finding garages close enough to our apartment were elusive.

How did things work out?  We definitely miss the conveniences of JH and the  wide variety of eateries and the ease of getting into the city on weekends and just strolling on 37th avenue.  But we found out there were a number of things we didn't miss at all.  We didn't miss the honking of car horns at all hours, searching for parking, having friends who  lived outside the city and needed to visit us by car not come because they couldn't find parking or not knowing if your new neighbors would be stomping around at all hours of the night or playing loud music.  Here are some of the things we love about Bayside.  We have a driveway and a garage  and aside from not needing to look for parking it is nice when you come home from  shopping that  you can walk five feet to your door and unload or you can come home late at night and not feel uneasy walking home.  We have space for everything since there are only two of us since we don't have children.  We have a backyard where we grow tomatoes, cucumbers and other things and we can BBQ and entertain outside.  We are less then 10 minute walks  to Little Bay Park and Fort Totten.  We are also close to the Bay Terrace Shopping center which has some nice stores and the main drag on Bell Blvd has a number of pubs and places to have  drinks and a burger and the stores are getting more diverse each year.  The dining is surprisingly good with many Greek, Chinese, Japanese, and Korean eateries.  There are  Colombian, Peruvian and Mexican restaurant too.  Don't get me wrong it is not like JH for eating but  it has  a pretty good selection.

What do I do when we miss JH?  We take a 20 minute drive and spend a few hours in JH and eat there.  I will always love the Heights but  I feel living in Bayside and being able to visit JH so easily is the best of both worlds.  I would love to hear where others would want to live or if they already left JH where would they go.  I am also aware for some people  there is no other place they would want to live and plan on staying in JH for a log time.
