Neighborhood Chat / What's new in Forest Hills?
« on: February 11, 2008, 05:17:47 PM »
Even in the thick of February malaise, not wanting to bother or be bothered, you've probably heard some of the news-noise going on in Forest Hills this past week--

Mazoltuv Barukhova was the media's black widow last week, arrested on Thursday by the 112th precinct for hiring her uncle-in-law to murder her estranged husband last October to regain custody of her daughter. Gothamist summarized the details two days later (why the delay?) while Splitting Hairs mused on taking justice into your own hands.

The Sunday Times real estate section profiled a diehard Manhattan couple who discovered the charms of Forest Hills, but then opted for Kew Gardens because they couldn't find a 1-BR apartment in FoHi for less than $400K. Those in the real-estate know in Forest Hills proper find that a dubious excuse at best.

The old Wine Gallery, site of many a first and blind date, closed and in its stead will be the Bonfire Grill, with uptown, upscale food for those who pine for restaurants a la the UES. Meanwhile, at the low end of the food chain, rumors about longtime pizza institution A&J's were put to rest...or were they?


Come visit; it's safe and delicious now.
Karyn/Splitting Hairs in Forest Hills

Greeting from one express subway stop away! Here are some local happenings from Forest Hills:

  • Local yoga studio Bamboo Moves has organized a petition to bring a green market to Forest Hills. There's strong interest among FoHi residents in seeing this happen. http://www.queenscentral.com/forums/topic/417?replies=3

    On the Grill, a Middle Eastern place on Queens Boulevard, has issued a falafel challenge--eat 5 in 29 minutes and they're on the house, along with a $50 gift certificate for the restaurant. http://gothamist.com/2008/01/16/competitive_eat_1.php

    Foodies and health nuts are abuzz about the new organic cafe, Tierra Sana, taking shape on Queens Blvd. and 67th Rd. http://splittinghairsinforesthills.wordpress.com/2008/01/10/tierra-sana/

    The world is in shock about the death of Bobby Fisher, an event that has hit Forest Hills' Polgar Chess Center and the neighborhood's large contingent of emigre and homegrown chess players in a personal way. http://susanpolgar.blogspot.com/


Enjoy!
