Regal Heights?

My mom recently suffered a major stroke that has left her extremely weakened, to the point where i know she can't live on her own anymore. i want nothing more than to have her live with me, but realistically, i don't think that's a terribly viable option -- i am clueless about medical care issues, and no matter how much i love her, i don't think i can learn that.

so i was wondering if anyone had any experience, positive or negative, to share about regal heights, on 35th avenue. it has good ratings from the state, generally high marks from other agencies, and, well, it looks nice. but i wanted to know if anyone had any hands-on experience.

thanks.

Sorry to hear about your mom. Although I'm not familiar with Regal Heights, I'm a speech pathologist who has had a number of experiences working in rehabilitation centers and nursing homes. Are you looking for a residential setting with a rehabilitation center. Many places other than Regal Heights offer a high level of rehabilitative services. Clients return to their homes with support services (i.e. aides, visiting nurse services etc.). If the rehabilitation center deems her unsafe to go home, then you will require long term residential care. I don't know your religious affiliation but Queen of Peace residence is par excellence for both.
