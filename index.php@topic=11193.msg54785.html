I moved here from Astoria about a year and a half ago.  While I do love it here, I will admit it's an adjustment. 
Pros -

- train service - there are always at least 2-3 7 trains to the N when transferring at Queensboro.  Don't know why, but it seems to be a rule.  You will likely always wait for an N or Q while 2-3 7 trains have already come through. When the 7 takes the weekend or night off - which is often - you can take the express E, F, or local R and M at Roosevelt and 74th.  So you have tons of options.   Also a good one to know is the Q66 will bring you to Astoria up Northern Blvd to 35th Ave - perfect for the Museum of the Moving Image and Kaufman Cinema.  The R/M takes you to Steinway.

- great spacious apartments with plenty of closet space, elevators and laundry facilities.  Astoria is lacking in this.  Supers who work for a living and actually taking care of the building.  Another plus.  - if you do have to use laundry elsewhere - it is expensive and I've noticed that most places are horribly crowded and dirty.  So work to find laundry in your building.

- greenmarket - open all year round every sunday - best in the hood, other markets are average and similar to Astoria

Cons

- things shut down by 10pm.  If you're looking for food after that area, your best bet is Roosevelt.

- Food - lots of fantastic Indian/Asian/Latin American restaurants.  Most close by 10pm.  There are a few other cuisines represented but not as many options as Astoria has.  Waiter service is questionable at more than a few spots. 

- Bars/cafes - not many around.  Tons on Roosevelt but most of cabaret gentleman variety.  Terraza 7 is nice, Expresso 77.  For some reason, there are no outdoor seating places.   Perhaps there's a law against it.  Nothing like 30th ave. 

- No parks or gyms.   - If you are lucky enough to buy a coop with a courtyard - at least you'll have that.  You'll miss Astoria Park and Socrates.  Blink is opening in June and will be a god send.  And probably packed.

- Roosevelt Ave.  My opinion.  Others seem to love it.  It does great business of the cabaret trade and all that goes with it.  If you're a girl, don't walk it at night.  Drunks do follow you home.  I get off at 82nd and walk away from Roosevelt at night.

All that being said, the area is changing daily.  I do love it here and go to Astoria, Sunnyside, and Woodside on a regular basis since we have amazing transportation.  So it's no big deal once you get used to it.  You are not far from anything in the big scheme of things.

I came from Brooklyn five years ago, but considered Astoria when I wanted to move to Queens to be closer to work. Daisy's review is pretty accurate in my opinion.  My best friend lives in Astoria and she tried to get me to move there, but JH was cheaper, offered more space for the money and had more train options.  It also has better street parking than most of the spots I've seen in Astoria (there, many streets seem to have alternate side up to 4x/week, where JH only has 2x/week).  The 7 train being out of service on weekends is a problem, but at least there are plenty of other options...as opposed to waiting forever for the N/Q which I've had to do many nights on the way in from Manhattan. I still go to Astoria for the gym and BBQ at Butcher, and Martha's on occasion, but I like the new additions to JH and I'm glad I'm here.

I need to move to a bigger apartment soon, and I looked into Astoria but (in my opinion) it is still overpriced for what it is, compared to JH.

Good luck with your search!
