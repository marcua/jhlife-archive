For my money, I'd like to see the following open in JH:

1.  A bookstore - a real honest-to-goodness bookstore, new and used, where you could get lost in the stacks. Someplace that has good selections - children's, fiction, non-fiction, local, indy press, foreign language books, etc.. Would be nice if they carried foreign magazines and papers too -- there's enough immigrants in the neighborhood I would think for that.

2. A good shoe repair shop - if there is one, I don't know about it.

3. A nice bar/pub. Yes, we have Pat's Ready Penny and Legend's which are good clean divey bars, but a nice one like some down in Woodside, Sunnyside and Astoria would be appreciated.

4. A gym - a real one, one of the good chains hopefully. While I'd love an Equinox, I don't think that's realistic, so maybe a NYSC?

What I DON'T want to see any more of are more 99 Cent Stores, more discount furniture stores, more banks, more chain drug stores. I wish we had a strong BID that could work with our local elected officials and landlords to entice more independent business owners to JH.

There is also good soup in the Latin bakeries. Luckily it's a traditional to have soup in all kinds of weather in Central and South America. The homemade soup at El Tinto Rico on 37th is $3 and is very filling and tasty. I get the soup at the bagel place at 86th and 37th all the time, too. Homemade lentil and chicken noodle every weekday -- not on weekends, it seems.

The shoe repair guy on 37th next to the Russian deli does good work and is cheap.

The economics of bookstores is draconian. I don't see one working here given that English isn't even the main language for many people. The only way it might work is as part of an expanded version of a coffee shop but that won't happen either given commercial rents for bigger spaces in this area. Sad but true.

What I do think could work for JH is more optimization of the food. The street cart fair in the fall on 82nd was packed. If we had space for an El Mercado type concept that featured good food and crafts -- not junky stuff -- it could take off. But someone has to organize it. It kills me to see "curated" places like the Brooklyn Flea "showcasing" the food we take for granted on nearly every corner here.
