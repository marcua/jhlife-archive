« on: March 11, 2008, 04:53:27 PM »
Quote from: 84th on March 10, 2008, 11:51:51 PM
JH was gentrified from the '20's thru the mid '60's when it began its downward slide, and as a poster said, it's swinging upward now. 

Maids and housekeepers were the norm until the early '60s.  No one shopped on 82nd Street without being "dressed" complete with gloves and hats. 


A discussion with any of the many older residents of Jackson Heights who are still around, or even a look at the many old pictures of the neighborhood, will verify this.  The elegant architecture of the Historic District (the original development), with its luxury apartments, was built for and occupied by the "yuppies" of the '20s and '30s, not the working class, as mistakenly indicated by previous posters.  There was even a golf course with a clubhouse and tennis courts!  It was not until the mid-'60s that the neighborhood began to experience a swift downward spiral whose effects are still painfully visible. The fact is, were it not for MacDougall's vision of a community of upscale apartment dwellers, the architecture of Jackson Heights would not be nearly as beautiful or distinctive as it is.
