Quote
I actually like the little "wooded area" by the little kids' part of the current Travers Park. The concept is to get some plants in there, but not in such a way that people would trample and destroy them, requiring maintenance which we can't afford because rich people don't give money to JH. So maybe some areas with flowers and trees that are generally protected by gates.

Theplanesland and others make some very good points about what the park could become with vision and maintenance. We have a model that could be helpful going forward, one that builds upon the basics provided by the parks department, JHBG/Friends of Travers Park.

The “wooded area” in the kiddie zone was planted by the group’s volunteers. In fact most of the plants in the park were planted and cared for by our group: from the forsythia that will be blooming soon, to the ivy and the trumpet vine (orange flowers in summer) that cover the wall of the car dealership at the back of the park.

The same way we did that a dozen years ago or so could be expanded. WE HAVE THE MONEY TO DO MORE PLANTINGS AND THE EQUIPMENT TO CARE AND WATER THEM. (The corner at end of the swings has a bare spot that needs some help.) To do that we need more volunteers. We are all busy: with a jobs, looking for a job, caring for kids, caring for parents - sometimes all of the above at once. What makes volunteering doable is to turn it into fun and relaxation, getting your hands in the dirt. When we’ve succeeded, that’s been our secret.

Right now a lot of our time is being taken up with our Tree LC street tree project, greening 78th Street and opposing the landgrab in Flushing Meadows, all part of JHBG Green Agenda for Jackson Heights project. So we need more help.

We have the ear of parks department officials because they know we know what we are doing. You will be surprised how much more can be done fairly easily with help. If you want to help, contact me at LenManiace@gmail.com. I will get your name to the current leaders of our Friends of Travers Park committee.

All the best,

Len Maniace
JHBG vice president/GA4JH chair

Len, I definitely agree that there are many active members of our community, like yourself, that help make Jackson Heights the great place that it is to live. The more people are involved in your group or others, the better it can be.

Knowing that not everyone is able to commit to volunteering, I do think there is value in contributing to a dialog like this. As an example, you mentioned that several people contacted you recently to volunteer based on some of your posts yesterday. Talking clearly did something! It is great to see this discussion got the attention of yourself and Dudley of the JH Green Alliance since you both seem to be heavily involved with Travers Park and I feel confident that our suggestions will be taken into consideration. I hope people continue to contribute ideas. I know there are many creative and thoughtful people in our neighborhood.

I thought of a few more wishes:

Acquire the two vacant storefronts on the south side of Northern Blvd, between 78th and 79th. One was a Mandee and the other, a Blockbuster which is in the process of moving out. They are adjacent to the Garden School. I'm definitely grateful that our park is expanding but I think the reality is that even with the expansion, we will still not have enough public open space in our neighborhood. I think the discussion above of whether playing fields or places for sitting are more needed represents this. We need both. I realize this might be a longshot but this is a wish list.

A talented landscape architect that is receptive to the many ideas in our community that can balance our needs and wants with the practical issues of budgets and available space. I realize we are unlikely to get all of our wishes, but a talented team can bring us most of them.

Maintain space for our farmers market I don't think this is threatened, however it may make sense to rethink where it is currently situated in order to make room for more green space.

A fountain If an area is devoted to more passive activities (sitting, reading, etc.) this would not only provide a visually attractive feature, but the sound from the fountain would help mask the noise of the more active spaces of the playgrounds and athletic courts. This would create a more tranquil environment.

I'm not a stranger to volunteering and I definitely would not rule out volunteering for one of your groups in the near future.

  I like a lot of these suggestions but think if you go the next step and cost out these options that would help.
The asking rent at Mandees for example is $25,000 or more per month. This is way beyond the means of
NYParks to add to our JH world. This is where the volunteering comes in and why JHBG has been successful
long-term with grants and additions to our public projects. Monies are usually given in higher amounts
to communities that show they will commit to maintenance and pride in the improvements. So, if we have
a high volunteer count, Jackson Hts.moves further up the priority list, in some funders eye, as to worthy
projects to fund. I know it is hard for many to volunteer but you would be very surprised to see how busy
many of our local stalwart volunteers are personally and how much time they carve out for neighborhood
work. Seriously.
  As for landscape architects, I love the work that NYParks is doing with their teams and would trust them
to listen to the neighborhood from a multi-faceted solicitation of ideas - similar to this forum - plus lots
of face to face surveys and videos of high use times like mid-summer around 4:00-6:00 on a weekend
and the park is bursting at the seams! One of my favorite recent NY Parks projects is the Teardrop Park
in Lower Manhattan which takes a small space in a visionary direction. There is a lot to do there for different
members of the community without falling on each other. It does wonders for sound too.
  The Farmers Market location debate, as I understand, has gone on for a few years. I think it's in the
wrong place now long term but can't think of the solution either which is what I believe is the problem.
 As for a fountain, this is a liability issue that NYParks may not want to invest in, as one small accident
could be a major insurance headache and I think something like the Highline water feature running over
the stones on the ground is more feasible. I know the Flushing Meadows fountain is hardly ever on and it's beautiful.
 Jackson Heights has a lot of people with opinions - and this is hugely helpfull- but if we could get the boots
on ground volunteers up even 20-30%, the difference would be big in terms of seeing results and raising
our profile to park funders. Brooklyn has reaped many benefits green-wise in the last decade and would
love to see the same thing happen here.
   Spring is here! Look for volunteer opportunities with Jackson Heights Beautification Group and others to
