Maplewood NJ = Jackson Heights?
« on: June 12, 2016, 11:33:19 AM »
Saw this article a while ago and yesterday we went out to investigate our spiritual doppleganger in Jersey. 
http://www.timeout.com/newyork/things-to-do/the-coolest-places-in-new-jersey-for-new-yorkers

It was definitely worth a visit.  We noticed a number of similarities. 

1.  In JH, we are used to living with a high proportion of Latins and South Asians.  While there is bunching, all demographics tend to live amoungst one another.  Maplewood is the same way, except that there are almost no Latins, South Asians and the whites and blacks there are rigidly segregated. 

2.  In JH we have commercial streets thickly laced with restaurants that represent the population, 99 cent stores, pharamacies and botanics.  We have a plethora of supermarkets, fruit stands and fabric shops.  Again, Maplewood is the same except that it has none of those things.  In the four blocks huddled around their NJ transit station, they have bakeries, ice cream shops, working movie theaters and bookstores and upscale eateries.  Their Indian restaurant is a spitting image of Delhi Heights, except its a counter based shop that makes its trade on omelette (its called Amy's Omelette Shop), baked goods and coffee with a side poster offering about 7 Indian dishes.  The Mexican place is upscale and the Colombian place has outdoor seating and it fusion based. 

3.  Finally, the housing stock in the two hoods are the same, although Maplewood has almost no apartment buildings and Jackson Heights has almost no grand detached houses with huge yards and winding driveways.

So if you are looking for our sister city, look no further than Maplewood NJ, or perhaps Luanda, Dhaka or Paris.  Yes, all of those places are very much similar!

Glad to see you took no umbrage to my equating Maplewood to Luanda!

To be fair, we only spent a few hours there.  So while it seemed awfully segregated to us, as in we pretty much saw only white folk in the commercial areas by the train station, and pretty much only saw black folk along Springfield Avenue, I'd clearly have to acquiesce to the opinions of someone who lives there.  What part do you live in?  Maplegood, Maplewood or Maplehood?  Those cheeky designations as well as the perceptions of segregation in Maplewood are discussed in this refreshingly candid article.
http://patch.com/new-jersey/maplewood/how-integrated-is-maplewood

Don't get me wrong, I'm not putting Jackson Heights up on a pedestal.  Our black population % is tiny, like about half of your Latin population %.  New York City in general is very segregated, but it's weak to try to grope out a comparison by noting that some in JH live in pre-war others in post war, some with, some without gardens.  Sure, only looking at the Towers would skew one's view of JH, but should one be looking at the Towers, all one must do is cock their head to see housing from luxurious to middling.  Walk another 200 feet in any direction and one would see even a wider spectrum.  How long is that walk from Baker Street to Springfield Avenue?  Maybe its just easier to take the car.

True about Tandoori Chef.  Our visit did stretch over the late afternoon and since Tandoori Chef closes between 2:30 and 5:30, we might have just missed it like that.  And correct on "Amy's Omlete Shop", as my wires must have gotten crossed.  The sign out front says "Village Coffee, House of Gourmet Omlettes".  And you are right about there being a second Mexican restaurant.  Kudos to those 69 Mexicans listed in the census who support two restaurants!
