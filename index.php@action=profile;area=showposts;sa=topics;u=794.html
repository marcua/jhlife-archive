Community Groups, Activities & Events / does JH need its own Origami meetup?
« on: September 18, 2011, 12:54:34 PM »
I've been going to Manhattan to an origami meet-up where people just fold together. It is very relaxed -- no formal teaching, just lots of friendly exchanges of information, and folders of all levels from total beginners to professionals, from kids to the retired and everyone between.

However, I don't always want to get on the subway. I have recently met several other people in JH who like to fold, and it occurs to us that maybe we need a local meet-up!

We are thinking about starting with one Saturday afternoon per month.

The big question is location. If there are just a handful of us, we could meet at one of the usual cafes.

If you think you might come on occasion, if you can think of a good place to meet, or if you are connected to a business that would welcome folders, send me an email offlist.

thanks!

Community Groups, Activities & Events / ISO Tamil speaker
« on: April 18, 2011, 06:23:29 PM »
I was awarded a Fulbright to study the mathematical thinking of expert kolam-makers in Tamil Nadu. (If you can help me, you most likely know what what a kolam is!)

I will be starting the project in about a year.

Before then I would like to work on spoken Tamil. I will have a collaborator/translator in Chennai, but to do a creditable job I need to be able to understand the gist of what is being said.

I was able to converse about daily life 10 years ago, but have since forgotten all but a few words. So I have to start over.

I can pay, or else help you and/or your kids with math -- either enrichment or tutoring for school.

In particular, since you are presumably interested in things Tamil, you might be interested in the series of math activities I have created that use kolams. I have given many workshops on this material (mostly to K-12 teachers & students, but also to students at the college level) both in Chennai (sponsered by the Math Sciences Institute) and in the U.S. (sponsored by various university Math/Asian Studies departments and/or school districts).

ciao,

Neighborhood Chat / #5 plastic recycling
« on: October 11, 2010, 01:31:40 PM »
I have seen news reports that the city is going to start collecting other plastics besides the jugs and bottles currently on the list. But a quick perusal of the official recycling website didn't yield any more information -- in fact, it still says they have no plans to expand plastic recycling.

http://www.nyc.gov/html/nycwasteless/html/resources/plastics_nyc_add.shtml

Is there anyone else who is frustrated at not being able to recycle their yogurt containers, take-out containers, etc.? I've cut way back on what I buy, but haven't been able to rid myself of them completely.

I saw that Whole Foods collects them for Gimme5, and I have a fantasy of doing a mini-recycling program with neighbors -- take turns taking them into the city every few weeks. Of course that would only work if it was just a few people, in which case, one might ask, what is the point?

« on: April 26, 2009, 01:55:59 PM »
I always had cats growing up, but my husband is very allergic.

He grew up with dogs, and so we have been thinking for a few years about getting a dog. We've hesitated so far because we know that dogs don't like being left even for a few days, and we tend to take frequent short trips out of town, and every couple of years we go to India for 3-4 weeks.

I've been thinking of the possibility of sharing custody with another person/family. That would solve our problem, but I am wondering about the effect on the dog.
