A little over 50 years ago my mother would go to Travers Park with me in a baby carriage.  She found it to be cliquish even back then, so she hung by herself.  One day she met another woman with a young child in a baby carriage who also felt left out from the crowd, so they struck up a friendship while their kids slept side-by-side in their carriages.  Over 50 years later I'm still best friends with that other kid--it's a long term relationship that's so rare in this day and age.  The morale of the story is don't worry about other people.  Go, hang out and enjoy the neighborhood.  It really is a great place to live.

We moved to JH from Park Slope some years ago and have a young child. Travers does have a cliquish vibe. In my experience, trying to initiate a conversation or just a greeting is often more difficult than it should be. That isn't to say that we haven't met people with whom we do get along.

It's just that Travers, and to some extent JH, lacks a certain openness for new people from different races or classes to mix socially. Outside of an approved venue such your child's daycare or your religious institution. I'm referring to a basic civility towards strangers. Such as acknowledging my presence when my child is playing with yours and I'm standing right next to you. And I'm smiling or talking to you.

We are working professionals with a biracial child. I specifically brought up class because at least the times that we're at Travers, the divisions don't seem to be about race but about class or perceived class.  The groups themselves are often ethnically diverse.

I'm putting this out there for those who have had similar experiences. No neighborhood is perfect and JH is often portrayed as a multi-racial utopia.  You're not alone and there's nothing wrong with you. JH has some great things to offer, but like every place else, it has got some fundamental issues to work on.

May I suggest trying out some of the other kid-centric spots and events in Jackson Heights?  There is the Library on 81st Street with the children's room upstairs, Music Together and Music for Aardvarks classes, movement classes for kids at the Garden school and the monthly story time at Espresso 77 (which my husband and I own).

You might also join the JHFamilies Yahoo Group and the Google email listserve for parents of babies close in age to yours.

I don't get to the playground with my daughter as often as I would like and I find that when I do go, I'm pretty focused on her.  It may be that a change of scene would bring you around parents who are more relaxed and able to chat.  There are many good-hearted parents in our neighborhood and it is a great community for raising kids - don't give up trying!
