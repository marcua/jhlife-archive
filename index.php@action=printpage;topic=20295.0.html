Title: Re: Buildings with garden: choosing apartments with street or garden view
Post by: mkn567 on December 13, 2020, 10:46:54 PM
It's a toss-up. Something about the way these blocks were designed make them perfect echo chambers. I'm amazed, not bothered, by how clearly i hear foot traffic and conversation from my sixth-floor, front-facing apartment. I am closer to 37th ave though.

Also don't assume a window facing the back is guaranteed serenity. Parents, especially on the board, have a tendency to try to turn backyards into personal playgrounds for their children.

If you want great lighting, pick a higher apartment, eastern exposure, especially facing a building with only 5 floors.

Title: Re: Buildings with garden: choosing apartments with street or garden view
Post by: CaptainFlannel on December 17, 2020, 10:00:47 AM
OP, you'll also want to consider southern exposure versus northern exposure. Most of my windows have a northern exposure, which leads to little direct light about 7 months out of the year. The only direct light I get consistently all year comes for 20 minutes each day, at an angle that shows every bit of dust and cat hair.

I rented an apartment on 37th Ave years ago facing the little courtyard. It was certainly quieter than the apartments facing 37th Avenue, but I also missed being able to look at and gauge the weather and what to wear from pedestrians.
