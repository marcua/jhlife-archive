Build It Green! in Astoria

Build It Green! NYC, started in 2005 and is New York City's only non-profit retail outlet for salvaged and surplus building materials. Their mission is to keep construction and building materials out of the landfill, while offering deep discounts on their resale.  Each year, Build it Green diverts about 450 tons of material for being dumped in landfills.


On Saturday,May 7th GrowNYC's Office of Recycling Outreach & Education (OROE) is hosting a Stop 'N' Swap at the bi-annual Build It Green! What's Queens Green Drinks again?

Very simply, it's an informal social gathering/networking event for folks that are environmentally minded. Come and we'll introduce you to some of the other like minded folks in and around Queens. If we're lucky (which is likely) we'll cross pollinate, make new friends, catch up with old ones, and maybe even sprout an idea or three.

We have a different host each month selecting the venue of their choice. If you are interested in being part of the Queens Green Drinks Committee; if you'd like to help to get the word out; if you have suggestions for hosts; or if you'd like to donate your skills in building a website please let me know at queensgreendrinks@gmail.com.

June 2011 Queens Green Drinks!

Wednesday, June 22nd, 2011
6:30pm - 9:00pm
at Bohemian Beer Hall in Astoria

Hosted by:
The Astoria Park Alliance & The Community Plot

The Astoria Park Alliance is a coalition of vibrant, diverse, and generous volunteers who share a passion for Astoria Park. APA sponsors clean ups, participates in the NYC Greeters Corps, and seeks to facilitate communication between park users and the NYC Parks Department. Astoria Park Alliance has installed a Butterfly Garden, a bulletin board and most recently, garbage cans in the park. Our mission is to beautify, enhance and encourage greater community involvement within Astoria Park. Building a Community starts in the Park!

The Community Plot of Two Coves Community Garden (or CP for short) is a shared garden space located at the heart of Two Coves Community Garden in Astoria. It was created in 2008, the vision of a group of Two Coves members to create a space for the garden community to come together. Since then, it has grown into a place where everyone - garden members, neighbors, and friends - is welcome to garden, harvest, and learn together. Most recently, with the help, muscle, and support of dedicated community members, the CP constructed 9 raised beds - which were then installed, filled with soil and planted. We've also hosted Garden Together sessions with Crop Mob NYC and the Variety Girls and Boys Club of Queens, and held a  plant sale in partnership with Build it Green!NYC and R & R General Supply. Our goal is simple: to grow community, and some food!
