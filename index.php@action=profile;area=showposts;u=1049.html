I am happy here. I'm happy with the real neighborhood. But, being a fly on the wall of this forum that I see that we're not wanted here. I've heard it in the streets here, too. A woman a DD on 37th Ave and 75th street was saying, loudly, "This USED to be a nice neighborhood. I was over in Forest Hills the other day and it's a much nicer class of people. Here it's changed so much- you never hear English anymore." Then I left. This sentiment is clearly repeated again and again on various threads on the BB.

It's a great respite to me to be able to be among other people who share my language, shopping needs, culture, and pov, etc. It's a great pleasure to be able to live my entire daily life within the zip code. I love living here even if a few accoutrement are missing. That's why I am sad and angry we're just tolerated (barely) and not really welcome.

Neighborhood Chat / We're not wanted here
« on: August 04, 2010, 10:42:06 AM »
Reading through these threads has made me realize we're not wanted in this neighborhood.

We shop at "fronts" full of "imported sweatshop" merchandise (As though Macy's and even Bloomingdales doesn't sell imported stuff, often made in terrible conditions?!) We've gotten documents from the street in order to work and pay taxes for services that don't benefit us. We collect recyclables that apparently belong to the city. We rent a one-room apartment and share it between unrelated people.

I see that the the posters on this forum only want commuters working white collar jobs in Manhattan to actually live here. The rest of us are supposed to open ethnic restaurants for your enjoyment and sense of the exotic and then go live somewhere else. Although I've seen some voices of reason on this bulletin board, the majority of the posts here are in a crotchety voice of people pining for days gone by (when Jews and Black people were expressly prohibited from living in the neighborhood, legally by charter) or for a re-creation of yuppie colorless neighborhood like the Upper West Side at rock bottom prices.

Are you surprised we can actually read and use the internet?

What I'm looking for...
$600 max (incl utilities, plus internet)
I'm willing to split the cost of internet (and cable if it's already there).

Where I want to live
I'm looking for a place within 15 blocks (1 mile) of 74th Street & 37th Ave.

Why I'm Moving
My current JH room is in an apt where the other roommates moved out and the landlord wants to rent to his employees. I pay $600/mo incl. util on Northern Blvd & 85th Street in a 2 family house.

Who I am & Who I'd like to live with
I'm a youthful 32 y.o. female, non-smoker looking for a room in an apartment to share with females or males.

I'd like to live in a comfortable, no drama environment. I'm easy to live with. Generally, all my roommate situations have been good experiences and usually became good friends with my roommates in college and in NYC.

I have lived in NYC for about 10 years. I moved here from DC where I went to college. I grew up in Connecticut, but I was born in Korea.

I have a boyfriend who lives in Woodside who stays over sometimes.

My politics are progressive and I'm open-minded about all different lifestyles, although I don't think I can live with people who try to convert me to their religion.

At home I cook and watch TV.

I like 90s sitcoms (Roseanne, Home Improvement, That 70s Show), stoner flix (Harold & Kumar, Dude, Where's my Car?), foreign films (Old Boy, City of God), Food Network (Good Eats), and documentaries.

I play traditional Korean drum and love old-school heavy metal, but I won't subject either on my roommates.

What I do
I am respectful of other's things and privacy.

Paying my rent on time is my first priority each month.

I work 3pm-10pm in Jackson Heights as an ESL teacher, M-Th.

I wake up late morning so I won't interfere with a 9-5'ers morning schedule but I may make dinner after coming home around 10 pm.

I don't smoke or use drugs but enjoy beer or wine at home sometimes. I like to cook and share meals. I make mostly Korean, Italian, and Asian-fusion food. I'm not vegetarian but I try to make nutritious food.

I'm clean but not neurotic. I leave the kitchen & bathroom ready for the next person to use. I have no problem sharing cleaning duties, either (but I may need a gentle reminder).

My ideal roommate situation would be sharing a house or apartment with one, two, or three people who also have full lives and who are as relaxed and considerate as I am.

When I need the place
I have a place until March but I'm flexible and can move for Feb 1.

Pets
I don't have any pets, but I love most animals so if you have a cool pet, that's a plus.
