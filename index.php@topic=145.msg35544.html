Quote from: Chuckster on December 03, 2007, 11:52:50 AM
I've been in the neighborhood for quite a while now, but I can't seem to remember many of the shops at the station.  Perhaps because they were eyesores.  One place that closed and occupied a large spot was the OTB (Off Track Betting).  There also used to be a methodone (sp?) clinic right above it.  

The clinic used to be a billiards parlor prior. My memory is still pretty good since I lived on 74th off Broadway. Here is what I remember. OTB and a mens clothing store in the same alley as the upstairs clinic, A luncheonette next to the Q33 bus, Pizza Den, Hairsmith Barber Shop (which had a different name prior to that which escapes me), Fanny Farmer which became Frusen Gladje (sp?) Ice Cream, Bickfords Cafeteria which then become a combo Nathan's and Asian food by the pound. A German Deli which was right before the staircase to the downstairs.

Second level down was a video and pinball arcade which later became a record store, a coin shop/collectible store, flower shop, a head shop, another barber shop, shoe repair, a newstand, a cookie shop that sold fresh baked cookies by the pound.

Going back to street level there was a staircase next to Fannie Farmer that led up to offices that housed a dentist, a lawyer and perhaps a doctor. If memory serves there's a scene in the Hitchcock film The Wrong Man where Henry Fonda walks up that staircase and into the office area. Bickford's and the 7 train staircase can be seen in the film Queen of the Stardust Ballroom.
