The sad truth is I could probably make a better pitch for Danny Dromm than you could.

I may have my disagreements with his political leanings, associates, and tactics.  And I do.  I won't diminish his past accomplishments for the gay community in Queens.  I admire that he and a few others put themselves on the front lines for gay rights, and demanded an end to gay violence in Queens during the 1990’s.

You may or may not been around when Julio Rivera was brutally murdered in 1990 and the Police failed to carry out a proper investigation.  Danny, along with others, led the charge for justice.

As a founding member of the long defunct Pink Panthers, I know it was an uphill battle for legitimacy.

Fast forward to 2008. The issues have changed since 1990.  For the most part, it's no longer a life or death decision or a fight for equal treatment in most areas, at least in NY.  Some battles remain; those will be won too.  Let’s be honest with ourselves, the fact that we haven’t won the right to marry or openly serve in the military doesn’t prevent us from succeeding in life.  (Yes, I proudly served in the military too and I’m not a Republican).

Our past accomplishments--securing rights and respect--have allowed us to look outside of our own community and make political decisions that affect the larger community.  There are larger issues that affect our community, our city and our own personal interests.

For me, voting for a candidate solely because he is gay or has a strong record on gay rights is no longer enough to win my vote.  Mayor David Dinkins has made promises to the gay community and to large degree he failed to deliver.  And now we have a President who’s made promises and shows signs of reneging on them.  (Yes, someone got a shiny medal as a consolation prize).

I am not convinced that Danny Dromm is ready or prepared to make the leap from an activist to a community leader who could honestly and fairly represent our diverse community.  I knew him in the past, and from what I see and hear now, he hasn’t changed.

Apart from "who is the real Danny", his performance at the debates bares this out--perhaps this could be another topic.

Our engagement these past few days has done little to convince me otherwise. In fact, I see in your responses the same divisive, snide dismissal and belittling of opposing opinions, and the squashing of legitimate debate that Danny has used to advance his own activist/political career.  Do I want these qualities in a community leader.  No!

You could have the last word.

Hi earbears, I can certainly see how this race might cause confusion. Clearly I support Danny, and I believe a great deal of other people in the district do too.  

Some of the issues I have with Helen Sears are her lack of leadership, her inactivity, and the fact that she's so out of touch with her constituents. Sure she's done some things while in office, but problems that were small eight years ago have grown humongous over the past 8 years. What really turns me off to her is her willingness to throw her arms up in the air and say that nothing can be done. I have seen her in debates talk down to and practically yell at her constituents who asked her legitimate questions. She's very fond of telling us there are things we don't know and wouldn't understand.

Danny is approachable, he listens even when you don't agree. Sears is unapproachable and talks down to people. Try this experiment your self: it's clear where Danny's campaign office is, and he's quite often out and about in the neighborhood. The next time you see him, walk right up and say hello introduce your self and discuss your concerns with him. I guarantee he will listen and talk with you, not at you. He may not convince your or change your mind but he will treat you with respect. Then try the same thing with Sears, if you can find her.

Sears' logic for her vote on term limits is also flawed. She claims eight years is too short to see major capital projects through, and she needs more time to finish what she started. Does that mean we will not see any major new development in the area for the next four years ? Since I sincerely doubt she'll be running for a fourth term (even if she is allowed by yet another term limits extension) are we going to have a lame duck council person who has no incentive to work for her district (not that I think she's done much as it is)?

I like Danny because it's clear to me that he wants the job. I believe he really wants to make a difference here. He is not perfect, and there are a few points where I disagree with him. But I have seen Danny galvanize and build communities, I have seen him advocate for causes he believes in (beyond the LGBT community). I see in him someone who is willing to fight and use his position to advocate for change in our community. Unlike Sears who I feel simply phones in her job. Danny has faced and answered every accusation hurled at him, which is more than I can say for those that have done the hurling. Despite any flaws he may or may not have he is the type of person I want representing me in the City Council.
