Don't give up on cable just yet.  In general, cable is much better than DSL in NYC.

However, since you're using Earthlink you actually have two variables to worry about.  What's happening in your case is Earthlink is using Time Warner's cable infrastructure to solve the "last-mile" problem, that is, Time Warner is what carries the traffic from your house to Earthlink's gateway, which is how you access the internet (anyone who has DSL from someone other than Verizon is using Verizon to solve the last-mile problem in the same way).  It's quite possible Earthlink is failing you where Time Warner would not.

In JH you will either be using Time Warner's (TW) cable infrastructure or Verizon's (VZ) telephone infrastructure.  The FTC forces TW and VZ to open this infrastructure up to resellers; you can imagine how well that works.  There are a few boutique ISPs that resell DSL and one that resells cable, some with very good customer service, but in the end you (and they) will be at TW or VZ's mercy.  VZ's infrastructure is much, much worse because they're a gigantic incompetent telco in general and acquired/inherited numerous ancient telcos to get here (NYNEX, Bell Atlantic, etc.)  The NYC telco system is an absolute rats nest and it shows.

Anyway, as a customer of Road Runner (which is Time Warner's branded internet service), I can say the speeds are incredibly fast in JH by NYC standards.  I can download 1MB/sec at times (or 10Mbps).  You may want to give RR a try before taking the plunge into DSL.
