Neighborhood Chat / Re: Diversity Plaza Upgrade?
« on: August 28, 2013, 11:59:56 PM »
Some of us met with Danny Dromm's office today for a very productive meeting.

Here's what we got: First, the plaza is in a very early phase right now. What we see there is not the final product. DOT will be doing community outreach sometime in the fall to come up with a final vision. It is important that everyone who is available be there to make their voices and opinions heard. This will determine the eventual design of the space.

Second, whatever plan is ultimately drafted will not be implemented for at least another year.

Third, there is a chance this process might be sped up if enough community pressure is applied. This would require all of us coming together and petitioning.

Fourth and most importantly, since the final phase won't be here for a while, it is up to us the community to make this space work in the meantime. The city has its own schedule, which is going to be drawn out. For the time being, getting more immediate results will require people stepping up, getting involved, and helping out. SUKHI went out on a limb to get this plaza started. They are very small in capacity and both need and welcome help. So criticizing them will get us nowhere. Offering our assistance as a community will move this thing forward.

Those of us who met with Dromm's office today will be meeting again next week to talk about some next steps. Anyone who is interested in making this space successful is encouraged and more than welcome to join. Feel free to let know what days and times you are available to meet.

Restaurants & Food / Re: New Place at Site of Old U Dzika
« on: July 31, 2013, 12:22:48 AM »
I don't know what Party Pizza is either, but it is a shame that U Dzika didn't make it. That was a high-quality family-run business for the very short time that it operated in this neighborhood. I had the opportunity to speak with the owners several times before they closed. The husband came to this country many years in advance to start working and saving for this business. The wife--who interestingly was a vegetarian herself--oversaw the preparation of fresh Polish food that easily rivaled that of Greenpoint or Ridgewood. Their grownup kids regularly helped out in running the place, and all them would take the time to sit down and talk and eat with their customers. The food and atmosphere were amazing, and it really is too bad that it never attracted the customer base to survive. I would hope that Pizza Party rises to that level of neighborliness, and that more businesses like U Dzika consider this neighborhood a place to establish themselves. I also wonder if anyone else had similar impressions of this place. Comments were very limited when they closed a couple years ago.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Diversity Plaza Upgrade?
« on: July 25, 2013, 07:50:30 PM »
Reverting the plaza to a vehicular thoroughfare will not cause the junkies to spontaneously disappear. I saw many of those same people around that area of the neighborhood before the plaza was installed. On the other hand, planters and tables will also not cause them to disappear. It is often assumed that parks and open space alleviate surrounding social ills. Instead, in many cases, they simply adopt the flavor of their surroundings. That said, better maintained areas do tend to instill a sense of respect among visitors; the opposite it often true for poorly maintained areas. Though this is not a law of nature. This is a wonderful neighborhood, warts and all. So while it is safe to say that this plaza will not be without its nuisances, I believe it will eventually be an excellent space that reflects that overwhelming positive characteristics of the neighborhood. But it will require maintenance and oversight.

It should probably also be noted that the DOT installs these plazas in phases. I am a bit surprised myself that more has not been rolled out this summer. But the recent gravel surface treatment is a sign of progress. All of the city's DOT plazas have some local organization that oversees them. I know SUKHI is a local coalition of business owners, but I am still not sure who they are, or how involved they have been. I am sure Danny Dromm's office could put inquirers in touch with them. In terms of we the community steering the design of the plaza, my guess is that any public outreach has already been done, but I'm not sure. It might be worth contacting DOT to check. As it appears, the plaza is here to stay, though by no means near completion. I am looking forward to this myself. And its important to keep in mind that the DOT will pay for the study, designs, and installation. But it is up to the community to make the space work.
