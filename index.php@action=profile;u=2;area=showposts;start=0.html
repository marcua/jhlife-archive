Neighborhood Chat / Re: Covid mobile testing sites this weekend, Dec 18-19, 2021
« on: December 20, 2021, 09:14:20 AM »
Quote from: itsit on December 20, 2021, 07:20:45 AM
Is there any way to find out how Jackson Heights is doing right now with Covid positives, testing or any other measures? Asking because it seems like our vaxx rate was pretty high along with awareness of Covid spread.

You can go on the NYC.gov site and see the numbers. But things are changing so rapidly that the info you see there might already be outdated. For example, the map right now shows 11372 at 5.37% positivity rate (7 day average, Dec. 10-16). BUT since there's a lag in reporting, I am guessing that number has already gone way up. Also don't forget that currently we have a lot of people using the at-home rapid tests whose results wouldn't be reported at all.
https://www1.nyc.gov/site/doh/covid/covid-19-data.page#maps

I think a more accurate rate to look at would be hospitalizations for Covid, and not including people who test positive when they go to the hospital for another condition. I'm not sure those numbers are available.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Art supplies store
« on: December 15, 2021, 01:28:51 PM »
They already had their first class on Monday night of this week - figure drawing, or at least that's what showed on their IG (I am not certain if they had it or if it got rescheduled). Also they are making room to have gallery space where they can show the work of local artists. It's not really a gallery in the traditional sense but there is wall space to hang artwork -- above the shelving areas where the supplies are displayed. So you'd look up to see the work, which will hang from molding hooks supported by the existing molding.

There is a credit card surcharge of 3% on purchases, so bring cash if it bothers you to pay a surcharge. I understand why they are passing the charge on to the customer. Otherwise the store pays the fee, or else the products have to be priced higher.
