And you would know they are rabid, how???

Foxes, raccooons, possums, skunks, and increasingly coyotes (yes, now in Queens!) are all a natural part of the NYC landscape.  Just leave them alone and you will generally be fine.

Rabies is a problem in NYC, especially among the dense raccoon populations in Central Park.  Rabid animals will exhibit unusual behaviors, such as seeming tame or aggressive.  If the wild animal is approaching you, acting strange, or seems sick, then best to move away quickly and report it.  The NYC Dept of Health has been inoculating raccoons against rabies and monitoring throughout the city.  Jackson Heights is probably of little concern because we don't have nearly as many of these medium-sized carnivores as other parts of the city (esp. Bronx and upper Manhattan).

By all means, if you get bit or scratched then report it and go to a doctor to get the rabies shots.
