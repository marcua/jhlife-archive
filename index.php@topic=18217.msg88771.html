Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Queens Neighborhoods United and Viva la Comida fest
« on: September 10, 2018, 02:56:01 PM »
This year's Viva la Comida food fest, now organized by the 82nd Street Partnership, is scheduled to take place this Saturday, September 15th. Queens Neighborhoods United, the group fighting the battle against the 82nd Street development and Target has put out the following Tweet asking local politicians and political candidates to not attend the festival in protest of the organizer's role in the 82nd Street development plans. I know this has been a popular festival in previous years, so wondering how things will pan out.

The anti-Viva La Comida campaign by Queens Neighborhoods United is also on Instagram. The poster below is a modification of the original promo poster for the festival. It highlights the group's anti-gentrification stance by claiming that the festival attracts Brooklyn gringos, encourages the displacement of neighbors, increases local rents, and aims to put an end to small businesses, ultimately killing the community. It also makes a reference to the death of 82nd Street, Calle Colombia, calling it "the Gap Street".  The artist who created the poster, Instagrammer, yosoyserge, says the poster has been tweaked since this rendering to illustrate how the BID is displacing mom and pop stores.
