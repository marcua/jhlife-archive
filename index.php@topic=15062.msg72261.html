CAFE 75--the local bar you've been craving
« on: July 11, 2016, 11:09:13 AM »
I know there's been a lot of lamenting on this site about Jackson Heights not having a good local bar that's conducive to couples or even women on their own.  Turns out it's been there all along and I've been hanging out there a lot more recently.  Really friendly, welcoming vibe, lots of seating and comfortable booths, laid-back, fun, great music.  My wife and I got there often and she has been there as well with other female pals as well and always had a good time.  On first glance from both outside and when you first walk in it might seem like just another Roosevelt Avenue bar for dudes to ogle waitresses in miniskirts (there's some of that as well at C-75 but it's not imposing in any way), but it truly stands apart from the others.  Try it out one night--I think you might be pleasantly surprised.  And, if you're hungry, drop by either Spicy Tibet or Aroma Brazil for chow--both on the same block and both under-the-radar great.

Obviously, most of these posts are by amateurs.  Being a professional I have to chime in here.  Korean chicken places with craft beer, sports bars with 20 TVs, bowling alleys, pick-up joints, restaurants serving drinks etc are not places one goes to spend a lot of time drinking with friends.  They are places one might go once in a while but not a couple times a week or every week.  If you want to learn what a good bar is you might want to try visiting some of the pubs/bars in Woodside, Sunnyside, Astoria etc.  We do not have any nice old style bars here where locals can hang out/meet friends, where women can go and hang, where there might be a small drinking garden out back, where the old and the young can spend time together, a place that's an extension of your living room.  That is what the traditional NYC bar is/was.  We do not have this here, sadly.  It would be nice and in the future someone might get inspired (and get some) and open one.  There might be a market here for the right pub/bar (or maybe not.)  This is a wonderful neighborhood in many ways but this is one thing we don't have.  Maybe some day.

Okay, okay. I know the response on this recommendation has been various shades of negative and befuddlement.  Fair enough.  But, really, give it a chance or two.  The staff there is really friendly, it's a classic bar vibe--dark, lots of booths, great nooks and crannies in which to get lost--and you can program the jukebox via your phone and create your own soundscape.  My wife has gone there with girlfriends and had a great time and not ever been hassled.  It's not in any way like the other "gentlemen's sports club bars" along Roosevelt or even anything like the Scorpion Bar.   But, hey, if you don't dig it, you don't dig it.
