Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: ideas about keeping co-ops bedbug-free
« on: June 06, 2011, 08:12:15 PM »
The link below is a terrific guide on the Dept of health's website.   I learned ways to make my apartment a bit safer.  There are still far too many people unfamiliar with how to spot and remove bed bugs.  If the different agencies are all saying this summer will be bad regarding bed bugs, then people need to know how to tell if they have them and then what steps to take to quickly rid them. 

I've been educating my Co-op residents for weeks now ever since we discovered them in our building.  Bed bugs are in many buildings in NY, never mind Jackson Heights,  so the best defense after hiring a Pest Control Company is to be educated about them. 

They are disgusting, but they are BUGS!  Not aliens from Mars.  The more aware people are, the quicker they take action.  The quicker the action, the more likely to rid them.   Although like roaches and mice, they'll always be part of the landscape.

http://www.nyc.gov/html/doh/downloads/pdf/vector/bed-bug-guide.pdf

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Will Jackson Heights undergo the same transformation?
« on: March 10, 2011, 09:47:42 PM »
This thread should be joined with the 'Is this site too politically correct??' thread.  

Every thread that had even a slight "whiff" of gentrification on these boards  ALWAYS gets people bickering...   And THAT gets censured by the Mods eventually.    It cracks me up because I love good, heated disagreements sometimes.  Oh well.

In my opinion, I don't think JHs will undergo the changes like some Brooklyn neighborhoods for a reason already mentioned:  JHs has more Co-ops and private houses then rentals.  Hipsters usually are younger -  (just starting out in the work force) - so usually poorer and more likely to look for rentals.

And JHs is now possibly one of the more diverse communities in NY.  JohnPrester, you like researching: what are the demographics here?  So I think it will always have change (what place doesn't) but in the 24 years I've lived here the changes haven't been that dramatic that I would compare JHs to say Williamsburg.  Or Greenpoint for that matter.  

I love living here for the reasons: good and bad.   I wouldn't want to be here if all the reasons were BAD.  AND if all the reasons were GOOD, what would we discuss???
