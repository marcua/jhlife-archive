Maybe we feel more inclined to hold doors during the daytime, as we feel generally more safe-- but as this thread makes clear, there's no reason to assume a pervert is less likely to sneak in at 3 PM than 3 AM. I've lived in my building for over a year and still see new faces almost every day. It's a big building, and we only have a doorman on weekday evenings. So I have to ask the obvious question: how do you deal with the everyday situations of entering and leaving a busy apartment building?
I realize that personal gut feelings play a big role, but still...If you're entering at the same time as someone else, and you get to the door first, are you supposed to close the door on the other person's face? Short of issuing building IDs to tenants, how are we supposed to police ourselves?

Neighborhood Chat / Summer apparel
« on: June 12, 2008, 11:17:11 AM »
This may be a bit of a naive question, but here goes. Since moving to J.H. a year ago I've been impressed by how most everyone seems to tolerate and get along with people from different countries and religions. Certain cultures may stand out from block to block, but overall no one group seems to dominate. Still, I wonder how beneath the surface different people think of each other.
The heat wave had me thinking about this again. Walk down 74th street and you pass women wearing everything from full hijab to miniskirt and halter top. Do any of you know if there have ever been, ummm, tensions about this? I know this is a political can of worms, but I'm really just interested in how individuals deal with clashing standards of modesty.
