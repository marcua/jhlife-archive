Is this a new or used car that you purchased last year? No, you are not over thinking this
because it's very easy to get scammed at a place where you have never been before.

A common scam is that they will tell you that in order to pass state inspection you
will need to fix this and that otherwise you won't get the sticker. Think about it, the repair
shop makes nothing on a state inspection, actually loses money because you have a car hooked
up to a very expensive machine inside a bay at the garage. I had this happen to me once in
Brooklyn after leaving work, I had a "tune up"($450.00)done so I could pass inspection, my fault for waiting until the
next to last day before it expired.

I take my vehicle to Mobil on 69th St/Queens Blvd, have been going there for 8 yrs now, they are honest
with me but who knows how they are with new customers. Sorry but I have never been to those shops
that you listed, but I am sure others on here will direct you to a reputable place, good luck.
