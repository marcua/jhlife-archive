Neighborhood Chat / Re: 73rd Street nightmare

The city has told us that it will no longer enforce honking laws, issue tickets, etc.  Is that really a surprise, given how pervasive honking is, how unenforceable the rules/laws?  Now, Erica, you point to the myriad of problems here -- blocking the box, delivery hours, jaywalking, double parking.  yes, all problems.  And hopefully each issue can be addressed, problems minimized.  But this works best if added to another approach -- just learn to better tolerate such disturbances.  They are -- fundamentally -- an unchangeable thread of the fabric of urban life.  The problems cited are not unique to J.H. They are city wide, they are national, international.  Sure, we may want our neighborhoods to be quiet pedestrian zones, locomotion limited to feet and bicycles.  But that ain't going to happen, so best to try to accept and adjust.  Also, for pure pedestrians, it may be useful to try to imagine what it all this looks like from a driver's point of view.  Car and truck and bus drivers are, and will remain, part of the mix, after all.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Pakistani dining?
« on: December 31, 2012, 02:04:30 AM »
Try "Dera" on Broadway and 72nd.  It could not be more authentic.  Few diners who aren't So. Asian here, and yet it's friendly and embracing to one and all.  A huge lively room, half cafeteria, half table service -- you pic what you want at counters, they bring it to you with utensils.  Food is just great  Try lamp chops, kababs, all forms of chicken.  So many veggie dishes.  All come with nice raw salad.  And get a garlic naan, best in the hood.  You'll think you're in Karachi or Islamabad.  Very, very cheap too.  This is the real deal experience, the opposite of Jackson Diner.  Every out of stater Ive brought here loved it and wanted to return again and again.
