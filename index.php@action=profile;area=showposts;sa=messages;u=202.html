Neighborhood Chat / Re: Go JH! Help support JH businesses (and get some discounts for yourself)
« on: November 15, 2009, 03:26:43 PM »
Thanks Chuckster! 

People should know that the way the program works is that each business owner decides the discount for their store.  In my opinion, many of the offered discounts are quite attractive, others not so. 

In my experience business owners are quite receptive to well-intended and constructive suggestions/advice.  If you have an idea on how a business might improve upon their offered discount than I encourage you to kindly offer that suggestion to the shopkeeper. 

The Community Journal is a 100% volunteer-run local non-profit so we don't have the resources to do all the leg work.  So please help as you can and have some patience as the program grows and evolves!

In addition to now launching the Go JH! program (businesses begin accepting the Go JH! card on Monday (11/16) we also organize the Jackson Heights Film and Food Festival (which started in 2006) and the Clean Streets Program (although most wouldn't know it we have additional street cleaners 8 hours a day, Wednesday through Monday from 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. on 73rd and 74th Streets between 37th Road and 37th Avenue).  I don't mean to change the thread topic so if there are any replies let's please keep it to the Go JH! program.

Some folks who post on this board have been a tremendous help to past and present activities of the Community Journal.  If you would like to volunteer for the Go JH! program (or any of the others) your help would be greatly appreciated!  Feel free to shoot me an e-mail at bpufolkes@yahoo.com.

Bryan

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Go JH! Help support JH businesses (and get some discounts for yourself)
« on: November 14, 2009, 07:20:49 PM »
All feed back is welcome!  The goal is to have the best program possible for the community.

I guess what you consider a "lame" or "great" discount is in the eye of the beholder (or card holder in this instance). 

We're very excited over the launch of this program and hope to help the local Jackson Heights economy, to offer savings to customers especially during difficult times and to encourage folks to explore neighborhood shops.

Someone who has meetings, a large family or likes to freeze bagels may be excited over M & V's offer of 6 bagels free with the purchase of six.  They may also like the free coffee at M & V for cardholders.  Someone who shops regularly at the Jackson Heights Health Food Store may love the Go JH! offer.  Some people may love the 10% off at Lety's, Novo, Plaka, Pilatos, Maram, etc. 

As more businesses sign-up the more valuable the Go JH! program will be.  Currently there are 38 businesses participating.  So please let your favorite businesses know about the program so they can sign-up!

The current list of participating merchants and their discounts is here:  http://communityjournal.org/?page_id=163.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Re: What ever happened to the Jackson Heights Film & Food Festival?
« on: September 12, 2009, 02:44:02 PM »
Ravi,

Thanks for asking! 

Each year, since 2006, the Community Journal hosts the Jackson Heights Film and Food Festival in September.  We planned to screen films (as we have in years past) at the Eagle Theater on 37th Road between 73rd and 74th streets.  There was tremendous delay because of the Bollywood strike and then in early August the theater decided not to renew their lease because they couldn't afford the rent any longer.  We have been in conversations with the 82nd Street Triplex and recently worked out a deal with them.  We will be having a great one-day event on Saturday, October 24, 2009 - from 6:30 onward ... mark your calendars!  More details will be coming out soon!

If you would like to volunteer and get involved e-mail me at bryan@pufolkeslaw.com.  Thanks!

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Today is Opening Night of the 3rd Annual Jackson Heights Film and Food Festival!
« on: September 18, 2008, 07:05:33 AM »
The Film Festival component of the Jackson Heights Film and Food Festival opens tonight, Thursday, September 18th with Chop Shop a timely and poignant feature length film which focuses on life stories at the Willets Point "Iron Triangle" a local place in the middle of a development battle royale.

We also open with a 12 minute short film from Burundi, "Basket Boy", about a ten-year-old named Kivumvu (Kirundi for 'basket') who searches for the origins of his often-mocked name.
 
Avoid waiting on a long line.  Go to www.jhfff.org/buy.html to buy tickets online now.

The film screenings continue Friday through Sunday with award-winning films from all over the world which is reflective of the unmatched diversity in Jackson Heights. Collectively, the films give a platform to emerging artists and offer a window into different cultural perspectives. Beyond the quality of the work, many are selected on the basis of their ability to reflect universal themes while capturing an experience that is unique to a particular group or part of the world. This year's films are rich with geography. Setting itself plays a significant role in the series. From the outskirts of a dusty, industrial city in Pakistan to the heat and humidity of the Colombian jungle-each film captures its location with great authenticity and sensitivity to detail.

Visit www.jhfff.org for All the Details!

Capital One bank is the proud primary sponsor of the festival.

Sincerely,
Bryan Pu-Folkes
