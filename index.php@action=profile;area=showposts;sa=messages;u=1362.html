You are invited to attend the premiere screening of the documentary short Judith: Portrait of a Street Vendor
a film by Zahida Pirani
 
Judith: Portrait of a Street Vendor takes the audience on an intimate journey into the daily life of Judith, a street vendor from Guatemala who lives and works in New York City. Judith exposes the routine obstacles and struggles she and her fellow immigrant vendors face daily on the cityâ€™s streets and reveals her own hopes and aspirations as an immigrant worker, mother, activist and community organizer.
 
Note: Film is in Spanish with English subtitles
 
The screening will be followed by a panel discussion and reception.
 
WHEN: Saturday, December 3, 2011 at 3:00pm
 
WHERE: Queens Museum of Art
Located In Flushing Meadows Corona Park. Click here for directions:
http://www.queensmuseum.org/about/general-information/visitinfodirections
 
DETAILS: This event is free and open to the public. Please help spread the word and forward this invite!
 
For more information and to RSVP, contact Zahida Pirani at: zahida.pirani@gmail.com



About the Panelists:
 
Jennifer Arieta is the Development Manager and organizer with VAMOS Unidos, a social justice organization that organizes immigrant street vendors throughout New York City.
 
Kathleen Dunn teaches at Brooklyn College and is a PhD Candidate in Sociology at the CUNY Graduate Center. Her research focuses on street vendor organizing.
 
Judith Ruano is a member of VAMOS Unidos and is featured in the documentary short Judith: Portrait of a Street Vendor.
 
Zahida Pirani is an independent filmmaker based in Queens, New York.


 


This project is made possible in part by the Queens Council on the Arts with public funding from the New York City Department of Cultural Affairs.

The lack of integration does not have to do with cultures, as beautiful and diverse is, Jackson Heights lacks of places that foster social capital! Neighborhood Chat / Re: Eagle Theater
« on: October 02, 2009, 08:44:33 PM »
i work in a museum and i was one of the cofounders of Cinemarosa. (now i am not affiliated to the program)  Yes the ideal situation for a movie house is to have a 35 mm projector. Serious directors and film buffs will only let you show their films or will only come to see them if you show in 35 (the original format) Queens Theatre in the Park has a 35 mm projector and is willing to donate it, as the cost of using its is really high (union issue and you really need someone that knows the craft). The good news is that more and more filmmakers are moving into digital film. Which is way cheaper.

If you ask me, and this has been one of my dreams, even before the Eagle closed, the whole building (theate and restaurant)  shoudl become a cultural  space  and also a  host for the many activities by all the community based organizations in JH that dont have a space. Can you picture the vibrancy of that place?  Since I posted my previous comment, i started to think that is not such a crazy idea to create a nonprofit and then ask for government, foundation and private support. I did created and run a nonprofit as a side job when Cinemarosa started, it is not difficult. However the hard work is to get $$$ to get the place and run it. and it is not only a matter of money, but also time! which sometimes is more difficult to find that money.

Based on all the comments i think it is worthwhile meet up in person and see what we can do as a group. the Highline Park was started by a group of concerned neighbors. What do you all think?

BTW, i close friend of mine told me that since it was closed the place was taken over by the rats! More reasons to act fast! we dont want an infestation in JH! right?
