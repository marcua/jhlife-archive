 As an immigrant community for decades, Queen's has an enormous soccer base. The US  hasn't figured out how to monetize the sport yet but a number of professional athletes here have become owners of soccer clubs elsewhere. And not for nothing, moms seem to love their boys and girls playing soccer over many other choices now! Outdoors, lots of running, many different positions, frequent substitutions, lots of playfulness, less head injuries. I think the pitch you mention is part of St. Michael's Park and used to also be used for baseball. According to the link you posted, the project has been completed. The mini-pitch at Moser Playground is the only local project mentioned. The local CYO soccer teams have been very successful over the years and there are some men's leagues that have been playing in the schoolyards at IS 145 and Garden School. M Clancy also has had a successful Girls soccer team since they went co-ed, and Lexington School has a nice pitch. It would be nice if these facilities were available for soccer development programs, but insurance and legal considerations may make that unlikely. Nevertheless, soccer has been strong in the neighborhood for as long as I remember.
