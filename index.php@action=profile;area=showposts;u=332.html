Join the Transportation Alternatives Queens Committee on our 25-mile bike ride, beginning at the War Memorial in Astoria Park on Shore Blvd and looping up to and back from Flushing. RSVP to Sarah at fourdozen@gmail.com, space is limited!

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Summer Streets Feeder Rides In Queens
« on: July 30, 2008, 04:38:09 PM »
Transportation Alternatives will be leading feeder rides from a different location in Queens on each of the days of Summer Streets.  You can get more info here.

For those who don't know Summer Streets is a new program from the DoT where they will close Park Ave from the Brooklyn Bridge up to 72nd St to all car traffic and open it up to the community.  This is not a street fair, merchants along Park may be out of the street but you won't see sock vendors or funnel cake vendors (not that I have anything against socks or funnel cake).  Summer Streets takes place 8/9, 8/16 and 8/23 from 7am-1pm.

To sign up for a feeder ride send your full name and the neighborhood of the ride you wish to do to summerstreets@transalt.org or call 212.629.8080.   Riders will receive a special gift when they stop by the T.A. tables at Summer Streets.  Here is the schedule:

Saturday, August 9th
    * Astoria
      Meet up in Astoria Park in front of the World War I Memorial along Shore Boulevard
      Ride begins at 9 am

Saturday, August 16th
    * Sunnyside
      Meet up at Doughboy Park (enter at 52nd Street an 39th Drive) at 8:30 am
      Ride begins at 9 am

Saturday, August 23rd
    * Flushing
      Meet up in Flushing Meadows Park next to the Unisphere at 8 am
      Ride begins at 8:30 am

Riders should bring a a bike (duh), a helmet, water and sunscreen.

You can also check out the 30 sec PSA running on CBS here.

Even if you don't ride you should check out Summer Streets, it's a great experiment about opening up our public space for good times and if people don't come out that's what it'll remain, an experiment.  Plus if you do the feeder ride you'll get to meet me, and that is an experiment in itself.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Re: Tour de Queens, June 8, 2008

Came here to post the link to the video and I see Toddg has beat me to it.  I apologize to the virtual community here at JHL, I don't get over here as often as I would like.

Lapdanson, like LordB said, we capped the ride b/c the TdQ was organized by the all volunteer TA Queens Committee and we didn't want to have a ride that wasn't fun b/c we got more people than we could handle.  In the future I think the Tour de Queens will grow and grow just like the Tour de Brooklyn and Bronx did.  Also the TdQ was initially a hard sell for some people who didn't think there was a lot of demand for a ride like this in Queens.  Needless to say they were wrong.

We did sign up people day of, the heat kept some away so we were able to fill up those spots with need to register folks.  Also we let anyone who volunteered ride the ride regardless of the cap.  So we did try and keep spots available even after pre-registration closed up.

You can always keep on top of the TA Queens Committee by joining our google group.
