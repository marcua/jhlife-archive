Title: Re: Looking for a rental in Jackson Heights
Post by: Shelby2 on July 12, 2011, 08:37:28 PM
I don't know first hand if this would work, but what about walking around the neighborhood and stopping into the rental buildings to see if there is a super around or a phone number for management posted?

Walking around and stopping into the foyers would at least give you a sense of whether the buildings are well cared for.  I can understand not wanting to rent a coop because of the restrictions and the threat of not having your lease renewed, however, I would imagine that a big benefit of renting in a coop is that the building upkeep is going to be generally better than in an average rental building.  So it's just something to watch out for.

There have been posts in the past on this board about Washington Plaza, which is supposed to be a good rental complex.  Maybe you could stop by there too.
