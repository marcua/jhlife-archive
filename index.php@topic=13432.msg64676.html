Just a simple question, does this plan call for the elimination of any street parking?

No.

It calls for:
1. the creation of a bike lane ring connecting the parks in JH, East Elmhurst and N Corona, with protected bike lanes wherever possible;
2. lanes connecting this ring to Astoria Park, Socrates Park and Flushing Meadows.

Based on recent bike lanes added to other neighborhoods, I don't think there was loss in car parking space. Often, the car parking is unaffected (34th ave bike lane, for example). Other times, it's moved to the right of the curb, leaving the bike lane near the sidewalk. And when the street is not wide enough to accommodate a protected lane, the bike lane is designed as a sharrow, which are just drawings on the street reminding drivers that there are bicycle riders on the road.
 
ps. My personal take:
Believe me, I am not a "bike activist." I am not even someone you might call "politically engaged." I do like cars (tho I hate traffic jams).

My personal motivations to be working on this initiative:
- It is a simple solution to be implemented
- It would help mitigate our problem of lack of park space
- So my family can have safe and fast access to green spaces
- We'd reach nearby neighborhoods in half the time that public transit takes

Like I said: I like cars. (Especially when it's cold.)
