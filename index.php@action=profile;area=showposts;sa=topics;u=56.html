A Time of Great Need

The future of over 50 cats is at stake

As we celebrate the start of a new season, For Animals faces a huge challenge. We have lost our shelter space of over 10 years, and must vacate within a few months. Our shelter not only houses adoptable cats and kittens, but a separate area acts as a holding space for our TNR program.

 We are reaching out to our supporters to ask for assistance as we begin the process of looking for another suitable space. This is not an easy task: aside from the fact that rentals are extremely costly, most landlords are unwilling to accommodate animals.

 In an effort to make ourselves more attractive to landlords, we are trying to reduce the number of cats in our shelter. If we can't find a new space, we don't know what will happen to the more than 50 cats and kittens currently in our care so getting them into homes is a top priority. As a way of reducing our population we are offering free adoptions to qualifying homes.
