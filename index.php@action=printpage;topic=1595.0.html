Title: Jackson Heights Halloween Parade
Post by: toddg on September 30, 2008, 12:32:01 PM
The 19th Annual Jackson Heights Halloween Parade will be held on the afternoon of October 31st.  This wonderful event is the second-largest Halloween Parade in NYC, and (I've heard) the city's largest children's parade.  All are welcome to participate.

The Jackson Heights Beautification Group Halloween Parade Committee (http://www.jhbg.org/committees/halloweenParade/parade.html) needs volunteers to help stuff goodie bags for the kids, and to help distribute them for the parade.  To find out how you can help, contact them at 718-565-5344 or info@jhbg.

The parade starts at 5 pm at 89th Street and 37th Avenue.  It proceeds down 37th Avenue to about 76th Street, where goodie bags are distributed.

Here's a report on last year's parade (http://suddenworld.com/2007/10/28/the-little-halloween-that-could-the-18th-annual-children%E2%80%99s-halloween-parade-in-jackson-heights-ny/) from Sudden World.  And here are some photos (http://flickr.com/search/?s=int&q=%22jackson+heights%22+halloween&m=tags) from past years.

(http://farm1.static.flickr.com/119/286608028_c9b1640856_m.jpg)

And if you absolutely insist on checking out that other Halloween parade (you know, the little one in Manhattan (http://www.halloween-nyc.com/index.php)), you may want to consider joining Project Bueller (http://projectbueller.tumblr.com/).
