Neighborhood Chat / Re: Most ethnically diverse area of Queens
« on: October 18, 2010, 01:48:38 PM »
I agree that the subway station is the best place to hear multiple languages at one point, perpaps the Post Office.   This is due to the fact that although very diverse, Jackson Heights is not very homogenized.  The pot has not melted here, as this if so often an entry point for fairly recent immigrants, and by a generation they have moved on.  At times in the past we have had waves of Irish, Russian and Polish, Colombians, etc.  So one block will be very different than the next, but within the block it is not very diverse. Of course each wave left a few permanent residents.

I noted this one night walking home from the Vietnamese Rest. on 74th a little south of Broadway (excellent by the way).  That block was almost all oriental.  Going north only a half block to Roosevelt, it was primarily Mexican, going another half block Indian and related backgrounds, turning right on 37th Ave and going a block got me back to primarily Latino, and turning north on 77th, a mix of yuppies and related folks, with few of the previously mentioned groups.  Had I continued to Northern, it would have been primarily people from South America. 

This is what I love about Jackson Heights.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Our birds of prey.......
« on: February 19, 2010, 07:55:06 AM »
I have yet to see our local raptors, but have enjoyed them most of my life.  I am from Louisiana and every winter we got a lot of hawks migrating through.  I have seen two kills, one in my backyard of a  dove. 

The British School of Falcon in Manchester VT offers a class in falconry, using Harris Hawks.  I took one class and it was great.  I got to fly a hawk and then we went on a woods walk where the hawk follows along thru the trees.  Each person has his own hawk.  It was great.  Price was reasonable, I thought, considering how unusual this is.  There is the Equinox resort that is related to it, which is a fine old resort, but you can stay in a cheaper place, or just make it a day trip.  Driving time about 3 hours.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Jackson Heights Mardi Gras Parade
« on: February 17, 2010, 08:35:12 AM »
The fourth annual Jackson Heights Mardi Gras Parade was held Sat night on 37th Ave.  This year the original Krewe of Half Fast Flocking Flamingos were joined by a new Krewe, the Fatcats, who made a great showing on their first year.  The parade stopped to salute our old friends Expresso 77 and Rudy Volcano, and a very welcome new one, Table Wines, who returned the favor by giving the Krewes beads.  A final stop was at Novo's for beverages to warm up from the cold night.
