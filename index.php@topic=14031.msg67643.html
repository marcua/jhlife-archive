Look for an app called transit. Most buses have GPS now, and that app gives you real time information on where the buses actually are, when the next one will arrive, and the schedule. You can even have it ding you right before your stop - a real help at night, and when you are on an unfamiliar route.

I think a bus to the E and transferring to a downtown 6 at 51st to yet another transfer at grand Central is probably your fastest route. Taking the 7 in to grand Central will probably take longer, but at least you're more likely to get a seat.

rhyde, did you look at the report? The only two areas that the 7 is rated above the rest of the system are cleanliness (as defined by the MTA) and "ability to get a seat" which, if you ask anybody who takes it, is a near impossibility if you're riding between 7:30ish and 9 in the morning. There were any number of mornings that there would be two full trains stopping at 90th that would open the doors, no passengers would discharge and and no one on the platform could get on the "tokyo crowded" cars, not until the third train through would there be enough room to start getting people on. And it's not like these were right behind each other, 5 to 8  minutes between trains. So more and more people keep hitting the platform. After 9 it gets a little better, but trains get even farther apart. Look at the report, even the MTA says the wait times between trains is 2 to 3 times what there schedule says. I switched to the Q49 to E or M train (and then on to the 6) about 3 months ago and have experienced FAR fewer delays.

I lived in Sunnyside for some time, and the 7 train was terrible. Long waits, and when a train finally arrived it was packed and you couldn't get on. It was not uncommon to let 2 or 3 trains go by before you get on. I lived there far too many years before I realized there were two buses that would take me down to 21st and Ely St. where I could hop on to either the E or the F to get me where I wanted to go. It wasn't always easy getting on those trains at the last Queens stop, but the commute always saved me about 10 minutes that way.

And, it looks like with rezoning towers along queens blvd are coming to that area, with at least one building currently being demolished on Greenpoint and 48th St., and another slated to be demolished on 39th place. It's going to get much worse.
