Quote from: racert on October 27, 2008, 04:22:18 PM
Would a new store work? If so what would people like to see?

Few things off the top of my head:

1.) Climate control - the places already in the neighborhood have crummy store temperature control.  It was either on this board or somewhere else where I've read people complaining about buying spoiled wine from the existing shops.  If you're able to maintain the proper temperature, that would be a major PLUS from the start. 
2.) Store layout - A clean, aesthetically pleasing store layout would also be welcome.  One local shop simply lays out their wares in the original cardboard boxes with the prices on a hand-written sign.  Even making the extra effort to print out a sign on a computer would be welcome.
3.) Don't really think anything too esoteric is going to fly in terms of sales.  Stick to the basics, and that should work.  Middle of the road whites and reds.  JH is more mainstream, not a "Sherry Lehmann" market.  Seems like South American wines do well (Malbec) in the neighborhood. 
4.) Make sure you really understand the wants of the local market, rather then listening to people say things like: "Oh, I love Tokaji, you have to get some Tokaji"; "I'd love to find some decent Port wine locally"; "It would be nice to be able to buy Petrus rather than have to go to Morrell", etc. etc.  Those are exaggerations, and relates to #3.  If you focus on the obscure stuff, it's just going to collect dust on the shelf.   A few "left field" selections is good for the adventurous type customer, but don't go overboard.
