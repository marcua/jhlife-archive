Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Quote from: dssjh on February 01, 2012, 07:43:56 PM
i'm still curious to see proof that more foot traffic is bad for business in this neighborhood -- and how many users here regularly drove down that block of 37th road to buy jewelry or pani puri. maybe i am way off base and 99 percent of people in the area drive everywhere, but i doubt it.

You've actually hit one of the major tensions in the neighborhood, in my view.

Very few people in Jackson Heights drive. I could find the census stat, but it's a very small number. The vast majority of residents - especially living south of Northern Boulevard - rely on public transportation.

The issue is that the Indian shopping district, especially over the past 10 years, has grown to depend on non-resident shopping. Many of these shoppers are families who used to live in Jackson Heights (say, in the 80s) but "moved on up" to Richmond Hill, southeast Queens or Nassau County. They drive back to Jackson Heights via the BQE exclusively to load up on supplies, then drive out. Unfortunately, to redesign the neighborhood to cater to these nonresident shoppers would involve blowing up a couple of blocks' worth of apartment buildings to turn them into parking lots.
