Neighborhood Chat / Re: Closing a phone line from AT&T
« on: October 06, 2014, 02:14:58 PM »
Are you referring to a land line or cellphone? I'm not too sure about land lines, but for cell phones I know that you have 3 options:

1. Wait until there is a material change in contract: you are allowed to cancel your contract without an early termination fee if there is a material change in contract (meaning that they changed something in your contract). It can be something as simple as changing a fee or the cost of a service.

2. Switch to a service that pays off your ETF's (like Boost, Virgin or Ting).

3. Use a site like Cellswapper.com or Trademycellular.com to literally trade your contract for someone else's.

If you are switching to save money, phone companies often switch

Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Electrician recommendations - from fuses to circuit breakers
« on: July 03, 2014, 09:28:48 AM »
After I moved in February, I redid all the electrical in my apartment as well as upgraded the electrical panel. I originally just contacted electricians on Yelp but that was a mess. The electrician with the highest rating lied about being insured and ended up wasting my time (and wanted to be paid in cash).

I found a service online called Thumbtack that lets you write in the work you want done and electricians,etc bid on the work. I got a fair number of responses but ended up working with Josh at Riviera Electrical Corp. His pricing was fair, he was down to earth and could start immediately. He finished all the work on time and there were no headaches along the way.

One thing to note with them is that they get every project inspected by the city. A few weeks after they were done we did have o schedule a time for them to come back with the inspector. We saw this as a good thing, but others might now.

Anyway, here is their website: rivieraelectriccorp.com
