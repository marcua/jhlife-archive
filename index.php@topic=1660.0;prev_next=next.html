NJ dealers park cars in boro
By Jeremy Walsh
11/29/2007

The next time you see a car for sale parked on a residential street in Jackson Heights or East Elmhurst, think twice about where it might have come from.

Police from the 115th Precinct said car dealerships in New Jersey have been parking their wares curbside and listing them under phone numbers with 718 area codes.

Out-of-state car dealers are forbidden from selling vehicles in New York. But Officer Edward Sawtell, of the precinct's traffic division, said in the past month police have towed 20 cars marked for sale by six dealers from New Jersey. He said police have convinced four of the six not to come back.

The dealers park the vehicles in areas of high pedestrian traffic. Unfortunately, that often equates to areas of high congestion, such as 82nd Street and Astoria Boulevard, 94th Street and 24th Avenue, 82nd Street and 35th Avenue, Junction Boulevard and 34th Avenue, 108th Street and 35th Avenue and on Northern Boulevard between 80th and 89th streets.

"Junction and 34th Avenue is a parking lot for Delaware [plates]," Sawtell said.

Local residents looking for parking places share the hassles of this arrangement with prospective car buyers. Sawtell said the dealers also advertise their vehicles in New Jersey newspapers. If a person agrees to buy the vehicle, he said, the dealer hands the buyer the keys and tells him the vehicle is in Jackson Heights.

Sawtell said the cars often are not insured and can incur any number of summonses due to parking violations or other issues- - all of which the buyer must pay before he can register the vehicle.

"It's buyer beware," he said. "If you buy a car, check the VIN."

Will Sweeney, co-founder of the anti-congestion civic group Western Jackson Heights Alliance, said he was glad the local precinct knew about the problem.

"It's good to know that they're going one step further and finding out who owns these cars," he said, noting one in four cars he passes during his morning exercise sport out-of-state license plates.

But Sweeney said the cause of the problem is the lack of parking regulation in an area that has one of the lowest proportions of vehicle ownership anywhere in Queens. Census data from the year 2000 showed 38 percent of Jackson Heights residents owned cars, compared to 62 percent boroughwide.

"We should have some sort of way to put a value on-street parking," he said, suggesting an experimental residential parking permit program. "There are people who just come and park their cars. It really becomes just car storage.

Reach reporter Jeremy Walsh by e-mail at jwalsh@timesledger.com or by phone at 718-229-0300, Ext. 154.

The Daily News picks up the story...

'For Sale' vehicles hog street parking

BY JESS WISLOSKI
DAILY NEWS STAFF WRITER

Tuesday, February 12th 2008, 4:00 AM

Cops in a congested Queens neighborhood are cracking down on a shady practice that has aggravated the lack of local on-street parking - cars stranded there by far-flung dealerships.

The intersection of Junction Blvd. and 34th Ave. in East Elmhurst has become a favorite haunt for creative car dealers in recent years.

Dozens of used cars with neon "For Sale" signs in their windows hog street parking at the location - but they don't have license plates or registration or inspection stickers.

"It's problematic," said Will Sweeney, president of the Western Jackson Heights Alliance, a civic group. "We put up with a lot of pollution, and a lot of traffic. Any car that's taking up extra street space lends to more cars driving around looking for a parking spot."

But cops at the 115th Precinct, which issued the most parking tickets of any precinct citywide in 2007, linked the renegade practice to out-of-state dealers.

Those dealers used local salesmen on cell phones as point-people to conduct the lucrative transactions, the Daily News has found.

More than two dozen vehicles have been towed from the site since the practice was discovered in November, according to the NYPD.

Out-of-state scofflaws get free ride
BY NICHOLAS HIRSHON
DAILY NEWS WRITER
Wednesday, October 8th 2008, 1:39 AM

Out-of-state motorists are taking New York City for a ride.

Scofflaws with non-New York license plates are racking up huge parking fines while jamming streets - but the city won't pay the fees to find the deadbeats, the Daily News has learned.

Queens, in particular, pays the price as scores of cars with plates from Connecticut and Pennsylvania hog spaces in Corona and Jackson Heights.

What's worse, the cars often belong to New Yorkers trying to dodge high insurance premiums by registering their cars in another state.

"They're not paying their fair share to use the road that the other people right next door are paying for," said AAA New York spokesman Robert Sinclair.

Cops still ticket the out-of-state cars. But the city won't fork over fees to access driver databases of at least seven states, so it can't track down offenders to collect fines, officials said.
