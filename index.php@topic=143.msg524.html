The answer, as far as I can figure out, is that there is no reliable and complete list of such events in the neighborhood.

As part of my efforts to assemble this site's events calendar (now my official role here), I regularly search the Mayor's Community Affairs Unit's calendar, receive weekly mailings about street closures from NYCDOT, and subscribe to calendar updates from CB3.  Today's event never appeared in any of those places.  Neither did the Vaisakhi Celebration on April 13th (I only found out about that one because I saw a poster). 

My only guess as to how these large events happen without approval from City Hall, NYCDOT, or the Community Board, is that they are probably being approved by the 115th Precinct, which then doesn't bother to notify anybody else.

Basically, you should assume that there is a parade or event just about every Sunday from June, which kicks off gay pride, through October, when the Diwali events close things off.  And of course, then there is the Halloween parade.  This is a festive neighborhood.  The "no parking on Sunday" signs are a clue, and you'll see the blue police horses going up.  If you try and fight the spirit, you'll find yourself very frustrated.  Go with the flow... and learn to celebrate holidays you did not know even existed.  I think the festivities help define this amazingly diverse neighborhood.

But I agree... it would be nice to have a list of what is happening when.

All parades, street fairs, and other street events require that a permit application be filed with Community Board 3.

From there it is sent to the Mayor's Street Permit Office, as well as all involved City Agencies, including the DOT, Sanitation, NYPD, FDNY, and a few others.

The final determination on permiting is made by the Mayor's Office after consultation with the agencies.
