Quote from: theplanesland on November 12, 2012, 11:23:59 AM
I just wanted to weigh in as someone who never goes to Brooklyn.

First, I'm surprised nobody mentioned Legends. It's our neighborhood's best down-to-earth pub, with fine beer, a solid crowd and absolutely spectacular barbecue being sold in the back.

As for "restaurants that aren't Latin or Indian," we're walking distance from Sripraphai, the best Thai in the city, along with having a pretty decent selection of Thai that deliver here including Arunee, Spicy Shallot and Ploy. "Latin" is also a big tent as it includes the fancy Mexican at Coyote, the fancy Peruvian at Pio Pio, the down-to-earth Mexican at Coatzingo, and the Argentinian steakhouse La Portena, which don't serve any of the same things. Let's not forget Cannelle in the shopping center on 31st Avenue for pastries and Plaka, a pretty good Greek restaurant up there which delivers to most of our neighborhood.

As Jackson Heightsers we tend to go to Astoria and Flushing a lot. In Flushing, it's a lot of fun to eat in the food courts, spend a warm day at the Botanical Garden and shop at Target (hey, OK?) We also go to the science museum and Nixtamal in Corona with our daughter. In Astoria, there's the movie theater and a lot of young unmarried hipster stuff I'm incapable of perceiving any more.

For Thai, my favorite is Ayada on Woodside Ave.  http://www.yelp.com/biz/ayada-thai-elmhurst I can walk there in about 15 minutes.  Depending on where you live in JH, Sripraphai might be about the same distance.  I also like La Cabana for Argentinian http://www.yelp.com/biz/la-cabana-argentinan-steakhouse-jackson-heights

Would like to check out the Queens Botanical Garden sometime!

Lumping together all spanish speaking foods is a mistake. Mexican vs uruguayan is quite a difference. This area has S. America and Asia really well covered. It might not be able to be beat.

There isn't much European from actual Europeans though. That's true. And there isn't an art house, or great movie theater around, or rock venue. And there isn't really a place to have a conversation and meet a nice girl or boy along with a craft beer. There isn't much Middle Eastern food either. I don't believe it to be a single person's haven, but maybe I'm wrong. I think it's an amazing place for a couple who likes cultures and different people and reasonably creative, also for raising kids.

You choose what you want next door, and what you want 30 minutes away. I don't see a big change happening with JH b/c Astoria and LIC are undergoing the brunt of all yuppyization. If anything, less people will be able to communicate with each other b/c of dialects and language, but that's fine by me.
