Thank you for your message, and I understand that most investors are not familiar with alternative investments, and there are many misconceptions about them. I am not sure how you define "average investor", but in order to be able to invest into almost all alternative investments, you need to qualify as an "accredited investor". Having said that and to be clear, alternative investments are not for everyone! However, there has been a major shift in the alternative investment industry bringing the same offerings Family Offices invest in to accredited investors for much lower $ minimums. Incorporating sound alternative investments into investment portfolios is still something new to both accredited investors and even a majority of financial advisors, as many wire house firms still only offer these type of opportunities and portfolios to ultra high net worth investors. If you are interested to learn more about alternative investments and how to properly incorporate them into portfolios, I would be happy to meet and shed light upon your mentioned concerns about them. How diversified are your Investment Accounts?

Do you trust your Investment Accounts going into Retirement?


As a CERTIFIED FINANCIAL PLANNER™ professional with over 19 years of experience, I specialize in working with clients to properly diversify their investment and retirement accounts.

I see too many investors still having 100% of their money in traditional mutual funds, bond funds or even individual stocks. With such a structure, both growing your money and protecting it from downside risk is 100% dependent on good stock and/or bond market performance.  Such consistent good performance simply cannot be relied on year after year. In addition, you may also be missing a lot of other important pieces of the investment puzzle that tend not to be correlated  with the US or global stock markets, and that can be helpful in limiting losses.

Depending on your individual financial profile and risk tolerance , as well as your overall portfolio structure, such missing pieces may be alternative investments in Private Equity, Non-traded Real Estate Investment Trusts (non-traded REITS), Preferred REITS, Bond alternatives, CLOs, BDCs, Managed Futures, Commodities and Exchange Traded funds, etc..
