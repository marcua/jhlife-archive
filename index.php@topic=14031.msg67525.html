My wife and I will be moving into the neighborhood soon. I'll be using the 7 train from 82nd to commute into Manhattan daily. A few friends in LIC have complained about 7 train reliability. Frankly, it's ok. I take the 7 from 90th Street to L.I.C. everyday. My company allows staggered shifts, so I leave my house at 6:20 and go to the 2nd to last car so I always get a seat. Even at that time, the middle cars are standing room only. I leave my office at 4, and it's pretty crowded, especially with high school kids, who can be annoying.
I would say the 7 runs fairly regularly and on time during the week. Every once in a while, something happens and the train is severely delayed, crowded, or not running at all. I would guess this happens about once every 2 months. It happens last week and I ended up taking a cab to work. Again, it doesn't happen that often.
I can't imagine any of the other lines are that much better or worse than the 7. I know the 7 is actually rated fairly high in user polls.

I suppose it really depends where you are in JH and where you are going in Manhattan. I lived near 80th St. & 37th Avenue for a spell, and in good weather I walked down to 74th St. to catch either the F or E, both of which took me close to my office at the time. No seats in the morning rush, but faster than the 7. 74th/Roosevelt is a big transfer point in the system, so I think things are generally better for 7 train commuters in JH than they are in Sunnyside, where every train during the morning rush is packed to the gills when it arrives. The Q49, Q33 & Q32 all go down to 74th & Roosevelt.

The big issue with the 7 is that there are frequent weekend shutdowns.

Re: 7 train service + Q49

The Q49 is quite erratic, but the MTA Bus Time app (or webpage) is quite accurate if you use it to find out where the buses are at any point in time. On a rainy day I look at it before I leave my apartment so that I don't have to wait long.

BTW, if you want the E train, you could also try the Q32/Q33 which stop at 82nd and 35th on their way to the subway hub. (They go up 81st and 83rd respectively on their way back.)

The Q49 buses seem to disappear from the app when they get to the subway hub. So it's not great for finding out if you will be able to catch a bus to come back home. But the Q32 buses do not. I never take the Q33, so I don't know if those show up or not.
