Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

The survey is heavily slanted towards continuing the Open Streets program, but I guess we already know that seems like a done deal. I did manage to get into the comments my feeling that I'm actually safer walking on the sidewalk than I am in the Open Street, as I feel like I constantly have to watch my back for speeding bikes, scooters and e-bikes.

I forgot to mention in my comments that I feel that making drivers get out to move barriers to enter their block (say, if they live on that block or have a garage entrance on that block) is unfair. This is especially true for elderly or disabled drivers. Even though I neglected to put the comments in the survey, I did send an email to the DOT about a month ago expressing that sentiment.

During the worst of the pandemic shut down days, I did enjoy some of those walking experiences when I had nowhere to go except to walk around the block, but I think perhaps there weren't as many crazed two-wheeled vehicles in those days. When Covid really does finally wind down, I don't think I will need or use the Open Street at all. I know others hold different opinions.

If they are going to keep the Open Streets program, they really need to address some major issues. IMHO of course.
