I just moved to Jackson Heights two months ago with my boyfriend.  We are 24 and 22.  I guess we fit into the under 30 group.

Having lived in more than one terrible neighborhood in Brooklyn because it was all I could afford, I can seriously say that this is such a better place than many neighborhoods currently attracting twenty-somethings.  For the same price as a tiny apartment in a slum building in Brooklyn, I can instead live in the center of things, on a beautiful tree-lined street, steps from restaurants and stores.  And I don't have to worry about being mugged every time I come home from work.  I can't imagine why more twenty-somethings don't live here... (actually, probably it's because there is no decent bar...)  I'm pretty sure the twenty-something population will only grow in the next few years as more and more are priced out of Brooklyn and Astoria, and realize that Jackson Heights has more to offer, in any case.
