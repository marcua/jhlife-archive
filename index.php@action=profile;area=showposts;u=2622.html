Community Groups, Activities & Events / In Memorium ~ Rafael Hernandez ~ 9/11 Hero ~ Jackson Heights Resident
« on: September 29, 2011, 07:59:19 AM »
It is with regret that Queens Pride House announces the death of Rafael Hernandez, co-coordinator of our Borders of Hope 9/11 workers group. On Sunday, Sept. 25, Rafael died of respiratory failure due to complications from exposure to toxic dust at Ground Zero; he was only 49 years old.

Rafael Hernandez was a firefighter in his native Mexico before moving to New York. When the World Trade Center was attacked in 2001, Hernandez rushed into the Twin Towers to help evacuate workers before they collapsed and remained for months helping with removing human remains and clean-up; as a result, he developed a life-threatening respiratory condition.

"The 9/11 community has lost a hero, friend and community leader," said Pauline Park, president of the board of directors of Queens Pride House. "Rafael was an important part of the Queens Pride House family and he will be missed by everyone here," Park added.

Hernandez provided his help to Latino clean-up workers by organizing workshops, assisting them with applications, providing interpretation during attorney visits. "Rafael was an advocate for compensation and for the Zadroga Act, which became law earlier this year," said Rosa Maria Bramble Weed, director of the Borders of Hope (Fronteras de Esperanza) program at Queens Pride House, which provides support groups and counseling to Latino workers who did clean-up work at Ground Zero.

"Rafael would help his compaÃ±eros with medical appointments, and he facilitated our Saturday workshops, giving more than 40 workers ongoing updates on workers compensation issues and the status of the Zadroga bill while it was pending," added Bramble Weed. "Rafael leaves behind him a legacy of courage and hope that is an inspiration to everyone."

Rafael Hernandez was the sole provider for his three surviving teenaged children -- Sharon (18), Aurora (17) and Nicolas Rafael (16) -- who live in Mexico; Queens Pride House is setting up a fund to help support them.
