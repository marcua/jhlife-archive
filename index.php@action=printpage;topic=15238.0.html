Title: Re: What the heck is happening with the Bruson Building?
Post by: CaptainFlannel on September 08, 2016, 08:08:05 AM
To be successful, a business does not need to be affordable to *everyone* in a neighborhood. It needs to be affordable and valueable to enough people to make a profit. The equation on whether or not a full service gym would have enough demand in this neighborhood to make a profit involves a lot of factors, and I suspect the rent the business would have to pay is the biggest factor

JH is a neighborhood where one-bedroom coops in a post war building are going for $250K+. It's not a poor neighborhood.

Full service gyms aren't nearly as expensive as people think they are. Crunch in Greenpoint costs about $20/month plus annual fee on a month-to-month plan, giving members access to other "Essential" locations (which I think means the non-fancy pants locations), classes, online nutrition, tanning, hydro-massage services, etc. NYSC offers month-to-month plans for a single location for $29/month plus an annual fee. I'm a former NYSC member Sunnyside location, and they ran promotions frequently where you could lower that monthly rate. 

I'd gladly pay $20-30 dollars a month for a gym with towel service and that offers this kind of class schedule since I'm the type who is way more likely to go to the gym if I have an actual instructor led class that starts at a specific time. https://www.crunch.com/content/pdfs/Greenpoint_Fall_0906161236.pdf (https://www.crunch.com/content/pdfs/Greenpoint_Fall_0906161236.pdf)
