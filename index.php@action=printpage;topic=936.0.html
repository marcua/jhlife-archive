Title: 78th Street Play Street - 2008
Post by: toddg on May 23, 2008, 11:08:55 AM
Over the past couple of months, JH Green (http://www.jhgreen.org/), Friends of Travers Park (http://www.jhbg.org/committees/traversPark/park.html), the Western Jackson Heights Alliance (http://www.wjha.org/) and Transportation Alternatives (http://www.transalt.org/) have been working hard to open the block of 78th Street next to Travers Park as a "play street" on Sundays during the summer and fall of 2008.  Their vision is that the street could be used for a wide variety of active and passive recreational activities, to help relieve the extremely overcrowded conditions in Travers Park itself.  Some of the space may also be used to allow an expansion of the Greenmarket to allow participation of a wider variety of vendors.

A brochure describing the initiative can be downloaded here: http://www.jhgreen.org/proposal/78thSt.pdf (large file - 8 MB).

The 78th Street Summer Sundays Project has already secured the endorsement of many key stakeholders, and the organizers are working closely with the city and elected officials to bring the plan to fruition.  The CB3 Transportation Committee will be meeting to discuss the proposal at 7:30 pm on Thursday evening, 5/29 (at 82-11 37th Avenue, Suite 606).

If you're interested, there will be plenty of ways to get involved.  Once the proposal hopefully wins final approval, the organizers will be working to provide a wide variety of innovative programming in order to make maximum use of this new community resource.  This will require ideas and volunteers, and of course people to come hang out and have fun.  If you'd like to get involved, please contact the organizations above.

Got an idea?  Let us know here!

As Will announced (http://www.jacksonheightslife.com/community/index.php?topic=1243.0), this Sunday will mark the beginning of the 78th Street Summer Sundays Project.

Here are some key things to know:

  • There is no parking permitted on 78th Street between 34th Ave and Northern Blvd. on Sundays between 8 am and 8 pm from now through November.  Please help get the word out about this!

  • This street is yours to use.  There will be some organized activities, such as the childrens' bicycle workshop scheduled for this Sunday.  But mostly the street will be available for people do use as they wish.  It will probably take some time before people figure out the best ways to use this space.  But if you have an idea don't be shy about trying it out.

  • Volunteers are needed.  We need people to help clean up the street at the beginning and end of the day, help organize various activities and events, get the word out, etc.   This week, we need clean up volunteers at 6 am and at 7:30 pm (just show up if you want to help!).  To find out more about how to get involved, contact 78thplaystreet@gmail.com.

  • Sponsors are welcome.  We'd like to do some things that require funding (e.g. purchase portable play equipment, rent seating, hold special events, etc.).  Local businesses with an interest in helping to sponsor the play street are encouraged to contact 78thplaystreet@gmail.com.


So bring book and a lawn chair, bring a picnic, bring a harmonica, bring a lawn flamingo, bring a pal, and come hang out to make our newest "park" a success.

NatLopez and spanishfish --  Getting the Play Street set up the first time was a long process fraught with uncertainty.  It was hard to be completely sure it was going to happen until it actually happened.  Until the last minute, the necessary approvals weren't even in place to put up signage.  Now that all approvals are in place, outreach will begin in earnest to invite the community to participate.  And we'll try to come up with some attractive durable signage like the Summer Sundays signs in Travers Park.

Title: Re: The Future of the 78th Street Play Street
Post by: toddg on November 17, 2008, 11:52:12 PM
The Play Street is simply the greatest thing since babies and cute puppies.

That is... much like babies and cute puppies, it provides a means for complete strangers to begin talking with each other.  I have spent countless days with my kids in the playground, without ever striking up a conversation with a stranger.  But on the Play Street, I have met many people.  The conversations always start out about the same ("isn't this great?"), but then evolve in different directions from there.  It has truly become the town square our community has lacked.

Unfortunately, weekends are quite busy for my family, and we haven't made it to the play street for at least six weeks.  I volunteered a few times at the beginning of the season, but haven't been able to make the ongoing commitment I hoped to make.  Which brings me to my recommendation.  I think the Play Street has demonstrated its feasibility and value, and should be allowed to operate without ongoing volunteer supervision.  Beyond set up and clean up, I'd rather see our volunteer energies poured into a few, larger events on the Street (not all necessarily just for kids.... such as a neighborhood cookout, or an occasional stickball game).  To make this work, we would need more vocal support from our local elected officials.  A high-profile champion could go a long way toward facilitating the cooperation we need from NYPD and DOT.
