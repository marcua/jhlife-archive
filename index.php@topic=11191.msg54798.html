Pedestrian Safety Town Hall
« on: February 03, 2014, 11:07:47 PM »
Please join Congressman Crowley, Assemblymember Moya, Council Members, and Make Queens Safer for a Pedestrian SafetyTown Hall this Sunday from 4-6PM.

 It will be at the Renaissance School.

We have invited Reps from the 115 Precinct, the 108, DOT, DOE, Transportation Alternatives, and hopefully some PTA's or School Principals and hope that a series of questions and answers will help our community figure out how to Make Queens Safer.



Our group, MQS has a focus on education. We don't understand why the Dept. of Ed wasn't included in the Mayor's Plan for Vision Zero. School safety is important, and our kids' first steps to learning, should be safe first steps to school.



Beyond that, there are issues of street re-design, safe routes, slow zones and the ever important need to educate both drivers and pedestrians about how to practice street safety.



If you have questions, or even better, if you have answers, please join us.

Hagsrus, A year back, I was at the Home Depot light when an old woman got decked by a car. Head injury and bleeding. Waited for EMS with her, and a bunch of other concerned folks. Terrifying. And on 37th and 92nd, a mom and her two kids jaywalked around a double parked car only to get smashed into a black car driving across the double line to get by...they all went to Elmhurst that day. A cyclist on 85th and Roosevelt got knocked out of her shoes, and she may have even been permanently injured or died, I didn't stay as I had my tot with me. These are the injuries that aren't taken into account in most statistics...the little 7 year old rammed by a car at the bus stop on Northern and 48th? She has a scull fracture, and a long, slow recovery ahead of her...springtime in rehab relearning things.

So many near misses- and people don't get the fact that it's luck alone, when you don't take street safety and street redesign seriously. Now, I've resorted to yelling at drivers and pedestrians. Well, friendly yelling. Texting, blocking an intersection...creeping your car up while you look for directions? No! Trying to outrun the Q47 as it breezes up 35th? Crazy!
