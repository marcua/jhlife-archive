Chuckster,

Their reading about all this here raises the stakes, I think. Not because seeing the questions in advance will harm the process (though that may be the case in some kinds of fora or those seeking particular results) but because they can watch this unfold and simply stand idly by. The JHL crowd risks appearing weak, in my estimation, and while members of this forum may be involved otherwise (as I am), we risk losing the action-oriented aspect of this social network. Pouvoir, or potencia.

Personally, I like your question and think that it demonstrates how having time to think it over, and develop a response, may not be such a bad thing. I, personally, am not trying to trip anyone up by surprising them--and I think that we probably need to do all we can to make it fair and even. Certainly, they all have an answer to your question--I think that it's a great question since it will allow each to say what they want. You are right, I think, in that the way they answer, as well as temperament, how it varies, and our reception of it, is what the real story will be.  I could predict what some of the answers will be like, as we've largely heard it all before. However, I think that having it in writing will allow us to engage with it meaningfully and also to "stick to the facts" of what each candidate proposes. Too often in political rhetoric words, meanings, and ideas at their biggest become "plastic" in that they can come to mean a variety of things, and the genius of spin is being able to ride (when not dictate) the cheesy ambiguity it invokes. Remember the "meaning of is?" not to mention the legion of Bush policies that read like a middle school book report on a glossary of Orwellian new speak. The ability to parse what is said and connect it to reality -- or not -- is our job as members of the community.

I think it's important that this forum, as a kind of fifth estate, be used to hold elected officials, and those running for office, accountable to us here--just as constituencies organized by other means (tenants or homeowners, business owners, residents, as well as those organized by profession, identity, etc.) are able to leverage their status in the community to get a response, not to mention whatever dispensation they are able to garner. I don't think that any of the candidates would dare ignore any of the major constituent groups in the neighborhood, and I think that we should begin the process of activating our, ahem, potencia, err, our pouvoir, to be less anglo saxon about it...

Here is another question, and I'll just start rewriting the questions as summaries of what's been discussed here.   Some issues... What are candidates going to about:

1. Trash in the street/neighbhorhood.  Obviously, this is just a list, but should they be more question-oriented?
2. Traffic, especially congestion, noise, honking (this is actually a whole forum, not a question but it seems like the Transportation Study already underway will involve some integrated solutions and possible compromises.  Here is something:  What compromises does candidate-X feel that various stakeholders should be willing to make that the candidate-X is willing to propose.
3. Hate-crime and speech. This is an ongoing problem, as has been noted with the violent attacks on Leslie Mora, as well as the Sikh man some months ago.
4. ? and so on... this is just a very quick list.  If any are worth keeping, please modify by restating them in some manner...  Obviously,  we need more than just this so we can drop some, combine and/or split others...

Your thoughts?

Neighborhood Chat / 25th Council District--Jackson Heights Life Candidate Forum
« on: August 06, 2009, 08:54:58 AM »
I've been following the race for the City Council seat currently occupied by Helen Sears since late last year.

Currently, the candidates in the Democratic Party Primary election are Danny Dromm, Stanley Kalathara, and Helen Sears, the incumbent.

I hope we can get some conversation going about the merits of each candidate and who should represent Jackson Heights and the 25th Council District. Since the Primary is on September 15, it seems like the chances for meaningful dialogue are fast disappearing and Jackson Heights Life is the best place for a web-based forum. So, I'd like to propose that we use the Jackson Heights Life forum, and this thread in particular, to work-out some community-based questions for the candidates. After generating a list of questions, we could pick the best and refine them for submission to each camp--who would be invited to respond directly here or through an intermediary of their choice.

There is often ad hominem attack and "red herrings" where there might be basic information and reasoned debate. Before we can debate, however, I think we need some information from the candidates.

There have been a lot of issues raised on Jackson Heights Life that are, in effect, campaign issues. These include but are not limited to: trash, the vendor question, parks/greenspace, safety/crime, transportation/traffic, schools, health care, and the development and sustainability of local business opportunities, among others. Sometimes these issues are raised here but the corresponding sense of it as an election-cycle issue is not well developed and the matter is dropped just when it might  become productive to "do something" about it--Jeffsayyes's photography of the trash problem is a good example of what we might do as a start. It may be that that's the nature of the "web forum" as opposed to a more representative-journalistic function, in which members of the community do the developmental research and report back to the community in general (it's this model that I'll propose for JH at a later date).

We might post a series of questions here, and then, either by consensus or committee, act to refine the questions and pose them in a neutral/non-partisan way to each candidate, whether in an "interview" format or in written form. These interviews could be typed verbatim or presented uncut in audio or video. It might be that each camp would rather respond to written prompts, and in many ways that's easiest and gives them control over the message.

Please help by brainstorming some questions for the candidates.  What questions do you have for them? If we have more than a few, we can refine/narrow them later, and I'm happy to meet in person with other interested folks if there is interest.
