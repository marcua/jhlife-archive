Jim Leff has left the building. And sawed off his extra taco-clutching digits. And is generally recovering from his eight year Chowhound odyssey and from a horrendous ten week health-busting assignment which his (at the time) corporate overlords forced on him, causing him to gain 30 pounds, develop high blood pressure, high blood sugar, high cholesterol, and this repulsive, billowing ass (NSFW) (as thoughtfully captured by a NY Times photographer and splayed all over the front page of the food section, accompanying an article written by the reporter described here) all subsequently reversed via a long period of organic healthful foods, 90 minutes of daily yoga plus running and weightlifting, and hardly any chowhounding at all....writing books, playing music, updating the Slog, and feeling good in an undisclosed location where air's fresh and culture's non-existent.

I do need to get back to Jackson Heights soon, however, to update my street food guide  which an absurd number of people continue to visit, to my horror and dismay, in spite of its static decrepitude.

But what's to be "enigmatic"? Back when I was living and hanging out in JH, should I have been, like, running up and down the streets screaming en masse to strangers about how I'm, like, the founder of Chowhound? To borrow from Douglas Adams, I'm just zis guy, you know...

yay, jim leff has reared his head like putin from russia. you are an enigma because all the "photos" i've ever seen of you show you from the back or wearing the chowhound mask, sort of like wilson from home improvement, if i may be allowed to make such a lousy analogy. anyway, it sounds like you are very far from nyc these days. i hope you are still hounding out good local spots wherever you are and posting them, perhaps anonymously, for all to benefit from.

on a really unrelated note, i am more than halfway through the omnivore's dilemma, which has been a fascinating read. i will no longer view food in exactly the same light ever again. while shopping at whole foods yesterday, i was overcome with a feeling of guilt for buying "industrial organic" products. but it's not like it's realistic for city dwellers like me (or the rest of you on this board) to purchase produce only from farmers' markets or a farm share all the time. i realize my own limitations (laziness, sometimes frugality, other times frivolity) and do the best that i can in terms of making conscious decisions about what i choose to buy and fuel myself with. as a vegetarian, i (believe i) am already doing more than the average person to combat a multitude of environmental and agricultural problems.
