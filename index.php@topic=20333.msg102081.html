Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Back to the survey, agree with comment that the survey was poorly designed and definitely seemed skewed to only usage of Open Street vs. concerns for the greater quality of life impacted both on 34th Avenue and neighboring streets.  I completed it as best as possible to address my concerns and usage.  Recent day time walks during the week on 34th Avenue, there has not been that many people, I did see a vendor at 82nd and some gentleman with bags full of cans of beer--if I lived on that street a place to congregate and get drunk.  There was plenty space to walk ON the sidewalk and at the same time Travers Park was empty.  With all the attention to Open Streets, would be great if our community leaders and DOT focused on cleaning up the streets, monitoring traffic congestion and noise, and making JH more livable for all.
