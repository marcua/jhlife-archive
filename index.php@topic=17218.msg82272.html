Ai Weiwei: Good Fences Make Good Neighbors
« on: November 03, 2017, 11:23:10 PM »
Through February, Jackson Heights is hosting part of  Good Fences Make Good Neighbors, a citywide outdoor exhibition by Ai Weiwei "responding to the global refugee crisis and the political and social forces that divide us from each other."  Look up on lampposts on 37th Avenue, 74th Street, and 37th Rd for six banners featuring the likenesses of Anne Frank, Elie Wiesel, three individuals Weiwei recently photographed in refugee camps, and a 1910 immigrant through Ellis Island.



Corona is also hosting part of the exhibit - a series in the form of bus shelter advertisements on Corona Ave., 108th Street, and 99th Street.   See the interactive map for all installations across the city.
