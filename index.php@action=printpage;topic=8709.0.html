Title: Re: El Rico Tinto
Post by: rosie on August 09, 2013, 03:03:15 PM
I've been there twice now, and have been very impressed. The staff is super friendly, and the space is clean, bright, and cheery.

I stopped in last night on my way home from work to grab some empanadas to go. I tried the spinach, and they were very good! And Missmarty, to answer your question, they didn't reheat them. They were stored in a small box (sort of like a toaster oven? Not sure what it's called) that kept them at a steady, warm temperature.

Today hubby and I went in for lunch. You order at the counter and then they bring the food out to you, unless you order to go. I tried the veggie burrito, and he got the arepas con chorizo. Both were very good. They also have a pretty extensive juice menu, so we got the pineapple and the passionfruit (marancuya). Refreshing!

I think El Rico Tinto could be great for the neighborhood. I hope they do well!

Title: Re: El Rico Tinto
Post by: cultartist on April 25, 2014, 05:38:33 PM
Stopped by Rico Tinto today for breakfast.  They've long since regained their A rating and I truly believe this is one of the best restaurants in the neighborhood.  It gets most of its attention for its bakery items and coffee and empanadas--all great, by the way--but its shining stars are the items from the full menu.  Try the fish tacos.  Incredible.  Or the guacamole.  Fantastic.  Or, as I did today, the huevos rancheros.  Amazing.  Great ingredients, beautifully prepared and presented, tasty and even somewhat lighter than other Mexican breakfasts in the area.  I've been going on and on about this place since they opened but I find that they're better and better with each visit.  Along with Phayul, Ayada, Cositas Ricas and La Fusta, I think this is the best spot around.  Also, nice to see Rico Tinto and Arunee paying attention to their decor.   Seems to be paying off in both cases.
