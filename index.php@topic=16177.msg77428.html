Basement storage rooms
« on: March 21, 2017, 11:31:30 AM »
Hi all, does anyone know the logistics behind adding additional storage units to an existing coop basement storage room? I'm sure the specifics vary based on the actual vendor, but I'm curious if anyone has dealt with this recently, and has any advice, or insight? Recently moved to the neighborhood, and had hoped to take advantage of the storage units, only to find out they are all in use already. Not surprising, since storage is so tough in small NYC apartments. My question is- since there aren't enough units for all residents to have one, and since the basement is so huge, why not just add more? Thanks!

Re: Basement storage rooms
« Reply #1 on: March 21, 2017, 12:30:31 PM »
First, establish the existing situation.  Goto the basement and measure out a detailed layout.  Keep in mind things like emergency egress, fire codes and maintenance access to water pipes, electrical work and the boiler (these might be some of the reasons why it hasn't been done).  Then determine the number of apartments in the co-op, the number of storage units, the wait list, the size of the storage units and the amount of money being charged for their use.  Note the number of units which are barely being used or seem like they haven't been touched in ages.  Determine how they are being managed (if it self managed or managed by a company).

Second, compare your situation with other co-ops in the area.  See their fee structures and how they deal with demand.  When we were investigating bicycle storage, I took every opportunity to talk to shareholders at other co-ops (including this forum!) about how they dealt with bike storage.  In many cases, mine included, a free storage unit is assigned to each apartment, but there are plenty of others in JH which having waiting lists and fees.

Third, list the available actions.  Determine how many storage units can be built.  Get quotes from storage unit companies for all available scenarios (simple build, leasing, build/management schemes).  Weight that against self built ones.  Do a cost/revenue analysis.  Be creative with your list of available actions:  Perhaps a bike storage area would lessen the demand for storage units.  Maybe the storage fees are so low that people are careless about them and would move out should they be modernized.

Finally, sniff out a sympathetic shareholder.  See if it has been hashed out in the past (which it likely has) and see if your action list can avoid any pitfalls.  From there you either take it to an active board member or co-op meeting.  You major allies are going to be folks who are also on that waiting list as well as people who might be selling in the near future.

It's alot of work and it's much easier to just raise your hand at a co-op meeting, but it is important to you, it is best to be prepared, especially if it has already been addressed in the co-op.  Sometimes all it takes is someone to show some good initiative to get the ball rolling.  Good luck!!

My coop had this issue for years. They cleared out the storage room for 2-3 weeks, refurbished it and put metal storage bins which are probably 4'x4'x'4' each stacked two high, ONE for each apartment and numbered. No fighting over space anymore.
