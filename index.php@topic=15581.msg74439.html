This city park’s deadly berries nearly killed a 2 year-old-girl
« on: October 30, 2016, 09:34:47 AM »

http://nypost.com/2016/10/30/this-city-parks-deadly-berries-nearly-killed-a-2-year-old-girl/

It is frightening to know that children are exposed to poisonous, innocuous looking, berries when playing in our City parks.  I am surprised that someone in City Hall thought that it would be a good idea to grow poisonous shrubs in an area where toddlers, who are known to put objects in their mouths, are brought to play and frolic.  It is unbelievable that anyone in government would take the position that it is too much work or a hassle to remove this condition from all play areas specifically designed for toddlers and infants.  The nonchalant attitude of our Civil Servants who have decision-making power/authority and can eliminate this condition is appalling.  I wonder how they would feel if it were their child who ate a poisonous berry?  Would they still think it is not worth addressing?  The City government is in bad shape - a lot of incompetent people are in charge.

Well, the story is in the NY Post, so it is going to slam NYC government. If the mayor was one the Post liked they wouldn't have done a story, or one of their columnists would be criticizing the parent for failing to watch her two-year-old child.

Not sure how city government is in bad shape because one child eats a dangerous berry in a park. Back during the Bloomberg days we had tree limbs coming down in Central Park and on streets killing people.

It's very tough to create a world that contains no risks, especially in a setting that is supposed to be natural or semi-natural, like a playground. The city already checks trees looking for hollows that could indicate a dangerous condition and is now taking down trees that are otherwise healthy looking.

I suspect the city will start removing yews in playgrounds because NYC and the Parks Department are pretty sensitive to safety issues - if for no other reason, the city is a big target for lawsuits. As someone mentioned this is a plant that is very common all over America, because its tough and looks good and helps connect us to nature.
