I eat a lot of cereal, and often have found that you can find the best deals at drug stores such as Rite Aid.  Frequent sales, where you'll find a box for as little as 1.99 or 2.99. 

Pathmark on Northern is probably your best bet to spend less on groceries, but you really need a car to get there.  Same goes with Stop and Shop, but I find the rooftop parking creepy and night and don't go there as often.

Also, the fruit stands around here are great and usually cheaper than the grocery stores.

For items you frequently buy that aren't usually on sale, Amazon.com can be a great option, especially if you wait for the specials.  I like to have the 100-calorie pack snacks with my lunch everyday, and I am now loaded up on the oreo and chocolate animal crackers varieties.  There was a deal on Amazon for $15 off a purchase of $39.  Price without the extra discount was only slightly cheaper than grocery store, but with that discount, well worth it!  They will have deals like that on brands such as Kelloggs or Kashi every now and then, and that's when I stock up.  I also really like near-east brand couscous for lunch, which is hard to find around here and expensive.  I can get a pack of 12 for around $12 on Amazon.  Just make sure you pick things that say "qualifies for free super-saver shipping" and if you spend a total of $25 or more, no shipping charges!
