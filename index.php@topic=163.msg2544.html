Quote from: erospolitico on February 01, 2008, 04:47:38 PM
Please be aware that committee meetings are closed to the public

Actually, that is INCORRECT. The public may attend all committee meetings. The whole purpose for Community Boards is to encourage public participation. I'm not sure why 'erospolitico' posted this false information. Any resident is welcome at committee meetings.

As explained on the Community Board website:
The City Charter provides the opportunity for community residents to participate on committees. 

Residents may join a committee by writing the Board Chair requesting membership, with the Chair appointing members after consultation with the committee chairperson.

Public members may vote at committee meetings, with their votes tallied and reported separately.

Public membership provides an opportunity to draw additional expertise, opinions, and manpower into community planning and governance.
And it helps recruit and train potential Board members.

The public may attend all committee meetings. Non-member votes are tallied for information purposes and included in meeting reports.

http://www.cb3qn.nyc.gov/?p=1624

But it seems that you can't just "stop in" at a committee meeting.  You need to plan ahead, write the Board Chair, and then be approved by the Board Chair.  In other words, you have to become a member of the committee. 

Actually, you can just "stop in".  You do NOT have to be a member of a committee to attend a meeting. I have attended many myself. If you simply want to attend a meeting of particular interest to you, you may do so without having to contact anyone or getting approval from anyone. If you want to join the committee, you need to contact the Board Chair.

It is important to let everyone know that Community Boards are supposed to be completely transparent. There is nothing secretive about what the Community Board does. The purpose of a Community Board is to serve as the most local form of government and the most accessible.
