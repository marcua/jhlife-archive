I attended the meeting this evening.

It was informative, and I was impressed with the DA's staff as well as the Captain from the 115th Pct. they all sounded very knowledgeable about crime and quality of life issues in JH, and answered many questions from the audience. The QUeens DA showed up briefly and spoke as well. Peralta did a good job hosting and describing the issues our area faces. He focused a lot on Roosevelt Ave. in particular the illegal document trade that goes on there. Apparently, JH is no longer the "Mecca" for fake documents, the DA's office and the police have cracked down quite a bit and the bulk of that business has moved to The Bronx. That's not to say it doesn't still happen here.

Also apparently, crime is down in our area across, I believe by 7% from the last time stats were measured. There was however a spike in assaults, which for the most part were caused by domestic disputes and a several fights between drunk patrons leaving the bars on Roosevelt Ave. Apparently there have been 7 murders, 6 of which were committed by people who knew their murderer and one was the result of a botched robbery. So there are no axe wielding psychos running around the neighborhood killing random strangers.

Though the event got off to a late start, I appreciated the fact that it was indeed a town hall and not a thinly veiled campaign event for Peralta. he did briefly, and vaguely address the article in today's daily news, but otherwise the focus was clearly on addressing community concerns related to quality of life and public safety.

This is a disappointing development.  Every nickle of public funding could have been spent appropriately.  BUT, if the Corona-Elmhurst Center for Economic Development  can't demonstrate how funds were dispersed in a clear, simple, and transparent way, they have problem.   A needless, self-inflicted problem.  If an organization doesn't have an appreciation and  commitment to their record keeping and financial reporting responsibilities they shouldn't be given any public money.   

A couple of thoughts about the Daily News article.  I don't think it was the tightest piece of reporting I've ever read.   The Daily News article states "More than $350,000 in federal and state funds followed the first big grant."  But when?  Did they get the Corona Elmhurst Center get the money early on or later when they didn't seem to be a functioning entity?  It makes a difference.

"$350,000 of federal and state money"?  How much was federal money and how much was state money?  Federal money is one thing state money is something else.  While all public money, state or federal,  needs to be accounted for, knowing which pot it came out of is important in understanding this.

I really do think the Daily News should have been more precise and detailed in their reporting of this.  On the basis of their sketchy article I don't know what to think.  Reporting should clarify a story, not muddle it.
