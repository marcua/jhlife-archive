Neighborhood Chat / Re: Jackson Heights - Corona BID

Not only does the 82nd Street Partnership collect the ballots, they decide when to send them out, what the ballots say on them, and how long people have to send them back in. Business owners, property owners, residents, and supporters along Roosevelt Avenue are reaching out to every BID stakeholder to explain the consequences of a BID and that all stakeholders have a right to vote. This is an epic task, however, as there are over 800 businesses and 500 property lots within the proposed BID boundaries. The 82nd Street Partnership has quite a bit more resources at its disposal, and the support of the large real estate companies and corporate business interests that are pushing the BID expansion. If anyone would like to help out to inform stakeholders about their right to vote NO, please respond to this email thread and I can put you in contact with some of the business owners and residents who are leading this effort.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Jackson Heights - Corona BID
« on: July 09, 2014, 11:15:55 PM »
Thanks erospolitico. I'm sorry to say that you are misinformed about the process. Although the 82nd Street Partnership decided to call their ballots "Letters of Support" there is indeed a box on the ballot to vote NO. I have a copy of the ballot which I would be happy to show you. In my own conversations with the Department of Small Business Services (SBS), if someone does not vote, that will not be interpreted to mean that they do not support the BID. Only if someone is allowed to check the NO box on the ballot will their vote be counted as not in support. In fact, one of the ways that SBS will count the votes is to look at what percentage of the people who voted where in favor of the BID. If only YES votes are taken, that creates a distorted image of overwhelming support. Also, SBS does not specify what percentage of the stakeholders actually need to vote before they decide whether or not to approve the expansion. Regardless of how the ballots will be counted, the fact that the 82nd Street Partnership is going around pressuring immigrant business owners to vote in favor of the BID expansion is evidence of a deeply flawed process of gauging support.
