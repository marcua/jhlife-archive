Taggie --

Thank you for responding. I hope it was obvious that our intention was never to 'scare' children. We are hoping to reach out to parents -- we have the same goal. 

The reason why we were handing out the flyers was because it was the most effective way to reach parents. Over the past few days, many Garden School parents contacted the Jackson Heights Green Alliance and reported feeling like they are just learning the details in the past few days. Several parents expressed concern that the Garden School Board of Directors was not doing an adequate job of communicating with them -- they mentioned that they were learning information from sources like the Wall Street Journal and this listserve -- not from the Garden School administration.

Mayor Bloomberg, himself, along with Commissioner Benepe, were with Council Member Dromm at the site in the past few weeks. The City has been on the same page about the urgency and importance of this situation. However, the Garden School has told them that they are no longer pursuing the City's offer because of the timing of their cash flow needs. Also, we have not found a way around the ULURP process, which could take between 6-18 months to complete.

We are actively seeking to work with the Garden School parents to help save the field.

Lets work together to figure out a way to help the Garden School's cash flow needs in the short term to provide for the long term solution we all want -- the field to remain an open space.

-Will.

Dear cookie01031958,

In our eagerness to help people understand that we are all on the same side of this difficult situation, we felt it was important to approach the parents of The Garden School students directly. We feel that there has not been a clear line of communication between the school and the parents and we hoped to bridge that this morning.

We did not see or hear any children crying as a result of our activities but if we upset any of them we are extremely sorry, that was not our intent - we hope that is obvious.

We would like to point out that we are not only interested in getting more parks, we are interested in saving the Garden School. It has been an integral part of Jackson Heights for over 80 years and we would mourn its passing.

If the Garden School Board of Directors sells this land to a developer, we are pessimistic about the school's ability to survive after losing its most attractive asset - open space - which is critical to its education environment.  Also, any construction on that property will severely hamper its mission to educate the children. Who would send their children to that school with all the demolition and construction taking place 20 yards away?

Our aim is to raise money from the Jackson Heights community, the Garden School parents, the Board of Directors, the faculty and other entities and financial institutions in order to make sure that the school survives.

We welcome any help you may offer in this matter as we are an all-volunteer organization, and would love to work with you and other families to help save the future of The Garden School.

Thank you,

Dudley

p.s. I don't know what to say about the tall guy with the hat and shades. It was cold and bright, he cannot help being tall - and he is harmless.
