Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Neighborhood Chat / TWC internet problems in JH - they are trying to avoid fixing it
« on: September 08, 2016, 01:00:08 AM »
I know the issue of TWC internet connection was discussed here lately, but I decided to open a new thread, since it seems TWC are trying to avoid fixing the problem which cause the internet connectivity problems in parts of the neighborhood on a daily basis for the last 2 months (!!).
I've been talking with TWC for the last couple of weeks about this issue. Apparently they are ignoring a problem at a central device/station called "Hub L" and this cause the lack of service btn 8p-12a at least for 2 months (!!!). Putting the discussion about the alternatives aside for a moment (and we also consider them), I think the fact that we've been paying for a service which we didn't get is outrageous and their lack of transparency with regard to the issue is just disgraceful. Now, I wonder how to escalate this and who would be the best to reach out to (Dromm? FCC? NY attorney general which already is investigating them), and if there is any will here to join forces and put more pressure on TWC, not just to fix the problem but also to compensate JH residents who've been paying for months and don't get service.

Again, you might say bad TWC service is nothing new, however it is not just that there is bad service. TWC seems to hide the problem, ignore  the complaints on purpose and charge us like business as usual. We want to hold them accountable. Any ideas about how to do that?

I think we should act to make our neighborhood safer and demand more security in the streets from police as well as more lighting in the streets beyond 37th.
Please lets act together by calling our congressman Joseph Crowley whose office is located right next to where the rape occurred at 82-11 37th Ave. His office number for constituents is (718) 779-1400.
Also please let's call City Council Member Daniel Dromm at 718-803-6373  (he has been giving out flyers on this issue but we need further action!)

Would appreciate your further thoughts on this and what we can do as a community to prevent another terrible tragedy!
