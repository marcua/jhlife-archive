Trade Fair Strike - Boycott?
« on: March 13, 2013, 01:25:00 PM »
Not sure if folks are aware, but the meat department workers at Trade Fair are on strike.  Apparently the management is cutting hours, benefits, overtime pay, reducing people's position from full- to part-time, and so on.  Here's what I've found online about it.

http://laborpress.org/sectors/union-retail/1990-supermarket-showdown

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OSjZJoGfkYE&sns=fb

Is anyone involved in this or know more about it? Dromm has been involved and is supporting the workers.  I've always had a love-hate relationship with Trade Fair (love that's on my block and that it's 24 hours, hate that I have to check the expiration dates, that the management are rude, that they overcharge / don't honor their advertised practices).  I'm seriously considering permanently switching to Met for my supermarket shopping after this.

They seem to work in shifts, they're not out in front of the store on 75th & 37th all the time.  I passed by there today (on my way to Met!) and the strikers / locked out employees were making a lot of contact with folks on the street.  I've still seen people walking around with TF shopping bags (while the strikers were out there) so there are definitely people in the neighborhood who are going past the picket line.

Here's another incredibly disturbing video involving a delivery of meat to TF.  Don't watch if you're easily disturbed.  I've seen this happening at other stores in the area - trucks delivering like this park on the southwest corner of 75th & 37th in the early morning most days.  Not sure if this is a halal delivery (there's a halal butcher nearby and TF sells halal meat) but I'm appalled that food is able to be delivered like this.
