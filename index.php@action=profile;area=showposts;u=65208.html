Neighborhood Chat / Sunnyside Yard Proposal: 12k affordable apartments, transit hub, more
« on: March 11, 2020, 01:06:48 AM »
There has been a lot of coverage of the Sunnyside Yard development after the master plan was released this month.

Some examples:
https://thecity.nyc/2020/03/queens-critics-cloudy-on-sunnyside-yard-plans-14b-bill.html
https://nymag.com/intelligencer/2020/03/the-sunnyside-yard-master-plan-is-a-mirage-of-a-better-city.html
https://www.wsj.com/articles/new-queens-project-to-include-12-000-affordable-apartments-11583244000

I'm curious what people think of the proposal. 

It would cost tens of billions over decades, so it has that in common with Hudson Yards, but to me it looks like that's where the similarities end.  They aren't proposing to build as much luxury square footage as possible -- neighbors be damned -- in order to raise as much property tax revenue as possible.  Instead, all residential units are proposed to be affordable, there will be a modest amount of commercial space, and lots of green space.

My first impression is that if the city wants to make a huge investment in Queens in a way that is sensitive to the community, we should encourage them. 

Plus, with the transit hub, we would have fewer reasons to step foot in Penn Station.  Sorry, buried the lede.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Q49 bus to be eliminated
« on: January 08, 2020, 01:11:21 AM »
There's a lot that is bad about this new plan, but there are some silver linings, too.

For instance, the new QT4 is going to make us much better connected to Brooklyn.  It's good to space out the stops more so that the buses move faster.  The shorter routes like the new 66 come at a cost of needing to transfer (depending on your destination), but reliability is one of the big complaints right now, and this addresses that.

So, yes, we should pipe up and demand that the MTA fix the problems they created, but let's also be sure that we give them positive feedback about the things we want to keep.
