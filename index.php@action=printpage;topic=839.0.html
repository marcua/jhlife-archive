Title: JH "PhotoForum"
Post by: NYC Native on May 02, 2008, 10:53:04 AM
I decided to start  a Jackson Heights "PhotoForum"

Grab your cameras and start shooting.  Don't try to take the most perfect picture, we are just trying to have some fun.  Simply have a camera with you whenever you remember and simply go about your day as usual.  Don't forget that you are trying to convey a message and possibly a feeling or feelings for that matter.  Remember that the photo should be within the JH boundaries and not just in the Historical District.  Preferably of buildings and scenes of panoramic value but it could also be of people walking around that would inspire any sentiments.  JH does have it's own and varied fauna and its diverse inhabitants can sometimes cause laughter and sadness.

We should try to avoid backgrounds that are confusing because your message , if any, may get lost with it or "compete" and complicate the subject matter. If you are trying to isolate the subject try to center onto it and explain the photo if you feel it warrants one.  Some photos need no explanation but as a final touch you may want to title it to make it your own.

So grab your digital or 35MM and start your drive-by's or walk-by's for that matter!   :2funny:

Title: Re: JH "PhotoForum"
Post by: Chuckster on August 26, 2009, 08:58:58 PM
Chocopie, the way I've uploaded photos to JHL is by using an image hosting site like Photobucket (http://photobucket.com/).  Once you have a photo that you like uploaded to that site, what you do is just copy the image code that automatically appears at the base of the photo when you hover over it with your mouse.  Look for the link that has the following codes surrounding it:

(IMG) (/IMG)

Keep in mind that the codes must be surrounded by brackets rather than parenthesis like I have above.  Hit copy, and then paste that into the message box on your reply in JHL.  The image should appear on the screen when you hit preview.

Other members...feel free to chime in also.
