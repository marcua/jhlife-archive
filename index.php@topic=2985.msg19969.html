I just caught this thread and I was reminded of my trip to Vancouver and this quaint restaurant called Salt. The theme was the pairing of artisinal cheeses, small-batch cured meats, and wines, beers and Sherries. The space had a minimalist organic feel to it with a big communal table. I think this would be relatively easy to set up in Jackson Heights since it wouldn't require a full kitchen. It would satisfy those who want a glass of wine or beer and some gourmet food. I would consider investing in a venture like this.

Btw, the food and wine was delicious.

Still thinking about this one. One other person/couple expressed an interest.  I've been thinking alot about location recently. One place that I find appealing is the corner deli at 76th and 37th Avenue. It has a very wide sidewalk where an awning with outdoor seating could be placed. It seems like it might be the right size inside as well.

Another thing that caught my eye was a very oddly placed travel agency on 80th street, near the 7 train. The travel agency is located in the back, though a wide alleyway with alot of outdoor plaza surrounding it. It reminds me alot of the Habana Outpost in Fort Greene. It would be great to have something similar to that in our neighborhood.

Bonito, your suggestion is right on the money I think. The biggest killer for alot of restaurants is the schedule delay and the finances involved with setting up a full commercial kitchen. A friend of mine started up several restaurants in Manhattan that are all about not having a commercial kitchen (Solex, Bar Carrera). We learned this together quite well when opening a restaurant with a full kitchen near Joe's Pub. The restaurant is still open, but the kitchen wasn't opened on time and it's seen better days financially.

BTW, I love the name Salt. The book of the same name, by Mark Kurlansky is excellent. You should read it if you have a chance.

This past weekend we visited a new restaurant in Forest Hills called "aged". It had decent food and drink selections and would probably do well in our neighborhood (it was very meat heavy). The theme of the restaurant was about "aged" foods and it had a nice rustic woodsy vibe.
