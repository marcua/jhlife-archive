Title: Re: NYT Article - Wedded, but Not to Any Particular Place
Post by: ellenpratt on July 18, 2014, 08:06:16 AM
As a Realtor working mostly in Jackson Heights (I live here), I can say that there has been a surge in sales price this spring and summer particularly in 2 BR listings.

In June I listed a 2BR in a well-kept building just one street outside of the Historic District. The sellers were motivated so I listed it for an attractive price in the 380's. The day it hit the MLS calls started flooding in. When it was on Trulia and Zillow a day later things really went nuts. There were many offers over list price and in four days the sellers had accepted a cash offer in the 400's, very close to 10% over list. It's now under contract at that price.

So yes, JH is drawing attention as a great alternative to Manhattan and Brooklyn. Buyers get more space and spend less money and are discovering what this neighborhood really has to offer as a wonderful place to live.

ANY capital improvements recently made by your rental landlord should make you wonder if they are not about to attempt a conversion to a coop. Back in the early 80s, just about EVERY apartment complex in Queens that converted had three tell-tale signs:

1. New windows
2. Upgraded elevators and lobbys
3. New laundry rooms
