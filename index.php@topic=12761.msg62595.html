As disturbing as these incidents are, the likelihood that you will be caught up in violent crime is still INCREDIBLY low. Don't go by headlines and how they make you feel: look at the data for the last few months and years

http://maps.nyc.gov/crime/

JH, per capita, is still much safer than large swathes of Brooklyn and the Bronx, and comparable to parts of manhattan.

The fact that the numbers are so low per capita across virtually ALL of NYC compared to ANY other major city (>500,000) makes the data analysis difficult; low numbers mean a single stray violent incident can increase per month rates by 50%!

To dig deeply, look here

http://www.fbi.gov/about-us/cjis/ucr/crime-in-the-u.s/2014/preliminary-semiannual-uniform-crime-report-january-june-2014/tables/table-4/table-4/view
 
Comparisons with cities of >25,000 are incredibly favorable; we don't even make the top 100!

http://www.neighborhoodscout.com/neighborhoods/crime-rates/top100dangerous/
