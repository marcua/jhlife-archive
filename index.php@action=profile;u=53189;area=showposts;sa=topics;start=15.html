Neighborhood Chat / Price Gouging Anyone Else?
« on: September 20, 2015, 04:55:29 PM »
So, I had two price gouging instances in the same day for me last week. It all started when my partner and I were playing a splendid game of Badminton at Traver's Park. I noticed a Spanish mom and her kids getting ice cream at the ice cream truck, as I passed us I asked how much, she said $1.50 for a small cone. I said, wow, it's warm maybe I'll get one to cool off. I approached the ice cream truck that is usually parked parallel to 34th on 78th where the park starts. The woman (Spanish) instructed me that the small cones were in fact $2.50, I said well you just charged that woman a $1.50, she smirked and said no, no it's $2.50. I told her that price gouging is not right as I tried to find a license or permit number but I couldn't find any. Another Spanish gentleman who was walking his dog nearby overheard and said that this happens a lot. Is this illegal? I'm more upset of the fumes that exit the ice cream truck because it idles there for hours but thats another subject...

Later in the day we went to go check out Mario's Pizza (formerly Gustosa's) to try there slices. My partner has been going to Gustosa's for the better of 25 years and loved the pizza. We ordered 2 grandma slices, had a nice convo with one of the workers who was making the pie. Then another guy (delivery/counter guy) comes in and stays. We finish eating, ask how much, the original guy murmurs $6 but get's cut off by the other who says $7. Mind you, this is for 2 slices. A customer prior who ate there twice in the same week noticed that the prices were different in those two times as well as we met outside and talked for a bit.

So who knows, but I am sure this goes on a lot or not, anyone have any similar encounters?
