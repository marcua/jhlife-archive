Quote from: pipman on April 05, 2010, 08:28:53 AM
The concept of bagel stores with tables and chairs seems to work in Astoria and Manhattan and everywhere else, why not Jackson Heights?
Is rent on 37th Ave that much more?  If you are paying about $5-$6 for a bagel with cream cheese spread and coffee, it's nice to be able to sit for 15 minutes and enjoy.

I agree with you, pipman.  The problem arises when people overstay those 15 minutes, thereby not allowing others that same enjoyment.  That said, the table and chair concept is probably more successful in other neighborhoods because there seems to be a larger variety of these types of businesses in Astoria and Manhattan.  In other words, people have more options to choose from, so they're not all occupying tables with lengthy stays at any one particular coffee house or restaurant.  On any given day, I've had problems getting a seat at Lety's, Dunkin Donuts, etc.  These are places where people like to enjoy a cup of coffee, sit back and relax while participating in a chat with friends.

Hey, maybe the problem all stems from the lack of green space in our neighborhood.  If we had more public outdoor areas with seating, we wouldn't have to hang out at our favorite spots for so long.  Shelby2 also makes a valid point in that in order for businesses to thrive and succeed, there must be regular customer turnover.  I would imagine that's critical for any business, but especially during a depressed economy.
