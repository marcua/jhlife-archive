Restaurants & Food / JH Food Hub
« on: March 14, 2016, 01:25:22 PM »
Hello fellow Jackson Heighters!

I'm a local mom and came across a blog in Super Healthy Kids. It intrigued me enough to start a Wholeshare group since there isn't one in and near Jackson Heights. I learned that Wholeshare offers about 15,000 sustainable food products. Not only will it lessen my grocery trips, most of their products are priced from 20 to 50% lower than supermarket prices.

The group I created, JH Food Hub, gives us access to affordable, sustainable, quality food by organizing group purchases through Wholeshare. There's no cost to join our group on the system and see the products and prices available. Please visit http://www.wholeshare.com/join/10198 to join. Wholeshare also allows us to order as frequently (or infrequently) as we want, and they have low minimums and no delivery fees for most vendors. Their system also will allow us to add other local farmers who sell in wholesale quantities once we've started buying together as a group.

Here are a couple links that explain a bit more about Wholeshare:
 
Introduction Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RFV4swNl5kg
Article on Buying Club in Newburgh, NY: http://www.recordonline.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20130509/NEWS/305090340&cid=sitesearch

Thanks for reading!

Any questions, email us at: JHFoodHub@gmail.com
