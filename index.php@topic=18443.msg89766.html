My experience at JH City MD
« on: October 25, 2018, 07:44:27 PM »
I brought my spouse to JH CityMD on Roosevelt Avenue this morning. He had food poisoning and was slightly dehydrated.

While registering at the desk he passed out and fell to the floor. He came to, and we helped him onto a seat. The doctor came out and stood there. I asked her, Chien Jung Chu, to check his blood pressure and heart to see if we could go to Cornell Medical center. She said, "No, it is not his turn."

The receptionist called 911.

"Dr" Chu stood there for 5 minutes not doing anything. After a while (I do not know how long, maybe 15 minutes) the EMT's came and brought him to the hospital.

It depends what you mean by pressing medical needs. CityMD is a valid place to go for a flu shot, altough with all of the drugstores in JH, why bother. And, it may be a valid place to go if you superficially cut your finger. But it is not the place to go for any emergency that will need anything but superficial care. If you are having a heart attack, a stroke, cut your finger and can see the bone, or broke your leg, CityMD is useless. And, unlike my spouse who did not want to go to the ER, if you have passed out and fallen to the ground, you should not go to CityMD.

Many people have had good experiences at CityMD, but it is not a primary medical facility. And, the EMT in the ambulance told me that CityMD is not required to have either a doctor or Physicians Assistant at the facility.

CityMD is almost no better than going to the school nurse.
