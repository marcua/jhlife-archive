crocketnyc,

You asked "Why is Senator Peralta making us think that the IDC will produce the results when the Senate Republicans would rather have our 16 and 17 year olds locked up in Rikers for certain non felony crimes and traffic and vehicular violations?"

I don't know if the IDC can produce those results but the reason I want to let them try is precisely because of (many) Republicans' willingness to continue this barbaric practice.  "Raise The Age" has been cited by Peralta, Hamilton, Alcantera and the other  IDC members as one of their priorities.  There is at least a chance that if they sit at the table with the Repubs during budget talks and in other settings,  they could find a way to get Republican acquiescence for Raise the Age, Peralta's bill to allow higher education for undocumented New Yorkers, and at least a few other critically important bills.  I, for one, have had it with relying on an ineffectual Democratic minority that just holds press conferences and pushes for bills that won't be passed unless they can pick off at least a few Republican votes, but have shown no ability to pick off those votes.

Maybe what Peralta is trying to do won't work.  But, as his constituent, I want to let him try because I want the State to stop tossing teenagers into prison.

 And I want to try everything possible to resist Republicans who are bound and determined to be enablers of Trump's agenda.  Given the current numbers in the Senate, in the near term, I don't see any other way to accomplish that other than the path Peralta has chosen.
