Cafe 75
« on: August 14, 2015, 11:34:17 AM »
I've often griped about the lack of good bars for activities other than soccer and/or Paid Female Companionship but one that I'd forgotten about and overlooked is Cafe 75 on Roosevelt.  It's a really comfortable, friendly place with cheap, strong drinks and good music--and not too loud.  I've gone with the missus a number of times and always felt welcome (as opposed to other places that flat out refused to let her in!--hello, Flamingo!)  Recommended and well worth stopping in for one or two when you feel like getting out.

I've found two interesting places for drinks.
Tempo Librero, on 88th and Roosevelt, is a beautiful lounge. Nice music, cocktails and other than loud spanish music. Lounge! Wow, for JH. They are a gay bar, but usually super quiet in any sense early evenings.

Also, El Toro Bravo on Roosevelt and around 88th.. Interesting place. The food was really great. One half is restaurant and the other half is bar. It is easy to miss as it's tucked in a bit from the sidewalk and has a small window. They advertise very interesting margaritas including:
orange/habanero
rompope with strawberries (it's like eggnog, I think)

tamarind and chamo (??)
pepino con jalapeno
watermelon
guanabana
I haven't drank there, just had a meal.
I really liked the business owners at both places.
