Neighborhood Chat / Re: Indian Community
« on: November 07, 2011, 02:15:36 PM »
This is America, land of immigrants. When my ancestors arrived from Italy, they were not formally educated, could not speak the language (but, learned), had no health care, etc. Very similar to what you've described. I'm certain this is the same story for many, many immigrants. I can recall hearing similar complaints being made about the increasing Colombian population back in the 70s and 80s. I've lived in Jackson Heights on and off for over 3 decades. I remember when the Indians first began arriving, in the 1970s. When the Jackson Diner was, in fact, an American diner called -- The Jackson Diner, which, when it was going out of business, was purchased by a wonderful man, a small business, chef-entrepeneur from India. In its present location, it's now the most popular restaurant in J.H., by reputation. I welcome the Indian people; I always have. I find them to be a gracious, soft-spoken and peaceable people. I love the vibrancy of their culture, their exotic way of dress, their delicious food. In my opinion, the more of them the merrier. I also welcome the newly arrived Serbs and Croatians, Russians, French, Asians, Africans -- and the newest arrivals: Yuppies from Brooklyn -- many of which live in my building. This is the reason I've always loved Jackson Heights and why I've always moved back here after I'd lived elsewhere. It's so diversified. Some people have complained that Jackson Heights should be like Manhattan. Are you willing to pay twice as much rent? Everything comes with a price. If you can afford Manhattan, well, more power to you.
