There is a guy who frequently plays trumpet by the Foodtown on 76th and 37th ave.
An older gentleman that has taken to playing his electric acoustic guitar on the bench on 82nd street (in front of the GNC) or on the corner of 82nd and 37th ave.
There is also a guy who plays some spanish ballad standards in the fish and chips place under the 7 train right off of the 82nd on roosevelt.

It really is tragic that live music in the street isn't more present in Jackson Heights considering more than half of the neighborhood is Hispanic. Improvised jamming on the street is a standard in Latin America. Maybe it is because spanish towns are always built with a central plaza that is flanked on all sides by staple institutions (bank, church, post office). The plaza de armas is where people of all ages go to sit on benches or stroll or run errands. Because of that it is the ideal place for street performers and musicians. It is also well patrolled so you feel safe.

The 82nd development office had mentioned wanting to renovate the the triangle on Baxter avenue (not Manuel de Dios triangle). I'd mentioned the idea of emulating the plaza de armas on a smaller scale with the goal of luring musicians.
