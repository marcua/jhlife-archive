Ultimately we all have our own self interests, myself included.  The city government has a responsibility to provide police services to ensure that all citizens have safe environments to raise their children.  Whether there is prostitution 500 feet from where I live, or 5000 feet, it impacts me and everyone else in some manner - either directly by it's physical presence, discarded condomns, etc., or indirectly in the form of increased taxes to pay for police protection, social service agencies to deal with the physical and psychological problems associated with prostitution, increase in AIDS and other sexual diseases, etc.  Who pays for these things?  As they say in the economics department, "there's no such thing as a free lunch".  

Secondly, people in these areas deserve the same opportunities and safe living environments as someone living in the Upper West Side, Sutton Place, Gramercy Park, etc.  There seems to me to be an implication in many responses here that there's nothing wrong with a little bit of prostitution, that's it's "edgy", reflective of some sort of "gritty urban realism".  Well, I hate to be the one to inform you all - life is not a Snoop Dogg video!  There's a reason why Snoop Dogg and others of his ilk don't live in crappy neighborhoods anymore, for the same reason that Hugh Hefner sent his children to private schools.  The jokes on you if you really fall for this crap in music videos and the movies.

Thirdly, as for developing opportunities, I'm all ears.  What do you suggest?  A supply-side argument, perhaps creating opportunities by reducing income taxes and capital gains tax rates in order to stimulate investment and ultimately create additional jobs?  Or perhaps are you more favorable to the Keynesian school of economics, which considers what it perceives to be the failures of laissez-faire capitalism, and seeks more of a role by government in the economy, via government spending on social programs, job training, government investment in infrastructure, reduction in interest rates, etc.?

I have absolutely no idea what we're arguing about.  I really regret dredging up this thread from the back pages.

There is an active and vibrant nightlife scene on Roosevelt Avenue.  It draws crowds of people from all over the area, which in turn draws in some seedier enterprises looking to take advantage of the economic opportunity.  This same situation exists in similar districts all over the place.  I am thankful that at the present time, at least, we have been spared the worst of the violent and predatory elements that have been drawn to West Chelsea and other nightclub areas.  As I posted earlier, the 115th Precinct is bringing new policing strategies and additional resources in to improve the situation on Roosevelt.  I now see cops out there quite frequently.  Peralta is asking for more to be done about prostitution, but the article makes clear that the police have been working on this as well.  Now we have a promise from the Guardian Angels to patrol the Avenue... we'll see what comes of that if anything.

My family spends a lot of time on Roosevelt Avenue.  We shop and eat there, used to belong to a gym there, and my son takes tae kwon do lessons there.  Roosevelt Avenue is a reminder that we live in a large and complex city, not just in a suburban enclave next to one.  I feel that it is absolutely safe, and I have no hesitation bringing my children there, despite many influences I hope they stay away from, because I want them to have the street savvy they need to navigate urban life.
