Neighborhood Chat / Re: Speak English

I guess I came pretty late to this discussion (I just joined a few days ago! but I feel like adding my two cents - which is -

I was at Geno's last year - saw the sign - was hugely offended - and bolted to Pat's across the street. I am not American but my Mexican boyfriend is, and we both felt discriminated against. Not toward ourselves, but rather, who we represent - the large numbers of tourists who go to Geno's due to its fame, and the Mexican immigrants in the neighborhood. The pro-Iraq paraphernalia (I think it's pro-Iraq, it may be pro-police?) didn't help and just added to the feeling of unease we had about the place. But it was that Please Speak English sign which was the proverbial last straw.

I am aware that it is their business decision, so I made a personal decision not to patronize their store. But just imagine if one of you were in China and the store sign said Please Speak Mandarin and had (I'm just making this up) anti-Tibet or anti-Taiwan literature (or even anti-US!) You would not feel comfortable there. And it's the intent of the owner to make you not want to patronize his store.

Geno's is located in a neighborhood with a large number of Mexican immigrants. Ultimately the sign is about disrespect to the community.

PS: The thing that really pissed me off was that after having driven to Philly for some cheesesteak, they weren't even THAT good!
