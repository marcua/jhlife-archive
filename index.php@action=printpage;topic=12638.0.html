The buses are extremely frequent at some times and not so frequent at others.  If you're getting off work around 5 or 6 and arriving at the 74th station to take a bus home, they are coming around every 3-4 minutes at that point.  Later in the evening like 9 pm, they come about every 10 minutes and shift to every 15 minutes around 10 pm, and then drop off even more late nights.  That's the weekday schedule.  On weekends they are less frequent in general. 

The nice thing is, though, that if you're getting off at 74th and heading home to 88th/35th, there is a bus terminal and you either wait on the sidewalk inside the covered terminal for the bus to begin loading, or you wait inside the bus for it to pull out.  In other words, there's an element of safety and being protected from any nasty weather.

Here's a schedule http://web.mta.info/busco/schedules/q049cur.pdf and there are also apps for your smart phone or iphone that tell you exactly when the buses are going to arrive (based on GPS).

I now live on the 34th Avenue and 87th street.  90th street was dirty with vendor refuse, crowded, noisy and when I came home late at night, the problem wasn't that it was deserted: there was a "club" at the bottom of the stairs with tons of people milling about, cars blaring music etc. It was seriously unpleasant to walk down the stairs into that scene, especially after a stressful and extremely long day at work.  I did it because it was the closest station to my house and at midnight I needed to just get home.  I can't speak for others, I did this for seven years, and I'm not exaggerating. I lived in  the Bronx for twenty years, also coming home late at night; 90th street was worse in terms of overall experience. And yes, there were two daytime attacks the week before I moved over to 34th Street, and  two-three years earlier (or thereabouts in terms of time), a young boy was murdered on Halloween by a gang. Most nights it was fine, I lived a five minute walk to my apartment and just put on autopilot. I've been doing this late night urban walk home thing my entire adult life, I know how to handle myself.

82nd street is not perfect, but it is generally cleaner because of the BID daily cleaning, the stores are higher quality. and I think it is on the upswing. I can stop at Rite Aid on my way home, or the health store. So personally, I'd rather walk a little further to get home, to have a less stressful experience, at least in terms of my sensory experience.

The Q49 bus doesn't come as often at night, you often have to wait for it, or sit in it, at 74th Street. I thinkit's every 10-15 minutes, which can be frustrating when you just want to get home. But it is an option.  My husband and I have been taking it TO 74th street in the morning and taking the F to work, that has been infitely more reliable and faster than the & train.
