Hi earbears. I have volunteered there. I recommmend it.
There are some very good things about regal heights. It typically receives an "Excellent" rating from The Joint Commission; last year was no exception. It's secure. The recreation and PT departments are excellent. At the end of the day, though, it's a nursing home, a "skilled nursing facility."  The food is lousy, there's some disturbing behaviour by at least one person on each floor, and the RNs are ... seem so busy. I saw and heard some troubling things, of course. SNF's house difficult cases.
Good luck.

I went there after hip replacement a couple of years ago.

Physiotherapists were great, except nobody wanted to acknowledge that one leg was now shorter. I had to get my husband to bring me shoe inserts and tape so I could improvise something. It's not helpful to be told that the joint will probably settle in (it never has) when lurching around dot and carry trying to walk again! But basically they were very good.

Food - well, mostly what you'd expect. And don't assume you'll get your menu choice, even if anyone shows up in the morning to offer. And if you have any food allergies and requirements you may really have to struggle. My roommate had several conferences with the nutritionist but nothing seemed to stick for more than a day or so.

The nursing staff seem just plain overworked, but lovely people when you could get one! It's distressing to hear patients calling out for attention.

Unless they've changed, the room TVs had no option for private listening, and this was a major annoyance for me since roommate had it on all the time. I'd wait for her to fall asleep then turn it off. She was in a lot of pain so I didn't want to be too insistent.

The internet was rather unreliable for several days, and sites like Livejournal and Facebook were blocked.

My surgeon told me it would be eleven days, but turned out that was business days, with no PT on the weekends.

Overall I'd say they got the job done but I was glad to get away!

If your friend is otherwise healthy and capable, able to speak up for him/herself, and Regal Heights is in the most convenient location, then by all means go there. But I would not recommend it as a rehab or nursing facility for someone who is elderly or otherwise incapacitated and who would need more attention. The quality of care is borderline negligent and there is a complete lack of communication and coordination of care throughout the staff.

If you want a recommendation, check out Queens Boulevard Extended Care Facility (Woodside) or New York Center for Rehabilitation and Nursing (Astoria).
