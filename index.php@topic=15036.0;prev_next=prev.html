Even schools that appear to be "neighborhood" high schools, if they're any good at all, get applications from city-wide and have city-wide populations. Zoned high schools are often places of last resort where people who can't or won't bother to apply to high schools end up getting dumped. Riding the subway to high school is a rite of passage for NY teenagers. We need elementary schools to serve the burgeoning population of tiny people in this neighborhood and prepare them properly, without hideous commutes, so when they turn 14 they can fly free and have the city as their oyster (as so many of us did growing up!)
