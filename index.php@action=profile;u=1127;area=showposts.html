Neighborhood Chat / Re: Mentally unstable neighbor destroying property...
« on: August 30, 2009, 07:31:17 PM »
If the mgmt company are resistant to letting you  break the lease, you could possibly throw prior knowledge in their face. You should ask a lawyer, but it's something to do with the fact that if they knew about this guy before you signed the lease and didn't tell you, they had prior knowledge of an issue and the lease might be violated. I'm not sure how it works, but it could be an option.

We were in a similar situation recently and had to move because of extremely noisy upstairs neighbors (two moves in three months, with a baby=no fun!). Luckily, we got a top floor apartment in the same complex and management were accommodating. A lawyer friend advised me to bring up the prior knowledge thing if a new apt didn't come up and we wanted to move out of the building entirely.

Anyway, good luck. After being through it, my advice is to not consume yourself with getting him out, and rather try and get a new place.
