The recent thread on Sky Foods reminded me of a latent ambition to learn more about Chinese and/or Indian cooking. Why not, with all these amazing sources of spices, exotic veggies and other ingredients all around?
Can you recommend your favorite cookbook that covers the fundamentals of these cuisines? I'm not so much interested in the intense, takes-three-days-to-prepare kind of recipes. Rather, I'd like to learn new dishes that are relatively simple and can be worked into your everyday routine.

I love Fuchsia Dunlop's books. Both Land of Plenty (for Sichuan recipes) and The Revolutionary Chinese Cookbook (for Hunan specialties) are fantastic and superbly researched.

For Indian food, I recommend Madhur Jaffrey's books. She has many to chose from and recipes are focused on the home cook and have clear explanations and descriptions.

For Chinese, I would recommend the following:

http://www.amazon.com/Chinese-Rice-Noodles-Appetizers-Wei-Chuan/dp/0941676862/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1336227280&sr=8-1

It is a good primer to making the basic dishes and sauces, not to mention the fact that most can be accomplished in fewer than 20 minutes of prep time (consisting primarily of chopping which is to be expected in Chinese cooking).

To be sure, I have made quite a few from the book to the delight of dinner guests.  Good luck.

Just came across this post.  These books have always served me well:

The Modern Art of Chinese Cooking by Barbara Tropp is a good start.
Any of Eileen Yin-Fei Lo's books:  The DimSum Cookbook, The Chinese Way, The Chinese Kitchen
The Wisdom of The Chinese Kitchen by Grace Young
Asian Noodles by Nina Simonds
Chinese Cooking: The Classical Techniques Made Easy by Norman Weinstein
And for something a little different: The Classic Cuisine Of Vietman by Bach Ngo and Gloris Zimmerman
Authentic Vietnamese Cooking: Food from a Family Table by Corinne Trang and Christopher Hirsheime
Taste Vietnam - The Morning Glory Cookbook by Trinh Diem Vy
