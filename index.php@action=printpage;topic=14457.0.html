Title: Re: Reckless Drivers
Post by: toddg on March 01, 2016, 10:33:55 PM
Yes, drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians should all be more mindful -- and dare I say it? -- perhaps more courteous as well.   

But there are still too many dangerous drivers on the streets, and we need some real enforcement of the law to protect us from them.   Despite all of the hype of the Vision Zero era, police enforcement actions in the 115th Precinct against four driving behaviors that put pedestrians at risk (speeding, red light running, failure to yield to pedestrians, and illegal cell phone use) have not increased are collectively down from where they were several years ago.   (To be precise, tickets for three of the categories are up, but tickets against illegal cell phone use are down sharply).  There really has been no lasting uptick in effort by the police to keep our streets safe from dangerous drivers, largely because the police have limited resources, and efforts to hold drivers accountable in the courts are so rarely successful.

The City Council has tried to pass legislation to give police and prosecutors more effective tools to deal with reckless drivers, but they're really at the limits of what they can do.  New York State's laws dealing with reckless drivers are weak and out of date relative to the state of practice in other states.  We need action in Albany to get stronger laws in the books to address unlicensed drivers who injure or kill, drivers who flee the scene of crashes, and drunk drivers.   

The city also needs greater authority to deploy speed cameras and red light enforcement cameras, both of which are extremely effective at saving lives by changing patterns of driver behavior.   NYC has a highly effective program using automatic speed cameras for traffic enforcement, but state legislation caps their use to only 140 citywide, only at schools, and only during school hours.  Families for Safe Streets, a coalition of families who have lost loved ones to traffic crashes in NYC, is calling for legislation to authorize a speed camera at every one of the 2,000 schools in the city, operating 24/7.  I'll post more on this campaign when it becomes available.
