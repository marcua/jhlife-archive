Title: Re: Indian Food
Post by: Superclam on December 15, 2014, 07:03:43 PM
I'm no expert but here goes:
I order delivery from Indian Taj, largely because they'll deliver up to me. I like their food.
I bring out of JH guests to the buffet at Jackson Diner. It's also decent, but they have a wide variety, it's cheap, and you can always get a table.
Both of the above are what I call "American-Indian," typical dishes you can get anywhere that are no doubt tailored to American tastes, but I'm American, and I like it.
I go to Dosa Delight for dosas and other South Indian fare. They've never disappointed, but they're a bit small and get crowded at times.
If I don't have the kids with me, I'll take my wife to Delhi Heights. They're a bit more upscale, and they also have Chinese-Indian food.
A Pakistani friend of mine swears by Dera, especially the goat, but I haven't personally been there.

Post by: pipman on January 04, 2015, 04:52:13 PM
I would avoid going to the rest room in any of the Indian Restaurants in Jackson Heights.  There are many restaurants in the neighborhood where they often seat you without really cleaning the table from the last customer (a dirty napkin does not count). I always ask the waitress to clean the table.

As far as food quality, I stopped going to Jackson Diner many years ago--although it was the first place I went when I first came to JH--mainly because the food was not that good. Unfortunately the quality of Indian/Pakistani food has gone down in the neighborhood...it seems that Curry Hill in Manhattan has had a resurgence of good restaurants.  Here in JH I tend to go to Dera (72nd & Bway) for more authentic Pakistani food and sweets and Dosa Delight or Samudra for Indian vegetarian.  I want to revisit the buffet at Dehli Delights, it was very good when they first opened, but think this place may have changes hands.  Most of the new restaurants down there are Nepalese/Tibetan--Phayul and Spicy Tibet are both very good.
