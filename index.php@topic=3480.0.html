These guys or whatever we are calling them on this thread are human beings, that are less fortunate than many.
Although, some in our community may be just a pay check away from being homeless.  I understand how difficult it must be to see "these guys" around us, but the reality is that it is a truly sad, sad situation.  I always explained to my little ones what it meant to be homeless and today they are willing to lend a hand whenever possible.  Not sure what the solution is other than to offer "these guys" some food whenever possible, a blanket, or something that may keep them fed and warm.  I understand it isn't easy living in a shelter, in fact I have heard it is sometimes very dangerous.  My heart goes out to those that don't have a home and self medicate themselves to ease the pain.  I as well know many that are struggling in their own homes by self medicating.  

These are still human beings who need our community to assist even if it is in a very small way.  And just perhaps, we are seeing more of "these guys" around due to these crazy times that we are living.  So things may not get any better any time soon.  And for those that are annoyed, put yourself in their shoes for just one second and I think you will feel some compassion.  

Oh, and one more thing.  I see plenty of people that have a home, self medicate behind closed doors, look good, smell good, and as well "*(%&)" out of people.  :smiley6600:

About a week or so ago I noticed an orange van belonging to a homeless outreach organization (the name of which I forget) and two out reach workers trying to convince an individual to take them up on their services (presumably temporary housing, medical assistance, food etc.).

The problem is no adult living on the streets can be forced to go to any sort of housing or social service, they have to go of their own free will (unless the temperature drops below freezing I think). Which, sadly, makes sense since all the services in the world won't do one bit of good if the person they are intended for doesn't want to make use of them.

If the out reach workers had a more consistent presence I believe they would, over time, be able to convince the majority of the people living on the streets here in JH to seek some sort of shelter / assistance. But, as you might imagine these organizations are severely underfunded and over worked. We would certainly need the help of our elected officials and their discretionary money to help fund such an effort.
