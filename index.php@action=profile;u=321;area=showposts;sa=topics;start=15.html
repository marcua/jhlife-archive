Neighborhood Chat / Unnecessary Noise from Leaf Blowers, etc.
« on: July 24, 2010, 09:30:00 AM »

There is so much noise to endure in JH such as airplanes, car alarms, busses -- one that could be avoided is the use of high decibel leaf blowers.  Some communities have banned these (mostly California).  This morning the workers at 8409 35th Avenue started using a very noisy leaf blower around 8 AM.  State law allows use of lawn care equipment beginning at 9 AM on weekends.  But "unreasonable" noise created from this is not allowed.  Given that my windows were closed and air conditioning on a block away, I think the leaf blower used was creating unreasonable noise.   What's wrong with old-fashioned rakes and brooms.  This type of equipment causes pollution and should be banned in residential areas.  If you are bothered, call 311 (although it is now difficult to get through--maybe budget cuts?) or tell someone in that building.

Neighborhood Chat / Time Warner Cable outage due to fire/demolition at 84th St. and 37th Avenue
« on: February 20, 2010, 09:56:49 AM »
TIME WARNER TRIPLE PLAY = TRIPLE DISASTER
If you were affected by the 2 day interruption of service of Time Warner cable, internet, and/or phone service that was caused not by the fire but by the demolition of the buildings -- make sure you get appropriate compensation from Time Warner.   I believe this impacted buildlings on 84th Street between 37th and 35th.

Not sure why contractors at the site were not aware that TW cables were running through the fire impacted buildings.
I was told by contractor at site that Verizon and National Grid came out immediately after fire.   
When TW was notified they were slow to respond.  Problem was reported at 11 am on Wednesday and they did not enter site until after 4:30 pm.
Service was finally restored Thursday 6:20 pm.  Time Warner customer service was not helpful and they made it difficult to contact representatives until after city complaint was made.

Daniel Dromm's office and Community Board were notified and helpful and responsive. Of course there are questions for other city agencies, why there was not better control over the demolition and why were cables running through privately owned property.

If you have not, you may still want to call 311 and file a complaint with NYC Dept. of Information Technology and Telecommunications. Even if you were not affected this time -- you still may want to rethink having the Triple Play with TW.  No phone, internet and TV sucks!
