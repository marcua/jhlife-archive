Re: good Indian food restaurants?
« Reply #5 on: July 02, 2009, 09:59:35 PM »
Delhi Heights is the way to go if you're out to impress.  The food is excellent.  Stay with the Indian menu.  The bathrooms are spotless and it is easy to get to from the train station.  It is right on the corner of 74th St and 37th Road.  The best South Indian has to be Dosa Place on 73rd St between 37th and 35th aves.  It is just a few doors in from 37th.  They specialize in perfect dosas, the lentil crepe stuffed with different potato mixtures.  They also do excellent appetizers of iddly and vada.  The chole with puri is another excellent choice.    Everything comes with the very best coconut chutney and sambar.  We eat there with much regularity.  I hope you have the opportunity to try my two favorite Indian restaurants in the nabe.  Have fun!

not bad. not by any means. just totally middle of the road, not adventurous or interesting. the kind of indian food one might find in a suburb of anytown USA. since indian food isn't prepared one dish at a time like thai, asking for the spice/attitude to be ramped up isn't really as simple an option. maybe i'm missing something, but i look to south asian food to offer a little bit of a pop -- not necessarily heat qua heat, but something less than bland. it is a pretty room with really lovely service and like i said, it's not bad.
