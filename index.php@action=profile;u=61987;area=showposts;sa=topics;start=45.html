Neighborhood Chat / Should there be a cap on ride-hail vehicles?
« on: July 30, 2018, 10:51:21 AM »
New York City officials are moving to restrict the number of Uber and Lyft vehicles allowed on the road as part of a move to contain the massive growth in the for-hire vehicle industry that has been blamed for worsening congestion and low wages for drivers.

I'm all for less congestion on the roads, but I clearly remember a time when it was practically impossible to hail a yellow cab in the outerboroughs. The same applied when in Manhattan trying to get a yellow cab to take me home to Queens in the wee hours of the night. Companies like Uber and Lyft came around and made things easier for many. Now they say that there are just too many vehicles on the road, and the yellow cab industry is also suffering as a result.  Should there be a cap on ride-hail companies like Uber and Lyft in New York City? We're Fortunate in Jackson heights because we're well-served in terms of public transit, but what about the folks living in two-fare zones, or the disabled who are underserved by the MTA?

Uber and Lyft are facing a major crackdown in New York City

Restaurants & Food / Parva Bakeshop and Cafe
« on: July 18, 2018, 05:57:38 PM »
The family behind Seba Seba will be opening a new cafe on 82nd Street and Northern Blvd, scheduled to open in September.  According to the article, the new place will be located at the space formerly occupied by Conway's Funeral Home.

Cafe/Bakery to Open on Northern Boulevard by Owners of Seba Seba

David Velasquez, whose father and uncle established Seba Seba, said Parva will be separate.

He said that Parva will offer Colombian baked good similar to Seba Seba but will also sell traditional European items such as scones, croissants and bagels.

However, items such as scones and croissants will incorporate South American flavors. He said they include popular fruits such as passion-fruit, guava and soursop.

“We want to stand out. Anyone can offer a croissant, but not everyone can add our ingredients and make them the way we do,” Velasquez said.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Five candidates set to debate on June 21
« on: June 19, 2018, 12:30:28 PM »
The Queens Chronicle article is calling this event a debate, but it seems more like a forum:

Five candidates set to debate on June 21

Of the 10 combined Democratic candidates for governor, Congress, state Senate and state Assembly, five of them — four challengers and one incumbent — have confirmed they will attend the forum set for 7 p.m. on June 21 at the Jewish Center of Jackson Heights, located at 37-06 77 St. They include:

• Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, the Bronx progressive activist and opponent of Rep. Joe Crowley (D-Queens, Bronx);

• Jessica Ramos, a former aide to Mayor de Blasio who is taking on state Sen. Jose Peralta (D-East Elmhurst);

• Assemblywoman Ari Espinal (D-Jackson Heights);

• Catalina Cruz, former chief of staff for ex-Councilwoman Julissa Ferreras-Copeland, who is challenging the newly elected Espinal; and

• Yonel Sosa, president of the Association of Latin American Leaders, who is also challenging Espinal.

Crowley, Peralta, state Senate hopeful Moin Choudhury, Gov. Cuomo and gubernatorial candidate Cynthia Nixon were the other invitees.

Moderating the debate — co-sponsored by five borough community groups — is Chronicle Editor Christopher Barca.

The event will take place just five days before Crowley and Ocasio-Cortez face off in their Democratic primary race.

UPDATE:

This story has been updated to include the attendance of Assemblywoman Ari Espinal. Her office informed the Chronicle after press time that the lawmaker would be participating in the debate.
