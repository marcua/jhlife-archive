1. Would the punching bag hold?
2. does being on the top floor minimize the vibrations that my downstairs neighbors would feel?

I think you have two questions here, and each will likely require a different expert. First question is that it needs to hang in a way that it won't fall or destroy your ceiling, so I am guessing it would require someone to identify a joist or a beam that you can hang from and the joist or a beam would have to be strong enough to support the weight of the bag.

Second question has to do with sound and vibration transfer. This will likely require an acoustics and soundproofing expert- 1. they will need to figure out a way to decouple the connection point to the joist/beam tat the bag hangs from, because if not property decoupled, you will be sending vibration to the entire upper floor before it transfers itself to the lower floors. 2.- is the actual sound transfer. When you hit a bag, it usually produces a sound loaded with low end frequencies below 60hz. Much like a bass drum on a drum kit. That is the lowest and longest sound wave which is very difficult to stop from traveling through walls. And given that you will likely be doing some foot work, the floor below you will also take a beating.
