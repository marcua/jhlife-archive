Internationally acclaimed and chart topping Americana artist Jenny Van West kicks off a whirlwind 3 day/4 venue NYC tour this Thursday, 5/16 at Espresso 77.  Backed by a Queens-based band, the Outer Borough Boys (Jonny Meyers, Paul Trust and Seth Goldberg), this show promises one not to be missed!  Showtime is from 8:30-10:45PM.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / French Jazz at Espresso 77 Friday, May 10th
« on: May 09, 2019, 10:59:03 AM »
Born of Queens but with roots embedded in both African-American and European “gypsy” musical traditions, the Cold Club of Queens perform Jazz Manouche, or the Hot Club style of jazz popularized by guitarist Django Reinhardt and violinist Stephan Grapelli.  Founded in 1930’s France, this  unique sub-genre of Jazz is typified by driving guitars, fast licks and toe tapping melodies and grooves.  Come on out and experience the Cold Club of Queens’ distinct interpretations of jazz and Latin standards.  If you close your eyes and listen, you may find yourself whisked away to Parisian café from a bygone era.

https://www.facebook.com/events/285413925695389/?ti=ia

Restaurants & Food / The Death of Igloo Rolls?
« on: April 26, 2019, 07:21:25 PM »
Sad to see that Yelpers are reporting this place as permanently closed.  Anyone have confirmation on that? I’ve walked by a few times in the past couple weeks and yes, it hasn’t been opened but still looks like all the tables and gear are there.   I was hoping for a vacation situation.

It’s a shame, because they brought something new and exciting to the neighborhood. Never had the burgers, but I heard they were excellent. Certainly looked great.  The ice cream was very cool, my girls loved watching them make it.

It’s also a shame because two stores away the Middle Eastern restaurant also closed. I wasn’t completely blown away by them, but one has to wonder if there are limitations on what types of food can succeed in our neighborhood.
