Ok, went back to brunch this weekend at Novo to give it another shot.  On the positive side, the dishes tasted good, however two of the four of us found the food to be cold, like steak and ham at room temperature. 

I really wanted brunch to be a success, but it's obvious that they don't have their act together.  Started off being sat at a table with dirty linens on it.  The woman at the table next to us voiced to the server being upset her drink was taking too long.  By the time we were done I felt her pain.  Once I saw a server go out to get extra orange juice, and then 20 minutes later run out to get champagne.  Oh, and don't bother asking for coffee, they didn't have any!!!!  Ever hear of brunch not having coffee?  They said the machine was broken.  So, no champagne, orange juice or coffee.  Why bother having brunch? 

So they offer this all you can drink special for 20 dollars.   The drinks were waaaayyyy too strong.  Now I like a stiff drink, but it still needs to be drinkable.  We had to ask for a glass of tomato juice to water down our bloody mary's.  My friends who had the screwdrivers felt the same way.  I think the server wasn't really a bartender as well.  20 bucks for a meal and drinks is a good deal, but like the woman next to us we found it took a really long time to get the drinks.  They forgot our refill drink order once (mind you there were only two other tables seated at the time.  One server was busy texting in the corner while the other was chatting it up with a couple at the bar.)   and when we reminded the server, he brought over only two of the four drinks.  We reminded him about the other two and he said, "I wish I had four hands."  Dunno about you guys, but I've seen this wonderful invention called trays that can carry more than two drinks at once.  Just found it too be snide comment.  Three of the four of us had been in the food service industry for years, so poor customer service is a real big turnoff for me.  Not too mention dirty tables. 


Sorry Novo, I really want to like you..It could be such a success if it was managed right......How you stay afloat is beyond me.
