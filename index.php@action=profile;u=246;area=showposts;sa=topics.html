Neighborhood Chat / Pigeon Update and Fire Escape Legality
« on: April 19, 2009, 11:40:44 PM »
Hey folks!

So, about a year ago I posted in reply to someone's query about the Pigeon Problem that I thought I'd solved it with mothballs in socks, left out in nesting areas on my fire escape.

Alas, the pigeons came back and now the socks are convenient resting places. (And I have replaced them.) So, chalk that one up in the "doesn't work" column

I read the story in today's NY Times about the problems the phone company is having with parakeets nesting near transformers, so I realize this is not an isolated issue.

Anyway, I'm back to what to do about the pigeons. At the moment, we're at a stalemate: I throw away their nest, they build another one. (The goal: Not to let them create another generation of annoying coo-ers. There's just the two and they make enough noise that it wakes me up every morning.) But everything else I've thought of hasn't worked. (Note to all: Alka-Seltzer does not work, I haven't tried it and don't want to, but did research on the 'Net.)

The main issue is that they're on the fire escape. I don't know what legally you can even put on a fire escape (if anything). I'm tempted to get those spiky things that do seem to work, but again -- don't know that's really legal, even if it's not blocking egress (like on or near a stairway).

Does anyone know? The corollary to this is: If my putting stuff on the fire escape is illegal, can a birds nest be considered also a violation in some way -- a violation I can then turn over to my board or super?

Any thoughts are welcome. Thanks!
