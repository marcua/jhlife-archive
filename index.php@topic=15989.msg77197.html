Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Quote from: itsit on February 15, 2017, 08:37:34 AM
  Something that seems to rarely be done in JH development is to ask the people who are most likely to use the space what would help them in that neighborhood. No big town hall meetings but a survey with human contact to ask what people surrounding the area and using the commercial space would like/need the most. Yes, I am qualified to speak here living close by. These are a few thoughts for now. Agree that the proposed development is a behemoth and needs to be scaled back by two + floors at least. Above ground parking is a travesty. See the medical center on 72nd for their underground parking 2 blks away. The car wash does a good business some of the hours of the day but the space could be used in a much better way for the community 24/7. Am glad that this conversation is taking place though as the car wash and car dealer strip here seems more and more out of sync with the neighborhood and its needs.

  This particular area could use:
  - A decent sized grocery store - doesn't have to be huge but maybe like the Natural Foods store.
  - Not urgent but a decent shoe store would be great too. Others have mentioned a bread shop, cheese store specialties
  - No more medical offices as Northern now has lot's of them now and we have many areas of service covered. The facility   on 72nd and Northern is really great and provides excellent physical therapy, MRI and strength and bone related med services.
  - Not another pharmacy please! CVS does fine.
  - A new Queens Library with long lease is fantastic!!
  - There are plenty of restaurants too so not particularly needed really.
  - A senior center/rec center is urgently needed as we have loads of seniors and have a disproportionate amount of middle schoolers who could use activities indoors for those 200+ days when being outdoor for long is an issue.

Most of the things people want aren't economically feasible...they're stores/restaurants that are guaranteed not to do well, or they can't afford the rent in the first place. There's already a senior center on 75th & Broadway and there's tons all over the city...I doubt the city would infuse a ton of cash to have another one in the same neighborhood...same with a library. A rec center would be nice, but usually they're located slightly more out of the way from a central location most likely due to cost and the space required for one.

My mom goes to one in Chinatown that's pretty good. They have cheap meals, activities, day trips and such. One of my neighbors goes to one nearby in Queens...I believe in Sunnyside. There's a whole list of them on the Department for the Aging Website: https://a125-egovt.nyc.gov/egovt/services/service_result.cfm. I think a bigger issue is not the opening of new centers, but still having the funding to maintain the ones already in existence.

The only solution to the challenge you presented is to build a center across the street from every senior in Jackson Heights. There is no safe street in JH. 37th Ave, 35 Ave, 34th Ave, Northern Blvd., Broadway...all of those streets are just as much hurdles as Roosevelt, if not even more so, and you can say that for many streets in Queens. Anyone who is physically fragile needs some sort of caregiving provided either by themselves, family or Medicaid. Someone proposed a volunteer group or organization specifically directed for the elderly, which I think is potentially a good idea.

I do believe that some of the proposed items will eventually be done. The stuff like the cheese stores, a better library, etc. Perhaps not now, but down the line...just that it requires greater gentrification of the area, which should be speeded up by the L train shutdown, the Roosevelt Island tech campus, and the new 2nd Avenue line. I think if JH had greater rental stock, it probably would have happened already. I always like to say that once Bareburger comes to JH, then we'll have reached that point of no return

Community Board 3 Public Hearing was held on this proposal last Thursday. Well as of now we are dealing with a private developer whose main concern is profit. If CB3 and City Council approve this six- to eight-story structure that is completely out of character with the neighboring blocks on Northern Blvd near 75th St, then this could set a precedent for the blocks nearby.
