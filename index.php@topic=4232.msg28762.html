Daily News:

Expelled Sen. Hiram Monserrate boasted in the run-up to Tuesday's special election that he was operating an all-volunteer, grass-roots campaign.

It turns out the grass roots include almost his entire legislative staff, a paid consultant and a nonprofit that got city-funded video equipment from Monserrate, the Daily News found.

Records reveal 10 of Monserrate's 14 staffers and a paid campaign worker collected hundreds of the 3,000 signatures that got him on the ballot.

Another signature gatherer was Luis Jimenez, vice president of the nonprofit Latin American Talent Support, who used his Queens public TV show to attack Monserrate's rival, Jose Peralta.

Another signature collector runs a nonprofit group that has received taxpayer money from Monserrate. That harkens back to 2007, when the city Department of Investigation looked into whether workers at another nonprofit called Libre helped get him elected. No charges resulted.

This time the nonprofit is called Latin American Talent Support, which lists its address as the basement of the Corona home of Jimenez.

Reached by phone, Jimenez said he was the group's vice president and referred questions to its president, Cesar Camacho, who refused to discuss the issue.

Then-City Councilman Monserrate earmarked $5,000 in city funds for the group in 2008.

Agency e-mails show the city Department of Youth and Community Development let the group change how the money would be used after a late-2007 call from Monserrate's office. Under the grant, it was to be used to pay rent for a small office.
