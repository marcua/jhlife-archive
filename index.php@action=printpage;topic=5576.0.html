Post by: Griswold Contessa on November 23, 2010, 05:21:44 AM
  Thanksgiving happens to be one of my favorite holidays and being from the deep South I have some traditional dishes I serve every year.  I will admit that I have tweaked my mama's recipes to make them my own and thankfully she approves!  This year I will be smoking a turkey in a ceramic kamado grill using hickory chips for that wonderful smokey flavor along with rutabagas (yellow turnips), collard greens, macaroni and cheese, candied sweet potatoes, garlic mashed Yukon potatoes, cornbread dressing, giblet gravy, homemade cranberry sauce and for dessert we will have sweet tater pie and a sinfully rich black bottom pie.  A meal laden with carbs and mucho calories but what the hell it's only once a year!!  :smitten:

Well, my mom and dad were born in PR, I was born in the Bx but raised there until I was about 10 years old when I returned to NYC.  My Grandparents were either born or have roots in Spain, France (and on my dad's side his mom was born in Haiti but came to PR when she was like 4 or 5) - extreme mixture of races and nationalities is very common if You are from the Caribbean.  The empanada recipes differ from household to household.  In PR enpanadas, empanadillas are like fast (well, at least they were when I lived there...LOL).  I remember that my favorite was the empanadas which are much bigger than what we are used to here.  The ones you see here we call enpanadillas.  We don't make ones like the Colombians make with the stuffing with potato and shred meat and the corn-like shell.  My favorite was the crabmeat (made with blue crab) empanada or taco (nothing like the Mexican taco) followed by the pizza one which is made of mozzarella and a tomato paste like mixture with the pizza oregano and garlic powder.


We ended up making 2 types of empanadas...Ground beef with a sofrito base and recaito.  Sazon, salt to taste, cummin, pepper...after it's all done and cools off somewhat I added cranberry (instead of raisins) cracked walnuts, peas and carrots (from the can but drained and washed a bit) and diced up hard boiled eggs.  My grandma didn't use nuts or raisins regularly but she did during the holiday season.    I also made one with carne ripiada (very similar to ropa vieja or carne mechada with out any tomato products)  pressure cooked the chuck steak until it's shredded like, added peppers and onions and seasoned the same way I did the ground beef.  I didn't add any eggs or nuts to this one since my brother and his kid are allergic.
