Neighborhood Chat / Jackson Heights Resident, Acclaimed Concert Pianist Passes
« on: August 04, 2009, 03:04:24 AM »

Sarra Delfiner, the mother of New York Post reporter Rita Delfiner, died Saturday. She was 96.

Sarra Delfiner was born in Russia and was an acclaimed concert pianist in Vienna during the 1930s before leaving Austria for the United States in 1938.

She performed in an international piano competition in her homeland, finishing in second place.

Her favorite composer was Frederic Chopin, the 19th-century Polish virtuoso pianist.

Delfiner gave up serious piano playing to raise her family in New York.

She is also survived by Walter Delfiner, her husband of 71 years, her daughter, Tamara, and a brother, Grisha Arad.

A graveside funeral service is scheduled for today.

Source: NY Post

Neighborhood Chat / Vendor Uprising
« on: July 19, 2009, 09:46:11 PM »
While I am far from a supporter of Helen Sears, I do support he vendor-free zone partially.

I only believe in a modification, that defines a limit to vendors in certain areas.

A protest was held Thursday by vendors outraged by the communities feeling that the neighborhood is oversaturated, as seen on NY1

<http://ny1.com/content/top_stories/102427/proposal-to-limit-vendors-sparks-protest-in-jackson-heights/Default.aspx>

They have even went as far as requesting that more licenses be granted.

They also sight that a vendor free zone will change the character of the neighborhood.

My answer is, of course the character will change, for the better, when streets were not obstructed by vendors, and litter was not all over the place, as well as perpetually overflowing garbage cans.

Most business owners are being impacted as well, when food vendors set up in front of a dining establishment.

I fully believe that street vendors are negatively affecting our quality of life, and while extreme, Ms. Sears' proposition should be entertained.

Once again, this may be the only time I have ever agreed with the Councilwoman.
