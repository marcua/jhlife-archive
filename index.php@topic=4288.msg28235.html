I lived in one (35-16) for 9 years.  It's a nice small board, where everyone is a director, but there's also some negative to that in that EVERYONE is a director, if you catch my meaning.  As in you take turns being Secretary, Treasurer, et al.

The building I had was very nice.  Granted I lived on the first floor, right off the staircase, so the 'wlak up' aspect was not as much as factor for me as some.

The only shame is the lack of a usable backyard area.

We live on the west side of the street.  Some buildings have two apt's per floor, others have three.  We are also self-managed so we don't use a managing agent.  This is true co-op living, you will know your neighbors.  Luckily for us we love them -but the meetings can be a little difficult sometimes.  The buildings are very solid, I don't hear my neighbors, and the back garden is very quiet.  My Greystone building has a full time super and each night she collects trash via the dumb-waiter -very old-fashioned and convenient.  -It is truly one of the most beautiful blocks in NYC.  -As for the garden, I use it, and nobody has said anything.  I even went so far as to install a lovely wooden bench -and I'm hoping it inspires more garden usage.
