Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Okay - so yeah this is usually a red flag for Americans.... Me too. However just a little background information - the female owner used to be a sushi chef in Rhode Island, so I guess she took her experience there. You see this a lot of times where they try to class up the restaurant by adding in Japanese food, bc Japan is classy and all. I would have advised against it, but most of us know Phayul already from their other place and you can probably trust them to put out good food. I assume that they figure the new place is so big that they should serve so much other stuff... I have never run a successful restaurant so I don't know.


Remember Woodside Cafe - the chef used to work in an Italian restaurant in Manhattan, and he started with Italian dishes and pizza on the menu. Didn't last - BUT he now has fried momos in tomato sauce as an homage....
At Little Tibet - chef used to work in a Thai restaurant in India and in Queens. He has a thai noodle dish but its really difficult to even know that bc it doesnt say Thai in the menu I think and it fits in well.
Wasabi Point used to be Japanese - Tibetan , but then they eventually lost the Japanese part. Theyve  been promising me they are changing the name but no such luck. Great food though.
There was a Brazilian-Tibetan place in Woodside, but it's now Ottomanelli's burger.
