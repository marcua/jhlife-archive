Neighborhood Chat / Estimated ConEd Bills
« on: June 24, 2020, 01:20:21 PM »
I've been receiving estimated bills from ConEd since April. Originally the bills stated that this was due to safety precautions related to COVID-19, however my June invoice did not state this.

The estimates are based on the usage during the same month in the previous year.

Like most of us, I've been spending significantly more time at home and I'm likely using more energy. I'm not sure what to expect when a bill with an actual reading arrives.

Has everyone else been receiving estimated bills? Has anyone begun to receive bills with actual readings?

As many people have lost some or all of their income, I'm guessing there may be a very unwelcome surprise arriving from ConEd soon.

Neighborhood Chat / Donate Your Bike to an Essential Worker
« on: April 06, 2020, 06:08:07 PM »
I just heard about this on WNYC. If you have an unused bicycle that you'd like to donate to an essential worker, you can do so through Transportation Alternatives. For obvious reasons, people are avoiding public transportation and are looking for other ways to get around. If you are an essential worker looking for a bike, this is for you too.

Related article with more detail:

Real Estate & Home Improvement / How will COVID-19 Affect the Real Estate Market?
« on: March 23, 2020, 11:56:55 PM »
There's a lot of speculation about how the real estate market will be impacted by COVID-19. Personally, I think it will be impacted, but it will take some time for that to be clear. Real estate is slower to react than the stock market.

I think this has potential to drastically change both the housing market and the commercial market. So many of our small businesses will be hurting soon, if they are not already.

Here's a recent article on this topic:

https://ny.curbed.com/2020/3/10/21173411/coronavirus-new-york-real-estate-home-prices-covid-19

What do you think? How will it impact Jackson Heights?

Neighborhood Chat / Bioswales in JH
« on: May 16, 2015, 04:06:11 PM »
I noticed some green markings on the sidewalks on 75th Street between 37th Road and 37th Ave a while back similar to the markings that are made on the sidewalks when a street tree is going to be planted. They've mostly faded over the winter however, I more recently noticed some patched concrete in those areas. Now there are several green markings on the sidewalks on 76th between 37th and 35th Aves. I think we may be seeing some bioswales in these areas soon. This brochure details the process which seems to match what I've observed: http://on.nyc.gov/144I79E

Has anyone else noticed these anywhere or have any additional information?

If this is what is happening, I think this is great. Anything that adds more green space to JH and reduces water pollution is more than welcome.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Tree Giveaway - Jackson Heights
« on: April 02, 2015, 11:45:03 PM »
It's that time of year again!

Sunday, April 12, 10:00am-12:00pm
Location: JHSCRAPS, Corner of 69th St. & 35th Ave., Jackson Heights, Queens NY 11372
Host: Jackson Heights Beautification Group
Description: Pick up your free tree! In partnership with local community organizations, NYRP is giving away trees throughout NYC this spring.

To pick up your free tree, you must agree to:
•plant in one of the five boroughs.
•keep trees properly watered and maintained.
•plant your tree in the ground of your yard and NOT along streets, in city parks, in containers, terraces, balconies or on roofs.

Registration will be posted no earlier than three weeks before a giveaway date. If registration is closed, a limited quantity of trees will be available on a first come, first served basis.

Register here: https://www.nyrp.org/green-spaces/events/tree-giveaway-jackson-heights-beautification-group/

Trees available at this JH Tree Giveaway: Serviceberry, Paw Paw, Cornelian Cherry Dogwood, and Eastern Redbud.

Neighborhood Chat / PlaNYC Survey
« on: March 14, 2015, 10:58:10 AM »


Every four years, New York City updates PlaNYC to make sure that they are building toward a future that's resilient, sustainable, and makes sense for the lives of all New Yorkers.

This year, Mayor Bill de Blasio is making equity a lynchpin of PlaNYC -- and they have made the process more inclusive than ever before. Every New Yorker has a voice in this plan and can participate in building our city's future.

Please take a moment to take the survey: http://www1.nyc.gov/nyc-resources/planyc-survey.page

FYI - There's only one question: "How would you fill in the blank below? Go ahead and write as much as you want in the box.
One way to create a better New York City is to _____________."
