Community Groups, Activities & Events / Important Public Meeting on Queens Sewage Systems and Green Infrastructure
« on: January 10, 2016, 04:48:35 PM »
I just wanted to make sure everyone was aware of this upcoming public meeting on the City's plans to address the very serious issue of storm-related sewage runoff--especially since it affects one of the parks that we go to when our garden area in Jackson Heights doesn't feel like enough green for us.

The subject of the meeting is the relatively sexy one of green infrastructure plus the very unsexy but really important issue of overflowing sewage after storms.

Basically, every time there's a lot of rain, the sewage systems get overwhelmed with water and that makes it impossible to deal with all of the sewage.The result is that a bunch of it gets pushed out into the waterways. 

In Queens, this affects Alley Pond Park in particular--where the City's plan to fix the problem is to literally dump chlorine into an area that's been designated as a Significant Fish and Wildlife Habitat.
Other places in Queens affected by this plan include:
  • Flushing Creek
  • Newtown Creek
  • Jamaica Bay and its tributaries
More info below and attached.
Hope to see you all there!

WHAT: On the Citywide Long Term Control Plans Green Infrastructure & Municipal Separate Storm Sewer Systems
WHEN: Tuesday, January 12, 2016, 6:00 pm to 8:00 pm
WHERE:
LaGuardia Community College, Building E - Poolside Cafe
31-10 Thomson Ave, Long Island City, NY 11101

Summary
When there are heavy rains and the sewer system is at full capacity, a diluted mixture of rain water and sewage, also known as combined sewage, may overflow into local waterways as a combined sewer overflow (CSO). The New York City Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) is developing a CSO Long Term Control Plans (LTCP) that will identify and evaluate alternatives to improve the water quality around the New York City. DEP will provide a status update on the LTCP Program along with updates on the Green Infrastructure and Municipal Storm Sewer Systems (MS4) Programs.

Join us as we seek your input in developing this plan.
