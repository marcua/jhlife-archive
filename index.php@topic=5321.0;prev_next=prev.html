News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

We already know that we live in the most culturally diverse neighborhood. I suppose what some people, myself included, find distgusting is acceptable behavior for others.

What I don't get is the lack of consciousness. The bottom line is that we all live or work here. And if the neighborhood is going to continue to be a nice place to be then it's up to each individual to act and behave responsibly. People in general these days seem to act at times like they are the only one around and their immediate needs are all that are important.

The following illustration may be a little off topic but in what I think is the same self centered attitude. The other day I was trying to get down the stairs at the Roosevelt Terminal. On the steps between street level and the mezzanine was a young woman just standing there at a very busy time of the day texting on her cell phone. Probably she couldn't get reception at the bottom of the stairs. But she was just a few steps away from getting out of everyone's way where she could have continued what she was doing. People were climbing over eachother to get around her.

I'm sure we all have examples of similar enough situations. Is it me or am I just plain old fashioned?
