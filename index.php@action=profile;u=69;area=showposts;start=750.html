Restaurants & Food / 24 hour pizza pastry shop
« on: February 24, 2008, 12:50:26 PM »
I noticed as I walked down 37th Avenue today that there is a new pizza/pastry (yes another 2 in 1)store about to open in the 80"s.  What worries me is the "OPEN 24 HOURS" sign posted.  Traditionally, all the stores along 37th Ave. are closed by 10:30---11 the latest.  There are many apartment buildings that line that  strip, and in th evening brings a quiet, residential  "welcome home" feeling. Bringing people at all hours of the night can't be a good thing.  I live on 86th Street and on the weekends in particular, often get the spill over from Roosevelt Avenue and more recently from Northern. Cars parked blocking private driveways, loud,  mostly drunken people urinating on the bushes , swearing,car horns and music blaring can often be heard at all hours of the night.  While 37th Avenue maintains a rather small town charm, the advent of a 24 hour establishment doesn't seem to benefit our community. I was wondering whether or not this is going to be a new trend or if in fact it is not permissible. Does the community board have to approve this?

Restaurants & Food / Re: Possible French bistro in Jackson Heights?
« on: February 13, 2008, 09:27:00 PM »
I met the guy who was suppose to open up a French Bistro on 37th Ave. He was a really nice guy who was the owner and chef at Park Bistro,  who for the francophiles among us know, was a three star French Bistro. They sold the building where he was located and after looking in JH, opened up 2 great places in the city.  Moon Pie Pizzeria on Ave C and East 3rd and 169 Sullivan (a chic and  romantic little American diner place at the same address). I work close by and had dinner at Sullivan Street one night and got to talking to the owner. His name is Rich and I subsequently took my family to the Pizza place.  Both places may I add were incredibly good, family friendly and quite reasonable!!  When I told him I lived in Jackson Heights,  he gave my wife and myself  a free glass of wine.  He also said that he may still consider doing the Bistro thing in the future if an affordable place came up. He's a real Jackson Heights-o-phile who knew a lot about the hood and says he has a great garden party every year in his backyard with great food and wine!   I'm keeping my fingers crossed!!!!
