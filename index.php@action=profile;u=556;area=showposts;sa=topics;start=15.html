Community Groups, Activities & Events / Free Parenting Workshop in Spanish @ Queens College April 19 6:30
« on: April 11, 2016, 03:47:24 PM »
Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) has created a  parenting workshop entitled: “Open the Door to a Better Future for Your Adolescent,”   specifically for Hispanic families. It is being offered in Spanish and Free on April 19, 2016 at 6:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.  Childcare will be provided for children ages 3 and above. 

The workshop will focus on the challenges of parenting adolescent children using practical, scientifically validated and culturally mindful strategies to manage behavioral, academic, and interpersonal difficulties.  Specific topics will include “How To:”
•   Better manage your adolescent’s behaviors
•   Communicate effectively in a way that reaches your adolescent
•   Use your authority effectively
•   Help your teen to understand & value their cultural identity 
•   Support your teen’s dreams and aspirations
•   Help your teen succeed in today’s world
 
Participants will have the opportunity to ask questions as well. 

For more information or to reserve a seat, community members may call 718.570.0358.  When calling to reserve a seat please indicate name, telephone number and number of seats requested. (Walk-ins will be accepted if space allows). Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) is located at the Queens College Campus 65-21 Main Street Flushing, New York 11367 Suite 169.  Visit us on the web www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC   (Please see the Queens College website for directions www.qc.cuny.edu/about/directions )

Attached are flyers in English and Spanish for distribution or reference.  Please share this information with anyone who may possibly benefit from it.
Thank you for your help.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / QCPC BrainFit: FREE Depression Treatment Program @ Queens College
« on: January 31, 2016, 11:02:57 PM »
Please share this with anyone who may benefit from this information.  Thank you.

As part of their Community BrainFit Program, Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) is currently offering a free Depression Treatment Program.  Stress and Depression can drain your energy, hope, and productivity, and affect your relationships with those you love.  If you have mild depression that isn't improving, or you have moderate depression, CBT treatment may be very helpful to you.

Cognitive Behavior Therapy (CBT) recognizes that past events may have shaped you, but it concentrates mostly on how you can change the way you think, feel and behave in the present. It helps you understand your thoughts and behavior and how they affect you.  A screening is required, and if appropriate for CBT, you would then be eligible to receive 8-12 individual sessions at no fee. All QCPC programs are based on extensive research, and adopt a collaborative stance that encourages you to work on changing how you feel by putting into practice what you have learned.

For more information regarding the QCPC Depression Program, please email your contact information (phone number where you may be reached, best time to call you, and if it is okay to leave a message) to: QCPC@qc.cuny.edu or call 718-570-0500. Visit us at www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC Please share this with anyone who may benefit from this information.  Thank you.

Queens College Psychological Center is located on the Queens College Campus at 65-21 Main Street, Flushing, Queens.  For more information please call at: 718-570-0500 or email at QCPC@qc.cuny.edu Visit us at http: www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC The Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) offers compassionate, high quality, evidence-based, psychological services for children, adolescents, and adults. Treatment is provided by doctoral candidates in the Clinical Psychology at Queens College Doctoral Program who are closely supervised by experienced and licensed faculty. All services are confidential and available to the community regardless of ability to pay. Also available at QCPC: Individual and group psychotherapy for adults & children for a wide range of symptoms & behaviors.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / FREE Wellness Program for Immigrant Youth @ Queens College
« on: November 20, 2015, 10:13:24 AM »
Please share this information with anyone who may possibly benefit from it. Thank you.

I would like to draw your attention to a new FREE program for immigrant youth and their families: Youth In Transition (YIT). Queens College Psychological Center is offering culturally-sensitive, trauma-focused individual psychotherapy to immigrant youth (ages 12 to 21) and their families. Services are geared towards refugee adolescents who may be new to the U.S., and are experiencing stress, or difficulty adjusting.  This may include academic, occupational, or interpersonal difficulties, and a range of symptoms such as PTSD, depression, or anxiety.   A screening is required, and if appropriate all session would be offered at no fee. Our clinicians for this program speak English and Spanish. The YIT program may be reached directly at 718-570-0358.  It is located on the Queens College Campus, Queens College Psychological Center, Flushing New York
 
Other Services: QCPC's mission is to bring treatment programs based on extensive research to benefit the community directly. This community mental health service is a training and research center operated by the Ph.D. program in clinical psychology. For more information regarding our Psychological Wellness Programs, or Psychotherapy Services, please email your contact information (phone number where you may be reached, best time to call you, and if it is okay to leave a message) to: QCPC@qc.cuny.edu or call 718-570-0500. Visit us at www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC Queens College Psychological Center is located on the Queens College Campus at 65-21 Main Street, Flushing, Queens.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Children's Anxiety Group @ Queens College
« on: November 17, 2015, 08:50:09 AM »
Please share this with anyone who may benefit from this information.
 
Dear Community Member:

Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) is offering a Children's Anxiety Group for children ages 6 to 10, with unwanted fears, worries, and shyness. Facing Anxiety with Courage & Empowerment (FACE) creatively incorporates Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, psychoeducation & practice. This program is based on extensive research, with the goal of helping children learn (in a supportive and encouraging environment) to cope more effectively with anxiety. The group activities will help provide children with tools to face stressful situations more courageously.
 
The group is expected to start January 2016 and run on Thursdays from 4:30pm – 6:00pm for 16 sessions. Interested participants will be offered a brief phone screening followed by an in person interview to ensure that your child’s needs can be best met by this group. As a community service, most of the cost will be subsidized by the college, such that the fee for participation will be $95.00 (this includes the application, group screening, child group sessions, and parenting sessions). The fee may be further reduced in the case of financial need. No one will be turned away for inability to pay.
 
For more information regarding our children's anxiety group- Facing Anxiety with Courage & Empowerment (FACE), Psychological Wellness Programs, or Psychotherapy Services, please email your contact information (phone number where you may be reached, best time to call you, and if it is okay to leave a message) to: QCPC@qc.cuny.edu or call 718-570-0500. Visit us at www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC Queens College Psychological Center is located on the Queens College Campus at 65-21 Main Street, Flushing, Queens.
 

Other Services: QCPC's mission is to bring treatment programs based on extensive research to benefit the community directly. The clinic continues to offer treatment for problems such as depression, stress, anxiety, interpersonal/relational issues, dissatisfaction with current personal or interpersonal life choices, grief and bereavement, coping with medical illness or disability, trauma, self-esteem and body image concerns, parenting issues and adjustment to changing life circumstances. For children, typical problems may include separation anxiety, school refusal, social anxiety, fears, phobias, social skills deficits, Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), tantrums, aggression, depression, moodiness, impulsivity, defiance and disobedience.  The clinic team works closely with our child clients’ schools and does school visits when necessary. Fees for these services are based on a sliding scale from zero to fifty dollars (average fee is $10) that takes into account personal and/or family income and circumstances. This community mental health service is a training and research center operated by the Ph.D. program in clinical psychology. Advanced doctoral candidates in the college’s Clinical Psychology doctoral program provide treatment to patients under the close supervision of experienced, licensed psychologists on the Queens College faculty.
