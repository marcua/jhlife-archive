Neighborhood Chat / Safety and Learning for UPK Students
« on: September 23, 2014, 07:05:47 AM »
Every organization is struggling in September to accommodate the tremendous growth of Universal PreK. At 82nd Street Academics, we hired several teachers, assistants, and aides for the new school year. Our Orientation for New Staff class this Thursday will be the largest in our history.

What protects quality at these times? I believe it’s a staff to student ratio of 1:8 or lower. It protects children when one child has to be escorted to the bathroom or when staff take breaks. It allows for grouping of students with similar developmental levels for individualized instruction.

While the Jackson Heights schools are full, 82nd Street Academics has 8 spaces left at Highland Academics in Jamaica. The building is incredible, the class furniture is new, and we have 3 staff per room with 20 students. If you are still looking around, the difference may be worth the drive. Call 718 457 0429 for  pictures of the classrooms.

Neighborhood Chat / A Note to Our Community From 82nd Street Academics
« on: September 17, 2011, 08:57:19 AM »
A disgruntled former employee of 82nd Street Academics has made an amazing array of serious charges about 82nd Street Academics in The Queens Courier and has continued to embellish his original claims on several websites and blogs.  Although I've been touched by the many supportive comments of current and past families, I'm happy to say more about the questions raised.

A. Our licenses to operate programs are based on regular inspections by the Department of Health, the Department of Education, and other state and city agencies. We have posted a link to all the Department of Health inspections of the building on our web site www.82ndst.com. The inspectors go to kitchen facilities, examine refrigerators, examine our food safety certification, look to see whether or not there is paint chipping, bathroom maintenance and they have done this ever since we started. None of Mr. Dunn's claims are true. We certainly have to be vigilant about mice as do many of the co ops in this neighborhood. Orkin Pest Control works with the school and church and Mr. Dunn certainly helped with this too.

We are also happy to offer a building tour to any concerned parent. Please email me at Ronald.Tompkins@... for an appointment and we will go to the kitchen, bathrooms, and classrooms. The Queens Courier is sending their reporter to tour our school as well.

B. I will state for the record that Mr. Dunn was fully paid all wages due to him upon termination and he was paid no additional money after his and his demonstrators showed up in August. As you can imagine, I am constrained on what I can and cannot say about former employees. I therefore cannot elaborate
beyond these simple facts.

C. As every employee of 82nd Street Academics knows, we have a whistleblower policy so if Mr. Dunn or anyone else witnessed were something unsafe, unethical, or illegal, they would have the fullest of protections. I'm struck in both articles by how Mr. Dunn avoids the easiest of remedies that would examine these charges and resorts to forums such as this one where claims are easy and proof is unneeded.

These articles have triggered a number of questions from parents and friends. I welcome your thoughts.
