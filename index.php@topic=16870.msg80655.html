Rhinebeck is lovely, there is a fair grounds there where there are lots of great festivals--it may be worth coinciding your trip with one of them. Beacon is great, and the Dia is a really wonderful museum reachable by public transportation. If you are into hiking I also recommend Coldspring. There are some fun hiking trails you can get to from the train station and the town itself is really nice and has some good places to eat and shop.

I also suggest the North Fork of Long Island.  Greenport is the main "hub" of the area and has a walkable downtown area near the docks.

There are lots of wineries and it's prime farm stand season. It's not as fancy as the Hamptons; it's much more family-oriented and there are beaches everywhere.

I grew up in Cutchogue and would love to be able to live there and commute each day but it is WAY too far.

Greenport/orient - New England feel, cycling, winery, food, beach
Phonecia - Catskill hip town, camp, bed and b, hiking, cycle, tubing
Cold springs/fahnestock park - cute town,  winter cross country ski and snowshoe
Hyde park - history Vanderbilt and Roosevelt mansion/gardens, light hiking
Delaware water gap - canoe or tubing, beautiful water and nature. Can overnight with canoe
Fire island - ferry, no cars. True ocean beach

I've been to the F.I. Pines and to Cherry Grove;  I know that there are many other communities on F.I., so read up on which one you want to go to.  Other than the drive itself, which really isn't bad on a Saturday morning, but is hell on a Friday after work, it's a good relaxing day trip.  All you need is a beach towel, bathing suit and some flip flops.  The ferry will take you to the bay side of most of the F.I. communities, and from there it's an ever short walk to the beach side.  When you get hungry, head into town.

I did hear that one community prohibits any colored beverages on the beach - even non-alcoholic ones.  I'd avoid that one.  I think that's Ocean Beach.
