I think the increase of police presence may have to do with the "groper" still at large. There was even a recent piece on NY1  with Danny Dromm canvassing the area with the police. I'm happy to see the police.....well, sort of, although I don't think it will do much good to stop the kinds of crimes that go on behind closed doors (ie. sex trafficking, drugs, prostitution, car vandalism). Certainly car breakins are a problem for the people who live here, but that occurs in the wee small hours of the morning when there is one cop car for the entire sector. Under cover cops in strategic locations where the frequency of crime is high would work better and be more effective without the visible "police state" vibe. In addition a multi agency task force needs to go in and address the serious problems. When you see so many police all at once it's like overkill and makes me feel like I'm in a real unsafe place. A bandaide approach to a cancerous problem. I have never felt unsafe living here.
