This is a bit late, but I wanted to take a moment to congratulate and thank Danny Dromm on another issue as well.

Streetsblog has given Dromm its "Elected Official of 2013" award for his tireless work trying to make the streets in our community safer and more livable:
http://www.streetsblog.org/2013/12/31/the-2013-nyc-streetsies-part-2/
Their brief write-up mentions the Play Street and Diversity Plaza.  And it quotes Dromm as saying that in the future, he "plans to focus on transforming Northern Boulevard, Roosevelt Avenue and Broadway into complete streets" -- i.e. streets that safely meet the needs of all users, not just cars. 

But even this understates all he has been doing on this issue.  Just in the past month, he spoke at the Pedestrian Injury Summit at Elmhurst Hospital about the importance of Slow Zones and Safe Routes to Schools projects.  He and his staff have also been very supportive of Transportation Alternatives' advocacy for a safer Queens Boulevard, as well as the new Make Queens Safer movement starting up here in Jackson Heights and elsewhere around the borough.

So, kudos Councilmember Dromm on the award from Streetblog, and thank you for your work on this extremely important issue!
