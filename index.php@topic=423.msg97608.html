How to improve 65th Street?
« on: February 05, 2008, 05:47:11 PM »
The east side of 65th Street, between 35th Avenue and Broadway, is an absolute eyesore.  It's basically a garbage-strewn lot next to the highway.  I walk past it quite frequently on my way to Spicy Mina or the 65th Street Playground, and really wish that space could be put to better use.

Does anybody know about any plans for this space?  It looks like some effort is being make to make the Broadway intersection look nicer, but so far the area along 65th Street has been neglected.  I imagine it might be state-owned, which might complicate matters.

It seems to me like this would be a perfect location for a dog run.  Does anybody have other ideas?

 There has been huge improvement actually. There is a dog run which is very well run and a composting center that processes food scraps collected there every weekend. Last year there was a concerted effort by JHBG led by Nuala ODoherty to help the homeless enclave on 65th and clean years of trash out of there. It looks MUCH better than before but as noted, there is still a long way to go. And as suspected, there are issues with who owns the land - city, state, train authority, DOT- are all pieced together there. Very complicated to make change on that street but it really has seen improvement in recent years.
