To be honest, the prices in JH have gone up too fast, but now that I've purchased, I need that to continue. My wife and I have been searching and started with LIC and Astoria. Quickly realized we were late to the party and were priced out. I hear Sunnyside is also pretty frothy (at least Astoria and LIC have stuff to do), but I'm not a fan.

So the next "neighborhood" place was JH historic district. Stuff is getting snatched up so fast in JH - it's crazy.  We were outbid a couple of times. From what I understand Carlton House is a very well run building, good tenants, underground parking, and nicely renovated units.

IMO, the only downside of CH is the hike to the train. Fine if you work in midtown, but if you're down in FiDi, it's almost an hour door-to-door. Ultimately, CH had all the right stuff and the walk to the train is our tradeoff.

People may be willing to pay more for a sponsor unit, and especially a fully renovated sponsor unit.  I imagine there is some percentage of buyers who can't pass a board, due to being self-employed with unpredictable income (or many other reasons), so a sponsor unit is the only thing they will be able to get. 

Also -- regarding the price range of the studios:  maybe the sponsor won't be able to get $259K for a studio and it will sell for less, but they may be seeking this target population of buyers who need/want a renovated sponsor unit and only have $200-$250K budgeted for the apartment.  If you're a buyer and that's what you're looking for, your choices would be very slim and you might go straight over to that $259K studio open house for a look. 

The Carlton's high asking prices did seem surprising 6 months to a year ago, but prices in many other buildings have been catching up.  I think it resulted from a combination of availability of sponsor units, good renovations, seller's market, savvy realtors and possibly the chance to get a parking space with the unit.  Just a guess.
