Neighborhood Chat / Re: 73rd Street nightmare
« on: December 26, 2012, 08:17:01 PM »
By normal I mean a reasonable amount of noise generated by traffic is acceptable. The situation on 73rd street is not a result of the expected traffic densities of a big city. 73rd st is zoned residential, yet there has been no effort to curtail the rampant increase in commercial activities. The street width is unable to handle the vehicle per hour numbers that are currently using it. If one looks at the street widths of say the commercial districts of midtown Manhattan, or Greenpoint Brooklyn, you will find them to be significantly wider than 73rd st. It's narrowness, the high number of double parking vehicles and the density of pedestrian and vehicular traffic, combined with a aforementioned unbridled commercial development has created an almost constant degree of noise pollution due to horn honking.
This isn't the quiet back street of Canarsie we're living on, but it's not the entrance to the Lincoln Tunnel either.

However all that is beside the point. Why do you give a crap anyway, do you get paid per honk or something?
