Citi Bike share program - let's bring it to JH!
« on: June 30, 2012, 08:10:16 PM »
Hi Neighbors,

As I'm sure many of you know, the DOT is launching a new bike share program beginning in July, which will place bike rental stations in locations all over Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens. The idea is to encourage bicycling for short trips, especially to work, to relieve congestion on mass transit. For anyone who has ever considered biking to work, but hasn't wanted to make the commitment of investing in a bike for fear they won't like it, this is a great opportunity to try out cycling before making that big purchase.

Sadly, Jackson Heights is not on the map of proposed bike station locations. However, the DOT has a page where people can submit requests for additional locations, and I think Jackson Heights residents could greatly benefit from getting a bike station or two. Here is a link to the form:

http://a841-tfpweb.nyc.gov/bikeshare/contact/

Please pass this along to anyone you know in the neighborhood who might be interested in affordable cycling, and have them submit requests as well.

To learn more about the bikes, rates, and to see the current station map, go here: http://citibikenyc.com/

Thanks!
Erica

I, too, am worried about the potential for accidents especially since the city is not providing bike helmets.  Someone recently said that since NYC is more safe for bikes now the need for helmets has diminished.  Riiight.  

Let's clarify this gross misconception and reliance that any decisions at all regarding bicycling should even remotely rest on the usage (or non) of helmets. All a helmet is is a oddly-fitting piece of Styrofoam strapped to your head. Yes, they might help when you land on your head. But that's it. They do not decrease the amount of accidents. The effectiveness of helmets in preventing injury when a serious collision does occur is grossly exaggerated (the helmet manufacturers associations have done a great job at taking all government lobbying cues from baby car seat manufacturers).

All this being said, Jackson Heights (and other parts of Western Queens and Brooklyn not already included) will more than likely be included but it will take some time. The critical mass of riders needed to demonstrate the success of this program will come from the areas have already been selected.
