Seems like part of the real problem would be suckers that didn't take a walk around the interior garden before making an offer or buying.

Quote
"For the same price of an apartment in this neighborhood, you can find equivalent, in terms of size/quality, apartments in quite parts of the city and/or suburbs."

Actually, I've lived in my apartment for over 15 years and this is not true. I paid $100K for my apartment. Not available elsewhere, so I'm stuck.

Your apartment is probably worth well over $300K by now, which roughly means you've turned a down payment of $20k (assuming the standard coop requirement of 20%) in to $220K. Check out Realtor.com, and you'll see for example in Ossining, NY (a town with a MetroNorth station) 3 bedrooms starting at $145K and going up to $379K. Fifteen years and you might not have a mortgage at this point, but I suspect there is more flexibility there than you realize. The questions is, do you want to live in a place like Ossining?
