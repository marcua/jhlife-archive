Neighborhood Chat / Re: Voting in Jackson Heights
« on: February 21, 2008, 06:16:07 PM »
Of course you would be able to vote for either party in the general election, but to vote in the primary in NY you have to be registered for the party that you are voting for. If you are "no party" or independent, you are unable to vote for anyone in a primary (registered republicans vote for the republican candidate, registered democrats vote for the democratic candidate), but anyone can vote for anybody in a general election.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Graffiti
« on: February 14, 2008, 04:33:20 PM »
Glass etching cream has a ph of less than 7 (I think the ph of Ammonium Biflouride, which is found in most commercially available glass etching creams, has a ph of ~2) which makes it an acid. When using glass etching cream, people should be in a well ventilated area, and avoid prolonged contact with the skin because you can get serious burns. This type of graffiti is destructive, and a common concern in urban areas, and there is really no need to be condescending.

Here's some more information:
http://www.city.vancouver.bc.ca/engsvcs/streets/graffiti/acid_etching.htm

Restaurants & Food / Re: Cooking For One
« on: January 24, 2008, 10:50:02 PM »
The easiest meal I make for my husband and myself, though it is easily portioned down to 1 or up to 4:

-as many salmon fillets as you want to eat (costco sells them individually vacuum sealed and frozen, which while not as lovely and fresh tasting, is exceptionally easy)

-pesto (homemade, from a jar, whatever!)

Salt and pepper the salmon and coat with pesto. Bake for ~12 minutes (or until fish flakes easily when pulled at with a fork) at 375.

I side that with some green beans or asparagus and some rice. Salmon is my go-to for quick and easy with little or no prep.

For a more asian style recipe, I like this and sometimes instead of the mustard, I marinade the fish in teriyaki sauce and add a layer of wasabi spread to hold on the panko, though that might be a little spicy (not nearly as potent as wasabi is normally though!). They sell that at Walbaums.
