Title: A Question about Open Table
Post by: Stew on August 30, 2015, 11:57:31 PM
I have to decide whether Swim Two Birds should use Open Table to book reservations.

As you may know, Open Table charges a monthly fee plus $1 per guest booked through its site and 25 cents booked through our restaurant's site.

This adds up to a great deal of money over a year and the cost is passed on to guests. It also results in restaurants showing, on Open Table, that their restaurant is fully booked, when it isn't, to avoid paying Open Table's $1 per person fee.

So a couple of questions...

What would be your reaction if we used reservation software that didn't involve a per diner fee?

What is your view of Yelp generally and in particular of its reservation software SeatMe?

For background, Open Table has about 1740 restaurants in Manhattan, 178 in Brooklyn, 90 in Queens and five in Jackson Heights.

Thanks,

Stew

Stew,

I use open table when it is linked through the restaurant's website, but I have no particular loyalty to open table as a platform/service. Online reservations are convenient, but I think as long as you make it clear and easy to access from your restaurant's website, any service will do (I don't use opentable to browse restaurants; I don't find their restaurant selection to be *that* great. I'd rather choose where I want to eat and go from there).

I've never used yelp's seatme app. As far as reviews, I absolutely take them with a grain of salt, but checking yelp is a useful way to quickly get a read on a restaurant, and if I see a whole slew of negative reviews, it does give me pause before deciding to dine somewhere.

Very excited for your restaurant to open!

Wallaby has it.People (that I know) tend not to care about opentable vs seatme vs something else as long as it *works*. I don't know how many reservations are driven FROM opentable as opposed to TO opentable from other links like yelp, or websites or google searches.

While we're talking about technology

Lots of restaurants mess up their websites.  The website's front page should work on mobiles as well as laptop/pc browsers, and on this front page it should have
**in searchable text**, (not as a graphic)

i) opening hours,
ii) reservation link - clickable to whichever service works for you AND works on mobile platforms well
iii) address - which is a clickable link to google maps
iv) phone - clickable link to make the phonecall from a cellphone
v) A link to a menu in TEXT that is readable on a cellphone and pc.

Also, please resist the idea of making a super high end front page website that crashes on older systems or phones; I and others have aborted attempts to go to restaurants at last minute because their website simply doesn't work and I can't find info (badly formatted pdfs that cant be read on phones, menus in flash, stupid scrolling effects etc etc etc)! 

Clean, simple, quick loading FUNCTIONAL on as many platforms as possible. (bury the beautifully designed/resource intensive animations/pictures/social stuff behind the front page if you must).

It's an open secret that many of the restaurants that use Open Table disable it for peak hours.

In other words, they take reservations for peak hours by other means (e.g. phone and e-mail).

Of course, this defeats the whole point of using Open Table or any on-line reservation system. So why does a restaurant sign up? Open Table is essentially a marketing platform, and an expensive one, and in Manhattan it isn't really optional if one accepts Open Table's argument that people search for, and select, restaurants via Open Table searches. Outside Manhattan, its footprint, and therefore power, is much less.
