I'm a native JH person. Once I was a young parent with a young child in a co-op where there were rules about no playing on the grass. I and a group of other young parents tried to have the rules modified, but to no avail. Children are allowed in the gardens with supervision, but not allowed to play on the grass. This is our group's rules.I don't know of any children who have been permanently traumatized by running around the sidewalks instead of the grass.  Other co-ops have different rules.

Now I am a grandmother in JH, still living in the same apt. Have I automatically become 'them'? No. And I don't want to become demonized because I'm not of the younger generation. I was once, remember?

Two things should be noted.
1.
When people are interviewed and interview the boards for their acceptance, they are given the rules for the co-op. By purchasing, they have agreed to the rules. Perhaps this was before they had any children. Why should the rules change now that they are parents.

2.
The gardens aren't 'museums', they are gardens. Maintained at great expense every year. They are investments. Ask some 'old timer' about the Towers gardens before they were rehabilitated. Ask someone at St. Mark's about the cost in replacing the lawn where the JHELC children play.

It isn't easy. Parents have to supervise their children; children can be taught to appreciate the gardens. The key here is responsible behavior.

Please don't flame me! In fairness I haven't watched the video clips, only read the text of the article.
