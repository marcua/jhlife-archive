Please keep in mind that when Met Food first leased this space, they could have made the facade a lot glitzier... other Met Foods had mirrored storefronts at the time.  And while outside of the Historic District (and its rules and regulations), they did a relatively subdued job with their brick facade then and now.  They also took care of the trees in front of their store.

The recent changes pale in comparison to what has been taking place directly across the street in a blockfront adjacent to Hawthorne Court that should have been landmarked back in 1993.  The huge projecting illuminating signs/awnings from the place that replaced Mefhil, along with the pizza place, pharmacy, etc. are much, much worse that what Met Food has done. 

And as I always advise, vote with your dollars.... patronize only those businesses that respect our community.... those that don't install huge, out of scale, out of character, illuminated signs and awnings that crapify our quality of life.  Personally, I shop at Met Food since they never installed an illegal outbuilding, nor do they block the street with vegetables... like their competitor to the northwest.
