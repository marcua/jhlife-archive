Appliances - broken stove - not replaced
« on: August 14, 2014, 06:50:44 AM »
Neighbors, help me out. We  had a small kitchen fire (fully contained within the stove so no major damage) on Sunday morning. The landlord has not yet replaced the stove. Every time I speak with him, he is unable to give me a clear, definitive answer as to when the stove will be replaced. Today is the fifth day that we have not had a stove. (Both the oven and stove top are inoperable as determined by the fire dept and National Grid-turned off the gas.)

We are undertenants in a co-op and have never had a problem with the management company before. I don't know if the standard apartment regulations apply to us since technically we're subletters (we've have had one-year leases each year for the past 4 years.)

My questions - how long is reasonable for us not to have a stove? Is there any regulation about this? (We've been eating lots of sandwiches, microwave meals, and eating out - and we have food that's going bad in the fridge because we can't cook it properly.) If we don't get the stove today (we're supposed to, but we were also supposed to get it on Tuesday and Wednesday) what should the next steps be? I already took two days off of work to stay home for the delivery on Tuesday and Wednesday, my partner is staying home today.

I don't want to take any legal action and would like to avoid filing a complaint, but this is getting excessive. [5 days with no stove, 3 days of missed work, and extra expenses for food (food spoilage because we can't cook and eating out)]

Thank you!!

Re: Appliances - broken stove - not replaced
« Reply #1 on: August 14, 2014, 10:28:09 AM »
Seriously it happened Sunday morning, it is now Thursday. It's actually four business days.  He needs to go out and buy a new stove, arrange for delivery, arrange for it to be installed, and arrange for the gas to be turned back on.  Exactly how fast do you think this should happen?

You really need to be prepared for this to take a few weeks at a minimum. Possibly longer.

I live next to a building where there was a explosion in the kitchen because of a kitchen fire. The occupant of the apartment died. In your circumstances and at this point, it seems unreasonable for you to be complaining that you have some spoiled food and that you are eating sandwiches. It's unclear how this fire occurred, but it seems you're pretty lucky your landlord is not charging you for the damage to the property. Maybe I don't have all the facts and it's the landlord's negligence that caused these circumstances. Regardless, I think you need to just show some patience and realize you're lucky to be alive and lucky your landlord is addressing the issue, even if it's not as rapidly as you'd like.  If months go by, you'd have something to complain about , but really, 4 business days?  Even if you went out and replaced the stove yourself, I doubt you could get it done in 4 days.

Yes, it's terrible when you take time off from work and what you think is going to happen doesn't, I just went through this with a appliance that broke in my kitchen. I missed work and was pissed off, but I think 4-5 days without a stove is something you can deal with, especially since you have a microwave and a working refrigerator. And perhaps you got your stove today, in which case, you should just be grateful, because most people would not be so lucky as that.

I absolutely recognize that the circumstances are nowhere near comparable to what happened in the fire where the woman died. That was a tragedy and I feel incredibly fortunate that there were no injuries and no major damage other than the stove in our case.

The purpose of my post was to determine what is the standard procedure on this.  From what I understand, certain appliances/items in an apartment are required to be fixed within a certain amount of time - I'm not sure whether a stove would fall into this category and none of the literature that I found on the housing dept's website was helpful in this regard.

If the landlord had actually given us a firm date and told us that it would be at least a week or so to get a new stove I would have no problems at all with this. My problem is that we were told that the stove would come on Tuesday, then Wednesday, then Thursday (it didn't come today) and we planned accordingly, not leaving the apartment unattended for a minute on all three days. The landlord didn't put in the order for the new stove until today, despite telling us that it would arrive today. We were told that it would come tomorrow (Friday), so yet another day of work has to be missed. I just want a definitive answer and don't understand why a major management company can't provide an answer to this and why it's taken them now 4 days to put in the order for the appliance.

I also understand that it will take some time - the gas company has to come, etc. - which will take time, too. But being told that it's going to be there on Tuesday, and it not being here on Thursday night, is unacceptable.
