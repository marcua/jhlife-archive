Neighborhood Chat / Re: Woodside web Presence?
« on: August 24, 2010, 12:34:47 PM »
I live in Woodside (67th street & 41st Avenue) and I frequently walk up Woodside Avenue from the 61st Street/LIRR station and it feels perfectly safe.  I could also get home by walking up Roosevelt Avenue, but I feel that Roosevelt is a little sketchy (esp at night) and I frequently get catcalled .  No such problems on Woodside Ave.

If you're coming back from that station, I highly recommend buying your vegetables/fruit at Jin's Big Apple (it's about a block further down where Roosevelt Ave/Woodside Avenue merge) and I also like the laundromat/dry cleaners on Woodside Ave/64th.  Nice thing about being so close to JH is that most of the restaurants there will still deliver to you.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Desperately Seeking Study Locales
« on: August 06, 2010, 01:59:32 PM »
I don't know where you live in JH, but if you're not up too high, there is a park on Woodside Avenue in the 50s (I believe around 58th Street is where it begins).  You would walk down Roosevelt Avenue, and just past the 61st Street 7 train stop, Roosevelt Ave and Woodside Avenue intersect and the park is a few blocks down, between Roosevelt Ave and Woodside Ave.  Or, you could take the 7 train 2 stops down to the 61st Street--Woodside stop.

I've never been there, but I drive by all the time and it always seems quiet and not crowded. 

http://www.bridgeandtunnelclub.com/bigmap/queens/woodside/doughboypark/index.htm

Also, Astoria is full of places with outdoor seating.  If you don't work during the weekdays, it will probably be pretty quiet and you can get some studying done.  At night and on the weekend, those places get VERY crowded, though, especially in the nice weather.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Indian Sweets?
« on: August 04, 2010, 02:01:57 PM »
On a whim, I walked into Maharaja on my way home from work last night.  There's always a massive crowd gathered around the sweets and I've always been too intimidated to order (although I have sat down several times for their delicious all-vegetarian lunch buffet).  There was nobody by the counter, so I decided to stop in... and I'm so glad I did!   I just got 3 pieces-- a burfi, halva and something paneer-based.  They all were delicious, and not as sweet as other indian sweets I have tried (including the same types at other places).  Still sweet, but not overbearingly sweet, and everything was also very fresh-tasting.

I'll definitely be back.. luckily it's not actually directly on my way back from the subway, so perhaps I'll be able to control myself a little:).
