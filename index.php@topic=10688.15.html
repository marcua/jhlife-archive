this is great and also not so great.
On the one hand, it is great that a major retailer like the Gap is opening up on the neighborhood. If they do well, it will certainly attract other retailers to seriously consider the area.

On the other hand. A big retailer like this causes some issues. First, landlords with prime space will now hold out for these types of tenants and market their rents at prices that will be out of reach for many small business owners. Same holds true as existing leases expire.

Second, the success of a "secondary" retailer like this will lead to other secondary retailers coming in, and will over time firmly create the image of a less-than-prime retail shopping district. This means it will not attract the higher-profile brands like Austin St gets (VS, Banana, H&M, etc.), but it will be a nice step above the rag-tag stores with their very-enhanced mannequins selling little tops and derriere focused jeans.
