The problem is you can be carrying the virus and be totally asymptomatic and oblivious to it, or at the very least show no symptoms for days.. up to 14 days at a low percentage high side. Going out without one is simply disrespectful to others if you can help it. No amount of reasoning it away is going to change facts.

hfm, I completely understand that one could carry the virus and be asymptomatic, which is why I've been following guidelines such as limiting time outside. I have older family members who live alone that I would love to visit, but I can't right now out of concern that I might have this virus and not know it. A trip to a grocery store carries significantly less risk than visiting someone in their home. Like most people, I'm taking extra precautions when I am in public.

There are differing viewpoints within the medical community on public use of face masks. If you have not read the article I linked to in my post you were responding to, here it is again:

https://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2020/04/coronavirus-pandemic-airborne-go-outside-masks/609235/

The title says it all! People who don't recommend masks, people who do, people who wear masks, people who don't - Everyone Thinks They're Right About Masks! We likely won't know if masks made any difference until this is all over.

I don't think this is a time to put blame on any group of people - Chinese, Asians, immigrants, rich vacationers, young people, New Yorkers, people who don't wear masks, people who don't wear the right kind of mask, etc., etc. If you want to place blame, place it where it belongs - our ill-prepared government and our broken healthcare system.

Within the past few days NYC and the CDC have made the recommendation to wear face coverings. It's important to note that these are recommendations, not mandates. One of my fears is that these recommendations will provide another reason for people to feel entitled to place blame on others.

My biggest concern with these recommendations is that they will lead to further panic among the public and fuel another round of hoarding of supplies that our medical professionals and first responders desperately need right now. In a perfect world, we'd have enough for everyone. Unfortunately we don't.

If you have personal protective equipment that you would like to donate to NYC's medical professionals, you can do so through these websites:
https://www1.nyc.gov/site/helpnownyc/give-help/donate.page
https://ppetonyc.wixsite.com/nycppe/

I'm now (mostly) covering my dirty mouth in public based on the NYC recommendation. At the moment I'm using scarfs as that's what I have, but bandanas are on the way. Again, I'm in no position to recommend whether someone should or shouldn't wear a mask.

If anyone has a suggestion of a face covering to use while running, please share. The NYC Health Commissioner has stressed that face coverings should not be worn while wet. This is pretty hard to avoid when you are sweating and breathing heavily. Something inexpensive that doesn't restrict breathing would be ideal. I'm finding that I'm touching my face more in public when my face is covered to make adjustments - something comfortable while running is important.
