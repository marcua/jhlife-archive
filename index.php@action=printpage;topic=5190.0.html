Post by: causidicus on September 06, 2010, 09:56:13 PM
Thanks for the advice about being careful.  I know it came out of neighborly concern.  I will reiterate your point but with a few caveats.  I want to be clear I am not blaming the victim for not taking precautions.  Too often, victims, especially of gender- and sexual orientation-based violence, get blamed.

Regardless of the motivation behind the incidents, I think everyone has the right to live in his or her community without the threat of violence at any hour.  It is a sad state--that has unfortunately existed in many areas of this country to varying degrees at varying times--that people need to cautioned to walk outside.  I don't want to normalize such a situation.

Having said that, these incidents have a dimension that others do not, namely the element of hate and intimidation directed at not just an individual but a whole group.  Let's address the underlying issues.  One observation that I have made is the sheer boldness of the machismo out there and the general acceptance among some people.  What does it say when thousands of our neighbors vote for Hiram Monserrate AFTER his conviction and other troubles?

Also, what about all the anti-gay words I hear in Spanish walking in this neighborhood?  I just heard another one on Roosevelt Avenue tonight, probably directed at me.  The Colombians who live in my building have similarly used derogatory terms toward me and seemed shocked when I confronted them.  The use of the terms may be as racially motivated--my background is northern European--as they are motivated by perceived sexual orientation.  As my Spanish improves, so does my awareness of the depth and breadth of the problem.  I am not pinning all the blame on a certain group.  I have lived in many different places and homophobia manifests itself in different ways in each place.  There are non-Spanish-speaking people here who are also homophobic.  I am pointing out the issue that has made itself most obvious through my personal experiences in Jackson Heights.
