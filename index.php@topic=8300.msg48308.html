Folks in the food service industry tend to agree. There is an adversarial relationship between food service providers and the DOH largely because the inspectors' focus often time seems to be more about issuing violations (fines) rather than actually protecting the public.

For example, I've heard about inspectors sticking a thermometer in a tray of lettuce (that was out to prep for family meal) to measure the temperature and issuing a fine because the lettuce was above 40 degrees. Never mind that the training the DOH provides to earn a food protection certificate (required for supervisors) is about the danger zone for protein. What happens when lettuce is warmer than 40 degrees? It's not a significant vector for bacteria, provided it's been properly washed.

This is why when I stopped at Wendy's a couple weeks ago and ordered a sandwich with half an avocado in it, the avocado was ice cold and was rather unpleasant. As far as lettuce being a vector for bacteria, the last count I heard for the Romaine lettuce salmonella cases was 122 infected, one dead.  Once leafy greens have been cut, they are considered a "Potentially Hazardous Food".
 
It's not really hard to get an A inspection.  All restaurants should be clean and have hot water for their employees to wash their hands and store food above the ground and get rid of vermin and keep food at the proper holding temperature. 

Temperature violations are for food that is not actively being prepared.  "Foods may be held out of temperature during active necessary preparation." I suspect in the instance described that the restaurateur claimed that the food was being prepared but the inspector observed it just sitting there.

While some inspectors may be fishing for a bribe to overlook a violation, the violations are real.  If you've ever worked in a restaurant, you know all sorts of horror stories about what goes on in even the best restaurants.  While an A isn't a guarantee that the food is safe, I would never eat anywhere with a grade lower.  In fact, it's a good idea to read the inspection reports of any place you frequent even if they do have an A, as they can have up to 13 points of violations and still get an A.
