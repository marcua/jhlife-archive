Co-Op Bike Storage
« on: May 18, 2015, 07:56:48 PM »
Our co-op is kicking around the idea of putting in some bike storage in our generally underutilized basement common room.  We are a 16 unit co-op and it is pretty clear that to create a bike storage of 16 bikes would be unwieldy and perhaps not even fully used.  So if one is put in, it would be for less than 16 bikes, thereby meaning that not every unit would get an allocated space.  If this is the case, would it be considered normal to charge a fee for this and if so, how much would be appropriate?  It feels odd to charge for bike storage, but there could be a fairness issue.  Presently, all units have an allocated storage room and no fee is charged.  We have garages, but only those high enough on the senority list get them and for such a fee is paid.

Plus, if anyone is in a building that does have bike storage, it would be great to know what type of storage system is used and how well that is working out.

At my co-op they do not charge for bike storage. There are maybe 100 spaces for 220 units. As you said, most folks don't  use them. But it's a great amenity for those that do. I've been looking to buy in JH co-ops for more than a year, and every place I've looked at that has bike storage charges for $5 a month or they tout that the available storage unit (for rent of course) is large enough to accommodate a bike. Following up on Morninglemon's comments, there are a lot of buildings that don't charge and that indeed have storage (free) large enough to accommodate a bike. I've lived in two of the coops on the list that do not charge a fee.

In the past, my building considered installing a standing rack, not unlike the ones in the street. They ended up not doing it for unrelated reasons. In my current building, we have a ceiling rack that the bikes hang from. That's worked out fine, though there are more bikes than spaces on the rack. Additional bikes are stored in the basement. Hope this helps.
