Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

that's not all that unusual in nyc -- the concentration of illicit activity on major thoroughfares in a neighborhood that's mixed, socio-economically. flatbush avenue or fulton street in some sections of brooklyn, queens boulevard in some areas of queens. those streets are transient, and easy for people to blend in, or simply stroll up into the subway, should the "heat" go on.

that said, i see a lot less violent crime in this area than 20 ish years ago. yes, the fake document dealers and sex trafficking is a problem. yes, i wish they were not operating here. but the difference between that and the overt drug dealing -- by seriously armed individuals -- some years back is striking.
