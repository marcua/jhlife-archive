Neighborhood Chat / Re: Booming car radios

I have to say, I love hearing the music coming out of the cars in this neighborhood.  I've lived in all different neighborhoods in New York City in my 46 years, and the traffic in Jackson Heights has the most interesting selection...  Reggaeton in one car, bhangra in the next - it's one of my favorite things about the neighborhood!

P.S.  Admittedly, I only encounter this when I go for a little walk...  I live on a quiet street at the east end of Woodside, so maybe if I lived right above all the traffic, that would be different.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Thai restaurants in JH and surrounding areas
« on: November 24, 2007, 12:31:35 AM »
I go fairly often to Sabay, on Broadway near 76th Street.  It's relatively cheap (which is important for me a lot of the time), it's comfortable, it can be entertaining sometimes (because of the Thai rock and dance videos that they're always showing) and the food isn't bad at all.  Service is also pretty fast if you need takeout in a hurry (easy to grab something there before you jump on the train, which is down the street).

Neighborhood Chat / Re: HELLO!!! :-)
« on: November 23, 2007, 11:37:34 AM »
Hi.  I've been living at the east end of Woodside for about six months.  I've lived in every borough of NYC throughout my life (all 46 years) and grew up in The Bronx.  Although I technically live in Woodside (64th St.), I spend a lot of time around the 73rd-75th Street area (for Indian food and music, great supermarkets, the nice and fast E train...), so I kind of feel as though I am resident of Jackson Heights.

I arrived here via a Jackson Heights blog that I discovered because they linked to some reviews that I wrote about some CDs that I'd bought in one of the stores on 74th Street.  I do a blog on global electronic music, among other things.  (Maybe I'll send the URL for that...sometime.)  Anyway, I'm looking forward to getting more deeply acquainted with the neighborhood.  I've had to move a lot lately (bad apartment luck), but I'd like to settle in the Jackson Heights area for a while.
