We had it for delivery tonight.
The breakdown --
Looking at the menu, they have some Thai dishes that the others in our delivery range don't, seemingly from southern Thailand.
We had:
Po pia sod: Veggies were nice and crisp in the spring roll. Thankfully the included tamarind sauce was there because it would have been uber-bland otherwise.
Yum ma mong: a mango salad with shrimp, cashew nut, red onion, chili and scallion. A nice mix of flavors, but it was definitely mild compared to the som tum dishes in the area.
Gauy tiew nam tok moo: We wanted to try it to gauge it against Ayada, as their version is arguably our favorite thing on the menu there. It had a heat theirs lacks, but wasn't quite as well-rounded. Still, a pretty good rendition that's a notch below, but behind.
Kua klong moo: That heat we were expecting from the mango salad? It was in this dish in spades. Curry and herbs with ground pork and lime leaves. The curry heat concentrated down as there isn't really a sauce in this dish. The pork was nicely cooked, but this dish had a definite lingering burn. I'd have it again, but it's definitely on the short list of spicy dishes I've had in the last couple years.

Overall, Ayada's going to remain the No. 1 go-to, but the overall quality of our first go-round, along with the fact that there are some dishes on their menu that aren't available from the others, probably puts this place at No. 2 ahead of Arunee and well ahead of Black Thai.
KUA KLING  MOO /

Just checked this place out tonight and really enjoyed it. The lady couldn't have been nicer and the decor is quite elegant and a HUGE change from the old Arunee space that used to be here.

As mentioned earlier, many of the options are Southern Thai, a nice change of pace from the Northern Thai that can be found often. Now for the run down of what we ordered:

KA NOM JEEN GANG KIEW WARN GAI (Thai Pasta with Chicken, Basil Leaves, Eggplant
and Coconut Milk in Green Curry) - Really enjoyed this one, the green curry and coconut milk mix was excellent and had a nice amount of heat to it.

PAD PED MOO PHA (Sauté Wild Boar with Young Pepper, Mixed Vegetable and Mix in Chili Sauce) - First time seeing wild boar on a Thai menu, so this was a must-order. This dish really stood out - nice amount of heat and complex layers of flavor with galangal and green peppercorns.

PLAH SALMON (Grilled Filleted Salmon Salad with Mints, Lemongrass and Herbs in Red Chili Paste) - Refreshing and light dish, although I felt that there could have been a bit more lemongrass in it.

The mango sticky rice was quite tasty - very fresh ripe mangos and a nice presentation. 

Looking forward to checking out more of the dishes and the lunch special looks great, as you can choose from a long list of appetizers to go along with the main (rather than the appetizer just being a toss-in).

No liquor license yet, but the lady said that it should be coming shortly. She was very accommodating and said that we could BYOB if we liked. A big positive is that there looked like some nice draft beer taps including Long Trail (!!).

Really hope they succeed and highly recommend checking it out.
