Soccer celebration turns ugly
« on: July 28, 2011, 04:59:40 PM »
Queenscrap has picked up an ireport on CNN of the police arresting someone on the sidewalk near 85th St. and 37th Ave after the Uruguayan soccer victory.  The headline is "Police brutality in Jackson Heights?"

http://queenscrap.blogspot.com/2011/07/police-brutality-in-jackson-heights.html

Note that this is an ireport on CNN and has not been vetted by CNN.  It's just a video submitted by a CNN.com reader.

When I watched the video, things looked rowdy and it was easy to understand why police were dispatched to the scene.  I did not understand why more than 10 cops were needed to arrest one guy who was already face down on the sidewalk but the video doesn't show what preceded the guy's arrest and I didn't actually SEE any brutality in the video.

It was disturbing that it seemed like one of the officers tried to go after the guy who was filming with an iphone.  Do the police have any right to try to shut down cameras?

I live right nearby and witnessed most of what developed. Crowds, including women and children, had gathered to celebrate. Some were in La Nueva. They were rowdy, honking and blocking traffic but also ecstatic and cheerful, and not aggressive or violent. I am not sure what provoked it but all of a sudden maybe a dozen police cars, including vans and undercover cars, completely blocked the intersection and backed up traffic in all directions; the cops pulled out their batons and plastic cuffs with the expectation, I guess, to arrest scores; broke the crowds into smaller crowds on all four corners and soon forced them to dissipate. The show of power was absurd though I understand it was made to intimidate. I know they arrested a couple of people; those who were allowed to drive by made a point of cheering loudly and holding up the Uruguayan flags as they passed the cops. The latter were incomprehensibly upset and made a point of trying to pull the flags down. Go figure. The whole situation was resolved within 15-20 mins or so, though I heard another scuffle in front of El Chivito d'oro.  I saw several people trying to film what was happening, I guess this is one of those.

I have been watching all of the videos of what is being considered a celebration.

I have lived in Jackson Heights for over 25 years and have seen it all.

I am sensitive to multiculturalism having promoted the cause for over 15 years.

BUT, from all of the videos I see utter and total disrespect for law enforcement and harsh language being used against the NYPD, even when they were just moving crowds back.

Another issue is that it is not acceptable to assemble illegally and expect to be accomodated, we are not as free as the Star Spangled Banner says, there are laws that must be upheld.

But with all of the footage I have seen I have seen those who are clearly breaking the law, further break the law by verbally abusing the NYPD.

I am not quite happy with 115th PCT anyway, but their activities during this illegal assembly were warranted.

Those who say I am being culturally insensitive, so be it, but this situation could have bee dealt with legally, as we do have laws, APPLY FOR A PERMIT, simple.

We should not be subjected to these nuisances in Jackson Heights, congesting out streets even further.

My advice is that if the procedures were followed, the Uruguayan Victory would have been celebrated more smoothly, with the proper laws being abided by, and permits issued.
