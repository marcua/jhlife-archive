Title: Protected cardholder: a scam
Post by: Shelby2 on September 28, 2008, 08:10:52 PM
I think I got hoodwinked by my credit card company.

During the summer I received a notice saying that I wouldn't be protected against fraud unless I signed a form and sent it in to the company.  Guess I didn't read the fine print too well, because I signed it and sent it in, and guess what, a $49 charge appears on my statement this month saying I am now a "protected cardholder".

As far as I know, the credit card company is legally responsible if my card is stolen and used fraudulently, not me, so why would I pay $49 out of my own pocket to protect them?

After two calls I got the charge reversed, but even when I called to cancel (the first words out of my mouth, "I want to cancel" )  he said "I can help you with that" and then proceeded to run down a list of all the benefits protected cardholders receive.  I had to say "I'm calling to cancel, not to have you sell me something."

I usually don't fall for these things.  Not paying enough attention this time I guess.
