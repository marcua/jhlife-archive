Title: Re: Book Club in JH
Post by: CaptainFlannel on June 01, 2016, 06:24:57 PM
Will the book club be discussing a book on the 4th?

If not, and it's planning, may I suggest as a book club organizer, everyone be prepared to suggest a book, and then all those in attendance vote for their choice and pick a date/time. (My experience is if you don't nail down the details for the next meeting at your current meeting, things just fall apart. And, it gives people an incentive to participate and attend if those at the meetings are the ones doing the choosing.) Things to consider when pitching a book: length; length of time until the discussion; availability (is it in paperback? How many are in the Queens Library system?); subject matter and how it lends itself to discussion.

Title: Re: Book Club in JH
Post by: CaptainFlannel on June 03, 2016, 11:14:58 AM
I'd encourage the organizers of the group think about what the mission of the group is.

The book club I run is about meeting new people who share an interest in books, while also expanding our reading horizons. So, this manifests itself in a couple of ways: I want active participation from members for book suggestions so we all get to read things that we might not normally pick up; if someone hasn't finished a book in time for the club, that's fine as long as they understand the discussion will include spoilers; and hating a book so much the person can't even finish it is a perfectly valid experience of a book - just be able to articulate what you hated and listen to what others enjoyed or got from it.
