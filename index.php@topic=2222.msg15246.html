Hi amadan.  I've posted a couple of links that can be helpful in answering some of your questions about park space and family life in Jackson Heights.

The 78th Street Play Street initiative is something new to our community.  A few enterprising residents made this play street happen in order to address the lack of park space in JH.  While it's not an actual park, the play street runs alongside Travers Park, our neighborhood playground.  It was pretty popular last summer with both kids and adults.  You can read more about it here:

78th Street Play Street

The Jackson Heights Family listserv is a great resource in the community.  You'll find parents posting about many issues there, including some of the ones you're interested in learning more about.  Check it out.  It's also a great way to meet some of the families.  You have to register for membership in order to participate though.

Yahoo -- Jackson Heights Family listserv

JH does suffer from a lack of open / grassy park space. However, if you're willing to walk a little bit there's what was called Model Park on 85th and 30th Ave. I believe and there's also the landing lights park which is a strip of grassy plots leading towards the runway at LGA. Theoretically this strip is clear for low flying planes. But don't let that scare you There's also the playground just past 69th St. near the intersection of Broadway and 37th Ave. Not too bad a stroll from Washington Plaza, where I'll be moving to in a week ; ).

As for schools, while I'm not a parent I can say that I am a product of the local schools here and I turned out just fine. (though some might disagree ) There seems to me to be a lot to choose from especially for elementary schools. There's always PS 69, The Renaissance Charter School, there's an elementary school on 82nd St. between 35th and 34th ave., and one up on 37th ave. around 86th st. Then there's always the Garden School and St.Joan's.

As for walking around and things to do during the day, Astoria might win out on the more Main Stream amenities. However, Espresso 77, Letty's, and Starbucks make for a cute little cafe circuit.  There's an endless amount of diverse restaurants, many of which have great lunch specials (especially if you like Indian or Latin food).  I'm not sure but the JH Library may have some interesting day time programming etc.

It's a great neighborhood, and if you wind up in Washington Plaza, you'll certainly have some great neighbors ! ; )

There is a very active and supportive community of families with young children here.  I moved here six years ago, when my wife and I were expecting our first child, and we have found it a wonderful place to put down roots.  We lived in the Washington Plaza for our first year, and also found it a very pleasant and welcoming building. 

The lack of park space is the one major drawback of the neighborhood.  But Travers Park is a great playground/community commons, and we have two local organizations (JH Green and Friends of Travers Park) that work hard to ensure that it is clean, safe, and activity-filled.  My kids also enjoy the Hart Playground at 65th Street in Woodside, the playground opposite Elmhurst Hospital, and Playground 90 off Northern Blvd -- all within easy walking distance.  And soon several of the schoolyard playgrounds will be renovated and made available for use during off-school hours.

We moved from Astoria to JH in October, then had our baby January 8th. This is the second time i've lived here. It is much more dynamic than back in 2000.

We miss the parks and waterfront alot, but there is the marina at Flushing Meadows not too far away.(especially if you bike).

That park, is incredible and will sate all your outdoor needs. It even has a new ice rink and aquatic center.

I',ve found two new friends with new babies, while hardly looking. This is a very friendly baby neighborhood. Baby and child culture reigns. Just count the strollers.

In the end though, think about the space and the place. We really wanted to stay in Astoria, but the rents were ridiculous for the torn up, sub-divided, mom and pop owned places listed. plus, they tended to be far from transit and shopping.

You'll probably like it here. Especially if you find a big, roomy, sunny place. Good luck with the search.
