Neighborhood Chat / Re: Tennis courts
« on: October 26, 2008, 03:26:38 AM »
i know its kinda far from JH but Crocheron Park in Bayside is my FAV. park in queens. Its a moderate walk from the LIRR and im not sure how to get there by bus b/c I always drive there. But the drive/LIRR ride is well worth the exrta time to get there.

It has LOTSSSSS of green space, 4 ball fields, a little under 10 tennis courts, a waterpark during the summer for children, and a basketball court. It also offeres many nice walking paths and they even have 2 gazibos (did i spell that wrong? lol)....for the tennis courts they are *free* during the year untill about may through the end of auguest. Comes the end of may you have to pay either for the whole summer or per day...and btw these courts have the CHEAPEST rates in queens when it comes to paying for court time, trust me I did my research lol*

***It is a very well maintained park and right outside the tennis courts are VERY CLEAN and well taken care of public restrooms. The side of the park that is directly across from peoples nice homes, are where the courts and fields are and is the side that has a hugeeee parking. The other side along the grassy/meadow-ish part of the park, is a dead end street that if you take the street all the way down, theres a huge pond with benches and also you walk up the huge staircase and there is a bridge that is built over the cross island parkway and takes you over to the other side which is a water front view with a paved sidewalk and a section for bike riders and roller bladers.

Sometimes I forget im in queens lol


Anyway just thought id put in my 2cents

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Looking for a good pediatrician
« on: October 24, 2008, 11:30:27 PM »
Hello, i just joined this site and and i dont think i could have picked a better topic to make my 1st post on.

Forest Hills Pediatric is AMAZING. My pediatrician was from that office and I saw him from birth up untill I was 13/14ish I believe. There have been since then a handfull of new doctors there, but there are still 3-4 doctors that STILL work there that I used to see when I went there. Even the hopstials that the doctors have their main affiliations with (they give great referals if you need to see a Doctor in a specific field of medicine), are mostly (if not all) top notch hosptials in NY State.


When it comes to the transportation issue, whoever made the comment about taking an infant on the subway is completly right. Comming from Jackson Heights by subway is so easy, but I would wait untill the child is a bit older. Taking a cab from JH to Forest Hillls Pediatric is about a 15 min. ride, give or take 5-10 min for traffic depending on the time of day. I have a great cab service # that picks you up from your residence 5-10 min after you call them to pick you up. Since Forest Hills is so close of a ride, their rates are great aswell! If anyone would like that number I would be more then happy to share it with you guys.


I also want to share a quick story that I found amusing. About a year ago when I was working for a family in Forest Hills as the babysitter/caretaker for their 5-month old infant, when I first started with the family I had recieved all the contact information for their childs pediatrician. She had told me that the childs main pediatrician was a few blocks away and is an amazing doctor named Dr. Joseph Soloway. I could help but laugh out loud, and I told her that he was my main doctor from the age of birth up untill i was 14 years old. I am now 22 yrs old and I couldnt believe that he was still one of the head pediatricians at that office.


ANYWAY, I appologize for going on a rant in this reply, but I just cant express how amazing this Office is. Whenever I start a family I will most def. take my children there.


thanks for sticking through this long reply! hope this information helped!
