SO, it's OK if WHITE yuppies move into the neighborhood - but what about "shades of brown" yuppies? Or do we not assume that people of color can ALSO be young urban professionals. I'm not sure I understand the subtext when people thoughtlessly put white and yuppie together. Can't we get beyond that simple characterization? It is so subtly insidious that people probably don't even notice.

Here is my opinion - I LOVE the fact that this neighborhood is attracting a new crop of young singles and couples (both gay and straight) who are excited to be involved and excited to build a sense of community with their neighbors. These are folks ready to volunteer in neighborhood activities, serve on their coop boards, fight for more resources for our schools, and address issues like traffic, street cleanliness, etc. And I LOVE the fact that these professionals are both white AND puerto rican, black, cuban, philipino, korean, polish, british, argentinian....etc. That diversity is what makes me know that Jackson Heights is the place I want to make my home and the place I want to raise my children. And I'm not concerned about JH changing as a result of this influx. Frankly, the Historic District has always been a bit different, demographically speaking, than the surrounding neighborhoods because of the history of exclusion (for better or worse) that has been mentioned previously. But think about it, if you take a bike ride to surrounding neighborhoods, they do not have the kind of attractive housing stock or the kind of transportation access (think of north of Northern and some places in Elmhurst and Corona), to ever attract the crowd that seems to be interested in Jackson Heights right now. This is the crowd with jobs in Manhattan for whom easy access to midtown is vital. So even if JH gets yuppified as older residents start turning over apartments to a new crop of Jackson Height'ers, I have a feeling that we will always be surrounded by diverse communities that will keep us grounded in the reality of class AND ethnic diversity (and with access to great, authentic ethnic food - thank G-D! B/C that's why I'm really here! .

Can you tell that I love this place?
