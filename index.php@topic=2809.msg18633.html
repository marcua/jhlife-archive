It is my understanding that the NYPD generally does not get involved in determining and apprehending illegal immigrants solely for that reason. Most often they do not inquire as to a person's immigration status when they arrest them. There are of course exceptions and other complications to this general practice. However, illegal immigration is a federal issue and immigration laws are enforced by various federal agencies.

I would suspect that the prime target of this silly sting operation are people with prior offenses. Part of the whole stop and frisk idea is to catch individuals who may be wanted, or, to have an excuse to find illegal weapons / substances on someone. The 82nd St. sting allows officers to detain and frisk anyone who decided to look through the wallet, as I'm assuming there's probable cause to believe that person might be attempting to steal the wallet.

It would be very interesting to see how many people get confronted by an undercover officer on 82nd St. and out of those how many are detained and searched vs. how many are let off with a warning. I imagine there may be a notable difference in the race, sex, and age of each group.
