I don't know what the details are for her plan for free college -- and the devil is in the details.

That said, I think it is wrong to cast this discussion as meritocracy vs. whatever -- it's not really accurate to call Excelsior a "meritocracy".

The requirement to be a full-time student is a real problem for any but middle-class kids. If you have to work 20, 30, or even 40 hours/week, taking 4-5 classes is INSANITY. So maintaining a high GPA is as much or more about having parents who are paying your living expenses as it is about talent/work ethic.

But Federal financial aid, and Excelsior, demands students be full-time. I never got anywhere trying to persuade CUNY kids to go part-time because free tuition was too attractive. And they will really be screwed if the tuition turns to loans.
