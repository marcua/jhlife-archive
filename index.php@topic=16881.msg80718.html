Quote
Everyone's entitled to a summer break.

What folks are talking about is not a summer break. Small businesses that rely upon customers coming to the place of business to spend money post notices about their summer break prior to the summer break. Posting your regular business hours and then ignoring the stated business hours is not a summer break. It's being an unreliable business owner.

Louie's is taking a summer break right now. I haven't been by his restaurant, but he's a smart business person so I would wager there's a sign in the window that says they are closed down until 9/5 for a summer vacation, just like there's a message on their phone that says they are shut down and when he will be reopening. Chances are any notice Louie's posted about being on vacation was posted before they shut down so most of their customers knew in advance.

Even if the owner of Juju's wanted reduced summer hours, a smart business person would plan ahead for that, understanding that customers need reliability. You don't change your hours on a whim. You plan ahead and let your customers know. You post a notice in June that hours in July through Labor Day will be X. And then you stick to it. Because you know if your customers can't rely on your word for what your business hours are, they will just go to one of your competitors who is reliable.
