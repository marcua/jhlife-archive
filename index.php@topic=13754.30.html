Meanwhile, since you seem to be interested in bottled beer, which ones would you like me to serve, and why?

It's got nothing to do with quality. You can be a craft brewer and pump out pure garbage and probably be out of a "job" in short order. Read this: https://www.brewersassociation.org/statistics/craft-brewer-defined/

If you aren't interested in taps and are just looking for bottled and canned beer, I'd personally just get as many of the locals as you can. I like supporting good local breweries. We have some good ones around here. You'll probably have to pick up some of the big national brands as well just to please the masses. You are trying to run a business after all.

Try these guys:
Other Half Brewing Co. - Great stuff!
Six Point - They're popular around here, consistently decent and appeal to a wide range of tastes
Coney Island Brewing. I was getting tired of IPA's and searching for good pilsners, their pilsner is actually really good. So is their IPA.
Evil Twin is pretty good as well.

This is all just my opinion though.  There's a ton of other breweries around but I'm not sure which ones sell bottles and cans and which don't as I usually look for drafts. If you can manage some taps I'd support Single Cut wholeheartedly. I'm sure if you explain to a good buyer what you're looking for you won't have a problem getting them to give you some unique local stuff as well as some stuff to go to as crowd-pleasers.

Good luck!

There's clearly a demand for a pub in Jackson Heights. It would be great if someone would open one, serving good beers on tap and a selection of bottled beers.

I met the guys behind the upcoming tapas bar, referred to in your link, last week, and they are really nice people. Looking forward to their opening.

The problem in Jackson Heights is that there is a big risk in offering alternatives to the dining mainstream here. Except for two very traditional restaurants, there is no history of support. Somebody has to go first. In some ways, that's what Espresso 77 did, and their success was certainly a factor in my decision to create a full service fine/casual restaurant here. The upcoming tapas bar is also an experiment. If businesses like this can earn support, others will follow, maybe including a good pub.

We already have plans, if our restaurant gets support, to create either a traiteur or a wine bar. Traiteur isn't a common word in the U.S., but it means a place that sells high quality prepared foods. The closest New York match is places like Citarella and Dean &  Delucca, but they are much pricier than than what a true neighbourhood traiteur is.
