I can't recommend anyone here in JH but can definitely give you a recommendation for an excellent, legit massage place in Chinatown: Fishion Herb Center (see contact info at bottom).  It's definitely Chinatown -- not luxurious or spa-like by any means -- but it's clean, functional, and filled with customers, both Chinese and non-Chinese, all the time.  The best part is an hour-long massage is only $40.  Yes, $40.  (Used to be $35 but they raised the price about a year ago.)

If you're going on a weekend, I highly recommend making a reservation beforehand.  You can request a masseuse -- I recommend Rich.  He's a very nice middle-aged man from Beijing who is strong, strong, strong and can really massage.  I like deep tissue, almost to the point of being painful and Rich always delivers.  Rich starts with accupressure on your feet (don't freak -- he uses Vaseline petroleum jelly and wipes your feet off with paper towels) to determine which parts of your body are out of whack, and then massages for an hour.   

I've also used Sean, but he was just too strong.  My friends like going to some of the female massage therapists there but I found that they're not strong enough for me and I end up feel like I'm just getting poked and prodded.  I'm sure you can ask the receptionist (the woman who most often answers the phone has very good English) for a recommendation when you call.

FYI: You have the option of leaving your clothes on or undressing for the massage.  There's a sign in every room, though, that says that if you're going to undress, to leave your underwear on.  Your massage therapist will lead you to the room, leave you to get undressed (if you want) and get under the thin blanket before he/she returns. 

Again, I just want to emphasize that Fishion is not luxurious -- they use thin towels and blankets to cover you, the rooms are separated by walls that don't go all the way up to the ceiling, they line the massage tables with paper (like at the doctor's office), the place smells like Chinese herbs, and it's not totally quiet all the time.  I lived in China for a while and Fishion always makes me miss living there.  The massage, however, is excellent and for $40 it can't be beat.  I've referred many people to Fishion and Rich and they all go back regularly. 

Fishion Herb Center
107 Mott Street, above Canal (it's down a short, astroturf-lined alley)
212-966-8771

InSpa World in a word:   AMAZING

I went today and didn't want to leave.  I'll be going again and again.  I'm going to write a review on the link above for it because now I know why there are negative reviews -- mistaken expectations.  This is a bath house on four levels... not a new age spa.  It is incredible.  I've never seen so many different saunas, jacuzzis etc.  And the food was fantastic.  When I got there, I had a few pieces of sushi so I wouldn't have my stomach growling an hour later during my massage.  FRESH.  Then after my massage, I went to the salad bar and filled my plate half with fresh fruit and the other half with fresh veggies and angel hair pasta.  FRESH.  It costs $30 to walk thru the door and you can hang until closing.  Everything is a la carte: food, massages: body,hand,foot. 

So I went for three hours and had a one hour private Swedish massage which felt more like deep tissue she was so strong, ate and walked out spending $114.  You don't tip anyone, it's included in the prices.  I'll go into more detail at the link Todd so kindly provided.  But Abee you and your friends should be prepared to have a blast.  Oh and the place is immaculate and really interesting in design.  I can't say enough good things about this place.
