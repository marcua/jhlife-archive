BQE Fitness
« Reply #46 on: August 30, 2008, 07:15:03 PM »
I've been going to the BQE for more than two years now, since moving to JH from Manhattan (where I belonged to an NYSC by NYU for many years), and my experience has been quite good (compared to the moderator of this thread, who snidely trashed the place back in February--he'd obviously not been there in a while). Compared to how dingy and disorganized it was when I first joined, the place is now spacious and relatively well layed out (it's apparently grown by additions over the years), and the aerobic equipment and much of the free weights are new and plentiful. Complaints regarding cleanliness must have registered, because a cleaning crew is constantly mopping and wiping down equipment. The men's locker room, at least, is also recently renovated, with sauna and steam. There is no pool, but there are handball courts. There's also an area with punching bags for boxing and kick-boxing. The place is open 24 hours, as far as I know, and has aerobic and spinning classes, although I've never attended. I try to go midday, when there aren't so many people around. Late afternoons, of course, can be very busy. While the gym has a very diverse clientele, in keeping with the surrounding neighborhoods, it's predominantly Hispanic (at least midday). It's also diverse very diverse with regard to age, gender, sexual orientation, fitness level, etc. etc.. I've been told it's popular with NYC cops; there was an article in the NYT four or five years ago that described it as popular with cabbies, who liked it's proximity to the BQE expressway and came in the wee hours to work out. There are stairs between three floors--no elevators--in case this is an issue for you.

Regarding cost, the BQE is apparently much cheaper than a lot of places mentioned here. I rejoined before the renovation was complete and paid around $400 for 2 years if I recall. I'm sure it's more now. As for location, I live on 75th and travel to and from the gym by bike. The distance is perfect for bike, but too far for walking, and it's not near any train or bus.  Of course if you drive it's no problem, they have a small lot and I observe most people parking on the street.

The BQE works great for me and I think it's definitely worth checking out (in contrast to others who were commenting here last winter), but I hope a lot more people don't join!
 
http://www.bqefitnesscenter.net/

In the 20 years I've lived in Queens, I've belonged to and experience the following:
1.  BQE - close by but parking can be difficult during busy hours.  Wasn't always clean.  Definitely has had it's "ups and downs" over years.  Open 24 hours and not expensive.
2.  Bulova Buildings gym:  even closer by.  great parking.  Small, not a lot of equipment but it had a pool and jacuzzi.  More normal hours.  Moderate prices.  Not sure if it's still there though.   Belonged years ago.
3.  The Matrix:  GREAT GYM!   Lots of parking underground.  Great equipment, extremely clean and well run.  I think I was paying about $47 a month.  You don't have to do any contract.

I stopped my membership since I'm not in Queens much any more but it would be the gym I'd join again if I were looking.  You can get a two week free trial pass.  Ask them about it.
