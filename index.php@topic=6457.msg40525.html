There are already half a dozen grocery stores within a block of this location, and another half a dozen if you expand out to four-five blocks away.  I'm not sure what value the new shop will add unless it is a chain that actually bothers to keep the sidewalks in a state other than filthy.

There is a "For Rent" sign above the smaller storefronts that face onto 73rd St.  We can probably look forward to clones of the tailors, jewelers, halal meat, small dry goods shop, phone card / money transfer / etc shops that already seem overrepresented on 73rd and 74th.

Interestingly, did anyone notice the tiny salon and Colombian bakery that split the short-lived "Famous Pizza" location?  They both seem to be doing well!

Closing 73rd St to traffic would be awesome.  There could be pylons set up right after the parking garages for the two buildings with a very short lane for those cars to get in and out from broadway / roosevelt.  Two-thirds of the block would still be car-free.  Eliminating parking on the short road that was still open would keep it free of congestion.  There is something similar set up for a few co-ops off Woodhaven Blvd that are next to a road that used to be open to traffic through Forest Park (but is now closed off).
