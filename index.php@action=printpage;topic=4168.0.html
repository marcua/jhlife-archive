Thanks for asking...  I issued my first quarter 2010 report last week.  The report has been "improved" and now provides an analysis of properties within and outside the Jackson Heights Historic District, and looks at closings - by apartment size - for the original Queensboro built cooperatives (from Laurel Court through The Towers) as far back as 2003.  You can obtain a copy of my first quarter 2010 report by subscribing at www.jhrealestatereport.com

A quick summary for the 11372 zip code:

Houses:  While the number of sales rose (albeit off of a very low level), average sales prices continued to decline

Apartments: The number of sales declined compared to the year-ago and prior quarters, although prices remained relatively stable

The spring selling season has been somewhat muted.  The end of the Federal tax credit may have pulled some demand forward.

All of the details by property type (one-, two- and three-family houses, prewar and postwar coops, and condominiums) can be found in the first quarter report.
