Post by: JIF on May 29, 2013, 09:30:09 AM
Alas...the issue, as always here in JH... is the commercial rents charged by the landlords. 

It's ironic.  Residential rents are affordable in JH.  But commercial rents here are on par with the fancier areas of Manhattan.

That's why JH landlords choose 99 cent stores.  They pay the most rent...due to having the lowest overheads...no spoilage, minimal staff etc.

Proactive ideas are great...but bar owners will always face the same dilemma. 

The rents are too high in JH to take risks with an interesting bar or other business.

Single landlords own entire blocks of commercial premises here in JH...unlike many other 'hoods. Absent landlords have fixed commercial rents at exorbitant levels.

That's the only reason we're limited in our options of stores/venues on 37th Ave here in JH.

So JH has the most spacious, awesome, affordable apartments in all of NYC...but our commercial strips are functional and uninspired...due to the disproportionate residential/commercial rent disparity.

 JH...because of its historic and beautiful housing...is definitely the best neighborhood in NYC apartment-wise.  But unfortunately not commercially...

Title: Re: 78-23 37th Ave previously NOVO?
Post by: toddg on July 23, 2013, 11:22:22 PM
I wish JH's landlords were more creative about their tenants and more proactive about seeking a more vibrant commerical mix, but I doubt they're uniquely greedy.  I expect their interest in seeking top dollar is about the same as landlords everywhere.  I think the real question is why the market in JH will bear prices that are much higher than elsewhere.  And I think the answer to that is that the prices are an unfortunate consequence of the design of our beautiful planned community.  We have high residential densities in the historic district, but virtually no commercial frontage.  34th Avenue, 35th Avenue, and most of our streets are nearly completely void of commercial space.   And many of the commercial spaces along Roosevelt, 37th Avenue, and Northern Blvd. are actually quite small compared with the spaces available in many other neighborhoods.  There are just too many customers chasing too little commercial space.


What are the solutions to this?  One solution could be to build more commercial space.   That's virtually impossible in the Historic District, but could be achieved elsewhere with supportive zoning.  We could allow denser commercial development south of 37th Avenue on 75th, 76th, and 77th Streets, for example.   Or we could encourage higher commercial and residential development densities along Northern Blvd. west of 78th Street. 


Of course, there's no chance this will happen.   The community strongly resisted commercial development on 73rd Street, and would be unlikely to support rezoning elsewhere.   But that's a policy choice by the community, and I believe astronomical rents are its consequence.
