Here is a fascinating article on North Beach, which sat where La Guardia Airport sits today.

And here are some articles from the New York Times:

Talk of "Hush Money"; Officers Said to be Paid Not to Enforce Sunday Laws (May 25, 1891)

Twenty Thousand at North Beach (June 18, 1894)

A Rush to Long Island City (July 8, 1895) ... New Yorkers escape to North Beach to get booze on a Sunday

SUPPLEMENTARY SUMMER SEASON.; Great Rush to Long Island Resorts -25,000 at North Beach (Sept. 22, 1895)

SITE FOR HEINE FOUNTAIN; IT WILL BE ERECTED AT NORTH BEACH IN GREATER NEW-YORK (April 29, 1896)   (The fountain ended up in the Bronx)

*FEATURES OF GREATER NEW-YORK; North Beach, Steinway, L.I. (July 12, 1896)

GAMBLING AT NORTH BEACH; Many Kinds of Fraudulent Games Openly Operated Under the Very Eyes of the Police. HUNDREDS OF FOOLISH VICTIMS (August 2, 1897)

GAMBLERS HASTILY DEPART; Exodus from North Beach and Not a Dollar Hazarded by Visitors Yesterday. A WARNING SATURDAY NIGHT Coroner Haslam of Newtown Swears Out a Warrant, Which Is Held Up as a Red Flag of Danger -None Spared. NO REFORM AT CONEY ISLAND. Vulgar Exhibitons Still Rampant Raines Law Observed Only by a Few in Large Hotels. (August 9, 1897)

North Beach Attractions (August 21, 1904)

*North Beach Unique as an Amusement Resort; Although It Has a Clientele of No Inconsiderable Size, but a Trifling Fraction of New York's Millions Know Anything About It -- Patronized by the Foreign Element (July 2, 1905)


* = Must see!
