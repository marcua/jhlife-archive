The other thing that's kind of cracker jacks about health inspections is that the requirements often times don't meet the reality of how food is prepared and served. Health inspectors will ding restaurants for ridiculous things, like the temperature of lettuce. If it's above 40 degrees, that's a violation. Who cares that lettuce isn't going to invite bacteria to culture at 41 degrees? Things like soup, and gravy and protein are at risk for spoilage between the temperatures of 40-140 degrees, but only if they stay there for four hours. So one data point of temperature doesn't really tell you if there's a risk of bacteria growing if it's only been at that temperature for 20 minutes.

I recently learned that fine dining pretty much shuts down service when the health inspectors show up and staff ends up throwing a bunch of stuff out before the inspection actually begins, because how food is served in fine dining is contrary to the health department's requirements.
