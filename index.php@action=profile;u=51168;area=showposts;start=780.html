It has been widely speculated that there are several tiers of commercial rent for the same space in Jackson Heights. Some of our commercial spaces are managed by landlords or associations with broad networks of ethnic, family or neighborhood connections. They are often said to discount the asking-price rents to people who share an ethnic, family or neighborhood connection with them - people who "speak their language" and are thus likely to be easy tenants for that specific landlord.


Is anyone aware of the legality of this supposed action? Please not I'm not asking for a moral judgement here or "it should/shouldn't be legal", rather someone who is aware of the law.

 There are laws against, for example, discrimination based on race in residential property rental/purchase; irrespective of what "asking price is". e.g. if I offer one price to one person and another price to another and that differential is based on race, then I have broken the law, plain and simple.
