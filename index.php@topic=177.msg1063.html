I don't have much time to read for pleasure these days.  But an author whose work I have always enjoyed is Wendell Berry.  He is a passionate advocate for traditional rural values, and a critic of our urbanized, consumerist society.  I don't identify much with his values, perspective, or politics -- I am a suburbanite by birth and an urbanist at heart and much more of a centrist politically.  Yet this former tobacco farmer's social critique and clear values-based vision, combined with his beautiful writing, are nothing short of inspiring to me.

Here is one book I enjoyed:
http://www.amazon.com/What-Are-People-Wendell-Berry/dp/0865474370

A great book of essays for people who love the craft of writing:
http://www.amazon.com/Standing-Words-Essays-Wendell-Berry/dp/1593760558/

And two newer books I haven't read:
http://www.amazon.com/Art-Commonplace-Agrarian-Essays-Wendell/dp/1593760078
http://www.amazon.com/Way-Ignorance-Other-Essays/dp/1593761198
