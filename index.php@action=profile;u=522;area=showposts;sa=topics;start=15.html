Neighborhood Chat / Cynthia Nixon Fundraiser in Jackson Heights Friday, August 10
« on: August 09, 2018, 08:11:10 AM »

I have been actively working on improving our local JH public schools for over 10 years and in that advocacy, I have learned that often the solution is political. Albany controls much of the funding for our schools, they set teacher review laws and laws about the state test and graduation requirements. To make the changes we need to improve our schools, we need to change Albany!

We need a governor in Albany who will fight for our local public schools. I met Cynthia Nixon years ago as she has been an education advocate for almost 20 years. She was active with a group called AQE - Alliance for Quality Education. Cynthia's work to improve education drove her to run for Governor. Cuomo has said over and over that he will change things in Albany, but 8 years later the schools are still not funded, state test stress out our kids, and teachers still do not know how they will be evaluated. We deserve full funding for our schools, an MTA that works and health care. To achieve these things, we need to change things in Albany. Come out this Friday and meet her for yourself. Get your ticket now here - Ticket.

You will hear from Cynthia and have a chance to ask questions. There will be light food and drink.

Neighborhood Chat / First day of school issues....
« on: September 07, 2017, 09:49:37 AM »
So if you have/had issues with you local school this morning and want to know where to go to complain, make change or get answers, feel free to contact me.  I am one of the 2 parent representatives on the District 30 Community Education Council from Jackson Heights.  So if you are not happy with the response from your school and want to know where to go next, please reach out to me at FODoherty@aol.com.

Just to make it clear, District 30 covers 38 pre-kindergarten to 8th grades schools from Corona to Long Island City.  (So District 30 does not cover, Renaissance Charter School or any Charter School or PS 89 which is District 24).

If you have questions about your school the best place to start is usually the school's Parent Coordinators.  I have listed the local parent coordinators e-mail below.  The first day or two are usually very busy as they are often registering many new students. Generally if it is a class issues, sent a note to the teacher.  If it is a school wide issue contact the Parent Coordinator.  If you are not happy with your response, contact the principal.  If you still are not happy contact me and we can go to the superintendent Dr. Philp Composto PCompos@schools.nyc.gov). 

I hope you will all be active participants in your school.  I recommend that you:

(1) ask your child's teacher/parent coordinator for a contact list of all the other parents in your child's class;

(2) offer you contact information for a class list, parent coordinator list and PTA list;

(3) offer to be a class parent to help coordination communication among parents;

(4) ask for a copy of a parent's school handbook; (if they don't have one offer to revise the old one or start a new one);

(5)  join the SLT (school leadership team) or the PTA; and

(6) volunteer to help by contacting the parent coordinator and give them specific thing you think you can do to help.

If you want to be more involved in education issues you can join some local groups:

Neighborhood Chat / Gardening in Jackson Heights Every Saturday
« on: September 06, 2017, 12:08:27 AM »
Gardening – Every Saturday from 10 AM to 1 PM

From April to Thanksgiving the Jackson Heights Beautification Group volunteers garden each Saturday from 10 AM to 1 PM around Jackson Heights.  Everyone is welcome to join, we need your help to make the community bloom!  Just check the website to see this weeks location and drop by to help.  JHBG brings the tools, gloves, plants, and water for all the volunteers.

Fall 2017 Schedule:
September    9 – 69th Street at 35th Ave.
                  16 – PS 92 – 34th Ave. & 99th St.
                  23 – PS 149 – 34th Ave. & 94th St.
                  30 – PS 148 – 32nd Ave. + 90th St.
October        7 – IS 145 – 34th Ave. & 79th St.
                    14 – PS 280 – 35th Ave. & 94th St.
                    21 – PS 69 – 37th Ave. & 77th St.
                   28 – Leverich Cemetery – 72nd St. & 35th Ave.
     Tuesday 31 – Halloween Parade 5 PM on 37th
November   4 – Food Bazar – Junction Blvd. & 34th Ave.
                     11 – 64th St. & Northern Blvd.
                     18 – 69th St. & 35th Ave.
We garden Saturdays from 10 AM to 1 PM until Thanksgiving.
We are gardeners so we work rain or shine.


We have a planting schedule in the spring and the fall and during the summer we work to maintain our 16 different spaces:

1) 64th and Northern (farm on other side of BQE) – newest space
2) 69th and Northern (sun flowers by the gas station entrance ramp)
3) 69th Street and 34th Ave. (farm on hill by BQE)
4) 69th Street and 35th Ave. (composting and storage location)
5) 69th Street between 37 & 35th Ave. (garden by rail road track)
6) 69th Street and 37th Ave. (tulip garden)
7) Leverich Cemetery (35th Ave. and 71st St.)
IS 230 (34th Ave. and 73rd St.)
9) IS 145 (34th Ave. and 79th St.)
10) PS 69 (37th Ave. and 77th St.)
11) Post Office (37th Ave. and 79th St.)
12) Street Trees along 37th Ave.
13) PS 280 (35th Ave. and 93rd St.)
14) PS 149 (34th Ave. and 94th St.)
15) Food Bazaar (34th Avenue and Junction Blvd)
16) PS 92 (34th Ave. and 99th Street) – closed due to construction

We have added planters at the 90th Street triangle and need help keeping them watered.


Spring Planning Breakfast:

Each spring we hold a planning breakfast where people eat pancakes and propose gardening spots to work on.  Once the schedule is set, we have a group of about 10 – 30 people who meet each Saturday morning from about 10 AM to 1 PM.  Jackson Heights Beautification Group supplies the plants,  tools, gloves, and water.

Street Trees along 37th Avenue

Jackson Heights Beautification Group volunteers plant and water many of the street trees along 37th Avenue.  A dedicated team of volunteers have been installing tree guards around new trees and planting tree gardens around the trees.  The life of a street tree is difficult and we need to work to keep these trees healthy.  We have removed the paving stones around most trees so the roots can have acess to more air and water.  We break up compacted dirt and add mulch all in an effort to keep these trees healthy.

Is Gifted & Talented Best for My Child?

Wednesday, January 18, 2017, 7:00 PM – 8:30 PM
Community United Methodist Church
81-10 35th Ave, Jackson Heights, NY 11372
Subway: E/F/M/R/7 to 74th Street/Broadway/Roosevelt
A talk with Dr. Allison Roda, author of
Inequality in Gifted and Talented Programs
(Palgrave Macmillan, 2015)
The conversation will address:
- The history of gifted programs in education
- Roda’s interviews with parents across NYC in gifted and
general education programs
- Alternative models in gifted programs
Dr. Allison Roda is an assistant professor of education at Molloy College and
a research consultant at the Center for Diversity and Equality in Education
(CDEE) at Rutgers University-Newark.
