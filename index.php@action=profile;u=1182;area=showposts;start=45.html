Restaurants & Food / Join Farm Spot's CSA Winter Season!! (Deadline Dec 6th!!)
« on: December 01, 2013, 10:11:57 AM »
Do you wish you could get more organic, local veggies?

Then you should join Farm Spot http://farmspot.org/about/  We are a Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) program and our members pick up their shares at St. Mark's Church (33-50 82nd Street, Jackson Heights) from 5:30-8:15 on the Thursdays noted below.  Members are asked to volunteer a minimum of 2 hours of time during the winter distribution if they purchase any share. Most volunteer shifts involve working at distribution which is a great way to meet folks and get to know more about the CSA model.  Winter Season is a little different than the summer season which lasts 26 weeks and is weekly from June to November, but it is a great way to get a little taste of CSA to see if you like it. 

We have a lot of great options for the Winter Season including the Golden Earthworm Winter Shares (roots & greenhouse greens) Briermere Farm (apple and cider shares), Winter Sun Farm Flash Frozen Summer Veggie Shares and now Village Fishmonger CSF Fish Shares!! Below are the dates for the 5 Winter Share distributions which take place every 3 weeks. Mark you calendars!!

1 - December 19
2 - January 9
3 - January 30
4 - February 20
5 - March 13

Golden Earthworm WINTER SHARES - Sign Up Here! http://www.farmigo.com/join/farmspot/winter2014
The Golden Earthworm farm is nestled on 40 acres of pristine land protected by a land preservation program on the North Fork of Long Island. The farm was started in 1996 by Matthew Kurek, a chef turned farmer with a passion for growing beautiful, high-quality organic produce for the communities of Long Island. Since 2005, the farm has been co-owned and managed by Matthew and long-time friend and fellow farmer James Russo. Together, they grow over fifty different vegetables, berries and herbs for a CSA program with 14 local sites and 2 farmers’ markets. www.goldenearthworm.com


Golden Earthworm WINTER VEGETABLE SHARE - Certified Organic
Approximately 15-18lbs of mixed root crops and greens (see list below)  $150.00
Winter Share vegetables may include any of the following: Sweet Potatoes, Carrots, Beets, Potatoes, Rutabaga, Kohlrabi, Watermelon Radish, and possibly Kale.

Briermere Farms WINTER APPLE SHARE - Non-Organic
3lbs mixed apple varieties $25.00

Briermere Farms WINTER CIDER SHARE - Non-Organic
1/2 gallon fresh-pressed cider $24.50

Briermere Farms is located just a short drive away from Golden Earthworm Farm on Sound Avenue in Riverhead. The farm produces a wide variety of tree fruit and berries. Although Briermere Farms is not certified organic, some of their crops, including raspberries, blueberries and blackberries are grown in a way which would fulfill the organic standards.

Long Island is a difficult region for tree fruit production because of the high humidity levels which contribute to fungus disease pressure, and can decimate fruit quality (and sometimes the whole tree). At Briermere Farms, insecticides and fungicides are used only when needed to produce marketable fruit. The tree fruit is sprayed with 1 or 2 synthetic insecticides and fungicides at some point typically earlier in the season. No herbicides are used underneath the trees, and on-farm produced compost from leaves is the only fertilizer/nutrient source for their fruit crops. 

To Sign-up for Golden Earthworm and Briermere Shares - Click Here  http://www.farmigo.com/join/farmspot/winter2014


WINTER SUN FARM - Sign Up Here! http://www.farmigo.com/join/wintersunfarms/winter2013-2014

Winter Sun Farms is a small business based in New Paltz, NY that is committed to local food, sustainability, and fair prices for farmers and their food. They provide shares of flash frozen local summer vegetables and berries to members in the Hudson Valley and New York City throughout the winter months.

Members pick up their shares every 3 weeks at Farm Spot. On each package you will see the name of the farm that the veggies came from and their harvest date, so you know exactly where your food is coming from. At each pick up members will get 7 items, including 5 frozen packages, 1 jar of tomato puree, and a pound of fresh pea shoots! One share costs $149 and is a good size for a family of 2-3.

This year's share includes: Frozen Blueberries, Peppers, Sweet Corn, Green Beans, Edamame, Broccoli, Cauliflower, Collard Greens, Kale, Butternut Squash Puree, Jarred Tomato Puree, Fresh Pea Shoots

To see recipes with share items, who their farm partners are for this upcoming season, visit their website here!


VILLAGE FISHMONGER CSF - Sign Up Here! http://csa.farmigo.com/join/villagefishmonger/CSF
The Village Fishmonger's community-supported fishery ("csf") is the newest Farm Spot partner. They are a program where a member signs up for shares of seafood that village fishmonger gets directly from local fishermen. Operating year-round, the village fishmonger csf is your best source for weekly, delicious, fresh, responsibly harvested seafood.

Share Composition
Shares will consist of mostly filleted finfish and sometimes shellfish or other ocean species. if you're interested in receiving shellfish more regularly, we also have add-on shares for oysters, clams and scallops that you can choose from to supplement your main share. 
Unlike produce or animals that are farmed, the availability and variety of wild-caught fish is dependent on a number of factors even beyond season, such as weather patterns, fishing location, ocean currents etc. we’ll be choosing what’s best and available based on our fishermen’s recommendation and let you know what’s available via the today's catch section on our website. but we’ll do our best to diversify the species you receive for the duration of your subscription so you can explore the rich and diverse selection of seafood that are sustainably managed in the ny region.

Share Sizes
there are 2 share sizes to choose from:

medium share
~2 lbs. (or equivalent value) at $30 per distribution, good for 2 meals for 2 people
small share
~1 lb. (or equivalent value) at $15 per distribution, good for 1 meal for 2 people

To learn more about this new partner visit their website here! http://www.villagefishmongernyc.com

If you have any questions about the sign up process or program, email us at farmspot@gmail.com


We hope you will join us!!

Neighborhood Chat / Farm Spot sign up's extended to April 30th!! Sign up Now!!
« on: April 21, 2013, 09:38:24 PM »

We have extended the deadline to sign up for Farm Spot!!!

Are you interested in the local organic food movement?  Would you like to support a local farming family?  Want an easy way to drop off your compost?  Then consider joining Farm Spot this summer!

Click here http://www.farmigo.com/join/farmspot/summer2013 to sign up for a season of local organic produce! As always we want to try to get the funds to the farm as early in the season as possible to help prepare for the season ahead.

For Vegetables, we partner with The Golden Earthworm Organic Farm, in Jamesport Long Island (on the North fork) whose focus is on growing beautiful Certified Organic produce for their CSA members. Each week they take a survey of their fields, and harvest the crops that are at their peak and ready to be picked. They wash and pack the produce into individual boxes for every member and deliver them to St. Marks Church in Jackson Heights (33-50 82nd Street). Our harvest season runs for 26 weeks from the last day in May through November.

For fruit we partner with Briermere Farms. Their fruit, although not organic are minimally treated and are also picked at the height of freshness!

If you join, you will also have the option of ordering a la carte from Lewis Waite Farms, who bring together products from many upstate NY farms and sell them collectively here’s a list of the products you can order from them!  If you join you will also be able to order a Dairy Share from Milk Not Jails who are offering a dairy share again this year, we will do registration for this share once the fruit and vegetable registration has been completed.

Below are the Vegetable and Fruit share types, for all of the share details, click here. http://farmspot.org/share-types/

Vegetable Share is $587.08 26 weeks - May 30th through Dec. 5th
Fruit Share is $168.00 The value of each fruit share is deducted from the total season’s payment and fruit is delivered weekly until the full value has been delivered.
Herb Share 12 weeks $51.87
Veg. Subsidized Share A is 401.44 (please check the income criteria before signing up for the subsidized shares, this is on the honor system)
Veg. Subsidized Share B is 239.72

Tomato Shares - Golden Earthworm will be taking orders for tomato shares directly through their website when they open it up in August. They will only open up the application process once they know how many shares they will have. This will avoid the issues we experienced with the tomato shares last season.

Here is the link to the application. http://www.farmigo.com/join/farmspot/summer2013  Sign up now!!

Distribution happens every Thursday between the hours of 5:30 and 8:15 p.m. at St. Mark’s Church at 33-50 82nd Street, Jackson Heights. Enter through the 82nd Street gate, and come through the backyard. In good weather, we’ll be outside; in the rain, snow or cold, we’ll be indoors on the first floor.
 
For more information, Here is a link to our FAQ’s http://farmspot.org/faqs/  which list tons of information about how the program works!

Here are two important links, if you sign up, please save these for future reference.

« on: March 31, 2013, 09:16:07 PM »
Hi Jackson Heights!

Are you interested in the local organic food movement?  Would you like to support a local farming family?  Want an easy way to drop off your compost?  Then consider joining Farm Spot this summer!

We are now ready to sign up members for Summer 2013 for another great year of local organic produce! We will be accepting applications until April 15th. As always we want to try to get the funds to the farm as early in the season as possible to help prepare for the season ahead.

If you want to know more about the CSA movement, click here.http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community-supported_agriculture

For Vegetables, we partner with The Golden Earthworm Organic Farm, in Jamesport Long Island (on the North fork) whose focus is on growing beautiful Certified Organic produce for their CSA members. Each week they take a survey of their fields, and harvest the crops that are at their peak and ready to be picked. They wash and pack the produce into individual boxes for every member and deliver them each Thursday evening between 5:30 and 8:15 to St. Marks Church in Jackson Heights (33-50 82nd Street). Our harvest season runs for 26 weeks from the last day in May through November.

For fruit we partner with Briermere Farms.  Their fruit, although not organic are minimally treated and are also picked at the height of freshness!

If you join, you will also have the option of ordering a la carte from Lewis Waite Farms, who bring together products from many upstate NY farms and sell them collectively here’s a list of the products you can order from them!

Below are the Vegetable and Fruit share types, for all of the share details, click here http://farmspot.org/share-types/.

Vegetable Share is $587.08 26 weeks - May 30th through Dec. 5th
Fruit Share is $168.00 The value of each fruit share is deducted from the total season’s payment and fruit is delivered weekly until the full value has been delivered.
Herb Share 12 weeks $51.87
Veg. Subsidized Share A is 401.44 (please check the income criteria before signing up for the subsidized shares, this is on the honor system)
Veg. Subsidized Share B is 239.72

Tomato Shares - Golden Earthworm will be taking orders for tomato shares directly through their website when they open it up in August. They will only open up the application process once they know how many shares they will have. This will avoid the issues we experienced with the tomato shares last season

Here is the link to sign up!!  http://www.farmigo.com/join/farmspot/summer2013.  Sign up now, shares sell out quickly!


Here are two important links, if you sign up, please save these for future reference.
Account Management:
http://www.farmigo.com/account/farmspot/summer2013

Additional Payments:
http://www.farmigo.com/pay/farmspot/summer2013

If you have any questions, don’t hesitate to email us and ask at farmspot@gmail.com

We hope you will all join us this Summer!

Warmly,
The Farm Spot Core Group

Restaurants & Food / Le Gamin Comes to Jackson Heights this Sunday!
« on: June 27, 2009, 10:12:02 AM »
Last week my husband and I were in Park Slope and met the owner of Le Gamin, a French
restaurant in the East Village and in Prospect Heights, Brooklyn. He has a
mobile truck that he drives to the Union Square farmer's market and the
farmer's market in Park Slope, Brooklyn every week. We told him that we had
a thriving farmer's market in Jackson Heights as well as a play street on
Sundays where families are known to linger and work up an appetite. After
some lively discussion, and some delicious french fries, they have agreed to
bring their truck this Sunday to Jackson Heights!!

If you have a penchant for croissants, croque monsieurs, crepes and crave
vendor variety beyond Mister Softy, please come on Sunday and buy some
french food! They even have ice cream and Nutella.

Check out their menu:

http://legamin.com/LG_MOBI_MENU.html

-Braden & Sarah
