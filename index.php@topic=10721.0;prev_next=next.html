I live in Roosevelt Terrace.  I hear the wait for parking can be over a year.  I've been waiting over 2 years for the bike room, and from what I hear will be waiting another 2 years.  That said - the building is fine, maintenance is  fine - the usual glitches, misscommunications, and waits you'd expect in any large complex, also depending who your neighbors are.  The building is very clean - never had any problems.  There have been various grumbles - first about the elevators and when they were being repaired - the long waits, elevator going down on the weekend, but there are two elevators, and I really didn't find it to be a big deal.  I get no noise from the apartment above or below - some from the apartments to the sides.  There is also an ongoing grumble where some people seem to be reporting the building whenever there is a problem rather than trying to work through the management company and the building gets fined.  There was also something about a few really difficult tenants.

Overall fine, though.  Do be aware that at some point that building on 37th and 85th will most likely be torn down and replaced with a much taller building like the one next door.  Also to be honest, if I were buying again I'd think about the pros and cons of being in a small versus a large apartment complex.

If you have any specific questions please post them.

I know two long term owners and both are pleased with the complex.  Neither plan on ever leaving so they're not at all concerned with the 25% flip tax which will be paid by their estate.  This flip tax has kept the maintenance quite low which is a plus.  They plan to build a fitness center in one of the buildings.  There's a large lot they own (close to 37th Ave) that they plan to turn into a residence garden.  I also understand that Verizon paid $1 million dollars to place an antenna on the roof about 3-4 years ago.  I've always been impressed with the grounds and how well the buildings are maintained.  Good Luck.

A decade or so back when I was looking, I gave serious thought to Roosevelt Terrace. As others have mentioned, the buildings are well maintained, the terraces were a plus as was the ability to get a parking spot (with a reasonable wait list).

I remember what threw me off was the lack of inventory of larger apartments, I wanted something 1200sqft+ and the flip-tax. All I came across at RT were ~800sqft Junior 4's.

In the end, I ended up with an apartment that also has a 25% flip-tax (of profits), in slightly more than 1200sqft, but outside of JH (I am on the Woodside/Sunnyside border).
