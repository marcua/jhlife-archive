I did some research about the insurance issue and it appears that as soon as the kids are supervised by an adult at all times while being in the playroom it would not increase the insurance. There's no greater insurance risk than if they were moving along the corridors or elevators.

Also, it is a trend in Manhattan and having a playroom for the kids in your building does increase the value of the property as well as being used as a sell point.

The biggest obstacle to a playroom/gym is always the space for it, so if your building counts with one it's not really expensive to start with the basics.

I know there's some buildings in the area that have them. I would so much like to have a glimpse of them to have an idea.

The Berkeley now has activities for The Berkeley children in a huge meeting room that we have in the basement.  There have been several events, which I have not participated in, but understand they have included reading, arts, crafts, songs and play, in addition to a terrific Halloween and Christmas Party all geared toward the families of The Berkeley and of course the children.

Our beautiful gardens, have most recently been designed by our volunteer residents, including the Berkeley children.  The children set aside an area where they were able to start their vegetable garden.  Not sure how that's going, but I understand it was a terrific venture.  There is as well an herb garden.

The older folks love coming out and seeing the new gardens while the children play.  It's quite a joy to be able to meet our neighbors, and bring the children, and spend some time together without being hassled.  The noise level is not excessive and those in the gardens have been able to adjust.  There have been a few issues, but communication through our newsletter has been excellent.

So for now we do have a room that has planned activities for our Berkeley children, in addition to our gardens that welcomes all our residents and children.  The children are learning how to respect their gardens and are learning how to respect others around them who are trying to as well enjoy the gardens.  It's a process, but the children are doing rather well, and so are the adults.

The new board has done a magnificent job in making it all come together by changing the purpose of our gardens and utilizing our basement space to accomodate the little ones.

I believe many at The Berkeley are feeling a great sense of community that we have never enjoyed.
