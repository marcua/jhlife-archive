Title: Re: Wheelchair rentals and handicap-friendly activities
Post by: bellabella on February 24, 2011, 06:36:10 PM
Wheelchair rentals are very expensive. I would suggest you purchase a lightweight standard wheelchair. It doesn't have all the bells/whistles like swing away foot rests or calf pads but they are much cheaper and lighter and fold easy. Rite aid,CVS and other pharmacies usually have them on sale for under $100. You might also look at craigslist. We've given away ALOT of medical supplies (most new) and they always give them away on craigslist or sell cheaply. Acess-A-Ride is unreliable and I do believe you need to be a nyc resident and go through alot of red tape. There are alot of buses that go into the city and the MTA has a site where they list the accessible  train stations (also check to make sure the elevators are working) You might also consider getting her folding canes (if she doesn't already have any) so if she does decide to use the chair to rest the canes are easier to hold. I would steer clear of those big walkers with the three wheels and hand brakes I find they go too fast and sometimes the persons grip isn't too great for the brakes. Just a few thoughts. Enjoy your mom's visit!!
