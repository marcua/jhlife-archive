Neighborhood Chat / Pedestrian Safety Town Hall
« on: February 03, 2014, 11:07:47 PM »
Please join Congressman Crowley, Assemblymember Moya, Council Members, and Make Queens Safer for a Pedestrian SafetyTown Hall this Sunday from 4-6PM.

 It will be at the Renaissance School.

We have invited Reps from the 115 Precinct, the 108, DOT, DOE, Transportation Alternatives, and hopefully some PTA's or School Principals and hope that a series of questions and answers will help our community figure out how to Make Queens Safer.



Our group, MQS has a focus on education. We don't understand why the Dept. of Ed wasn't included in the Mayor's Plan for Vision Zero. School safety is important, and our kids' first steps to learning, should be safe first steps to school.



Beyond that, there are issues of street re-design, safe routes, slow zones and the ever important need to educate both drivers and pedestrians about how to practice street safety.



If you have questions, or even better, if you have answers, please join us.

Neighborhood Chat / Gianaris Press Conference on Unlicensed Drivers
« on: January 26, 2014, 09:46:39 AM »
Please join if you can.
Senator Gianaris to Renew Call for Suspended License Driver Crackdown After Another Deadly Crash in Queens

 

(Queens, NY) In the wake of yet another deadly crash in Queens involving a driver with a suspended license, Senator Michael Gianaris will join elected officials, community groups and transportation safety advocates to push for swift passage of his legislation cracking down on chronically reckless drivers with suspended licenses. This renewed effort follows the tragic death of Angela Hurtado, killed by a driver with a suspended license making an illegal turn. Senator Gianaris will be joined by Assemblymember Marge Markey, who will introduce the legislation in the State Assembly, Councilwoman Liz Crowley, Transportation Alternatives Executive Director Paul Steely-White, Make Queens Safer, the Maspeth Chamber of Commerce, and COMET (Communities of Maspeth and Elmhurst Together).

 


WHAT:

Senator Gianaris, electeds, community groups call for swift passage of legislation cracking down on suspended license drivers

 

WHEN:

Sunday, January 26, 2014

1:30 PM

 

WHERE:

Grand Avenue and 69th Place, Queens

Neighborhood Chat / Make Queens Safer
« on: December 05, 2013, 09:51:32 PM »


Hi. A few weeks have passed since our Three Children Too Many March brought attention to the lack of police enforcement, and street calming devices in our neighborhood. The families we honored, who suffered the loss of a child due to reckless driving have received some of the attention they deserve in City politics and policy making. We are gearing up to a bigger campaign and invite you to follow us on facebook. The group is called Make Queens Safer. We will be working with Assemblyman Moya, and Council Members Dromm and Ferraras in the coming months in order to be in the forefront of the Pedestrian Safety movement that is coming to NYC.




Offer your suggestions, cite what needs fixing, or simply follow along, as we work to keep our loved ones safe on our streets and in our community.

Thanks.

https://www.facebook.com/makequeenssafer

Neighborhood Chat / Three Children Too Many- March for Pedestrian Safety
« on: October 27, 2013, 11:27:47 PM »
I had this posted in Community groups section, but there wasn't even one comment. Maybe here you'll feel more at home chatting your suggestions or support. Thanks. Feel free to RSVP to the yahoo group- 3 Children too Many.

ok gang,
Some neighbors are organizing a March called Three Children too Many. Here is the original post on JHfamilies. If you would like to help with the organizing, have money to offer for signs, buttons, or educational materials, or if you have contacts in the media who might like to know...Or...Or....Or....if you simply think it's a good effort for JH, please let us know by joining the yahoo group or commenting here. Thanks so much.

WHERE: In front of Langston Hughes Library at 100th Street & Northern Boulevard

WHEN: Tuesday 11/12/13 at 5pm

 

Please come and bring your families so that together we can gather our united strength as we ask for our 3 Points.




1-WE must have more resources for Police enforcement of existing traffic laws.

2- WE must have more traffic-calming zones.

3-WE must have continued traffic-safety education for the children of our community.

 

Last December a young boy was killed on the corner of 81st and Northern Boulevard while walking to school one morning. With that bitter loss still on our minds, 3 weeks ago another young man had his life cut short at Broadway and 58th Street. 2 weeks later, again, this time a small boy, not yet three years-old was struck dead at the fast intersection of Junction and Northern Boulevards.

 

We repeat, "No More!"




Please come and lend your support to the other families of our community. Please March with your neighbors as we travel down Northern Boulevard to the 115th Precinct and then on to 82nd Street as we move towards the Post Office at 79th and 37th Avenue. With song and voices raised high we will be joined by community action-facilitators to point us towards  greater safety for our children.

 

Much has been done. We need more and we need it NOW!

Let there be no mistake that 3 children dead for walking through the streets of our community is 3 Children Too Many.




Please join us at www.3-children-too-many@yahoogroups.com for continued updates and more ongoing information about how to access safer roads for your family.




Meet at 5PM Tuesday November 12 at 100th St & Northern Blvd

OR join us ON OUR ROUTE on Northern Blvd.or 82nd Street or at 37th Avenue & 79th Street.

 

We will march Rain or Shine.

 

Please do not miss this important opportunity to support safety in your community.

 

Whether you own a car or use public transportation, we all walk.

Let us tame the recklessness that plagues our residential streets with important safety measures.

Our children deserve no less.

Neighborhood Chat / Healthy Streets Solutions!
« on: October 21, 2013, 07:25:15 PM »


Hi Neighbors,

A bunch of us are in the early organizing stages for a March, entitled "Three Children too Many" pedestrian injuries and death in our neighborhood. Information on that will be forthcoming in the next day or so. Please join us, it is important.

 

As I was researching things for the March, I came across a Meeting scheduled for Tomorrow to be held at Make the Road New York. I found the flyer at the Community Board 3 meeting on Thursday, and wanted to check a few things out before I posted. Here is the info from the flyer...

 

Do you need recreational space for Children?

Are cars speeding through your blocks?

How can you make your street safe and more comfortable??

Play Streets

Speed Cameras for school zones

Street Design Improvements

 

Let's mobilize your community to create safer, healthier and more vibrant streets.

Tuesday, 10/22

4 to 6PM.

Make the Road New York

92-10 Roosevelt Ave

 

Yes!! Yes!!! Yes!!! I say...Let's get it all. But, it is so difficult to get information, this meeting would be perfect for us as a community. So, I contacted Transportation Alternatives and they will do this meeting again in November. If anyone can attend tomorrow, please do. There is a lot happening in Jackson Heights and we need to be aware of it, in order to be a part of the solutions!

For example, did you know-

*We can try to get funds and resources to have Northern Blvd deemed a "Congested Corridor"

*Three schools in our neighborhood have been chosen for the Safe Routes to School program from DOT. Others are considered 2nd tier priority P.S. 69 among them.

*JH has been chosen for a neighborhood Slow Zone, but the area currently covered just isn't big enough

 

This is an informational post for you. If you would like to get involved we will be organizing as Three Children too Many.

 

Our first goal is the March, and like I mentioned, someone is going to post about it soon.

 

Our second goal is campaigning for safer streets, and the meeting mentioned above will really help us create a vision and find the proper resources.

 

Please let me know if anyone can make it tomorrow.

We will have a separate yahoo group for Three Children too Many, so as not to burden this listserve in the future.

Thanks for your time. I hope this interests you all.
