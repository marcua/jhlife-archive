Hi,

I'll be honest and tell you that I think you will have trouble finding a two bedroom close to the express train or in the historic district without paying a broker fee.  The nicer buildings generally use brokers. 

However, you could get lucky and find a sublet in one of the condo buildings with no fee.  But again, I think owners generally use brokers becasue it makes their lives easier and the tenant is the one paying the fee, so what do they have to lose? Just check craiglist every single day - that will help your odds of finding what you are looking for. 

I do often see ads for no-fee apartments stuck to light posts in the neighborhood, but they are usually for places outside the range of streets you prefer. 

I don't know what to tell you - apartment hunting in NY is the pits.  But maybe with the market being what it is right now you could negotiate a much lower fee with a broker.  Another thing to consider is that many of the rental buildings are rent stabilized, so you wouldn't have to worry about a huge increase each year.  A condo sublet wouldn't have the same benefit and you could end up paying more in rental increases than if you had paid a fee elsewhere. 

good luck in your search, and I'm sorry I don't have something helpful to offer.
