Quote from: greatelmhurst on September 14, 2016, 12:36:13 PM
in terms of amenities, location and financials(low maintenace), what do you think the best building is?

I don't think that low maintenance is the only way to measure good financials.  There are buildings with maintenance fees that are higher than what you'd find in an original coop but that does not necessarily mean the coop with the higher maintenance is in poor financial health.

One thing I have heard about smaller coops, where there might only be 5 to 10 apartments that make up an entire coop, is that if something major needs to be fixed, the assessment on each individual apartment can be very high.

My comments are just based on my layperson's understanding of coop finances, so of course it's best to educate yourself as much as possible before buying.  Or maybe you already have owned a coop in the past and already know what you're getting into.

If not, there is a website called The Cooperator that has useful articles.  Also sometimes the Times runs articles like
That Riveting Co-op Financial Statement

If your order of priority is as you state -- amenities, location and financials (low maintenance) - I imagine you will be a daily commuter to Manhattan which makes the buildings on 34th Avenue such as The Towers, Chateau and Terrace View off-putting because of the 15-20 min walk to the subway.

Some of the buildings which I like are:
1) Colton - this is a condo with a great rooftop terrace, doorman and a bike room. Located on 35th Avenue and 76th St so less than 10 min walk to the subway.
2) Hawthorne Court - private elevator into your apartment, wood burning fireplaces, private garden courtyard
3) Belvedere East and West - great private garden with a live fish pond, large apartments and also close to the subway.
4) Linden Court - on 37th Ave, also a garden apartment complex, and great historic details.

The biggest issue you will run into is inventory. Three bedrooms don't come up often and when they do, expect jaw-dropping pricing ala http://www.mpcproperties.com/queenscooplistings.php?cid=196&m=1&i=390

As far as maintenance goes, if you are paying < $.85/psf, then I would say it is pretty reasonable.

In terms of subway access, there's also the buses to get you there. I have to admit I don't use them during rush hour but when I take them at other times they are usually on time and most have a GPS for apps. I'm less enamored of the Q32 and Q33 just because the route they take has more traffic and is often a little slower, but both get you to the 82nd St. station on the 7. 74th St. is a terminal for the Q49, Q32, & Q33, so a quick check on an app on your phone can tell you whether or not a bus that goes near your building is leaving soon, or if taking the escalator up to the 7 train is a better option.

I don't know about the buses that run along Northern or where they take you (likely down to Queens Plaza, no?)
