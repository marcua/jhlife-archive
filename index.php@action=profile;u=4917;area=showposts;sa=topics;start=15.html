Restaurants & Food / Ricky's
« on: January 13, 2018, 07:11:45 PM »
I've been pissing and moaning about the lack of good, basic, healthy, non-spicy, non-carb-heavy dining options in the neighborhood. Our forays to the local diners have been less than inspiring, and there just isn't a sit-down place where you can get, say, roast or grilled chicken or baked salmon with potato and vegetables, and eat in a pleasant, comfortable space and not off plastic trays. (Let me interject that we need non-spicy, non carb-heavy food for health reasons, not because we are somehow against the cuisines of other nations for xenophobic reasons.)

So we just tried ordering dinner from Ricky's after having done so just once a few years ago, with unsatisfactory results. We were very pleased! Ordering via Seamless, it was fast and accurate and the food was quite good. Salad and vegetables, in particular. We ordered full chicken and fish dinners--and the food was well prepared and tasty and generously portioned. I think the best alternative for this type of basic food in Jackson Heights. Recommended!

Neighborhood Chat / Pharmacy Chains vs Mom and Pops
« on: February 17, 2015, 04:14:31 PM »
I am all for the Mom and Pop shops, personal service, etc, but there are a few things that are unfortunately pushing me to give more of my business to the chains vs the small pharmacies—even though I am philosophically opposed to the takeover of chain drug stores.

One, the convenience of longer hours. I had some items I needed a few Sundays ago, and only a small amount of time in which to get them. I went by Frank's Pharmacy, but they were just locking up at 3 PM. So I went to Duane Reade.

Second, the quality of goods. I went to Frank's Pharmacy in December to buy all my gift wrap and gift tags. The only gift wrap they carried was so extremely substandard, thin, and cheap cheap cheap, it disintegrated in your hands when you tried to wrap it around a box. I literally could not do anything with it -- it kept tearing. So when I needed gift wrap for Valentine's Day, I again went to Duane Reade.

We had been loyal Maram customers and went nowhere else while they were still around. But now--I'm pretty much going to Duane Reade. And that's how it happens.

I wish that Frank's—for one example because I am not really familiar with the others—would step up the quality of some of their merchandise. I know people love the place. I had not used them before, and I have to say I have been disappointed in this regard. As for the service at the pharmacy itself, I have found Duane Reade and Frank's to be equally responsive and nice and personalized and helpful.
