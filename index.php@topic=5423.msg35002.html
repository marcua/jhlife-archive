According to the indictment, Monserrate played a role in the group's selection of board members and staff.

He then had the council allocate $300,000 to the group as part of the discretionary funds that council members have to give out. Next, Monserrate directed the group, known as LIBRE, to help with his Senate campaign.

Monserrate and others "made specific material representations and material omissions to the city and to the council so that LIBRE would receive funding for services it provided in support of the 2006 Senate campaign," the indictment states.

Some LIBRE executive directors also submitted expense reports to the city in which they failed to disclose the group had spent money on the campaign. One, Javier Cardenas, pleaded guilty yesterday to mail fraud and conspiracy charges and is cooperating with prosecutors

In addition, LIBRE helped with a council re-election bid for Monserrate in 2005, paying thousands of dollars to canvass residents of his district, according to the indictment.

In 2006, LIBRE conducted a voter-registration drive using money allocated by Monserrate, the indictment says.

"Monserrate used LIBRE's voter-registration activities to his personal political advantage in the 2006 Senate campaign," it says. The advantage came from having the database of eligible voters from the registration drive.

The city cut off funding to the group years ago when questions were raised about how it was spending taxpayer contributions, WNBC-TV reported
