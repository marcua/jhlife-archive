Neighborhood Chat / Re: 84-01 37 Ave to be Heard by Landmarks Commission
« on: December 05, 2010, 08:59:05 PM »
The Landmarks Preservation Commission (LPC) review will be this Tuesday, December 7.  The following points relate directly to the work the LPC does and, therefore, will likely receive consideration if mentioned (as opposed to issues outside the scope of the LPC)--
 
* The building as designed does not provide green space along the 84th St. faÃ§ade unlike nearly every other similarly positioned 6-story building in Jackson Heights.
 
* The building copies Georgian Hall, which is directly across 84th Street, too literally.  Simply copying what is across the street shows laziness in design, defying our neighborhood's tradition of unique and identifiable buildings.
 
* The building will block access to light and air for the southernmost windows of 35-43 84th St.  Although "lot line windows are not guaranteed"--as we have heard countless times, blocking access runs contrary to Jackson Heights's reason for existing, at its inception and even today: to help residents escape the dark, towering, and cramped style of city living by allowing for open space, light, and air.
 
* The Community Board was not adequately informed of the materials being used for this building before they voted.
 
The LPC may require the owner to change his plans to address these issues, but we need to make these points clear to them.  It's easy to get involved.  Here are some ways this can be done:

Speak at the Hearing
 
The LPC meets on Tuesday, December 7 at 1 Centre Street, 9th Floor Conference Room, the application for 84-01 will be heard at 3:45 pm and they are asking speakers to show up at 3 pm. 
 
Council Member Dromm and other community leaders will be speaking.  If you have the time, I would highly encourage you to speak at the hearing as long as it relates to the landmark issues and not, e.g., the nature of the fire.  Also, so we can be sure to hit all the points, please approach Council Member Dromm's staff to let them know you are interested in speaking.

Send a Written Statement
 
If you can not attend the hearing, write a statement focused on the landmark issues.  Let Council Member Dromm or his staff know (718-803-6373) so they can get the letter to the LPC.

Sign and Distribute the Petition
 
There is an online petition that can be used as well as a hard copy floating around: http://www.ipetitions.com/petition/840137thave/

Take it to the Press
 
This week's Queens Chronicle ran the following short-sighted and uninformed editorial, towards the bottom of the page and entitled "Save an Empty Lot?":

http://www.zwire.com/site/news.cfm?newsid=20451865&BRD=2731&PAG=461&dept_id=576258&rfi=6

If you are so inclined, please contact the editor to let him/her know how you feel.


Thanks!

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Jackson Heights Sees Surge In Alleged Anti-Gay Crimes
« on: September 06, 2010, 09:56:13 PM »
Thanks for the advice about being careful.  I know it came out of neighborly concern.  I will reiterate your point but with a few caveats.  I want to be clear I am not blaming the victim for not taking precautions.  Too often, victims, especially of gender- and sexual orientation-based violence, get blamed.

Regardless of the motivation behind the incidents, I think everyone has the right to live in his or her community without the threat of violence at any hour.  It is a sad state--that has unfortunately existed in many areas of this country to varying degrees at varying times--that people need to cautioned to walk outside.  I don't want to normalize such a situation.

Having said that, these incidents have a dimension that others do not, namely the element of hate and intimidation directed at not just an individual but a whole group.  Let's address the underlying issues.  One observation that I have made is the sheer boldness of the machismo out there and the general acceptance among some people.  What does it say when thousands of our neighbors vote for Hiram Monserrate AFTER his conviction and other troubles?

Also, what about all the anti-gay words I hear in Spanish walking in this neighborhood?  I just heard another one on Roosevelt Avenue tonight, probably directed at me.  The Colombians who live in my building have similarly used derogatory terms toward me and seemed shocked when I confronted them.  The use of the terms may be as racially motivated--my background is northern European--as they are motivated by perceived sexual orientation.  As my Spanish improves, so does my awareness of the depth and breadth of the problem.  I am not pinning all the blame on a certain group.  I have lived in many different places and homophobia manifests itself in different ways in each place.  There are non-Spanish-speaking people here who are also homophobic.  I am pointing out the issue that has made itself most obvious through my personal experiences in Jackson Heights.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Chifa Restaurant - new Peruvian / Asian fusion
« on: August 12, 2010, 11:44:24 PM »
I think it's great that many of our restaurants have really put Jackson Heights on the culinary map.  It's one of the reasons I love living here.  So, I am generally not one to speak up against a restaurant or another small business owner unless I feel something is seriously wrong.

I dined at Chifa last night and was utterly disappointed.

First, the wait for my food was tremendously long.  At a Peruvian (fast!)/Chinese (even faster!) restaurant, I am ordering the fastest item on the menu: a half chicken.  It takes a millisecond to chop my chicken with a cleaver.  Why am I waiting nearly 40 minutes?  The calamari I ordered as an appetizer was served before my chicken, but it was still a significant wait.  What came out was a soggy, MSG-infused sampling of junior squid bits.  Nothing like the crispy delight promised on the picture on the menu.

The wait was not due to the fact Chifa was busy.  There were at most four tables with customers and at least three people working the eating area.  One appeared to be helping the takeout customers.  As I left, one waiter was hunched over his fried rice at the bar-like area in the back.

I noticed that other customers were also waiting a long time.  The gentleman who came in after me and ordered something far more complex was served sooner than I was.  That's not saying much; he also waited a considerable amount of time.  Before he left, he turned to the couple sitting near him and appeared to be complaining (maybe about the food or the slow service).  I could be wrong.  Maybe I am transposing my frustration on him.

When I politely asked where my dinner was, the waiter asked, "Como?"  I didn't bother talking to any staff after that. Otherwise, the wait staff was not overtly rude and did refill my water and take my money fairly quickly.  I think they perceived I was irritated and wanted to leave.

What I was served was a lukewarm old chicken that was edible but still very clearly stewing in grease for a long time.  The wings were dried rock solid.  The rest of the chicken was slimy with fat and grease.  The first thought that came to my head is that they did not have chicken but, instead of telling me, made a grocery store run to buy one, as crazy as that sounds.  The green sauce that came with the chicken was the only saving grace since it helped the otherwise sad specimen of poultry go down.  I asked for ketchup and got a few packets from the waiter, who reached into the big cardboard box on the table across from mine.

The salad that came with my meal was a small side plate of chopped bits of old iceberg lettuce.  No visible dressing.  No other vegetables.

I looked around and the dishes the other diners had in front of them seemed only marginally more appetizing.

As for the atmosphere, it seems fairly clean.  Other than that, it was shoddily designed.  The row of booth seating against the wall is too narrow to allow even small people to sit comfortably.  The furniture seems cheap, like it was recycled from a 70's Chinese takeout.  The lighting fixtures seem new and modern and, thus, out of place.  The men's bathroom door is not even really a proper room door but, rather, a hinged door to a supply closet.  The walls and ceiling seem like they were left over from whatever was in there previously.  The Latin music--mainly covers of popular Latin music by other Latin musicians--was not too loud, but when added to the soap opera that was playing on the TV in the back of the eating area, it was too much.  The kitchen did not seem properly shielded from the public.  (Either purposely expose the kitchen or hide the kitchen at a sit-down restaurant.)  Boxes of supplies were on top of tables in the eating area.  The whole restaurant just looked hastily thrown together.

All of this would have been excusable had the food been as fabulous as indicated on JHL.  I see that, save two people, all of the firsthand comments seem to be from people who joined JHL specifically to created and puff up this thread.  Also, is it really right to attack Pio Pio if you are a business owner or manager, as I assume "chifa_restaurant" is?  Shouldn't you really be concerned with providing quality food, service, and overall experience regardless of what other restaurants around you are doing?  Restaurants are not cell phone service providers.  I have no connection to Pio Pio's owner--I like some things about that restaurant (quality of food) but have some minor beefs about other things--but please run your business properly before pointing your own very greasy finger at others.

Pets and Animals / Re: Raccoon Sighting
« on: May 15, 2010, 12:16:46 PM »
There are lots of raccoons around here.  When I lived on both 76th Street and 77th Street, just south of Broadway, I saw these creatures a lot.  Actually, I heard them fighting/mating more than I saw them.  There are gangs of raccoons in the quiet spaces between many of the apartment buildings.  I don't know the proper word for a group of raccoons, but I think "gang" accurately describes their behavior.

I also saw an opossum on my doorstep.  At first, I thought it was a mutant rat!

Looking for cool people from all over the world who definitely want to meet their neighbors?  Here are a few suggestions from my own experience.
