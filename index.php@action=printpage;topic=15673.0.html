Title: Re: We have a fuse box!
Post by: Shelby2 on November 26, 2016, 07:02:05 PM
I had a fuse box for several years before I switched over to a circuit breaker. I had the work done when I redid my kitchen, as it seemed to make sense to do it at that time. One thing the former owners had put in was a dedicated line for the a/c in the living room. I think if they hadn't done that, it would have been hard or maybe impossible to run a high-BTU a/c, which is what the living room needs due to its large size.

Title: Re: We have a fuse box!
Post by: Ed on November 29, 2016, 12:45:51 PM
We had our fusebox replaced with a circuitbreaker box back in 2003 by Queensborough Electric and it was only a few hundred dollars. They didn't have to run any new wiring to the panel, apparently that had been done some time in the 90s. But that is one area of concern, particularly if you're trying to upgrade your electrical load. You want to make sure the wiring in the riser and in the panel can handle the increased load, otherwise you can burn out (emphasis on BURN) the wiring inside walls or risers.
