They are VERY sweet and too much so for a lot of American palettes. You can buy by the half and quarter pound also so maybe ask for a sampling. My favorites are hot hot jilebis (the orange gooey pretzel-like things) and gulab jamuns (brown fried doughnuts in syrup) and rabri (a white pudding like thing very popular in winters among Punjabis) and soanpapdi (layers of a flaky sweet crust). But tastes vary one person to another. My mum loves rosgullahs (white balls in syrup and rose water) and I can't really stomach those too much. Good luck! Oh and the sweets will be great at this time of year - right before Diwali and Durga Puja.

I walked into Raja Sweets (37th Ave and 73rd St) this evening, looked at the big selection of sweets, and was overwhelmed.. nothing labeled and I had no idea where to begin.  I was about to walk out, when a really nice man who worked there asked if I needed help, so I decided I had to give something a try.  I pointed to a green square (color of pistachio with nuts on top--I figured it was pistachio).  Took home my $1 treat and it was pretty good.  It wasn't overly sweet (good thing--I'm not a fan of food that tastes like pure sugar, like that orange pretzel-looking thing referred to above).  My guess on what it was--seemed like a paneer (indian cheese) base, definitely pistachio in there and on top, and what looked like coconut?  There was some other taste to it--I was trying to decide if it was lemon or lime?  And maybe some type of spice that I've tasted in indian food--a subtle amount.  It was pretty good and I look forward to my next experiment!

When I first moved here several years ago, I went bonkers purchasing Indian sweets for a while.  I would purchase 4-6 various types every time I went to a party, and them dice them up into smaller pieces for people to sample.

Chocoholic and ignorant westerner that I am, my favorite type is chocolate burfi.  But I suspect this isn't very authentic.

On a whim, I walked into Maharaja on my way home from work last night.  There's always a massive crowd gathered around the sweets and I've always been too intimidated to order (although I have sat down several times for their delicious all-vegetarian lunch buffet).  There was nobody by the counter, so I decided to stop in... and I'm so glad I did!   I just got 3 pieces-- a burfi, halva and something paneer-based.  They all were delicious, and not as sweet as other indian sweets I have tried (including the same types at other places).  Still sweet, but not overbearingly sweet, and everything was also very fresh-tasting.

I'll definitely be back.. luckily it's not actually directly on my way back from the subway, so perhaps I'll be able to control myself a little:).
