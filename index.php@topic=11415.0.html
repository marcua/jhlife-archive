Can I surmise that you don't drive? Here are two scenarios, and the safer one is usually the one where you actually see a car passing under the red light:

1) driver is cruising, operating safely (that'd be me!), the light turns yellow, but going slow enough so that the light changes before the driver completes the intersection.
2) driver is cruising, the light turns yellow, the driver speeds up to get through the light before it turns red, YOU don't see a car running the red light, but the action is very unsafe.

I think the drivers you see making flagrant and heedless traffic infractions are those that get special treatment, such as cops and their relatives. I have heard people brag that they just show their NYPD card, whatever that means. 20mph, cameras, lights; what's to stop them?

Most drivers are scared silly to make a moving violation. It is pretty harsh punishment.

I say active enforcement of drivers who speed and fail to yield is the way to go. But it won't play out that way. The usual resolution to societal problems is to oppress everyone with more and more rules so that sooner or later some little old lady transgresses and, voila, we are doing our job. 

If the City is serious about safe roads, the NYPD need only do their job between the hours of 2 and 4 on a Saturday night. It is insane out there. Speeding, DOI, you name it. It's bad.
