Dealing with Discriminatory Businesses
« on: January 01, 2009, 05:39:43 PM »
One of the great things about the neighborhood is that, while no place is perfect, the general vibe is about making all people feel welcome.

One pervasive problem that makes me feel most unwelcome is the posting of discriminatory (mainly sexist) signs in many shop windows.  I can't walk a few blocks without seeing "Delivery boy wanted" or "Looking for female assistant" or some such illegal advertisement.

Does anyone have an idea on how to address this issue in a constructive way?  It is so ubiquitous that it does really seem to need an organized response.  I have raised the issue with Council Member Helen Sears, Chair of the Women's Issues Committee, but, of course, received no response.

Thanks for any ideas!

That's a tough one. I may suggest going to the JH Merchants Association and the Bangladeshi Merchants association. Or better yet talking to the Queens Chamber of Commerce about having them help you approach individual businesses to educate them on proper hiring practices.

If you approach from an educational rather than a punitive point of view you'll probably get a better reception. Also having the backing of the Chamber of Commerce or some such organization lends credibility to your cause. SO you don't come off like a crazy "activist" who yells at store owners about their bikes covering flowers... but that's for another thread.
