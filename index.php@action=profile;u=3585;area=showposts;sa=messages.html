News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Honoring the Heroes of Elmhurst
« on: May 08, 2021, 02:37:17 PM »
Queens World Film Festival is inviting everyone to celebrate the Heroes of Elmhurst and kick off International Nurses Week
on Monday, May 10 at 10:00am in front of Elmhurst Hospital Center at 79-01 Broadway, Elmhurst, NY 11373 (facing the The Frank D. O'Connor Playground).
 
As part of the honors, Queens World will present a handcrafted plaque and a few other nice things.
Please join us!  If you are interested, here is a FB event page:
https://www.facebook.com/events/190834982765110?ref=newsfeed

MONDAY MAY 10, 10:00 am
LOCATION: 79-01 Broadway, Elmhurst, NY 11373 (Facing the The Frank D. O'Connor Playground)
Run of Show:

10:00 am -- Community, hospital supporters and local filmmakers gather in the The Frank D. O'Connor Playground across the street from the hospital with their bells, whistles, pots and pans ready to show their appreciation for the Heroes of Elmhurst
10:25 am -- Appreciation Celebration with applause and cheers.
10:30 am --Welcome and Opening Remarks, Helen Arteaga Landaverde, Elmhurst CEO
Preston Cato of the Queens World presents Helen with the plaque on behalf of the festival
10:40--Elected Official Remarks and Proclamation Presentation                                     
10:50--Richard Calvache thanks Elmhurst for taking care of father Edgar Calvache, who was hospitalized at Elmhurst due to COVID-19
10: 55--Closing Remarks-Rose Green, NYSNA Rep
11:00—11:10--Program Concludes

Community Groups, Activities & Events / 9th Annual Queens World Film Festival - LGBT Friday Screenings
« on: February 16, 2019, 01:00:01 PM »
Friday March 22, 2019 
Zukor Theatre
35th Ave and 35th Street
Astoria, NY 11106
 
Filmmakers around the world are asking many of the same question: How do we love ourselves? How do we love others? Am I lovable? Am I capable of loving? Join us at the historic Zukor Theater for an entire evening of incredible films focused on knowing, being, wondering, living. The festivities include three separate screening BLOCKS:
 
BLOCK #1 6:00pm - It Gets Better: Six short films with messages of hope for our LGBTQ+ youth.
BLOCK #2 8:15pm - Out and About: Six Untold LGBTQ+ Stories. Soft R Rating.
BLOCK #3  10:30pm- Drag Dreams: Two films that assert being fabulous is never out of season.

Soft R Rating. More info at https://www.queensworldfilmfestival.com/events/2019/lgbtq_films/
