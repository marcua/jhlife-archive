Personally, I'm not actually as far-left as Ocasio-Cortez. I'm definitely more of a centrist. A number of the things she's called for would in fact mean higher taxes (Medicare for all isn't going to pay for itself). That being said, however, my wife and I recognize how fortunate we are, especially considering neither of us come from wealthy upbringings. And we believe other people who have yet to reach their goals should be afforded the opportunity to do so. Sometimes that means getting help from your neighbor. So, I’m fine with being taxed if it means we rise as a whole. I vehemently believe in the idea that you're only as strong as your weakest link, and that a test of your virtue is how you treat the most vulnerable among you. She may not represent MY day-to-day living. But she represents my ideals for how we should treat one another.

As for Crowley, I've honestly never known enough about him to care. And that's probably part of the problem. He DID take his re-election for granted. I was made abundantly clear of her stance on issues because of her pervasiveness online and on the streets of Jackson Heights. And to Lilybell's point, skipping the debate was a gross affront. But I think the nail in the coffin for me was their divergence on campaign funding. Her refusal to take corporate donations aligns with my beliefs 100%. There is WAY too much money in politics. And in a city that is increasingly defined by the whims of REBNY, her commitment to lead a grassroots campaign was a breath of fresh air.
