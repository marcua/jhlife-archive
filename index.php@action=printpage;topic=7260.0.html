On the in the previous post, Jeff quotes the developer as saying they'll serve "American" food.  I see no need for "American" food here, but if that's what they have in mind, here's my wish list of quintessentially American fast food joints that would be fun to have there:

1. First East Coast outpost of Tandoori Chicken USA (http://www.tcusamobile.com/main.php)... a longstanding cult favorite in the SF Bay Area.  Their Tandoori Chicken Sandwich is killer, and unlike anything currently available here.
2. First Queens outpost of The Kati Roll Company (http://www.thekatirollcompany.com)
3. Street food-style dosas (http://dosatruck.com/)
4. Korean (http://kimchitacotruck.com/press.html) or Indian (http://www.tacolassi.com/tacos_be_with_you/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=68&Itemid=466) style tacos
5. The reincarnation of Timmy O's Frozen Custard (http://www.yelp.com/biz/timmy-os-frozen-custard-corona)

Of course, less uniquely "American" concepts could also do well here:
6. A larger space for the existing 37th Road chaat house
7. A bubble tea vendor (http://www.facebook.com/quicklynyc)
8. Vietnamese sandwiches (http://www.vietnamese-sandwich.com/)



In this narrow window of time we have to fantasize about what a "Jackson Heights Food Court" might be like (before we're confronted with the disappointing reality), what would you like to see there?

Just following up on lalochezia's statement that we've discussed this before ... it's a combination of some of these things:

* Many properties owned by ethnic business assocs. which want to 'keep it in the family'
* Otherwise, very dense neighborhood has extremely low commercial vacancy rate
* When vacancies do appear, owners prefer to flip to national retail for higher rents
* The Census profile of the neighborhood makes it look like a low-income area, so retailers who go for higher-income demographics don't think they can build a market

It's sort of amazing that Espresso 77 exists, considering all of that.
