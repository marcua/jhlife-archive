JHELC and the greenspace NEEDS YOUR HELP!
« on: June 10, 2011, 04:12:27 PM »
Hello JHELC parents and community members:
 
WE NEED YOUR HELP!
 
Don't you love having a picnic in the grass on a warm summer day? JHELC is teaming up with Millamar Landscaping to create a  playspace that the children and community will enjoy even more! With your help we can say goodbye to the barren yard and hello to fresh cut grass.
 
 
JHELC  would also like to install 9 ceiling fans in the parish hall  to help circulate the fresh air and keep everyone cool and comfortable.  This project will help not only benefit our little friends at the preschool but all who use this space.
 
You can help us in several ways.
 
-- We are collecting change at the JHELC preschool in our Change for Change money drive.
 
--- You can also donate via our fundraiser website, http://jhelc.chipin.com/jhelc-play-yard-and-ceiling-fans.
 
---Spread the word.  Tell your friends and family about this special cause.
 
Our goal is to raise $5,000.00 by June 23rd.  Yes, we know its a short period of time, but when we work as a team we can do anything! If we meet our deadline, we can get started as soon as the children graduate.  Wouldn't it be wonderful if everyone returned to a great big green lawn!
 
Won't you please help us in this cause?

Good questions, PA.  I don't know details, but I'll try to add a bit more context.

JHELC is the Jackson Heights Early Learning Center, a preschool and summer camp with two locations in the neighborhood, one of which is at St. Mark's Church.  The lawn in question is the lawn at St. Mark's Church, which is used by JHELC, as well as by Farm Spot and many other community groups and events over the course of the year.  It is indeed one of the few patches of quasi-public grassy space in the neighborhood, and as such it suffers from overuse. 

St. Mark's Church has been generous in allowing groups the use of its grounds and facilities.  I'm not sure about the rules for use of the lawn, but I believe that the church encourages community members to visit its gardens, which are beautifully maintained by the neighborhood's Garden Club. 

I hope funds can be raised to improve the lawn, and will be contributing to the cause.

The gardens at St. Marks are maintained on a regular basis by 6 people, ranging in age from 16 to 82. 3 have full-time jobs elsewhere, 2 are teenagers and 1 is a senior citizen with a heart condition. Sounds like the painting 'The Spirit of '76' but they are very enthusiastic!

The play area has for the past 50+ years been open to responsible parties and the Jackson Heights Early Learning children. A few years ago, the Second Jackson Heights Film & Food Festival held the food component there. Good food, shade from the trees and live jazz on an early autumn weekend. That was a lovely community event that those who attended will remember fondly.

However, the toll taken on the ground is pretty severe when you add in the Flea Markets, CSA, general church activities and the hot weather.

It would be nice to see what a professional landscaper, Millamar, can come up with in terms of a sturdy living surface. Green, yay!
