Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I'd LOVE LOVE LOVE a bookstore in the neighborhood. I'd also like more restaurant options north of Northern...I know it's residential but sometimes I am too lazy after work to go to 37th Ave I am really happy with the diversity of food options though.
El Coyote has a great brunch special but it would be nice to have a few more options.
And for those looking for a great burger, there actually is a place in the neighborhood for awesome burgers...Cassidy's on 75th & 31st Ave.
I think that most people who desire these things really have to put their money where their mouth is if/when places we desire open up...I suspect that many of the places we say we'd like to have in the neighborhood would close for lack of consistent steady business. I know I am guilty of not shopping as often as I should or would like to at great local places.
I'd also really like a great public high school in Jackson Heights although by the time that ever happens, my daughter will probably be in college. It would also be great to have more public school options in general, it seems like every school is overcrowded.
