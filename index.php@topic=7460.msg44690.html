TEACHER BIO

Suran Song is a visual artist who, in 1999 when she took her first class yoga class, could not bend to touch her knees without tightness. She came to yoga skeptically and slowly: her Korean father was Professor of Eastern Philosophy at Bryn Mawr College where he taught a critical history of Asian religions outlining institutionalized misogyny and hypocrisy. As his approach was not popular in the atmosphere of the 1960's, he resigned his post to serve as Associate Director of the college library. Suran's Greek mother, also a librarian, merrily maintained Greek Orthodox traditions in the household.  Always curious about the sublime and civics, Suran majored in visual art, and traveled extensively for 10 years making performance art rock music.  In 2004, she found her yoga teacher, Pema Chakan, and Himalayan Yoga. Suran has taught yoga to people of all ages in many settings: at Trevor Day School, Asphalt Green, Middle Collegiate Church, Matrix Fitness, Oasis After-school, and Lenox Hill Neighborhood Center. The Himalayan Yoga Teacher training was an intensive three-year long course, which Suran completed in 2008. Suranâ€™s art and yoga practice is about bringing together opposites and the keeping sympathy: Suran makes art that uses yoga as medium. The inspiration of Sanskrit and the civics of politics influence her work as a painter and performance artist.  She is very excited to be awarded the 2012 Create Change Artist in Residence & Honorarium at The Laundromat Project here with JH Laundromat.
