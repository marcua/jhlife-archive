Yeah, its probably the grocery stores. I know a few people of South Asian decent who make the drive in from Long Island and Connecticut (or even just from parts of Brooklyn) to pick up items they can't find by their homes.

Quote from: theplanesland on February 12, 2018, 10:27:36 AM
Quote from: missmarty on February 12, 2018, 10:03:53 AM
The lack of any valuable traffic enforcement in our neighborhood is a key contributor. Have traffic studies been done recently? I am just an occasional driver, but as a pedestrian and cyclist, I find it hard to believe anyone can travel by car here. We should call for a traffic study of the the 37th, 35th, Northern, Roosevelt corridor, which would include the impact of for-hire vehicles.

The neighborhood is clogged beyond belief and double parking and circling blocks looking for parking are making it a quality of life issue. Also, as I walk blocks, I look at the number of out of state license plates and it averages about 7-10 per block.

Over the weekend I decided that we have a coveted restaurant neighborhood that people drive to from other neighborhoods. We also have too many food delivery services such as Fresh Direct, and probably an addiction to on-demand shopping such as Amazon.

It has gotten worse. I'm happy to live in a neighborhood others travel too, but there are opportunities to improve how they travel and park here. Schools with parking, such as I.S. 145, 89, and others could offer spots. The BID could offer a shuttle from subway. We could have taxi stands...

But enforcement is lacking and its causing a hell of a lot of unnecessary angst. And honking!

In my experience, it isn't the restaurants - it's the grocery stores. (But I live in the low 70s.) A lot of those out-of-area/out-of-state plates are shopping at the grocery stores along 73rd and 74th. Those merchants really should have provided parking, but they didn't ...
