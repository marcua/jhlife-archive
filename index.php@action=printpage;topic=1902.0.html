Title: Music on 82nd St.
Post by: normelrod on November 27, 2008, 11:57:21 PM
I live on 83rd St. between Roosevelt and 37th Ave. on the backside of the building overlooking the stores on 82nd St.  Every year, the stores play holiday music up and down the block.  It used to start after Thanksgiving and run through New Years.  This year it started last week.  The music plays all day, from about 9:00 am to about 9:00 pm, and I can hear it clearly in my apartment.

Because I work from home, I'm here pretty much all day, every day, and the music is driving me crazy.  I understand the idea is to put people in the mood to spend money.  But does it really help?  I feel like I live at the mall.  I'm just wondering if anyone is in the same situation, or has any idea what I can do about this.  Or do I just have to deal with it?
Title: Re: Music on 82nd St.
Post by: francis on November 28, 2008, 02:13:03 PM
I don't think you need to live with such a disturbance all day, although I'm not 100% sure.  There are laws that govern how loud music should be.  If I were you, I'd find out exactly what the law is.  If the stores are not within the law, call Helen Sears' office and make a formal compliant to her and the police dept. If it bothers yous neighbors, ask them to call as well.  I'd start by calling 311.  Good Luck!
