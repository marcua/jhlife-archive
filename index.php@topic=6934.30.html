I was thinking more about this issue.  I am also a relatively recent transplant to JH.
. When I looked here I was amazed at the spacious and elegant affordable apartments. With gardens to boot! Incredible value housing stock for New York. So I took the plunge.

It seemed to me that 37th Ave could easily have the potential to be a Smith Street or Court St in B'lyn.

Now I know that is quite unlikely...due to the situation with the commercial district landlords..no astute businessman in his right mind would open here with the exorbidant rents.

So I accept that if the commercial situation were different I wouldn't have been able to afford my apartment.  Apartment prices would also be higher. The second rate retail keeps the district affordable.

So in a way...it is both good for affordabilty for average income folks...and also bad for speculating...I have decided to love JH for what it does offer. And am happy to go to Manhattan or B'lyn...the transport is so good to JH...for what JH doesn't...and probably might never have.

thank you for the well considered post.

not venturing a guess as to your economic background, or that of anyone else here, but mine is such that jackson heights -- as it stands now -- is far nicer than anyplace i've lived in my life (greenpoint as a kid, 40 years before hipsters found it, lower east side 1981-85, bed stuy, flatbush, astoria in '88 or so).

the thing some people don't realize -- and you seem to get -- is that 99 cent stores and the like are a necessity for many of our neighbors. a lot of people have to take a long subway ride in order to eat cheap mexican food on roosevelt and do so happily.

and, for me, the draw was, and is, the quality and price of our actual homes. seriously, a 1200 square foot art deco apartment with original details, 20 minutes from manhattan for about 80 percent less than a similar place, a similar distance away, in brooklyn? c'mon, i spend more time at home than i do in any bar -- and have done so for the past quarter century.

again, thanks for the post. i don't think JH is perfect. but having lived in many nyc 'hoods over my 47 years of life, i'll rank it at the top.
