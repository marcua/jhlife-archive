Pets and Animals / Re: Raccoon Sighting
« on: May 14, 2010, 04:52:38 PM »
I grew up in the forest and am pretty familiar with raccoons. Raccoons are nocturnal, and are generally afraid of people. All they want is food; they're scavengers. They're probably going through garbage cans or other trash, looking for tasty bits and scraps. For the most part, if you leave them alone, they will leave you alone.

This being said, if you see a raccoon during the day, something is very wrong, and usually those guys turn out to be rabid. I would call animal control right away if I saw a raccoon running around in the daytime. They can also be very vicious when they have to be, so I would recommend people not letting their dogs approach them or run around outside, unsupervised (probably goes without saying, anyway). In a dog vs. raccoon fight, it'd be a toss-up, even for a very fierce dog.

It is very strange that they are here. I wonder where they may have come from. This is a very inhospitable place for them, for the most part, even though there's ample food for them here.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Fast Food Restaurants
« on: April 18, 2010, 11:14:50 AM »
Yuck.

I had a friend who worked at a Taco Bell inside of a Target. She said that they used to get the meat shipped to them in giant plastic bags, and that it was a dull grey color. They had to add red dye to it to make it look more like "meat." She also said that, interspersed throughout the meat were these large flaps of skin or tendon that looked very gross; some had what looked to be fur attached to them. She said that when a customer was particularly rude to her or her staff, they would make sure that that customer got a flap in their taco. I never ate meat at Taco Bell again.

Before I had a sort of personal diet and lifestyle revolution, I used to have a weakness for the BK Whopper. I'd eat one once a month or so. Now, though, I walk past it on 82nd Street and I smell that gross smoke smell they pump out of there and it makes me feel a little nauseous. I never eat fast food. I haven't for a couple of years. I see people on the train eating it pretty much every day (especially Chinese folks in Flushing eating McDonald's), and I wonder how their insides must be faring.

Whenever I think about eating sketchy meat, I think about all of the horribleness associated with that choice. It's not only that you're ingesting who knows what chemicals and hormones and additives either given to the cow(s) or added to the meat in processing; you're also contributing to the suffering of a living creature raised and slaughtered in terrible conditions, perpetuating an unsustainable system that exploits animals and humans alike. Why would you want to make all of that awfulness part of your body? Why would you choose to contribute to the continuation of a fundamentally destructive way of living?

Sorry for the preaching.

I love the Jewish Community Center thrift store!  It's small, but it's a real thrift store- not a store calling itself as such and selling used stuff for a ridiculous amount of money, nor is it a thrift "boutique" for hipsters.
