^^I've done that with the independent pharmacists. It tells you a lot about how messed up healthcare is in the U.S. The pharmacy may charge $200 to your insurance company for a 30 day supply of a drug, and only receive a fraction of that as payment from the insurance company. An independent pharmacist is more likely to charge you say the $70 they'd get from an insurance company than a national chain like CVS, which will insist on collecting on $200.

I've used independents to fill prescriptions when my insurance company put fairly arbitrary limits on some medicines, and when I've been on a high deductible plan where I was certain I'd only meet my deductible if I ended up in surgery that year for an appendicitis or some other emergency surgery.
