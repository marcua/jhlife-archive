Neighborhood Chat / Re: Comprehensive list of JH businesses needed for 2009 map
« on: August 18, 2009, 08:34:36 AM »
Hi everyone,

Thanks for the discussion. I'm happy there's so much interest in the 2009 map. I've picked up the latest round of recommendations and will do my best to fit everything in. At this time we are pretty full and I won't be able to add any more. Thanks for all your help!

A few clarifications: I'm afraid I won't be able to feature health care providers. That would be its own map since there are so many great doctors, therapists and practitioners in the area. This is primarily a cultural map for stores and restaurants. I may or may not be able to feature the various schools in the area. I feel this is pretty well covered elsewhere online. But I'll see what I can do if there's extra room.

I will add the old established restaurants that were suggested. As far as cafes, I've got espresso 77 and Ricky's (both sponsors of the map), but will do my best to include some of the other cafes that were mentioned. I'd like to try to get the bowling alley in since I'm a fan of that place and think it's a great part of the neighborhood. Let me see what I can do.

Please note: These listings are FREE and done as a courtesy because I think it's of value to the community to have something like a comprehensive business directory. I know times are tough for small businesses right now. I'm sure it won't be perfect for everyone and apologize if anyone gets left out. I will do my best! I sincerely appreciate all the suggestions and the passionate feelings everyone has about the neighborhood.

The maps will be available at the sponsors' locations. You will all know when it's finished and when you can pick up your copy. Most likely you will have to purchase something at the business to receive a map, but it should be nominal. It's up to the businesses themselves how they decide to distribute their copies of the map. Right now the sponsors involved are MPC Properties, Limoncello's Restaurant, El Coyote Restaurant, Ricky's Cafe, Espresso 77, HSBC Bank and Jackson Heights Health Food. Each location will have 100 copies to give out.

If a business wants to be highlighted beyond the free listing, they can get in touch with me. To have their logo featured on the map at their address costs $100 (you get 25 copies of the map). To have the logo featured PLUS an advertisement on the sponsor page, the cost is $225 (50 copies) for a half banner ad and $450 for a full banner (100 copies).

For 2009, Michael Carfagna (the map's main sponsor) and I have approached businesses directly and invited them to be sponsors. As of today, we are waiting to confirm the last sponsorship spot, but until we get confirmation, it is still available. Email me directly if you're interested. davidmheatley [at] gmail [dot] com. There are many spaces available if you just want your logo featured for $100. I'm open to gift certificates as partial payment if that's an incentive. Please spread the word if you know of any businesses who might be open to this. Have them get in touch with me by August 25th.

Thanks again! The business plan for this has been evolving as I go. Right now I'm a one man operation. Thanks for your patience and understanding.
