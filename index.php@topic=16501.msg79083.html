I like the idea of competition, but I did the math and FIOS is as expensive or more expensive than Spectrum. I always assumed that the concept of switching to FIOS would be that you'd save a substantial amount of money, but in reality you don't. This is the primary reason why it's not installed in my building. At my location, Spectrum's connection is rock-solid with consistent speeds (35/6) and it's only $42/month. FIOS does have 50/50 available for $42/month, but it only lasts for 12 months; afterwards it's $55/month, and after 2 years, it jumps even higher. In addition, you'd have to purchase a new modem, or be forced to rent one for $10/month. Personally, I'd rather not have to deal with them having different price tiers for different customers, and having to beg them for discounts every x number of months.

Back when Spectrum was Time Warner/Roadrunner, I did not sign up through them directly. I signed up through Earthlink, which offered a lower rate, albeit for a lower speed. It's the same network and I get billed by Spectrum/Time Warner. They've had the same deal for over 2 decades: $29.99 for the first 6 months, and $41.95 thereafter, no extra taxes or fees. That's the rate that I've been paying since the 90's whenever I've lived in NYC. I use a modem I purchased used off eBay, so I don't pay a modem fee.

On the Spectrum website, it lists the retail rates for broadband. It's $64.99 for 60/4 speed, so you're paying approximately the correct price. You do get double the download speed as compared to my plan, but I don't care about higher speed. I can use the Web and stream HD video perfectly fine on my connection.

If you want WiFi, you can purchase a WiFi router and hook it up to your cable modem. Or have someone you know do it if you're not comfortable enough. I actually find it quite ludicrous that Spectrum charges a monthly fee for WiFi. You'd basically be paying them $5/month in perpetuity for plugging a wire into a device one time.
