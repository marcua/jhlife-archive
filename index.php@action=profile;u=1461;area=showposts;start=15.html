Neighborhood Chat / Re: Are you learning a new language?
« on: July 13, 2017, 08:39:08 PM »
Currently learning Mandarin. I started off with ChinesePod's free lessons, and will probably sign up for a month in the future and download a bunch of their material at once to study with, which Benny Lewis the polyglot recommends.

Right now, I'm using Glossika and doing pretty well with it. It's based on learning full sentences - it has a total of 3000 in each language course. You practice the pronunciation, diction and translation of the sentences provided, and future sentences build on the ones you've already learned. Finished Book 1, and just started Book 2. After Book 2, you're supposed to have around B1 fluency...B2 after Book 3. Of course, this is only for listening and speaking...for reading characters, I will have to use other resources.

I also plan to use Glossika in the future for Japanese. A blogger always had trouble learning, and Glossika was wildly successful in getting him to fluency in a relatively short time.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Feedback for Carshare pilot program in Jackson Heights
« on: June 17, 2017, 12:58:37 AM »
Quote from: toddg on June 16, 2017, 08:47:36 PM
Our entire transportation system is heavily subsidized. 

Car owners who park and drive on the streets for free are subsidized.  Public transit passengers are subsidized.  Even "subscribers" of private services like ZipCar and Citibike are subsidized because they are not paying for the use of the streets as part of their cost of travel.

This proposal would take a space that one neighborhood resident uses to store their vehicle for days at a time, and lease it to a mobility service open to anyone in the neighborhood.  That one parking space will benefit many more people over the course of a day than one space typically does.

Leasing of a few on-street parking spaces for car share companies has been tried in Washington, DC, Northern Virginia, Chicago, San Francisco, Boston and other cities, and has proven to be a popular and successful strategy.  I'm definitely for it.

That's what I was thinking as well. I don't understand those complaining about losing parking spaces because a successful carsharing program would mean substantially less car ownership and therefore, more parking spaces available overall.
