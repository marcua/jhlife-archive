Neighborhood Chat / Opportunity for local businesses-- Tax break for hiring young workers
« on: March 15, 2012, 04:24:08 PM »
Hi all,
Through my day job at a local nonprofit, I recently learned about the NY Youth Works program, which is sponsored by the NY Department of Labor. Businesses can earn up to $4000 in tax breaks for hiring youth through the program. The certification process is straightforward-- you can do it all here:

https://labor.ny.gov/careerservices/youth-tax-credit.shtm

So far, the only NYC businesses to get certified are McDonald's and a Cuban restaurant in Manhattan! This seems like a great opportunity that nobody knows about. So, if you're a local business owner and in need of full- or part-time help, consider checking this out. The tax breaks are only for youth who are hired on or before July 1, 2012.

Neighborhood Chat / Overheated Apartment?
« on: November 14, 2010, 11:08:04 AM »
Hello all,

I've been wanting to ask this group for a long time: how many of you live in buildings where the heat is kept uncomfortably high? My family lives in a 1930s-era apartment building in which the radiators turn off and on according to some centralized machine-- we don't even have the little knobs that you can use to adjust the level of heat coming from the floor unit. And it is often hotter 'n hell in here.

Now, my husband tells me I just have warm blood because I'm Irish and come from Boston. And it's true that when I was growing up my dad would turn the thermostat down to 55 degrees at night and we usually wore sweaters and thick socks to bed. But earlier this week when the weather was near freezing, I noticed lots of people in my building had their windows open, even on the top floors which supposedly are cooler. So I know it's not just me.

It seems illogical, what with the cost of heating oil and the fact that so many people don't have enough heat and global warming and all that, that local landlords wouldn't want to save some $$ by better regulating their thermostats. So what do you think is going on? And is there anything that we, as tenants or as a neighborhood, can do to encourage heat to be distributed a bit more rationally?

Neighborhood Chat / more police activity last night-- possible homicide
« on: November 05, 2010, 09:49:38 AM »
Something is brewing in the neighborhood... we were woken up twice last night by incidents near the corner of 35th Ave. and 75th St.

The first incident was around 1:30 AM. I woke to the sound of running footsteps outside our window. "He's over here," a man said. Soon there were sirens flashing. I heard an ambulance pull up. As I tried to fall back asleep, I heard an officer speaking to one of his colleagues, or perhaps into his radio. The snippet I caught was "...bullet in the head and someone took a baseball bat to him." A little later my husband got up to look out the window. There was a "Crime Scene Unit" vehicle blocking the road, and men in suits-- homicide detectives.

This morning there were still detectives around the crime scene. They now appear to be gone.

The second incident was around 5 AM. "I need a cop! I need a cop!" someone was yelling. Turned out a drunk livery cab driver and had crashed his car into a street lamp. A passerby had grabbed his keys away and was trying to get help. Before we could dial 911, no fewer than four emergency vehicles pulled up-- fire trucks, police cruisers, an ambulance. The drunk driver was quickly cuffed and taken away.

A disconcerting night, to be sure. I've been looking for information on that first incident, what we assume was a homicide, but nothing so far.
