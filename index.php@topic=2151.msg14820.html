      This sort of thing is deplorable.  Every time I hear of someone being targetted because of appearence, religion, gender, disability, etc., I feel it lowers us as a community to have such hateful people among us.  Worse, the attackers are young adults (the "future" as it were.)
      Hating someone because of differences is a taught quality, though in this mode technological day and age, these values the kids adopted may not be from mom and dad, but peers at school, or even hate groups on the internet.  As parents and teachers, we must be mindful of all that our kids are exposed to.
      Likewise, there is ignorance as well.  My own child was the victim of a hate crime last year (perpetuated by an adult no less), and though I can forgive ignorance, I find that it is harder to forgive ignorance that is paired with malice.  Likening it to the case above, these young men who attacked the Sikh youngster do have the right to hate (the nebeift of being in a free country is also at times a drawback) ... however, they do not have the right to act criminally based on that.
      The only way to effectively combat hate crimes in my opnion is a combination of eduaction against ignorance, and consequence for actions such as the attack from the article.
