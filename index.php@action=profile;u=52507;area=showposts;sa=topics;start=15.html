Restaurants & Food / Let Us All Praise Terraza 7
« on: November 15, 2014, 01:36:56 PM »
And not that it needs any praising.  We all know how good it is.  Been there a lot recently and, as always, amazed at the consistent excellence of the music.  And the drinks.  And the vibe in general.  Great 70's influence fusion jazz band last night--not entirely unlike Weather Report--and I was a little disappointed to find the place wasn't jammed packed, being a Friday and all.  In other words, come out and support this great club, a jewel in our neighborhood.  It would be a shame to see it go away (not that there's any talk of that).

Restaurants & Food / Roosevelt Diner
« on: July 05, 2014, 03:56:03 PM »
Just stopped by the new Roosevelt Diner.  It's between 79th and 80th.  Grabbed a menu and it looks really promising.  A good mixture with some things that lean towards the healthier palate (salads, salmon over greens, egg white omelette) as well as heartier fare.  Looks like a good hang--clean, plenty of seating--and the people I spoke to briefly seemed very nice.  And it's open 24 hours.  All that's left is to actually try the food.  Anyone out there been there yet?  Seems like a really great addition to the neighborhood.

Restaurants & Food / Favorite Neighborhood Restaurants/Dishes--What about you?
« on: June 18, 2014, 03:34:37 PM »
After a particular good week on the local chow circuit, it felt like a good time to lay out a few of my personal favorites in the hopes that some of you might discover a new place AND in the hopes that you might have a few recommendations of your own. 

HIMALAYAN YAK--Gyuma (blood sausage), Momos and especially the Kickstarter.
UNIDENTIFIED FLYING CHICKEN--well, yeah, chicken.  But also the best local beer selection.
PHAYUL--Probably my favorite local restaurant.  Pretty much everything.  But the momos are the best around.
RICO TINTO--great breakfasts, guacamole, fish tacos, cafe con leche
AYADA--best Thai food around.  Honorable mention to Arunee (extra points for proximity) and Zabb Elee
COSITAS RICAS--24 hour availability is key.  But great soup, Cuban sandwiches and breakfast.
LA FUSTA--Skirt Steak, spinach, broccoli, pasta, vibe--slight edge over Boina Roja
TIA JULIA--truck on Roosevelt and 92nd.  Best tacos in a neighborhood with many great choices
TACOS MORELOS--best and most varied mole choices in town.
PATA PAPLEAN--Thai bar across from Ayada.  Drinks and vibe are great but surprisingly good bar food, as well

and you?  What are your favorites, least favorites, wish list?  One thing for sure--Jackson Heights (and Elmhurst) is very likely the most varied and best food neighborhood in NYC.

Restaurants & Food / Bars? More than a Bunch of Dudes?
« on: June 14, 2014, 10:03:44 PM »
There are a fair amount of bars in the vicinity of Jackson Heights and Elmhurst.  Some that are halfway decent.  Just had a couple of margaritas at Cafe 75 while watching some World Cup and listening to U2, Gorillaz, Radiohead--not too bad.  And the ReadyPenny has it's moments--cheap, strong drinks, sometimes, decent tunes, sometimes abhorrent dated speed metal and bad post-grunge nonsense.  Fine and dandy.  We do the best we can in a bar-challenged neighborhood.  But what particularly sticks in my craw is that we are that rare neighborhood in which there is no place for a woman to drink in a bar--with friends, with her S.O., whatever.  Every bar, be it of the dime-a-dance, gay disco, working man's sports bar is primarily there for dudes and dudes alone.  Maybe a few exceptions along Northern?  Great!  You might be right.  But it would be amazing to see a few new places that could show up to correct this mono-gender trend.  It's boring.  It's unimaginative. It's predictable.  On that note, the most obvious exception is the amazing Terraza 7 but I went by last night and saw it was closed down for a "work order."   Anyone know anything about that?

Restaurants & Food / La Esquina Del Cameron
« on: June 11, 2014, 03:49:01 PM »
Been enjoying the outdoor weekend sidewalk ceviche stand on the corner of Roosevelt and 80th for a while now but the newly opened weekday counter inside is fantastic.  I just tried the fish empanada and it blew my mind.  They slice an empanada right there before your eyes and load it up with seafood, avocado, cilantro and onions and it tastes delicious and surprisingly light.  Here's the article I saw yesterday that lured me down, somewhat like those smoke fingers in the old cartoons.

Restaurants & Food / Phayul
« on: October 19, 2013, 09:45:39 PM »
With both cold weather and cold season approaching, it's hard to say enough good things about Phayul, especially the perfectly spicy and most likely perfectly medicinal Thenthuk.  The hand drawn noodles are so tender and so light--and it's a delight to watch them being pulled and formed right before your eyes and only seconds before they end up in your very own soup.  I was still fighting off a lingering cold when I walked in and only hours later felt completely healthy.  Final score:  Thayul 1, Head Cold 0.
