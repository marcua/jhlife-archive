Title: Re: Anyone live in any of these coop and know how good the sound proofing is?
Post by: ljr on March 15, 2021, 11:12:42 AM
I'm in Hawthorne Court, and we do hear the upstairs neighbors walking in shoes, and sometimes talking or the sound of the TV if it's very loud. But we are on the first floor, just above the super's apartment in the basement. When we moved in, the super's apartment was being renovated. I went down there and had my husband stomp around in our apartment to see if I could hear it, and in this case, I couldn't. There must be a thicker layer between the first floor and the basement, and also, I don't think the footprint of our apartment aligns exactly with the super's apartment. So we felt confident that we wouldn't have to worry about noise traveling downstairs. So maybe consider a ground floor apartment for this reason? The apartment across the hall from us is over the laundry room, so they really don't have to worry about sound traveling to the apartment below.

Post by: CaptainFlannel on March 15, 2021, 11:53:37 AM
^^I am sorry you are going through that. Children make noise, and if someone doesn't want to hear the normal sounds of children, they should go buy a house in the middle of nowhere with the nearest neighbors being a mile away. (And that being said, people out in the country figure out ways to be loud and annoy their neighbors. I knew people whose neighbors let their teenagers run their ATVs up and down the hills all hours of the night.).

I hope you'll reconsider moving. Eventually your cranky neighbor will stop and your kid will get bigger and stop being so loud (but kids are loud sometimes), and if you have to go to the coop board and management company and explain that you are being harassed to get him or her to do so, I hope you'll do it (document all the nonsense - dates/times, description). There are things of course you can do to make sure your kid isn't exceptionally loud - put down carpet, use those spongy tile things in the play area, every body wear slippers in the house (that might be a big ask for a toddler, I realize), take your kid out to a playground every day to burn energy, do your best to not let them run all over until after 8 am.

Given the level of escalation (and Covid), it might be hard to do, but you might consider asking your neighbor to take a walk/coffee with you to discuss as kindly as possibly how you can both live near each other peaceably.

Good luck!

Title: Re: Anyone live in any of these coop and know how good the sound proofing is?
Post by: Lilybell on March 16, 2021, 08:23:25 AM
Washington Plaza has great soundproofing for the apartments next door, but not vertically. My upstairs neighbor didn't have padding under his thin area rugs and it was maddening - my ceiling light fixture made jangling sounds every time he walked through his dining area and it sounded like an airplane hangar every time he vacuumed. I am pretty noise-tolerant but it became unbearable after a while. It's not as bad now that he was made to get carpet pads but I can still hear him slamming cabinets and stomping around. The people next door have parties that I barely hear but I can hear the dude upstairs sneeze. Weirdly, I rarely hear his tv or music.

Honestly, it's my biggest annoyance living here (along with the poor ventilation - if your neighbor smokes you will smell it like it's in your own apartment).

However, if you purchase a unit you can probably pay a little more to have extra padding added when they replace the flooring.

Title: Re: Anyone live in any of these coop and know how good the sound proofing is?
Post by: BEB on March 23, 2021, 08:48:18 PM
When we were looking to buy an apartment we saw a very nice 2 bedroom apartment where the owner, whom I believe was an architect, put in cork flooring. If I remember it correctly he described it as the engineered floating variety that could be laid DIY-style without glue and thereby be easily removed.

Probably not a super cheap solution but it was very attractive and warm underfoot, in addition to being sound absorbing. When I did further research I noticed it comes in lots of patterns and color options. Some is even made to look like wood.  More general info here: https://www.greenbuildingsupply.com/All-Products/Flooring-Cork#lstBottomCont

It may even be possible to put down a thin rug pad first to protect your floors and provide more sound protection?  I did research about duro design's cork options as there were several showrooms in Manhattan that had samples. https://www.duro-design.com/  Good luck to you!
