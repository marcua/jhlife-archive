Energy slammers in the hood
« on: November 02, 2009, 11:28:30 AM »
I was interrupted this Sunday by a representative of "Just Energy" asking to see my Con Ed bill so he could assess my savings.

Basically he was going to from apartment to apartment trying to get people to switch their energy providers.

While I am understand the theory behind choosing your own energy provider, the sneaky way that these companies go about getting customers to switch is pretty disgusting.

Anyway, I turned him away pretty quickly but then I heard him trying to convince my elderly neighbor and I confronted him asking to him to leave the building. He said that he had the right to be there based on something other which seemed wrong to me but he left eventually.

If you do a quick google seach for Just Energy scam or energy slamming, it'll bring up the info you need.

I'm sure we are not alone here.  It has been a long time since I last met with one of these scammer. so here's the story.

A few days ago, a lady came to my store and asked to take a look at the utility bills. She opened the conversation with "I'm with ESCO and I'm here today to make sure the utility companies in this area are treating people alright. First I thought she was a researcher or inspector working for some kind of public service department. Whatever that was, it wouldn't hurt to show her the bills and let her finish her report/job. So I showed her the most recent bills. Then she moved to the next step saying that she could help me lock down the current rates so that I would benefit from the fixed rate when the bills became higher during summer time. I realized that most likely she was a scammer and wasting my time. (In order to change rates, I will need to switch the utility supplier. so she worked for a utility company other than national grid or con edison.) I had a computer right in front of me. I google searched "ESCO scam" and a few reports came up on the page. Then I told her that she was free to leave. During the entire conversation, she never mentioned that she was from "JUST ENERGY". She left me a business card with "JUST ENERGY" on it.

Another company named "Atlantis Business Consulting" was in the neighborhood selling energy supplies. The company representative explained that they selected a couple of the best energy providers on the ESCO list that had good reputations and provided lower rates than Con Edison or National Grid. The business model sounded legit but I refused to try it out. Then she asked politely if I could help her company out by giving permission to monitor my gas/electric accounts for a year.  She believed that she was helping out everyone by fighting against Con Edison/National Grid that overcharged their customers. Again I refused to reveal my information on gas/electric bills. After she left, I visited a few nearby stores and found that one store manager signed up a release form with this lady. By signing this form, not only the manager agreed to let LibertyPower (one of ESCO) monitor his accounts for a year, but also agreed to switch the energy suppliers for a year. I suspect that this is the form that she tried to get my signature on. I don't know if this company has visited the residential buildings.
