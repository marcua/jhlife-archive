To all interested:

Although I do not reside at The Linden Court or The Greystones they are indeed stunning.

I am an owner/resident for 23 year of a co-op unit at The Berkeley.  A building which has 200 plus units. 

In trying to decide which would be the better investment, I would consider the financial obligations of having to live in a smaller building as opposed to a larger building. 

Example:  If a roof has to be repaired in a smaller building the expense is divided between 8, 10 or 15 units.  If the roof has to be repaired in a larger building the expense is divided between 200 plus units.  The same applies to the expenses relating to pointing, windows, boiler, etc.

Best,

Linden Court is lovely but since it is very close to the 7 line it is very noisy everytime a train goes by.  Greystones are very nice and the block is somewhat private since the buildings face each other. Most of their two bedrooms have a second half bathroom and the kitchens are relatively large.   I live in Hampton Court ( 78 & 79 between 35 & 37 Ave) which is also a walk up and has a full garden. The 2 bedrooms here have a  galley type kitchen and on the 78 Street side have one bath.  On the 79 Street side there are three & some two bedroom apartments, some with two full bathrooms.  Hawthorne Court (76/77 St 35 & 37 Ave) & Elm Court 79/80 St 35 &34 Ave) have similar designs.  They are elevator buildings and the elevators open into the apartment, 2 apartments per floor, fireplace, 1 1/2 baths and a sun room.  They also have full gardens.  good luck
