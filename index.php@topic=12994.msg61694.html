Hi Stephanie, I will let others weigh in with their opinions, but when I have a radiator problem the first person I call is my super.

Your super will examine the issue and determine if the leak is coming from the radiator itself or from a pipe in the walls.

If work needs to be done that you are personally responsible for as a shareholder, at that point you may want to call your own plumber.

stephaniesez,

Most buildings have an official repair protocol, which typically starts with filing a maintenance request with the building management, who then alert the building super or send someone to inspect and potentially repair the problem.

Even if you go to your super directly and ask him to fix it (which is common) I'd still recommend that you notify your management in writing of the problem and the repair that was made. Maintenance history of a unit can help identify or rule out any future problems that may occur, and help management monitor if the problem is reoccuring.
