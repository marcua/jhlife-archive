Corona (with Jackson Heights following quickly behind, as well as the bedstuy/south bronx areas) is has the highest rates of childhood obesity in the city.  This map is old, but I doubt it has changed. http://www.nytimes.com/2010/09/05/nyregion/05obese.html

I'm not talking about bougey concerns here or suggesting that J Heights sucks unless all of our eateries be replaced by french cafes. No. That is not who I am.  My issue with this neighborhood has nothing to do with gentrification but the quality of life problems that perpetually plague the working class in this neighborhood.

I am a woman of color, I am an Ivy-League educated Queens-bred, NYC public school product, and I am an educator.  I want to make our communities better. And it is undeniable that there are scammy businesses in our community that prey on the poor.  I'm sick of any suggestion that JH is less than perfect is automatically assumed to be gentrification related or met with the response that other parts of the city are just as bad, if not worse.  That doesn't solve the issue at hand.

i'm not suggesting your concerns are bougie, and i'm really not a consumer of fast food. i think i've been to mcdonald's once in the past year, the others not at all -- aside from popeye's, which i truly do love. and i agree that lower-income people (particularly kids) are targeted by purveyors of unhealthy food, which is troublesome.

but we do have excellent produce stores -- from the indian ones below 74th all the way up 37th to junction. roosevelt and northern, not so much, although the mi tierra supermarket is pretty excellent. and we do have relatively affordable, relatively healthy options at the south/central american and south asian places that are so dominant.

how do we solve this? i don't think we can easily legislate fast food out of existence, or force landlords to bow to our demands in terms of who they rent to. we need a lot more education and outreach to let people know there are better solutions in terms of nutrition. i'd pay more taxes to help this -- although i'd prefer to see some of the tax breaks afforded to the owners of multi-million dollar condos rescinded first.
