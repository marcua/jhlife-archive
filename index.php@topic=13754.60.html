I would like to suggest Goose Island IPA… because it is delicious!  Tap beer would be really nice and would give beer drinkers a place to go and eat well made food.  As far as the "craft beer" debate, sometimes people create new terms to refer to an already existing class of things, i.e., certified used car. Here, "craft beer"  usually refers to small independent brewers.  I personally do not care one way or the other but do recognize the marketing behind the rebranding of products/things.  As far as small brewers go, they have really elevated the quality and flavor of beers made in the U.S.  This is especially true when one thinks of what options existed back in the days, i.e., Miller, Bud, Coors, Heineken, and that's about it.

There are A LOT of beers that I like. I order anything sour or with the word farmhouse in it, which means it can get funky. I like bottle conditioned beers, which mean that it gets bubbly naturally from yeast rather than from inserting carbonation. I have just started getting into IPAs recently, faves so far have been from Southern Tier and SingleCut, but I'm really just starting on all of this. I don't like to promo my stuff but I order the beers for Little Tibet, so we'll have the stuff I'm interested in trying. I usually buy in very small quantities though because I get bored and keep wanting to try new stuff.


BUT, other than if there's a special reason, I try to buy from small companies.
