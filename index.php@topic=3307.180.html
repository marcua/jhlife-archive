You can't just kick me out! Ousted state Sen. Hiram Monserrate files lawsuit vs. Senate

Ousted state Sen. Hiram Monserrate will get his day in court - again - next week.

Monserrate, booted from the Senate  Tuesday, claims in a federal suit filed Thursday that his civil rights have been trampled.

"I will continue to serve. I will continue to help be a problem solver and deal with the many, many needs [of] my community," he told reporters.

The Senate voted 53 to 8 this week to eject the former Marine, city councilman and cop in light of his conviction for misdemeanor assault on his girlfriend, Karla Giraldo.

Monserrate argues the Senate didn't have the right to expel him and that there should be no special election to replace him.

Monserrate lawyer Norman Siegel said the ex-senator will be back in court next Thursday before federal Judge William Pauley, who yesterday said immediate action wasn't warranted because the Senate isn't in session.

An aide to state Attorney General Andrew Cuomo said Pauley's decision to deny an immediate stop of the Senate's decision "speaks for itself. We [will] continue to represent the Senate in this matter."

Co-plaintiffs in the suit are six voters who say they were disenfranchised by the Senate's decision to kick Monserrate out.

One of them, the Rev. Nancy Torres, runs a Queens nonprofit to which Monserrate, as a city councilman, directed at least $30,000 in fiscal year 2008.

As the media gathered to hear from Monserrate, Council Speaker Christine Quinn endorsed Queens Assemblyman Jose Peralta to replace him in the scheduled March 16 special election.

Quinn, who is openly gay, took a swipe at Monserrate for his Senate vote against legalizing same-sex marriage last year.

Joining Quinn in backing Peralta were seven Queens Council members, including Julissa Ferreras, a onetime Monserrate ally who now holds his Council seat.

ckatz@nydailynews.com
