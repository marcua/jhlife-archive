Neighborhood Chat / Re: New Building to Come - 84-01 37th Avenue
« on: September 03, 2010, 10:42:38 AM »
The Landmarks Committee of Community Board #3 reviewed the proposal for 84-01 37th Avenue last night.  I was unable to stay for the full meeting, and therefore did not hear the Committee's comments. 

The building has one story of commercial storefronts along 37th Avenue, and the five residential stories (with 45 units) are set back 10 feet from 37th Avenue.  It is brick, of neo-Georgian style, and is a near-exact copy of Georgian Hall, which sits across from the site (between 83 and 84 Streets on the north side of 37 Avenue).  There is a balustrade atop of the commercial section, along with pediments and a balustrade at the roofline.  There is a 16-car underground garage with the entrance on 84th Street, adjacent to the other residential building just to its north.  The new building is being built to the lot line, so the windows that face 37th Avenue in the adjacent buildng will be blocked up.  (These windows were built on the lot line, and there was always a risk they would one day be blocked.)  There looks to be a storefront or two on 84th Street, along with the entrance to the apartment section of the building.

From the renderings shown, the building is not objectionable.  The materials used will be an important determinant of how successful it is.  I did not see much detail regarding the storefronts and sign bands, and how they will look.  The proposal will go before Community Board #3 and then the Landmarks Preservation Commission... probably in October.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: New Building to Come - 84-01 37th Avenue
« on: August 12, 2010, 11:19:05 AM »
The Landmarks Preservation Commission will hold a public hearing to review the proposal from the owner/developer.  This will not be a simple rubber-stamp process, and the community will have the opportunity to to comment, along with the Historic Districts Council, our elected officials (should they see fit to do so), and any other party who makes the effort.  Typically, a proposal like this is reviewed by Community Board #3 before it makes its way to the LPC.  You can contact the LPC's public affairs officer to be put on their email notification list... or simply find out how the community will be notified when a public hearing is scheduled.

I rather doubt that any new construction will begin within a matter of months.  First the LPC must approve BEFORE they can pull permits from the other City agencies.   Then there is also financing, and other issues.

Remember, your vigilance and attention to this matter is necessary!

Neighborhood Chat / Re: New Building to Come - 84-01 37th Avenue
« on: June 14, 2010, 02:54:26 PM »
The owner will need approval from the Landmarks Preservation Commission prior to being able to obtain permits from any other City agency.  There will be a public hearing for the proposed new building that the public will be invited to speak at.  Having attended LPC public hearings, the commissioners are not shy about sending owners "back to the drawing board" with their proposals.  Picking at the color of the bricks, size of windows, etc., is not out of the question.  So it will behoove every interested party, especially those who have concerns about the owner's intentions, to appear at the public hearing (If you don't make the time... you can't really complain). I am sure these details will make its way to this blog.  Finally, the owner's proposal will also have to go before Community Board 3's Landmarks committee... that is typically done prior to the LPC public hearing.  So keep an eye out for that as well.
