I wanted to make sure everyone knew about a relatively new restaurant in the 'hood called Urubamba - they're Peruvian.  The place is located on the corner of 37th Ave at 86th or 87th Street - (718) 672-2224.  I went there about when they opened, pre-liquor license and had the most sumptuous meal. The Parradilla chicken was to DIE for. Plus, the service was great.

I passed by the other night and it was dead inside, and that hurt my heart.  I would love to see this place make it...so if you are looking to try something new, give it a shot!  You won't be disaapointed.

They have received some great reviews (google "Urubamba").  here's one:

On the recommendation of some local acquaintances, we visited a Peruvian restaurant at 86-14 37th Avenue (not sure if this is the one mentioned below) that is right next door to Tomo, the new Sushi place. It was also recommended to try the Ceviche.

I have to say that this place was excellent. The Ceviche was to die for. We had the mixed seafood and it comes with two kinds of beans, one marinated and one slightly roasted. Amazing.

I had the tamales also, which were great, with a cilantro/fish/rice soup. Others had the grilled salmon and the lamb stew. Everything was great, especially with the Peruvian beer.

We'll definitely be heading back.

I haven't been to Urubamba yet, but I went Inti Raymi a few weeks ago, and it was exceptional.  The review on the front of the restaurant claims it is the oldest Peruvian restaurant in NYC. 

We seemed to be the only people in the place who were not Peruvian, and the waiter was genuinely surprised when I said I knew nothing about Peruvian food.  We were actually surprised by how crowded it was at the unfashionabley early hour of 5:30.  The waiter made lots of helpful suggestions for both my meat-eating friend and my non-meat-eating tastes.  My 22-month old LOVED the yummy yellow beans and rice and the fried sweet bananas which I had ordered as a back up if she didn't like other things.  I had a sort of fish soup, (I'm sooo sorry that I don't remember the name) and my friend really feasted on some beef preparation.  The grown ups enjoyed a couple of glasses of sangria.

I've lived here for ages, and I had never gone there before.  But I will be back!
