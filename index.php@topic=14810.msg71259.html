Ugh. I am so sick of Time Warner. I have to call them every year when my "promotional" package expires and they jack my rate up $25 or more. Last year the package I opted for included increased internet speed. I never noticed any increase, but I suspected it was an issue with my computer being slow...and, well, frankly I just didn't feel like calling them about it. So a few days ago I called to negotiate a new package. After listening to several options, I chose one that happened to include the high-speed internet, because it was the lowest price. The woman on the line told me I'd need to replace my modem because she'd just checked and it isn't compatible with the higher speed. I was like, "Whoa, whoa...wait a minute...what? I have supposedly had the higher speed for a year! Why didn't the person I spoke with there a year ago check my modem and tell me it wasn't compatible?" We went back and forth for a while and then she put me on hold for about five minutes. She came back and offered me a small rebate. I just went ahead and took it. And now TW is coming to my home this week to give me a new cable box and modem.

I realize Time Warner may have new offers as new services become available (like being able to DVR six programs at a time!!). So I get why there's some value in revisiting your package every year. But this whole thing of year-long promotional packages is such a scam! They're counting on you letting it expire and paying full price for a few months before you notice. I'm so glad my electric and gas companies, and even my phone service, don't pull that crap.

I'd say it depends on just how "non-techie" you are.  Installing the modem is pretty simple - you pretty much just plug it in and then call the cable company and they do something on their end to activate it.  They should even help you with getting that part set up.

It's getting anything else taken care of after the fact where you're going to be on your own.

I will say that I've had broadband in one form or another for 10+ years and the vast majority of time I've had problems with my internet connection it was on the provider's side of the line of demarcation.

But if you're not all that comfortable with that sort of thing then $10 a month really isn't that much for the peace of mind.
