Title: Re: Aldi in Rego Park
Post by: voxcontinental on April 10, 2011, 01:20:10 PM
Aldi must be the most polarizing store I've heard of. My best friend was forced to shop there for his invalid Dad, and he HATES the place to this day. My Niece in Mn. won't go to the one near her, as it is for "poor people." (Well, many of those poor people at this attractive Mn. Aldi are likely millionaires, but that's another story.) Our sweet friend from Germany was distraught at the lack of Aldi's in NYC - she just loves the chain.

Aldi strikes me as a downscale version of their sister chain, Trader Joe's (both chains have common ownership - some sort of complex arrangement).

If you won't buy a product that doesn't spend millions on advertising, then Aldi is not for you. However, if you get the very simple fact that most of these no-name brands are made by the same companies that make the well-known brands, there are stupendous bargains to be had at Aldi.

I'd avoid the tubes of ground beef, but then I avoid ALL mass-market ground beef. It is great-looking but mostly unsafe garbage. (Only safe way is to cook the heck out of it.) Otherwise, Aldi has great finds. I wish there was one close to us here in JH.
