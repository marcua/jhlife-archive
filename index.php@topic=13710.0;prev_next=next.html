HELP! Need restaurant for old ladies who are scared of "ethnic" food.
« on: September 09, 2015, 11:44:34 AM »
Hi all,

Stew is ruining my life by not being open yet! I have three elderly women visiting this weekend and I am looking for someplace to take them for lunch on Sunday that isn't Indian or Thai (two of them are very unadventurous eaters even though I think they would like Arunee).  I live on 73rd/35th so under 80th street is a necessity since we will be walking and I don't want to make them trek too far.  I've taken them to Armondo's before the fire and Novo before it closed.

So, any advice?  Uncle Peter's is a bit too far and I have to admit I haven't been all that impressed the times I've been there. El Rinco Tinto is good but I'm hoping for something with more of a sit-down restaurant feel. I really liked the Argentinian place that was next to Armondo's - isn't there something a bit similar up 37th a bit?  I can't remember the name. 

I wish they would be here on Saturday so I could take them to see Despaña (they are all from Spain and would love it).  I am going to try to talk them into Arunee but don't know if I will have any luck.  My poor mom will be with us and I feel bad because she loves Thai but is stuck with the old ladies who always want seafood - oh, a seafood place would be good (not sushi) - I hate fish but I'll suffer through it if there's a good one nearby.

thanks
