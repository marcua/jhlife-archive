Neighborhood Chat / Re: Outdoor Seating
« on: July 31, 2016, 10:46:00 AM »
No doubt sidewalk seating would add a more European feel to the nabe, European. Given the population density of JH, however, my feeling--and everyday experience--is that our sidewalks barely allow for two 'lanes' of two people walking side-by-side in opposite directions as it is. I'm having a hard time envisioning even a single row of tables added to storefronts; and where the sidewalks might conceivably be wide enough, those storefronts are occupied by businesses (RiteAid, e.g.) that are rather unlikely to house restaurants/cafés in future.

It's a lovely vision, to be sure, yet in terms of current infrastructure very difficult to realize. The historical fact is--unlike, say, Astoria--JH was intended by Mr MacDougall to be a residential/bedroom community for (upper) middle-class WASPs. 37th Ave, 82nd and 74th Sts might serve as commercial strips for basic services, but anything bordering on entertainment (like people-watching from a sidewalk café) would never have been part of that original plan, making it that much harder to design now, 100 years after the fact. It simply cannot be woven as organically into the fabric of this community.

Fortunately, what JH does provide to its many residents, for instance, is a respite from being watched by eight million other people. Its leafy residential side streets and (semi-) private apartment gardens are an obvious, and rare, haven from the hustle and bustle of urban life. In other words, we don't need to have what every other Manhattanite wants: a house in the country. Luckily, too, JH also has great access to public transportation, such that places like Greenwich Village, with a clearly much longer-standing café culture, are within (usually) easy reach. And that kind of neighborhood diversity is also part of the beauty of NYC, after all.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Car alarm on 85th Street
« on: June 13, 2013, 10:53:40 AM »
Of a similar nature: car horn being used as a 'doorbell'--to the extent, in one instance, where 9-1-1 had to be called. Unfortunately, the driver had left by the time police arrived, and a plate number of the scofflaw's vehicle would not suffice for further pursuit. Apparently police themselves would have to witness the crime (still on the books even though they're taking down the signs), before any ticketing can be done.

I mean, okay, none of this constitutes a capital offense, but it does affect what little 'quality of life' we have in JH. And I would think those who live here by choice do so because they prefer to leave the hustle/bustle of urban life in Midtown. What's the point of living in a historic garden district otherwise?--shEEEsh!

Possible future solution, whether car alarm or 'doorbell': if your cell phone has video + audio, try taping this truly un-neighborly activity when you call the cops. At least then there will be a record, should the malfeasant have absconded already before the police arrive.
