Alas, a year or so ago, Katie Honan at DNAinfo provided excellent coverage of the neighborhood.  Now DNAinfo is gone and she has brought her talents to the Wall Street Journal (covering City Hall, which certainly needs it!)

For super-local coverage, we still have Jackson Heights Post.  For wonky coverage of city-wide issues, Gotham Gazette is excellent.  For snarkier, less-wonky coverage, Gothamist is always fun.

Some great NY political reporters to follow on Twitter are @katie_honan, @nicolegelinas, @GloriaPazmino, @Azi, @JimmyVielkind, and @nahmias.

I think it's awesome that these elections are inspiring people to tune into politics more closely. Like they say, if you don't turn on to politics, politics will turn on you. For serious city/local policy and political stuff, I definitely recommend Gotham Gazette - just a great resource. City & State is good. City Limits is especially excellent, focused on important urban policy and investigatory stuff. If you're into audio, the editors of Gotham Gazette and City Limits have the podcast Max & Murphy. It is a just goldmine of state and city policy and politics. And as of a couple months ago, it's airing on WBAI 99.5, once a week I think.

For the very local neighborhood news, I feel like that can be tougher, which sucks because it's so so important. DNAinfo closing last year was a loss. Brooklyn definitely has a lot of local beat reporters. But Queens? If anyone has recommendations here, I'd love to know.
