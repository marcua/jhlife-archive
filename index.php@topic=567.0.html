Hmmmm....In my opinion, I just don't think this map is a true representation of the Jackson Heights community as it is today.  It excludes, by far, a large part of the immigrant population that doesn't shop Fresh Direct, Starbucks or Espresso 77.  Not everyone lives in the beautiful garden apartments that the small cartoon strip boasts about.  I know many of the people who don't and have been directly affected by the changes that the neighborhood has been experiencing.  From a marketing standpoint, I realize that it's never a good idea to focus on the darker side of a topic, and perhaps this is the reasoning behind the creation of such an exclusive map.

While I understand that the community and its demographics have changed considerably within the past few years, it remains a fact that a significant number of poor immigrants still call Jackson Heights home.  This map is fun to look at, but at best, it's a good marketing tool.  Just my 2 cents.

I like the map.  And I imagine the artist fulfilled his commission.  And yes, it's true he was hired by a real estate developer to make the map.  Perhaps if he could have made all of his own decisions he might have included different items.  Or not.

And, maybe this should be a different thread, but. . .

I wonder if everyone here has read "Jackson Heights: A Garden in the City" ?  I got my copy at Happy Kitchen or maybe it was Inner Peace.

An interesting bit of history about Jackson Heights is that the whole reason for much of its existence was due to a real estate developer named  Edward McDougal.  When he heard that the Queensborough Bridge was going to be built in the early 1900s, he arranged to buy large tracts of the farms that previously existed in what we now think of as JH.

The first paragraph of the book is called "Speculative Frenzy."

Much like the present-day real estate developer who commissioned this map, these developers also produced promtional material about the neigbhorhood and I'm sure they focused on exactly what they thought would draw buyers to the area.  One ad says:

"Hear what a Garden Apartment Family has to say -
says mother "Until we came to Jackson Heights I never dreamed of having all the advantages of living in the suburbs combined with the conveniences of a beautiful, modern apartment.  Well, we have that ideal combination here, and all of us are very happy in Jackson Heights.

says father "Words cannot express my satisfaction with owning a Garden aparmtnet at Jackson Heights. From the viewpoint of enjoyment, I have a Golf course, Tennis Courts and a Club House right at hand. From the financial standpoint, I consider this the soundest and best investment I ever made."

I just think it's worth remembering the history of the neighborhood and I highly recommend getting a copy of the book.
