Title: BQE Fitness
Post by: kuros on August 30, 2008, 07:15:03 PM
I've been going to the BQE for more than two years now, since moving to JH from Manhattan (where I belonged to an NYSC by NYU for many years), and my experience has been quite good (compared to the moderator of this thread, who snidely trashed the place back in February--he'd obviously not been there in a while). Compared to how dingy and disorganized it was when I first joined, the place is now spacious and relatively well layed out (it's apparently grown by additions over the years), and the aerobic equipment and much of the free weights are new and plentiful. Complaints regarding cleanliness must have registered, because a cleaning crew is constantly mopping and wiping down equipment. The men's locker room, at least, is also recently renovated, with sauna and steam. There is no pool, but there are handball courts. There's also an area with punching bags for boxing and kick-boxing. The place is open 24 hours, as far as I know, and has aerobic and spinning classes, although I've never attended. I try to go midday, when there aren't so many people around. I would prefer to pay upfront for 6 months (or a year) rather than hand my credit card over, and authorize monthly payments. These automatic payments can be hard to stop (lots of horror stories about big franchises taking additional months to cancel a membership, or charging a cancellation fee, etc). If I join a gym, I'm going to exercise, period. I am certain that a year from now, I'll still want a gym. So paying upfront is only a risk if the gym sucks, or goes out of business. I can usually tell if the gym is no good (and I can tolerate a crummy place, if there are no other choices). Health clubs are a big source of consumer fraud (pre-sales but then the gym never opens, etc) so there are laws that are somewhat protective. If the facility is big, it's unlikely they will go out of business, but these gyms seem to be the type that are hard to cancel (look at reviews on Yelp or Google for horror stories). So, I prefer paying upfront.
