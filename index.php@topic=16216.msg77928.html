There's been a decades-old debate among cyclists about whether unprotected bike lanes make matters better or worse for cyclists.  Some experienced cyclists say they prefer not to have the lanes.   There are many reasons for this, including the false sense of security that Jeff mentions.  Unprotected bike lanes also tend to create an unrealistic expectation among drivers that cyclists should always stay in the bike lane.  Anybody who has been on a bike in an urban environment knows that there are many reasons why cyclists must leave the lane from time to time.  But unprotected bike lanes do help make the streets a bit safer and more welcoming for cyclists, which many cyclists do appreciate.

On 111th Street, the city is installing protected bike lanes, which are physically separated from vehicular traffic.  The cycling community has been unified in calling for these to be installed citywide.  I think it's a great improvement.
