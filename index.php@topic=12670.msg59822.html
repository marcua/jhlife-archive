My initial thought is that it would be a flop. The reason why is that I feel that the demographic of JH values cost and practicality over status and hip factor. The vast majority of residents here are middle-class, lower middle-class and working class, so they care deeply about value. And they are families, couples, and older folks who are more settled in life, so there's less or no desire to be trendy. I feel that if I went around the entire neighborhood and did an inventory who dressed in the clothes that Anthopologie sells, the amount would be less than 10%; perhaps it might not even reach 5%. TBH, I rarely see anyone here that I would consider fashionable or attractive in terms of style. There's probably a higher chance of that sort of store opening up in LIC, and the hip JH'ers would go there to shop

The demographic of jackson heights is changing rapidly. Initially there might be a curve, but there are plenty of consumers here who would be enthusiastic. Your real challenge is the sky high rents, it many other places you would be ok. Here, you could have a decent clientele, and still have difficulty paying the rent.  But residents besides these dreaded hipsters would shop at a housewares store, as long as it included nice but practical items, not just trendy stuff. Jackson heights could use a nice housewares store, what we have currently is quite terrible. Just be balanced about your stock, remember even hipsters get married and have kids. And don't overreach in terms of your lease, choose a good spot at a sustainable rent. And don't trust a landlord who says you don't need a firm lease,we,re all family here. I've heard of too many people who get burned that way.

Your sample size is too small on this board for feedback.

Do the math; the actual math. Do an actual business study with people that KNOW, rather than open up a whim.
 What is the demand? The foot traffic? How will you drum up business? How much profit is there per item? What do you need to sell/month to break even? How will you compete with amazon? How will you differentiate yourself from other queens/manhattan places? What is your cash buffer? What is the projected rent, overhead, wages, insurance, utilities, taxes, permitting costs and their respective annual increases? How much slack time - not selling anything - can you deal with? How will you respond to people being sick, suppliers not supplying, catastrophe?

Too many people start businesses and learn this stuff on the fly. Be better prepared than those people.

I like the idea but I don't think it would work in JH.  At least not yet.  The commercial rents are crazy and you would need a lot more foot traffic and repeat customers than you might think.  I lived in Boerum Hill before I moved to JH, and clothing boutiques had a really hard a hard time staying alive there, and the rent near Smith Street is much less than in JH.

I don't think high-quality clothes are the way to go - you need to sell something that will keep people returning on a regular basis.     

Edited to add:  I love the idea of a housewares store!
