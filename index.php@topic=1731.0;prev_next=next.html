Our heat is on.  I'm not sure about the rules in terms of rules for coop buildings.

A bedroom at 66-68 degrees actually would fall within the guidelines for rentals in NYC given the weather so far this season, so if coops have to follow the same rules then this would be considered acceptable.

Here are the rules for "heat season" (Oct 1- May 31)

Between the hours of 6:00 AM and 10:00 PM, if the outside temperature falls below 55 degrees, the inside temperature is required to be at least 68 degrees Fahrenheit; and,

Between the hours of 10:00 PM and 6:00 AM, if the temperature outside falls below 40 degrees, the inside temperature is required to be at least 55 degrees Fahrenheit.

http://www.nyc.gov/html/hpd/html/tenants/heat-and-hot-water.shtml

If your co-op is following the law when it comes to turning on the heat, it will be a bit cold in the Fall.  Most buildings have a thermostat set to the temperature required by law which ALWAYS seems too low this time of year.   Fortunately, I wear a sweater for a week or so and then it's finally cold enough for heat.  Our heat came on recently, too.

The flip side to not having enough heat is once Winter rolls around, most co-ops are so darn hot, people open their windows and let the heat escape (which costs money.)  After living here all these years I've never been able to figure out a better way to regulate the heat in our building. 

This is normal though.
