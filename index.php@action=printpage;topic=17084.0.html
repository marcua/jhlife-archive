Last week, I had to try to wake a drunk guy on the curb where he would have been backed over by the driver of the parked car he was under, and the week before in our own yard, just off Roosevelt I had to explain why a different man was "sleeping" in our entryway.

Last week, I also did some excellent rogue gardening, adding to the trees we planted on 90th Roosevelt with 4 large planters of lovely green.

I used 311 app for a variety of quality of life and traffic issues that are not being addressed, and beseeched the elected officials to get rid of the moving trucks that are parked around 90th and Roosevelt all day long.

Many on this board gave Francisco Moya the council seat. What are your expectations? Do you realize that he'll appoint the lady who currently answers his phone to the assembly seat? All we get from that is more foot soldiers for Joe Crowley's empowerment. (I think he does a good job on national issues)

So many in this neighborhood are great debaters of national issues and national politics while getting too cozy with the mindset that their property values are rising and rising. At what cost?

Is there a Roosevelt Ave coalition that will actually be willing to get this road in order?
My family and I are hard-working, declining middle class and most likely months away from making the decision to leave NYC, my home of nearly 30 years.

I recently helped a friend move from Harlem to Astoria. She won the affordable housing lottery, which sent her to a low-rent refugee settlement in the stench zone of the East River. It comes with parking and many other settlers who will build up that part of Astoria where single family homes are slowly getting sold off. It's depressing as hell that this is the method for affordable housing, when beautiful communities like ours, Elmhurst and Corona and East Elmhurst have sustained generations of good families.
Until now.
I do recognize that the 80's and 90's must have been worse in so many ways, but I can't imagine how our elected's and community members are willing to let us go backwards so fully.

But mentioning the BID also brings up the fact that I'm sure many of the small business people on Rosie don't want it to get -too- nice, because then they would be gentrified out of their livelihoods. That's why the BID was killed: existing business owners were concerned that it was a stalking horse to force them out and replace them with national chains, as has happened on a lot of 82nd. The businesses along Rosie tend to be busy - they're all rented, not abandoned - and I bet that they fear multiplying their rent a lot more than they fear Senorita Puta leaning against the 7 train pillars. In fact, I'm pretty sure she buys drinks and is part of their community.

JH happens to be a bunch of non-overlapping communities who coexist and don't talk to each other much. But let's respect that and not try to force the other ones into becoming us. (While advocating for a reduction in things that hurt everyone, like gang violence.)
