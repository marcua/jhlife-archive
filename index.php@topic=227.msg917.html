There is really very little you can do to reduce structure-born noise in these old co-op buildings other than have as much "soft" items in your and your neighbors apartments. If your co-op has a building architect reviewing renovation plans, they can force everyone to put in sound board and sound attenuation batting on walls and floors. The blown in insulation is not going to do much to reduce the sound "into" your apartment from your neighbors. It's not money well spent in my opinion. The problem stems from poorly isolated buildings and management not enforcing proper renovation plan reviews and procedures.
