A couple of things to note. The census measures median *household* income. The median figure is where half of what's being being measured falls below the median, and the other 50 percent follows above. So, this doesn't mean that the average income in Jackson Heights is $50K, it means that 50% of all households - which includes single people, couples, and couples with kids - makes more than 50K and 50% makes less.

A two income household making $75K a year isn't rich. Add a couple of kids, and they're even less rich.

Quote from: CaptainFlannel on February 05, 2015, 01:15:33 PM
A couple of things to note. The census measures median *household* income. The median figure is where half of what's being being measured falls below the median, and the other 50 percent follows above. So, this doesn't mean that the average income in Jackson Heights is $50K, it means that 50% of all households - which includes single people, couples, and couples with kids - makes more than 50K and 50% makes less.

A two income household making $75K a year isn't rich. Add a couple of kids, and they're even less rich.

I dug into the Census numbers that someone posted earlier in the thread and compared the neighborhood income with that of Long Island City, a nearby neighborhood that supports many of the type of places that I would like to see.  All numbers are based on U.S. Census Bureau, 2009-2013 5-Year American Community Survey.  Here are a few things that I found:

- In raw numbers, Jackson Heights has 46% more households with income over $75,000 than Long Island City.  (7121 vs. 3832)
- In the higher range, Jackson Heights has 27% more households with incomes of $200,000 or more than Long Island City  (702 vs. 687)
- When you take into consideration married couple families, Jackson Heights has 52% more married couple households with income over $75,000 than Long Island City (which would seem to be a case for more kid-oriented businesses)
- Jackson Heights population is 54% greater than that of LIC and only 29% of the households in Jackson Heights have income over $75K vs. 35% of the households in LIC.  But in raw numbers, we have more people with higher income.

My point is, if Long Island City can support some (high) number of American/French-inspired gastropubs, I would think Jackson Heights could support one.  In addition to one decent health club.  Not to mention one good playspace for kids.
