Hi Eddie!  I'm over 40, but not by much.   I love where are neighborhoods are situated.  We're so conveniently close to each other, and each neighborhood had so much to offer, especially in terms of food.

In Jackson Heights, La Nueva is one of my favorite places for good food at an affordable price.  I like to people watch there as well, especially when they have the some of the bigger soccer matches being broadcast on TV.  Pio Pio is another one of my favorite places to go with friends for its lively atmosphere and that awesome, green hot sauce.  Their sangria is pretty good too.  I've also frequented Legend's for the beef short ribs...excellent!  I also love walking Roosevelt Avenue.  Just yesterday, I decided to take a stroll on the strip and it was hopping with people, more than usual, lining up at the local restaurants and shopping.

I do a lot of hanging out at Astoria also.  My brother was living there for a few years, so we got to hang with him on various occasions.  Cup and the Neptune Diner are two of my favorite places for breakfast.  Being the huge movie fan that I am, I absolutely love going to the Museum of the Moving Image.  I understand that it's undergoing some expansion work now, so I look forward to its reopening.  What can I say about Astoria Park?  That was one of my favorite hang outs as a teen, and I still like going there.  I recently tried the new south western restaurant, Mojave.  It was a bit expensive, so I don't know how much I'll frequent it.  I'm interested in trying Trattoria L'Inconntro.  I stepped in to check it out and the ambiance sort of reminded me of Vincent's Clam Bar.  It had somewhat of an old world vibe to it.  What's the scoop on it?  Astoria has so many great places for eating and shopping....I could go on and on.  It's one of my favorite Queens neighborhoods.

HURRRAAAYYYY!!!!!    I'm over 40, too.  And I used to live in Astoria before I bought my apartment here in Jackson Heights so love both neighborhoods.  I still spend a lot of time in Astoria working at Kaufman Astoria Studios  so still feel very connected to that community.
