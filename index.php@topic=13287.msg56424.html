I don't fetishize small businesses the way some others on this forum seem to, but I'm nevertheless reluctant to believe any real good will come of this. Here's the BID's appeal to property owners (http://jhcoronabid.org/stakeholders/property-owners/):

Quote
A BID protects your real estate investment by stabilizing or increasing property values within its district. Studies have shown that on a whole, BIDs in New York City are increasing the vitality and attractiveness of their communities, and this is reflected in a substantial increase to commercial property values. To read the study done by the Furman Center, click here.


I'm all for cleaner streets, but who are the ultimate beneficiaries of this project? Property owners, it seems. And if existing small businesses get priced out, then all those niche upscale fantasies of ours-- pubs, cafes, bookstores, burger places--probably won't stand a chance either.
