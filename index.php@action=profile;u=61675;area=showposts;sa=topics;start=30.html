Neighborhood Chat / JH hardware store closing after 79 years
« on: March 29, 2018, 12:43:05 PM »
First Jackson Hardware, now this (Archer is currently offering up to 50 percent off on hardware supplies) — via JH Post:

Archer Hardware Store, which has been in Jackson Heights for 79 years, will close Saturday.

Vasantal “Victor” Mehta, 72, has operated the store at 73-21 Broadway with his wife, Jayaovaben, for 35 years. The pair have decided that its time to retire.

Mehta, who moved to Queens as a young man from the Indian province Gujarat, purchased the hardware store from a man named Mr. Goldstein after answering an ad in the newspaper.

Mehta, in recent times, has tried to sell the store, but he has not found a buyer.

https://jacksonheightspost.com/archer-hardware-store-close-79-years

What hardware stores do people go to now? I like the one at 88th St & 37th Ave—friendly, helpful, well-stocked. A little pricey but if that's what it takes to stay in business, so be it. I'd rather support these small places with knowledgeable service than Home Depot.

Neighborhood Chat / Book Culture is now open in LIC
« on: December 08, 2017, 11:35:48 AM »


Book Culture, an independent bookstore with three locations in the Upper West Side/Morningside Heights area, just opened its fourth bookstore—this one in Long Island City, at 26-09 Jackson Ave (right on the way home for lots of us in JH). According to their newsletter, they will be open (tentatively) till the "early evening" today, with regular hours starting tomorrow:

Monday - Friday: 9am - 10pm
Saturday: 10am - 8pm
Sunday: 10am - 7pm

Queens now has three independent English-language bookstores, up from zero (by my count) just five years ago:
  • Astoria Bookshop (31-29 31st St, Astoria)
  • Kew & Willow Books (81-63 Lefferts Blvd, Kew Gardens—weekend hours only)
  • Book Culture (26-09 Jackson Ave, LIC)
(For used books, there's also the tiny Turn the Page Again, at 39-15 Bell Blvd in Bayside.)

Yes, we still need one in Jackson Heights (to complement Libreria Barco De Papel, which is great)... but let's keep these all open for starters! (Astoria Bookshop, for one, lets you order almost any book to your door right from their website... something that's nice to do if you're ever tempted to order online—you can still shop local. https://www.astoriabookshop.com/)

Neighborhood Chat / Increased weekend evening service on 7 line
« on: November 15, 2017, 09:34:53 AM »
The MTA is adding trains to six subway lines as the agency grapples with declining and shifting ridership.

The boost will come to the 2, 3, 7, N, W and Q beginning next spring in a bid to increase those numbers on nights and weekends, when the MTA believes there is an unmet demand due to changes in ridership patterns.

The 7 line will get the biggest service enhancement — four new round-trip trains will run on Saturdays between 6 and 8 p.m. and another eight will be added on Sundays between 4 and 8 p.m.

Full story: https://www.amny.com/transit/mta-subway-service-1.14966934

This along with the new Q53 SBS to the Rockaways is good transit news, for a change.
