Honestly, here's my dilemma:

I would love to buy a 1 bedroom apartment, but I'm working with a 'studio' budget. Southridge is the only complex with 1 bedroom apartments that I can afford. So, the question is, would I rather buy a studio apartment in the Historic District, walking distance to the E/F express train, 15 min. to work, etc. or would I rather sacrifice location to own a spacious 1 bedroom in Southridge? Both have pros/cons.

I'm not totally put off by Southridge's requirements and, in general, I've heard good things about the complex. It's just the location that gives me reservations (the long walk to the subway, the dreaded 7 train, and the dip in property value). But on the other hand, I'm not sure if it makes sense to invest in a studio, when outgrow it in 4-5 years, but who knows? What I do know is, both options are cheaper than renting.

Argh! Why is house hunting so hard? I thought it was a buyer's marketing?   Thanks for all the helpful feedback!

Any extension of the historic district would likely include just the West side of the street, which are nice brick row houses. My friends own one of those, they have two stories, a basement, garage and backyard. They bought it 15 years ago, I wish those prices were still around!
The market in New York has stayed pretty strong, which is good for Owners & not as good for Buyers. There was no real crash like what was seen in other parts of the country. That being said, prices are probably as low as they'll get, and interest rates are extremely low, so it is a good time to buy a home.
My personal advice (which you can take or leave as you see fit) is to look at a house/apartment as a home, not an investment. Home values will go up regardless of where you are, probably more in the Historic District than Southridge, but the initial cost may offset that.
So it seems your big decision is size vs. travel time, and that is up to you to decide.
I guess the reason Southridge is so cheap is because of its location. I will tell you from personal experience there are no "hidden surprises" like lack of heat/hot water, dirty hallways, stuff like that. As has been said, they are nice buildings that are well maintained. Also, we just received news that our maintenance will NOT be increased this year, which indicates the buildings are in good financial shape.
Regarding the time to sell, from the internet I see that the unit below me took 11 months to sell, and another took 6 months to sell. So while it takes a long time, the "never ever sell" stuff is a bit dramatic. They did look at our paperwork quite thoroughly, and a buyer has to make certain income requirements and have a certain amount of $$ in the bank. If you live in the building, this is good, if you're trying to sell, I'm sure it's frustrating.
Well, I think I've rambled on enough, best of luck in whatever you choose to do!

I was curious and took a look at craigslist and noticed two for sale.  One for $119K & the other for $129K.  The one for $119K maint is $440, the other $526.  This is very tempting for someone on a modest budget.  I would go for space rather than a studio in a prime area.  Someone mentioned Roosevelt Terrace.  I did notice a one bedroom with a 20 ft terrace for $169K with a similar maint which is a lot more for someone on a budget. 

I wouldn't be too concerned about when it comes time to sell.  Selling is a problem all over JH's.  When JH's was at its peak in 2006 apartments sold quickly regardless of where they were. 

Don't stretch your budget.  I would go with Southridge.  I know a senior couple who raised two children in Southridge.  They're financially well off and could move to prime JH's but they won't.  They really like Southridge.
