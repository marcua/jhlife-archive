We went last night.

Not sure Dudley was ready for the crowd. There were two elementary-aged boys serving as hosts, explaining that this was a soft opening with a limited menu. A young girl--maybe 6?--was helping bus tables, carrying one plate at a time VERY CAREFULLY. It was charming as hell, if somewhat chaotic. Dudley might have been the only person taking orders?

Anyway, the pizzas were good and the snap pea salad was lovely. I had an excellent cocktail. I saw loads of neighbors who were clearly thrilled to finally have a place like this.

Highly recommend a visit if you haven't been. I'm looking forward to returning in the next couple of weeks.

Congrats to Dudley and team.

We went tonight. Holy crap this was great.

Pizza could stand toe-to-toe with any good neighborhood neapolitan-style pizzeria in BK (I would compare it favorably, for example with fornino in williamsburg) . The snap pea salad was spring on a plate. The wine was reasonably priced for quality glasses ($8-14), generous pours, and even had a beaujolais "san soufre" (without sulfur) which was fresh and quaffable.

The space is warm and well thought out with bar seating, regular tables by the giant-light-filed  windows and an interior section of caramel-colored banquettes. A Beautiful neighborhood bistro, walkable to for most of jackson heights!


They were rushed for the 2nd night - it was slow and there were definitely service hiccups, but  once they get their "new restaurant kinks" sorted, I look forward to even greater things.

GO. SUPPORT this amazing business. Exactly what people on this board and neighborhood have been clamoring for for years!

Finally a neighborhood restaurant for people who want good wine with good food in a modern, convivial, tv-free atmosphere.
