Quote from: Jeffsayyes on April 27, 2016, 08:32:28 AM
Sounds like the woman had good intentions but was in over her head.

It happens more frequently that people imagine.  A person begins with a soft heart for animals and rescues them from various bad circumstances.  The problem is, though, that some people can't stop.  They are unable to realize that they personally can't save every animal that's in need so they become collectors.    The volume of animals then makes veterinary care, neutering, etc. prohibitive so the population increases and the living conditions deteriorate.

Although the "rescuer" began with good intentions,  it IS animal cruelty and, when these conditions become known, local humane organizations, in concert with local governmental officials SHOULD step in and save the person from his or her self and the poor, neglected animals from a slow, agonizing death.

My two cent's worth....
