Luckily, the worst culinary experience I've had since moving here two years ago is no longer possible in that neighborhood with Bud's Ale House in the land of ex-establishments.
We went there after a movie and experienced -- hot wings that lacked even the slightest heat they claimed, a "take" on the Chicago Dog in which the ingredients were all ground into a relish on the snap-free dog that looked like lawn trimmings after you've washed them out from under the mower with a hose and onion rings that used the thinnest onion slices ever and breading so chewy I could almost blow bubbles with it.
To top it off, whoever was running the place that night insisted on having the hip dance music played at such a volume that it was difficult for my dining companion and I to carry on a conversation in a place that was three-fourths empty.
Our server was nice, but that was it. I didn't expect gourmet, but it's not hard to do basic sports bar food right and that place was abysmal.
So, be glad that place isn't an option anymore.

Another vote for Astor Room, although I understand that they are closed temporarily for a remodel and will re-open on Friday with a new menu and a grand re-opening event.

Another good, less fancy option, particularly if you like craft beer, is Sunswick 35-35. They offer simple bar food, but it's well done, and the service is always pleasant. They have a sandwich on the menu modeled after the famous Primanti sandwich (it's a Pittsburgh thing) that is my favorite. And they have about 20 beers on tap, all usually quite good, and many local.

For something unusual, you can check out Cevabdzinica Sarajevo for Bosnian food on 34th Ave (BYOBish). Snowdonia is a good gastropub on 35th Ave and 32nd St, with good food and beer options. Mamu Thai Noodles has just opened on 36th St and 36th Ave, behind Kaufman. Also on 36th Ave are Arharn Thai, Chela & Garnacha, and Arepa Cafe. You won't lack for options!
