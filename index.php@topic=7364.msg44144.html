I invited people to discuss this matter because Isaw the article and thought it was an important issue to discuss. I believe that alcohol abuse in JH is a big problem. If you believe that limiting the number of liquor licenses is a big waste of time and that having fewer establishments serving or selling alcohol would make no difference, that is a legitimate opinion. I personally believe that 361 liquor licenses in a residential community is excessive and that in this time of contracting resources, fewer licenses would be easier to police than more.  I don't think that having 300 licenses in Jackson Heights (or just not more than 361) means people will have to start brewing bath tub gin. But I'm glad people are weighing in and sharing their opinions.
