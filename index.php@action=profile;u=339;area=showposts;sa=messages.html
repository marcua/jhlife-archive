Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hosted by the Friends of Travers Park and the Jackson Heights Green Coalition at Terraza Cafe in Jackson Heights.

Friends of Travers Park is a committee of the Jackson Heights Beautification Group. Friends started out cleaning up broken bottles and removing graffiti at the park; then planting shrubs and perennials. More recently, we've run Family Fun Day, back-to-school book-give-aways, Storytime in the Park, and Summer Sundays in the Park, a multi-cultural performance series. Recently we've begun to work on open space and other environmental issues, including creation of a Green Agenda for Jackson Heights.

Jackson Heights Green Coalition is a community group dedicated to creating sustainable public spaces in our neighborhood. Our goal is to create and/or expand parks, playgrounds and other green spaces in our area through outreach, education, and leadership.

Very simply, it's an informal social gathering/networking event for folks that are environmentally minded. Come and we'll introduce you to some of the other like minded folks in and around Queens. The Real OCTOBER Queens Green Drinks
Saturday, October 25th 4-7pm*

Hosted by Build It Green, 3-17 26th Ave, Astoria

Join us for at Build It Green's fantastic and big warehouse space turned Queens Green Drinks venue and see first-hand how they keep reusable materials out of NYC's waste stream. Our generous host are opening their doors and providing free sangeria carefully crafted by Donation Coordinator Forrest Gillespie and live music and performances from their multi-talented staff! Beer will also be available. It's an event not to be missed!

Build It Green! NYC, is New York City's only non-profit retail outlet for salvaged and surplus building materials. Our warehouse has everything from panel doors to high end refrigerators and shutters to movie props. Our mission is to keep these materials out of the landfill, while offering deep discounts on their resale. We are sponsored by Community Environmental Center (CEC) www.cecenter.org. Founded in late 2004, our warehouse opened in February of 2005.

Build It Green! NYC sells salvaged and surplus building materials at the warehouse -- great products at half or below their new price. And you help keep perfectly useful material out of the landfill!

*Note change in time!

How do we get to Build It Green by public transportation?

Walk 1 block NORTH on 4th Street.
Turn LEFT onto 26th Avenue.

By Bus:
Take Q18, Q102, Q103 to 27th Avenue and 4th Street.
