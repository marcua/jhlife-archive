Updates from the Grow a Park campaign.

We have raised over $270,000 in pledges so far! We still need to reach our target of $500,000, so if you have not pledged yet please send an email to pledges@growapark.org with your name, address, phone number and pledge amount.

Mayor's Office

Today (Friday the 18th) Deputy Mayor Wolfson is meeting with the Board of Trustees and the administration of the Garden School to iron out the details of the City's offer for the athletic field. We remain very hopeful that the result will be good for the school and the community! We will keep you informed as soon as we hear something about this.

Outreach campaigns

There are teams of people targeting the local merchants, you may have seen some stores sporting the "We pledged!" signs in their windows and work continues on reaching out to banks, philantropists, celebrities and foundations.

We are also working hard on increasing awareness of our campaign and there has been a lot of interest from the press, most recently The New York Times covered the story - links to that article and all the others are in the press section of our campaign website, www.growapark.org

I'd also like to highlight the series of articles that the New York Daily News has been featuring on the Garden School issue and the Grow a Park campaign. Links to the articles can be found on our website.

JHBG Newsletter

Our partners, the Jackson Heights Beautification Group released their newsletter in which the Grow a Park campaign was extrensively covered. If you haven't received their newsletter you can contact them through their website at www.jhbg.org and become a member - they are a very valuable organization in our neighborhood who offer a lot of great projects you can become involved in, from a clean streets movement to the musical performances of Summer Sundays, and more.

Letter writing campaign

Our letter writing campaign was a huge success which resulted in over 600 letters from neighborhood children to the Mayor asking for more park space in our neighborhood. We are planning to make good use of all of this hard work very soon.

Finally, we are hoping to work more closely with the Garden School over the coming days and weeks, to support their outreach efforts to parents and alumni in order to continue getting pledges which will help alleviate the school's financial needs until the money from the City comes through.

Thank you to everyone who has pledged and volunteered and offered advice and suggestions!

Get Involved,

Dudley
Jackson Heights Green Alliance
dudley@jhgreen.org
