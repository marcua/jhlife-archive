JH Food Group - Sunday in Colombia o Ecuador
« on: October 27, 2008, 03:52:37 PM »
ok, here's another choice for the Sunday night Jackson Heights Food Group.

The challenge was to find a way to spotlight one of the Latin American cuisines of the neighborhood. There are so many and so many restaurants, it was hard to sift through and find ones to spotlight a cuisines. of course, there are many confused into bars/clubs. there are others which are not that great quality or run by managers and not people concerned with their cooking.

Through recommendations, articles, walk through visits and figuring what I thought of as authentic, I've narrowed it down to 2 places in the heart of jackson heights which spotlights the culture of the neighborhood.

El Meson Colombiano
http://www.yelp.com/biz/el-meson-colombiano-jackson-heights
one of the best colombian food places in the area. the staff seems to take pride in their food.  prices are reasonable/on par with the rest of the big colombian places. nice environment. gets crowded after 7, so I would recommend getting there at about 615 so we can get some attention and also get a feel of what goes on as the night goes by.

Hornado Ecuatoriano
76-18 Roosevelt Ave.
http://www.yelp.com/biz/hornado-ecuatoriano-astoria    --- this is the astoria location though
http://www.project-me.com/2006/04/hornado_ecuator.html
the namesake is a skin-on roast pork dish. which always sounds good to me.
varieties of corn is a common element here. the plates have those large kernels of corn and they have some popular corn drinks. It's definitely distinctive from the mexican and colombian places that I am accustomed to.
if you want guinea pig, you have to special order it and its 35 dollars for a plate of 2. which is interesting. there are actually 2 hornados on roosevelt, one in the 70s, one in the 80s. the menu is very undescriptive. some different stuff in there im not used to. seems pretty legit.
oh my god, check this out
http://nymag.com/daily/food/2007/11/riding_the_v_line_guinea_pig_o.html
prices are a couple of dollars more across teh board here:
http://www.menuism.com/links/show/d1EH2ijWWr3BqKaby-t3N_?type=AllMenus

So there are my choices I send to you, for the second meeting of the Jackson Heights Food Group. Anyone have any preferences?
Also for the date... nov 16th or 23rd
