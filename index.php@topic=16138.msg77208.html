Quote from: Hot2015 on March 13, 2017, 09:28:10 PM
Why are 1 bedroom and junior 1/2 bedroom apartments in the Berkeley sold at higher prices than other coops such as Dunolly Gardens or Warwick? What are the pros of the Berkeley buildings relative to the nearby buildings?

950 sq. ft is pretty big for a one bedroom/Jr4.  Some 2 bedrooms sold in JH don't have that much square footage (though as other posters noted, it may be a bit awkwardly laid out).  It helps that the Berkeley has a fantastic block long yard.  They are smart over there as well, investing in a website.  The $542 a sq/ft might not have been reached in the Warwick, a wonderful pre-war, but last October a cute 800 sq/ft one bedroom went for $375,000, which is $469 a sq/ft (and given how things are moving around here, I'd give odds that it would have sold north of $500 sq/ft if it closed last week).   
https://www.zillow.com/homedetails/7612-35th-Ave-APT-5O-Jackson-Heights-NY-11372/122587904_zpid/
 
f you really want to get a good feel for the differing co-ops, take the JHBG's tour this spring.  If you are lucky enough to walk with Daniel Karatzas, you will seriously learn everything you want about JH (he also has a book out, on sale at Espresso 77).  Also find out about building finances.  That's a major bender.

I had always thought that the mantra stated that if you were going to sell, just fix it up a bit and save the money, as you won't get a dollar for dollar price increase on the renovations you are making.  But I've noticed as of late, the well renovated, turn key flats are commanding prices that cover those renovations and then some.  Could be my population is just skewered though.
