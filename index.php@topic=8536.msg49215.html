I find pigeons and their poop (and, by extension, any human who feeds pigeons) all repulsive, I just don't know that there's much to say beyond that. The solution to the problem is to round up the pigeons and dispose of them, but I don't think anyone would really publicly endorse those measures, and have fun getting paint-bombed by PETA if you do. You could scrub the sidewalk under the 7 train three times a day, every day, and it would still be covered in bird crap. The fact is, the birds are plentiful, that's where they hang out, and they crap a lot.

I live in Washington Plaza, and there's one tree next to the lower fountain that the birds love. The maintenance team sprays down the walkways every day, but sure as sh--, every morning there are piles of it right under that tree. Under the 7 train is that times a million.

I read a book a long time ago, White Teeth by Zadie Smith. There was one character who was so frustrated with birds crapping all over his awning that he would kill the birds with his meat cleaver (he was a butcher). His mantra was, "The sh-- is not the sh--. The pigeon is the sh--." It wasn't a great book, but I always remembered his mantra.

Anyway, it's not that people aren't in agreement with you, I'd guess that many are. I just don't think there's much that can be done aside from crossing Roosevelt as little as possible, and at other locations (there tends to be more crap under the stations than in between them) when it's absolutely necessary. And publicly shaming people who feed the birds.

I disagree with that attitude that there's not much to be done.
  • Several times per week if you walk by at night you will see there is a truck that gives out handouts of food. I am not against handouts of food but in this case, the place ends up a complete mess the next day as people sit on the street there while consuming their food, and then some of them just dispose their garbage on the street. Oftentimes the trash cans are completely full. Solution here is to a) empty the garbage cans more frequently and b) revisit any sort of license that this handouts truck has. All the other food establishments in the area have licenses and need to adhere to practices - why not this one?
  • I walked by today and there was a puddle of human urine. I'm not sure if it's from the same people getting food handouts or not, but either way it's disgusting. More police presence in this area would stop this because urinating on the street is illegal.
  • They just paved the sidewalk under the train there, but they left it with a rough concrete surface rather than a smooth one. This makes it impossible to scrub any bird crap away! Sure even if they scrubbed it every day there would still be some bird crap, but since this is such a high-traffic area near one of the biggest subway stations in Queens, it needs some regular attention. As of now, I don't think it gets any.
  • As a long term solution they could cover up the underside of the tracks so that there are no spaces for a bird to nest.
  • There is a momo cart that sits almost practically under the tracks there in the stench of the urine and bird crap. Whatever you do, don't eat there.
