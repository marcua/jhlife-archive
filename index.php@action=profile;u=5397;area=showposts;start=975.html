Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: NYT - JH House on 86th Street listed for $1.45 million ?!?
« on: September 09, 2014, 10:56:23 AM »
 Lot's of Jackson Heights kids don't attend the public schools here. There are other options locally with parochial,
independent and charter. Forest Hills and Bayside schools are currently SUPER crowded whatever their previous
reputations have been. This makes for a cottage industry of extra's all over the borough like test prep and college
counseling which really should be included in the school day. Our local public schools work well for a number of
people but they are not really that diverse as in certain cultures favor one school over another and sway the
stats there so they do not look at all like the neighborhood diversity when you see schools let out for the day.
  I would look at our schools visually for starters and see if you picture your kids in with the groups coming or
going from each school. Or will you be one of the many parents commuting with their kids very early/late
elsewhere or putting them on a school bus to some farther away Gifted/Talented scheme? We do have choices
here in JH though and by HS students are usually on the track to specialized schools or some program matching
their interest. The small HS's here (Garden School and Renaissance) and the Parochial schools have had some
outstanding college placements that I know of so if you think long term, remember to include them.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Queens Day, Aug20th, US Open- free concerts, kids activities,Mary Poppins@night
« on: August 19, 2014, 04:17:08 AM »
  The US Open will host its inaugural “Queens Day” on Wednesday, August 20, featuring Queens-based performers, exhibitions and family activities throughout the grounds of the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center in Flushing, N.Y. Local Queens-based musicians, from jazz, to rap, to traditional Indian, will perform throughout the second day of US Open Qualifying.  Also, in conjunction with the Queens Economic Development Corporation and Chamber of Commerce, information and other offers for Queens businesses will be provided to fans and visitors.
 Doors open at 10:00am. Play begins at 11:oo am.
 Queens Day will include performances from: Mind Open, instrumental and jazz band, Bethany Wild, vocalist and songwriter
Uruza Performing Arts, N’YCE, Derrick Anderson, rap artist, Mestizo, Latin dance,Miss Reign, percussion, poetry and hip-hop
Roopa Mahadevan, Indian Fusion American soul singer, City University of New York, jazz band,Ayazamana Ecuadorian Cultural
Center. The Queens Museum and New York Hall of Science will each have exhibits during Queens Day.

 Also, a free movie screening of “Mary Poppins” will take place in Flushing Meadows-Corona Park, beginning at dusk, at
111th street, near the carousel.
 

                                Doors open at 10 a.m. Admission is free. Play begins at 11 a.m.
