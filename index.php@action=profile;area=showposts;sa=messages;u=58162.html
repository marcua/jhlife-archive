Neighborhood Chat / Improving Safety on Northern Boulevard
« on: September 19, 2018, 11:16:36 AM »
We live near the new boulevard of death in Queens. Five pedestrians were killed near our section of Northern Boulevard over the past 12 months.

Here's an important petition asking that the city to solve the problem.

https://campaigns.transalt.org/FixNorthernBlvd-The-New-Boulevard-of-Death

Please take a second to sign it, your signature is important and takes one minute.
 

Here the people killed:
Jose Gomez, 71, killed September 13, 2017 on Northern Blvd. near 85th St.
Myriam Nino, 82, struck on January 5, 2018 at Northern Blvd. and 90th Street in Jackson Heights, died on January 20th
Giovanni Ampuero, 9, killed on April 28, 2018 on 70th Street and Northern Boulevard
Carlos Gavilanes, 47, killed on May 21, 2018 on Northern Boulevard between 100th and 101st Streets
Unidentified male, 70, killed on September 9, 2018 on Northenrn Boulevard and 108th Street

Neighborhood Chat / Important meeting in Sunnyside tomorrow
« on: December 18, 2017, 11:41:18 AM »
Tomorrow there will be an important public meeting in Sunnyside to discuss the redesign of 43rd Ave and Skillman Ave.

The redesign includes measures that will make the streets safer for everyone — there have been more than 340 people injured in crashes on those two streets since 2010.

But this is especially important for Jackson Heights cyclists:
1. those streets are dangerous for cyclists (one died there earlier this year)
2. those streets are the most direct route to Queensboro bridge and to Parks in LIC and Astoria.
3. the redesign includes a protected bike lane.

More info on this video:
https://www.facebook.com/qnsbike/videos/1565037263572926/


TUESDAY Dec. 19, 6pm-8pm
Public meeting with NYC DOT to discuss 43rd / Skillman Ave Redesign @ Sunnyside Community Services: 43-31 39th St

Neighborhood Chat / Public meeting to improve bike infrastructure in our area
« on: October 23, 2017, 01:13:01 PM »
NYC DOT will hold a first meeting this Wednesday to collect community input for the Big Jump Project at the Queens Museum. This is your chance to have a say if you want more and better bike lanes and other types of cycling infrastructure in the neighborhoods of Corona, East Elmhusrt, Elmhusrt and Jackson Heights.

Big Jump Community Advisory Meeting
Wednesday, Oct. 25, 2017
6:30 PM to 8:00 PM
Queens Museum
RSVP here: https://www.eventbrite.com/e/big-jump-community-advisory-committee-meeting-tickets-38770017165?mc_cid=6400061f7a&mc_eid=a9d14892ea

What is the Big Jump?
It’s a three-year community-driven planning process that aims to improve cycling safety while strengthening bicycle infrastructure within the neighborhoods of Corona, East Elmhusrt, Elmhusrt, and Jackson Heights. This initiative will include programming to encourage residents to use alternative modes of transportation and will draw on local community input to develop on-street improvements such as general traffic calming designs, new bike facilities and traffic flow enhancements.

What's next?
Over the next year, DOT will partner with residents, Queens Community Boards 3 and 4, elected officials and civic groups to hear what neighborhood residents want in an effort to develop community-centric programming, projects and events.

Neighborhood Chat / Sunday Oct 22 #BiketoberfestNYC in Jackson Heights
« on: October 18, 2017, 05:27:32 PM »

#BiketoberfestNYC in Jackson Heights
Sunday, Oct 22
11 am to 3 pm
(With Bike Ride at 1pm)

This Sunday, Oct 22, NYC DOT will be distributing bike-related goodies and information at Travers Park. In addition, at 1pm, our friends from Make Queens Safer will be leading a bike ride in our area.

What will happen:

• A bike mechanic present will offer free services like pumping tires and fixing brakes.

• Bike education material and free giveaways like bike lights, bells, and tote bags.

• NYC DOT will collect feedback from the community on what they like/don't like about cycling in our area.

• New and inexperienced riders will get the chance to learn more about how to commute by bicycle and to move around our neighborhood.

• Share information on rules of the road and existing bike routes.

• Answer any questions local residents may have regarding the biking or the bike network with assistance from DOT’s bike program staff.

• There will be a guided bike ride departing from the station at 1 PM led by Make Queens Safer. It will be a short ride (about an hour, a few miles) showcasing local points of interest by bike and safety infrastructure. This will be a slow, safe and hopefully fun and instructive ride.

Just show up! It’s FREE!

Dear Mr. Plaza and Marco, thanks for signing the petition. And also thanks to the 24 other people who also signed it since we posted this update here a couple of days ago.

Marco, we are not affiliated with Transportation Alternatives, but we have a good relationship with them. Some people who volunteer with us are TA members and we share some common goals. But we are just a neighborhood group. Many of us also drive, most of us are not really activists, per se. Our ranks are  neighborhood residents, a good number of parents and seniors, originally motivated by the goal of riding a bike to a park safely. Also, we're focused only in Queens (mostly on Community Boards 1, 2, 3 and 4). You can read this thread, it's how Queens Bike was born:
http://www.jacksonheightslife.com/community/index.php?topic=13432.0

As for better access to Queensboro bridge, Transportation Alternatives is currently campaigning specifically for this goal. They meet every first Tuesday of the month at 630p, normally at Queens Pride House (here in JH). More info: https://www.transalt.org/getinvolved/neighborhood/queens

For those who have not signed yet, please take a second. We're approaching our delivery date, Thursday: http://www.qns.bike/petition
