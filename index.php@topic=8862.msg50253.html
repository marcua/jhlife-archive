Diversity Plaza and the 78th Street Plaza are both part of the DOT's Plaza Program. Not all of the plazas that have been created in the city in recent years are part of this. As 83Street was told at Dromm's office the final version is a few years away from being completed. The plans for plazas that have come out of this program are very impressive and seem to be much more permanent in appearance to what we currently have in place at both Diversity and 78th Street Plazas.

Here is a link to the list of plazas that are part of this program: http://www.nyc.gov/html/dot/html/pedestrians/publicplaza-sites.shtml

Diversity Plaza was only selected last year in the 5th round of the Plaza Program. If it is truly the work of one business owner and his wife as some have mentioned, they should definitely be congratulated as I am sure there was a lot of competition.

I'm familiar with the Myrtle Avenue Plaza which was part of round 1 of this program in 2009. The plans for this plaza were only released earlier this year and construction is not expected to begin until next spring. It sounds like there may be similar frustrations in that community as well in terms of the length of time it is taking, but it is getting done: http://www.myrtleavenue.org/blog/index.cfm/2013/8/29/NYC-DDC-Says-Myrtle-Plaza-Construction-Will-Begin-in-the-Spring

Thank you for all of those that organized the meeting with Dromm's office and have coordinated the meeting with SUKHI. It is definitely important that the community is involved. I will attend if I can.

We had a very positive and productive meeting this evening among neighborhood residents and the folks at SUKHI. About a dozen people showed up, which is already many more than the three that met with Dromm's office last week.

We are well on our way towards forming a local coalition to improve this plaza. Tonight was very encouraging and everyone offered their personal concerns and ideas about this space, so we have our foot in the door. But we will need even more people to make sure that this initiative makes an impact. Anyone who has concerns about this plaza or good ideas about its improvement is encouraged to join us.

We will be creating an email group to communicate with each other, drafting petitions to submit to city agencies, and coming up with ways to assist SUKHI in making this space attractive. We all have a sense of what the problem is, but this is an opportunity to be part of the solution. If you are interested in getting on board, please reach out. Everyone's participation is very much welcomed and needed.
