Welcome! Parking in the neighborhood is extremely difficult, no getting around that. A few suggestions:

1)Jackson Heights has great access to public transportation. This is your best option. If that is impossible:

2)Check to see if any of the buildings with parking rent spaces to non-residents. Several years ago this was sometimes possible, although I have to imagine it has become more difficult.

3) Walk through the neighborhood and look for private houses with "parking space for rent" signs . They show up every so often.

4)Check all of the lots in a decent radius, though I imagine they will all be pricey, as space is at a premium. Good luck!

Eva,

Welcome to the neighborhood!

I live in on 80th street and parking is a nightmare for me! I get about a ticket a week (mostly due to pure laziness or forgetting street cleaning zones)

The summer weekends are the best at finding spaces, most people seem to go away

During the week, it's pretty terrible, average amount of time to find a spot is about 15 minutes

My building has a garage but there is a 7 yr waiting list, I am on year 3 and 90% of the garage members do not even live in the building!! It's only $115 a month too, ugh

Also my car has taken abuse from parking in the streets, last year I turned in my leased bmw with over $2500 worth of damage to the front and rear bumpers and the car only had 17,000 miles in 3 years, my current CRV has already had the front bumper replaced from parking and the rear doesn't look the greatest. Welcome to Queens:)

If you can find a spot on the cheap, jump on it!
