I'm the "Beverage Director" there. I pick out the beers, ciders, etc. I try to get local, sour, bottle conditioned and beers that are found in South Asia.  We don't have draft so it's only bottles. Sometimes I get a little crazy with ordering but we mostly have between 10-15 beers. I have some regulars but mostly I buy just one case of each beer so we have a lot of variety, and because I get bored easily. Comparatively, they are priced very well, especially the more expensive ones. We get some really nice beers but the prices they sell at in other places just wouldn't fly here.


It's been an interesting learning experience with the distributors, the government, and the public. Happy to discuss more.

Sorry to be the voice of reason, but BYOB is illegal in the State of New York, unless the restaurant has a liquor license.

The penalties are typically a minimum of $1,000 for a restaurant in the form of a Criminal Court Summons, and this hurts the chances of applying for a license in the future.

https://www.sla.ny.gov/bring-your-own-bottle-byob
