When the neighbor smokes
« on: May 27, 2014, 02:33:39 PM »
I'd love to hear your advice on this situation. Our neighbor across the hall is a widow and lives alone. She is a sweet lady and we always chat when we see each other.  She used to feed the neighborhood's feral cats, but she recently had some heart problems and now is mostly homebound.
Now her main hobby is smoking. She was always a heavy smoker, but after her health problems it's gotten worse. She stays inside and smokes day and night. She rang another neighbor's doorbell at 1 AM, looking for matches. The smoke drifts through our window and under our front door. We've got a baby and four year-old; we don't want them to get asthma. I also worry she'll set something on fire.
I'm not looking to get her evicted. Ideally I'd like her to get some help, though apparently she refused to have a home health aide, probably because they'd ask her to stop smoking. We've tried talking with her and she apologized for the smell, but didn't stop.  There's probably no chance of her quitting at this point.
So I'm looking for tips! Know a great senior services organization that works well with the stubborn homebound? Ways to clear the air of cigarette smoke or prevent it from entering the apartment?

Sorry to hear about your neighbor's troubles and the troubles it causes you.

You really don't know what a person goes through mentally or physically when they have a serious medical issue. I can imagine that being more housebound would possibly mean also having more time on her hands, maybe some boredom too. Any smoker can tell you that boredom combined with things weighing on her mind would tend to make her smoke more. I would also think that being in most of the time also means all of her smoke is in her place whereas getting out would put the smoke outside directly.

I'm sure everyone, doctors included, have told her she needs to quit especially with her heart condition. It is her choice and you really can't force a person to quit. 

Ringing anyone's doorbell or telephoning them at 1 am is never polite unless you are absolutely sure they are up and would welcome the visit. As for setting the place on fire, assuming she isn't mentally impaired, there are just as many chances for a fire from candles, faulty wiring, bad extension cords, overloaded circuits, ovens etc. etc.

Now regarding your concerns, you could repectfully mention your discomfort. Maybe you both could keep your windows that are closest to eachother shut. Filter your air or use the a/c. As for the door, there are gaskets that could be installed at the bottom of yours. That will also keep critters from crawling in and help stop menus from coming in under as well. Ask your super about them or visit Home Depot.

You have your rights linsey, and so does she. Talk to her, try to be sympathetic rather than attacking, and then become pro active about your apartment.

If the smoke is only coming in through a window and the front door, there is at least some fortune in that it's not coming through vents, light fixtures, etc. What I would do is keep the window closed year-round and seal it as much as possible. Same for the front door when it's closed. You can also add some house plants that absorb chemicals from the air and consider a high-priced air purifier.
