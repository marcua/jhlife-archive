It occurred to me yesterday that one of the problems we often talk about here and with other neighbors who are not on JHL is traffic congestion in our streets. One of the contributing factors to that congestion are these livery cars that circle around and around looking for fares. They also idle in bus stops, and other sometimes legitimate parking spots. We have all asked for stepped up enforcement of traffic laws to help reduce the congestion we encounter on a daily basis, hence the enforcement of this law.

Now I realize this particular incident happened at 11pm, a time when our streets are not particularly congested. Perhaps the fact that the officer let the driver off with a warning is an indication that he was sending a message to this driver and the other drivers who work in that area all times of the day that the laws regarding picking up fares in the street are being enforced here. As word spreads more drivers will avoid JH and in theory this will result in less congestion. 

While this may be effective, it is not ideal for the drivers or those of us who rely on the service they provide. Why not create a taxi stand where the cars can wait for fares to come to them (engines off to avoid polluting the air). Years ago there was a taxi stand on Broadway just east of 75th St. probably at a time when yellow cabs were more plentiful and less Manhattan oriented. But, if we relax livery law enforcement and designate one specific area for these cars to congregate then we are solving both problems in a win-win fashion. Additionally assembling all these cars on one common area makes it easier for the police and TLC to occasionally check up on them and make sure they are all up to snuff.
