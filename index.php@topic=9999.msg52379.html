I went in and took a look at this place when they were selling as condos.

First of all, I'm weird but I LOVED the view of the 7 train. there are apartments about 10 feet away from the tracks and you can see all the way down the line. You have a great view of all the peopel standing on the other platform too. As for the noise, it was definitely silenced quite a bit considering it's proximity to the train. Honestly, I get a ton more noise in my current building from being on the streetside and the children upstairs from me (don't know what the soundproofing is like in the Roosevelt building though, floor to floor). They told me it was built for magnitude earthquake 10 or something like that....

whatever,

but yeah, the flooring, walls, kitchen are total new crap. And the layout has all small rooms that are awkwardly placed. I really cannot believe people are actually paying money to live there. paying a lot of money. But then again, if you want something spotless, where else are you going to go in JH/Elmhurst/Woodside? I think a lot of people don't appreciate classic stuff  - thats why they don't consider JH housing. You can argue all you want about it's beauty, but if it's not new, lots of people (with money) don't want it.

There is another redeeming aspect.... A lot of those apartments have balconies. Plus, the location is as close to the train as you can get - convenience is king.

It looks like a penthouse at The Roosevelt has set a new rental record for the area:

A Penthouse at The Roosevelt Rents for More Than $4,000 a Month

The Roosevelt, the new rental development located at 40-07 73rd Street off Roosevelt Avenue, just set a new rental record. A penthouse unit in the building rented for $4,100 a month which, according to Trulia, is the most expensive apartment to ever rent in the neighborhoods of Woodside, Elmhurst or Jackson Heights. The apartment features a large open living area, two private terraces which total over 750 square feet, two full bathrooms and floor-to-ceiling windows.

There are four units out of 31 left in the development, which launched last October. Citi Habitats is not negotiating rents, although the apartments are no fee and there is an offer for two months free on a 13 month lease. Broker Mike Schulte tells us that many of the renters are transitioning from Brooklyn or Manhattan. “We knew we needed the right person for this penthouse unit,” he said. He also reports that a City MD is moving into the commercial space downstairs.

http://queens.brownstoner.com/2014/06/a-penthouse-at-the-roosevelt-rents-for-more-than-4000-a-month/
