Neighborhood Chat / Good Samaritans
« on: February 15, 2016, 07:40:36 PM »
Yesterday AM while the temperature was -1, I, and my companion, came upon a man who had fallen on 34th and 87th.  He said he had dislocated his shoulder and could not get up. He was in a lot of pain.

I don't know how long he was on the pavement by the time we got there.  We were walking our dogs without our phones, and a passerby lent us his to call the man's wife.  After he (the phone lender) left to go to work, I flagged down the first car I saw, and those people lent me their phone to call 911 and the man's wife again. 

Another gentleman gave me his scarf to cover the fallen man's ankles which were exposed to the cold.  If you are reading this, I have your scarf - please PM me so I can return it to you.

The ambulance arrived pretty quickly and got the man and his wife to safety and warmth.  I think we were out there for nearly an hour.  I came pretty close myself to getting frostbite and I hope the man (Joe is his name) who was on the pavement is ok.

To the man on the bicycle and everyone who passed by and offered to help - thank you.
