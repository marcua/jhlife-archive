The shop on 86th Britton is very friendly.
I also like Hospital de bicicletas Mexicana on 91st just off Roosevelt. NYTimes did an article a while back on the owner- I can't find it quickly. They will fix a bike and make it ridable, but if you're a stickler or have a high end bike it's probably not the place.
But, an amazing place to see in action on a busy day.

The best bike shop nearby is 103 on 43rd Ave- New York Bike Shop. They do high end repairs and the owner has years of Manhattan experience in other high end shops.

I just organized a tour for kids and two mechanics from Tony's in Astoria rode with us. They have a racing team and were super guys.

Of course, there's Cigi on 93rd/37th. Their regular guy, is quite gruff but I do pray they stay in business.

Keep an eye on Qns. Bike and Make Queens Safer on fb or Twitter for local rides for families. The one I helped organize yesterday in Astoria was called Pedal to Parks and we're hoping to do a Jh/Corona/Elmhurst/East Elmhurst one before end of June. You're right, 31st Ave lane was completed and it's a great route all the way to the Astoria Waterfront.

I was recently shopping for a bike and I visited many shops. My favorite by far was Nomad Cycle in Astoria. They specialize in both vintage and new bikes and they have a full service shop. They also offer *free* classes on topics such as roadside repair. the staff seems extremely knowledgeable and trustworthy. They are definitely willing to take the time to explain things. I went to a few shops that seemed like they were only interested in making sales. At Nomad Cycle they seem to be genuinely interested in bikes and the biking community. It is also a very nice, uncluttered space.

I ended up getting a used bike through a friend and I managed to break the chain on my first ride. They were the first shop that came to mind for a repair. I was in and out very quickly and I'll definitely be back.

They are located a few blocks from the 36th street R train station, convenient to JH.

Nomad Cycle
36-58 37th St.
Astoria, NY 11101
E-mail: info@nomadcycle.com
Tel: 1-718-786-5600
http://nomadcycle.com/
