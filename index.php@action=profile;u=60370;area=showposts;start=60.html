Restaurants & Food / Re: brewery here
« on: March 26, 2018, 01:19:55 PM »
Visited the brewery this weekend. So many taps! When we were there most were Mikeller beers but as things kicked they sometimes replaced with other guest breweries like Other Half, Hill and Bissell! Check out what's on here: https://www.mikkellernyc.com/menus/#on-draft

Pint pours tend to range between $6 and $7.50. They also offer a 4oz taster size and some higher ABV beers have an 11oz pour option. They are also selling to-go pint cans of their flagship beers which are to be made on site like Say Hey Sally (pilsner) and Henry Hops (IPA). Really great (I think adorable) NYC/baseball themed merchandise is also for sale.

It sounds like the food menu is a still being firmed up. We really enjoyed the cheese steak sandwich done by the Whitman's restaurant folks in the East Village. The space is very large and welcoming. I for one will be back there soon. If you like beer this is worth the trip!

Here's a little backstory about this brewer, his estranged brother and their respective new Queens breweries coming online: https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/21/nyregion/evil-twin-mikeller-breweries.html

Neighborhood Chat / Re: traffic bollards
« on: January 11, 2018, 07:30:46 AM »
It seems to me that 35th ave is kind of a blind spot with the DOT.

In early October I requested that they consider doing the delayed green light to allow pedestrians to cross before traffic proceeds..just like they did on 37th ave (called in their terminology: Leading Pedestrian Interval Device) on the Southwest corner of Travers Park. Got word yesterday that they will not do it saying there were "no significant turning vehicular/pedestrian conflicts."

This is a block that abuts the main grocery stores for this part of JH, has a park on it, a house of worship and a few schools. You can often hear cars honking when the light turns green and no one moves because people are crossing.

Plus this street is seemingly now an alternative car route to the Grand central parkway when the BQE backs up and google maps and Waze redirects traffic. This literally happens daily around 3pm...just as the street is flooded with students from the local PS! It's a total mess. But I guess they observed it at a different time.

I think you should keep applying pressure to get those removed. The DOT is fairly responsive to these online complaints/suggestions..even if they don't always come back with the result you're after. 

Good luck!!!
