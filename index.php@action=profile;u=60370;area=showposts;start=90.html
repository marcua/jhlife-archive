Neighborhood Chat / Re: catering trucks
« on: December 14, 2016, 07:53:02 PM »
Just two cents from a former film technician: A non-catered meal is called a walkaway. It is generally done when there is no adequate place to cater a meal. In union labor rules the production needs to allow 1 hour off for walkaways as opposed to 30 minutes from "last man through the line" for a catered meal.

Please keep in mind these people are working 12-14 hour days not including their commutes...so anything that minimizes extra time at work can literally saves lives by the end of the week. By Friday, when many productions are so far behind, shooting can start as late as 7pm and carry on well past dawn...becoming what is known as a Fraturday.

So y'know what...these folks working in the neighborhood might actually encourage them to come back when they can actually enjoy a meal on their own time... But getting these hardworking individuals through their work week safely should always be top priority...and catering plays a role in this.
