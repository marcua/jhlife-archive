Community Groups, Activities & Events / Love Express Film Screening
« on: July 29, 2017, 05:27:28 PM »
"Join us for a film screening of Love Express, featured by the Queens World Film Festival and director by Patrick Chen.

The film is set on the 7 train line that operates from Manhattan to Queens, New York. It transports a mix class of commuters, predominantly Hispanics and Asians, to their station. Even though these commuters are different by their ethnicity and neighborhood; they all share the 7 line as a community.

As his reason behind the film, Patrick Chen states:

“As a subway rider, I have witnessed love, hate, sadness and happiness in these MTA trains. It has become an unphysical form of connection with these strangers that bind us together as New Yorkers. I wanted to focus Love Express on the perspectives of young Chinese-Americans and their lifestyle. It was important for me as a writer to capture a respectable persona of Asian Americans that delivers a positive and honest perspective of their characters in an everyday scenario that captures the “slice of life” moment.”"

For more information please go to the following link;
http://queenshistoricalsociety.org/events/love-express-film-screening/

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Queens World Film Festival
« on: November 28, 2013, 10:19:01 PM »
Queens World Film Festival will be screening 2 films at the Queens Museum
Saturday, December 7th at 6:00-8:00 pm

The 2 films are grouped together under the title: Graphically Speaking
Graphically Speaking presents two films whose imagery is manipulated to examine an emotional underbelly, exploited to the point of expressionism. These images appeal to the subconscious to impart story elements. It is not necessarily linear or non-linear storytelling, rather a layered approach that folds back and forth upon itself.

WAY by Elizabeth Pasieczny
Welcome to Nowhere (Bullet Hole Road) by William Cusick (Founder’s Choice QWFF 2013)

Come see these two special films in a world class facility and join us for a talk back with the filmmakers, casts and crews following the screening.
