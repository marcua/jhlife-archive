Sammy's Halal - Chicken or combo over rice without any sauce, so I can taste the food and keep it lighter and more healthy.

Mexican Food trucks on Roosevelt - There are better ones towards Junction, but they're too far away from me. Steak tortas, chicken tacos or quesadillas.

East 21 Boy - 3 roll sushi lunch special. Usually use a random Seamless coupon I get in my e-mail to make it cheaper.

Louie's - 2 slices of Grandma Sicilian. Best in NYC.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Pot busts and gang activity in Travers Park
« on: March 14, 2017, 10:41:17 PM »
Quote from: jeanette on March 14, 2017, 04:56:31 PM
Rogue, what do you think about public drunkenness?

Is there anything that offends you?

Do you have kids? (personal question, don't answer if it offends you, ha ha)

I look at it this way: non-supervising adults are not permitted within a certain distance from a child's play area. Smoking pot, smoking anything is an adult activity. No one should be smoking pot within that distance. It's not about offensiveness. If you can smell it, it is entering your nose, and behind the nose of a two-year-old is a developing brain where neural connections are being established. No one is going to do a study to find out how thc affects a baby's brain development, but I wouldn't doubt that there is some effect.

Air pollution is detrimental to a child's brain development. Again, not about inconvenience/offensiveness.

Breathing in secondhand smoke is basically the equivalent to smoking. It's been proven in tons of studies to substantially increase risks of multiple forms of cancer and cause many other health problems. It's particularly problematic to babies and young children as their bodies and lungs are still developing.

There is a difference between something like smoke stacks/vehicle exhaust and secondhand smoke. When people smoke in places like their apartments, restaurants, parks, beaches and other public places, the smoke is more often than not being breathed in directly by others due to proximity. Whereas the other forms aren't and only contribute to general air pollution. And if you look at the AQI (air quality index) for NYC, what you realize is that it is actually one of the best metropolitan cities in the entire world for air quality. And it's mainly because of its walkability, and ubiquity of public transportation, hybrid buses, modern cars with lower emissions, and lack of factories and industrial areas.
