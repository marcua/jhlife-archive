New School in Jackson Heights!
« on: June 16, 2009, 09:10:06 PM »
Hello parents, students and friends!

Jackson Heights' very own Renaissance Charter School plans to open a sister school! The new charter school called Renaissance Charter High School for Innovation hopes to open for the 2010-2011 school year, close to our original campus on 81st street and 37th avenue. We will be serving the high school students in District 30 which includes LIC, Astoria, Sunnyside, Woodside, and of course Jackson Heights.

Just like the original school, Renaissance Innovation will continue to develop leadership and provide amazing support and opportunities for its students. We have a three tiered educational model of core classroom instructions, portfolio-based annual individual projects and hands-on, experiential learning.

As an alumni from the Renaissance Charter School, I can honestly say that a second school with the same model would highly benefit the young people in our neighborhood. The smaller class sizes that gives opportunity for individual attention as well as the dynamic programing really gave me and many other students an amazing educational experience. The current Renaissance Charter School (TRCS) is at capacity. We need this new high school to further provide an empowering education to the many other young people in our communities

Renaissance Innovation plans to build and sustain a strong relationship with the neighborhood and the community. We would love to discuss our plans and hear your thoughts. Please feel free to personally email me or post your questions and comments up on this thread. If you would like more information or to meet with us, don't hesitate to ask.

We are currently seeking the support of our community in the form of a letter. Any letter of support would be highly appreciated. If you or someone you know is a 7th grade parent, please get in touch with me personally for a petition that will added to our charter application. This is very important to our application as the current 7th grade students will be the eligible freshmen students in the opening year. This is not to register your child but to show your interest in the school.

Thank you for reading this
I hope to hear from everyone soon!
-Leanne

Check out our website: http://renaissanceinnovation.org/

when I say 'not granted a location until the application has been processed,' that's just what I mean. When you found a school, you can't call dibbs on a building before your plan has been approved. You're right that it doesn't always happen late, but sometimes it does. The school is not due to open until September of 2010, so the DOE has no obligation to give us space until much later in the process.

To answer the question about buses - yes, elementary and some middle school kids are bussed in. It's true, there are quite a few middle schools in Jackson Heights. But as far as I know, the current Renaissance is the only public high school. And with so many middle school children advancing into high school from the schools in the neighborhood, the need is clear to us.

Oh and by the way, the school will support about 125 students per grade, for a total of 500. I imagine the makeup of the population will be similar to the original Renaissance - a majority of local kids (meaning Western Queens) but also quite a few kids from all over the boroughs.

Thank you everyone for your feedback. It's good to know we've gotten a conversation started.

Emma
