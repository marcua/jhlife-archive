April at Espresso 77 - the big thaw has started and the music is heating up...
We've got amazing music, art and literary events going on this month, including
an opening party for a show of new paintings by E77's Afzal Hossain!
Please come join the fun!

All music shows start at 9pm, unless otherwise noted
TONIGHT! Friday, March 31 - Kvetch: modern klezmer
Saturday, April 1 - Jon Christopher Allen: rock and soul
Sunday, April 2 - Eldad Tarmu: (starts at 8pm) Jazz Vibraphonist (don't know what vibraphone is? This is your chance to find out!)

Tuesday, April 4 - First Tuesdays Reading Series: Featured speaker - Sokunthary Svay
Novelist and short-story-writer Svay was born in a refugee camp in Thailand after her parents fled Cambodia once the Khmer Rouge regime collapsed. She is a Pushcart-nominated Khmer writer (poetry and nonfiction) and musician from the Bronx, New York.
Sign-up for open mic starts at 6:30pm, program starts at 7pm

Friday, April 7 - J. Armen Meschian and Friends: Acoustic rock with Latin soul
Saturday, April 8 - Violet: Modern rock and dream pop.

Wednesday, April 12 - JH Artist Talks

Just a reminder - our monthly Jackson Heights Artist Talks event is tonight at 7pm - hope you can make it!

This month, we will be featuring photographer Adam Nadel and painter Christopher Lowry Johnson. Each artist will speak for approximately 15-20 minutes about their work, and then take questions.
7 pm at Espresso 77
35-57 77th St., Jackson Heights

Hope you can make it!
Linda Ganjian, JH Artist Talks founder
Julie Nymann and Afzal Hossain, Espresso 77

Adam Nadel
Nadel's solo exhibitions on disease, war, and human rights, have been exhibited at over two dozen venues around the world including The Field Museum in Chicago, Hotel de Ville in Paris, The Emir Palace in Abu Dhabi, The National Museum of Ghana, and the United Nations Headquarters in New York City.
 
Nadel, a former Staff Photographer at the Associated Press in NYC, works both domestically and internationally for publications such as The Times of London, Time Magazine, The Telegraph (UK), and The New York Times, who nominated his work out of Iraq for a Pulitzer in 2006. His touring museum projects on civilians and war, disease, and environmental issues, have been funded and supported by the National Science Foundation, Magnum Foundation, Malaria Consortium, and the World Heath Organization, and New York Foundation of the Arts Fellowships (06,13), among others.
 
Christopher Lowry Johnson
Christopher Johnson lives and works in Queens, NY. Primarily a painter, he received a BFA from Penn State University and an MFA from The School of the Art Institute of Chicago. He has exhibited in New York City since the mid 90s including in several solo shows at Plus Ultra and Winkleman Galleries. His art has been reviewed and/or reproduced in publications including the New York Times, the New Yorker,  Harper's Magazine, TimeOut New York, and Art Forum. Examples of his work were also reproduced and discussed in Art: A Sex Book by John Waters and Bruce Hainey. In his paintings, a variety of generally familiar kinds of imagery are  used to depict themes of melancholy and longing as well as grotesque overabundance.
