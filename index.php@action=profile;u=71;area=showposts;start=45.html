Restaurants & Food / Re: Gabbana - new restaurant/lounge opening in Corona
« on: January 09, 2011, 11:33:58 PM »
I went here on Saturday night with some friends. We had a reservation at 8:30 and left around 11. There were people, but it was not crowded. Service was good, ambiance was nice and the food was amazing. Seriously amazing. Like, I'll talk about it to anyone that will listen the day afterwards. I've eaten a lot of Latin American food since moving to Jackson Heights, and was expecting something along those lines- but nicer. What we received was phenomenal.

The ideas were all what I expected- fish, pork, arepas, oxtail, guacamole, paella- but the execution of the food was superb. The chef was really able to elevate these dishes to be something beyond what I expected. Even the guacamole was *better* than I was expecting- and I have eaten "high end" latin food before (Rosa Mexicana during Restaurant Week). It was brighter and fresher than guacamole I had had before. The acid was different some how- tangier that I was used to. Maybe it had lemon as well as lime? Whatever it was it was really good.

I'd like to go there again, maybe try one of their cocktails (we drank wine) and some of the other stuff on the menu- everything looked really good. Between 4 adults we had 4 entrees, 3 appetizers, 3 alcoholic drinks and 1 non alcoholic drink and it came out to be about $100 a couple so it wasn't even outrageous for a nice night out. If anyone has been thinking about trying it, I wholeheartedly suggest you go for it.
