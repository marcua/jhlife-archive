I really don't think this statement was made to offend anyone.  Of course, there are people out there who need food stamps for a legitimate reason, and it is perfectly reasonable that one would have an occasional indulgence, like Haagen-Dazs.  However, there are many who abuse the system, have no intention of trying to make a better life for themselves and don't use the funds they are given responsibly.

If one chooses to not take care of themselves and buy horrible food, and they receive funds that allow them this, then I guess that's fine--it's their health that they are ruining.  But it's sad to see when a person has children and they are buying a cart full of processed and expensive junk.  It's less expensive and healthier to purchase fresh vegetables, fruit, rice, etc. for their children, perhaps with a few indulgences.  I think the Haagen Daz was used as an example, because with a cost of about $5 for a tiny pint, it is something that those of us who work and pay our own grocery costs may think twice about before buying.  Yet, someone who did not have to pay for their food has no problem freely loading up on items such as that without thinking twice about it.

My personal peeve is people who rely on government assistance and yet they spend their money on cigarettes.  You see this all the time, and it's absolutely disgusting.

Perhaps this discussion would do with a strong dose of facts?

"88% of food stamp households have gross incomes below the poverty line ($18,100 for a family of four in 2002)."

The anecdotal examples above are basically just that. A non-representative example that has little do with the reality of those on food stamps. I highly doubt that "many" are abusing the system (where's the factual basis for this?) as most are just simply trying to get by. As for the the ice cream example which started out this entire discussion, perhaps their child a birthday party or maybe they were celebrating a landing a job. Frankly, we don't know but to condemn the system based on one apocryphal instance is pretty reactionary.

Having said that, while I understand having a broad range of options under the food stamp program alleviates the stigma and provides the recipient with more choice, I'd probably be more comfortable with restricting candy and icecream purchases.
