I hear you rasert.   And truth is, for me as a musician, I've never been fully comfortable with the idea of crowdfunding.   But once I saw artists starting to use it creatively and as a way for friends and fans to pre-buy albums, it started to make more sense for me.  Like having perks where $10 gets you an album, $20 gets you an album plus signed poster, $50 gets you an album, poster, plus VIP show, etc...  Then it becomes and I'll scratch your back you scratch mine sort of thing. An opportunity for the fans to connect with the band and for the band to further their art through extra financial backing.

It was for this reason I posted earlier on this thread for E77 to consider adding perks at a lower price point.  This way it might feel less like you are backing them financially and more like you are being rewarded through customer loyalty through perks.   Like $10 gets you a free coffee beverage of your choice,  $20 gets you coffee and an entr√©e of your choice. $35 gets you the same but Throw in a T-shirt, etc.  Them it becomes more like what you said as far as you are basically pre-ordering. Showing business loyalty, if you will.
