Title: Re: Suggestions where to find this special cove tile?
Post by: NYC Native on January 27, 2011, 11:52:47 AM
@ carrefour

Depends on the mud or thinset, most of the time if you hire a true ceramic contractor they know how to modify the installation so as to accommodate a thinner or thicker tile, as long as the face profile is the same.  A lot of this older installations have a very thick amount of cements material and might be tricky to fit in.  The contractor or yourself can use a grout scraper to remove the entire grout.  Since this is an old installation you will need patience.  If need be you might want to use a grinder with a special disk. Take a small chisel and lightly hit with the hammer and be careful because you can break the adjacent tiles.

Tap the broken tile with a small cold chisel and lightly hammer it until it breaks up.  Once the tile is out, then the decision will have to be made on how to remove the cement/glue used.  My guys like using high velocity power tools but it requires a little practice.  After the area is clear it's just a matter of setting the tile deeper until it is at the same level as the other.  Are you going to remove the grout and re-grout the walls?
