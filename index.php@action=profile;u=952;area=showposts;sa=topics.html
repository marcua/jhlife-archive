Restaurants & Food / Fresca La Crepe in Woodside
« on: June 19, 2010, 01:51:38 PM »
There is a new crepe place at 61st St & Woodside...we went there today and it was actually pretty good!   They have savory buckwheat crepes with some classic (ham, egg, cheese) and nouveau fillings (tiger shrimp).  And the lighter-colored dessert crepes with classic (butter & sugar, Nutella) and nouveau (coconut, S'mores).

We tried the veggie crepe and a beef, onion, and gruyere savory crepe on special, and a nutella and banana for dessert.  All the crepes were soft and not too thick or chewy.  There isn't much seating, but the place is cute and you could definitely eat standing up outside.

The chef told me she opened in Woodside because she has an aunt that lives there and the rent is cheap.  This place is her first restaurant...kind of reminds me of Nixtamal and Timmy O's in that someone is trying something new in a place where the cost of entry isn't too high. 

Definitely would recommend you try this place out, especially those that were hoping for Le Gamin in our neighborhood!

Restaurants & Food / Tortilleria Nixtamal and Timmy O's (Corona)
« on: July 18, 2009, 04:10:14 PM »
We don't go to the food group because we have a one-year old who is a bit of a wild card at restaurants, but on the way to the Queens Zoo today we stopped by Tortilleria Nixtamal and Timmy O's.  Both were fantastic!

At Nixtamal we had the fish tacos and three kinds of tamales (verde, rajas, and italiano).  The fish was fried very nicely...kind of crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside.  But the tortilla is clearly the star.  Very flavorful and perfect texture without being too heavy.  We bought a pound to take home!  The verde and rajas tamales were both solid, but we didn't think the tamales were as fantastic as the tortillas.  The italiano is a fun one to try for a different flavor: italian sausage, mozarella and tomato sauce.  They also have some good soda choices...we tried an apple-flavored one whose name escapes me.

The owner let me take the kid in the basement to see them fermenting and grinding the masa.  They have a tortilla machine up front, but it wasn't operating at the time.  A NYTimes reporter was hanging around the place looking to interview people who came to buy masa, and he quickly found a couple all the way from Connecticut.

Great service and can't wait to go back...hopefully the food group can report on more of the tacos and the pozole!

Timmy O's is just a block or two away.  Timmy was the only one there and seems to do most of the work himself.  He makes frozen custard...see here for an explanation (more egg yolks and butterfat than ice cream!):

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frozen_custard

Timmy was very talkative, so he told us a bit about the store.  He's from Long Island, but traveled to a culinary institute in St. Louis (my hometown) to learn how to make frozen custard.  There are dozens of custard places in St. Louis, and he went to most of them while he was there!  Most he asked me about I had never even heard of.  He sources his custard mix from a local high-end dairy, but the vanilla flavoring he uses comes from a supplier in St. Louis he makes up real vanilla for custard places.  Timmy has a proprietary mix made from Madagascar and Seychelles vanilla beans that he is quite proud of!

Daily he has chocolate, vanilla, and a "flavor of the day" which was dulce de leche.  He'll let you try all three for free...you can get them mixed as "concretes" (a St. Louis term for frozen custard with ingredients blended in), but honestly the custard is better on its own.

I wish both of these places were closer to 74th St, but rents are probably lower in Corona!  Try out both and help them stay in business!

Real Estate & Home Improvement / Questions for Co-op board members / RE professionals about tax assessments
« on: March 29, 2009, 11:36:34 AM »
Hi everyone,

My family and I closed on a co-op in JH in November 2008.   During 2008-'09 our building had about 60 rental units that were converted to co-op status and sold by a sponsor, so a significant fraction of the co-op residents are new owners.

Earlier this year the co-op informed everybody that the maintenance fees would increase by 5% starting in April to cover a higher tax assessment.  Even though the RE market has flattened or dropped in the area, the tax assessments apparently take a few years to catch up.  Fair enough.

This weekend everyone received a letter along with their maintenance bill stating that we are being charged an additional tax assessment this month of about $0.65 / share.  In our building that works out to almost a double maintenance fee for the month of April.  This is on top of the 5% increase in maintenance that kicked in this month.

Here is where things get really confusing.  Most of that additional assessment fee will be covered by the $400 NYC tax abatement, STAR , senior citizen programs, etc, EXCEPT FOR NEW RESIDENTS.  The managing agent claims that new residents are not eligible for the tax abatement if we bought after Jan 1st 2008, so we will have to pay the full assessment.  The management also suggests that they are taking the tax abatements to "balance the budget", and not necessarily to cover higher RE taxes (although this point is not at all clear in the letter).

If you have read this far, then I would like to request your opinion on the following questions:

1) Does it seem right to charge an additional tax assessment if the maintenance has already been increased 5% to cover the higher tax bill?  And maintenance fees in our building are higher than what I've seen in RE ads for Jackson Heights, even though the building finances seem to be in good shape.

2) If new residents are not eligible for the tax abatement because they weren't owners during the full period the abatement covers, then why are new residents "eligible" to be charged a full tax assessment?  It seems like the management is having it both ways, but only in the ways that favor taking more money from us.

My suspicion is that someone on the board or at the management agency saw a one-time opportunity to make a windfall off of the ~60 new owners in the building by charging this assessment when they knew the abatement was not available.  Back of the envelope calculations would suggest that this additional tax assessment amounts to $40,000-$60,000 for the co-op.

Thoughts?  Other similar experiences?

Neighborhood Chat / Bellerose another Jackson Heights?
« on: February 23, 2009, 06:45:20 AM »
Anybody else see this article?  Apparently some old white folks are afraid that Bellerose is becoming another Jackson Heights!  It could just be one or two guys...it's hard to tell from an article such as this one.

http://www.nytimes.com/2009/02/22/nyregion/thecity/22froz.html?_r=1&ref=thecity

I lived near this commercial area for about six months while waiting for renovations on my JH apt to be finished.  Like many urban fringes, it has been in commercial decline for awhile (a combo of low population density and aging).  If anything, the South Asian businesses are adding some much needed life.  And the businesses are all on major commercial streets (mostly Hillside Avenue just east of the Cross Island)...it's not like they are opening inside residential neighborhoods.  The pattern of development here is very much suburban, with strips of mini-malls and then separate neighborhoods of single-family houses with lawns.

The "City" section in the New York Times lately seems to exist mostly for people to complain about how the city is changing or otherwise doesn't meet their desires and unrealistic standards.
