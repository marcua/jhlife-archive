What exactly is gentrification?
Gentrification is the phenomenon of affluent folks moving into less wealthy neighborhoods, renovating homes and attracting new businesses. ... Or, as summed-up by the Centers for Disease Control: “Gentrification is often defined as the transformation of neighborhoods from low value to high value.”

Gentrification is a general term for the arrival of wealthier people in an existing urban district, a related increase in rents and property values, and changes in the district's character and culture. The term is often used negatively, suggesting the displacement of poor communities by rich outsiders.

Well, you picked what is probably the second most expensive coop in the neighborhood after the Towers for your example, so it's not an apt example.
No one is arguing that prices are going up for real estate. But gentrification requires a location that has few amenities like supermarkets, drug stores and banks. We have more than we need.
For gentrification, commercial rents have to be cheap to allow for the places that start to change an under-served neighborhood (like cafes and hipster bars) that starts the cycle of rents starting to increase. JH has prohibitive commercial rents.
Call it what you want, but it ain't gentrification. All I'm saying is that we are defining urban growth incorrectly when we call it gentrification. Bushwick is gentrifying. East New York is probably next. JH is not. Plenty of people get priced out of "urban growth" neighborhoods too, but it's still not gentrification.
