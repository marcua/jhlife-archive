Neighborhood Chat / Re: Update on 84th Street and 37th Avenue
« on: March 08, 2013, 11:38:19 AM »
A bit off the topic but why are so many storefronts emptying out recently?

We are new to the neighborhood and have sadly watched as Mehfil Indian restaurant disappeared, as well as the nearest dry cleaners, Rudy Volcano, that little internet cafe-type space (granted it was dumpy), the weird corner store that sold phones and medical equipment, most recently Novo, which personally I liked, and even that Latin dance studio on 75th St on the way to the subway is now empty. The Dime Bank on our corner expanded--why does a bank need that much space? Do I ever see anyone in there? Okay maybe one customer at a time and a lot of empty, pristine desks.

Mehfil, which we loved, was replaced by another Indian fast food joint with glaring fluorescent lights and the ubiquitous Coke dispenser in the dining room. I read on one of these forums that the rents are outrageous and the landlords just take the empty spaces as tax deductions and thus don't care. There are people on this site speculating on what might take Novo's place, while I am worrying that nothing will.

Now that we have been here over a year, and the novelty of 1001 cuisines at bargain prices is beginning to wear off--and even though we like those offerings-- I am starting to feel disgruntled at the lack of certain basic things, like a nice place to have brunch on 37th on a Saturday, or to have a drink with friends.

It does seem like there is a strong market for Espresso 77--good quality food and drink, nice setting, a place to meet friends. I love it there--and I also like JuJu's very much (ever tried their desserts?), though they are open very limited hours.

I spent one Saturday with a friend roaming around to find a nice brunch or lunch spot and honestly, after walking the length of 37th and finding nothing, we wound up at Uncle Peter's on Northern, a bit of a shlep.

On 37th, there are downscale, kind of depressing and dumpy coffee shops (nicer lighting and decor and fewer hand-scrawled signs tacked to the windows would work wonders), or places where when you walk in, they have to search to find an employee who speaks English to deal with you.

Duo Fratelli seems to have a large Italian menu, not just pizza, yet you eat off plastic trays there on the few crowded tables. Not a pleasant "eating out" experience.

We were interested in trying one of the Latino places in the 80s, but they did not seem interested in us. We asked if they had a lunch menu, and after some confusion, they finally muttered something about $19.95, without proffering a menu, so we just moved on.

I know I sound like what used to be called a yuppie and will probably be castigated for it, but I don't understand why there aren't at least a few more upscale options in a neighborhood that would seem well able to support them. Our neighbors bike into LIC in nice weather to have brunch. There just isn't anything here.

I find myself going to Astoria for certain things, too, like nail salons. The ones around here are either unpleasantly small and crowded, unsanitary-looking, or once again, they have to find an interpreter to deal with a non-Spanish speaking customer. I really wanted to find one I liked here, and I expected to--but was disappointed.

I am not that demanding or snobby, either. I used to live in Manhattan, yes, but in Washington Heights, not the Upper West Side.  We love so much about Jackson Heights--but I'm beginning to be frustrated about what's missing, at least for me.

I'll stop now--so people can start attacking me for my elitist attitudes!
