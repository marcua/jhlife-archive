By now I'm sure that most baseball fans know that Mets Manager, Willie Randolph, was fired overnight after the Mets' win over the Los Angeles Angels (9-6).  I feel ambivalent about this firing.  Why did Mets management wait until the team played in the west coast to fire him?  The news didn't break here until a short while after 3:00 a.m. New York time.  I suppose they didn't want the media to come down on them this morning.  That's pretty inevitable though because I'm sure the next round of newspapers will blast management for their questionable tactics.

The issue here is not so much if Randolph should have been fired.  It's the way that it was done that I find objectionable.  In my opinion, he deserved more respect than he got from the Mets organization.

Along with Randolph, pitching coach Rick Peterson and 1st base coach Tom Nieto were also fired.  Bench coach Jerry Manuel will serve as interim manager.
