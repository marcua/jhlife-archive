Shelby- Cash and Carry stores are restaurant supply outlets.  It allows retail food operators to buy in bulk without having to buy too much of anything.  Its good for smaller retailers who dont have stellar credit or tons of cash on hand.  Costco operates this way for both resale and non-resale consumers.

Restaurants & Food / Re: New pizza place in the hood!
« on: November 10, 2010, 03:16:50 PM »
Hey foxy- i love south bk pizza, but last time i went, it was very greasy and wet.  they've just opened an outlet in manhattan, too, methinks.

Louie's does sell whole pies.  but i like to see what they have in the window and decide based on what looks best.  some of their pies use fresh mozzarella and some of them have fresh basil, too.  The grandma slice rules. 

Even better than the pizza are the homemade pastas, though.  The tender, eggy fettucine reminds me of something you might find in Istria, Slovenia, or Croatia.  Really special stuff.  I haven't made my way around the entire menu but I know they offer a ribeye and some other mains.

Enjoy!

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Thinking of Moving to Jackson Heights-- Questions
« on: February 11, 2010, 03:35:44 PM »
Here is one answer to your question about commute times.  I live 1 block from the 82nd Street 7 train and 6 blocks from the Roosevelt Avenue stop on the E, F, etc trains.  For a time last year, I was commuting to my office in Soho to and from my home and also to and from a friend's place 1 block from the Astoria Blvd. stop on the N and W.  The commute to my office was the same from both Queens locations.  Hope this helps.
