That video is adorable!

This thread brings back memories of when my husband and I decided to videotape our first dog while we were out.  We had her confined to a specific area in our apartment, so it was easy to position the video camera to catch all her activity.  While we were out for approximately 2 hours, she went about jumping the safety gate we had installed in order to prevent her access to the kitchen.  She was a small dog, so it was amazing to see how she managed to climb over the gate and then climb back.  The camera also caught her repeatedly sniffing our shoes and then napping by them.

I now realize how it's possible for a dog to easily get in trouble when not supervised.  My current dog has a crate that we used when she was a pup, and to date she remains comfortable with it.  We haven't had to place her in the crate when we leave her alone, yet when we return, we find her stretching as she comes out to greet us.
