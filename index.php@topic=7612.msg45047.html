I been wanting to give them a try but have no idea how and what to order. I enjoy the fact that it brings a sense of community and variety.

Well, as someone very white who only speaks English, here's my experience.

In front of the stands they usually have, as a display: a tostada, an order of cheese flautas, a roast corn, and the fruit of the day, which is either a box of chopped-up watermelon or a mango carved into the shape of a flower. Also, hidden, they may or may not have tamales.

There are two options. For everything, you can answer the question of "espicy?" with "yes" or "no." Beware, if you ask for it espicy you'll get it espicy thanks to added green sauce. Only for the tamales, the options are chicken or pork.

So just go up there and say, "one order of flautas," or "one corn and one chicken tamale," or whatever. They'll give it to you in a takeaway container and tell you how much it costs in English. It's very difficult to spend over $10.

Re: Piggy backing off farmers market
« Reply #7 on: July 27, 2012, 12:47:14 PM »
They probably do have food handlers licenses, but probably do not have permits. Someone might have a rolling cart with ice cream, and that probably has a permit, but the others definitely do not. Most do pay taxes, but it's most likely at a rate similar to any other cash business - lots of honor system going on.

Just some info, you cannot legally obtain a mobile vending permit anymore. The waiting list is 15+ years, and you cannot get on the list anymore. If you want a permit, you can get one for $400 a week, or $20,000 for 2 years -- current prices. This may or not be illegal b/c permits are non-transferable.

And if you are worried about the cleanliness... just think about what happens when your food goes through the closed off back-kitchens of every TGIFridays. I had friends who worked there in HS... these are the people you DO NOT want touching your food. Transparency is your best protection, and word of mouth - not a card with a picture on it.

--But yeah, I want to see what happens when the police start showing up and giving them tickets. I know some people would defend them, probably create a scene. It's an interesting issue.
