The key is that soul food is the cultural food of people descended from slaves in the American South of the 18th and 19th centuries. In the Great Migration between 1910 and 1970, millions of those African-Americans moved from the agricultural South to the industrial North, bringing their soul food traditions with them. This made areas like Harlem, South Central Los Angeles, and the South Side of Chicago centers of soul food. As that was nearly a hundred years ago, there have been many generations of soul food in these cities by now!

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Migration_(African_American)
