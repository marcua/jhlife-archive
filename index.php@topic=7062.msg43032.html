Noise in general is a fabric of life issue in JH.  My experience is that if you complain you will be mocked and told to move to Park Slope at best.

One Saturday I came down with a mind-blowing migraine and was in bed.  A store across the way from my building brought out a sound system and was blasting music and announcement on a loop.  My husband went downstairs and complained to a cop on the corner.  The cop told my husband he asked the store to stop and they ignored him.  However instead of giving them a ticket for public nuisance, he did nothing.  Finally, after my husband told him I was sick he want back and told them to knock it off.  Unfortuately this is often the "reality" we are forced to live with.

I can definitely emphathize with you and I wish you the best.  You deserve to live with reasonable quiet enjoyment in your home.  If I were you I would call 311 and report them as trespassers, which from your post they clearly are, even without the noise.

This doesn''t always work but it does sometimes, so it's worth a try.

If you try not to speak out of anger, I find there's a better chance of being heard.  Go outside and talk to the kids calmly, from the heart, as if they were your own kids.   Tell them that you work at home and that the noise is really interfering with your ability to do so.  Ask them if they could be mindful of their neighbors and also if they could possibly play somewhere else.

Kids have a surplus of energy and, depending upon their ages, they like to mess with grown-ups a bit, especially if it seems to impress their friends.  Basically, though, they want to do the right thing and they need someone to show them how to do that.

There's every chance they will be fresh and not at all receptive to what you say but I've often been surprised at the result when you I speak from my heart.  As I said, it's worth a try.

Best wishes!
