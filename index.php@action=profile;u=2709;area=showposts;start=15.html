Neighborhood Chat / Re: JH Buildings discussion
« on: October 16, 2012, 12:24:11 AM »
I was really more focused on the particular unit, I knew I wanted all the things that make apartment living tolerable/enjoyable (and usually also relate to resell ability): top floor, elevator, architectural details, laundry.

You want solid financials and policies that fit you. As far as "lesser" building I don't think that requires any inside info.  I think so much of that is simply when you walk up to it are you disappointed that it isn't some other building down the block?  If the location, entrance, courtyard and units are all something that speaks to you then it probably also appeals to others.

I don't like anything overtly image conscious or uptight.  I wanted a responsible but laid back building and I feel like I totally lucked out with what I found.  Its the Hampshire House on 79th, I've never heard it mentioned among the elite buildings of JH but I feel like its a hidden gem, its got cute red awnings out front, the units have character details, the elevators play music and most of the times I go into the garden I feel like I'm Mary from the Secret Garden.  Plus most of my neighbors are fantastic, there are still a lot of sponsor units (technically this is a drawback) rented out to a bunch of really wonderful little old ladies and I like the not just community but almost familial feeling to the place.

Its going to be your home so go to as many open houses and different buildings that you can and see what feels most comfortable to you.  talk to neighbors if you can.   oh and check the bed bug registry
