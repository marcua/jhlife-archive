does JH need its own Origami meetup?
« on: September 18, 2011, 12:54:34 PM »
I've been going to Manhattan to an origami meet-up where people just fold together. It is very relaxed -- no formal teaching, just lots of friendly exchanges of information, and folders of all levels from total beginners to professionals, from kids to the retired and everyone between.

However, I don't always want to get on the subway. I have recently met several other people in JH who like to fold, and it occurs to us that maybe we need a local meet-up!

We are thinking about starting with one Saturday afternoon per month.

The big question is location. If there are just a handful of us, we could meet at one of the usual cafes.

If you think you might come on occasion, if you can think of a good place to meet, or if you are connected to a business that would welcome folders, send me an email offlist.

thanks!

Origami meetup, Sat Oct 8th.
« Reply #1 on: September 30, 2011, 01:44:17 PM »
Recently I've been running into people in the neighborhood who enjoy paper
folding, including Christina and Karen who ran origami sessions on the
playstreet in September.

I occasionally go to a meet-up in Manhattan, where people (mostly adults, but
they welcome children with their parents) gather to fold.

All levels -- from the beginner to the professional (yes there are
professionals!) -- come to the meet-ups. There isn't formal teaching, but the
community is generous -- the unwritten rule is if you learn to fold something
from one person, go and teach it to someone else.

We are going to try to get such a group going here in JH. We expect to start
small, so our first meeting will be at Juju's at 3:15, Saturday Oct 8. In
general, we will try 2nd Saturdays for a few months, and take stock. I plan to
be there for 2-3 hours, so stop by!

I'll provide no-frills origami paper and some books. My own tastes lean towards
the mathematical (tessellations, modular, and connections between folding and
math) but I have some beautiful books on animals and toys as well.

If there is anyone else on this list who is interested either in coming or
volunteering to teach a model contact me offline.

thanks!
Sunita

Nice, Kevin. I should have thought of putting up pics.

And several folks with kids have written me asking if it is ok to bring them. One of the nicest things about the Manhattan group is that all are welcome, and we would like to recreate that same kind of feeling here in our neighborhood.

One clarification: The adults that come are there to fold as well, so the focus won't be exclusively on teaching kids (as it was at the workshops on the PlayStreet).

So if you are planning to come with your child, you should plan to fold yourself! I.e., learn a model along with your child, so that you can go on to practice together and your "teacher" can go on to learn a complex model from someone else, or work on some other challenge they have set for themselves.
