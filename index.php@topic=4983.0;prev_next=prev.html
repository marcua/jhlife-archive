handbills, posters and flyers
« on: October 13, 2009, 03:58:10 PM »
I read somewhere that John Liu's campaign was going to be fined for its illegal postering during the election season, and that it's a per-offense penalty. I can't find the article now and yet that's not why I'm writing here.

I noticed postering on the poles on 79th & 37th Ave -- "Donate your Car" it reads -- apparently a non-profit. While I've called 311 about that, and you might too (if it bothers you; I was mostly calling to find out the rule but did, in fact complain), I also asked about the postering on cars.  This has come up here before, with, I think, the general consensus that this fell to the 115th Precinct and that they really don't care about this.

So... I don't blame them.

However, I did call Innovative Vision Care and suggest that they run around and pick up the flyers that were distributed overnight on every car in the neighborhood. I notified them of the law. Their number is 718-424-5500.  I'd rather not get them in trouble, so I didn't file a complain and the nice woman who answered the phone was unaware of the law and that the penalty for it was per-instance.... (You'll note that John Liu's fine--if actually handed down--would amount to well into 6 figures)...

Anyhow, give them a call and encourage them, as I did, to send an employee out, on this beautiful Autumn day, to pick up all that trash.

If this is something that bothers you, lets run a little blacklist here. If you like it, and appreciate the advertising, call them to let them know that, too, and perhaps you might take some business there.

Thanks!

New York City Administrative Code prohibits the posting of fliers to any city owned property unless it's posted by the city itself or its agents.  I know that car windshield ads are also included in some part of the Admin Code so as to prevent sanitation issues.  Apparently, the code grants the police and sanitation department the authority to track abusers and ticket them based on phone numbers listed on the ads.

Francis,

Here is what I have done with a few serial postcard droppers, windshield leafletters,  flyer posters and it has worked quite a few times:

I either call or email and tell them I am from Community Board #3.  I know they usually think I represent C.B. 3 when I say that but I AM from C.B. 3 - I'm not lying.  I then tell them  that several neighbors have noticed that they are littering our neighborhood with illegal advertising.  My line is that dispensing this type of litter is not only not the road to success in Jackson Heights, it's illegal.  I say that,  if they persist, they will lose our patronage and they will also be hit with heavy fines.

I don't send the email or call anonymously because I don't think it carries any weight that way.  I give my name. 

If enough people take these flyers and call or email, the business owner will eventually get the message.
