First, to save people the walk, as I saw many confused people as I walked up 81st just now, the library is typically closed Saturday during the Labor Day weekend.

Also, on that note, we are lucky that, among many library Saturday closings effective this upcomning week, our is one of I think 17 that will remain open on Saturdays.  (In fact, a disproportionate number of 6-day-open libraries are in our area of Queens, no doubt to address the greater population issue.)

The sad thing is, just two or three years ago, almost all libraries were open Saturdays and a select few (including ours) were open Sundays except the summer.

I would hope that the closings are temporary recession measures, but I feel as a parent and teacher that this is an issue that our local political leaders may want to get involved with at some point.

Here is what I wrote on the Queens Library Facebook page in August when they announced the closings and budget ...


I remember when so many branches like Jackson Heights were open 7 days a week. I understand the impact of the recession on the library system, but I can't help but think there must be better ways to save money than Saturday closings.

For example, raising the overdue fees to 35 or 40 cents a day would generate more revenue, and it still not a horribly overpriced fine.

Or staff libraries partially with education major college students who earn credit for their service. (My college did this for working in the day care center for future teachers, it was life experience.) And most college students have to hit the library anyway ...

Have more book sales. So many books are donated to the libraries, yet very few make it to the annual book sales. Have the sales twice or three times annually per location.

Thinking like this won't solve the whole library budget problem, but it would lessen it's impact considerably.
