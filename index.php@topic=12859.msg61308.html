Thoughts in a nutshell...Ooh lala is just so, so so.

Ordered takeout from them the other night. Price seems reasonable but I can't help but notice the lack of anything along the lines of sides or salads. To me, if you have all the ingredients for a salad already why not include it? A crêpe for me might make a nice lunch but is a bit small for dinner. I want a soup, salad or some french fries with my meal.

You'll notice some typos regarding the menu.   Starting with the name I think it's spelled Oh la, la rather than Ooh la la.  On the menu you will notice that there is a section where you can build your own crêpe. Great! However, the girl I spoke to you when I called and I said I wanted to build my own first told me 'no' and then told me to wait and handed the phone off to someone else.  This made me skeptical as to whether they would get my order right so after I placed it in I asked him to repeat it back to me.  Unfortunately, even after having them repeat it back to me The order was done incorrectly. They added bacon to a crêpe that I wanted to be chicken avocado and Swiss.  This was disappointing, especially since when I went to pick up my order all four employees there were just hanging around. They were friendly but obviously had nothing to do at that moment.

Overall, the taste was fine. I suspect that there chicken is of the preformed chicken breast kind that you might see at Subway. Try it and let me know if I'm correct. It just looked like it had fake grill marks on it.

It is kind of annoying that they charge $.50 per take-out container. With such limited seating I imagine this is where most of their orders are going to come from. 

On the whole, I'm glad to have them in the neighborhood and hope they get over their growing pains and become successful. A long time ago I mentioned to just made 4 you that it would be great if they offered healthy versions of their crepes instead of just ones doused in cream sauces.  They sort of smiled and said okay but never moved further on that front. Maybe this place will fill in that gap.  If they don't expand their menu, I might order a salad from just made for you and a crêpe from oh, la la.
