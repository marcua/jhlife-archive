Quote from: dssjh on February 01, 2015, 02:46:12 PM
it is interesting that there are a lot of people posting here saying "well, i can't find [fill in the blank] in jackson heights," but when someone offers suggestions, the response is "i didn't know that was here."

it seems to indicate that there's a tendency to not pay any attention to what's right in front of you. that's not a criticism, just a note that we need to look around a little more. there are some great things in 11372 - and yes, some voids, but some things that might fly below the radar. look around, you might be surprised!

Part of the problem, is that these places are not all concentrated in one area.  If you shop at Food Town, buy pastries at Lety's and coffee at E77, you might never run across the health food store or Shear success.  There is also a lack of community space where people can advertise their businesses (E77 notwithstanding).   

Reasons I leave the neighborhood:

1) Schools.  My kids didn't get into any charter schools and now I send them to private school in Jamaica Estates.  (They are 13).  We were zoned for PS 69 and my kids were small and quiet and I found it too large, too noisy and too overwhelming.

2) After school activities for kids; art and ballet in LIC.  My son went to classes at Jackson Heights Arts club classes, but needed a more structured approach to prepare for his LaGuardia audition.  Loved the atmosphere LIC Ballet - not so much at Dance Dynamics.

3) Butcher.  We used to buy at La Portena when they carried beef, but that was shut down.  And they had a very limited selection. We also go to Ottomanelli in Woodside.

4) Yoga.  (LIC).  I can't seem to find yoga in Jackson Heights that fits into my schedule.

5) Gym (when I went).  I went to Curves until it closed and have been frustrated in my attempts to find something clean, not overcrowded and even remotely convenient.  (Preferably with classes.)

6) Restaurants beyond Columbian, Thai, Chinese, Peruvian, and India.  We eat out a lot in LIC - Manducatis Rustica, Corner Bistro, Cyclo, LIC Market, John's Smokehouse, Tournesol.  Mostly because we are there a lot for the kids. (See #2)

7) Movies.  Art house movies.  Blockbuster movies.  Really frustrating that I have to go outside the neighborhood.  But a few bad experiences at the Jackson Heights Triplex and bad sound turned me off that theater.

Parks.  Anywhere but Travers. Although I appreciate all the work that went into making the play street a reality - it was too late for our family to really take advantage of it.

Don't get me wrong - there are many reasons that I love this neighborhood, the multi-culturalism of it and my neighbors not the least of those reasons.  But a Bareburger wouldn't hurt.

I would add to the comments that encourage people to look around and check out what is under your nose. Suran's yoga space on 90th (link is in previous post) operates excellent and low-cost classes every weeknight except Friday and on Saturdays. She is a gem. Peter at For Your Hair on 76th is very skilled and reasonable. He is also recommended in previous posts. We have tons of fresh fruits and vegetables on offer in the area and a great farmer's market. Why pay for an overpriced brunch when you can have huevos puercos and an arepa at Rico Tinto? I have a Rite Aid on my corner that is open 24/7. The subway is two blocks away. I have been in NY for over thirty years and have never lived in such a convenient neighborhood or in such a nice apartment at reasonable cost, a feature i value greatly as a freelancer.
