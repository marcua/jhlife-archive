Neighborhood Chat / Re: Gentrification in JH?
« on: March 10, 2008, 09:38:35 AM »
Quote from: sixj on March 10, 2008, 03:53:48 AM
What struck me was that I had not, till then, thought of Jackson Heights as a place where gentrification seemed rampant at all, and in fact, I'm not sure I se it now. But I'm new, so maybe what appears to me to be a predominantly latino population with sizable sub-populations of east Asian, south Asian and Anglo families is in fact a vastly different place from a few years ago. I don't know. It just seems diverse to me, but maybe that diversity is actually newer than I'd realized. I would be really interested to hear from people who have lived in the area for 5, 10, 20, 50 years -- what is different now? Has it really changed? Whether it's gentrification or not is a whole other question, I guess, and I'm really just more curious about whether it's really changing at all, and if what the direction and speed of those changes are, if any.

Hey SixJ, I totally agree with you. I lived in Park Slope Brooklyn for several years, and that is what I would describe as rampant gentrification. JH has a strong community of diversity here, and I think the cool thing about JH is that everyone seems to respect each other for our differences. Beyond ethnic diversity there is a very strong LGBT community and a high level of acceptance of this community as well. Some people in NYC live only in one borough or neighborhood their whole lives (or since landing here like me) and I consider myself very lucky I got out of Park Slope's new found attitude and into a place like JH to experience that the rest of the world IS a larger place than just NYC or the US.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Fresh Direct
« on: February 20, 2008, 11:04:15 AM »
We have been using FD since they first started up, and were so thrilled when they came to JH. We order at least once a week, and really we get mostly staples, as well as their seafood. We don't have a decent fish market in JH so FD really makes up for that. Also since we don't have a car, going to Trader Joes or other groceries are a major pain, FD really makes that a lot easier. I do wish they would figure out a better way to package there groceries, or do like a box exchange program something that would cut down on the cardboard boxes. FD's has pretty good produce and they have a lot of organic produce that I've been more interested in getting lately.

That being said, I've been considering FarmSpot when it starts up again in the spring, an will check it out as well.
