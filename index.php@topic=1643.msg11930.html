Best Cheap Eats in the Area?
« on: October 14, 2008, 07:15:16 AM »
A comment from another poster about the price I paid for an Indian dish made me think...  Are there good cheap eats out there that I should be trying first?  Suggestions on all kinds of food (including dessert) and the location of the place in question would be appreciated.  I'm interested in vegetarian, but I'm sure others would love to hear meat bargains as well.

My suggestions:

1.  Maharaja lunch buffet--all-vegetarian indian buffet, from 12-4pm everyday.  They will bring you naan or roti and then you serve yourself.  Always a good selection of tasty dishes and I leave extremely stuffed every time.  Always a dessert included as well.  I believe the price is $6.99 on weekdays and $7.99 on weekends, but correct me if I'm wrong.

2.  King of Schwarma falafel cart in Astoria--Broadway & 30 St, Astoria, right by the Broadway subway station (N/W), in front of C-Town.  $3 falafel sandwich... so good, and the staff there is really friendly.

If you go to the supermarkets in Astoria, most of them have grape leaves made by the staff in the cases by the coldcuts.  I would try Bravo International Supermarket (on 34th Ave, I believe cross street is 35th St.) or Trade Fair (Broadway, right on the corner of Crescent).  I'm sure there is a place you could pick up homemade hummus and pita for cheap, but I'm not sure where.  If you post on www.astorians.com, I'm sure someone will get back to you within minutes!  You could also pick up delicious spanikopita as part of your meal (spinach pie).  The homemade one at Omonia (Broadway & 32nd or 33rd St) is really good, but I'm sure there are many more.  I think it costs under $5 for a big slice.  Omonia is only a few blocks from my favorite falafel cart (Broadway & 30th St)

Its a little out of the area but not by much.  Often I visit Lao Bei Fang Dumpling House at 86-08 Whitney Ave in Elmhurst.  Yesterday, I had 8 fabulous Fresh Pork & Chives Fried Dumplings for $2.50 no tax or tip.  You can also have them steamed for the same price (10 minute wait).  You can purchase 50 Frozen for $11 (which I do) and easy to prepare.  Toss the dumplings in rapidly boiling water for 10 minutes.  You'll love these dumplings.  The place is not fancy but where else can you get a great lunch for $2.50.  Lots of other items on the menu (yes, its in English) such as large bowls of hand drawn noodle soup pork or beef starting at $4.   You can also have the 8 dumplings in soup for just $3.25.
