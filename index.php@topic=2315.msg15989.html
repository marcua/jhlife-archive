Causidicus, my friend, if my injecting a little levity in to a post to keep the conversation lively and civil is going to be considered "smarmy"  then I would suggest you are the one being histrionic. It's clear this is an important issue for you, and I can't help but feel your approach to it is a little myopic.

You have singled out a specific group of people and suggested they are largely, if not solely, responsible for the perceived increase in crime in Jackson Heights. You talk about $2 Dance clubs and  "bars reminiscent of the home country that never seem to refuse even the drunkest patron" so it's clear you're identifying one specific group. I don't think it's wise to pick one group and blame them for a neighborhood's problems based solely on their immigration status and country or region of origin.  When we say that this group of people does "X" solely because they are immigrants, it runs dangerously close to generalization and I don't see how it can be an accurate point of view. 

I think my whole issue with the "immigrants commit crimes when the economy goes sour" theory is the fact that it plays into a xenophobic point of view. I am not calling anyone xenophobic, merely saying it's the beginning of a slippery slope that solves nothing. Instead of looking at people based solely on their country of origin why can't we look at another common thread ? Why not look at issues related to crime on an economic level. If it is true that immigrants are among the poorest in our society and are more apt to commit crimes when there are no other options, then they are doing it because of their financial status not their immigration status. Taking nationality out of the picture for a moment makes it easier to deal with the real issue.

Perhaps instead of criticizing my posts, you should focus on discussing solutions for the problems you have identified. For example, you suggest that part of the solution is to "reform immigration", yet you don't really say how it should be reformed. I agree with you  (believe it or not) that the way we handle immigration in this country needs some serious fixes. On the local level the immigrant population in JH needs a way to better connect to the greater JH community. Education and improved access to social services would certainly ease the transition I'm sure many immigrants deal with when they come here. This would probably do more to lower the crime rate within the immigrant population than round ups at the $2 dance bars etc. But in the bigger picture the solution is better opportunities and services for the poor, despite their status, combined with better policing.

I put this on a different post, but on second thought, it belongs here.  Years ago one of our Community Board Chairs asked the city to put satellite police stations at various hotspots in the neighborhood.  Based on a model used in other places, the city would rent a storefront on let's say 37th between 81st and 82nd, staff it with 5-10 officers and have it open 24hrs. a day, everyday.  It's a way of decentralizing the precinct.  It allows the officers assigned to the satellite to get to know the people and businesses in the immediate area, and to respond quickly to any problems.  Plus, the presence of a satellite stationhouse helps to deter crime and other antisocial behavior.  Just a thought.
