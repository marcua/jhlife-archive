Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

This makes me sad. I fear this is the not-so-new normal. Small businesses cannot afford the overhead it takes to pay the rent, stock the shelves and pay for the costs of having employees (not sure if Pauglina had employees or not). What has this city done to help small business owners? In my opinion, zilch, nada, nothing (please prove me wrong here- I welcome it).

Every single elected official should be championing the self employed and entrepenurial inclined members of this city and searching for ways to lessen the blow of the capital needed to start something new. I cannot even imagine how much money the owners/investors of the Queensboro have sunk into that project.....years later still nothing. How can you succeed when you begin so far behind the starting line?

To the owners of Pauglina- thank you for trying something new. Thank you for believing in this great neighborhood. Thank you for taking a chance. I hope you are able to move on to something bigger and better. Wishing you all the best.

I'm sure I'll get some hate for this, but I'm not surprised at all Pauglina closed. Yes, it seemed to have expensive rent but more importantly because it barely had anything to buy!

I tried multiple times to buy something because I wanted to shop local and it mostly only had expensive soap, candles, very expensive art, and a few knick-knack plates. I actively searched there for my Secret Santa and friend's birthday and there was very little to buy. Of course it's closing. The café in the back was incredibly slow. It often looked like it was closed because the lighting inside was so dim that when you walked by it was dark.

In comparison, I often shop at Lockwood. They have a variety of really cute things and change their merchandise.

I feel bad for the owner(s), but I don't think this shop closing is a negative sign for Jackson Heights. I just think the store legitimately didn't have much to sell.

Unfortunately I agree that the concept was very flawed, and not just for JH. It might have done well if it had chosen to be a drinks-and-pastry type cafe with ample seating. But who would want to go into a gift shop, order an espresso, and sit awkwardly on a sofa in the middle of the shop? This notion never appealed to me at all-- not comfortable, to me anyway. As for the gift shop selection--I agree. I tried to shop there for gifts and just could not find anything I wanted to buy. How many scented candles or incense sticks can you buy? Or little tins of hard candy? There just was not anything I liked. The merchandise seemed to be extremely quirky, and not appealing to a broad array of customers. It seemed like the owners created a shop that appealed to their tastes, but their tastes were very sort of odd. It was not well thought out and just did not work. Lockwood seems to be doing okay and Inner Peace has been here quite a long time. Both have a broader selection and include things for kids, which is smart for this neighborhood. So I agree--it's not the neighborhood or the landlords in this case, it just was a concept that did not work.
