i work in a museum and i was one of the cofounders of Cinemarosa. (now i am not affiliated to the program)  Yes the ideal situation for a movie house is to have a 35 mm projector. Serious directors and film buffs will only let you show their films or will only come to see them if you show in 35 (the original format) Queens Theatre in the Park has a 35 mm projector and is willing to donate it, as the cost of using its is really high (union issue and you really need someone that knows the craft). The good news is that more and more filmmakers are moving into digital film. Which is way cheaper.

If you ask me, and this has been one of my dreams, even before the Eagle closed, the whole building (theate and restaurant)  shoudl become a cultural  space  and also a  host for the many activities by all the community based organizations in JH that dont have a space. Can you picture the vibrancy of that place?  Since I posted my previous comment, i started to think that is not such a crazy idea to create a nonprofit and then ask for government, foundation and private support. I did created and run a nonprofit as a side job when Cinemarosa started, it is not difficult. However the hard work is to get $$$ to get the place and run it. and it is not only a matter of money, but also time! which sometimes is more difficult to find that money.

Based on all the comments i think it is worthwhile meet up in person and see what we can do as a group. the Highline Park was started by a group of concerned neighbors. What do you all think?

BTW, i close friend of mine told me that since it was closed the place was taken over by the rats! More reasons to act fast! we dont want an infestation in JH! right?
