Restaurants & Food / Re: New report calls Jackson Heights a "food swamp"
« on: April 13, 2018, 08:32:35 AM »
I think the issue that is not discussed is the balance between cooking at home and buying ready-made food.
For people of many cultures, including mine - I come from a small European country, you cook your healthy food at home. I won't pay a full meal price for a salad, I can easily and quickly made one myself. I cook vegetarian meals at home too. And for this JH is great, with abundant and reasonably priced groceries at every corner.  I'd rather have Mi Tierra than Fairway next door for fruits and veggies. I stopped at a grocery store in UES (and not the fancy part) last week hoping to get some fruit, but at $5.99 for a small box of blueberries, no thanks.
I eat outside twice a week. And I'm looking for something yummy and interesting. If it's a bit greasy, so be it.
I love the variety of the neighborhood.  I think it's also important that it exposes people to different ideas and experiences. You can pick and cook whatever you want.
I'm not saying that having other options would be bad of course, but IMHO, it's not an issue here. When I think of a food-problematic area, I imagine a suburb where you can only go to Walmart, DD, KFC, and a BBQ place.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Jackson Gyro
« on: February 08, 2018, 07:42:35 PM »
I'm Greek (not Greek American) so I feel I should contribute here, although I have only tried two sandwiches so far.

I can highly recommend the pork doner, which is actually what gyro is in Greece, not the lamb/beef minced thing. Very tasty and not greasy as is often the case. Very filling and great value too. The chicken doner is good too, although not at the same level IMHO.

As someone else already mentioned, Greek restaurants typically focus on meat or fish dishes, not both, although obviously they often do here to expand their clientele.

I will definitely be back often.
