Neighborhood Chat / Travers Park improvements
« on: April 29, 2008, 06:36:12 PM »
Hello all! Recently, Helen Sears and the Parks Department met with the Community Board's Parks & Recreation Committee to present some changes they want to make in Travers Park. Also present at the meeting were representatives of Friends of Travers Park and JH Green.

This Thursday the Parks & Recreation Committee will be meeting and while the issue of the proposed improvements probably didn't make it on the Agenda for this particular meeting it can be brought up once the Agenda items have been dealt with. These meetings are open to the public. The Thursday meeting is at 6 PM in the CB#3 District Office (82-11 37th Av Suite 606).

Following is a list of the improvements that the Parks Dept. wants to make to Travers Park:

- Replace the play equipment in the older kids play area with new equipment that will accommodate more children and present greater challenges for them
- Replace the water sprinklers to improve the current ones and add some toddler-friendly ones
- Add a sandpit to the smaller kids play area
- Remove the headless turtle
- Install an arch at the side entrances to the park saying "Welcome to Travers Playground"
- The chess tables near the ball fields will be removed because the new play equipment will have a larger footprint
- The benches for the tennis court will be along the fence on the 78th street side
- Extend the waterline to reach the athletic field plantings for watering purposes
- Change the watering access point to make them easier to use

This would most likely take place over Winter-Spring of 2009 if all goes ahead as planned.

During some discussion after the event the one item that most people didn't want was the sandbox because of several issues (taking up valuable running/walking space, cleanliness, potentially hazardous).

Another issue was a request for a safe surface for the sprinklers area other than concrete. The Parks Department said they researched this and couldn't find a good alternative, some research we did indicates otherwise although it might prove costly. I still want to look further into this when I get the time.

If you have any suggestions or comments you'd like to make about all of this please respond here or come to the meeting on Thursday, all thoughts are welcome.

Helen Sears made it quite clear that there's not going to be much money coming into the park in the future which makes it difficult to contest any of the proposed improvements but the community should certainly have a say in what gets done, and where. This is why I'm posting this, since as residents of J-High we use the park more than most people we have a better idea of what's really needed, plus I think it's good for everybody to be informed of what's being planned.

Dudley
