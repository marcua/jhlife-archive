A small group of us decided to go to Meson Asturias for dinner this past Saturday.  It's been many years since the last time I visited Meson, but everything seems to have remained the same with respect to the the food, the service and the Flamenco show.  I suppose that may be the winning formula that attracts its loyal clientele.

My group wasn't overall impressed with the food at Meson, and this brought back memories of back in the day when my family enjoyed going there primarily for the dance show.  This still holds true for me because while the food is respectable, I think the pricing remains a bit on the high side.  We ordered the Paella Valenciana, Arroz con Pollo y Chorizo, Camarones al Ajillo (Garlic Shrimp), Pargo (Red Snapper), Trucha (Trout), Calamare and Asparagos Blancos (White Asparagus).  Caldo Gallego (Galician Soup) is included as part of every meal.

The Flamenco show has always been a favorite of mine and apparently most of Meson's diners, as the dining room became quite crowed at about 9:00 p.m. just as the show was about to begin.  We arrived at 7:00 p.m., and fortunately were able to stretch out our meals until show time.  On the whole, I'd say that we had a good time, but any future visits would be for the show first and the food second.
