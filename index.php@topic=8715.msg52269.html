I'm sorry to be Debbie Downer, but I just wanted to relay a chat I had with a coworker - he was bitching about his gym two days ago and I asked where he went and he said Blink (the one in Nolita or Noho).  He said it was great when it first opened and then it just fell apart and it got dirtier every day.  You really get what you pay for ($20) -no towels, no classes, no free weights, teeny locker rooms (that might be because of the location) and they never clean the bathrooms.  I was suprised that they only have machines.  I'm not going to join a gym that doesn't have free weights or classes.

He said he's been trying to quit and it's been a nightmare of them being extremely difficult and pulling out a bunch of hidden fees.  Just be very careful if you join and get the cancellation policy spelled out for you very clearly. 

I went online after talking to him and I saw a bunch of similar complaints. 
Hopefully this one will be nicer but the cancellation policy will still be the same, so just be aware.

I work in White Plains, and belong to the Blink there. It's pretty terrific. Complaints about there being no classes, towels, etc. are pretty irrelevant since the gyms are designed to be very basic (and thus the low cost). Their gyms are huge, have tons of equipment (especially cardio), are super clean, and have adequate locker rooms. They are not owned by Equinox, but Equinox does have a share in Blink. I belonged to Equinox in the past and I find Blink to be maintained (and cleaned) as well as Equinox.

I was told that sales for JH will begin in early 2014 with a summer opening. The Astoria gym is opening in December.
