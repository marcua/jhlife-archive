Sadly having looked for commercial real estate for my business myself, I have found out that it is a fact that commercial landlords can sometimes make/save more money by leaving a high rent location vacant and taking a tax write off than renting the space at a lower rate.  Instead of taking in say 3500 per month, the landlord instead takes a 6500 "loss" on the space thus reducing the tax bill.  The realtor I was working with told me this was common practice, and that if the landlord owns many buildings, it is a way to increase profit.  I AM NOT AN EXPERT but it probably explains the many over-priced spaces I looked at sitting vacant, as well as the situation on 37th ave.  Sure wish I knew of a solution.
