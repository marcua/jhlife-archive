 Not everyone likes the heat timers - or thinks that they work- but we do and they have been a godsend. We used to have
the windows open in every room with the radiators on only in the bedroom and kitchen. It was fine in the morning when
we awake but sometimes in the late night the heat was blasting and nothing helped. If we turned off all the radiators, the
place was that bit too cold to stay healthy.

  Would highly recommend trying the heat timers. Also if you can get your building interested in the NYC's program called
Retrofit Accelerator, the free program they provide to buildings that need upgrading in this area and might not know how
to go about it- https://retrofitaccelerator.cityofnewyork.us/

They can help assess where the entire building can update its heating and other systems for energy savings and for comfort.
Also the city is now interested in overheating as a problem and you can call 311 with this issue. Apparently it is not very
healthy to live long term in an overheated place and you can get Doctors backup if you need which could force the building
to correct course. But before it comes to that, try the other stuff. Good luck!

Our apartment has 8 rooms and 2 baths.  We removed the heater from the kitchen, leave the heater in one of the guest bedrooms off, leave the heat in the dining room and the 2nd guest bedroom on, turn off the heat in the living room, leave the heat on in the family room and both bathrooms and leave open windows in the master bedroom and in the living room.  It's still hot, and every now and then we open the window in the family room to cool the place down, but it's easier to cool the apartment down than to heat it, so we don't complain.
