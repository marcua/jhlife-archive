Neighborhood Chat / D. Keith Mano and Jackson Heights
« on: October 28, 2016, 11:36:38 AM »
D. Keith Mano died recently. He was an author who I wasn't familiar with but the obituary piqued my interest so I read and recently completed one of his novels. The novel was called "Topless" and as in many of his novels it addresses Mano's rigorous Christianity but with a twist-the novel is about an Episcopal priest who inherits his brother's topless bar. 
The point of all this is that it takes place in Jackson Heights. The bar in this novel is called The Car and is on Northern Boulevard in 60's . It sounds like it could have been Jaxs, the diner, in some previous life or maybe it was simply invented as some of the things in the novel seem to be such as The Jackson Heights Free Press newspaper. Mano was from Queens -grew up in Whitestone and seemed to know Jackson Heights fairly well. The police in this novel are accurately portrayed as coming from the 115th Precinct. Anyone else familiar with his books or  perhaps even knew him?

Neighborhood Chat / 74th Street Station
« on: March 20, 2014, 02:39:34 PM »
I am a regular user of this station both the subway and the 7 line.This is the hub station for Jackson Heights yet it seems to be festering in neglect. The subway lines are always foul and dirty, a perfect environment for the ever larger rats that have made this station their special preserve. The walls are dirty, the stairs leading out of the station on 37th road are nearly always covered in litter. Recently the elevator to the 7 train has been stopping on the platform with the doors not opening for 30 seconds enough time for anyone with the slightest bit of anxiety to start wondering how long a response might take after pressing the alarm button.

Does the community have any access to the station manager on a regular basis? Only regular and frequent accountability to users of the service can there be any hope of turning around this disgraceful situation.
