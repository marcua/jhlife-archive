From what I understand, the assemblyman is usually the main decision maker on choosing District Leaders.

In this case with little advance notice,  he chose to replace the incumbent,  Jessica Ramos.

Since this is a Democratic process,  Jessica Ramos has chosen to run against the Queens County Democratic Party candidate who was chosen by the assemblymember, which is costly, but makes the voters choose.

While this was a Party decision, it does not seem to be a fair process,  thus an election will prove the best outcome.

New Visions will be having a nominating meeting on Thursday and Jessica Ramos will have my vote, in my opinion she has been an excellent District Leader.

New Visions is one of the most progressive Democratic Clubs, and open to all.

The by-laws even state that a member does not have to be a Registered Democrat, with the idea that within the new immigrant population there are many people who are Permanent Residents, thus ineligible to vote, so the by-laws were constructed to include this population who side with Democratic ideas, while ineligible to register to vote.

The meeting is and always has been open to the public.
