Re: Speak up for a safer Queens Blvd
« Reply #8 on: November 16, 2013, 04:33:46 PM »
I don't think encouraging additional pedestrian traffic along such a road would be a good idea, rather it would make a dangerous situation more dangerous, and clog a street that is decidedly automobile centric with bikes who are unprotected and vulnerable.  The local sections of that road are extremely chaotic as it is, but at least cars and buses have their own armor.

Noone seriously would suggest putting bike lanes along the Grand Central Parkway, or LIE, and if there were a biker he/she would certainly be pulled over and fined.  Queens Boulevard should be held in the same regard, at least where it turns into a highway-type of street once it passes east of Sunnyside.  Some streets are suitable for mixed use, and some simply aren't.

People crossing the boulevard to visit the myriad of stores and malls along it are a different matter, and one not easily solved.  Some countries build pedestrian bridges over giant streets like this as a matter of course, and it would probably be a good (and expensive) solution here, both for car-traffic as well as foot-traffic.

The automobile genie is out of the bottle in this city thanks to Robert Moses and it isn't going back down now, and certainly not so in deep Queens.
