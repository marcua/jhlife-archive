Rob -- good news on two fronts:

1. You don't have to worry about that scenario coming to pass this year!  According to FiveThirtyEight.com, a site that simulates the election 10,000 times daily based on pretty sophisticated models and the latest polling data, there's currently only a 0.15% chance of that happening (and a 96% chance overall of Obama winning). 

2. There is a movement afoot to abandon the Electoral College and adopt a National Popular Vote.  Interestingly, a constitutional amendment isn't necessary.  All that's needed is for states representing a majority of votes in the Electoral College to agree to award their electors on the basis of the national popular vote.  Model legislation has been developed, and so far four states (IL, MD, NJ, HI) have passed it into law.  Once states representing 270 electoral votes pass the legislation, it will automatically go into effect.
