Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Washer in Coop?
« on: August 26, 2014, 10:36:12 AM »
Don't assume that just because they put down a washer/dryer in the listing that the owners are in compliance with the coop board.  I bought my apt with a washer/dryer already in the unit - neither were hooked up but they were included in the sale.  The seller's agent attested that they were allowed in the apts.  Much to my surprise, I was then told by the board at interview that they are not allowed because the old pre-war pipes can't sustain the load.  Those machines got thrown out (thankfully they were pretty old machines and in hindsight, the lack of hookup should've been a clue that something was not right).  We were fooled.

Thanks for the additional info!  (Thank you both for responding, btw)  I did some additional digging online and confirmed that Con Edison Solutions is the ESCO arm of Con Edison.  Why the utility has an ESCO is a mystery.  In any event, during my digging, I found this useful website that lists current pricing as well as pricing history (by month, going back several years!) for Con Edison and the ESCOs in NYS.  http://www.newyorkpowertochoose.com/ptc_choose.cfm?CFID=1002707&CFTOKEN=27447549  Assuming that the rates listed are accurate, I compared my monthly 2013 rates per kWh to the rates listed for Con Edison and found Con Edison Solutions overall had lower rates in 2013.  However, my 2014 monthly rates were a lot higher.    Based on the chart, some of the current ESCO rates look pretty attractive, but I'm also wary of hidden fees and low intro rates that disappear after 2 months.  The pricing chart only tells you who's cheaper but not their reputations.

I'm thinking of ditching my ESCO and going back to Con Edison (not even sure how I got into the ESCO to begin with), but would like to hear from anyone who would recommend their ESCO as an alternative and also has competitive rates with Con Edison.  Thanks so much in advance for your input!
