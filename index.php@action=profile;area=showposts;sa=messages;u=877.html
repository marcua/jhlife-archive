Neighborhood Chat / Re: 37th and 35th avenues: One way?
« on: June 09, 2015, 03:36:55 PM »
Quote from: Beech Court on June 05, 2015, 05:58:46 PM

ABSOLUTELY NOT!

Sometimes you need to pick and choose your battles. Yes traffic is sometimes congested and etc etc. However, if you were to make 34th and 35th Avenues both one way you solve one problem and create new ones.

The divider mall was put back onto 34th Avenue in an effort to keep traffic down. It was being used as he alternate lanes to Northern Blvd. traffic especially during rush hour. And 35th Avenue gets the same spillover from 37th Avenue, 34th Avenue, not to mention bus routes.

The more you open the streets to traffic the more of it you will see. And THAT means more speeding, more noise, more fumes, more radios late at night and a whole host of other quality of life issues.

Leave well enough alone. Don't do it.
Dear Beech court
There is no battle to be chosen - I was simply asking a question based on my observations of pedestrian safety and traffic problems.  To be clear, I was asking about 35th & 37th avenues, not 34th. (I agree that divider on 34th is a good solution.)  That being said, if I were to make a proposal, it would not be to open up more lanes for more traffic, it would be to put controls, and hopefully limit it.  For example, I would suggest moving the bike lanes from 34th to 35th, with only one lane of car traffic.  Similar to Kent Avenue in Brooklyn, the bike lanes would be adjacent to the south curb, with a parking lane as a buffer.  There is more noise (honking etc) and more fumes when traffic does not flow properly (see Roosevelt Avenue.)  I don't claim to  necessarily have the answer, but I do think there is better way.  Based on Todd G's informative response, it seem efforts have rightfully been focused on areas in around the low 70s.  It seems there is a growing problem elsewhere in the neighborhood, and it should at least be worthy of community dialogue.
