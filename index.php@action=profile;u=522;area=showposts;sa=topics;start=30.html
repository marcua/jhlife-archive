Neighborhood Chat / Join JHBG before the end of the year
« on: December 29, 2016, 10:45:38 AM »
How do you build a community?  It takes effort. You have to give back to your community so that you can truly be part of the community, but how?  It may be as easy as helping at your building barbecue or helping an elderly neighbor. 

One way to build a community is to join the Jackson Heights Beautification Group (JHBG). JHBG has been helping build a better Jackson Heights for 28 years.  Membership is $25 a year, but we happily accept larger donations.  Membership is tax deductible and at $25, a bargain, costing only seven cents a day. Donate before the end of 2016 and get your tax deduction in April.   

Membership empowers you to build a better community.  You are joining a group of people motivated and dedicated to improving Jackson Heights.  JHBG's role is to help motivate and assist people who have a passion that will help  our community. We have passionate people who:
- garden green spaces
- run a dog run
- direct the Jackson Heights Orchestra
- provide historical tours
- compost
- care for street trees
- remove graffiti
- advocate for local schools
- react to local issues
- hold an annual art competition
- produce the Halloween parade, and more.

The question is what is your passion and how can JHBG help you make a better Jackson Heights in the new year? Start by joining JHBG, clicking here or going to http://www.jhbg.org/donate and becoming a member.  We look forward to working with you.

E-mail us at Info@jhbg.org with your ideas on how to make Jackson Heights a better place to live next year.
Jackson Heights Beautification Group
PO Box 720253
Jackson Heights, NY 11372

718-565-5344. General Inquiries: info@jhbg.org

Neighborhood Chat / JHBG Halloween Parade
« on: October 29, 2016, 01:20:46 AM »
The annual Jackson Heights Beautification Parade will be held as usual on Halloween. This year Halloween lands on a Monday, so the parade will step off at 5 PM. There will be limited parking on 37th Avenue all day Monday.

The parade starts on 89th Street and runs to Food Town on 76th Street where 3,200 goodie bags are handed out.

Most local schools march as a group. We organize schools by balloon colors. Kids should meet up with their schools no later than 4:45 PM at 89th Street just north of 37th Avenue.  We also have smaller groups who march together such as  girl scouts/boy scouts/music schools/dog groups/ect. If you want to register a group, reach out to JHBG or me at NualaODohertyJHBG.org.  Of course any and everyone can march.

If you children attend a local JH school, make sure you know your school color so it is easier to find your group. (See the attached map with school colors.)

It is an open parade, so anyone and join or leave at any point. (There are no barriers along the block, only at the corners for traffic issues.) This is important for parents rushing home from work so they can meet up with kids. Beware as traffic & parking in JH from 4 PM to 8 PM around the parade is a nightmare.

For smaller children feel free to join in later as it can be a long walk or bring a stroller.

The end of the parade is a little hectic (from PS 69 to Food Town) where JHBG volunteers hand out the goodie bags to kids.  ‎ If you have multiple kids or older kids, have a plan on where to meet up after the parade.

This year for the first time we will have a red carpet near Dime Savings bank at 76th Street.  Families can have a "Photo Shoot" on‎ the red carpet thanks to Katha Cato of the Worlds Queens Film Festival.

This is the day for Jackson Height's to show how creative and loving community we are.  Please join the fun or come out and cheer us on.

 

Nuala
