Would a tea house or wine bar work in JH?
« on: June 27, 2008, 10:11:18 AM »
http://www.chowhound.com/topics/532717

An innocent remark of mine turned into a heated debate on chowhound. What do you guys think?

My two cents: I moved here for the food and the diversity. I speak Spanish and a few other languages, and I have absolutely no qualms sampling food from street carts or steam tables or fried chicken joints. To me, JH and its surrounding neighborhoods are a glorious amalgamation of cultures and peoples. It's not merely Indian/Bangladeshi and Colombian/Ecuadorian/Mexican/Peruvian - I see little pockets of Eastern Europeans, Filipino, Nepali, Korean and Thai as well as others who don't fit into the above categories. It frustrates me when others people - the media, other foodies - try to impose a monolithic image of JH as a Latino or Indian community only. It's not just that. And to summarily dismiss the notion that tea shops or wine bars would work here or are wanted by the community because...what? they can't afford it? they can't appreciate it? it's pretentious and gentrified? only white people or yuppies drink tea or wine? is elitist. I feel that these people are trying to mold JH into something that exists in their minds.

Starbucks and E77 are doing well. They are drawing a very diverse crowd, and as I've mentioned in CH, are clearly fulfilling a previously unfulfilled need.  There are people willing to pay inflated prices for coffee. Sure I can take a train a couple stops or go to Manhattan for tea or wine or fancy coffee, but why not keep the money and jobs in the neighborhood? I don't drink coffee and sometimes I just want to grab a quick cup of tea and pastries before heading onto the train. And I don't think one or - gasp - two tea places or wine bars would dilute the "character" of JH. A sign of further things to come? Would JH fall to the yuppies once they've inserted a wine bar?

Wow, that was quite a rant for the morning. I am off to make some tea to calm myself down!  :angel:

Forgive this naive non-drinker... I can't really picture what you mean by a wine bar.

I've seen tapas bars with nice wine selections.  But we have one of these already, at Jardi.

I've been to a Viennese Heurigen, which is like a wine version of a beer garden (it was a hoot, and they had great grape juice!), but I doubt that's what you're talking about.

Is a wine bar something like a tasting room at a wine shop or a winery?   Or something more cafe-like? 

In any case, I don't wish to side with the people trying to pigeonhole Jackson Heights, but I'm not sure I can envision a place focused exclusively on wine doing very well here.   It wouldn't appeal to the nightclubbing/sports bar crowds on Roosevelt and Northern, and I don't see too much of an evening/late night scene otherwise here in Jackson Heights.  I would think a wine bar would need to be part of a good full-service restaurant (like a bistro) to make it. 

On the other hand, we have a vibrant and under-served daytime scene -- a tea place would be a great fit.
