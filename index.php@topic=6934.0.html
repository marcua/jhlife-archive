The New Building at 84th Street and 37th Avenue

On July 19, 2011 the Landmarks Preservation Commission (LPC) held its final hearing

the LPC since December of last year has resulted in a much improved building. Key
to these improvements were the comments and suggestions of the JHBG, Councilmember
Daniel Dromm, the Historic Districts Council and other concerned residents.

Personally, I am disappointed in a number of things about this building.  First, I think the design looks like they went to WalMart and bought it.  It is so unoriginal and cheap looking.  It's a cookie cutter of the building across from it.  Adds nothing to the neighborhood.  Most importantly, I am disappointed that it was approved and feel the Landmarks people let us down by allowing this building.  Couldn't they have built something smaller, fewer storeys?  Because of its size it will mean more residents thus more congestion on the streets in the hood.  It is a rental unit which probably also means they will have more residents per apartment unit and more drain on services.  These will not be first choice apartments.  This is strictly about getting as much money per square footage.  I'm also disappointed because this sets a very bad precedent for 37th Avenue.  I can forsee all the sites along 37th Ave. being rezoned for tall buildings.  I'm not against tall buildings per se.  They can be built along Northern Blvd where there is a wide street and their impact would be minimal.  This building is just an example of cowboy zoning.  The irony is that all these compromises that the developer agreed to were probably already what they expected anyway.  Developers always expect to have to cut back on their plans so they ask for the most and get what they expected all along and then brag that they "compromised."
