Neighborhood Chat / IF YOU HAVE A CHASE CREDIT CARD!
« on: June 14, 2009, 09:44:16 PM »
I was going through a pile of mail on my table today and came across an "important change in terms notice enclosed--please read" notice from Chase credit card (you know, those notices a lot of people simply throw out).  The notice states that they will be "making some changes to your credit card account in response to market conditions and to maintain profitability on your account"; i.e.--INCREASING the interest rate.  It then states that you can accept the increase by doing nothing, or you can choose to not accept the rate increase, in which case you have to close your account (you will still owe your balance, but wouldn't have to pay it back immediately--you'd continue to get a monthly statement but not be able to charge anything else to the credit card).  I just called them and cancelled my account.

So, if you have a chase credit card, you may want to look into this.  I already hate Chase because I previously had a Wamu card, and Chase purchased Wamu.  I always had paid my Wamu card after I received the "reminder" notice from them via email, something I had set up on the account.  I had a balance on my Wamu card that I had transferred over with a 1.99% rate until 2010.  When Chase took over, I didn't receive my reminder notice, and I missed a payment.  I realized this a few days later and I called them, explaining the situation and asking if they could waive the late fee this one time, and that I would immediately make a payment.  They refused, and then I notice that because of my late payment, they had jacked my rate up to 22.24%!  They also refused to change that.  Now, I completely acknowledge that it was ultimately my fault that I missed the payment, but what happened to customer service?  I hate them, and once I finish paying them off, will never do business with them again.

Also, if you know of any other forums to post this to warn people... can't let them get away with this!
