Welcome, cookie79!

It's hard to answer your question... all things are relative.  It really depends on where you moving from, and your expectations and tolerance of inconvenience.  I park my car on the street.  If I can't find a spot late at night, I park it at a meter and move it to a residential space around 7am, when spaces are more plentiful.  This is a bit of a hassle, but for me, it beats the roughly $175/month it would cost for a garage space.

Parking is difficult but not impossible.  Around 4-5pm there's a significant amount of turnover so it's not as hard to get a spot.  If a street has had street cleaning earlier in the day, say, from 8:30-10am, then it will be quite easy to find a spot around 5pm because there are people who go to park on the newly cleaned street at 10am and then vacate their spot at 5pm when they go home to where they really live (this is just my theory to explain what I've noticed).

If you come home late at night, you will probably have more trouble parking than during the day, but if you don't mind a walk, the parking is always more plentiful across Northern Blvd in what is technically East Elmhurst.  I have only resorted to crossing Northern Blvd to park a few times in a few years.

I drive everyday to and from work.  Normally I get home around 6 PM.  You basically just have to get lucky.  There are times when I pull down my street and get a spot immediately, and there have been times where it seems like EVERYONE is out looking for a spot and it's taken 45 minutes.  It can be really frustrating at times becuase all the streets are one way and the lights aren't timed out so great.  There are also a lot of 1/2 spots because some people can't park or want to leave extra room to get out.  I ended up buying a smaller car (I'd suggest getting a smart car- you'll always have a spot  )  Past that, the benfits of this neighborhood far outweigh the parking situation.  Maybe someone should think about sponsoring a program to put parking spot lines in the street?

You know, one of the great things about living in JH is that you really don't need a car.  Food, dry cleaners, bodegas, restaurants, etc. are all an easy walk.  An excellent transportation hub is available to us, including two express trains to Manhattan at 74th St., making mid-town three stops away, and a variety of local lines taking you everywhere from Flushing Meadow Park to Forest Hills to Astoria to the Queens Mall.  Bus service (including express buses) can take you down Northern Blv or to Rockaway Beach or to various Manhattan spots.  LIRR has a station at 61st in Woodside, and can take you out to the Island easily.

I have had a variety of cars over the years.  One got stolen.  One got towed and disposed of while I was out of town arranging my father's funeral.  One got canabalized on the streets... everything from the sideview mirrors to the bumpers to internal parts disappeared before I just dumped the piece of junk.  I always get excited about having a car... and I always feel strangely relieved when it is gone.... somehow freer. 

Now that I've got a baby, I take car services more than before, but it is still cheaper than parking and gas and either car payments or repair payments. 

Go green.  Dump the car!!!!
