Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

they just don't get what's wrong about it.  They (meaning the students I've spoken to- not teens as a whole) don't understand the concept of respecting the property of others. decrease in graffiti. 

We're talking about adolescents and teenagers here and expecting a level of emotional maturity that simply does not go with their age.  They are self-centered and emotionally immature, as befits their stage of development.  I'm an "oldster" now but I can tell you that, the only reason I did not do more damage and get in more trouble as a teenager is because our parents quite wisely kept us penniless and home at night, for the most part.

Do not construe this post as my condoning graffiti - I'm as upset about it as my neighbors.  I'm just saying that only further development and having personal property of their own to defend will teach youngsters the level of respect we seek.
