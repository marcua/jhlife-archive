Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Impact of Subletting and Dog Policy on Apartment Values
« on: June 21, 2010, 09:40:40 AM »
I realize that this tread is about co-op apartments in the neighborhood but I wanted to chime in anyway because the "pet-no-pet" policy is an issue that has impacted my life for the last 4 years.  We've lived in our present rent stabilized building for 13 years.  We have two 12 year old dogs.  A few years ago, the building was sold to a new owner who prompltly decided that a no-pet rule was in effect.  At the time about 20% of the tenants had companion animals and all received notices that their tenancy was being terminated.  Several of the tenants gave up and left without fighting.  I stayed and have been tangling with the ll in court for almost 4 years.  I won the initial round - I kept my dogs because the law says that if they've lived on the premise openly for more than 3 months without objection by the owner that they can stay.  Since that first (of three, so far) victory the ll has refused to give me a new lease - in violation of a court order.  My point is that even in rental buildings the anti-pet bias is alive and well.  My pets are not a nuisance, do not defacate and urinate in the apartment or halls and are in no way threatening to others.  I cannot say the same for the new non-rent stabilized tenants and their children.  Thanks for letting me vent.
