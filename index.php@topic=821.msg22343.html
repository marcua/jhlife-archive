A bit more on the Travers park improvements and opposition by local park advocates:

New York Daily News
Jackson Heights activists rip $1.7M plan to fix up Travers park
BY Clare Trapasso
DAILY NEWS WRITER
Thursday, September 24th 2009, 4:00 AM

There isn't a lot of park space in Jackson Heights - and it's about to get even worse.

Local advocates are up in arms over a $1.7 million playground renovation at Travers Park spearheaded by City Councilwoman Helen Sears.

The rehab - which advocates say is unnecessary - is set to shutter half the park for up to a year. Work could begin any day now.

"The renovation wasn't needed," said Len Maniace, chairman of the Friends of Travers Park. "And it's going to take the only park that serves Jackson Heights south of Northern Blvd. out of service."

Maniace would rather see the money, from Sears' discretionary budget, spent on getting more park space or on a new tennis court.

But Sears said the space was sorely in need of repairs.

The swings and slides and places to climb and crawl are made out of wood and metal, and were installed in 2004.  Apparently, after spending $500,000 in 2004, they had a great ribbon cutting in 2005.  http://www.nycgovparks.org/sub_newsroom/media_advisories/media_advisories.php?id=19618  I do not understand why, after a few short summers, everything is being bulldozed.  The "big kid" play area looks like a war zone.  Today, even though it was play street day, the toddler area was mobbed, with lots of big kids trying to get on the toddler slides and taking up space that toddlers should have been enjoying.

$1.7 million dollars, to undo everything they did with the $500,000 sthey spent on the park 5 years ago?  Not to build on the original work, but to bulldoze the original work?  I'm outraged.
