i didn't mean that to be insulting and i didn't mean to disparage the dish (i find it pretty tasty sometimes).

but it was actually invented in the U.K., and has been embraced there and here. the more Americanized Indian restaurants -- like the long-departed Mehfil and the recently departed Indian Taj -- served it. Angel, like its sister restaurant Adda, is more dedicated to making dishes that are full-on Indian.

And i do hope you can find a nearby place to satisfy that craving -- i know the feeling from craving other dishes.

here's a little story that lays out the origin story pretty well...
https://yorkshirebusinesswoman.co.uk/did-you-know-that-chicken-tikka-masala-doesnt-actually-exist-well-not-in-india-anyway/

edit to add: i've been to this place in Astoria and thought it was good (i only ordered veg dishes) and they seem to have it on their menu.
https://www.yelp.com/biz/tikka-indian-grill-astoria
