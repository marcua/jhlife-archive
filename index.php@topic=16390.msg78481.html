Re: Best place to find good fruits and vegetables?
« Reply #9 on: May 07, 2017, 10:21:23 AM »
Agree with what's been said previously, and I just want to add Patel Brother's to the mix- I often get produce basics there: greens, onions, garlic, citrus, herbs.  High turnover, so produce isn't sitting and going bad. Prices are excellent and I think quality exceeds many other local options (the greens and herbs are especially great!)

re: the health food store on 84th and 37th ave- I go there for certain pantry goods that can't find elsewhere (almond butter etc) but I've been unimpressed with their produce. Prices are exorbitant and while I'm willing to pay more for organic or small farm, I think because maybe it's not a high traffic store items sit and mold, etc. And the selection is quite small. In terms of dry and pantry goods, they do offer a lot of products that you'd be hard pressed to find elsewhere in the neighborhood though.

Farm Spot, the Jackson Heights CSA (community supported agriculture) offers very fresh, local, organic produce for half the year. The season starts June 1st and runs into November. Deadline to sign up is May 18th. (Of course, it's not the same as buying produce at a store, but since it's timely I thought I'd mention it!)
farmspot.org
