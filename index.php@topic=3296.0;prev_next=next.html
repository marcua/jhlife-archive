FESTIVAL 2009 â€” Poetry & Memory

WHEN: June 18-20, 2009

             June 18 6:30PM - 9:30 PM: Workshop
             June 19 8:30PM - 11:30 PM: Open Readings
             June 20 All Day: Outdoor Festival

WHERE: Garden School, Jackson Heights, New York (click here for directions)

PROGRAM DETAILS:

Thursday, June 18, 6:30 PM - 9:30 PM: Workshops

The workshops will be held at the Garden School. Reflecting the theme, Poetry & Memory, the Panel discussion will focus on the following ideas:

    Memory is personal and collective. It plays a role in how we, personally and collectively, construct and understand our experiences and is often at the root of how we feel about our collective and individual selves. Because it makes the past tangible, poetry is one of the ways we make memory. It is why Wordsworth called poetry "the spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings: it takes its origin from emotion recollected in tranquility," and why the poet David Barber has said that "the future of memory is more and more bound up with the future of poetry." These workshops will explore the role of memory in the making of poems and of poems in the making of memory.

The workshops are free, appropriate for all ages and levels of experience. Over the next week you will be able to see themes of each workshop and register online for them. The panel discussion is open to the public and does not require pre-registration.

Friday, June 19, 8:30 PM - 11:30 PM: Open Reading

The JHPF open reading will be held at a location to be determined in Jackson Heights from 8:30pm to 11:30pm. Sign-up for the open mic will open at 8pm. The reading will feature the Queens College Poetry MFA program and any members from the community who want to share their work.

Saturday, June 20, All Day: Outdoor Festival

The full-day poetry festival will be held outdoors at the Garden School.
Highlights include:

    * Readings by an amazing line-up of featured poets
    * Readings by winners of the student poetry contest (which is still going on now)
    * Several bands, local artists, food and drinks!

Keep checking back for more details!

Announcement from the festival...
The Second Annual Jackson Heights Poetry Festival is here. Tonight brings the Workshops and Panel Discussion. Workshops will run from  6:30-8pm, followed by refreshments and a panel discussion from 8:30-9:30. Even if you have not had a chance to sign up - please feel free to come and join us! The event is held in the Garden School - 33-16 79th street. The entrance is on 79th street, between 34th avenue and Northern Boulevard, and more detailed directions can also be found on www.jhpfest.org.
Check further details out here.
 
Also coming up -

    * Friday, June 19th - The Open Reading featuring the Queens College MFA students will be held tomorrow at Terraza Cafe in Elmhurst, off the 82nd street stop on the 7 train (the usual place for First Tuesdays). Live music to follow.
    * And Saturday, June 20th!!  - All day Festival at the Garden School, in Jackson Heights.  We will start the festivities at 10:00 AM, and continue throughout the day with readings, live music, an Independent Book Fair, Arts & Crafts for children and plenty of food and drinks. Also we will be presenting awards to the winners of the city-wide student poetry contest. Rain or shine we hope to see you in Queens for an awesome day!.

We look forward to seeing you at any or all portion of the Festival!
