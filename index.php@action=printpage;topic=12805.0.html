Title: "That's a park?!"
Post by: Cb88 on January 09, 2015, 01:41:33 PM
Anyone else notice the bit of guerrilla artwork at the Manuel de Dios Unanue triangle near the 82nd st. station? (Photo attached). I love little urban interventions like that -- it critiques the park while at the same time improving it (the plywood it's painted on also acts as a sort of bench, protecting one's rear end from the metal spikes that I guess are meant to discourage loitering).

The triangle was listed in NYCGo's JH tourism section as a "green space" to visit (http://www.nycgo.com/slideshows/must-see-jackson-heights-queens-nyc/10/). I have to say, if I were from another neighborhood and decided to visit this "park" based on their recommendation, I'd probably be a little disappointed (unless that Andean musical trio happened to be playing -- then it'd be worth the trip, but only then). Anyone know who's responsible? JH's very own Banksy? My hats off!
