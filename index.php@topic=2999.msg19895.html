Hello,

I know nothing about The Birchwood House, but I understand that the insurance liability for a roof terrace and/or deck is exorbitant.  The board may be taking that expense into consideration in addition to others I have mentioned below.  I have come to find out that there are only certain amount of people that can be on the roof terrace and/or deck at any given time, in addition to the fact that it as well does become an annoyance to the shareholders below the terrace and/or deck.  In addition, having a terrace/deck takes managing and that is an additional expense and requires much cooperation from all the shareholders.

I was promised a garden 22 years ago in my co-op and finally this spring the shareholders have gotten together and made it happen.

It is quite unfortunate, that people chose to purchase at The Birchwood believing that the roof terrace would be opening, but there are so many other priorities in running a corporation that perhaps that is something to consider, especially during these difficult times.  It is very frustrating not to have those things that we want, and I would know as I had a doorman and now I have to open the door myself.  WHO KNEW?

Some of the shareholders in my building purchased believing that the gardens would be landscaped shortly thereafter, and they were truly upset about the corporation not investing $1.2 million to make it happen or whatever the number was.  But the board decided to allot $5K to the garden committee and the shareholders have made it happen.

We now have a beautiful garden, with herbs and the children have their own area where they can go and plant their flowers and vegetables, and yes the children can play in the gardens.  Although, speaking for myself, respect those buildings that have other rules as it relates to children and their gardens.

In short, the priorities of our corporation has changed with the economic climate that has changed.
In the case of our building, gardens were and are not a top priority as far as spending more than $5K.  The roof terrace isn't a top priority either, but there are discussions about that happening in the near future as well.  Our board is clear about priorities and the budget, and is doing a tremendous job being frugal and extremely cautious with unnecessary expenditures.

In the case of The Birchwood, the roof terrace and/or deck may not be a priority, but perhaps it will happen when the economy turns around, the insurance liability issue is settled, and the safety of and the participation in this project of all shareholders is ironed out.

Best always,
Marlene
