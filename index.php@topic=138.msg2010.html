I'm not exactly sure what kind of business I would like to see - many things come to mind including a bookstore, wine and cheese shop, a high-end deli, or a bistro.  What I am sure of is that I would like to see the new business well-planned and well-thought out.  Sure, it sounds great to have a salad bar.  But if the turnover isn't good and the things don't look fresh, or if all the offerings have too much mayonnaise on them (like so many salad bars in average supermarkets) then I know I won't be shopping there.  If a bookstore goes in that doesn't have a good buyer so the selection is poor or questionable, or the space isn't pleasant, I know I won't go.  If a wine shop goes in that has the same selection and prices as the two that are already in the neighborhood. . . I won't go.  Same goes for a fish/seafood store where the fish doesn't look any fresher than what's already in Met Foods and Trade Fair.

Running a business is a tough proposition.  Not only does the price point have to be correct and the products or services something the neighborhood needs, but the space has to be inviting and pleasant as well.

I recently read about a new bistro going into Brooklyn's Ditmas Park near Newkirk, a neighborhood where the real estate and rent prices are approximately the same as Jackson Heights, though available amenities including existing restaurants are much much fewer.  Here's the link http://www.nytimes.com/2008/02/03/nyregion/03journal.html?ref=nyregion
