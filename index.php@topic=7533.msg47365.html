Our Park. Our Future.

When our community comes together - anything is possible.

We were able to create the 78th Street Play Street (and the now permanent plaza there) and we were able to help orchestrate the Parks Dept purchase of the Garden School lot because our community got educated, got organized, and got involved.

Our community needs to come together again. Flushing Meadows-Corona Park is under siege with three corporate development proposals that, if approved, will harm the park and potentially destroy its usefulness to the community as a public green space. The proposals to build a shopping mall, a soccer stadium, and an expanded USTA site will reduce this already vulnerable park to being merely a corporate sports complex that is increasingly dominated by automobiles and trucks.

But we can make a difference. Just like we did with the PlayStreet and the Garden School field. That is where you come in.

We need you to come out on Monday, April 22nd at 7:30pm to the Queens Museum of Art for a community workshop on Flushing Meadows-Corona Park. This is your opportunity to share your vision for this park. This is your chance to make sure that elected officials understand that we want to improve the park, not destroy it.

This workshop is interactive by design.  It will facilitate individual feedback through a smaller group table setting. This will allow everyone to share their ideas and explore potential improvements. Please make every effort to attend this important community event.

I was able to attend this workshop on Monday and I thought it was a very organized and positive overall. There was a great turnout and, like myself, I think the majority in attendance were against all of the proposed developments.

Here is an article from the Queens Chronicle about the event:

http://www.qchron.com/editions/queenswide/searching-for-a-new-vision-of-fmcp/article_c3aa3607-e913-5869-a92d-a62eb0b6405c.html

As mentioned in the article above, City Councilwoman Julissa Ferreras is proposing a Flushing Meadows-Corona Park Alliance similar to the Central Park Conservancy and the Prospect Park Alliance:

http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/queens/public-private-alliance-proposed-flushing-meadows-article-1.1326515

During the meeting I was under the impression that this would be something new for the park and I thought it was odd that there was not currently such a group. After doing a little research I found that there is a FMCP Conservancy already in existence, which is also mentioned in the Queens Chronicle article above. I'm a little confused as to why a new group is being formed instead of working with the conservancy. It seems that I am not alone:

http://www.qchron.com/opinion/editorial/pat-dolan-and-the-park-she-loved/article_277242c9-b0b8-5dd0-8bc3-17789e9535f1.html

One of the most interesting components of this event was when those of us in attendance broke into smaller groups and discussed what we love about FMCP, what we would like to see changed or improved, and what we do not want to see in FMCP. It was great to hear everyone's ideas and to share my own. After the meeting I came across the FMCP Strategic Framework Plan which I believe was released in 2008. Many of the ideas discussed at my table as well as in the rest of the workshop are present in this plan. It can be found on NYC Parks' website:

http://www.nycgovparks.org/parks/fmcp

One person at my table mentioned restoring the Flushing River which currently runs underground through much of the park. I had no idea. This is also mentioned in the plan above and this would also result in the creation of a new festival grounds space which would be twice the size of Central Park's Great Lawn. This would be in the same location as the proposed soccer stadium and I think a much better idea for this space.
