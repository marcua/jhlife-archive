It does not take 45 minutes to get to midtown. I commute from JH way down to the financial district every day and it takes between 45 minutes to an hour door to door.  When agents say it takes X minutes to get to midtown, they are referring to the first stop in Manhattan, not Penn Station.  So yes, sometimes it takes more than a half hour to get to lower midtown.

But it does not take 45 minutes to get to midtown unless there's a delay. Even when I take the slow boats (R or M). I never take the 7 so I have no idea if it takes longer on that line.

The subway ride on the express F train from Roosevelt Ave to 42nd Street is about 16 minutes if there are no delays.  Add a 10 minute walk to the subway........5 minute wait time.......and another 5 minutes to walk to where you are going and you've got a 36 minute commute.  It's easy for that to extend to 40 or 45 minutes if there is just a little bit of train traffic.  The E train commute is a wee bit longer since it runs down 8th Avenue.  The local M or R train....add 5 to 10 minutes.  The 7 has a zillion stops so I avoid that for the most part.  I would agree that a 30-40 commute is accurate (door to door) if there are no delays.
