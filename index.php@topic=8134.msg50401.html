The Merlin was around in January and was identified by a guy i know who was doing the kestrel count for the city (he also does central park birdwalks)
The kestrels like the top of roosevelt terrace and later in the day the school on 87th st and 37th ave. They have been around for a few years but they don't nest in either building ....the red tailed hawks i see in spring and fall... The only nest i know of is the one in the Unisphere at Flushing Meadows but that is too far away ....there is also a Cooper's Hawk that likes to sit on buildings between 37th & roosevelt near the pigeons by the 7 train. I have never seen a peregrine in the neighborhood.....mike

This type of misunderstanding of nature and ecosystems is part of why the world has been allowed to get so out of balance.

Every creature kills something for sustenance.  Even if you're a vegan, you are eating plants that were once alive.  Hawks and other predators are doing what they were created to do -  keep  the species of their particular prey strong.    There are precious few creatures other that humans that will kill wantonly and even cruelly, without any need to consume what they kill.

Whether you find them hideous or not a world without predators would be a much poorer place indeed!  I sincerely hope you are able, some day, to see the beauty of nature in all its fullness.
