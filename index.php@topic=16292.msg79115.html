Designated quiet hours in co-ops gardens?
« on: April 16, 2017, 12:09:46 PM »
Hi, I'm curious if any other of the garden co-ops have designated quiet hours (aside from the usual nighttime hours.)  We have had a lot of families with young children move in during the last few years and, unfortunately, some of them are very inconsiderate about using this shared space, letting their kids run around shrieking at the top of their lungs for hours on end. (And, frankly, some parents are obnoxious when politely asked to please either get their kids to quiet down or take them to the playground or playstreet.) One suggestion I would like to bring to the board is the idea of having designated garden quiet hours. For example, on a holiday, quiet hours from 3-7, or on evenings from 6-8, when residents may be having dinner. This allows family time in the garden but also quiet time when other residents can open their windows and enjoy some peace and quiet.

I'm wondering what others either think of this idea or if other coops have done anything like this. While there is an "excessive noise" clause in the house rules, it is not observed or enforced. (I have tried.)  Any thoughts?

If shareholders are letting their kids shriek at the top of their lungs, they are violating house rules against unreasonable noise. I think asking for the creation of quiet hours is a poor move as a first response since it's likely there are already rules in place that shareholders can't use the garden after a certain time of night and there is already rules against unreasonable noise.

I think a better first response might be to talk to your neighbors and see if others are annoyed with the parents who aren't parenting their kids when in the garden, then presenting some kind of petition to the board to educate shareholders about what excessive noise is in the garden (little kids giggling while playing? No. Shrieks? Yes), and then enforcement of the rules.

At the end of the day we live in a congested city and we've chosen to live in close proximity to one another, so we all have to put up with some noise from our neighbors, and we also have to make an effort to not disturb our neighbors with excessive noise.

I think Dunnolly Gardens has play at least one playground in their garden, doesn't it? Anyone know how they manage noise from the kiddos there? Kids are going to make a lot of noise when in a playground, so do you just have to deal with it?
