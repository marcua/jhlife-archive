News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Community Trade and Classifieds / Train-a-thon!
« on: April 20, 2011, 08:43:31 AM »
     For those of you who don't know, my son loves subways.  (Yes, the
trains, not the fast food!)  Since he was young, he'd pour over the maps of the
trains and buses ... it was a big thing when the Q19B became the Q49, and the V
was discontinued.  It's beena goal of ours to ride as many trains as we can in
one day, and I promised him that we'd do it soon.
      Recently, I joined QCPAC, a type of support group of parents of children
affected with autism.  (Yes, for those of you who don't know, my son is autistic
... specifically, he has Asperger's syndrome.  Without getting into the daily
grind or school battles he faced, let's just say that it's been a rough road,
but I am proud of the many accomplishments he has made and continues to make
daily.)
     Anyway, I decided that perhaps we can combine his upcoming event with a
chance to do good for the QCPAC group, a charitable organization always in
need.     
     So, he and I are proud to announce our event is now a Train-a-thon!
     On Monday, April 25th, he and I will take to the rails!
     Similar to many walk-a-thons I did as a student, where people used to
pledge me per kilometer walked for the March of Dimes or similar groups, I have
talked to Lucas about dedicating our adventure to our new group.
     Therefore, we are asking for sponsors to pledge tax deductable donations to
QCPAC ... as little as $1 per train line he rides can go a long way to helping
our friends at QCPAC continue to be the great resource they are.  And it will
give our madcap dash through the subway system of New York City an even greater
purpose.
      So, if you are interested in sponsoring him, please reply to this by inboxing me.  Thanks.
