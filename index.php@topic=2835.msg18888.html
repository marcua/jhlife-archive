Check out the LaGuardia Landing Lights Park.  This is from the nycgovparks.org website

"This unconventional park was created in 1958 when the Port Authority surrendered nine parcels of land to the City for park purposes. They are situated in lots that follow the flight path to the airport, stretching from 78th Street and 25th Avenue to the Grand Central Parkway. Their placement is mandated by Federal Aviation Administration regulations, which require a swath of clear land in approaches leading up to airport runways. The parkâ€™s landing lights are maintained by the Port Authority, and the land itself can only be used for park purposes, according to an agreement between the Port Authority and the City."

If you're interested, google search "laguardia landing lights" and you can get the full description.  It is nice to lay on the ground on a summer night and watch the underbellies as they land.

When we were kids, the field on 82nd Street just above Astoria was a dirt bike track, and we used to ride until planes came by, and they filled the sky over us.  It was very close, you felt like you were only a few stories away.

We used to go to the Marine Air Terminal observation deck all the time when we were young to check out the planes. There was even a record recording booth there so you could make your own 45 vinyl.

Also, Elmjack Little League had a playing field right next to it and that was another great place.

My family's home was on 80th Street & 24th Ave and was taken by the city via eminent domain for tail end of Landing Lights Park.

We also used to fly kites at Our Lady of Fatima field - the northern part, and often the Port Authority police would come by and tell us that our kites were showing up on the radar so we had to lower them. We always lifted them higher after the police left.
