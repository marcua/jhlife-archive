YMCAs are religious organizations with expensive dues, hardly community centers.  You could get involved with the St Joan of Arc Youth Council, Men's Club, Ladies Club, etc. or any of the organizations sponsored by Community Church, like Scouting, if you're looking to get involved with religious organizations in JH.  You don't mention the Y in Forest Hills, so I assume you were just listing a few.

The Parks Department has Lost Battalion Hall in Rego Park and the pool in Flushing Meadow Park, which have lower membership fees, particularly for seniors.

A real community center would be expensive, probably in line with what a small school costs these days.  Maybe if it was combined with a senior center and public health facilities, federal funding could be incorporated.  I can't imagine where it could be built, though.  When I saw the subject, I thought maybe this had already been announced.  We can dream, though.

YMCAs are religious organizations with expensive dues, hardly community centers.  You could get involved with the St Joan of Arc Youth Council, Men's Club, Ladies Club, etc. or any of the organizations sponsored by Community Church, like Scouting, if you're looking to get involved with religious organizations in JH. 

The dues for adults are expensive for the YMCA.  YMCA provides very low costs for kids about $200-300 per year with no joining fee. A lot of families have sent the kids to learn swimming and take other sports/art classes in the YMCA and also attended the summer/holiday camps. Long Island City YMCA also provides free (low cost) on-site after-school care for the local public schools.  Each year, I would say thousands of families have utilized the service provided by Long Island City YMCA. I haven't seen non-religious community services on that scale that have been offered in St Joan of Arc or Community Church.

If there were a local YMCA at Jackson Hegihts (I know it's a pipe dream), I wouldn't have to travel to Long Island City YMCA several times a week.
