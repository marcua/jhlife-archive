Neighborhood Chat / Re: Anything better then time Warner internet?
« on: December 28, 2017, 10:51:01 PM »
You are talking about internet cost, but most of your cost is from your television service. I'm not sure what you mean by two hookups, but let's go over what you need and how much it costs. The fastest internet available shouldn't run you more than $100 per month. Once you have high speed internet, you don't need cable TV. Local channels are available over the air for free. You can add premium channels for about $15 per month each. If you like all those other cable channels, you can add Sling TV or DirectTV Now or one of the other services for $20 to $50. Unfortunately, if you have to watch local sports, you're probably stuck with cable, but out of town sports packages can also be added on. Buy your own cable modem and wifi router and you'll save on equipment rental fees as well. For phone service you can buy an Ooma Telo and get the same service your cable company charges $30 a month for $4 a month. There's an article on Cable Cutter Newsabout saving $2000 a year. Also, even if you don't drop your current provider, talking to them about cutting some of your services will probably lead to a reduction in your current bill. If your building offers RCN, you an get gigabyte internet for about $50 a month. Good luck and don't be afraid to try to change things up.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Crashes, near misses and dangerous intersections
« on: November 21, 2017, 07:54:47 AM »
To answer your first post, I have seen enforcement on both Roosevelt Ave - 75th Street and 37th Ave intersections.  This usually involves giving tickets for failure to yield and registration/inspection violations.
To answer your second post, if you raise an issue about car safety, you cannot ignore pedestrian, bicycle and illegal delivery vehicle issues.  These are all interrelated.
Traffic is ruled by laws, but it is the observance of the laws that determine whether they are effective.  Too many drivers, walkers and bicyclists act as if the laws do not apply to them.  Each person that flouts the law renders the social contract underlying the law null and void.
When the don't walk is flashing, don't cross the street.  Allow cars to turn.
When there is a pedestrian in your lane, wait for the pedestrian to complete their passage before continuing.
If you are riding one of these electric bikes or scooters, get rid of it, get a legal scooter or motorcycle or bicycle, and stop for red lights and yield to pedestrians.
The DOT recently installed a delay between car green lights on 37th Avenue and other areas. This makes it much safer for people to cross.  However, rather than adding the 7 seconds to the red light green light process, they deducted the 7 seconds from the amount of time drivers have to pass through the light legally.  This makes it more likely that frustrated drivers will go through an amber light (or even a red light) and puts different pedestrians at risk.
Drivers, get a bluetooth already.  What's wrong with you? Put your phone away.
Pedestrians, stop texting long enough to look where you're going, especially when in an intersection.
Finally, stop pushing your infant's stroller off the curb to wait for the light to change or for traffic to clear.  Is the extra second you gain by this stupid practice really worth risking your child's life?

Restaurants & Food / Re: Oceanic Boil Restaurant
« on: September 03, 2017, 11:10:50 AM »
Never heard of this place until last night. Driving down 37th Ave. and see a sign for Cajun food in Jackson Heights! How could this have opened without my notice? Had no idea what to expect. No, folks, an Oceanic Boil is not some kind of skin affliction. It is a delightful little addition to the JH culinary mix. In case you haven't had a New Orleans style seafood boil, it's sort of the seafood equivalent of a chopped salad. The server puts your choice of boiled seafood into a plastic bag in a large wooden salad bowl with potatoes and a half ear of corn and your choice of sauce and shakes it up until everything is imbued with the sauce. We had the 1 lb of mussels and the 1 lb of shrimp with the spicy garlic butter and Bay seasoning (the traditional New Orleans seafood spice mix) and it was delicious. You'll have to work for your supper, as the shrimp are served with the shell and head still on. The waitress will offer gloves, and if you are digging into the shrimp or crawfish, you'll appreciate them.
Most of the main courses are boiled, most of the appetizers are fried. We spilt the Cajun Seafood Mac 'n' cheese appetizer and it was plenty for both of us and very tasty. Well worth a visit, and I wish someone had mentioned this place recently, so I hope this bump helps someone else looking for something different. Oh, the bill came to about $40 before tax and tip. Two $13 entrees, an appetizer, one glass of wine.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Aggressive and threatening drivers in crosswalk
« on: January 14, 2017, 09:43:50 PM »
Pedestrians are their own worst enemies in Jackson Heights.  Too many stand in the street while the light is against them,  jaywalking, start crossing while the "Don't Walk"  is flashing,  and most egregiously, distracted by their phones.  While pedestrians always have the right of way,  even when running out between parked cars,  cars have to be able to turn while the signal is green.
The solution is to move crosswalks to the middle of the block,  rather than on the corners.  Barricades or fences prevent pedestrians from crossing at the corner.  This way cars don't have to compete with pedestrians to make turns before the light turns red again.
This is used on 6th and 5th Avenues during the holiday seasons and works very well to allow cars and crossing pedestrians to co-exist. Until the knuckleheads figure out they can jump over the corner pedestrian barriers to avoid being civil.
