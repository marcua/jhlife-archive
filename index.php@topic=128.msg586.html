Looks like you're either:
1.) Opening your eyes and realizing how the world really works
2.) You've stopped drinking those cups of 'diversity Kool-Aid'

I used to believe much of the rhetoric put out by the 'diversicrats', I considered myself one of those 'enlightened white liberals' even.  But 9/11, along with the wisdom of age (now approaching 40) has helped me see the light.

I enjoy learning about foreign cultures, even having friends from other cultures as well (including my wife). 

However, as you insinuated, people in Jackson Heights for the most part stick to themselves.  I've never seen say: a housewife from Bangladesh, a day laborer from Ecuador, a computer programmer from India, a white guy who plays guitar in a rock band, and a banker from Hong Kong sitting in say Lety's or Dunkin Donuts discussing politics or exchanging their favorite recipes for chicken curry.  (And I don't expect to see it in the new place on 77th nor in Starbuck's either, when and if they ever make it to JH). 

Let me also make perfectly clear - Nothing would make me more happy than to see the above scene!  But, it's just not reality.

Diversity goes against all of human nature and our hard-wired reptilian brains.  Most people prefer the company of others of 'like minds', we don't want to have to constantly explain our practices/habits to outsiders/foreigners who may not understand why we do the things we do. 

Type in 'Robert Putnam' into Google, and read some of the recent stories about his research.  He's found that neighborhoods with the HIGHEST levels of diversity have the LEAST amount of social trust, even amongst people of the SAME ethnic and/or racial group.  In fact, he admitted that he suppressed these findings because he was afraid of how "those on the right (wing)" would use that information.  Well, that's the intellectual equivalent of the Catholic Church suppressing Galileo!

I've often wished we could just have a big, general party on 37th Avenue, from say, 74th - 82nd streets.  I suppose it could be something indoors if we had an appropriate venue.  Instead of focusing specifically on "diversity" and "different ethnic groups" it could just be a party or festival celebrating one thing we all have in common--Jackson Heights, Queens, NYC.  Obviously each of the the many cultures here would be represented so the diversity would be inherent, and it could interesting and informative as well as a lot of fun for all.  Call me naive, and even corny, but I think it would be great to have an event that would bring every one together (or at least those who are interested in participating): "Unity for the community," if you will.  It could be constructive and productive for the neighborhood as a whole. 

We already have so many parades and events and groups that celebrate just one part of the community at a time, maybe now there is an opportunity for something fun that ties it all together?  Any other dreamers out there want to help organize it?
