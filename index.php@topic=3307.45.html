What a long, strange, and very unfortunate tale.  Here are some details of Mr. Monserrate's career prior to the Democratic Organization of Queens County anointing him State Senator:

- As part of a Latino officers organization, successfully sued the city for discrimination and won a large cash settlement.
- Retired from the police department due to mental health issues.
- Enters public political life and reputedly engages in very rough fund raising and election tactics. 
- On primary eve 2001, has an altercation with a tow truck driver who is attempting to repossess  his car.  He hits the driver with his car.  This is lost in the tragedy of 9-11.
-  Ponders running for Congress against incumbent congressman and leader of Queens County Democrats, Joe Crowley.
-  Is an instrumental player with the non profit organization LIBRE.  $400,000 of city grant money subsequently can't be accounted for.
- In what proves to be very foretelling, Monserrate attends a fundraiser for Republican State Senator Joe Bruno.

Which brings us to the State Senate primary election in the summer of 2008.  Make no mistake about this:  The elected political establishment was very, very aware of Mr. Monserrate's dubious record.  Not one of the District Leaders actually cares for Mr. Monserrate.  They know full well what he is all about.  Their political calculus was based on fear.  From Crowley down to every district leader.

So in what will likely go down as one of the grandest blunders in local political history, party leaders decide attempt to placate Mr. Monserrate by clearing the road for him to become a state senator. 

And so the district leaders set about savaging John Sabini.  And voila, we have Senator Monserrate.  So what has happened since becoming Senator?

- Mr. Monserrate is in Albany for about fifteen minutes before he starts up with the Gang-of-Four business.
- The sordid and awful domestic violence event.
- And of course, the great Senate coup of 2009. 

Now that Mr. Monserrate has seemingly imploded enough not to be a threat, Rep. Crowley green lights the coup-de-grace with the following statement:

Sadly, I agree Mr. Monserrate isn't going anywhere soon.  He is going to go, but it is going to take a little time to play out.

He is not the kind of guy to bow to pressure.  And from his perspective, why should he go?  There are few careers that can quench a politician's thirst for power like public office.  If he quits, what is he going to do, be a has been lobbyist? I think he will try to ride it out.

There is also a big Albany component here.  Democrats need all hands on deck to control the senate.  Politically, Democrats have to pick their poison.  Live with Monserrate through the next election cycle (when he presumably gets defeated) or somehow expel/force-out Monserrate and deal with a dead locked senate until the seat can be filled.  It is a problem.
