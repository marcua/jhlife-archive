Donations for Miguel Torres' family
« on: January 05, 2013, 02:18:19 AM »
Posted on JH Families site by Doris Torres, email address is dorisortiztorres@yahoo.com

"Hi All:

Thank you so much to those who were present at the Candlelight Vigil held last Sunday for Miguel.  It was very moving. Special thanks to Maribel, Laura and Marlene for contacting the media and politicians.  I am so proud to be part of the Jackson Heights community, always so ready and eager to contribute with any community events. 

Some of you asked if you could contribute with money to help Miguel's family with funeral expenses. 

Those of you who wish to make a donation to Miguel's family should contact the Parent Coordinator at IS 145 and inquire about its money collection for Miguel's family. 

Alternatively, you can drop off the money with me. I will be at Expresso 77 from 9:15 to 10:15 Saturday morning.  Please place your donation in a sealed envelope, and a note to the family, if you wish.  I will put the sealed envelopes in a large manila envelope and give it to the family on Sunday. 

Again, thank you all so much.

Happy and Healthy New Year to you all.

Doris Torres"

Re: Donations for Miguel Torres' family
« Reply #1 on: January 07, 2013, 08:24:53 AM »
Posted on JH Families site by Doris Torres, email address is dorisortiztorres@yahoo.com:

"Just to update you on the donation's for Miguel, I was Abel to raise only $250.  The money came mostly from my family and & $40 from a person in the nabe, who is a friend of mine.  I gave the family a card with our condolences, and only listed the names of the families who contributed.

I sat at E77 for an hour but no one showed up.  I guess it was a bit early for most people, and short notice.  Unfortunately, I was not able to set another time to collect donations as my schedule is tight on weekends. My son, Abel, gets homework on weekends and has other activities.  In addition, today was the Tres Reyes Magos Day and he was too excited.

I went to the wake tonight.  Before arriving, I was feeling a bit uncomfortable about having raised only $250, but felt relieved when I saw the family's friends outpouring of contributions.  I was very happy to see how much support the family has received.  I can only pray for Miguel's parents to get the strength to continue on.

There were lots of people, particularly devasted young kids from school. 

Regarding your donation, I think IS 145 is still taking donations, so you may be able to still do it through the school.

Hope yo see you soon.

Doris"
