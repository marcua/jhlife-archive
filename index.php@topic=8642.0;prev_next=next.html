Best Indian Take Out?
« on: July 30, 2013, 11:14:19 PM »
Just curious - What are your favorite Indian take-out places?  Sometimes on the walk home from 74th Street, I get a craving for Indian food but I just want to take it home, instead of sitting down and eating in the restaurant.  Where you can get some sort of dinner combo - curry with some meat AND veggies (even some potato or peas would be nice) in it (not just meat and sauce), and rice, nan, etc, under $10, clean place, and good food.  Does anyone have a favorite they'd like to share?  Food carts are fine, as are restaurants as long as the wait isn't too long.  Thanks!

the vegetarian places are more likely to offer take-out platters (and if you're ever in the mood for that,rabjhog by the duane reade is excellent). but for meat and veg, i'd suggest al-naimat (next to pizza boy). the widest variety of choices, which rotate, and i've never gotten overly gristly/unpleasant meat there).a little too oily on some dishes, but good overall.

you could also go into indian taj and get a carryout plate from buffet, which allows you a few small portions of different dishes

I frequent Al-Naimat quite often for take out.  The food is already prepared but not heated.  I have a choice of 8oz container or 16 oz container.  Last night, I ordered a 16oz container and selected 2 entrees (a boneless beef and a boneless chicken) all served in one container. A lot of food for just $6.  The many selections of vegetarian dishes are just $5 for a 16 oz container.  They have an incredible home made green yogurt which is complimentary.  The 8oz serving of meat is $4 and the vegetables $3.

When I want chicken kebab its Kebab King on 73rd Road.
