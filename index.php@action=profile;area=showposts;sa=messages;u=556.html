About the Group:
Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) would like to let you know about a program for girls (ages 9-11 years). QCPC’s “Group Therapy for Children of Divorce/Separation” seeks to teach a variety of empirically supported strategies to help children adapt to changes brought about by divorce or separation in their family. The group will aim to correct misperceptions about divorce, teach relaxation skills, increase social support, and provide coping skills. The group seeks to support young girls against the stresses of divorce/separation and provide skills to benefit them through their lives.
Contact: 718-570-0500. Please leave your name, phone number, good times to reach you and that you are calling about the “divorce group”.

Mindfulness for Girls: A weekly group setting, which empowers girls to understand emotions and ease stress.
   
About the Group:
Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) would like to let you know about a program for girls (ages 8-10 years) and their families. QCPC’s “Mindfulness for Girls” Program seeks to teach a variety of science-based mindfulness practices to young girls to enhance stress management, social skills development, emotional intelligence, academic performance, and cognitive functions. Mindfulness is open, moment-to-moment awareness held without judgment, even when what is occurring may be unwanted. Mindfulness offers great benefits to health and well-being by shifting the nature and perspective of a child’s relationship with their own experience. This group seeks to support young girls against the stresses of modern society and give them skills that will benefit them throughout their lives.

Enrollment Details    
•   We welcome any girls (age 8-10 years) that you identify as possibly benefiting from the “Mindfulness for Girls” program.

•   A free, in-person screening is required. Referral information will be provided to girls whose needs may be best met through other services.
•   All group sessions will be offered at no fee, consistent with QCPC’s commitment to providing services regardless of a family’s ability to pay. (Families may contribute a $10 dollar materials fee if they are able to)
•   The first group meeting will tentatively be in February 2020 and the group will meet weekly for approximately 10 weeks.
•   Group meetings will be on Monday afternoons (after- school) and take place on the Queens college campus.
•   Parent/guardian sessions will offered (during group session time) to teach skills and introduce parent/guardians to the mindfulness practices being taught to their daughters.
•   Services are provided by advanced doctoral students in our Clinical Psychology Ph.D. Program at Queens College, City University of New York who receive specialized training and are closely supervised by experienced and licensed clinical psychology faculty. 
•   All services are confidential.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Queens College: Child- Social Skills, TF-CBT, Neuropsychological Assessment
« on: September 12, 2018, 03:12:21 PM »
Dear Community Member:
As part of their community service mission, Queens College Psychological Center (QCPC) continues to provide low fee and free psychological, psychosocial, and educational programs to benefit the community directly. Below are some of the services available this Fall (2018).  Please share this with anyone who may benefit from this information.  Thank you.

1.   Social Skills Group for Boys (ages 7-8) with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) QCPC is offering a social skills group for 7-8 year old boys with ASD.  Using evidenced-based techniques, children will be taught critical skills  to improve social interactions.  The group will also offer a parent training component so that parents can continue practicing these skills at home with the child.  The groups are scheduled for Wednesdays 5:30pm-6:30pm for 12 sessions. The cost of group participation is $250.00 which includes the application, screening, child group sessions,  parenting sessions and feedback session.  (The fee is subsizdized by the college and may be further or completely reduced in the case of financial need. No one will be turned away for inability to pay.) If interested please call Queens College Psychological Center at (718) 570-0500.  Interested participants will be administered a brief phone screen, followed by an in-person interview to ensure that the child’s needs can be met by the group.

2.   Trauma Focused Cognitive Behavior Therapy (TF-CBT) for Adolescents (13-17) QCPC has an opening for two Trauma Focused Cognitive Behavior Therapy (TF-CBT) slots for adolescents ages 13- 17. TF-CBT is an evidence-based approach to treating and resolving symptoms that can occur after traumatic experiences. This treatment involves the child and caregiver in building coping and safety skills and in creating a narrative of the traumatic experience. This treatment is provided free of charge as a service to the community.

3.   Neuropsychological Testing for Children: Again this year, we will have a very limited number of low fee slots for comprehensive Neuropsychological Testing of children. (Due to demand that far exceeds available openings, children with medical/neurological issues will be given priority). Please contact QCPC for information.


For more information please contact us at QCPC@qc.cuny.edu or  718-570-0500. Visit us at www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC   QCPC is located on the Queens College Campus at 65-21 Main Street, Flushing, Queens.  Mental health problems are treatable and QCPC's mission is to implement effective, compassionate, culturally responsive, and accessible (free and low fee) psychosocial treatment and educational programs to benefit the community directly.  Treatment is provided by doctoral candidates in the Clinical Psychology at Queens College Doctoral Program who are closely supervised by experienced and licensed faculty. All services are confidential and available to the community regardless of ability to pay.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / ADHD Social Skills Group, Boys ages 8-9 @ Queens College
« on: January 08, 2018, 12:51:18 PM »
Dear Community Member:

Friendship is an important part of every child’s development – having even one or two close friends can help to increase a child’s self-esteem, independence, and confidence. Making (and keeping friends) for boys who struggle with ADHD can be difficult. They may struggle to pick up on verbal and nonverbal cues or to remain calm and regulated when social situations get difficult.

Queens College Psychological Center is currently offering a social skills group aimed at boys ages 8-9 with these difficulties. Treatment is evidence-based, breaking down peer social interactions into discrete skills and helping parents develop strategies so they can maintain progress at home and for the long term. Some sessions will also be devoted to practicing the skills that have been taught. The group is comprised of once weekly group sessions held in tandem for both parent and child. Individual feedback sessions will be held for each participant after the last group session. Interested participants will be offered a brief phone screen, followed by an in person interview to ensure the child’s needs can best be met by the group. As a community service, most of the cost will be subsidized by the college, and the fee is $250.00 (this includes the application, group screening, child group sessions, and parenting sessions). The fee may be further reduced in the case of financial need. No one will be turned away for inability to pay. Groups will tentatively run on Tuesdays at 5:00 beginning Tuesday January 16th  and run for twelve sessions through April 17th.  The group will meet every Tuesday in between these dates, with the exception of Tuesdays February 20th and April 3rd.

For more information regarding our children's Social Skills Group for 8-9 year old boys with ADHD, please email your contact information (phone number where you may be reached, best time to call you, and if it is okay to leave a message) to: QCPC@qc.cuny.edu or call 718-570-0500. Visit us at www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC Queens College Psychological Center is located on the Queens College Campus at 65-21 Main Street, Flushing, Queens.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Social Skills Group for girls with high-functioning ASD @ Queens College

Please share this with anyone who may benefit from this information.

Friendship is an important part of every child’s development – having even one or two close friends can help to increase a child’s self-esteem, independence, and confidence. Making (and keeping friends) for girls who struggle with aspects of autism (i.e., delayed and/or difficulty with social interaction and communication) can be difficult. They may struggle to pick up on verbal and nonverbal cues or to empathize with another’s perspective.

Queens College Psychological Center is currently offering a social skills group aimed at girls ages 8-11 with these difficulties. Treatment is evidence-based, breaking down peer social interactions into discrete skills and helping parents develop strategies so they can maintain progress at home and for the long term. The group is comprised of once weekly group sessions held in tandem for both parent and child, and run for 6 consecutive weeks. Groups will reconvene after the winter break, and run for an additional 6 sessions, followed by an individual feedback session for each family. The additional 6 sessions will focus on providing opportunities for children to practice the skill taught in the first 6 sessions. Interested participants will be offered a brief phone screen, followed by an in person interview to ensure the child’s needs can best be met by the group. As a community service, most of the cost will be subsidized by the college, and the fee is $250.00 (this includes the application, group screening, child group sessions, and parenting sessions). The fee may be further reduced in the case of financial need. No one will be turned away for inability to pay. Groups will tentatively run on Tuesdays at 5:00 beginning Tuesday November 7th and run for six sessions through December 12th.  Six more sessions will be held after the winter break.

For more information regarding our children's Social Skills Group for girls with high-functioning ASD , please email your contact information (phone number where you may be reached, best time to call you, and if it is okay to leave a message) to: QCPC@qc.cuny.edu or call 718-570-0500. Visit us at www.qc.cuny.edu/QCPC Queens College Psychological Center is located on the Queens College Campus at 65-21 Main Street, Flushing, Queens.


Other Services: QCPC's mission is to bring treatment programs based on extensive research to benefit the community directly. The clinic continues to offer treatment for problems such as depression, stress, anxiety, interpersonal/relational issues, dissatisfaction with current personal or interpersonal life choices, grief and bereavement, coping with medical illness or disability, trauma, self-esteem and body image concerns, parenting issues and adjustment to changing life circumstances. For children, typical problems may include separation anxiety, school refusal, social anxiety, fears, phobias, social skills deficits, Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), tantrums, aggression, depression, moodiness, impulsivity, defiance and disobedience.  The clinic team works closely with our child clients’ schools and does school visits when necessary. Fees for these services are based on a sliding scale from zero to fifty dollars (average fee is $10) that takes into account personal and/or family income and circumstances. This community mental health service is a training and research center operated by the Ph.D. program in clinical psychology. Advanced doctoral candidates in the college’s Clinical Psychology doctoral program provide treatment to patients under the close supervision of experienced, licensed psychologists on the Queens College faculty.
