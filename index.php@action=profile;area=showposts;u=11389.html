Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Maintenance fees...
« on: December 12, 2011, 05:15:00 PM »
I'm a first-time buyer, looking for a one bedroom in JH, too! Don't go stealing my apartment! LOL

From what I've learned in my search, maintenance has everything to do with the size of the apartment and the financial status of the building. It's always ideal to buy in a building with a healthy reserve fund and no underlying mortgage. These buildings will not have frequent maintenance increases b/c they have the reserve funds to cover capital improvements or unexpected expenses. On the other hand, if you buy in a building that has a large underlying mortgage, has recently refinanced, and/or has low reserve funds, if anything goes wrong (unexpected roof damage, or any number of unexpected expenses, etc.), they'll either have to increase the maintenance or tack on a monthly assessment fee (worse case scenario, they can do both). So, before committing to an apartment, ask about the financial status of the building; also, have your attorney diligently review the building's financial statements; b/c this will be the biggest indicator of whether or not your maintenance will increase in the near future and, potentially, it may also indicate how often it will increase. Hope this helps! And g-luck!
