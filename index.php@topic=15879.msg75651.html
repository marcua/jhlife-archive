The LED lights have many advantages - improved visibility (because it's white, not orange), improved energy efficiency, better durability, less light pollution (since they are directly entirely downwards). But yes, they do mess up your night vision, and they are kind of "harsh" looking. It would be nice if they would vary the color based on location, e.g. pure white on highways, redder on residential streets, etc.

Really, though, I think we'll just get used to it. People had the same complaints when electric lights replaced gas flames.

I thought I heard that other neighborhoods who had them first had successfully requested some dimming down of the intensity. You should call 311 and ask where is the best place to express your opinion.

Personally I can not understand why there is no diffusion on these things. I think the bulbs' glare is just too intense when simply walking down the street.

Here's a bit more about it in this story: https://www.nytimes.com/2016/07/12/nyregion/new-yorks-led-streetlights-receive-a-lukewarm-reception.html?_r=0

-On Brian Lehrer’s radio show in May, Mayor Bill de Blasio promised to send out crews to turn down the lights where they present problems. “Once again, the citizens know best,” the mayor, a Democrat, said. “They were too bright in many cases, initially.”

And

"That might just happen. The City Council is considering a bill to lower the color temperature on the LED fixtures. This would reduce glare but also, perhaps, clarity. And after the Department of Transportation received 150 complaints last year, it began installing lower-powered lights: 72 watts compared with 78 watts."
