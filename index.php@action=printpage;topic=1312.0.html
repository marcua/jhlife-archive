Title: Re: Helen Sears' Zoning meeting last Tuesday at Jewish Center?
Post by: Aronan on August 05, 2008, 11:42:25 PM
I did attend the meeting. And I wouldn't have known about it either ecept for the fliers I saw about 2 hours before the meeting. I walked down 37th ave earlier that morning and saw nothing, when I went out in the late afternoon there they were. the Councilperson's office is certainly on their gig.. ugh. Strangely enough the event was well attended mostly by Helen's regular constituents   

For the most  part the meeting was a presentation on zoning given be the Director of City Planing for Queens. It was a watered down lesson on how the city is divided in to zones (Residential, Commercial, & Industrial) and what those zones mean. The major concerns that Helen laid out to be addressed were how tall buildings could go in JH, and what could be done about houses that have paved over their front yards to create parking spaces etc.

The bottom line is most of JH's residential areas are zoned for R6 or R7 which are buildings that top out somewhere between 6 and 10 stories. Apparently there is no real restriction on how high a building in these categories can go, however the higher a building is the further back from the street it must be set, which after a certain point makes building really tall buildings impractical in our neighborhood. At least that's the jist of what was said.

As far as paved yards is concerned the city recently passed a law the requires home owners with yards to maintain their yards as such and gives incentives to new developments to include more green space in their plans. So while JH has to deal with the yards that have already been destroyed we can rest assured that there are laws in place to protect the remaining yards.

I left before the Q&A period so I'm not sure what else was discussed. I don't know if they touched on the over developement of 73rd and 74th Sts. as a zoning issue or not, but I do plan to follow up with Ms.Sear's office on that.
