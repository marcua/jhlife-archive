Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

It may be true that there is a lot of foot traffic on Roosevelt Avenue, but many of the businesses are illegal fronts. Prostitution, drugs, and a variety of other illegal activity bring people to the area that would otherwise not be here. If you take a close look, there is nothing "charming " about it. Sex trafficking is not charming. Illegal social security cards is not cute. Selling cocaine and other drugs is not something that creates  a "bustling" neighborhood. Sure it brings people to the neighborhood, but for all the wrong reasons. Landlords don't care either way as long as they are getting high rents.  Creating a BID is long over due and makes the people who do business here have a stake in the neighborhood. I'm really disappointed that it has be slashed and wish it could be extended to 74th Street.  It is by far the best thing that could happen for Jackson Heights. Legitimate businesses have nothing to loose and everything to gain here.

On any given Saturday afternoon, there are crowds of people along Roosevelt Ave. and the 82nd Street corridor on either side of Roosevelt.  I think that very few of them are there for sex trafficking, cocaine sales or illegal SS cards.  On the contrary, I think that the vast majority of these people are patronizing clothing stores, 99 cents stores, appliance stores, restaurants and food stands and the various other businesses which line the avenue. 

There are issues on Roosevelt, particularly later in the evening -- and sex trafficking is obviously unconscionable.

But to broadly characterize the majority of businesses on Roosevelt as illegal "fronts" and the people who patronize them as lowlifes or criminals is highly misleading and creates a false picture of the neighborhood.
