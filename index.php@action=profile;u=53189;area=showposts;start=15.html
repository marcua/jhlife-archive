Neighborhood Chat / Re: bike shop tune up
« on: April 20, 2016, 07:36:02 AM »
One of my hobbies consists of selling/fixing vintage bikes in Jackson Heights so I can recommend a good shop in the area. My f/t gig prevents me from taking on new clients but if you need a good, reliable, cheap fix- check out FastBike on 8742 britton ave elmhurst- literally 3 stores down from 88th/Roosevelt. I stumbled on this shop after I visited all the shops in the area that couldn't fix a bearing issue, but Roger at FastBike fixed it in minutes. The shop is Spanish speaking and I don't speak a lick of it but after time we communicate like old friends..
I have nothing against Cigi's/Bill's but there prices are typical NYC prices, there's a few good shops in NYC that are cheap- do great work and have good customer relations, I think that's important. I have a lot of friends that own shops, I can also recommend Enochs (midtown west) and even closer to us- if you want to take classes and work on your bike at there shop or build a custom frame or just be amazed by a cool shop-check out my buddy Damon who owns Nomad Cycle in Astoria. He has some of the rarest bicycles you will ever see!

Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: JH in "On the Rise: 8 NYC Neighborhoods to Buy or Rent in Now"
« on: April 08, 2016, 11:15:10 PM »
This is great news for the community. My wife is a JH native and she has seen the neighborhood change many times over. It's super affordable to rent/buy- has so many benefits- transit-proximity to major subway hub, the diversity, great restaurants, farmers market, close knit neighbors- I love it here but it's always good to escape the city- if you can. The past few years we have spent a couple summer months at our beach house and the winters in Cali- just to get away- I've met many JH residents who have homes upstate so I find this to be pretty accurate. I also love the fact that JH lays in a historic district which cannot be compromised with luxury buildings- I think that's what Brooklyn and Manhattan are lacking in most parts, if you head to Clinton Hill or Williamsburg you often see the original architecture being masked by a hideous all glass luxury high rise. That will never happen in JH and I think that's swell.
