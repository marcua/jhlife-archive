I though we could start our own movies review forum.  Not only new movies but also DVD releases wether they are old or new.  It doesn't matter if the movie was from 1968 or it was a made for TV movie.  A general description of the movie with its official rating would be appropriate if available and description of your favorite dialogue or part if you wish to expand why you recommend or not the particular flick.  Your overall opinion of the acting and a "this movie would be enjoyed by" (i.e. children, families, immature males, feminist etc etc.)  This system will allow the JHL members to leave a general or detailed reviews for movies.  Let our members posted opinions save you a few bucks as well a dreadful time at the theater or.

Iron Man

Who would have though that the 1963 Tales of Suspense would mushroom to these heights...IRON MAN.  100 million $$$ the 1st weekend and with Downey Jr?  Man oh man, there is hope for me after all.  I was a comic book reader until about the age of 16 or so...well the hand me down type since I couldn't afford to buy my own until I started working at the age of 12 (well that and the 2 for 1 price Chinese kung fu flicks I used to watch in the Fort Apache section of the South Bx...lol  ).  I must admit that anything that is a Marvel based movie I will see regardless of critics.  I usually don't expect too much but simply a nice time at the theater enjoying and reminiscent of my teenage years.  Actually tales of suspense ended in 1968, the year I was born and the 1st year with it's own name...IRON MAN.  There was Volume 1 through 332 from 1968 to the mid 90's and the IRON MAN 1 - 15 from 1970 to 1994 of the 1975 Giant Iron Man. 

There was a whole bunch more and even today the comics sell but these were the years that I can relate to (up to the 80's that is).  Well I'll make it short...LOL.  I loved the movie because I expected a lot less.  Downey was funny and he actually did very well in his role as Stark.  From his stuck up metro-sexual personality to the enlighten humanitarian 180 turn after he realized his companies weapons were responsible for thousands of death.  His close call with death simply catapulted his self consciousness and decided to use his genius for good.  I really enjoyed the fact that just as the Spiderman movies there was an actual plot and was sufficiently close to the original package and story of the Iron Man of the early years.  The movie has been out a few weeks but if you have not seen it throw your prejudices out the window and go see this child of the 60's creation from Marvel Comics.

Tom Hanks  - Denzel Washington - Jason Robards  - Mary Steenburgen
Being AIDS WALK day I though it appropriate to choose the Oscar winning Philadelphia as a movie to recommend.  While this flick is 15 years old I happen to catch it when I turned on the tube (well, plasma) this afternoon.  I actually purchased the movie on DVD in the mid 90's and saw it 3 or 4 times.  Times have changed and at least in the USA and developed countries AIDS has become a manageable disease similar to diabetes or the likes.  Many people are still ignorant or homophobic but many might change they're minds if they see this powerful movie about one of the most powerful subjects in recent history.  While Philadelphia is a courtroom drama no other movie before 1993 had touched this subject of gay people with HIV / AIDS in the big screen.

Tom Hanks plays the gay lawyer who contracts HIV allegedly in the "stallion" movie theater.  Tom Hanks, their best attorney in the firm gets terminated when the managing partners discover about his illness.  They mastermind a way to terminate his employment and soon he finds an attorney that takes his case.   Denzel Washington plays Hanks lawyer, defends him and sues the firm successfully. While this film will get sidetracked on occasion, the film delivers and it does it in a big way.  Antonio Banderas plays Hanks' lover and while not Banderas most memorable role,  in my opinion the most memorable movie he has participated on.  I found Washington to be funny and very engaging and Tom Hanks had so many powerful scenes that it would be silly to say that this was not one of his best roles ever.  Jason Robards plays the cold and uncaring managing partner that spearheads the termination of Hanks and he plays his role quite convincingly.

I saw this movie after a review and controversial comments made by various groups, gay and straight. in early 1994 (the movie is from 1993)  While I too was somewhat homophobic at one point of my life just as many of you were finding out that one of my best friends and a relative had contracted this disease made me curious about the subject.  As they say, when it hits home... Today, not gay people but heterosexual women have taken center stage as the fastest growing group of the HIV virus.  Buy this movie and see it by yourself because it will move you (is that a tear in your eye you cup cake...lol)  I guarantee you will be less judgmental if you are one of those still throwing that first stone. I honestly recommend this movie to everyone that enjoys good acting, dialogue and with an important subject!
