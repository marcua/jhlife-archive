Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Not surprised that it closed down. A health food place has tons of potential in JH - look at Just Made 4 U - but this business' execution left much to be desired. I hyped up this place and showed the menu to a number of people, and they were all "meh" about it.

1) Bad name
2) Horrible eat-in ambiance/decor
3) Menu was too limited to mostly breakfast-type items
4) Overpriced for those said items

Like someone else said, they did not have a strong identity. They didn't stand out for any reason, either with signature items or competing on price. They also advertised as an Italian and Mediterranean place and there is literally nothing about it that is that.

Personally, I also disliked that sign they placed outside that stated they had the best acai bowls in NYC.

When I first moved to Jackson Heights, I thought wow this neighborhood is gonna change real fast due to gentrification. Much like how I saw premises around me when I lived in Brooklyn get fancy like wildfire. 

But now, after several years here, I have completely learned to appreciate this immigrant neighborhood and can see that it doesn't really change.

Everyone's attitude to food is different.  Mine is that I believe the Tibetan places are the best.

And if I want something else, I feel lucky I can hop onto one of the 5 trains and zoom into Manhattan or elsewhere.

So I am actually happy with the status quo that currently exists here.  And since we are about an 80% immigrant neighborhood, I am perfectly appreciative of the level/quality of what is here.

It is absolutely correct for where we are in my opinion.
