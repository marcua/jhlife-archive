There is a unique light setup at 90th and Roosevelt. All traffic must turn right from 90th onto Roosevelt. It's a constant red arrow that turns to flashing yellow--and stays that way for about four, maybe five cars to pass through--then turns yellow for a second or two like a traditional light. There is no green. You are supposed to stop and proceed right with caution during the flashing yellow.

There is no signage explaining anything. You'll be waiting forever if you wait for a green light. Basically, it's the same as turning right on red, but more clear to pedestrians that it is not their turn to cross.

It used to be a stop sign, but this gives drivers a chance to go when there are so many pedestrians.
