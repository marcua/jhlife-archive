The context for Clinton's "Sister Souljah moment" was that Democrats were seen as overly dependent on identity-group politics, and therefore couldn't risk offending one of their core constituencies.   By criticizing Sister Soujah, Clinton signaled that he was a Democrat who would forge a new type of politics.  Nobody said Clinton had and connection to Sister Souljah as an individual; but (fairly or not) she became a symbol of a politics that he needed to show independence from.
