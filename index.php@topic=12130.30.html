Re: Frederick Wiseman movie: In Jackson Heights
« Reply #39 on: November 11, 2015, 12:03:06 PM »
I saw the film and liked it quite a lot.  It's really great to see Jackson Heights on the big screen and so beautifully filmed.  My favorite scenes were some of the short-to-medium length ones, such as the taxi-driver class, a lovely funny scene in a laundromat, a performance at Terraza 7, a short scene in a pet store and the various religious services.

But the film is way too long, and part of the problem, I think, is that Wiseman really loves meetings.  There are some really powerful accounts of migration and discrimination told at the meetings of Make the Road NY, and some interesting discussions of retail gentrification, but in my view some of these scenes go on for way too long.  This was the same problem I had with Wiseman's film of UC Berkeley that played at the film festival at Lincoln Center last year.  In that one there were these endless meetings of university administrators.  The problem was, these didn't seem like real meetings--Rather, they were opportunities for administrators to speak to Wiseman's camera about the problem of underfunding of higher education. I think there was a little of that problem here too--some of the meetings didn't feel like real meetings but rather the use of a meeting form as an opportunity for people to speak to the camera. I guess even though Wiseman's documentaries are celebrated for doing away with talking-heads, it seems to me that he smuggles his talking-heads in via these scenes.

Lastly, I agree with the previous commentator who mentioned the film's focus on Roosevelt Ave. I'm surprised he didn't pay more attention to 74th Street as well, as it seems like it would have fit his vision well. I also think there was a missed opportunity to show more places in the neighborhood where different communities come together. The scene at the farmers' market was very brief, and there were no scenes that I can recall in the public schools. If you're going to show meetings, a PTA meeting might have been a good one.
