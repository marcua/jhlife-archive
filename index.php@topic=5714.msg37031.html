Wow.  I was going to start a thread about Colony just before Christmas and then thought "oh who gives a damn about them" and then here's a thread talking about them.

Yep.  I've lived here for a long time and usually went to Colony before they burned down and was happy to see them back a block over in their new location. 

The people working there were never overly friendly; actually they weren't what I would call friendly at all but I don't shop to make new friends... I shop to buy things that I want.    When I was there Dec. to buy bottles for Christmas presents, I was taken aback by the level of rudeness I encountered.  I don't know if the younger guy working there was the owners' son.  I gathered he was family because when I commented on the extremely loud rap music playing his reaction was along the lines of "this is our store and we can do what we want."

I basically said "yes you can and I can shop somewhere else."  So this young guy says "good, who cares." 

Honestly, there's a HUGE difference between store owners being unfriendly and just plain obnoxious.

I decided to spend my money elsewhere.  I'm not what you'd call overly sensitive and I don't want people to kiss my a** but I expect store owners/employees to be polite.  I wasn't rude.  I was just stating the obvious: the music was very loud and distracting.  Buh bye Colony.

I have shopped at Colony for many years.  Like lots of people I tend to shop for liquor and wine where it is most convenient.  For me, Colony fit the bill.

But I must say I agree with all of those who feel it isn't a particularly inviting place to shop.  I would stop short of characterizing the employees as rude.  At least I have never seen them be rude.  But they aren't very friendly either.

I for one can get past the somewhat sour Colony shopping experience.  But I have come to another opinion of them that is somewhat harder for me to work through.  I don't think they particularly like the business they are in.   They  seem to have very little passion, interest or excitement about wine or spirits.  I think they would just as soon run a dry cleaner or card shop .   

I wish them no ill will and am glad they made it through the fire.   And I still go there for my liquor staples.  Convenience is hard to argue with.  But I do find myself going there less and less.
