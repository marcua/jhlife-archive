Restaurants & Food / Re: Restaurant Grades in JH
« on: September 11, 2018, 11:11:57 AM »
I don't think is De Blasio, that is how health inspection works, not just in NYC but other cities as well.

Usually standard is one schedule inspection (as in they call the restaurant and tell them when they come by) and one random inspection (they just show up randomly) and from time to time they also do stand in (as in inspector just stand by the cook and watch them cook and check temperature of food being served.

Afterward, they will give you a timeframe to correct all the issues, and re-inspect the restaurant during that period is when they have Grade Pending sign. After the follow up if the issue is not corrected that is when they post the actual grade. You could have an A previously then get inspected with a C but then you corrected most of issues and end up with a B.

Some of the issue are not correctable probably due to aging of the restaurant or how the way kitchen layout not allow them to meet new standard. But yeah I won't go to any place that is C or less (that just mean the owner take no effort to correct their mistake) or if is a simple restaurant I expect an A, such as a sandwich shop (subway) or place sell few items and simple like chipotle or any other fast food.

There is also issue with other cultural food and language barrier issue so those I am okay with B. There is good article about roast duck hanging in Chinatown, and how the restaurants there hire an expert to tell health department it is safe:
http://opencitymag.aaww.org/the-roast-duck-bureaucracy/
