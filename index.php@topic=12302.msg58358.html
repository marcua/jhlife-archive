Duane Reade @ 73rd - shady!
« on: August 17, 2014, 03:14:13 AM »
i'm not a huge fan of the chain in general, but they have some items I just cannot get (or would have to wildly overpay for) in the neighborhood, so I end up there a couple of times a month. I've noticed that about one of every three times I buy an item that's on sale "with card," the card discount does not get reflected, requiring a complaint, and a delay for people behind me on line.

this past week, the branch on 73rd lost me for good, though. had the circular, which had a deep discount on Colgate toothpaste, and tried a purchase on Tuesday or Wednesday, and no discount was reflected. I asked the cashier, who summoned the manager -- and he pointed to the two varieties in the ad and said "only the ones in the picture are on sale, the others are regular price." I didn't want to make a fuss, so I just asked the cashier to void that item and went on with my purchase.

needed to stop in again yesterday and happened to see that one of the pictured toothpastes was back in stock -- and the sale was still on -- so I grabbed a couple and went to the register. again, no discount. this time, the same manager replied with "it says selected varieties only." I said that the item I had was in the photo with the sale price and he told me "that's not one of the selected varieties on sale." when I brought up our conversation from 72 or so hours earlier, he shrugged and walked away. I shrugged, left my otherwise rung up and bagged order on the counter,  and walked out. done with that place until i'm sure he's gone.

Funny but I was just having a similar conversation the other day about this same store.

They have you stand in that pen waiting for a cashier. There is only one cashier working. The next thing you know there are 8 or 10 people in the pen with you. The cashier can see and is well aware of the backup. Eventually they might pick up the intercom and ask for help. Eventually help might come. Eventually you might get out of there.

Contrast this with going into a privately owned shop. There they will remember your face and smile when you walk in.

At the chain stores the workers just don't care, they get paid either way. The managers are the same also, just a higher position. And this same lack of care goes for many other sorts of business where the customer is made to be just as faceless as the workers. Put all the same people in their own shops and watch how fast they remember, or how quickly they learn, what real customer service is all about.

I do 100% of my drugstore business at the CVS on 72nd & Northern, and I highly recommend it if you're nearby or willing to make the trip down there. Of course, it's not perfect - not the cleanest/modern-looking, inventory for less popular items can be spotty, have to deal with self-checkout - but it's great for doing deals.

1) Although not as good as in the past, CVS still has lots of great sales, which are 100% honored. You can even get a raincheck for a particular sale or ExtraCare bucks deal and have it honored in the future.

2) They e-mail you weekly 20-30% coupons off full-price items. Also, coupons will print out on your receipts or they can loaded onto your card from your online account. The coupons will automatically scan when you're checking out.
