missmarty,

Please do support the stores you like. Excessive competition leads to zero profits and zero quality. But on the other hand, these owners are hard working people who had worked for others for decades. They wanted to start their own business. Their families are here. So they will spend their earning here rather than sending it away. I will support them, particularly when NYC isn't a very friendly place for a small business, not to mention the landmarks laws here. First-time business owners will very likely make mistakes and end up paying the violations that they can not afford.

The rent they are paying is way below $11k since that space had been vacant for nearly a year before they moved in. It's, for the most part, a game of supply and demand. There was once an opportunity for a restaurant to be built there. After careful research, the potential tenant moved on because of the landmark issues and health-related concern. Basically every landmark application takes about 3 weeks. If it is denied and a re-submission is required for whatever reasons, it takes another 3 weeks to get a reply. Also, there's a big apartment building across the street, it will be an art to properly place the exhaust vents. If the wind blows the exhaust air toward the apartment building, or the residents there "feel" that the air quality isn't as good as before, additional funds have to be reserved for the lawsuits. These additional start-up cost and ongoing cost have to be taken into consideration. Plus, I don't know if it will be the best idea to add a bakery store half block away from La Nueva.

Again, the rent isn't even close to $10k but it will be interesting to see how well they will do. Wish them best of luck!

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Energy slammers in the hood
« on: February 07, 2011, 09:12:52 PM »
I'm sure we are not alone here.  It has been a long time since I last met with one of these scammer. so here's the story.

A few days ago, a lady came to my store and asked to take a look at the utility bills. She opened the conversation with "I'm with ESCO and I'm here today to make sure the utility companies in this area are treating people alright. First I thought she was a researcher or inspector working for some kind of public service department. Whatever that was, it wouldn't hurt to show her the bills and let her finish her report/job. So I showed her the most recent bills. Then she moved to the next step saying that she could help me lock down the current rates so that I would benefit from the fixed rate when the bills became higher during summer time. I realized that most likely she was a scammer and wasting my time. (In order to change rates, I will need to switch the utility supplier. so she worked for a utility company other than national grid or con edison.) I had a computer right in front of me. I google searched "ESCO scam" and a few reports came up on the page. Then I told her that she was free to leave. During the entire conversation, she never mentioned that she was from "JUST ENERGY". She left me a business card with "JUST ENERGY" on it.
