Title: Your thoughts on the Sunday Greenmarket?
Post by: zubloop on September 14, 2011, 07:04:50 PM
Hi All,

I'm a writer and I live in Jackson Heights. I'm working on an article about the Jackson Heights Greenmarket for Edible Queens. It would be great to hear from some of you about the market. The article will appear in the winter issue so I'd really like to know about your experiences shopping at the market last winter and what you're looking forward to this winter.

While it will be cool for this to be a thread, if any of you are willing to be quoted in the piece, I ask that you send me a private message and include your full name (as opposed to a handle).

I have no sway with the Greenmarket, so there is no need to ask me about whether or not we'll get another poultry farmer (though I sure hope we do!). But in the years that I've been regularly shopping at our Sunday market, I've really been impressed by how much it has grown since going year-round last winter. And talking to farmers and the Greenmarket folks, it seems that our market is a resounding success. 

Some questions to consider should you want to play along:

How does the presence of the market influence your shopping habits?

What items are you thrilled to get during the winter?

What are your thoughts on how the market functions as a community hub?

Thanks!

Title: Re: Your thoughts on the Sunday Greenmarket?
Post by: taggie on September 23, 2011, 03:33:27 PM
We are using the Greenmarket as an axis for all of our food shopping. If we can get it there and it's in the plan/budget or whim, we prefer
to make the purchase at the market. Try to experiment with something new each week and eat some stuff that not so great for you along
with some that's fantastically healthy hoping it all evens out. The selection is impressive especially since, as others have said, it's so new.
The food has taste! and this is something hard to get used to when the summer ends most of the time. But now we can consider ourselves
fortunate to have these year round choices. Thanks to everyone who works to make this happen.
  Two wishes, if I may. One is to allow the food vendors around the sides to have a real home at some place in the market. Am not sure
what the protocol is here but some of the food is fantastic. Second is a place to sit. And I don't know where this is except in the street
or to take a slice of room away from the inside of the asphalt in Travers Park maybe? I know everyone enjoyed the tables farther down
on Playstreet and even when it was otherwise quiet, someone was always sitting at a table.
