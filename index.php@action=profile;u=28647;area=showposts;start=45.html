Neighborhood Chat / Re: Illegal housing landlord gets busted
« on: March 28, 2013, 04:54:42 PM »
There were certainly serious undercounts in Queens, and in particular in Jackson Heights, despite campaigns to encourage all residents to complete the Census with a sense of security. Satellite dishes, doorbells, and mailboxes are all soft methods to estimate numbers of occupants. And I agree that the city seems to turn a blind eye until something horrible happens. In general, I would be cautious not to regard illegal conversions and occupancy as the ultimate problem; these appear in reality to be symptoms of a much broader issue, at the core of which is the city's absolute failure to encourage sufficient development of not only so-called affordable housing, but low-income housing as well. While community sentiments over quality of life issues and safety are perfectly reasonable, I would assume that the vast majority of residents occupying these units do so out of sheer lack of options. As for the homeowners, again the apparent issue is more likely a symptom of a larger crisis. Many homes are sold with basements already finished, the illegality unknown to the buyer, who is in turn assured that this extra income will cover the oftentimes predatory mortgage agreements. Communities often pile blame, accusations, and outrage on the homeowners and tenants of these dwellings, regarding them as criminal opportunists (I have not seen this expressed on this forum, just to be clear). But I suspect that were we to more closely examine the situation, we would find that these homeowners and occupants alike are living in far more precarious situations than we think. And while we shouldn't disregard individual responsibility, the issue seems to trickle down from much vaster economic and policy machinations.
