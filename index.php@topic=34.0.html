New Trader Joes in Queens
« on: November 11, 2007, 03:34:23 PM »
Has anyone been yet?  I just went this morning at 11 am.  The parking lot was quite full but I still got a space.  On a Sunday morning it was a breeze to drive there from JH.  I drove on 76th St. to 45th Ave to Queens Blvd to Woodhaven to Metropolitan.  I came back on Metropolitan to 69th St. all the way to JH.

It's a nice store - very wide aisles - guess they did their homework before building the store.  There's a wide range of products there and it's 10 times more pleasant than shopping in the Manhattan one.

No wine, though - you'll have to go the Manhattan TJ's special wine store for that.

I went around 5 pm on Saturday, and the place was packed.  But typical for a Trader Joes, as busy as the store was, the check-out lines were a breeze. 

Traffic was pretty bad at the time I went there -- my choice of GCP -> Jackie Robinson Pkwy -> Metropolitan Ave. --> TJs wasn't the best choice.  It took about 45 minutes each way.  I'll try your route next time, but it may slow too at that time of the weekend.

Over the years, I've been a regular shopper at two other Trader Joe's locations (the others were in California and out on Long Island).  Compared with those, our location does seem to have wider aisles, and a larger selection.  Hooray!

Perhaps I can get my wife to join this board and share some of her famous TJ shortcut recipies.  In the meantime, here's a site for members of the cult... http://www.traderjoesfan.com/

We had a car last weekend, so we drove to TJ's. It was extremely inconvenient to drive to from Jackson Heights unfortunately, so we'll probably not be doing this again anytime soon. I think there is a bus that goes nearby, but by the time we take the train to Queens Center and figure out the bus number and schedule, we might as well have just taken the R train to Union Square.

BTW, the Queens TJ location wasn't crowded at all when we were there. We  were only waiting behind one person in line. The parking lot was crazy yes, but there is plenty of parking behind by Sports Authority.

If I had a car already and was going to be in the area anyway I would probably go again, but I wouldn't make a special trip to to this location. I really wish they would have been wiser and built this near better public transportation.
