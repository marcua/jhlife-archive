Jeanette- cars are running red lights. Massively. Especially on 35th ave. Cars don't stop at stop signs- especially in the one-way, no through street zones of Elmhurst from here to the Mall. 

 I'm all three, driver, pedestrian, and cyclist, and I can tell you, despite being a major advocate for safe streets, I have really bad behavior. Being involved in safe streets, hearing from folks who've lost loved ones, and raising a kid have helped curb my speeding through a yellow light, jay walking, and going through reds on a bike. It's bad behavior, and to all of you- it's true, pedestrians need to learn how to cross a street, and why it's important not to run in front of a bus. We were probably all raise with the expression "someday you could get hit by a bus" it's a reality now, and we've got to translate that to our streets. There was a Town Hall in Astoria last night. Polly Trottenberg, the new DOT commissioner was there, Queens North NYPD was there. I heard with my own ears that Major Arterial Roads, like Northern, and Queens Blvd, will be Vision Zero Priorities. That is big for us. DOT has new pedestrian safety campaigns, and even discussed completely re-examining truck use in the city.

The NYPD stresses over and over calling 311. The data is reviewed daily and creates "impact Zones " that lead to better enforcement.
In the mean time, here's something wonderful happening in honor of Allison Liao. If you know a school that might be interested here in Jackson Heights- please let me know. April is distracted driving Awareness Month.

Our dear friends, and partners in MQS will celebrate a small, very small victory tomorrow, when nearly 2000 parents, students, community members and administrators sign a safe driving pledge at PS173.

If it might be possible at your school- please let us know. We didn't know Allie, because she was killed, but we do a lot in her honor.
