Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Quote from: cultartist on July 24, 2015, 06:07:52 PM
Well, there are at least a dozen "sports bars" within 10 blocks of that location, all offering sports on big-screen TVs, cocktails and provocatively clad women hired by the establishments to converse and dance with the patrons.  Not that any of that is bad.  It serves a purpose.  And it is indeed probably a great time if that's what you're into.  It's just that there are already SO many bars (all proudly advertised as "sports bars") that have this covered.  Maybe the new joint will offer a twist on the old formula, maybe there IS such incredible demand for yet one more of this type of bar, all fine and dandy.  It just would be nice at some point--and I know I've brought this up before--to have one, just one bar in the neighborhood (aside from Terraza 7) where a single woman or couple can go, a bar not geared towards dudes dudes dudes and more dudes.  Or am I wrong in thinking that this kind of place might do well in the neighborhood.  And, really, this is not a complaint on the neighborhood or a dream for gentrification--just the hope for a little variety in the local bar options.  Maybe Dudley's place or Stew's place will take care of that.

We already have an large unsuccessful sports bar that took over the former Dragon Seed location...this really had the potential of being a cozy family destination.  I walked by when they had all their big screen TVs on-empty, mind you.
We could use diversity but the majority in the neighborhood has to be open to it. This is not a wish for gentrification, only for diversity.
