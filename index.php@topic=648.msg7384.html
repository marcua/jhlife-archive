Bachata is a type of music that was considered to be sort of "lowly" by many Dominicans because it was usually heard in the "bayus" or lowly bars of the "barrios" of the DR.  Themes usually touched the subject of relations gone bad...heck, we used to call it "musica de cuernos"  :2funny:   But since it was mostly the Dominicans that came from many of these barrios they also imported the music and for a while in the 90's it was really popular among their community.  Still is among the Dominicans and many of the Central Americans.  The music became more refined as more capable musicians started to record  bachata  (just as the case of merengue).  The dance is only 3 or 4 steps but it's simplicity (not unlike merengue) makes it a popular type of dance and music among the smaller queens clubs and many in the Heights.
