OK, being a city resident, I should know better than indulge in griping.  However, cut me some slack as I approach 40.  

Having been a resident of JH since 2007, I have never thought much of the noise pollution emanating from planes in/out of LaGuardia until this month. Sitting on my balcony over the last two Saturdays, I feel as if I am in the test engine room at Boeing, sans the benefit of earmuffs.  This morning, within a span of mere 8 minutes, there were 5 takeoffs/landings.

While I am cognizant of the fact that such flight paths are dictated by wind conditions and other overriding prerogatives, I am wondering whether residents are simply bereft of options, short of moving out of the hood.  I would certainly appreciate your thoughts.

the shift in takeoff patterns is generally due to work on one of the airport's runways. as a resident since 1995, i remember a couple of summers that were particularly bad -- somewhere around 1999 or 2000 or so. it's something that passes.

but, without being disrespectful, surely you did take note of LaGuardia's proximity when choosing jackson heights as a plae to settle, right? frankly, we're lucky, since we're generally in the landing pattern, when engines are already cut and relatively quiet. the folks in college point, and the southeast bronx really take the brunt of the noise.

there's really not much i can offer in terms of advice, other than to live with the noise for the 4-5 weekends a year when it's really awful and appreciate the 47-48 when it's not. we're not likely to close the airport, make airplanes quieter or lessen frequency of takeoffs or anything like that.

and, yes, one just went over my place, so i know what you're talking about.
