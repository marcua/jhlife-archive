Neighborhood Chat / Re: Quality of Care at Elmhurst Hospital
« on: December 20, 2020, 07:03:14 PM »
In August of 2018 my husband had a major heart attack and was taken by ambulance to Elmhurst Hospital.  I was so concerned about the level of care he would get there; however, they saved his life. He went directly into the ER and was in a great deal of pain. The only issue we had was that we had to wait for the cardiac surgeon, who did the procedure to clear the clot and put in a stent, as he was not on the premises and had to be called.  One would think that there should be a cardiac surgeon in the hospital ER for all shifts.  We had to wait about 15 to 20 minutes for him to arrive, and with a "widow-maker" heart attack, time is of the essence. However, that was our only issue.  My husband was treated as soon as the surgeon arrived and spent 4 days in the CICU, where again, we had no complaints about his care. My husband is a doctor himself, so he can be a bit critical when it comes to medical care and he has high expectations. Under the circumstances and considering that Elmhurst I believe is considered a trauma center, it was the right place to go when time was of the essence, especially since were were about five blocks away. However, we were not particularly happy with the after care provided by the cardiac clinic and he has since switched to a cardiologist in Manhattan.  We are very grateful for the life saving care he received at Elmhurst Hospital though, and he would return there if another emergency of this type took place. Hopefully we won't have to do this again, especially now in the time of COVID.
