Restaurants & Food / Re: Are there any Brooklyn-like bars in Jackson Heights?
« on: August 30, 2011, 09:41:09 AM »
Quote from: NYC Native on August 29, 2011, 11:10:26 PM
I'm not sure what a Brooklyn Bar is...   

My dad, 1 sister with her husband and a few cousins live in Brooklyn. 

What is a Brooklyn bar?  I know Broklyn is big on "beer" bars but other than that...I'm not sure what else Brooklyn is known for as far as bars are concern.  Is it gay bars, is it Irish bars, bars with beautiful bartenders, dive bars, "front" bars, tapas bars, Latin Jazz? 


Years ago I went from JH to Park Slope back to JH.  Back then, a "Brooklyn Bar" in Park Slope meant either a dyke hangout or an old school place, like Snooky's, or a combination of the two.  When the neighborhood started to change again, old bars became hipster hangouts.  My favorite place to drink was O'Conner's which was located on a very different 5th Avenue.  It was an old Irish place that was more or less the same since the 1930's.  The only things that changed were the jukebox selections and the clientele.

My point is, O'Conner's was not that much different than the Ready Penny.  I wouldn't walk into either place and order a sidecar, but both were/are bars that change a bit depending on their patrons, yet remain very true to themselves.  For me, a "hipster" bar doesn't have to be manufactured, and it doesn't have to displace what is already there.  I know there are a lot of people who don't like the Ready Penny, but in my humble opinion it strikes the right balance in that it's a throwback that blends all the different elements of our neighborhood.  You can have a shot and a beer, while a hipster reads a book at the bar, the old timers are searching for a cigarette, the South Asians are playing pool and the jukebox is blasting either an old Irish drinking song or something by Johnny Cash, which the hipsters see as cutting edge.  I love it.
