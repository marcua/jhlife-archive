Jackson Heights needs to take back it's streets. I have been here six years and believe me it was a shock when I discovered that this place wasn't just "vibrant and diverse" (the reasons we moved here)

I work late all the time. (10-11 pm)  I ride the "cleaning lady express" because it's me and the cleaning ladies and janitors.  Just people coming home from work.

Generally speaking as long as you stay with the crowd and blend in, you'll do OK.  If you see someone drunk or otherwise threatening, move away.  Most of the time I don't see people like that on the train. Wehn you get to JH late at night move along like you know where you're going and you're not going to stop for anyone. (I lived in the Bronx for 25 years and learned how to "travel" the trains and the streets.  But anywhere in NYC you should be careful and look like you know where you're going.)

 My suggestion is to stay with the crowds, keep a low profile.  But really try not to be on Roosevelt ever except as a conduit to getting someplace safer, and certainly not after midnight.  It's not safe period, day or night.
