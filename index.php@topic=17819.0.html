I couldn't find the original thread on this, but the Times now has a story on the high schooler challenging Peralta for his state senate seat:

In New York City, Mr. Chowdhury plans to challenge an incumbent state senator, Jose Peralta, in a Democratic primary in Queens next fall. In New York State, candidates must be 18 to hold state or local office, and Mr. Chowdhury will turn 18 just in time for the 2018 primary.

Mr. Chowdhury comes from a working-class immigrant family in Queens (his father works in a deli and his mother delivers newspapers). He said he became disillusioned with Senator Peralta’s record after learning that he had joined the Independent Democratic Conference.

Some background information on Jessica Ramos from the Queens Tribune:

Ex-City Hall Aide Mulls Run Against Peralta

She admits, “At this point, I’m only considering.” The idea of running had been stewing in her mind for some time, but Ramos knew that if she intended to run, she would have to leave her position at the mayor’s office as the director of Latino Media. On Dec. 1, she did just that.

“I’m focusing on having conversations with my neighbors [and] getting a feel for problems across the district,” she said of her potential bid. “A lot of my neighbors feel betrayed. Jose was the second Latino elected official in Queens.”

As the daughter of Colombian immigrants, Ramos said that she felt a personal connection to the state senator.

“I had looked up to him for a long time,” she said. “His joining the IDC was a real betrayal. I feel duped.”
