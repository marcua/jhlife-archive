Hi all,
My wife and I are looking to move into JH within the next year. We are two twenty-something students/professionals, married, with two dogs and one cat. We are moving from Manhasset, on Long Island. We spend most of our time working and studying in Manhattan. We love the neighborhood-y feel of JH, the food, diversity, historical charm and affordability of JH. I want to ask for input on how safe we will feel as a queer couple in JH? Have you had experiences that were particularly great or particularly bad? Were there any difficulties getting apartments? Being served at a restaurant? Walking down the street holding hands? Or is JH truly the dream community that I imagine it is? Thank you!!

I've lived in Jackson Heights with my married partner for about 30 years now and have always found it to be a generally safe place to live. I am originally from Brooklyn and in comparison have found it to be a much safer place. I have never been singled out for my sexuality.  That's not to say that "bad' things can't happen....they do from time to time.  This is New York city and moving from the Island will certainly be a big adjustment.  As in any urban setting you have to have your eyes opened and be aware of your surrondings.  That said,  Jackson Heights has been the perfect  choice for us. The close proximity to the city coupled with garden living in an affordable house/apartment is a rare find.  Most of all, you will find your neighbors to be most welcoming. Once you know it, it's like living in a small village.

My partner and I feel very safe in JH. I agree with Petster: this is NYC; always be aware of your surroundings. Crabby mentions the recent crimes - don't let that deter you. These could and do happen anywhere. They are absolutely unacceptable. We avoid Roosevelt Ave late nights altogether. Our experience has been one of JH being very Queer-friendly, or at least live and let live. There are visible gay-owned and operated businesses, Queens Pride Center, Out Jackson Heights dinner group, bars/clubs, and the Queens Pride parade rolls right on down 37th Ave every year without incident.

I am a married father of two girls, an infant and a toddler, and I would say that the other couples we know with toddlers/infants, mostly people who have moved to JH in the last few years, are pretty liberal and would not feel comfortable if anybody were to discriminate on the basis of sexual orientation in their presence; I count myself in that group.  When we moved here, we knew that it had one of the oldest gay communities in NYC.  From the straight dad perspective, I do not think, nor would I expect, for anybody to cross the line.  However, if you are making out on Roosevelt and 82nd at 2:00 a.m. and waving your IPhone around, people may mess with you - there are seedy bars with seedy characters along Roosevelt Avenue.  That's not to say that JH is not generally a safe and family friendly neighborhood to the extent that you exercise NYC common sense, i.e., avoid seedy parts of town after midnight.  In any case, in the year that we have lived in JH we have not witnessed anybody openly discriminating on the basis of sexual orientation.

On a slightly different note, welcome to the neighborhood.

My partner and I have lived here for almost 6 years now and have not had any negative experiences here related to being in a same-sex couple. A few dirty looks when we're walking down the street holding hands, but that's almost exclusively from older women. (And maybe I'm just projecting) No problems in shops, restaurants, our building, etc. Frank's Pharmacy is wonderful and lets me pick up my partner's prescriptions (they were also active during the AIDS crisis in the 80's - very gay friendly), our city council member is gay and very vocal in speaking out for LGBT issues, we have the pride parade & Queens pride fest here every year, etc. No real problems that we've experienced. It's also more common to see queer people of color here than in other neighborhoods, especially at pride-related events in June, so that might be a plus for you.
That said, there is the occasional hate crime / gay bashing that happens near Roosevelt Ave late at night. But there are also lots of bars/clubs there, and many that are gay bars/clubs.
It's a great place to live, we are both very happy here. The only downside - we need a bigger/better/greener park!

Our councilmember, hon. Daniel Dromm was the first openly gay councilmember in NYC.  We have enough openly gay businesses that making a list would be tedious.  Frankly, its "normal" enough here that I had not thought of it till you posited the question.  Granted I don't have to directly deal with that issue, but it's never even come up in conversation with any LGBT friends.  That's not to say it never happens, but I can't think of any place "better" in the boroughs with regards to that issue.  If you do move here, dream in a decent bakery into our little Shangri-la.

Side question, and this is really just me curious.  For a decade, about a quarter century ago, I waited tables, jobs ranging across Manhattan and Queens.  I had never once even caught a whiff of someone not being served due to their sexual orientation.  Even my muchacho comrades who harbored some pretty horrendous personal opinions knew gays were better tippers on the average, so they just privately sucked it up.  And since I left the business, it's plain as pie that public attitudes on the matter have improved considerably.  So my question to you, or really anyone in the LGBT community:  Have you actually been denied service at an eatery, dry cleaner, gun range due to displaying openly gay affections?
