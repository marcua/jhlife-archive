Title: Re: Jackson Heights is 4th most overcrowded neighborood
Post by: JDinJH on February 26, 2016, 08:33:10 PM
In response to those who constantly cry about affordable housing, if you can not afford to live in a specific neighborhood, then you should move to a neighborhood you can afford - I did.  When I hear "affordable housing," I am essentially hearing people asking the government (and tax payers) to subsidize their housing costs.  If you want nice things, you should do what many people who have nice things do, find a job that pays well and do it, and then when you make money, save some of it.

As far as overcrowding, I do not care, for the most part, so long it does not create a nuisance.  On the other hand, it is important to also note that very often individuals living in overcrowded apartments suck up a lot of public resources in our society and return nothing - I am talking about taxes.  It is a bummer to sometimes feel like the only tax payer.  But, no matter how you cut it, it is absolutely worth paying all those taxes to live in NYC - overcrowded and all!
