Powerful Rep. Joe Crowley's ethics get closer eye

BY Ben Lesser and Michael Mcauliff In Washington
DAILY NEWS STAFF WRITERS

Tuesday, June 15th 2010, 4:00 AM

Lobbyist fund-raisers for powerful Rep. Joe Crowley are being asked about their dealings with the New York Democrat and seven other legislators, his office acknowledged Monday.

House ethics cops apparently want to know whether their donations swayed lawmakers' votes on the financial reform bill that passed the House last year, two lawmakers in the preliminary probe told The Hill newspaper.

A Democrat familiar with such preliminary investigations said they often amount to nothing, but a letter from the House Office of Congressional Ethics obtained by The Hill says probers are especially interested in fund-raisers held from Dec. 2 to Dec. 11.

Crowley's campaign raised a bit more than $40,000 in that span, federal records show.

A Crowley spokesman said no wrongdoing will be found on his part, saying, "Congressman Crowley has always complied with the letter and spirit of all rules regarding fund-raising and standards of conduct."

While it was unclear what the watchdogs were seeking, an ongoing Daily News investigation of Crowley (D-Queens, Bronx) has found that he often relies on lobbyists and political action committees to raise cash.

James Delaplane, with the Washington outfit Davis & Harman, which has several financial services clients, raised $19,750 for Crowley at an event last year.

"My clients have supported [Crowley] for a long time," he said last month, well before news of the preliminary probe broke. Delaplane said yesterday his firm has not received letters from the congressional probers.

Davis & Harman founder Thomas Davis did donate $500 to Crowley on Dec. 11, though there was no other sign it might bear on the investigation.

Crowley has raised lots of cash from lobbyists and political action committees with business before his Ways and Means Committee, including real estate interests seeking favorable tax treatment. But Crowley told The News prior to the probe it was "crazy" to suggest donations have influenced his votes. "I have never, ever, ever sold my vote," he said.

With a little old driver, so lively and quick,

More rapid than eagles his coursers they came,

"Now Dasher! now, Dancer! now, Prancer and Vixen!

And then, in a twinkling, I heard on the roof

He was dressed all in fur, from his head to his foot,
And his clothes were all tarnished with ashes and soot.
A bundle of Toys he had flung on his back,
And he looked like a peddler, just opening his pack.

His eyes-how they twinkled! his dimples how merry!
His cheeks were like roses, his nose like a cherry!
His droll little mouth was drawn up like a bow,
And the beard of his chin was as white as the snow.

The stump of a pipe he held tight in his teeth,
And the smoke it encircled his head like a wreath.
He had a broad face and a little round belly,
That shook when he laughed, like a bowlful of jelly!

He was chubby and plump, a right jolly old elf,
And I laughed when I saw him, in spite of myself!
A wink of his eye and a twist of his head,
Soon gave me to know I had nothing to dread.

He spoke not a word, but went straight to his work,
And filled all the stockings, then turned with a jerk.
And laying his finger aside of his nose,
