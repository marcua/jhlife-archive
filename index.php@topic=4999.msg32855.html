I think all of these HS schools listed have potential for certain students. In my opinion as a teacher, the NYC schools have
been finally running pretty well with teachers getting a decent living wage and some new technology. This is probably
as good as the public schools will get for some time due to crazy budget cuts and the hoopla about tenure. I do think that
small schools are wonderful for many younger kids. If you can afford anything special, it's well worth it as a long term
investment and if they want to call them charter schools, fine. But I still think Jackson Heights deserves a medium sized
high school that is dedicated to this neighborhood, similar to Forest Hills or Cardoza or my fav. example, Eleanor Roosevelt
in Manhattan which a number of families have chosen over the huge specialized schools. If you do not want to go to a
specialized school (some of them are tiny and strange) the zoned school for JH seems to be Bryant  in Woodside which is
supposedly improving but still problematic. By the way, Park Slope for all it's lovely kid favored stuff also has no decent
local zoned high school.

My daughter is going into 8th grade at IS 230. We moved here mid-year in 6th grade and IS 230 was her zoned school but we have been very pleased overall with the administration and the teachers. I think that over 40 kids from the 8th grade class last year were accepted to specialized high school and that number has been consistently going up. Based on my observations alone, I think that IS 230 is more diverse than IS 145 but I am not entirely sure about that. I do think that as with any middle school, a parent needs to stay really involved because kids so often struggle at that age.
There are a lot of after-school activities and quite a few field trips including overnight trips each year and my daughter has been much more engaged in learning and her grades have improved significantly since she started at IS 230. The huge drawback of the school is that it's overcrowded but I think that the teachers and the school safety officers do a really good job with crowd control Almost all of the teachers and school safety officers know my daughter by name and she's not a troublemaker, which to me is a sign of an engaged faculty and staff.
I think that anyone interested in any school should take the time to drop in for a visit and talk to the admins and teachers to figure out if the school is a good fit for your kids.
