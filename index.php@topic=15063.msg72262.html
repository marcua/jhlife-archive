Hi Lindsey,

I'm so happy to hear that another kitty will get a loving home.

A few points:

  Some people use a litter box that has a cover over the top but quite a few kitties do not like to be enclosed with their "business".  The tops are removable so, if kitty is reluctant, you can keep the top off.

The way to keep the odor down is to keep the box clean.  There are several good quality scoopable litters that facilitate cleaning but, even with scoopable litters, the box needs to be totally emptied and washed every once in a while.

Best wishes and good health to you and your new family member!

I've had cats for many years. They've preferred the enclosed box with the opening on the side (not the top). As for the litter I prefer the non-clumping litter. I feel the clumping litter tends to stick to the box  and scooper making cleaning harder. I clean the box 2x a day at least and wash with bleach weekly or more if needed. They do have flushable litter but I wouldn't want to clog anything-especially in an apt. You might also consider adopting two kitties. They love the company!! Good luck on your new addition.

We use the Clever Cat litter box. http://www.petco.com/shop/en/petcostore/clevercat-top-entry-litterbox

Our cat took to it as an adult. We're in a one bedroom, so it sits right next to our toilet under a pedestal sink. The top collects litter that gets stuck in their paws.

Earlier I had one of those regular hooded boxes the cat just walks in and out of, but I noticed that sometimes our cat went from popping a squat to darting like a bat out of hell as soon as she was finished with her business, resulting in litter everywhere in her wake. I think she sort of does it now on occasion, but what that means is she hops out on to our toilet seat, and while finding litter scattered on your (closed) toilet seat isn't awesome, it's better than finding it spread all over the bathroom, hallway, and bedroom.

I think for older cats these can be good too if they can climb on top of them. Older cats can become arthritic, and sometimes squatting down is problematic. I've seen people just using a box with no cover because their cats were picky having problems with an arthritic cat accidentally peeing outside the box because it can't squat low anymore (poor kitty, and home owner!)

Good luck with your new kitty!

We had the Modko but eventually found it hard to clean. The liner would get pushed down and the litter would get clogged around the sides of the liner. Try to get one with high sides if you don't get one with a cover, because the cat will spill litter over the edges.

We use the self-raking litter maid, but we use a reusable tray bought on eBay instead of the costly cardboard trays. We use the fresh step crystals, which never gum up the rake. We don't cover the box at all. When we had work done on the place prior to moving in, I had the contractor put an outlet in the utility closet where we keep tools and cleaning supplies on the upper shelves. The box is on the floor in that closet and we leave the door slightly ajar so she can come and go.

I bought the litter maid 7 years ago and it's still going strong. The plastic trays I replace every few years.
