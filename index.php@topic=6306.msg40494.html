well, I'm just getting into coffee this year. Unfortunately I am finally able to finish an entire cup of diner coffee now, while a year ago it was intolerable. I would never go to starbucks because it is the epitome of national chain, lowly employed locals, and our money seeding people outside of the community -- but seriously I don't look down on people who go there, I'm just trying to understand what makes people go there. Also, Is their internet free? isn't there a fee? 

I do hear you that e77 you feel guilty for taking up space, therefore rushed and self-conscious. I only go there when I have someone picky about coffee with me. I do not go there to hang out, it's not really my crowd, and I'm not too into the coffee that much. I definitely see how someone would feel self-conscious there, and maybe I do too. also, it's too easy. I try to go to places that I'm trying to learn more about the culture at all times. I have a problem where I can't just relax.

lately, I've been going to JoJu in elmhurst. New vietnamese sandwich shop. Free internet, space is not too limiting, and I like the iced coffee a lot. vietnamese style coffee is pretty damn good, from what im learning now. it's essentially like an espresso iced coffee, and they used condensed milk. The music is really nice, and I can get a lot of work done there easily. especially in this hot weather. the people watching is really good there too, b/c the area is so busy. once in a while a cute girl will walk in too... I dig the food too. simple, don't have to think about it too much. owners are nice, 2 couples around my age who I am rooting for to succeed.

Sometimes I will go to rainbow bakery b/c it is extremely close to my place. I believe you can get free wifi there, and the people watching is great there too. you sit right up next to the window, and b/c people are so busy, no one seems to notice you at all.

also, queens kickshaw, but that's a ways away. i usually go there for a specific reason other than coffee.

sometimes I do work at the library because it's cool, but it's still very distracting there, and no caffeine or music.  and I always have hope for plugging my computer in, but it  rarely happens.

- so, JoJu in elmhurst is my spot now.
