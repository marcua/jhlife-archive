Starting up a business takes a tremendous amount of time, money, and effort. I've known a couple of entrepreneurs who've started a local business with varying degrees of success. So unless you're independently wealthy and plan to hire a staff to do all the work, my first suggestion would be to go into the kind of business that YOU want to run. You'd better have a solid business plan, be prepared to work from dawn til dusk (and quite possibly even longer hours) knowing you'll most likely lose money until you build up a steady clientele. This isn't something anyone should do on a whim, especially given the start-up costs.

 There is a healthier food store/cafe/juice bar type place on Broadway in Astoria not far from Best Buy. Maybe 44th Street? Salad bar is outstanding. Think mini Whole foods.

bags getting off the subway correctly, people will pay in this neighborhood if they are getting value. As much as the dollar stores do ok, there
are plenty of people here who can loosen their wallets for something they really like or need. The JH Greenmarket is a prime example of that.
