Neighborhood Chat / Rudy Volcano: Warning
« on: December 25, 2009, 04:17:37 PM »
Just a warning to people in the neighborhood about this local business. Until this evening, my family and I were patrons of this establishment.
Yesterday evening, my son and his mother stopped outside the store, which has a train on display in the window, on their way home from holiday shopping. My son was looking in the window, apparently too close for the tastes an eldery gentleman who apparently works there, who came out. Now if the man had told him not to look so closely, that would be just be rude but acceptable ... however, this gentleman took it upon himself, before saying anything, to forcefully poke our child in the shoulder with his finger. Repeatedly. Our 6 year old child. Our 6 year old autistic child.
When told that touching someone like that, especially a child, was inappropriate, the man tried to play it off with a joke, saying maybe he should get our son a cloth to wipe off where he was breathing. Of course, after again pointing out that his comments and action were inappropriate, my son and his mother left immediately to come home and tell me.
My first reaction was to involve the police, mostly because they were extremely helpful a while back when my child, then 4, was being stalked. But, after I thought on it for a few moments, I do recall seeing the owner before, and am thinking that's probably the best way to direct this, at least at first. Besides, I'm not looking to send anyone to jail for bad judgment, but its really the owner who should be notified of his employee's actions.
In the interim though, as I recall seeing similar warnings on jhfamilies at yahoo about another store that had less-than-child- friendly staff, I am posting this as warning to others.
I do not mean for it to turn into one of these crazy board of a thousand reply things (like the whole Gustosa's thing, or anything Monserrate on the jhfamilie  board), and really am not looking for replies or threads ...
Nor am I advocating for a boycott or petition ...
I just feel that parents should be given a heads up. Especially in a kid busy holiday season.
Definitely not the best way to start Christmas Eve.

Jackson Heights of Yesteryear / You know you've lived in Jackson Heights a long time ...
« on: November 26, 2009, 10:36:54 PM »
... when you can recall that phone numbers like 672 were NR2 ...

... when everybody went to PS 69, from 69th Street to 88th Street ...

... ditto IS 145, all the way to Corona ...

... (and all the 6th graders fit in one trailer) ...

... Travers Park was just called the 77th Street Park (ironic the playstreet is on 78th, but I still think of the park as the old 77th) ...

... the local precinct was the 110th before we earned enough crime stats to get the 115th ...

... trains had double letters and the N was the R or RR or whatever ...

... the old lady upstairs when you were a kid was alive in the 19th century (truly!) ...

... the only Halloween parade was running from the park in a line before the major craziness began ...

... people sat on milk crates and folding chairs just off 37th Avenue (there was no internet then!) ...

... everyone roller skated at USA ...

ah nostalgia ... I'm in that mood I guess
