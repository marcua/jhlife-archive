Neighborhood Chat / Re: Cost of Living in Jackson Heights
« on: January 18, 2018, 12:57:22 PM »
Hi JHalum.  I was in a similar position getting out of college several years ago.  I was done with the roommate thing and decided to get my own place in JH.  The rents have gone up quite a bit in the last few years but he should be able to swing a studio apartment in JH on that salary.  He will need to get a few years work experience before upgrading to a 1BR.

Something similar to this could be a good option (https://streeteasy.com/building/72_10-37-avenue-queens/607).  Assuming he works in midtown since nearly all the CPA firms are, he can be door to desk in less than 30 mins.  He might be a little bored as JH doesn't have the bar scene that the East Village does but he should be studying for the CPA exam anyways.
