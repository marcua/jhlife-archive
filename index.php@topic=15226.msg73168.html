Here's what you do.  Go to Macy's and pick out your dream mattress.  Their salespeople can be very helpful.  When you know the name of the exact mattress you want.......go to Sleepy's to purchase.  Just tell them you want the "Stearns and Foster/Dreamaway" (or whatever the silly name is) and they will sell you the same Stearns and Foster mattress from their line.  It will have a different name attached to the brand you desire.  Don't be alarmed.  Each mattress seller has separate names attached to their products to make it difficult for the consumer to comparison shop.  The Sleepy's price should be cheaper than even the Macy's sale price.  I've done this twice in the past and it has worked out great.

I hate mattress shopping. I've done what was suggested before, and gone to all the mattress stores on Queens Blvd across from Target, plus the Macys furniture showroom. The salespeople are aware of this strategy and none of them would tell me the name of the mattress I liked. I ended up ordering something off Sears.com sight unseen. 7 years later, I'm glad to be rid of the mattress.

I recently got a new mattress, was looking to avoid the earlier experience, so looked specifically at direct-to-customer brands, like Casper. I made an appt at the Casper showroom in Soho. They give you a pillow, lead you to a room with a queen sized bed, and you try it out for 30 minutes. You can nap if you want. I jumped around on it, rolled around, laid down, etc. After you're done, you can ask any questions you want. I also went to the Leesa showroom in Soho and did the same, and ended up ordering one right there. I also used this peer-review blog for mattresses that i found very helpful: sleepopolis.com

PS. Its been about 40 days into the 100 day trial for my new Leesa, and I like it!

What an incredibly helpful conversation. Thanks to you all for your feedback. I am adding Leesa to my list of ones to check out.

Shelby2, So have you fully committed to S&F brand? Also can I ask, was the bed you slept on at your friends' place in their primary sleeping space or in a second bedroom?

I am reluctant to try and sway you but ours was a truly terrible experience. What we found was basically when you run into trouble with a large mattress maker there is this test used to prove if what you have is defective. It is a joke. Without allowing you to lay on it at the same time, this person supposedly comes out to measure the depth of the indentation. If it is more than 1.5 inches deep they will replace it. Otherwise sorry charlie. I didn't go through myself because I didn't want to pay the fee for their visit. Without my weight on it I couldn't get that measurement so why would they. But man, was this thing unbearably uncomfortable. After owning it for only 18 months I found myself waking night after night, half of my body falling asleep because of my trough. We felt we had little choice but to dump it and go with a cheaper Ikea one to get us though. Thankfully that thing has been a champ for the past 2 years. And for the record my husband and I are both of average weight for our heights so that did not factor in creating this issue. We also had this placed on a box spring on a frame, not a slat or platform bed or on the floor..so not sure how that factored in as well.

Also as a sidebar Stearns & Foster and Bassett are both owned by Sealy. It could stand to reason that the quality of those three brands is comparable despite premium pricing. Something else to consider perhaps?

Before my Sterns and Foster debacle I had a lovely (I think it was) Serta that I bought over the phone from 1-800-mattress. It was the very cheapest sized queen bed on offer. That thing held up amazingly well for over 15 years. Who knows what that brand's quality has become over the years. But it sure was great while it lasted.

One last thing: What I find most maddening is how the major bands now exclusively make one sided mattresses (irrespective of pillow top or not). So they are no longer are flippable. Basically that seems like you are shaving off half the useful life. Anyone come across a brand that is still made two sided? Please share. I'd gladly go down that old tech path and add my own pillow top or memory foam mattress topper. Let's face it this process is not so fun..so I want to find something good and have it last at least a decade.
