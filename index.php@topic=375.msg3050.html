I stopped by Plaka this weekend for dinner and was pleasantly surprised.  The dining room is pretty spacious and the decor is pleasingly low key.  There's a fresh seafood display where customers may choose what type of seafood they would like to have prepared for their meal.  The items look very fresh...darn allergies of mine.  I had to steer clear.     There's also a grill by the entrance where meats are prepared.  The service was great.  The Greek salad is HUGE, and definitely enough for 3 people.  I ordered chicken shish kabob.  The chunks of chicken had been marinated in a lemon/olive oil/oregano sauce.  The chicken was incredibly moist and flavorful.  It came accompanied by a mild citrus flavored rice dotted with chopped red and green peppers.  It was a nice accompaniment to the chicken.  Sourdough bread, along with an olive oil and balsamic vinegar dipping sauce were served before the meal.  I will be returning to try other items from their menu.  I've heard that their yogurt dessert is pretty good.  It's topped with honey and chopped nuts.

I was there last Thursday, and I don't know about this place.  I really wanted to like them, but...

While it's a nicely decorated and comfortable dining room, the service was a wreck (they were out of Spinach Pie, but were making some in the back and the waitress wasn't sure if it'd be ready in time for my order, the dolmades which were ordered as a cold appetizer took a 1/2 hour to make and were served hot, I had to ask 3 times for my beer which was ordered as soon as we sat down, and my girlfriend who asked for a salad with her fish, got steamed greens instead), and the prices are really high (entrees between 15 - 25) for what it is - which is probably why the dining room was nearly empty.

The orders they did get right - a chicken soulvaki with Roasted Lemon Potatoes and a Grilled Porgie were really delicious, but there's no reason I'd go back if I can get good Greek food at a more reasonable price with better service in nearby Astoria.

I have resuscitated the thread -  :tickedoff:

Plaka is a family thing now adays.  They lack service because they are more of a family thingy.  They have gotten better with the food because now they order smaller amounts of seafood.  Like many restaurants that try too hard...some of their dishes just don't cut it.  Great locale.  I like the octopus and If you are a seafood person by all means go.  Skip the appetizers and go directly for the seafood grill.  Shrimp, octopus, stuffed clams, sole, and calamari all delicious.  If you MUST HAVE an appetizer go for a salad and move on.

They should work on their wine list.  I don't know about Greek wine but if I were them I would look into a more international wine list...(or a California list).  Their house wines are sucky  but I always feel like family there.  Joke around with the daughter, she is sweet and her mom had struggled as a NY mom for a while.  Give them a chance but if you are not a seafood person skip the joint.  Yes, I had better in Astoria but the seafood grill is as good as it gets in Queens.  If you are looking for fried calamari go to Kyklades on Ditmars Blvd and 35th... :smitten:
