Wine bar, to me, is another way to say Bistro.  The only difference is they have a bigger selection of "wine by the glass" instead of having to buy bottles.  The food can be American, Italian, Continental, Mediterranean etc.... again, my definition maybe not everyone else's.  The few tapas places around here that I've been to are more Hispanic (loud spanish language music) than Continental.  And I love all kinds of music so it's not the language part that bothers me, it's the obnoxiously loud part.  Again, my own pet peeve.  But a wine bar to me has Jazz or International or Classical music in the background at a volume you speak comfortably over OR no music which is true for most restaurants outside of the ethnic ones that either have loud music or TV's turned on.  "If you build it, they will come."

A wine bar is sorely needed in the neighborhood and would serve a vast unmet need.

But one thing I don't understand, and I don't wish to provoke a fight! 

On one hand people, the "anti-gentrifiers" seem to be against a wine bar (seeing it as a harbinger of further "yuppification"), or at least don't think it will work, since the neighborhood is composed of "working class types" with limited funds and/or simple tastes. 

On the other hand, some people seem to be saying that it's condescending to assume that people in the neighborhood have simple tastes, i.e. people only drink Corona beer out of the bottle (or something like that!), or that they're not sophisticated enough to appreciate wine.  There is a demand out there for such a wine bar, but no one is willing to step forward and start one up, since they are making incorrect (stereotypes?) assumptions about the neighborhood. 

These arguments seem to be opposite ends of the spectrum, and contradictory.  Which one is it?  Or is it like many things in life, a nuanced answer, a little bit of both?

I don't think Jardi is a wine bar. A wine bar, is a location that you go to because it specializes in the variety of its wine and the knowledge of it's Sommelier. A wine bar happens to also have carefully selected small dishes (sometimes full plates) that can be paired with the wine. All of this is done so that the customer gets a better experience with the wine.

Jardi is a Tapas place that also serves wine. I don't think they have an on staff Sommelier who is extremely knowledgeable about the wine. Don't get me wrong, they have nice wine, but it's definitely not the same experience.

An amazing new wine bar to try (in Manhattan) is called Solex. An acquaintance of mine works there. The wine and food is paired deliciously.

http://www.yelp.com/biz/solex-new-york-2

Oh, and yes, I would love a tea house or a wine bar in this neighborhood.
