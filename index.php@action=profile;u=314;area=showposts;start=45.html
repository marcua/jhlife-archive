Neighborhood Chat / Re: gyms in the area
« on: September 13, 2009, 09:22:25 PM »
Yeah, you can spend as much or as little as you want. $50- $100/month seems about standard. I know Ballys is cheaper. But you get what you pay for. The one in Elmhurst is inconvenient and not very nice. You can even spend nothing and get a great workout in your apartment or the park. It's all about the atmosphere and extras your want.

Keep in mind too, that gyms make their money from people with good intentions who end up not using the gym but still paying the monthly fee. I recommend trying out different gyms to see what you like. Most any gym will give you a free day or week to try them out. Just don't give in to the hard sell right away. This approach will help you see what's really important to you and make an educated choice. It will also help you see if your heart is really in it. If after 2 weeks of workouts you find you're not going anymore, at least you won't be stuck with a monthly gym bill.

Restaurants & Food / Re: Where's the Ambiance?
« on: May 28, 2009, 12:31:47 AM »
The restaurants on Roosevelt are essentially what you'd expect to find underneath subway tracks. There are some great places (Tacqueria Coatzingo for Mexican, El Sitio for Cuban, Rice Ave. for Thai). But with the exception of Rice Ave. the decor is lacking. There's a great place on 83rd St. just on the Elmhurst side of the tracks called El Meson Colombiano. It's a quaint little restaurant with an owner who might just sit down at your table and order for you. I've had some fabulous meals there. Armondo's on 37th Ave. at 75th St. has good Italian food, a good vibe and really attentive service. Urubamba on 37th Ave. at 86th St. looks like a really cozy place for Peruvian food, though I've never been. I can't speak to the vegetarian options, but there's a nice brunch spot in Astoria called Cafe Bar. It's located at 34th Ave. and 36th St. not too far from the Steinway stop. There are no brunch spots in Jackson Heights as far as I know. But that will change if the neighborhood continues to change. Happy eating.
