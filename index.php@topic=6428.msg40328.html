I saw this in the City Room section of the NY Times and then found the source on the ConEd site.

At 2:00 this morning, ConEd reduced power in Western Queens (Glendale, Fresh Pond, Ridgewood, Rego Park, Middle Village, Elmhurst and Woodside).

They are also asking us not to use washers, dryers or air conditioners.  I have to say that a humid, 100 degree day is exactly the reason I have a/c in the first place!

The voltage reduction was done as a precaution to protect equipment and maintain service as company crews work to repair the problem.  Con Edison has asked customers in these areas not to use appliances such as washers, dryers, air conditioners and other energy-intensive equipment and to turn off lights and televisions when not needed until the equipment problems are resolved.

Well sorry to say it but aging electrical infrastructure or not, AC units devour energy like it's nobody's business. As our city's population increases, as the summers keep getting hotter and energy keeps getting more and more expensive, is it even possible for the infrastructure to be able to keep up with exponentially growing demands?

Recently we've taken a step back in our house and sworn off the AC and invested in hardcore fans. Not the cheap plastic Lasko variety at Duane Reade which will give you weak breeze at best, but a hard core metal, loud as a jet engine fan. Like the kind I remember growing up with that's hard to find these days. I think because less people use fans these days, one has to search pretty hard to get one that will do the job it should be doing. Drug store fans...simply don't cut it.

This is the one we have in our kitchen as an exhaust fan that pulls the hot air out of the apartment:
http://www.ventingdirect.com/air-king-9166-20-3560-cfm-3-speed-whole-house-window-fan/p441363

It doesn't "cool the room" like an ac but provides significant air circulation that makes the hot temp more comfortable to be in. Is it the same as AC? Hell no...but our electricity bill is a tiny fraction of what it would be if we were using the AC. And the heat is tolerable with it. It also helps to set up fans in a way to have one positioned in one window point out as an exhaust and another pointing in as an intake. It creates nice cross ventilation in the apartment.

There was an article about house fans in the times recently that mentions the same fan as well:
http://www.nytimes.com/2011/06/16/garden/bringing-in-the-big-fans.html?pagewanted=all

I'd be happy to answer any questions about my fans if you have any.
