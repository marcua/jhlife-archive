Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

VETERANS ENROLLMENT & MEDICAL SCREENING EVENT
 
On behalf of VA NY Harbor Healthcare System, I’d like to let you know about an upcoming Veterans Enrollment and Medical screening event.  The event is scheduled for Saturday, July 15, 10am-4pm, at VA’s St. Albans, Queens Campus.
A priority for VA Secretary David Shulkin is preventing the high number of suicides among Veterans who have never been seen or medically assessed at a VA hospital.  Committed to the idea of “0” Suicides, we are letting Veterans know that they can receive Same-Day Assistance including Anxiety/Depression Screenings.
Other on-site screenings and guidance will be available on topics such as:
·         Housing
·         Dental Screening for eligible Veterans
·         Military Injury Claims
·         Burial Benefits
·         Services for Women Veterans
·         Services for recently discharged Veterans (OEF/OIF/OND)
·         Referrals for less than Honorably Discharged Veterans
Veterans are advised to bring a copy of their DD-214
Veterans with questions should contact VHANYHCommunityRelations@va. gov or call 1-888-762-0132
Many Thanks,
Lyn Johnson, Marine Corps Veteran, Outreach Specialist
VA NY Harbor Healthcare System
Phone # 212-686-7500 ext 4218
Lyn.Johnson2@va.gov

Community Groups, Activities & Events / No Garbage, Recycling, Organic Collection, or Street Cleaning on Memorial Day, M
« on: May 26, 2017, 12:00:03 PM »
From Assemblyman DeDekker

No Garbage, Recycling, Organic Collection, or Street Cleaning on Memorial Day, Monday, May 29

The New York City Department of Sanitation announced that in observance of Memorial Day, there will be no garbage, recycling, or organics collection, nor street cleaning on Monday, May 29, 2017.
Residents who normally receive Monday trash, organics or recycling collection should place their material out at curbside after 4 p.m. on Monday, May 29 for pickup. The Department previously collected recyclables the week following the holiday, but is modifying its holiday service at this time.

Additionally, all Sanitation Department administrative offices will be closed on Monday, May 29, in observance of the holiday.
