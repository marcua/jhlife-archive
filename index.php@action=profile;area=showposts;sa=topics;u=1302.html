Neighborhood Chat / Supermarket Rip Offs!
« on: May 15, 2010, 09:57:55 AM »
I am getting really tired of the attitude in Met Supermarket on 37th/77th.  Been going in there for 10 years and there is only one person who appears to be somewhat pleasant - the lady who takes your bags!  Cashiers for the most part are surly, mangers or whom I believe to be managers are cocky and arrogant.  BUT...what really gets me is when they do not honor what they advertise and stonewall and are damned rude.  Case in point  try to get the English muffins on sale this week.  Circular says buy1 (at an inflated price) and get two free - original, honey wheat, whole grain.  They say no it is only for the original.  They even ripped the sign down in front of the display.  I know it sounds trivial but they have such an attitude and this is not the first time they have done that.  Is there any recourse or official place to lodge a complaint.  Yep, I am on a soap box now.  They got me nuts! Love to start a picket line to call attention to their less than fair practices.  At least at Trade Fair if you have a problem they usually resolve it to the customers satisfaction.

Anybody else experience similar bad attitude and customer service like this at Met???

Restaurants & Food / Finally a Place for a Decent Burger in the Neighborhood ( sort of)
« on: February 20, 2010, 11:12:41 AM »
Been passing it a number of times and decided to try it last night.  Cassidy's Ale House on 75th & 31st Ave opposite the shopping plaza with Canelle!!!!!!   is exactly what you would expect. An Irish style pub, tap beers and pub food.  I finally found a hamburger that is drip down your chin good. :rockon:  The place was packed and a bit noisy with partyers (sp!) and the requisite TV's tuned into every sport channel and alas one FOX channel, but I won't hold that against them.  Burger was delicious, my partner had a grilled chicken sandwich with ham & cheese and Blue Moon on Tap.  Just what the nabe needs, now why can't we get something on 37th Ave like this?  Could greedy landlords charging exorbitant rents have something to do with it????Hmmmmmm   sorry into icons today.

Restaurants & Food / Good Hamburger/Salad/Glass of Wine?
« on: November 02, 2009, 12:45:11 PM »
Ok so we have great Indian food and at least 2 million restaurants to choose from, likewise for Mexican....is there a place to get a good hamburger, a nice salad a glass of wine....a place to chill after work or for those nights you just don't want to cook!  If anyone is serious about a restaurant/bistro/osteria/pub  please open one.....you will be packed.  The misconception that everything in the neighborhood has to cater to a Latino market misses the mark. Latino restaurants are great, but there are many other sorely needed variations aching for a home  in Jackson Heights.  Whomever opens the first place will make a fortune.  If I had the $$ I'd do it in a heart beat.
