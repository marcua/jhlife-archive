Since they were running fiber I asked them if it was for fios availability.  They said no, it was just for regular telephone service since the copper in our neighborhood is in bad shape.  (After a certain number of issues with copper wire they declare it a chronic and replace it with fiber because it doesn't take long for it to get very expensive to continuously repair wire.)

That said, fios could theoretically run on that same fiber.  Depends on if they buried enough of it to handle the bandwidth required for telephone service (small, typically 56-64K per concurrent connection) and the fios service (high bandwidth requirements for tv and data).

Jackson Res, as an alternative to switching to fios, you might want to have a tech come over and switch your modem. I had that done today after my internet had been out since Wednesday AM. The tech knew immediately what my problem was before he even got upstairs, and told me they remotely upgraded all the Arris modems and they've been having troubles since. He set up my modem and activated the router in the new modem so I don't need a separate wireless router anymore. I was getting at most 17 mb/s, and now I'm getting over 100, as advertised. On Thursday I was pricing fios packages, and now I find myself in the strange position of being pro TWC.
I hope this helps.
