I agree with nearly every point except that I don't think free parking should be eliminated.  Residents pay enough to live here, and paying for parking on your own street is just plain unfair.  Residents with addresses should get one permit per household for free (if they want it), then a limited number of additional permits can be for sale to non-residents (shop owners, for example) or others.  The solution to the traffic problems should not come out of the residents' pockets when there are so many other ways to alleviate the congestion.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Jackson Heights Neighborhood Retail Wish List
« on: February 05, 2008, 01:35:01 PM »
We really need a casual bar - good beer selection that invites a laid back but not old fogie crowd. A cross btwn Novo and Legends. or even a wine/cheese/microbrew beer/tapas place?  I know that's a lot to ask, but it sounds SO wonderful.  i would go every day!

A salad bar/health food place with smoothies, salads (not a germ filled salad bar, but a hale and hearty add-in type counter), soups, and organic products would surely do well - a place to get take out after work or stop in for a bite on weekends. There's a new one in sunnyside that is nice, but it doesn't have a full range of fresh foods.

I have no pets, but have heard that there is no good pet store/groomer/walking service for miles, so that could be lucrative.

There is a craft/quilting/sewing place in woodside called quilter's notion, so that might be too much competition (they have great classes, for anyone who is interested).

I think a book store is great except that they are very hard to keep in business with all the chains.  I love independant shops, but if I can get the book for $5 less on amazon, I'm more likely to buy it there.  I hate to admit it, but it's true (for me and for many, I think).
