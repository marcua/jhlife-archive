Thanks!

I also think you should get off at 74th St because I kind of think of it as one end of the neighborhood, so it will give you a place to start.  Walk up 74th St. (crowded; many Indian stores including Patel Brothers), and then take a right onto 37th Ave, which I think of as the "main drag" -- although there are others!  Walk a few blocks on 37th, stop into Espresso 77 on 77th just off 37th Ave. for a drink to fortify you on the rest of your journey.  Go back to 37th and continue on there for a few more blocks as far as the Post Office and Starbucks, which are between 78th and 79th Sts.  Take a left on 79th or another block nearby, and walk up one block to 35th Ave.

35th Ave. is much quieter and all residential.  Take a right and walk along a few blocks up to 86th. and take another left so you can see the beautiful garden homes between 35th and 34th Aves.  Take a left on 34th Ave (also all residential), go four blocks on 34th Ave to 82nd St. and then walk 3 long blocks all the way down to Roosevelt. And if you're tired already you can just get on the 7 train right there.

This will let you see the two main train stations, the two busiest side streets (74th and 82nd), allows you to walk several blocks on 37th - the main drag -- also allows you to get a feel for the quieter part of the neighborhood up in the mid-80's streets with the garden homes.  And you still get to have your espresso at espresso 77.  Of course there are many other options for eating and drinking but that would be a whole other thread.

By the way, Northern Blvd. is also a main drag - very commercial -  but since it's further from the subways I think it's not always considered to be that way.  

In terms of hearing about people's thoughts about the neighborhood, there are a couple of threads that might be worthwhile including the recent 10 Cool and 10 Suck things of JH 11372 and So, seriously, should I make the move to Jackson Heights with my family and Any LES or East Village Transplants Here?

Hi There,  yes, it is good to walk around Roosevelt and 37th Avenue to get a feel for the commercial side of the neighborhood but it can be crowded and overwhelming.  I suggest that you also walk up 35th Avenue and 34th Avenue to get an idea for the more quiet residential parts of the neighborhood.  34th Avenue is a little farther from the train but much more quieter (no bus).  There are beautiful buildings Chateau and Towers face each other on 34th, church, park, bike lane, farmers' market, and nice green median in road.  I would suggest making a big loop so that you see a little bit of everything.  Good luck and have fun.

We moved here from Park Slope in 2002 and, while we love our apartment and like a lot about our new neighborhood, there is a lot we miss about our old one. There isn't too much in the way of vegetarian fare (I can only hit the east asian restaurants a couple of times a month), Hispanic and Asian diaspora is pretty well covered, food wise, but not much else. It would be great if there were local restaurants more into the organic, local food thang.
The retail strips (82nd street between Roosevelt and 37th Ave./ 37th Ave. between 74th Street up to Junction and beyond/Roosevelt Ave ad infinitum) waver between national chains (mostly drug stores) and 99 cent shops. Lots of nail salons, cheap clothing stores etc. No book stores, no interesting little boites to stop in for a glass of wine and chocolate ganache, etc.

The buildings can be pretty wonderful, a lot of the floor plans did get changed around during the scary 70s and 80s. We're in a pre-war just outside the historic district, on 34th Ave. and it's quiet, safe and BIG....
