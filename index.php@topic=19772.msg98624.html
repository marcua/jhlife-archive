I don't think prepared food is considered an infection vector for viruses. Assuming the food has been thoroughly cooked, you're more likely to find active virus on the packaging it from the delivery person since the virus lives longer on non-porous surfaces and is most commonly spread person to person. All indications are that shopping in a store can be more hazardous than getting a delivery or picking up depending on how much close contact you have. Of course, they've changed their mind about this virus before and I don't blame anyone for having an abundance of caution. The Verge had a recent article about this: https://www.theverge.com/platform/amp/2020/3/17/21183919/food-take-out-delivery-order-safe-restaurants-coronavirus
