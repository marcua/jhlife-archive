Title: Re: Tax Filing Services
Post by: John Prester on April 08, 2008, 10:13:26 AM
Check on 77th Street, between 37th Ave. and Roosevelt.  There's a small house on the right hand side (when walking towards Roosevelt Ave.) that has a banner saying something about "free tax assistance".  I've seen a line outside the door a few times.  "Caveat emptor" - I don't know anything about them, what their intentions are, or how reliable they are. 

You can also do it free yourself on-line, check this page on the IRS website for list of providers that allow you to do your return for free, if you earn less than $54,000:

http://www.irs.gov/efile/article/0,,id=118986,00.html



Title: Re: Tax Filing Services
Post by: toddg on April 08, 2008, 10:28:23 AM
Some of the groups serving the immigrant community also provide free assistance, e.g. http://www.chhayacdc.org/

This is probably also a good time to mention:

1. If you have any elderly neighbors who may not ordinarily file taxes, please let them know that they need to file a tax return this year if they want to receive their federal economic stimulus check.

2. If you do know someone looking for free tax assistance, warn them away from scams that offer to provide their tax rebate in cash on the spot.  These are often loans at an extremely high interest rate.
