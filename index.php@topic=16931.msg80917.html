tingling fruit (mango)

I am reaching out to my neighbors hoping someone here knows something about this:

I bought sliced mango at Fruitadona, presumably cut and packaged at the store. It looked overripe but I needed to add to my purchase in order to use a credit card. When I bit into it, I experienced a tingling sensation. I've had this happen to me before when eating sliced fruits; melons, I recall, and maybe strawberries too. I had been told years ago that a preservative added to the fruit causes this sensation. I tried googling it, but came up with just food allergy, which it is not. Does anybody know what the heck I am eating? Maybe someone who's been in the food/restaurant business? There are no ingredients listed on the container.

Is it possibly not an additive but just what happens to tropical fruits as they get old?

Should I throw it out? (In the past I have always thrown it away.)

It's happened to me enough times that I really want to know.

A few theories:

If the fruit was overripe, they may be starting to ferment and that could cause a tingling sensation.

Sliced fruit is sometimes dusted with ascorbic acid to prevent browning. That's probably the preservative you were thinking of. The reaction of acid and water can cause fizzing. My guess is that you got a double-whammy with sliced fermenting fruit.

Allergies are also possible, depending on what you mean by tingling. I'm mildly allergic to mangoes, melons, papaya, and kiwi--- I can still eat them, but they make my mouth and throat feel itchy and numb.

Mango skin is related to poison ivy. http://web.extension.illinois.edu/cfiv/homeowners/120726.html

If the mango still has little specks of skin on it, as in not 100% peeled, and you have this allergy, you will get the tingling and swelling sensation.

http://web.extension.illinois.edu/cfiv/homeowners/120726.html
