Good Buildings in the low 70s?
« on: March 26, 2009, 06:09:31 PM »
Hi,

I'm hoping to move into the JH area. I'm looking for a good building in the low 70s, close to Roosevelt. My husband will be using the LIRR at 61st to commute out to Long Island, and so we'd like to be close enough that he could walk there. It doesn't have to be in the historic district but I'd like a place that will hold its value pretty well, but allows sublets. I'm also open to a Woodside address though I'm under the impression that Jackson Heights Co-ops tend to hold their values better (though I could be wrong).

Also do landmarked buildings tend to hold their value better than unlandmarked buildings?

Any advice? Thanks so much!

I like both the building and the area.  It's the Berkeley, located at 3965 52nd Street in Woodside off of Skillman Street.  Its actually a complex of three buildings but the one on 52nd Street is Independent of the other two and is self managed.  Its beautifully maintained and has Evening Doorman service although the maintenance is very reasonable and includes electricity & gas.  I recently looked at a one bedroom (750SF) with a maint of $477. The LIRR is just a 10 minute walk.  The Berkeley is actually in Woodside but just two blocks from Sunnyside.  You should take a look at the building and area.  The building has 11 Floors with nicely maintained grounds which includes benches for owners.  A large Park a block away with Running Track.    Skillman St has lots of interesting restaurants including a French Pastry Coffee Shop.  The area has a small town atmosphere.

Technically, the Historic District starts in the upper 70s and runs to the mid 80s. (Landmaks Committee map here: http://www.cb3qn.nyc.gov/?p=29789) However, there are many classic 30s buildings just west of the historic district in the low-mid 70s, built at the same time as many buildings in the district and with similar appeal.

If you're talking about walking distance to the 74th St express trains (a reason I chose this area) you might look at Filldor Court, Jac Towers (where I live) both on 72nd Street, the Myfair and Surrey on 73rd Street and The Salem on 37th Avenue. The Salem is certanly the closest to the subway, although the street area in front of the building can get very busy on the weekends with weekend shoppers in the area - check it out first.  If you're looking to rent instead of buy, Washington Plaza is a very well-maintained rental complex with a beautifully restored fountain in the center courtyard.

Just inside the district, the Berkeley, and Jackson House on 77th are still very close to the subway.

Best of luck in your searching!
