new article about Edible Queens

Hey, we're tasty, too, sez editor of new food mag
BY Clare Trapasso
Tuesday, December 1st 2009

FOOD lovers can look forward to an early Christmas gift.

A new quarterly magazine, Edible Queens, chronicles the borough's thriving food scene. And its creator hopes it will help put the borough on the map as a food destination on a par with Manhattan and Brooklyn.

"People in New York ignore Queens," said editor, publisher and food lover Leah McLaughlin. But, she added, "I actually think there's a lot more passion and emotion in the food in Queens."

The second issue is to be published Dec. 16.

McLaughlin said she rarely ventured into Queens, except occasionally to Jackson Heights for spices, until she moved to Long Island City about three years ago.

"The minute I moved into Queens, the city opened up again," McLaughlin said. "What makes Queens food special are the people that prepare it. They're much more concerned with paying homage to where they come from."

She decided in January to launch the magazine, three months before she lost her job as an editor at Prevention magazine, she said.

She had heard of Edible Communities, a national network of about 60 magazines - including Edible Manhattan and Edible Brooklyn - that helps editors set up their own publications, and believed it was a good fit.

In September, she distributed 20,000 copies of her free first issue to borough restaurants and businesses. It was such a success that McLaughlin hopes to eventually double the number of copies printed.

The winter edition will feature stories that include a culinary shopping tour of Astoria, how to make the perfect cup of coffee and recipes for Lunar New Year.

McLaughlin said she'd also like to start supper clubs in Astoria, Flushing and Corona. She envisions them as get-togethers where local celebrities or historians talk about the local culture and history and how that ties into the area's food.
