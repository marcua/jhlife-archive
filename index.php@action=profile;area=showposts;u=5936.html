Neighborhood Chat / Push for a Q32 extension from Northern to Astoria Boulevard
« on: August 06, 2013, 03:14:41 PM »
Good afternoon,

Concerns have repeatedly been brought to us about the need for direct Manhattan bus service from the Northern part of Jackson Heights which lost it with the discontinuation of the QM22 in June 2010. What has also been addressed to us is that the MTA is looking to cut the frequency of Q33 bus service next month (and cut the Q33 back from LaGuardia Airport as well) because they are creating a new airport bus from the Roosevelt Avenue Subway, called the Q70 which is going to run via the BQE and Grand Central. There will be reduced service along the 82nd Street corridor and coupled with the loss of direct Manhattan bus service from North Jackson Heights in June 2010, the best way to improve service on 82nd Street in North Jackson Heights is to have the Q32 bus extended from Northern Boulevard and 82nd Street to Astoria Boulevard and 82nd Street. We have already been told that Senator Peralta likes the idea and is interested in being helpful with us and this effort. Feel free to sign the petition in the link below:

http://www.gopetition.com/petitions/extend-q32-bus-into-north-jackson-heights.html



P.S. Our petition to bring a Metro-North station to Western Queens was a success as we got a story published in the Daily News. Right now we are still waiting to see if Metro-North's Penn Station access plan would go through as getting a station in Queens is contingent on the approval of that plan by the State Legislature where Republican state senators from Long Island are reluctant to approve the plan.

Neighborhood Chat / Our efforts to get JH an Express Bus in both directions for once
« on: December 18, 2011, 06:26:22 AM »
Hello JH'ers,

Recently one of your fellow JH'ers suggested that our efforts in Jackson Heights should be concentrated in making improvements to the QM3 Express Bus as the MTA has shown willingness to revise existing bus routes to make up for discontinued bus routes. Currently the QM3 only serves Jackson Heights in one direction only heading towards Douglaston during the PM rush. The bus stops that the QM3 serves are not even marked. One idea that has been proposed to us by your fellow JH'ers is to split the QM3 into two. Given that the QM3 runs to and from Douglaston, which is walking distance to the Nassau County border and serves Little Neck, Bayside, Auburndale, and Flushing as well. The QM3 would be split into two, with one route serving Douglaston, Little Neck, Bayside, Auburndale, and  Flushing; with the other route serving the Jackson Heights/East Elmhurst/Corona area. The Express Bus would operate on Northern Boulevard since that is where the QM3 currently operates along.

We are doing a petition drive in JH for this starting this week and we also have our online petition that you could sign as well. If you signed our QM22 petition over the summer, it is encouraged that you sign this petition as this is separate from the QM22. Here is the link to our petition:

http://www.change.org/petitions/mta-bus-company-local-elected-officials-and-local-civic-associations-provide-express-bus-from-jackson-heights-to-manhattan#

We also need volunteers to help us out in the neighborhood, whether you want to help us and be out there and petition or if you want to help us and join us with our political efforts. Feel free to email bounadhanhic@yahoo.com.

For you references here is a QM3 schedule

http://www.mta.info/busco/schedules/qm003cur.pdf


We look forward to working in JH to help make an Express Bus in both directions to Manhattan a reality and thank all of you in advance for your support.
