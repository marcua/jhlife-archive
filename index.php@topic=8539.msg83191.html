I can't comment on JH cell service but I can say that I've had great success using an AT&T Microcell device to provide excellent cell service where there was practically none before. You can buy one from AT&T, although sometimes in the past they gave them to people who had a terrible signal. But I got two of them on eBay for about half price; they're also common on Craigslist. The key, in buying a used one, is to make sure the seller has deactivated it; if not you won't be able to activate it on your account. These units rely on your Internet service, so you'll need a broadband connection, but I assume you have that. Verizon has their own version as well.

I'm guessing that this isn't as much of an issue anymore.  I still know that Verizon has pretty bad reception in our building, and AT&T is bad, but at least it's better than Verizon, but the reason why this matters much less is because of wifi calling.

If you have a smartphone, the chances are that one of the options the phone gives you is to transmit your cell phone call through your home wifi when the cellular signal is less than optimal.  The phone is then supposed to switch back and forth, if needed, so that your call does not disconnect.

This has certainly solved any issues I had with AT&T.  I enter my apartment and put my phone in a location where it switches to wifi calling.  I have not had an issue since that time.
