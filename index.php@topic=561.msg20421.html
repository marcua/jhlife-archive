I stopped into the new salad bar at around 5 p.m. today. The food did not look fresh AT ALL.
I think it is pretty telling that even though we are in a New York neighborhood, you can't even get a decent salad here. I have lived in Jackson Heights for two years, and I cannot understand how the neighborhood got its reputation for being such a great place to eat. It is known in Manhattan as being "exotic," I guess, but who wants to eat Indian and Latin food every day? I am Italian, and I don't want to eat Italian every day.
There are no decent Chinese restaurants in JH, and I have gone to Elmhurst, and went to Great Wealth and it was disgusting.
None of the restaurants that I have been to - and I have been to a lot of them - have any kind of good atmosphere, although Arunee Thai has great food. You cannot even find a good hamburger around here.
I think it is also pretty telling that when people are looking for a "nice" restaurant to go to, people tell them Novo.
And when people are looking for a decent bar, some answers tell them to go to that sad, sad Penny place.
This neighborhood is lacking in so many things - it has a lot of potential. While rents are relatively cheap, at least by NYC standards, it is still, overall, an expensive place to live, and it would be nice to not have to go to another neighborhood for things like a good burger, or a decent breakfast that isn't at a greasy spoon.
