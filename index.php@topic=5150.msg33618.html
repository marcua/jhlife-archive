Does anyone know of a "green" cleaner in the area?   That means one  using the carbon dioxide cleaning process.

Some dry cleaners advertise themselves as organic when they actually use a hydro-carbon solvent called DF-2000. While it's not as toxic as PERC, which the EPA lists as a neurotoxin and a skin and eye irritant to workers who are exposed to it, it is petroleum-based and thus contributes to global warming.

I got this info from http://www.thedailygreen.com/going-green/tips/735#ixzz0xe1jKfxm
