So....because I haven't been able to watch either the Knicks or Rangers since the first of the year AND because they've started charging me near 150 a month for standard Roadrunner and 1 cable box with basic service and no movie channels I wanted to dump Time Warner.  I was about to place an order for Verizon/DirectTV when  I was informed by my super that he could not provide roof access to the installer because my building management company only allows Time Warner in this building and has had this policy in place for many years.   How is it possible that I can SEE Direct TV dishes on the roof next to me, but I'm not allowed to switch? Has anyone else had this problem? I'm honestly pretty aggravated that my building management company can ban me from selecting the TV provider I want. 

I wonder if Time Warner knew this when I called them up...it would totally explain the pay up or f**k off attitude they gave me when I called because my promotional pricing had expired.

It may be some work on your part, but if you get the whole building involved, then they should be able to allow other providers. We were all going back and forth for a year about getting fios, and finally the building made adjustments so we were eligible.
