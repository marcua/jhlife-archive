 We have had at least two people die in our building who lived alone and were not found right away. Both were private people
but not anti-social by any means. Dying alone can just mean that you live by yourself and were not expecting visitors or
deliveries around that time which produces the circumstance in which Mr. Bell was found. That he was hoarder and drifted away
from society over the years is a more tragic story to me. We currently have someone who I could see this story being written
about again in a few years as she is seeming to follow this trajectory. It is difficult to intercept the social decline without
seeming to interfere with someone's privacy even though you suspect issues. I do believe that a number of seniors are,
in fact, alone when they pass away.
 We have a senior who throws away delivered meals as she doesn't care for the food but loves the company of the delivery
person. The food waste bothers me to no end but I know the visits are a joy to her. I hear often in conversation mocking
comments about "old people". I find them fascinating and have for years. There was program during the Carter years to
visit seniors and they paid a dollar an hour and another agency would fill in the rest. Friends and I loved these visits and
learned so much from our wise ones.
  Hopefully, this article will help deliver more senior services to Jackson Heights which is lacking compared to other parts of the
city.

Talking about what goes on in my life does not make my day any better, nor does it pay my bills.
