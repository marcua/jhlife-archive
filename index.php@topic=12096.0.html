After a particular good week on the local chow circuit, it felt like a good time to lay out a few of my personal favorites in the hopes that some of you might discover a new place AND in the hopes that you might have a few recommendations of your own. 

HIMALAYAN YAK--Gyuma (blood sausage), Momos and especially the Kickstarter.
UNIDENTIFIED FLYING CHICKEN--well, yeah, chicken.  But also the best local beer selection.
PHAYUL--Probably my favorite local restaurant.  Pretty much everything.  But the momos are the best around.
RICO TINTO--great breakfasts, guacamole, fish tacos, cafe con leche
AYADA--best Thai food around.  Honorable mention to Arunee (extra points for proximity) and Zabb Elee
COSITAS RICAS--24 hour availability is key.  But great soup, Cuban sandwiches and breakfast.
LA FUSTA--Skirt Steak, spinach, broccoli, pasta, vibe--slight edge over Boina Roja
TIA JULIA--truck on Roosevelt and 92nd.  Best tacos in a neighborhood with many great choices
TACOS MORELOS--best and most varied mole choices in town.
PATA PAPLEAN--Thai bar across from Ayada.  Drinks and vibe are great but surprisingly good bar food, as well

and you?  What are your favorites, least favorites, wish list?  One thing for sure--Jackson Heights (and Elmhurst) is very likely the most varied and best food neighborhood in NYC.

Is a dish eligible if I can't remember its name?

Arunee has a new dish on its menu that's essentially a version of a Burmese salad, although they don't call it that.  These salads are an intense mix of different flavors and textures -- some soft, something dry and crunchy, some salty, some spicy.   Arunee's is very good, but unfortunately doesn't appear on the takeout menu so I can't look up what it's called there.   

Crazy Crab in College Point has the two varieties of this I've seen elsewhere, "ginger salad" and "tea salad," but I haven't had a chance to check them out yet.

Ayada -- pretty much everything, but the beef nom tok soup with noodles (listed in the noodle dishes) is something we get every time. The papaya salad with some form of seafood is one of the good rather hot bites around.
Taqueria Coatzingo -- tacos, of course (partial to al pastor-also like the platter -- and lengua), cemitas, posole.
Thinking of it, we're blessed to be in a good soup neighborhood. The above two establishments for starters and that doesn't even get to Phayul. The beef noodle one at Thai Son on 74th also.
Ru Yi -- Is it up at the level of the top Sichuan places elsewhere? No. But everything we've had so far has been solid and it's nice to have that style of regional Chinese cuisine available in our part of the neighborhood.
Unidentified Flying Chicken, as mentioned, for both the chicken and beer.
The roast chicken at Pio Pio.
Louie's for pizza. Usually we get Grandma or Sicilian with extra pepperoni.
Arunee - if not quite Ayada, it's still solid and its closer proximity is a plus.
For dessert -- carrot cake from Lety's (or any number of their other desserts except cookies, actually).

Bonus elsewhere in Queens: Octopus chickpea salad at MP Taverna, on Ditmars, not far from the end of the N/Q line. One of our favorites all-around.
Bonus when having to go to Manhattan: Pretty much everything we've had at Black Tree, a place on Orchard just off Delancey that builds its core menu around one different local protein each week - pork, duck, lamb, seafood, beef, rabbit, etc. Cool vibe and very good beverages.

I'm a newcomer to the neighborhood but the standouts so far:

- UFC's Soy Garlic wings. Fairly typical Korean-style fried chicken which means it's typically awesome.
- Thai Son's pho.
- Arunee's beef noodle soup. Possibly the best thing you can do for a hangover.
- Kung Fu Bubble Tea.  Sweetened bubbles make this the perfect drink to binge and crash on.
- Eim Khao Mun Kai's chicken and rice. I hope you like garlic.
- Zabb Elbee's papaya salad. It comes five ways. I tried the crab last night. Marinated raw crab. Excellent stuff.
