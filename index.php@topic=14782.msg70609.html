My boyfriend and I went to Oceanic Boil tonight for dinner.  Here's my initial take:

Atmosphere:
It's a cute place--they've clearly put a lot of time and energy into making it a unique environment.  Yes, there are two TVs (as has been debated at length in the thread), but they are unobtrusive and the sound is muted.  There are light fixtures with edison bulbs, custom metalwork chairs (more comfortable than they look!), and colorful paint work. The waitstaff is friendly--you can tell that they are eager, and they care.  The soundtrack was Shakira, turned down low.  It's a crab-feed kind of joint, so they put down kraft paper on the table and hand out gloves and crab crackers.  You'll need both. 

Food:
They are launching with a limited menu--no raw bar, so far.  We asked the proprietor, and he said that they hope to get oysters and other raw bar items in soon, but that sourcing is fairly involved.  Right now, they have  the appetizers and the cooked items.  We got the fried calamari, the snow crab, the shrimp, and the fries.  The fried calamari was delicious and disappeared quite rapidly.  The shrimp are served shell-on, head-on--simple, perfectly cooked--with drawn butter.  The snow crab was two sets of legs.  They were delicious, though quite a bit of work to crack through to get the meat out.  The fries were fine, but really nothing to write home about.  They are served with hot sauce. 

Drink:
The beer list is limited, at least so far.  There are a couple of domestics, Heineken, Sapporo, and Guinness.  There are some wines, and a plum wine.  Sapporo works well with the food, as does the plum wine. 

Conclusion:
It's definitely a work in progress, but a great work in progress.  I think they need to make it clear that you'll be working for your dinner--it's a casual crabfeed joint, not a take-out or delivery place, and not a place where dinner comes served up neatly on a plate.  Heck, there aren't knives, forks, or spoons!  A cool concept and a unique execution.  I hope the neighborhood will give it a shot!

Just dined in JH's newest restaurant, Oceanic Boil.  Providing an alternative cuisine to our typical chicken joints, this industrial chic spot offers baskets of fried seafood and fries, including shrimp, oysters and catfish.  And in coming days, they will offer an array of raw oysters.  Both the shrimp and oysters were expertly fried and coated with a well seasoned batter. French fries are the best in the neighborhood.  Though the menu is limited, I give the very cordial owners high marks for bringing a new dining concept to the neighborhood.  They do serve beer and wine.  Alas, no desserts yet. I suggest they introduce house made beignets to the menu and perhaps some New Orleans style coffee.  Hopefully,in the coming days, the owners will fine tune the menu and service.  Give it a try.
