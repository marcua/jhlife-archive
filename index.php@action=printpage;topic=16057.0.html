Title: Kitchen reno by Corrigan Services (highly recommended)
Post by: knatchez on February 26, 2017, 07:52:25 PM
If you’re thinking of doing work in your apartment, you should give Eddie Corrigan a call first. We recently finished a gut renovation of our kitchen and could not be happier with the result. We had several unique challenges—we moved a steam pipe, updated the electrical, and combined Ikea and custom cabinetry—Eddie handled it all with total professionalism at very fair price.

Looking back, we wish he had also done our bathroom.

Eddie is licensed and insured. His crew is polite and tidy (as far as any reno is tidy). And he knows the buildings. He works all around Jackson Heights, especially the pre-war and garden co-ops.

What I think really sets Eddie a cut above other contractors is how flexible and adaptable he is. We had the design plan, but when problems came up—and they always come up—he finds creative solutions and doesn’t gouge you for unexpected costs.

Title: Re: Kitchen reno by Corrigan Services (highly recommended)
Post by: knatchez on February 27, 2017, 08:02:49 AM
The steam riser was split in two with the main riser being set into the wall, and a capped pipe run around the stove to the back wall in the event a future owner wants to connect a small radiator. The pipe is exposed where it comes up through the floor, so we had the ventilated door on the bottom row custom made to prevent the heat from building up inside and warping the wood.

Our dumbwaiter is still in use, alas. Otherwise we would have looked at doing something similar. Although I actually think that the shaft would be good for running the conduits for wall-mounted air conditioners—the ones with exterior compressors. But, of course, you have to sell the whole building on that plan.

I also had used Corrigan Services by Eddie Corrigan for some renovation work in our apartment, and unfortunately, I had a completely different experience compared to others who had provided positive comments here. I don't think there is a perfect contractor out there. My advice is to be sure to get quotes from different contractors and go with your gut instinct vs. relying solely on reviews from others.  I recommend getting everything in writing (which means unfortunately that in most cases you do the writing yourself and have the contractor cosign). Be firm when contractors do not complete tasks that had been outlined in the signed work proposal by pointing that out and making sure those things are being done. I recommend touching base with the contractor more than once a day - better if in the morning, middle of the day, and end of the day. Communication is the key.

Title: Re: Kitchen reno by Corrigan Services (highly recommended)
Post by: Miss Chatelaine on October 16, 2017, 01:27:22 PM
I’m happy to be able to add another positive recommendation for Eddie Corrigan, who renovated my kitchen recently.

It was my first time renovating so there was a steep learning curve, but Eddie was patient with my lack of knowledge (not to mention excess anxiety) and helpful every step of way. I never got the feeling that he took advantage of my inexperience. 

The job was pretty straight forward with the exception of my walls--evidently the plaster lathe walls in my kitchen are bowed in assorted spots around various pipes, but they did a remarkable job of getting all the cabinets to hang evenly, flush with each other and flush with the wall all along. (Now I understand why the old cabinets had bizarre random gaps everywhere, including between the wall and cabinets). In addition to looking good/normal, I’m confident they are all secure and I can load them up.

One bowed wall also presented a challenge for the counter but Eddie had them use a scribe when they measured so the counters sit beautifully flush all along as well. He also took extra care with the backsplash installation so the spacing between tiles would remain even. I’m not sure how any other contractor would’ve handled this wonky wall situation, but it seemed to me I was reaping huge dividends from his experience and integrity (and also the high quality of his craftsmen and team.)

Speaking of walls and quality, they also skim coated (and painted) a hallway ceiling and wall, and OMG what a revelation….I had no idea regular plaster walls (even good condition ones) looked so bad until I saw the magic of skim coating. I can’t say I would have it done on every wall in my apartment, but I sure am looking forward to having it done in select other places.

Hot2015 makes some good suggestions above as now I can see clearly how important it is to connect in some way with one’s contractor. Also, if I ever renovate something again I would quadruple the level of details on my lists, at minimum. I wouldn’t hesitate to hire Eddie Corrigan again though as I really appreciated his high standards and experience.
