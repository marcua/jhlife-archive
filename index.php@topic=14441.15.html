It also makes sense that these people are in Jackson Heights or Flushing since most of them don't speak fluent English, want to be near those who speak the same language or are the same culture, and work nearby or need the public transportation options. They are the people we take for granted: the ones who serve the food, bus the tables, cook the food, make deliveries, work the cashiers, shelve the supermarkets, and do the manual labor. I know a house nearby me that was sold, and the basement was cleared out; it had partitions and 8 South Asians were living there.

But even if you're not low wage labor...my friend has a decent-paying professional job and he still does a roomshare in a 2-family house that has over 10 individuals living in it. Everyone is just trying to live to see another day, and hopefully come of it for the better...or for their family to experience better.
