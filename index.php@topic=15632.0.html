Quote from: Rhino on November 11, 2016, 02:23:46 PM
Which is true, but why is the fine significantly higher than for a motorist?

It's not, actually.  The catch is you don't get the full fine/points on your license unless you're actually given a ticket by a cop. The red light cameras are $50 to the owner of the car, since they can't prove who was driving it at the time.  Otherwise, a ticket for running a light can run $100.00 to $300.00 for a first offense not including the NY State $88.00 surcharge.

That said, that cop probably needed to make their nonexistent quota and stopping cyclists on 34th Ave is going to be hella easier than doing it on 37th where it's really needed.
