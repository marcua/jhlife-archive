Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

First off I should say Iâ€™m not so much learning the language as learning the script. Although Bangla vocabulary clearly comes with the territory, most of the signs are surprisingly in transliterated English. I have always been a script and typography enthusiast, as well as an armchair linguist. I had also started to notice similarities between Near Eastern cultures and South Asian cultures, especially, not surprisingly, where the two geographically touched, liked Afghani and Pakistani cultures. Indian movie musicals are also very popular in Near Eastern and North African communities, and I love movie musicals in general, so I began to start recognizing Hindi and Urdu words from hearing them repeatedly in songs. My knowledge of the Arabic/Persian script allowed me to read and write the Urdu words, many of which were of Persian or Arabic origin. My contact with and subsequent love for Urdu led me to take some interest in the South Asian scripts and how they worked, but I never really learned one.

Then I moved to Jackson Heights, and I was in the unusual situation of being in a largely Muslim community but not being able to read their signs. (I spent a fair amount of time in Egypt and Morocco, so knowing the Arabic script was quite helpful then, but useless for Bangla.) Bangla is closely related to Hindi and Urdu, so I decided not to let the daunting South Asian scripts frighten me, and I dove right in and bought dictionaries at Butala Emporium and started reading online. Now I love it.
