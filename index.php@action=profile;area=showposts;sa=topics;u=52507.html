Restaurants & Food / CAFE 75--the local bar you've been craving
« on: July 11, 2016, 11:09:13 AM »
I know there's been a lot of lamenting on this site about Jackson Heights not having a good local bar that's conducive to couples or even women on their own.  Turns out it's been there all along and I've been hanging out there a lot more recently.  Really friendly, welcoming vibe, lots of seating and comfortable booths, laid-back, fun, great music.  My wife and I got there often and she has been there as well with other female pals as well and always had a good time.  On first glance from both outside and when you first walk in it might seem like just another Roosevelt Avenue bar for dudes to ogle waitresses in miniskirts (there's some of that as well at C-75 but it's not imposing in any way), but it truly stands apart from the others.  Try it out one night--I think you might be pleasantly surprised.  And, if you're hungry, drop by either Spicy Tibet or Aroma Brazil for chow--both on the same block and both under-the-radar great.

Neighborhood Chat / BAR
« on: February 05, 2015, 09:37:26 AM »
Been enjoying the flurry of discussions about what can/can't/will/won't work in Jackson Heights in general and the Bruson Building in particular.  Who knows?   We might be just spinning our wheels but it could also focus attention and inspire some ideas that can work.   

I think that there is room and demand for a good, local bar.  Don't get me wrong--I often frequent the superb Terraza 7 but that's more of a lure for live music and not always an easy place to just hang out and talk (small, minimal seating).  My wife and I also go to Cafe 75 and the Ready Penny but they are both mostly suited for watching sports and the latter cranks a little too much metal and 80's power ballad schlock for my taste. 

And Lord knows there are a plethora of Gentlemen's Bars up and down Roosevelt.  No shortage of those.

Most of those bars (well, Terraza excepted) are more geared to a male clientele.  I wonder where, for example, a woman would feel comfortable and welcome grabbing a beer on her own after work.  Would there be a demand for a place that would fit that bill? 

Seems like the neighborhood could sustain a bar that was geared less towards sports and TV and more towards a good beer selection, an exciting and varied music selection and an all-around good, non-dude-centric hang.

And, hey, Dudley's joint just might be just that place!  Can't wait for it to open.

And, in practical terms, how difficult do you all think this would be in terms of zoning and cost?  And would it be popular?

In the meantime, our neighborhood is fantastic, maybe the best neighborhood in the 5 boroughs, and it's a daily joy to live here.

Restaurants & Food / How About a Great, Local Food Court
« on: January 28, 2015, 03:32:32 AM »
All of the talk of what could or should go into the Bruson Building, all of the talk of what we'd all like to see in Jackson Heights, and all of the talk about what could manage to pay the high, local rents--how about an amazing, truly local, non-chain food court worthy of the diversity and great food offerings of our neighborhood.  Imagine!  Our own version of Smorgasburg or even the Flushing Golden Mall but instead with, for example, a dozen different kiosks offering the best momos, arepas, tacos, dosas, thalis, halal items, Indian sweets and much more.  The vendors could combine on the high rent and other costs and not only would it provide a service for the neighborhood (including a shared seating area for large and small groups) but also would be a nice lure for visitors, a way of showing some of the things--diversity, great food, friendly community spirit--that we offer in a way that makes Jackson Heights so special.  Take the extra care to make it look great, friendly and inviting, great music (representing all of our local residents).  Seems like something that could work.  What do you all think?
