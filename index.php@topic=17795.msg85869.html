My mom received a call earlier today from someone posing as my nephew.  The caller claimed that he had been in an auto accident while driving in New Jersey, and was now in an Atlantic City jail for DUI.  The poser also left an accomplice's number for call back purposes.  This second person stated that he's an attorney, and that for $2300, can post bail for my nephew. He was rather eager to get a debit card number.  Hmmmm....  This is the second time this type of scammer tries to pull this stunt on my family.  Has anyone else experienced this?  Btw, a quick call to my nephew confirmed that he's OK.

My stepmother got a call from someone claiming to be her "oldest grandson" and in some kind of trouble. My stepmother (figuring it was a scam) demanded to know the name of this oldest grandson. A name was not forthcoming, so she hung up.

Another time she was shopping at CVS for some gift cards, and the cashier insisted on finding out who she planned to give the gift cards to. A bit taken aback, she explained the gift cards were for the giving tree at church, where you choose a needy family to give to. The cashier was relieved and explained that those particular gift cards she was buying are popular with the grandparent scam (with the grandparent being tricked into buying gift cards for the person who turns out to be the scammer), and possibly other scams as well.

The senior center in my parents' town gives workshops on scams for older people to avoid. I wonder if those are available here, and if people would attend.

I recently noticed a video on the NYPD site that is in Mandarin, subtitled in English, where the Mandarin-speaking cop explains a new phone scam that is targeting Chinese residents. In fact, I've gotten several robo calls lately in Chinese, but since I can't understand a word, I just hang up. Maybe scammers are calling!

Their goal is to trick you into sending money before you realize it’s a scam.
