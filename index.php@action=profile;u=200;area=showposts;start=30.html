Aged Salice Salentino!

This week we are featuring a dynamic red from Puglia (the heel of Italy’s boot). Cosimo Taurino’s family has been making wines using the ancient grapes of southern Italy for seven generations. His Salice Salentino is made from a blend of Negroamaro and Malvasia, which is aged at the winery until it is ready to drink.

The “current” vintage is from 2010 and shows a wonderful balance of earthy, spicy and fruit flavors. It's a wonderful opportunity to savor a wine that has been allowed to properly age, a luxury which has disappeared for most wine drinkers in our modern age.

The Queensboro will be featuring the 2010 Salice Salentino by the glass starting Wednesday night. Because of its balance, it matches a range of food - pizza, burgers, pastas- but we especially like it with Chef Tony’s Double-Cut Pork Chop with grilled peaches… delish! Come on down and try and glass- we love it and think you will too!

Not only are we offering this wine by the glass today, but if you get a bottle we'll knock 25% off the price, and as the Italians from Puglia say, "that's a heel of a deal!"

Thursday at 8:30 - the Teej Trio perform!

We have another new local band for everyone to enjoy this Thursday!

TJ Adorno is a local guitarist and song writer from Jackson Heights, Queens. He has been active throughout New York's creative music scene since 2014, performing in genres such as: Jazz, Hip-Hop, Neo Soul, Rock, and more. Also known as TEEJ, he is the frontman and band leader of his projects The SidePocket and TeeJ Tr3o. For more information, booking, or other inquiries, visit teejmusic.com.

Beer and Cheese!


On Tuesday September 24th, at 8:30PM Cheese doesn’t have to Stand Alone!

Hello cheese lovers! We’re always excited when cool new neighbors move in, so we were really thrilled when Steffen Kenney opened Stand Alone Cheese in Jackson Heights.

This gem of a store not only sells some amazing cheeses, but also has bread (some of it from the baked-at-the-Q Little Wildbranch Bakery), charcuterie, ice cream, exotic canned goods, a friendly approachable attitude and much more!

We want you to meet these guys, so we’re hosting a Stand Alone Cheese Night here at The Queensboro.

On Tuesday, September 24th at 8:30pm, Steffen will lead a curated tasting of fine cheeses from his shop, each paired with some of our fresh, delicious beers. Five cheeses, five beers and lots of wisdom, all for $25 per person. 

Seating will be limited so we strongly recommend booking in advance.

Leave us a note saying "CHEESE!" in the reservation so we know you're booking a space for this event.

If you can’t make the tasting, fear not! We’ll be offering a Stand Alone Cheese plate the rest of the week (or until it sells out).  What’s more all of the cheeses from the tasting are available for purchase at Stand Alone Cheese, 79-07 37th Avenue.

A little more about Steffen:

Steffen's journey into the world of cheese began 20 years ago, as a student in Philadelphia. A part-time job at Downtown Cheese in the Reading Terminal Market turned into a full-time passion. Steffen was drawn to the diversity of cheeses from around the world and the stories behind them. He enjoyed how discussions about cheese often transformed into conversations about life.

After moving to New York, Steffen work at local institutions Ideal Cheese and Artisanal Fromagerie and Bistro. He also guided food and beer tours throughout the city. Steffen dreamed of opening a shop in Queens to provide quality cheese and specialty products to an underserved part of the city.

When his friends at Table Wine  encouraged him to check out 37th Avenue, Steffen knew that he had found just the place: an amazing community hungry for cheese, and on June 1, 2019, he opened Stand Alone Cheese in Jackson Heights.[/size]

Wino Wednesday Continues!

Everyone loves a cool glass of white or rosé on a summer day, but have you thought about switching it up with a chilled summer red wine?  Cooler temperatures accentuate the wine’s natural fruit profile while tamping down the alcohol.  It's perfect with pizza or grilled meats or just to quaff with some small nibbles, all while keeping your cool.
 
We’re pouring a lovely Dolcetto with a nice chill this summer at The Queensboro and on Wino Wednesday this week, we’ll be offering it will a cool price tag as well - $6 a glass all night long. Its a chance to try something new and maybe put a different tint on your summer wine drinking.
