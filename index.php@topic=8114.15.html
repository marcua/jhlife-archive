The malling of Flushing Meadow
« Reply #16 on: September 14, 2013, 04:43:42 PM »
Big shopping malls have a long history of killing nearby business areas. The one proposed for Flushing Meadow Corona Park will suck the life out to downtown Flushing, perhaps stealing away Macy’s. More on this near the bottom of this post.

The developers really think the people of Queens are rubes. They say shoppers attracted to the mall will bypass the foodcourt and seek out restaurants in Corona and Jackson Heights, sort of like Lincoln Center patrons strolling across Broadway to eat at local restaurants. What a joke.

All the transportation improvements discussed by the developers at last week’s public hearing were road improvements. Queens does not need more auto-centric development.

The rest of NYC and cities around much of the world are realizing smart transit and pedestrian travel is what good urban spaces are all about. The developers are serving Queens some warmed over 1960s suburban development.

There’s also some kind of a deal going with this proposed BID extension. It's supposed to go up to 114th Street, but I suspect as a trump card to get the mall approved they will talk about running the BID across the GCP saying mall funding for the BID will bring all kinds of benefits to the Roosevelt Avenue.

AS FOR FLUSHING - The mall developers need to get some big anchor stores and the new Sky View Center mall that opened up a short distance away on Roosevelt already has a BJs, Target, Best Buy, Bed Bath & Beyond, Marshalls, Old Navy, Bob's Discount Furniture. So what’s left

Stealing Macy’s away with the promise of easy parking would have to be in the mall plans. Sandwiched between Corona and Flushing, this mall is not going to get a Neiman-Marcus or anything upscale. The last upscale mall that opened in Queens, Atlas Park, failed.
