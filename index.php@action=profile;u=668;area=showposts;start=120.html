Neighborhood Chat / Re: 10 cool & 10 suck things of JH 11372
« on: June 22, 2009, 01:06:12 PM »
I heard awhile back that the bakery on the corner of 37th and 85th? was applying to get an outside seating cafe  liscence. The process takes at least four months, and this was about 6 months ago. The rules and regulations are daunting, and about ten pages long. The requirements are quite stringent, and i dont think most restaurants on 37th ave would qualify. The sidewalks are cleary wide enough to meet approval, and im sure 37th ave is zoned for it, but thats the least of it. I think the cost involved prohibits alot of these places from serving food outside. Rent has to be paid to the city, and extra insurance needs to be had for each seat. i dont think their can be outside seating on the side streets, because they are zoned residential. Also, i dont think most of the existing restaurants are wide enough to meet standards. I think most restaurants that have outdoor seating on city/public property, existed before the Zoning laws were changed in 1980. Its much more difficult to put tables and chairs outside these days

Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: plumber hunt
« on: June 22, 2009, 12:26:43 PM »
I don't want to say who not to use, but i had someone who was recommended on this board check out a leaky toilet that was leaking from the second floor bathroom.  I wasn't to happy. He said the kitchen ceiling had to be broken to fix the leaky pipe. Uh Oh, does that mean breaking parts of the 100 year old tiled wall as well? Yup, but he said "you could find the same 100 year old tiles on e bay or craigslist."
  HA Ha. Ha
 i asked how do you know  where the leak is coming from? His answer was the pipes are 100 years old, and that's what it was. And he told me he has been doing plumbing for the past 30 years. Yup, he said "that's where its coming from."  No Doubt.       Hmmmmm.
   And i asked "how much would i get to pay you to do  this?"  $1800. Nice.... And that's not including repairing the damaged walls and ceilings.  I had serious doubts. I spoke to a friend who is a super.  "Wax Ring." I said what? "Wax Ring."  He came over, we lifted the toilet, and  yes, the 100 year old wax ring was indeed decomposed. We went to that great little hardware store, on 88th and 37th, A $10 wax ring, and a little epoxy later, my ceiling has been dry for the past 2 months. So everyone, get multiple estimates BEFORE committing to the first one that comes buy. And dont take recommendations as fool proof. They help, but buyer beware.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Any LES or East Village Transplants here?
« on: May 21, 2009, 10:55:17 AM »
Hi, I have a business on the LES, and also in the Village. As much as i love the city, i cant wait to leave after I'm done working! The weekends there are torture, like you were saying with all the bars and clubs. Ive lived here for 20 years, first in a 1200 sq foot pre war, than a private home. When i was younger, i lived in the city, and got it out of my system. As you get older, space and quiet mean alot. As far as the commute, i took the subways for years, but now i drive in, but fortunately, the opposite times of rush hours. You need to take the time and check out a few open houses and see what the apts are like. I don't know what your space is like, but jackson Heights is known for its large pre war Apts. Everything is cheaper here, that's for sure, and you ll get almost everything you need on 37Th ave. Except of course books and clothes! But, we have good supermarkets, Meat markets, and a number of really good restaurants, that are affordable. And one bonus is that you can actually have a car!! The Jackson Heights Beautification Group is having a walking tour in June. Its a great way to get a feel of the neigh boorhood. Best of Luck
