About four times a week for the last ten years I have shopped in C-Town.  It is very close to where I live and therefore convenient for me to shop there.  I have always found the cashiers and managers to be pleasant and helpful.  I have no issues whatsoever with their customer service.

What they don't have, is a particularly good selection.  But this is largely because they a the smallest of the local groceries.  They are probably half the size of Met Food.  You can't stock what you don't have space for.  If I am cooking a big dinner I immediately abandon C-Town and head off to Met Food or Trade Fair on 76th.  C-Town is certain to be missing something that I need.

As for the generally appearance and cleanliness of the place, well I would charitably say it is so-so. But I would say the exact same thing about Met Food or Trade Fair.  To me there is not much of a difference between these stores in the cleanliness department.

One undeniable criticism of C-Town is their prices.  Especially on staples.  $2.09 for a dozen extra large eggs?  That is easily fifty cents more than the other stores.  Their milk is expensive too.

On a related matter, the Bravo on Roosevelt Avenue (which is also owned by the same folks that own C-Town) between 85th & 86th is undergoing total renovation.  It has been closed for a couple of months now while they work on it.  I will be interested to see how it is when it opens.
