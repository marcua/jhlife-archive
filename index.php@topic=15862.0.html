Aggressive and threatening drivers in crosswalk
« on: January 13, 2017, 08:54:39 PM »
I am not tolerating the threatening and aggressive drivers in Jackson Heights any more....and you shouldn't either.

This is not the first time this has happened, but it was the most aggressive driver I have encountered and it appears that it occurs almost every time I walk out of my apartment now.

Today, I was on the crosswalk with a white walk sign (corner of 84th and 37th) when a huge SV tried to cut in front of me as I was walking. Two older ladies were also on the other side who feared crossing, despite a white walk sign. I stopped in the middle of the crosswalk to help the two old ladies to cross. The driver honked continuously and shouted something at us. He then sped off around us while I was still helping the elderly ladies across the road.

My daughter also had a similar experience when she was walking on crosswalk a few blocks up on 37th. The driver got out of his car and got up into her face, cursing her the whole time. She was shaken. All she was doing was crossing the crosswalk on a white walk sign. No-one came to her aid despite his threatening behavior and a crowd watching on the sidewalk.

The crosswalk makes it clear that the pedestrian has the right of way and I refuse to live in a neighborhood that is made unsafe by these drivers.

So, I am going to:
1. Protect the elderly and assist them in the crosswalk if they appear to be fearful.
2. Stand up to the bullies on the road. Show them it is not ok to bully people who are in the crosswalk. Say something. Yes, say something that reinforces to the driver - the right of the pedestrian to walk safely in the crosswalk.
3. Document the aggressive driving behavior in photos and videos.
4. Give a smile to the driver who respects the crosswalk.

It's up to all of us in the Jackson Heights community to take a stand and do something. The police are nowhere to be found. Please join me to make this area a safer place.

Solutions: (1) more speed bumps; (2) turning the 37th Avenue, in part, into a no car zone, i.e., no traveling east/west, but cars still would travel north/south; and/or, (3) red light in all directions to permit pedestrians the opportunity to cross the street.

I like the second option.  Cars traveling along 37th Avenue are extremely aggressive and do not seem to have any regard for pedestrians.  37th Avenue has high foot traffic and a large number of businesses and restaurants.  Turning 37th Avenue into a pedestrian zone would be consistent with the character of the neighborhood and would encourage a greater sense of community to the extent that people would be encouraged to utilize the public spaces.  Restaurants would be able to set up shop outside on warm nights and this would enhance the sense of connectedness with the neighborhood.  It would also increase property values.  Street musicians would have a place to perform and artists could also have space to practice their craft in a comfortable place area and the community will be able to interact with the artists and watch them create masterpieces.  As far as cars go, there are plenty of other roads that would still be accessible to them and the business would still be able to have things delivered to them by virtue of roads open to vehicle traveling north/south.  Something like this would transform the neighborhood and make our lives a lot better to the extent that we would have a lot more public space and potentially a lot more green space in an urban context.

Pedestrians are their own worst enemies in Jackson Heights.  Too many stand in the street while the light is against them,  jaywalking, start crossing while the "Don't Walk"  is flashing,  and most egregiously, distracted by their phones.  While pedestrians always have the right of way,  even when running out between parked cars,  cars have to be able to turn while the signal is green.
The solution is to move crosswalks to the middle of the block,  rather than on the corners.  Barricades or fences prevent pedestrians from crossing at the corner.  This way cars don't have to compete with pedestrians to make turns before the light turns red again.
This is used on 6th and 5th Avenues during the holiday seasons and works very well to allow cars and crossing pedestrians to co-exist. Until the knuckleheads figure out they can jump over the corner pedestrian barriers to avoid being civil.

Man, 67, killed after struck by SUV while crossing Queens street
(NY Daily News)

A motorist fatally struck a Queens senior as he crossed the street just one block from his home Wednesday night, police said.

Henry Boimel, 67, was crossing 76th St. near 37th Ave. in Jackson Heights around 7:30 p.m. when a 45-year-old man driving a Toyota Rav4 slammed into him, cops said. Dinesh Bhattarai, also of Jackson Heights, was driving east on 37th Ave. when he made a right turn onto 76th St. and struck Boimel.

EMS workers rushed Boimel to Elmhurst Hospital Center with head trauma, but he could not be saved, officials said.

Bhattarai stayed on the scene, and police charged him Thursday morning with failure to yield to a pedestrian in a marked crosswalk.
