OK. My lack of knowledge of the details of Thai food is going to show, but ... we always ordered from Rice Avenue when we wanted things like massaman curry, Pad Thai, Pad See Ew, the boring comfort food that's available in most NYC Thai restaurants. Since Rice Avenue is now out of business (or at least their Web site is down and their phone is dead), I've been calling around but most of the other restaurants in the neighborhood seem to serve a different kind of Thai food (Esan?) that doesn't have any of these familiar standbys. Title: Re: Western-Style Thai that delivers?
Post by: NYC Peromyscus on February 15, 2011, 10:15:43 AM
Also a fan of Ayada, but wouldn't really consider them "Western-style".  They do have the classic dishes you are looking for, though (and many other things...salads are great, although some are incredibly spicy!).

They deliver incredibly fast to 73rd St, so the food gets to you in better shape.  The menu and quality are similar to Nusara Thai in the shopping plaza further down in Elmhurst, but the speed made us switch to Ayada for our go-to Thai delivery!
