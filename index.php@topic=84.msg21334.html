37Th Ave, Roosevelt Ave, side streets...You name it, the rents are horrendous.  The smaller shops are even worse.  I have been searching for a small store front in high population density areas.  I didn't care if it was Jackson Heights, Forest Hills, Rego Park and even Corona or Kew Gardens.  Since I am planing on branching into resales and short sales soon (I exclusively sell sponsor owned units at this time) and I will be hiring a trainee once I locate a reasonably priced shop.  The prices for store fronts are ridiculous in Jackson Heights.  I see tons of desk for rents and tiny 100 to 150 sq feet office within other offices being rented for $600 to $1500 per month.  Business are sharing spaces, renting desks and there are plenty of offices available but the landlords wont budge.


Now that the market is down Real Estate are closing their big fancy "over the top" offices and are rethinking their overhead approach.  I know a couple of attorneys doing the same as well as a ton of house builders.  The only way rents will go down will be when vacancies don't get filled.  LIC and Astoria are a prime example of this.  Some of the least expensive square feet can be found in those places.  Don't be fooled by the two main strips in Astoria...look it up. High traffic + high Coop's / Condos density = overpriced commercial space.  I have seen $100 per sq ft for the smaller shops in some areas in Jackson Hts, Astoria and Forest Hills .
