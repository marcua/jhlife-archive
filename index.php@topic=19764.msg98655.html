Here at Sawkill farm the wellbeing of our customers, employees and animals is always a top priority.  We believe deeply in the ability of food and community to strengthen our health and happiness, especially in trying times. We want you to know that we are following all Greenmarket, New York and CDC guidelines for preventing the transmission of the Covid-19 and are taking this seriously.

As of now, all NYC Greenmarkets are operating normally.  Greenmarket is in direct contact with NY State and has been assured all of the markets can stay open. 

In addition to following health and safety guidelines
we are offering advanced online ordering for our NYC Greenmarkets.

If you would like to place an order in advance, please reply to this email or send an email to kallie@sawkillfarm.com.

  We will then weigh out your order and send you an invoice to pay online.  Once paid, you will be able to stop at our market booth and pick up your pre-paid and pre-packaged order.  You can find a list of available cuts at the bottom of this email.  Please send in your order before 2pm on Saturday.  Orders will be filled on a first come first serve basis.

NY Times, April 1, 2020 - One of our regular farms, Lani's, is mentioned in this article
Why Outdoor Farmers’ Markets Matter More Than Ever

Tenzing Chime now has one job to do at the vegetable stand where he works, a measure of how much the coronavirus has changed the simplest rituals of neighborhood life.

He only touches the cash.

Like him, all his co-workers wear masks, plastic eye shields and gloves. One slides leafy greens into clear plastic bags. Customers point at the bagged vegetables from behind a rope, as if choosing diamonds from a jewelry case. Another worker puts their selections in plastic bins and slides them down the table toward the register, like handbags at an airport security check.

The city’s 50 outdoor farmers’ markets have always been a precious commodity for New Yorkers, providing fresh produce for urbanites and supporting the quiet work of local farmers.

But now, during the coronavirus crisis, some shoppers said the greenmarkets were more important than ever. They said they felt safer shopping outdoors than inside grocery stores.
