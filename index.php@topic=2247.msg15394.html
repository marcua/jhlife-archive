You guys are embarrassing me...You are still NOT getting a discount!   :2funny:
They (Angelo's lumber) don't install but any contractor/carpenter can.  Crown moldings come in 8' pieces and should be cut to fit on site.  I buy my stone from New Hoa Stone in Flushing.  After the cabinets are installed they will come to measure but will charge you $100 for the visit in the event you decide not to buy from them and will credit you upon purchase.  You can buy granite from $25 per foot up to $45 for the darker fancier stones.  They will install and deliver for  about $300 flat fee.  Your contractor can install the backsplash right after the countertops are installed.

If you want to keep the cost down simply duplicate the sizes that you have and if possible join 2 or more cabinet sizes together and replace them with one.  For example, say you have two 15 inch wide base cabinets next to each other now.  These cabinets cost about the same price as 30" cabinets so instead of ordering two 15" cabinets at $129 each you may want to order one 30" cabinet for $149 for a total saving of $109 just on those pieces alone.  I noticed that many buyers go to the ikea site and I find it to be a great site but if you decide to buy from them make sure it is delivered assembled.  This will save you and your contractor time and Money.

If you keep the height of the cabinets the same as the current one you only need to do minor wall repairs thus saving you a bundle in drywall work such as making the walls PLUM and skim coating.  Furthermore, if you start changing the location of sinks, lighting and gas lines you will spend anywhere from $500 to $2000 more.  The difference in price can jump dramatically when you go from a 30" in height to say a 42" and the most dramatic thing you can probably do to your cabinets is accent lighting which could get somewhat expensive if you don't plan before you install your cabinets.  At the end of the day it's all about you people...whatever makes you happy :smitten:

What part of "You guys are not getting a discount"  you didn't understand...  :smitten:  We do all of our floors installations...wood or ceramics / stone.  We only sub-out the sanding and polyurethane work.  We supply the stain finish and tell them how many coats for the wood.  All the installation is done in-house.  It is actually a lot of fun to take different color tiles and mix them up then I code the cuts like cut A may be a a 6 x12 cut and cut B maybe a triangular cut. I'll use some paper design and then letter or number each set of tiles.  After it's all done we would dry install it in the next room or in the same room if possible. 

The process is quite simple but more intricate designs will take a couple of hours just coming up with a final product and cutting all the tiles may take a worker all day before they begin installing the next day.  Unfortunately most of the clients already have a vision of what they want and will not allow too intricate of a design because they feel it may not look right.  There are many in Jackson Hts. that have really good taste like one I did a few months ago...he chose a white kitchen from IKEA and we updated the electrical to place recessed lights.  The client took general advise and he chose a galaxy granite and slate floors as well as multicolor backsplash and got a really nice kitchen at the end.  I think we scratched his fridge door  but stuff happens.  The Manhattan folks like hearing ideas and will be more daring in allowing us to come up with an aggressive design as long as the colors are not too loud.    Bronxville clients just cut a check and hire an interior designer which usually works out fine (usually but not always).   :2funny: 

Actually we like working with Professionals that way we get lots of credit for the work and we can always blame someone else if the client doesn't like the end product...
