Title: Re: Fish Markets
Post by: jsh on March 24, 2008, 11:55:00 AM
I've gotten some great fish at really reasonable prices from Sea Tide.  The one caveat is that not everything looks great on any given day, so you have to go with what looks fresh rather than assuming you'll be able to find anything you want.  However, there have always been a variety that look good so I've always left happy.  I also got great shrimp with the heads on at least once which were fantastic (shrimp w/heads=very very fresh b/c the heads spoil very quickly).

I also really like Marino's in Astoria, but Sea Tide is more convenient, as I can walk to it or take the 7.

Title: New Fresh Seafood Stall in Compare Foods - Northern Blvd. btw 86th & 87th
Post by: Griswold Girl on August 31, 2008, 09:18:48 PM
Wanted to get the word out.  I was in Compare Foods tonight (Northern Blvd. bet. 86th and 87th) and was very pleasantly surprised at their brand new full service seafood counter.  It is rented out by a private party which you pay directly for your seafood.  Everything is brought fresh daily from the market and I was really impressed as they had about 15-20 different kinds of fish, 6 different kinds of shrimp, clams, baby octopus, squid etc.. etc...  They also have little signs which tell you whether the catch is wild or farmed and its country of origin.  Also, the prices are surprisingly very reasonable.  As seafood eating Baltimore-born girl, this is a much needed improvement to the neighborhood.  Hope you enjoy too!
