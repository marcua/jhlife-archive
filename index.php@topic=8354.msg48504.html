My parents were definitely not adventurous eaters when I was growing up. We did have a culture of eating out quite often, as many Jewish families do - But that was regularly Chinese takeout 1x a week, Italian hot heroes every 2 weeks or so, and diner 1x a week. Also, Italian deli cold cuts, and places like Chicken Holiday were a regular thing. My mother cooked, but strictly out of necessity and she rarely used any spice or seasoning. We never got anything delivered, and I loved going with my father to pick up whatever - it was so fun holding the big tub of chicken and holding it on my lap, with the fried chicken steam warming me up and all the warm smells,mmmmmm. I can still smell it.

So, my mother was not adventurous at all. She never ate shellfish (she was suspiciously allergic of many things), and she never liked to try anything spicy. That being said, she was(and still is) very comfortable amongst other cultures and a very accepting person.

My father would try ANYthing that was put in front of him - but that was more so of just trying it for the sake of putting something in ones mouth. I could never imagine him really having a craving for any food or being particularly interested in the inner workings of a culture.

So no, it was never an adventure. I still don't consider it an adventure, but I do like having different stuff all the time. I also like the challenge of breaking into a culture and making friends. At lunch in school, I always sat at a new table every few months, and I was friends with everyone from the bad kids to the nerds.

I always liked traveling locally, finding interesting shops and stuff. I'm just lucky I landed here and this was able to flourish. I'm hopefully going to have a more permanent residence here, so I'll be able to do even more.
