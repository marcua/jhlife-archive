Thanks for organizing this, Jeff!

I brought two friends along; one who is a lover of momos and one who had never experienced a momo and quickly became enamored with them. We joined forces with another trio that proved to make our crawl more informative and enjoyable. One was a reporter for a Tibetan television station who focuses on NY based stories and planed to do a piece on the crawl, her husband, and his cousin who recently opened a restaurant focused on momos in Idaho: http://www.momodumpling.com/. Yogi, the restaurant owner, had scheduled his trip to visit family in the area to coincide with the momo crawl as he thought it would be a good way to do some research.

Although Phayul was one of our favorites, our group of 6, which included 2 vegetarians, all agreed that Norling had the best momos of the 9 restaurants that we visited. The husband and wife had discovered Norling a while back when taking refuge from a rainstorm during a visit to JH. They have been going back ever since specifically for the momos and I completely understand why. The momos at Norling are very nicely seasoned and extremely fresh. The ginger really stands out amongst the flavors.

This was a great way to sample several restaurants in the neighborhood in a few short hours. I will definitely be returning to a few of these that I had not been to before. My friend who had never experienced these dumplings before loved them so much that she headed back to Manhattan with 50 frozen chicken momos in hand!
