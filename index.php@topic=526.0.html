Hey all I'm from the astorians board, but I thought you might be interested in a fundraiser the Transportation Alternatives Queens Committee is putting on.

All the info is above.  All proceeds go to making the Tour de Queens a reality.  $20 donation gets you in.  For $40 dollars you get entry plus a Transportation Alternatives membership (which usually goes for $35 and includes a subscription to Reclaim the TA magazine and discounts at local bike shops.  Just in case you don't like fliers the info is below too:

WHEN: Tuesday March 11th 7p-10p
WHAT: A roller race fundraiser and auction to raise money for the upcoming Tour de Queens (scheduled for June 8th permits permitting)  Come participate or just watch folks roller race with their bikes, enjoy drink specials and put some money down in an auction for lots of really neat items like a solar bag and a HYmini.
WHERE: The Creek and the Cave a fun bar located at 10-93 Jackson Ave.  $20 cover at the door, $40 entry and TA membership.

Sponsored by the Transportation Alternatives Queens Committee

Hey all,

Just a reminder about the Queens Roller Race this Tuesday, March 11th at 7pm.  We did a little practice session this weekend, and riding and balancing a free standing bike on top of rollers is well.. an experience that should make for interesting entertainment to say the least.  Let's add Wiley Norvell to the amateurs riders list to spice things up a little more as well.

Plus, we've got great auction items including:

* A HYmini (http://hymini.com/) which is a solar and wind powered device that comes with a bike mount that can charge up your  cell phone or ipod while riding around town
* Cyclist apparel and accessories
* A SOLAR backpack
* Gift certificates to the Creek and the Cave and Tuk Tuk
* Bike lessons (in case you never learned.. I know you are out there)
* A $100 gift certificate for VHS to DVD conversion which may be yours for less
* Designer apparel, including Ed Hardy, Polo and Ralph Lauren
* Original framed artwork by Tour de Queens logo designer and Queens artist Kelly Mola and Helen Ho
and more!

Come on out- it's fun, environmental, and you get to have a cheap drink, hoot, and holler all at the same time.
