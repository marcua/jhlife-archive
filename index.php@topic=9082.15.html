dssjh shows a great sense of class and dignity for his/her apology.

In my perspective, people who think that ther's no purpose behind protections for people who are targeted by virtue of their special class have limited experience with this experience.

I am white, born and raised Catholic in a northern suburb, and had a grandmother who was Jewish (from Corona actually). Living in the Bronx after graduating from college in the early eighties, I was often called a "dirty jew" by Arab merchants, because I have a physical likeness to my grandmother's side of the family. The first time it happened, I looked around because I couldn't imagine that someone was calling me a "dirty jew". I've had men follow me (at 9 o clock in the morning on my way to the subway, not in some remote, dangerous location) and threaten rape and other bodily harm. My husband is a mixed race black man, and well, I won't go into his experience, but let me just say, the targeting was overt.

I think that people are well intentioned when they say we've moved past race and other classes of discrimination, but based on my experience, and my observations of the smaller and larger world, we haven't. I'm not trying to play the  victim, and I don't want others to play "vicitims" either, but this is just the truth, the sad truth unfortunately.
