Adding my list to what I posted on a different thread....most have been covered here and good to see some consistency on what people would like in JH...   Maybe someone should officially survey the neighborhood....

The main reasons I leave the neighborhood and spend money elsewhere is to:
1) go to a good food market with fresh selection of foods (produce, fish, meats)
2) get a meal outside of the ethnic Asian/Latin cuisines available here (quality brick oven pizza, upscale burger, brunch, mediterranean/falafel, quality sushi are all on the list)
3) go to a full-service gym such as NYSC. They seem to be opening all around, but on in JH probably would do well an be a better alternative to Blink over the 7 line

Also a larger cafe would be nice where you can work on a laptop/read a book.

Some specific food places that would work that seem to be everywhere else in the boroughs (even though may be chains) and I would be welcome here include: Brooklyn Bagel, Caffe Bene, Cafe Grumpy, Five Guys, Bareburger, Panera's, Petey's (Astoria burger place--haven't been yet but has good reviews)...

Yes it would be wonderful to get one of those nicer restaurant chains in. But the next thread here titled "JH is not a wealthy neighborhood" or something to that effect, points out, using hard data, that JH is actually one of the poorest neighborhoods in Queens with only a small area (historic district) having a higher median income level. Which is probably why (along with mysteriously high commercial rents) these chains are not here. But I have noticed that there are many restaurants here that are not cheap, and that seem to do booming business. I am thinking of some of the South American places that feature steak, where I see many large families dining, and where prices are reasonable but not lower than prices would be in the chains mentioned. A lot of people here spend good money dining out. But the chains are probably looking at those income statistics and rejecting this area because it looks so unpromising. Is there a way around this? Someone mentioned Bed-Stuy as an area that would look similar in the stats but is beginning to attract higher income residents and the businesses that go along with them.
