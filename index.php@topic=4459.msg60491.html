Well, my wife and I have lived in the Carlton House for about 4 years now.  We are very happy.  The real treat, for us, is having a doorman.  It's great for packages being delivered, messages etc.  The staff is wonderful.  We like most of the people living in the building, as well.  In our section there is a nice camaraderie and the younger residents are quick to help out the elderly.  We have an apartment that looks east so we don't have a balcony but we have an incredible amount of sunlight because there are no buildings.  We overlook houses.  The building is very well maintained and there are plans, now that the lobby has been refurbished, to renovate the other floors.  I think you will like living here.

As has been stated before on this thread - The Carlton House does not have a land lease. 

There are many buildings in Jackson Heights that became coops back in the 80's and they do not have land leases either.

As many on this board know, The Carlton House was the first post-war(1947) cooperative completed by the Queensboro Corporation.  The massive development did not do well, so they rented out the units instead.  Their next and final coop, The Greenbrier(1949) was on a much smaller scale.
