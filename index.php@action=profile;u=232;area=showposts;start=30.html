Neighborhood Chat / Re: Short Survey to help out a student
« on: May 18, 2008, 03:56:50 PM »
Female, 45, of Russian-Jewish descent; I've lived in JH 4-1/2 years; I don't work in JH; I use public transport; I own my apartment:

What major changes do you think have taken place in Jackson Heights (in terms of economic development, density, housing, ethnicity, transportation, etc.)?

Since I've been here, what I've seen are more families with young children moving into the historic district co-ops; I can't speak to the rest of the neighborhood. Also, somewhat more upscale retail.

What problems in Jackson Heights cause you concern?

My #1 concern is that we don't have enough green space, especially playground space for children. My #2 concern are the high commercial rents on 37th Avenue and the stranglehold that a few landlords seem to have over commercial property.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Holiday/Year End Tips for Building Staff
« on: December 29, 2007, 02:40:20 PM »
I'm the only person I know that has a Marxist tipping policy. 

I don't see any reason to tip the super more than the porter; the porter works just as hard, if not harder, and earns less. I tip him twice what I tip the super at the holidays.

I do believe in tipping for exceptional service. But I'd rather tip for exceptional service at the time of the service.

Handing $100-200 to a super who makes as much as I do seems out of line. I'd rather hand (and do hand) that money to an organization that feeds and houses people who really need it. I don't see the need for middle-class charity.
