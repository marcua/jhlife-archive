"Ganador" would refer to a male while female would be "ganadora".  The translation has a specific meaning depending on the sentence structure.  "Ganando" would really mean winning and "gano" is to have won as in past tense. When you "gain" it takes a bit of a twist and could mean to receive or increase and would be from the verbs "recibir" or "subio".  Anyway, bottom line is you got it wrong dear.  You put a coma after "No, soy ganador / a".  When you put the come you mistakenly disagreed with my statement and you basically stated that "NO" you are not a loser but a winner and of course we all know that to be false. What you meant to say was "No soy ganadora!".  Which could also be written "Yo soy la perdedora" or I am the loser (perdedora meaning a female loser)
