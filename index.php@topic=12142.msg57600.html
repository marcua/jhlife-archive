I just realized this sounded kind of cold, but it's true! There's a ton of great info about the neighborhood on this forum.

There's great ethnic foods, the rents prices are somewhat reasonable and there's great apartments in the historic district with gardens.

Astoria it is not, but it's a pretty safe neighborhood with great diversity. I suggest you come here at night on the subway into 74th and walk around the neighborhood. Spend some time eating at restaurants and walking around. There's also a great Farmer's Market on Sunday in the early morning till 3PM I think in Travers park on 34th ave and 78th.

Walk up and down Rooselvelt from the high 60's through 85th. Turn and finish the loop down 37th ave through the same streets. Things get a little quieter up at 35th ave due to hardly any business until you get to the lower 70's. There's a bunch of great stuff.

We live right off 37th ave and it can get annoyingly noisy with garbage trucks all night long almost every night (used to be only a couple nights its gotten much worse, do we really need so many companies every night?), bad saxophone players playing the same 4 songs badly all day long (I feel like bad person hating this guy but if you had to live in earshot of his bad playing all day you'd lose your mind as well..) I actually liked the guy the first 2 weeks..

So just be careful of what you're getting into if you want to get an apt right on 37th, I wish that's the one thing someone told me before we moved in.

JH is definitely a double edged sword, but I still like it.

Start by going to some open houses of apartments.  You could even go to ones that aren't a match for you, such as a 2 bedroom even if you're only looking for a studio, just so you can see the inside of more buildings.  Also attending a farmers market on a Sunday is a great idea because you may be able to strike up conversations with potential neighbors.  And Juju's Bagel shop and Espresso 77 are both good places to stop by and talk to people who live in the neighborhood.  The two nice wine stores in the area, Table Wine and Addictive Boutique Winery, both have tastings that you could attend and meet people who live nearby.

I personally don't have an issue with safety in the area.  I perceive it to be equally as safe as Astoria.  It's always good to be mindful when you're on the street after dark.

My parents knew Jackson Heights as a pretty dangerous place as well, because that was the impression 20+ years ago. A lot has changed. It's not the suburbs, but it's a pretty safe place with lots of families. A couple of my friends just bought within the last month, and lots of people are finally put it on their radar. Maybe take your parents for a walk around and see what they think.

I've lived in Jackson Heights for most of my life enjoying its wonderful tree lined streets, its unique and beautfully designed buildings and surrounding gardens.  Our apartment buildings  are humane in size (only six stories) so that we as people are not dwarfed by the gargantuan monstrosities that ruin neighborhoods.  We can enjoy the sky and each other.   As part of the safest city in the world, Jackson Heights is one of its best neighborhoods.  It's safe, prosperous and friendly with good schools and businesses catering to every need.  Transportation to everywhere by  planes, trains and buses are a block or two away.  It has the best of everything and it's affordable!!!!!!
