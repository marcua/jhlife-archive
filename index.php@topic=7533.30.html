I think Peralta's the one who is out of step here.

It's unfortunate to see him taking the side of the backroom dealers and dismissing the legitimate concerns of his constituents.   Yes, the idea of a soccer stadium has broad support.  But we haven't seen realistic proposal handling parking and deliveries at a stadium located within the park, and until MLS comes forward with something that makes sense, the idea that the entire footprint of the stadium will be within the Fountain of Industry is laughable.   We expect our legislators to look out for the community's interests and it doesn't look like Peralta is looking out for us very hard.   A soccer stadium located west of CitiField would have far better transit and vehicular access, and wouldn't have the same adverse impacts on Flushing Meadows Park.  I can't think of any reason he shouldn't be supporting that.

As for the suggestion that these are distinct projects that shouldn't be linked together, I strongly disagree.  The mall, tennis center expansion, and soccer stadium all use parkland and will all have permanent impacts on the park.   There are some positive elements to each of these proposals.   But the EDC and the Parks Department should be coordinating them, ensuring that impacts on the park are mitigated, both during the construction period and into the future.  The emerging coalition of groups that is drawing attention to these projects is demanding that the government do its job.    Anybody who suggests at this stage, before any of these questions have been answered that we look away, there's nothing to see here, it's all good... has earned my distrust.
