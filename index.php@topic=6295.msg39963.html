jessicaramos

Re: Calle Colombia Way??
« Reply #10 on: June 24, 2011, 03:41:44 PM »
82nd Street means a lot to me and the roots of my heritage.  It's the heart of the Colombian community's businesses along 37th Avenue, not to mention, where we as teenagers from other Queens neighborhoods came to hang out- it's where we got/get off on the train to find our empanadas, our salsa, and the walk up to Northern Blvd etc. Those four corners of 82nd Street and 37th Avenue also bring back fond memories of Colombian activism- where we rallied for our double citizenship, Colombian presidential campaigns, etc. 

As a Colombian-American myself, it does make sense to me that it is there. And as a resident of 82nd Street, it fills me with great pride every day I walk by on my way to work and see the sign because I remember the contributions of all those who came to Jackson Heights from Colombia in the past 50+ years. It inspires me to keep on...and in the end, I'm sure many of my peer Colombian-Americans (not even an anecdotal survey) feel the same.

Additionally, let me say that letters were sent to Ms. Giovanna Reid about it, although I do not know if the street "renaming" was ever officially discussed in a community board hearing.  I do know that the letters were sent because I drafted them myself when Hiram Monserrate was a Council Member. (I put the word renaming in quotes because as someone said before, the street isn't technically renamed.)

Hoping for a fantastic July full of celebration of American and Colombian Independence!
