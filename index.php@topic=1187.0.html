What do you eat for breakfast?
« on: June 30, 2008, 01:40:38 PM »
I just read an interesting article in Time Magazine entitled Chicken for Breakfast.  Prompted by spotting a McDonalds offering a chicken sandwich for breakfast, the writer asks the question of "...why we accept certain food at certain times."  There's an accompanying video whereby he tries the sandwich at breakfast time even though his brain is craving what he considers a more traditional breakfast food such as eggs.

As a kid in Ecuador, I remember adults eating just assorted fresh baked breads/rolls with real butter and accompanied by a cup of coffee.  Nowadays, when family visits us in the States, they crave soups and heartier meals and sort of frown on the egg thing.  So, what do you guys eat for breakfast?  I'm a cereal person.

I love steel cut oatmeal as well, but am sometimes lazy and impatient about preparing it.  There have been a few occasions when I've gone to City Coffee Shop for breakfast and have seen people eating rice and beans.  It looks good, but I'm not sure if I'm ready to eat that type of meal during the breakfast hours.

Here's another one of my little Ecuadorian stories...this one from when I was a visiting teen.  I remember sleeping over at my aunt's house and waking up to the aroma of food being cooked.  My morning breakfast that day consisted of tallarines de pollo (spaghetti with stewed chicken).  It definitely was not what I perceived as a traditional breakfast in my mind, but it was absolutely delicious!  My cousin seemed shocked that I would prefer eggs to a meal like the one I had been presented with.
