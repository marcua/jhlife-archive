Re: Need for a Safer 111th Street Along Flushing Meadows-Corona Park
« Reply #19 on: July 27, 2015, 11:46:48 PM »
Yes!  While there are open questions the city is trying to address, the proposal is absolutely a win-win-win for pedestrians, cyclists, and motorists alike.  The proposal would convert a southbound traffic lane to new parking spaces along the median, more than offsetting a small loss of parking on the eastern curb of 111th Street (to make room for safer, more visible pedestrian crossings) and on Corona Avenue.   Since this is a very lightly traveled street, NYCDOT's analysis shows that normal traffic can easily be handled with a single lane in each direction.

What are the open questions?  First, the community is concerned that there will be traffic congestion on major event days.  NYCDOT has already done a lot of analysis on Mets game days and other events, and concluded that with effective traffic management and some signal retimings, there wouldn't be a problem.  The community wants them to continue to collect data and demonstrate that there would not be a problem even during the US Open. 

Second, some in the community have expressed concern about the changes on Corona Avenue, and how it might impact access to the Rego Park Health Care center.  If there is merit to these concerns, it's likely NYCDOT will come back on Wednesday with changes to address them.

I'm confident these issues will be addressed, but less confident that the Community Board's and Moya's hostily to the proposal will be appeased.  It's really important for people who support this proposal to participate in the workshops if they can.
