Washington Square News
Postcards tell stories of the homeless
Hyein Lee
Issue date: 3/12/08 Section: Campus

When Bobby Sukrachand took a train to Jackson Heights in December 2006, he was just looking for authentic Thai food.

But after he got off the subway, a man yelled, "Yo, Mafioso, want to take my picture?"

The man was Tommy, accompanied by an ex-Navy Seal named Fay. That marked the surreptitious meeting of Sukrachand and a group of eclectic people sharing a home on the corner of 74th Street and Roosevelt Avenue in Jackson Heights.

"There was a crew. They were very friendly and we just started hanging out," Sukrachand said. "It took a few months before I could start taking photos and gain the trust and confidence of the people."

That became the core of Sukrachand's senior thesis in Tisch, where he is studying photography and imaging. On the back of photographic postcards, he tells the stories of the homeless living on that corner. Each postcard has a snippet of the subject's thoughts on the back.
