this is'about the overall destruction of new york city,

once upon a time, neighborhoods had individual character.

you could go from the lower east side to the upper west side and actually see a difference between the two.

you didn't need to have a starbucks, a bye bye baby, a wine bar, a bistro and a macaron vendor within walking distance of every apartment.

people dealt with not having french food in astoria, alsatian wine in jackson heights and barbecued pork in williamsburg.

there was a sense of community and of communities. now, many people desire homogeneity. i can point to the whiners who moved into this one area of greenpoint that's anchored by a live poultry/slaughterhouse that's been there for 30 plus years. condos have started to rise there, and now the people who've lived there for eight days suddenly feel empowered to whine 'i didn't see the slaugherhouse while i visited the site 19 times in six months....get rid of it or i will sue.'

you're not saying this, and like i said, this isn't directed at you. but it's true....new york city is basically a thing of the past. it's turning into a bigger milwaukee, a less interesting cleveland. a latte in every pot, a hardware store online if you're lucky.

i'm glad i got to see the real thing.
