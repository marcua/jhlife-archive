Greener Living in Jackson Heights
« on: February 22, 2008, 05:05:13 PM »
I came across this enlightening video addressing the issue of how product consumption affects our world.  This animated video begins with an explanation of how the extraction of the earth's natural resources are processed in order to manufacture the products we use every day.  It continues by focusing on production, distribution, consumption and disposal of mainstream items such as computers, coffee cups, iPods, etc., all with the concluding message that it is our responsibility to create a more sustainable and environmentally friendly world.

The video runs approximately 20 minutes, but it's engaging and fun to watch.  A true eye-opener!

DECREASE CONSUMPTION OF PLASTIC BAGS

Perhaps we can encourage local businesses, especially the supermarkets, to move towards decreased use of plastic bags.  Local businesses may be averse to going cold turkey and eliminating plastic bags altogether for fear of offending customers, but perhaps they can be made to see the light if the community voices its desire for decreased plastic bag consumption.  Perhaps they can even be persuaded to think of going green as a competitive advantage: (1) decreased cost of plastic bags - possibly transferring the savings to the consumers, (2) improved PR - the supermarkets don't cater to many outside of JH...a positive image for the first supermarket to go green may really help with community relations.

The JHBG, which seems to be very influential in the community, can in return take positive steps to promote the heck out of any businesses that participate in and take well defined steps towards eliminating/decreasing consumption of plastic bags.  Perhaps local churches can help spread the word too.

Perhaps local businesses can be encouraged to cover some of the cost of producing large cloth bags in exchange for endorsements and advertising space on the bag?  Perhaps MPC would be interested in putting its newly designed logo on the bag?  Consumers can purchase these bags at local participating stores for a few dollars.  Make sure we have a good number of participating stores so that this movement can have some momentum going forward.  Put a catchy funny design on the bag, spread some neighborhood pride!

I can only dream of walking out my front door and not seeing yet another supermarket plastic bag blowing past my face...

I love the anti-plastic bag campaign.

I have a canvas bag right here from the Good Food Store in Missoula, MT, it has the Good Food Store logo on one side and 10 advertisements on it from big health/organic product companies on the other.  I can definitely imagine a Jackson Heights bag with a JH logo and ads from local businesses.  Grocery stores could sell them at the check out counter (next to In Touch Magazine).
