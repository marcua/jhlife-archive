And now this article in Crain's... http://www.crainsnewyork.com/article/20130509/BLOGS04/130509869

Peralta won't comment on legal fees
CHRIS BRAGG
MAY 9, 2013 12:01 A.M.
Updated: May 9, 2013 2:33 p.m.

On Wednesday, court papers revealed that Queens state Sen. Jose Peralta was one of nine people secretly taped by Queens state Sen. Shirley Huntley—eight of whom, according to federal prosecutors, are under investigation, though not accused of wrongdoing.

At the same time, Mr. Peralta, a leading candidate for Queens borough president, has paid $31,000 since the end of 2011 from his separate state Senate account to the New York City law firm Stroock Stroock & Lavan. The campaign finance disclosure does not list the specific purpose of the expenditure; the most recent payment to the firm from Mr. Peralta's state Senate account, made last December, is listed simply as "legal fees."

Mr. Peralta's campaign did not comment despite multiple requests from The Insider on Wednesday evening and Thursday. Two lawyers at the firm also refused to comment.

Stroock Stroock & Lavan is well known in the New York political world because of one of its attorneys, Jerry Goldfeder, is a campaign finance compliance expert. It's not clear that the fees were for that purpose. Mr. Peralta's spending from his state Senate account stands out in some ways: Most of the big payments Stroock Stroock & Lavan and Mr. Goldfeder have taken in over the years, records show, have been for compliance work in New York City, where enforcement of campaign finance regulations is quite active. In contrast, the corresponding state laws are weak and their enforcement porous, which makes Mr. Goldfeder's expertise on state campaign compliance less in demand.

Stroock Stroock & Lavan does have an attorney who specializes in criminal defense work, Joel Cohen. When The Insider called and asked if he is representing Mr. Peralta in any sort of criminal probe, Mr. Cohen said, "I don't talk about who my clients are," and hung up.

The Insider also attempted to call Mr. Goldfeder several times to ask about the spending. He eventually responded by via email Wednesday evening, asking about the topic of inquiry. After being being told via email that it concerned Mr. Peralta's payments to the firm, Mr. Goldfeder did not return follow-up emails and phone calls seeking comment.

Mr. Peralta's Queens borough president account, which is separate from his state Senate account, has paid the firm $23,500 since last August. That spending is listed, however, as "campaign consulting."

A spokesman for Mr. Peralta, reached by phone on Wednesday, said he would look into the matter, but did not respond to further requests for comment. The senator did release a statement earlier on Wednesday, stating, "I am confident that the authorities will find, if they have not already done so, that I have engaged in no wrongdoing whatsoever."

In 2009, when he was an assemblyman, Mr. Peralta's name came up in a Daily News article about $500,000 in member items he steered an allegedly inactive nonprofit. He has not been accused by the authorities of anything.
