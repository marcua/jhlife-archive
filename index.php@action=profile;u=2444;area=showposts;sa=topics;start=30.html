Community Groups, Activities & Events / Kehillat Tikvah Community Second Seder, Saturday April 23
« on: April 03, 2016, 03:15:24 PM »
Our lay-led seder will be held Saturday April 23 at 5:30 pm in the Community Room in a residence on 75th Street, between 35th and 37th Avenues, Jackson Heights.  For the exact address, please call 718-569-1887.

 The Passover Seder is a festive meal at which we remember and celebrate the struggle for freedom from generation to generation. Our Kehillat Tikvah Community Seder is open to people of all ages and backgrounds. Join us as we share:   * A Delicious Buffet Dinner * Camaraderie with Neighbors and Friends * Stories and Songs of Freedom * Asking the four questions in 300 languages.

We are providing a catered dinner.   Checks can be made payable to Kehillat Tikvah and mailed to PO Box 720649, Jackson Heights, NY 11372,  or make your payment online at https://jhtikvah.wordpress.com/online-donations-and-payments/ and indicate "seder" in the comments area. No one will be excluded due to financial hardship.

Reservations are required. Please fill out the attached reservation form: https://jhtikvah.files.wordpress.com/2015/03/sederform2015.docx  You can mail it to the address above, or email it to kehillat.tikvah@gmail.com. Deadline for receipt of reservations is Friday, April 15, 2015.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / KEHILLAT TIKVAH PURIM CELEBRATION, SATURDAY MARCH 26
« on: March 22, 2016, 09:04:03 PM »
Celebrate the Joyous Holiday of Purim פורים
Saturday March 26, 4-8 pm
For the address in Jackson Heights, call  718-569-1887

Sing, dance, party and shake off those winter blues with singer MARIE MULARCZYK

Costumes are welcome.  Mask making for children and adults, a raucous reading of “The Whole Megillah,” and a light supper served with Hamentashen

Purim is a time of telling jokes, funny stories and songs.  Bring your jokes to share with us. Open to everyone in the community!

Marie Mularczyk O'Connell is an accomplished singer of Jewish, Irish and American folk music. She is part of Temple Beth-El of Great Neck's Friday night live Shabbat band where she is Cantor Lisa Hests' assistant. She is the early childhood music educator at Shelter Rock Synagogue, Temple Beth-El of Great Neck, Hollis Hills Jewish Center and the Education Alliance.  She is a member of the folk trio Mountain Maidens where she plays guitar, banjo, dulcimer, spoons and more. Marie is looking forward to celebrating Purim with you in music, dance, and celebration.

Suggested donations: $10 for adult members of KT; $15 for non-members; Children are free. As always, “more if you can, less if you can’t”. Checks can be made payable to Kehillat Tikvah and mailed to PO Box 720649, Jackson Heights, NY 11372. Online payments can be made at http://jhtikvah.wordpress.com/online-donations.  Donations can also be made at the door.  If you have questions, please contact our Treasurer at kttreasurer@gmail.com.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Martin Luther King, Jr/Mitzvah Day of Service, January 18, 10am.
« on: January 07, 2016, 09:41:05 PM »
Kehillat Tikvah invites you to join the Jackson Heights community for Martin Luther King, Jr/Mitzvah Day of Service, January 18, 10am.
The Renaissance Charter School, 35-59 81st Street, Jackson Heights, NY 11372.

MLK, Jr Day of Service honors Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.’s life and teachings of justice through the principles of nonviolence.  We will celebrate Dr. King with learning about volunteer projects that strengthen our community.
Mitzvah Day is in the Jewish tradition of Tikkun Olam, repairing the world by reaching out to others in their hour of need and enriching their lives and the community as a whole.

A light breakfast will be available at 10 AM and the program will start at 10:30 AM.

Come and hear some of Dr. King’s speeches, and stories by community members about their experiences as refugees, past and present.  Learn how you can volunteer for different organizations providing services to communities. Music will be provided by the Jackson Heights Community Chorus.

Please support our community food drives by bringing non-perishable, canned or packaged food (NO glass bottles). Please bring children’s books (new books preferred) for ages 3-8 years old for Elmhurst Hospital’s Reach Out and Read program

Volunteers are needed.
To volunteer, contact Peggy Heeney at 718-803-0060 x111 or pheeney@renaissancecharter.org. On weekends, Eileen Pentel at 718-569-1887.
Attached is a flyer for you to pass along: https://jhtikvah.files.wordpress.com/2016/01/mlk-mitzvah-day-flyer201623.pdf
