Well, wishful thinking here as well but I would even appreciate higher-end ethnic food where atmosphere isn't an afterthought like Casa Enrique or Tortilleria Nixtamal, both of which are decorated nicely and patronized/loved by visitors and locals alike.  I do feel that even in terms of ethnic food we are limited by the quality and general atmosphere of the area. While Jackson Heights is definitely a culinary destination for certain types of cuisines (Himalayan, Colombian, Ecuadorian), we definitely do not do the other categories that we are known for justice.  My South Asian friends cite the temples in Flushing or Manhattan restaurants like Chola, Dhaba, Tiffin Wallah, Saravana as their choices for the best, authentic Indian dining (each restaurant is explores different regional cuisines).  Even La Portena which is very cute could use a little spice in the kitchen - it's not cheap, but the food is tired and not inventive and Argentinians prefer El Amacen and Cafe Argentino; both better and cheaper.  On the Mexican front Casa Enrique in LIC, Ponche in Hell's Kitchen, and the aforementioned Tortilleria are culinary heavyweights without sacrificing price or atmosphere.  We are blessed with a population of global chefs with some serious homey/cart-cooking skills - wouldn't it be great if we had an ethnic restaurant with food that's slightly elevated to not meet the bottom line?  I know a few places on Northern are beginning to pop up like this and that's great - love that Pisco Bar.  I wish it were closer to 37.  As the kids reviewing Taco Bell in Jeff's post so astutely pointed out, atmosphere is a very important ingredient to an overall dining experience.
