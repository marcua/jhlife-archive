Title: Re: Surge of cockroaches/water Bugs/palmetto bugs
Post by: wlirfan on August 17, 2016, 11:12:43 AM
I've lived in NYC most of my life, for the past 42 years, and my co-op is the only place that I have ever lived in where I do not see roaches or rodents.  I actually think this is more a factor of the renovations that we did when we first moved in - my father did almost all of them, and he was very careful to seal every single crack and possible point of entry.  Water bugs are different.  We would find one every now and then, in the summer on hot and humid days.  Finally we realized where they were coming from -- the drain of our master bathroom.  The second bathroom has a cover that prevents them from climbing in.  The master bathroom does not.  Since we realized this, we close the drain after each shower.  No more water bugs.

Title: Re: Surge of cockroaches/water Bugs/palmetto bugs
Post by: Chingwa on August 18, 2016, 03:48:25 PM
A few years ago I discovered the best way to deal with roaches is to mix water with a few squirts of dishwasher liquid, shaken up, in a spray bottle.  Just a few direct squirts of this soapy mix will render even the biggest roach dead within a few seconds.  They may freak out and scurry around but soon they are twiddling their legs often flipped onto their back shaking in the agony of their death throes. It's glorious. They just keel over and die, no more swatting, mashing, or bugguts :)

The plus side is it's non-toxic to us humans, pets, kids etc.  And the already soapy water makes cleanup a breeze.

As far as keeping these demons out of your apartment to begin with, I'm not sure.  My building gets them every year.  Strangely I've seen LESS this year than usual.
