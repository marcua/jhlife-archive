Some observations after my first visit:

Beautiful, large produce section with some items I'd never seen before. Smallish selection of organic produce.

Small store but decently wide aisles.
Bad coffee selection.
No organic baby food.
No beer at all, but maybe this is to come.
Only five checkout lanes -- I wonder how that will work out.

All in all though, a massive improvement over the old Met.

It's nice to see that all that was predicted on this thread came true.  It was indeed a brand spanking new Mi Tierra supermarket after a gut renovation.

I've been waiting for this place to open for months and when it finally opened my fridge died.

But I went in yesterday to look around.

-Fish stand looks great.  There was Brook Trout!  There are tanks under the counter.  Not sure what's going to go in them but I think crustaceans.  I'm going to polish my fish gutting skills because there is so much whole fish there.
-Deli counter looks good.  I'm glad there is one because the Met didn't have one.
-Meat section expansive
-Produce section must be seen to be believed.
-Other sections look average but good.

Not sure why the kids are riding scooters in there but my daughter picked up on the festive atmosphere and starting dancing around the store.  The previous Met was a much more somber experience for both of us.

In all:  this place looks/is great.  So happy!
