There's a new arepa place that seemingly popped up overnight - it's directly across the street from the health food store, on 37th between 83rd and 84th.   I think it replaced a baby clothing or children's party clothing place.

It looks cute and cozy inside- they have a table or two by the window, with a counter at the back, and there were several people dining in there tonight when I passed by.

It's called Arepas Pues Mixtas.

My wife and I tried this place yesterday.  The arepas were great, but ordering was a bit of a trial.  The couple behind the counter got in an argument about whether we were skipped in line (we were not), then the man subsequently got in a testy exchange with the mother of the two girls that didn't skip us.  Apparently the arepas guy couldn't understand the girls' Spanish (they spoke English with NY accents, but the mom did not), and the mom didn't like.  The owner smoothed things over, though.  A non-Spanish-speaking couple came in while my wife was ordering and tried to ask what they have, but the guy didn't understand.  There were attempts at translation, but it all ended with the couple walking out.

Probably all just growing pains with a new business in a diverse area.  They don't have a menu, but the fine print on the bottom of the poster in the window (mistaken for ice cream scoops above...the arepas in the picture do actually look like ice cream!) lists what they have.  Arepas with pollo, carne, chicharrone (sp?), or camarones.  I suggest just getting the "mixtas"...it is huge, and has a layer of carne, followed by pollo, and ending with an awesomely crunchy chicharrone layer.  It is all covered in a tangy pink mayonnaise-like sauce.  The arepas themselves are freshly grilled and tasty.

I finally ate here as well.  I got a shredded chicken arepa with pink sauce ("salsa rosada") that tasted like Russian dressing.  The place is extremely clean and the woman behind the counter showed us their latest inspection certificate with zero violations.  Regarding the language issue- I think if they had something very simple written  menu that explains what they serve in both Spanish and English, it would help.  They didn't seem to have a written menu at all.  Maybe they were not expecting that many non-Spanish speaking customers, or people who are unfamiliar with arepas who want to try them.

They also told us they originally started selling arepas cooked on a grill in Flushing Meadow Park, and then opened an arepa place on Northern around 86th which has been there for a few years, and that this place is an addition for them.

It was quite busy around 7pm when we were there so hopefully they will be successful.
