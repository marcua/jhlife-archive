Jackson Heights - Boundaries

I came across this DNAinfo interactive map [see below link] where users can draw lines identifying a neighborhood's boundaries.  The finished drawings represent what each individual perceives as his / her neighborhood.  I found this interesting, considering that Jackson Heights proper is frequently misrepresented in news media.  The neighboring communities of East Elmhurst, Elmhurst and Corona tend to mistakenly fall into "Jackson Heights" territory.  Upon completing my drawing, I noticed that my boundary lines were way off of what others consider Jackson Heights.  The question is, who's right?

That very well may be. I grew up in this area in the 1970s and early ‘80s and had many friends who lived on the other side of Northern. I feel sure every single one of them to this day think they grew up in, and are from, Jackson Heights.

If memory serves, even the “upper Ditmars” area on the other side of the Grand Central was sometimes referred to as Jackson Heights.

In my mind, East Elmhurst was the area on the other side of Astoria Blvd. between it and the Grand Central (like from 82nd through that whole residential part going up toward Citi Field.)

My father grew up in Elmhurst and I asked him about this very subject not that long ago. He wasn’t terribly interested, but when pressed he confirmed a place called “East Elmhurst” did exist when he was a kid. In those days though, it was very far away ☺

I don’t agree generally with any theory about trying to capitalize on any possible “cache” of Jackson Heights at present, though that’s not to say there’s not some realtor doing it. If anything, it seems the rise of the area being East Elmhurst is more about distancing and distinguishing itself as its own separate and distinct place.

Also, thinking about the streets in the the 70s on the other side of Northern, up to say the shopping center, it may not even have had houses, or many houses, until around the 1960s or 70s, so maybe it just took awhile for that area to settle on its identity. It would be interesting to know how and when that particular area came to be developed..
