Not to beat a dead horse but I don't think Mehfil being gone really sank in until I saw the new place up and running this weekend.  There was a part of me that was hopeful for the possibility that they maybe kept it in the family and leased the space to a cousin or something that was going to keep parts of Mehfil's original menu alive, but the menu is completely biryani and I'm so, so sad.  Mehfil was always my favorite place in the neighborhood - because of its location on 37 ave it really felt like "ours" and not just a 74th street gawkfest for people who pat themselves on the back for coming out to exotic Queens.  The food was also by far the best Indian food in my opinion - I liked how it tried to be more elegant and upscale in its presentation, like closer to the delicious, upscale Indian joints you find on 57th street in Manhattan.  Does anyone have any suggestions for places that are similar to Mehfil's vibe and food quality?  I moved here a year ago and because Mehfil was right around the corner we actually barely tried other Indian places. This place is pretty well known among the Indian community in north / central NJ.  I've had the biryani at catered parties and it's pretty good...Hyderabadi style and spicy if you order it spicy.  Some of the other stuff on the menu also claims to be Hyderabad-style but haven't tried it yet. They also supposedly cook their biryani the traditional way with dough on top rather than foil.  At least the NJ location has better biryani to my taste than most places in JH (which is mostly greasy and bland, although that new Ananda Bazaar on 74th is decent).

I miss Mehfil as well, although at least this storefront is not another Dunkin Donuts or some other odious fast food.  Yes, Paradise is a chain but so are a lot of the other S. Asian businesses...Patel Bros. and ISP have locations all over the country, Jackson Diner folks own a catering hall and multiple restaurants with other names, Nanking, Tawa Tandoor, Maharaja have multiple locations, etc.  It's actually pretty hard to make money off one small restaurant.
