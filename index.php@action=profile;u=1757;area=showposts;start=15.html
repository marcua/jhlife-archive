Neighborhood Chat / Re: NYT: Young white people can't get their Bloody Marys
« on: July 20, 2015, 01:56:12 PM »
When I first came to JH in 2006, my husband was one of the 5 percent of people who was not Hispanic, Caucasian or Asian. He is black. Neighborhood people would speak to him in Spanish and when he explained that his family was from a English speaking Caribbean island, they would tell him that he was lying and that he was ashamed to be Hispanic. That was not cool. My neighbors would tell me to get out because I was not Hispanic and Jackson Heights was only for Hispanic people.  I am not making this up nor exaggerating.  I now live in a different building which has many mixed everything (race, sexual orientation etc.) families. What a relief that was. There is no law that you have to be any one thing here. This is NYC. My family came to NYC with the potato famine, mother grew up in Corona, and I think I have a right to choose where I want to live. I choose someone from a different background than myself to marry and yet we have many things in common. (education etc.)

 And young people are coming to JH for affordable real estate. If they make a financial commitment to living here, there is no crime in wanting a place to have  brunch, complete with bloody maries.

Moving to Riverdale, for an easier commute on Metro North. Too many late mornings. With our long hours, my husband and I need a more reliable, easier way to get back and forth. If we were coming home at an earlier time, we'd probably not be making this decision.

Jackson Heights is a very lovely community, which got a bit dog-eared over the years. Gentrification is a definitely a mixed bag for any neighborhood, but hopefully it will bring with it better supermarkets, cleaner streets etc. for the people who live here. In my ten years here I have meet many wonderful people, which is JH's greatest strength. I will especially love the JH dog people, who create a micro-social community of their own.

Maria is one of the loveliest, kindest and most responsible pet sitters I've ever had. She has lived in Jackson Heights since the '50s. My dog adores her, and I am sorry we can't manage to have her continue walk/watch our baby. We hope to bring her up to our new apartment to watch him on long weekends when we can't take our dog with us. I can't recommend her enough. she is more than a sitter, she is a friend to the animals. Again, please feel free to reach out to me, for any questions. I'll be in JH for a few more months.
