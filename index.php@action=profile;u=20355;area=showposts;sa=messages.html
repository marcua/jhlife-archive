Neighborhood Chat / Re: Internet providors
« on: May 13, 2014, 02:49:34 PM »
I hear you about hating Time Warner. They are the WORST. Your other options are limited:

1) Verizon DSL—much slower than cable Internet—but cheaper.

2) The elusive and much sought-after Verizon FiOS, which only exists if you live in a building lucky enough to be wired for that and as far as I know there are only a handful in the 5 Boroughs.

3) RCN, an alternative cable provider, but I'm not sure if they provide service anywhere in the neighborhood. Worth looking into, though.

That said, if you stick with Time Warner, I would definitely recommend getting your own cable modem. Get one made by Motorola through Amazon or NewEgg. You'll have to call up Time Warner's tech support and tell them you want to use your own modem and after bouncing you around through several layers of support they'll finally do a provisioning request to allow MAC address of the new modem to be used with your account. It will take a couple of days for the provisioning request to go through, then you'll need to return the modem to a TWC service center (there's one at the Queens Mall). After that you may, or may not, have better service but at least you won't have to give TWC that extra $5 every month!
