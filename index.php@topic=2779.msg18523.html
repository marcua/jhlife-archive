I can't tell you about the move from the LES, but I do commute every day to the financial district.  The E train goes directly to the WTC stop.  It takes around 45-50 minutes.  You can also take the R to Lex and switch to the 4 & 5.  If you're lucky, the 4 & 5 can knock ten minutes off the trip.  All in all, figure just under an hour.  I have many friends who live on the LES and while it can be a fun place, you don't have the same sense of community and tranquility you can get here.  The tree lined streets this time of year make the extra time commuting worth it.  Also, my friends paid twice as much for a place 30% smaller than the one we have.

Hi, I have a business on the LES, and also in the Village. As much as i love the city, i cant wait to leave after I'm done working! The weekends there are torture, like you were saying with all the bars and clubs. Ive lived here for 20 years, first in a 1200 sq foot pre war, than a private home. When i was younger, i lived in the city, and got it out of my system. As you get older, space and quiet mean alot. As far as the commute, i took the subways for years, but now i drive in, but fortunately, the opposite times of rush hours. You need to take the time and check out a few open houses and see what the apts are like. I don't know what your space is like, but jackson Heights is known for its large pre war Apts. Everything is cheaper here, that's for sure, and you ll get almost everything you need on 37Th ave. Except of course books and clothes! But, we have good supermarkets, Meat markets, and a number of really good restaurants, that are affordable. And one bonus is that you can actually have a car!! The Jackson Heights Beautification Group is having a walking tour in June. Its a great way to get a feel of the neigh boorhood. Best of Luck

The way I see it, it is all about compromise.  I would rank what is most important to you.  If it is peace, tranquility, more room and affordability are most important--then Jackson Heights is your place.  If you still want some night life, bars and quasi-hip culture then Jackson Heights might not be the best place for you (unless you are Latino and/or LGTG). I have found it to be a very good compromise for me.  I don't hang out much during the week but can take a cab or subway easily on the weekends to get together with friends.  If you have a car even easier.  I have to say that I have met some really nice people here (one couple from the L.E.S.).  Most have moved over here to start families.  Good luck.

I agree with Griswold Girl -it is all about compromise.  My partner and I moved here about a year ago -from Hoboken.  One of the reasons we moved was to escape the 'bar crawl' crowds and the noise associated.  Take the subway here after work and sample the many restaurants, and then go for a walk -in the historical district.  One of the nicest things about living here is that we actually know our neighbors -and they know us.  We have no regrets at all about the move and the commute is a non-issue for us.  (it's better compared to Hoboken) If you move to JH it's likely you'll have more space, less stress, and possibly spend less on living expenses each month.  Good Luck.

We moved to JH from East Village a few years back, because a studio was just too stifling after a while. And with a lower rent, we got 3 times more space. In EV, we stopped going outside from Fri evening till Mon morning to avoid puking individuals. I have to say that JH is not necessarily quieter for us though, as we faced the back in EV and have windows facing traffic in JH. But we didn't move to JH thinking it would be like living in the country...we like that it does still feel like a city. Neighbors could just be as loud or annoying. At least, the apt now is bigger so we can walk around....in fact, many weekends now we are content to just be at home. We cook more.

Commute wise:  In the beginning I did dread the long express stop before Roosevelt Ave. I would fall asleep and wake up feeling it's taking forever. It does take a while to get to LES to meet friends, but super easy to get to P.S. 1, MoMA, Flushing, mid-town and we ended up loving our lives. If you live near the 74th St stop, rather than 82nd, then the commute will be shorter.

I think when people say that JH is cheaper they are comparing market rents.  I would agree with popular opinion and say that rents are cheaper in JH than in many other desirable locations like Manhattan and parts of Brooklyn.  However, I am also aware that rent prices have dropped in Manhattan and I don't think they have dropped the same way in JH because the rent prices here were always a good value and not particularly overpriced.  From my vantage point, I don't think sales prices have dropped here at all either, in the way that they have dropped on high-end properties in Manhattan and new condos in Williamsburg (for example).

I lived at 2nd and 2nd for over 10 years but now live in Jackson Heights.  I love my new neighborhood and appreicate it for how different it is from the East Village.  Groceries are easier to obtain and less expensive.  The people in my neighborhood really appreciate living here.  Our Sunday farmer's market is top notch and a real joy to frequent. 

I take the E train from Jackson Heights to Soho (Canal Street stop, one stop from WTC) every morning.  My time on the train is about 20-30 minutes to Canal Street.  Sometimes I wish the trip were a tad shorter.  But the nice thing about JH is that no matter where I am in Manhattan in the evening (or where I need to go in the AM before work), I can go directly by train between JH and either the east or west side because we have so many different trains which come to the neighborhood.  There are few other neighborhoods in the city that allow u to do this.

Feel free to email me directly for more info.

Good luck in your search and decision!

I lived on 8th St and C for a year before moving to JH. I will be honest, I was a bit sad for a while when i first moved. In the EV I would walk out on my doorstep and the world would be at my feet - people travelled far and wide to come and hang out in my neighborhood. However, I think of it like this. Living in the EV, I hardly spent any time in my apt. I now pay less than half of what I paid in rent in the EV and have so much room!! Why was I paying so much money on a place I hardly was and wasn't happy with? I also noticed that my friend who lived in JH would get home before me whenever we went out - this is because she could take almost any train to get home where as I was stuck with the F or L and STILL had a 15 min. walk! Best of all, where I live is like a summer home - big beautiful windows and trees and quiet. You can get to the city in a blink of an eyelash but sleep in a relaxing, quiet neighborhood and have cash left over for those vintage boots you've been eyeballing AND that delicious dinner WITH dessert!

Yes, it is a compromise. And right now JH always was the right choice for me.
