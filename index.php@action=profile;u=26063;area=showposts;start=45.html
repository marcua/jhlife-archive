Restaurants & Food / Re: Greenmarket purchases
« on: October 26, 2014, 01:03:28 PM »
I almost always get a hunk of cheese from the guy on 78th right near the corner (I forget the name). He's always generous with samples so I try to get new things (and he takes credit cards, which is a plus). I often will get baked goods at the stand near the corner of 77th- they have great coffee cakes and the like. Ronnybrook is also great- I especially love their ice cream, which is cheaper and tastier than some of the brands at the super markets! I tend not to buy too many veggies because I'm also doing a CSA farm share and it's a struggle to get through that every week, but sometimes I can't resist some of the unusual produce at the stand that's maybe 3 or 4 in from the corner on 78th- they have a wide variety and cook up samples so you can try new things. This summer I was addicted to their fresh chickpeas and fava beans! Also can't go to the market these days without picking up some cider, as well

Pets and Animals / Re: New dog owner in Jackson Heights
« on: June 21, 2014, 05:07:43 PM »
I will second a recommendation for Doctor Tello's office- very friendly and helpful staff. The dog run at 69th is alright, but if you have a car I'd recommend going to Sherry Park down at Queens Blvd. It's much bigger, so dogs have more room to run, and there are often kiddie pools set up in the summer- my pup loves it. The other advantage is that there always seem to be other dogs there- I've found 69th to be a bit hit or miss as to whether there's anyone else for my dog to play with.

Restaurants & Food / Re: El Rico Tinto
« on: August 09, 2013, 03:03:15 PM »
I've been there twice now, and have been very impressed. The staff is super friendly, and the space is clean, bright, and cheery.

I stopped in last night on my way home from work to grab some empanadas to go. I tried the spinach, and they were very good! And Missmarty, to answer your question, they didn't reheat them. They were stored in a small box (sort of like a toaster oven? Not sure what it's called) that kept them at a steady, warm temperature.

Today hubby and I went in for lunch. You order at the counter and then they bring the food out to you, unless you order to go. I tried the veggie burrito, and he got the arepas con chorizo. Both were very good. They also have a pretty extensive juice menu, so we got the pineapple and the passionfruit (marancuya). Refreshing!

I think El Rico Tinto could be great for the neighborhood. I hope they do well!
