Supermarket Rip Offs!
« on: May 15, 2010, 09:57:55 AM »
I am getting really tired of the attitude in Met Supermarket on 37th/77th.  Been going in there for 10 years and there is only one person who appears to be somewhat pleasant - the lady who takes your bags!  Cashiers for the most part are surly, mangers or whom I believe to be managers are cocky and arrogant.  BUT...what really gets me is when they do not honor what they advertise and stonewall and are damned rude.  Case in point  try to get the English muffins on sale this week.  Circular says buy1 (at an inflated price) and get two free - original, honey wheat, whole grain.  They say no it is only for the original.  They even ripped the sign down in front of the display.  I know it sounds trivial but they have such an attitude and this is not the first time they have done that.  Is there any recourse or official place to lodge a complaint.  Yep, I am on a soap box now.  They got me nuts! Love to start a picket line to call attention to their less than fair practices.  At least at Trade Fair if you have a problem they usually resolve it to the customers satisfaction.

Anybody else experience similar bad attitude and customer service like this at Met???

I've never noticed people at the Met, specifically, acting that way, but it's rare to find a happy grocery worker. Also, it's been my experience that when employees of a certain establishment (regardless of what it is) are systemically unpleasant or rude, it's usually because they are not being treated well at their jobs or are otherwise unhappy with some part of their lives. Your experiences there don't sound fun, but it also sounds as though these folks are in a bad way. I would feel sorry for them.  On top of that, if you know that this is the kind of treatment you will get when you go there, having gone there for 10 years, then (not trying to sound harsh or mean) you should know what to expect by now, right? Maybe try to keep it in perspective, so that when you don't get the good deal on the muffins or when people there are belligerent to you, you can remember that you're dealing with sad and unhappy people, and maybe cut them some slack. And, if you don't like the way the business is run overall, there are plenty of other grocery stores!
