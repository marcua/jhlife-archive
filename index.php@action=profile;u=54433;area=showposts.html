« on: June 16, 2016, 03:15:26 PM »
If anything it seems to me JH is in less of a bubble than much of Brooklyn and Manhattan.  I bought here 2 years ago because it just seemed ridiculous how much real estate you could get here given almost all factors I could compare to places I saw in brooklyn, manhattan and LIC/astoria.  If anything it seems to me this may only be the beginning for real estate in JH, since rising prices will also lead to even more desirable businesses and stores locating here.  Really the main thing we don't have here compared to places in Brooklyn that are still twice the price and the same distance from Manhattan is certain types of businesses, but those may come as the residents who seek them out arrive, only further improving value here.

Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Luxury living takes off in Jackson Heights at posh new building
« on: August 30, 2014, 06:41:15 PM »
Are people really renting those apartments for those prices?  I know the building reasonably well since I put an offer on one of the 3br units and viewed a lot of the units just before they went rental almost a year ago.  At the time most of the units had been sitting on the market for years.  I liked some of the units, but even the triple-pane glass and privacy coating on all the 7-train-facing windows seemed quite insufficient and I don't think I'd have expected they'd have an easy time renting any of those apartments.

The construction looked alright, and it did stand out as being seeing fairly modern compared to most of what is available in the area.  There were a lot of places where they really skimped on materials and space, though.  All the counters seemed to be two pieces of granite sandwiched together with the seam visible on all of the countertop edges, which seemed very odd.  And the bathrooms were about as small as you can comfortably make a bathroom and the bedrooms were not especially spacious and some of the floorboards I saw were warped.  I'm not sure it would be considered a luxury building in other more expensive neighborhoods like LIC, williamsburg, downtown brooklyn, or anywhere in manhattan.  I guess it's all relative, though.

The rent prices appear to all be about $1000 more than the monthly payments would have been on buying any of those units, which seems really exorbitant and unrealistic so I'm skeptical, but good for them if they actually found people to pay that...
