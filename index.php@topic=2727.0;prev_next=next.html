Hi Adam,

Good for you for doing research on a coop that you are interested in.  I just wanted to mention, though, that soliciting opinions about buildings on this board may not yield the same results as talking to people in person, say, by going by to look at the building during the day and seeing if you can catch anyone there to see if they can tell you how they like it.

Because coops are investments, it stands to reason that the current shareholders will not go online to post negative reviews.  Also, because this board is not as anonymous as it may seem (as many members here run into each other in real life) people may also not want to go on the record expressing an opinion one way or the other in such a public way, even if they have something positive to say, perhaps because they do not want their screenname associated with a specific address, or any other reason that has to do with protecting their privacy.

Sometimes people may offer opinions when they don't necessarily have much current information.

I would also advise you that many things can change in a coop over the course of 10 years.  I don't know anything firsthand about the building you are asking about, but I believe that there were many buildings that were once on the brink of financial disaster a decade or two ago that now have quite handsome reserve funds.

It's always good, whether you plan to rent or buy, to go look at the building you are considering at different times of the day or night to check for traffic noise, neighboring establishment noise, a creepy nighttime feeling, or whatever else you want to check out.
