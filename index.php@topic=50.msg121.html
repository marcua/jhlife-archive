News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Hi.  I've been living at the east end of Woodside for about six months.  I've lived in every borough of NYC throughout my life (all 46 years) and grew up in The Bronx.  Although I technically live in Woodside (64th St.), I spend a lot of time around the 73rd-75th Street area (for Indian food and music, great supermarkets, the nice and fast E train...), so I kind of feel as though I am resident of Jackson Heights.

I arrived here via a Jackson Heights blog that I discovered because they linked to some reviews that I wrote about some CDs that I'd bought in one of the stores on 74th Street.  I do a blog on global electronic music, among other things.  (Maybe I'll send the URL for that...sometime.)  Anyway, I'm looking forward to getting more deeply acquainted with the neighborhood.  I've had to move a lot lately (bad apartment luck), but I'd like to settle in the Jackson Heights area for a while.

Howdy 

I'm Andrew. Born and raised in Jackson Heights (OK well I was born in St. Vincnet's Hospital, but they brought me home to JH just as soon as they could).

Grew up here, went to PS 69, IS 145, an did a stint in Astoria @ Bryant High School (Class of '93). Nice to see a fellow alum on here  NYCMacUser. 

I worked in film and theatre production for a long time, and after a 4 year gig @ Queens Theatre in the Park I left the world of arts administration to be a teaching artist. I can, at times, be found at the Renaissance Charter School (founded by my 4th grade teacher) creating theatre with the 9th, 10th, and 11th graders. If you're curious to see what I do, we made a documentary about a year ago: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9hufzu2ylAo

I'm having a great time rediscovering my neighborhood, I've found a new network of friends, some great bars  , and have been getting a little more involved in the political scene. I'm happy we have a place online to share ides and keep on eye on JH's next (or current) transition.
