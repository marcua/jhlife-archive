How About a Great, Local Food Court
« on: January 28, 2015, 03:32:32 AM »
All of the talk of what could or should go into the Bruson Building, all of the talk of what we'd all like to see in Jackson Heights, and all of the talk about what could manage to pay the high, local rents--how about an amazing, truly local, non-chain food court worthy of the diversity and great food offerings of our neighborhood.  Imagine!  Our own version of Smorgasburg or even the Flushing Golden Mall but instead with, for example, a dozen different kiosks offering the best momos, arepas, tacos, dosas, thalis, halal items, Indian sweets and much more.  The vendors could combine on the high rent and other costs and not only would it provide a service for the neighborhood (including a shared seating area for large and small groups) but also would be a nice lure for visitors, a way of showing some of the things--diversity, great food, friendly community spirit--that we offer in a way that makes Jackson Heights so special.  Take the extra care to make it look great, friendly and inviting, great music (representing all of our local residents).  Seems like something that could work.  What do you all think?

An amazing model is the Gotham West Market (http://gothamwestmarket.com/).  The developers figured out that they could get people to buy overpriced apartments all of the way over on 11th Avenue by creating a curated high-end food court/bar/hang-out space on the ground floor -- essentially a public living room.   They've created something quite unique.   But I think it only works by having a high density of oversalaried 20- and 30-somethings living upstairs.   In Jackson Heights, there just fewer people with the disposable income and/or disposable time needed for a place like this to flourish.

That said, I think the JH Food Court is a perfect location, and could be much, much better if the owner cared enough to recruit more imaginative vendors.
