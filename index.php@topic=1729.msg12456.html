Free Learn to Ride a Bike class

Don't know how to ride a bike?  It's not too late to learn!

Bike New York will host one final Learn to Ride class for adults this season on Sunday, November 9 in Jackson Heights.

Bikes and helmets will be provided free of charge.  All you need to bring is a warm layer or two and a good attitude.  A few dollars for a hot drink or snack from the nearby farmers market might not hurt, either.

LEARN TO RIDE CLASS
Sunday, November 9
9am - 1pm
Jackson Heights Play Street
78th Street between 34th Ave. and Northern Blvd., adjacent to Travers Park

You must register in advance at http://www.bikenewyork.org/education/classes/learn_to_ride_adults.html.

Please direct any questions to emiliac@bikenewyork.org.  Thanks!

Check out the Bike New York website for other dates/locations for Lucas. If not, I can teach him. I did two "teach a kid to ride" sessions over the summer. The technique involves taking the pedals off, and getting the kid to push off until he feels comfortable balancing and steering the bike. Then put the pedals back on and run behind them, holding only the seat. Do that between 20 and 300 times and they'll get it! My kids learned in about 2 to 3 hours.
