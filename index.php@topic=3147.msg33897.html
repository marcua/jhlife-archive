Brunch at La Gloria
« Reply #9 on: January 12, 2014, 06:18:09 PM »
A few years ago when my wife and I discovered La Gloria, we were psyched.  It seemed to be a restaurant that cared about its appearance and offered an interesting take on cuban food.  Loved the paella and we saw some great live latin jazz on a friday night.  Not to mention it has an awesome outdoor section (great for summer sangria)! 

We were also even more excited to find out they had brunch.  For 10 bucks you can get fresh baked muffins, a mimosa, and cool brunch choices like chorizo sliders.  So excited were we about finally having a great sorely needed brunch option in the hood that we would invite out of JH friends to come over to experience.  The response was always the same.  "Yum!" and "This place gets it!"

Unfortunately I have to report that our opinion has changed.  While it seems cool to have a DJ spinning during brunch, we noticed that over time the music got waaaayyy too loud (other members of this community have posted on this).  Once we braved the volume and found ourselves screaming to talk to each other, and the next time (hoping it was a fluke) we didn't even bother going in (too loud for children).   

Today we decided to give it another go, and we were shocked.  Originally, the music was originally old school funk, soul and some 80's hip hop, which is cool.  Now however they have a DJ spinning 90's gangster rap.  Not that I have a problem with this music, I've spent my fair share of time listening to Wu Tang, but between the blasting bass and the constant F Bombs, N words and pot references I felt embarrassed for taking my child there.  At first my wife and I thought, "Well, maybe they don't want kids or families," but then we saw a children's menu!  There were at least 6 children there under the age of 10 forced to listen to music that teacher's would get fired for if they played it in a class. 

Another disappointing point was that we noticed diners no longer got the fresh muffins.  As far as my meal, the potatoes were at room temperature and the eggs in the eggs benedict were completely solid, when they are supposed to be runny.  My wife however loved her cubano and her fries were hot and crispy.

So, while in the past I have raved about this place and their brunch, I have to sadly say they actually don't get it.  I know I write this at the risk of sounding like an old fuddy duddy, but I truthfully couldn't believe what I was hearing, with kids in the room.  Bad taste!

I'm interested in hearing others chime in with their thoughts.  Maybe after a few posts I'll send a link to this thread to La Gloria's email so they will get the feedback. 

In the meantime.  I'm keeping my fingers crossed that the wine bar/brunch joint planning to open on 82nd and Northern will be awesome!

Re: Brunch at La Gloria

Stopped by La Gloria yesterday for their brunch (party). There was no confusion about it -- advertised as an all day (through the night) party with some pretty good hip-hop DJ's spinning (tony touch, cre-8, butchrock).

Got there around 3:30p, and while there was a good crowd in there, we didn't have any problem being seated immediately without a reservation. The brunch menu was varied between cuban and dominican cuisine. Immediately upon ordering a $12 entree of pollo saltado (smallish plate of white rice and some orange colored chicken with peppers), we were given the included mimosa. The DJ was playing a mix of old school to 90's hip-hop music up front and the crowd was mostly the hip-hop crowd from Corona, Jackson Heights, Woodside, Astoria, etc. Lots of filipinos owing to the fact that the DJ (butchrock) was also filipino. The volume was definitely on the high side - I wouldn't bring a crowd here if you plan to chit-chat and catch up.

Right about 5p, the DJ packed up and moved over next door to the bar area. The crowd quickly followed suit and by 6p bottles were being paraded out with sparklers in fairly quick succession. I didn't see any tabs being settled so I assume these were mostly complimentary bottles for the promoters and their friends. Beers were $7 each.

Great place to listen to good hip-hop tunes but not a good place to have a brunch with the kids.
