Interesting indeed. While I'm with the OWS movement in spirit, the places these peaceful demonstrators should be camping out is in front of their local elected officials' offices. We can only change the system when government makes the financial services industry accountable for their fraudulent schemes, and also practices good fiscal responsibility itself.

The OWS movement is woefully misinformed, especially with their proclamation to "Resist Austerity." Even taxing our corporations and "the 1%" up to wazoo is not going to turn around our economy, erase our deficit, or restore prosperity to the middle and working classes. Major austerity measures and cuts to social programs will have to be part of the long-term financial planning that our nation has to undertake now. No political party or presidential candidate is going to have a magic bullet that will turn our economy around without sacrifice. We'll see how seriously our current politicians take their oaths when the 12-member super-committee issues their recommendations on November 23.

The best we can hope for is that when we are asked to dig into our pockets a little deeper, that sacrifice is proportionally and equally shared by corporations and individuals at all income levels, and that as a nation, we finally prioritize education, public health, and the environment above special interests, big industry, and the military.

Quote from: StevenGrey on November 16, 2011, 02:41:53 PM
Interesting indeed. While I'm with the OWS movement in spirit, the places these peaceful demonstrators should be camping out is in front of their local elected officials' offices.

The reason they aren't doing so is part of a strategy: the movement understands that it is not yet big enough, or influential enough, to get government officials to do what they don't want to do (which is hold their major campaign contributors, and potential post-incumbency employers, to account for looting the economy.) So the movement is out in public as a way of raising awareness and attracting more people to the group. Only once it is much bigger could it effect the kind of change you're describing.


[
Quote
]The OWS movement is woefully misinformed, especially with their proclamation to "Resist Austerity."[

Not at all. They're encouraging resisting austerity as a reflex. Governments around the world have considered austerity to be the first (and sometimes the only) step towards restoring the economy. OWS is about a shifting of priorities. If austerity is necessary, let it be the last step, or somewhere further down on the list than step #1. Let it come after asking those who profited from the recent boom to pay their share, after restructuring the financial system and after reconsidering whether we should be paying to defend foreign governments from vague threats. We resist austerity now because if we don't, the powerful will simply impose austerity on the less powerful and declare the job done, their privileges intact.
