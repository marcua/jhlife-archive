Community Groups, Activities & Events / Make 2020 Your Year of Living Purposefully
« on: January 05, 2020, 09:16:44 AM »
Are you navigating a transition or about to embark on one? Feeling “stuck”? Or, perhaps you need to kick-start your dream or reconnect with what’s most meaningful for you. If so, join us for an afternoon of reflection and self-discovery to ground yourself in your purpose and take practical steps toward its fulfillment.

January 12, 2020
2 PM-5 PM
Jackson Heights, Queens

In this 3-hour workshop, you will:
· Understand the difference between your activities and your purpose
· Highlight and explore your “moments that matter” for clues about your purpose
· Craft your “purpose statement” to focus your choices and keep you grounded you on a  daily basis
· Create a vision board* to express, clarify and maintain focus on your purpose
· Make an action plan to live your purpose

*a collage of your selected images and words used for visual inspiration
$50 a person
RSVP to ngoldman@heightshubstorytelling.com for our address

This workshop blends individual exercises such as reflection, writing and collaging, with paired and group activities to help you articulate your purpose and get input from other participants. It takes place in a comfortable and welcoming home setting, with healthy snacks and comfortable seating. FYI – there is a cat.

Workshop Leaders
Paul Kimmerling is a corporate consultant, workshop leader, certified life coach and spiritual director. His curiosity about purpose led him to create his interview-based podcast, People On Purpose. With his guests, he explores the numerous ways individuals discover and express their purpose. Paul expresses his own purpose by crafting collaborative and creative explorations for groups and individuals to aid their discovery and learning. www.PaulKimmerling.com. Paul@PaulKimmerling.com.

Nancy Goldman, Ed.D. is a member of faculty at New York University where she teaches adults to lead themselves and others in the workplace through storytelling, creative thinking and mindfulness practices. She believes in the power of narrative to transform, and sees vision-boarding as a way to story your future. www.NancyGoldman.com. Dr.NancyGoldman@gmail.com

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Storytelling show and open Mic at Terraza 7
« on: September 09, 2019, 09:12:02 PM »
“The Long and Short of It” is a monthly storytelling show and open mic in Jackson Heights. Come to hear master storytellers tell and share stories that will put you in suspense, change your thoughts on the world as you think of it today, or just give you another perspective on what somewhat else has experienced. And you can put your name in our bucket to participate in our open mic by telling a story of your own.
 
 “The Long and Short of It” is hosted by Nancy Goldman, producer and host of “Come Over For Coffee,” a storytelling home concert in Jackson Heights. In addition to performing at The New York Story Exchange and Word The Storytelling Show she has presented a TedX talk about the power of personal storytelling and teaches a course about storytelling in organizations at New York University.
 
We are often asked who attends these shows and what to expect. If you like stories, are inquisitive about other people's lives and enjoy meeting new and interesting New Yorkers then this is for you. Shows are held on the third Monday of the month from 7pm to 8:30pm at Terraza 7 located at 40-19 Gleane Street, near 83rd Street and Roosevelt Avenue. You can purchase tickets at the door or email heightshub@gmail.com for ticket information.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Laughter Yoga in Jackson Heights
« on: September 05, 2018, 08:16:38 AM »
Hi Everyone,

You've heard the expression "Laughter is the best medicine."  Well, it's true.  Laughter Yoga is a form of exercise that uses laughter to jog your internal organs. Anyone can do it.  No yoga experience necessary. You just need to clap, breathe and laugh.  There's no impact on the joints but there's positive impact on the body (it brings more oxygen to the brain; strengthens the immune system; and reduces pain and stress.)

I'll be hosting monthly laughter yoga sessions on the following Tuesdays from 7pm to 8pm:  September 18, October 16 and November 13.  Suggested donation is $5/person.

Email heightshub@gmail.com for location and further information.

Nancy

Neighborhood Chat / World Listening Day Event - Wednesday, July 18
« on: July 14, 2018, 11:08:49 AM »
Wednesday, July 18th is World Listening Day. To mark this occasion, we invite you to experience and honor the power of listening by joining us in a sound walk. A soundwalk is any excursion whose purpose is listening to the environment. During our soundwalk, we will stroll through some typical Jackson Heights streets, and focus our attention on what we hear as we go. By remaining silent during our walk, we allow our sense of hearing to come to the forefront of our experience.
 
We will meet at 7:00pm at a nearby Jackson Heights location, do a brief “warm-up” exercise to focus our listening skills, and review our walk plan. Then, we’ll walk together for 20-30 minutes. We’ll regroup afterwards to share what the experience was like.
 
Want to join us? Need more information? Please email heightshub@gmail.com.
 
For more information about World Listening Day, please follow this link:
https://www.worldlisteningproject.org/2018/01/world-listening-day-2018/
