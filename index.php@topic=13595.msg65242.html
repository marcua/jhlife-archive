Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

"It'll be rush-hour pain over the next three weeks for riders on the busy Queens Boulevard line.

Starting Monday, commuters will have to book extra time during their morning and evening train rides on weekdays through Sept. 4.

The Metropolitan Transportation Authority is cutting back service on the E, F, M and R lines for maintenance work on the express track.

Speeds on E and F express trains will have to slow to 10 mph through work zones. Meanwhile, some E and F trains will run local, meaning fewer M and R trains on that track each hour."

i work in the same area and take the E daily from Jackson Heights. the ride takes about 35 minutes. it is a reliable train and there are rarely serious delays with this train. it also runs frequently during rush hour which is a plus because if you miss one, the next train will be arriving momentarily. it can get crowded but it clears out by the time you get to midtown. i have never had to miss a train because i couldn't get on so i do not think the crowding is that bad (that has happened to me with the 7 train a few times).

sometimes i take the F to West 4th Street if that stops at the platform first and just transfer to the E and take that down a few more stops to WTC. it's an easy transfer, right up the stairs, and usually takes the same amount of time as taking the E directly.

I dont think of it as very crowded compared to many other lines.

The return trip is even more civilized, you can always find a seat if you need it as WTC is the end of the line.
