Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Carlton House - your feedback
« on: January 03, 2015, 10:03:32 AM »
As has been stated before on this thread - The Carlton House does not have a land lease. 

There are many buildings in Jackson Heights that became coops back in the 80's and they do not have land leases either.

As many on this board know, The Carlton House was the first post-war(1947) cooperative completed by the Queensboro Corporation.  The massive development did not do well, so they rented out the units instead.  Their next and final coop, The Greenbrier(1949) was on a much smaller scale.
