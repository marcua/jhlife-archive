Eater Map: Where to Eat in JH
« on: August 08, 2019, 02:41:23 PM »
Robert Sietsema is out with his eagerly awaited map of places to eat in Jackson Heights.

His list:

1. Taste of Lahore
2. Cannelle Patisserie
3. Al-Naimat Sweets & Restuarant
4. Mustang Thakali Kitchen
5. Samudra
6. Taqueria Coatzingo
7. Café Salamanca
8. Raan Kway Teow
9. The Queensboro Restaurant
10. El Perro
11. La Boina Roja
12. Copacabana
13. El Maguey
14. Picanteria El Austro
15. Papa's Kitchen
16. Juanita’s
17. Pizza Sam
18. Tacos Morelos

All winners (Mostly)!  I might quibble a bit at the edges of this list, but they're largely solid suggestions.

But there are so many more treasures that didn't get included.   What's on your list of can't-miss JH eateries that should be included on Eater's map?   I'm going to put my thoughts together and post later tonight.

Okay, here's my must-not-miss list:
1. El Rico Tinto
2. Urubamba
3. Bistro Eloise
4. Arepa Lady
5. Unidentified Flying Chickens
6. Delhi Heights or Sagar
7. La Esquina Del Camaron Mexicano
8. Kitchen 79
9. Pio Pio (okay, it's a chain that can be found elsewhere, but they do a great job and remain extremely popular)
10. Many-way-tie for superb Himalayan restaurants, especially Lali Guras, Himalayan Yak, Lhasa Fast Food, Phayul, and Little Tibet.

And I can't resist a few substitutions as well...
- Arepas Burger instead of El Perro
- Dosa Delight instead of Samudra
- El Chivito D'Oro instead of La Boina Roja

These sorts of lists are always a bit suspicious. You have to consider whether the source has some sort of personal relationship or familiarity with the establishments that make the cut. I also find it rather useless to just have the businesses named without some comment about why they were picked: Was it for a particular dish they are known for or is everything on the menu wonderful? Do they get customer service and ambiance done better than their nearby competitors? I've had disappointing experiences with particular establishments on both of these lists, so if people are going to give more of their personal preferences or recommendations here, I'd like to know why.

Sietsema also doesn't like writing about places that other people have written about a lot recently, and I think that's a good thing. For instance, he is avoiding the whole momo trend, I think in part because he thinks it's overheated. Which it is, at the moment. Good but overheated, press-wise.

I agree that Coatzingo has declined in quality ... where am I ordering from now? Mostly ERT and Morelos, I guess. Also I think Salamanca is a weird choice, but he does make the point there are very few Spanish restaurants in NYC.

Now that I write that, I wonder if Sietsema is living a bit in the past. The Colombian hot-dog situation, Boina Roja, Salamanca, Coatzingo - they were all awesome in, like, 2008, right? And they may have passed their prime.
