Title: The Queensboro!
Post by: Dudley on February 27, 2018, 09:34:18 AM
Hello JH Life! You might remember me from posts such as "Hey the Queensboro is coming soon!"  ;)

Well, guess what - it is coming soon, and we are super excited about it.

For those of you who might not know the story, myself and two other neighborhood guys have been trying to open up a great neighborhood restaurant/bar for several years. We've lived in Jackson Heights for years, we're parents of Jackson Heights kids, and we're into the Jackson Heights community - I'm part of the JH Green Alliance that helped bring the Rory Staunton field into existence, helped make the 78th Street Plaza a reality, etc... we're involved in Farm Spot, in our kids' schools, we hang out in the neighborhood and we love it here.

We finally found a space, signed the lease, raised some money and started construction, and then ran into delays and roadblocks. We've worked our way through them all, and we are finally almost there! Construction is completed, our partnership remains strong, and we have closed out almost all of our permits. It's difficult to say exactly when we'll be able to open our doors because the DoB works in mysterious ways, but it's definitely not a long time away now - a matter of weeks!

I must say with all sincerity that it's looking really great!

(https://i.imgur.com/D1CsRC1.jpg)

(https://i.imgur.com/3P1omgd.jpg)

(https://i.imgur.com/d6vf5Xi.jpg)

We're going to have mostly Queens beers, a delicious range of crafted cocktails, and - what I'm most excited about - amazing food by Chef Tony Liu who has worked as the executive chef in several Keith McNally restaurants. He is the real deal, his food is going to be delicious and exciting, with nods to the neighborhood as well as seasonal dishes using green market ingredients. To give you an idea of what the menu will be, we're structuring it as an American style neighborhood bistro, and to top it off we have a monster pizza oven that's going to be put to good use, and not just for pizza!

We're going to hire locals as much as possible (almost all of the resumes we've gotten so far have been from people who live nearby), we're keeping the prices reasonable, we're going to be there for small groups, big groups, parties, special occasions, afternoon snacks, brunch!!!, dinner, lunch, quick drinks, slow drinks, late night drinks, romantic dinners, family gatherings, great music at a reasonable volume, a place to hold the meetings for your community group, to plan political action, to impress a date...

But now we need your help, Jackson Heights!

All the delays we ran into have been tough, so it would be very helpful to us at this point to get some money to help pay for the last few things that we need. We have in the past asked some close friends to buy gift cards that they can use later - basically paying now for future drinks and food - and we're widening the net in the hope of raising more funds now. We have also asked for investments from friends and family, if that is something you'd like to find out more about feel free to contact me directly (dudley@thequeensboro.com), but if you're able to help us by purchasing a gift card now, we'd really appreciate it.

You can do that here:

http://thequeensboro.com/support/ (http://thequeensboro.com/support/)


I'll keep an eye on this thread and answer any questions you may have, or anything else that comes up.

Thank you!

Seasonal American Fare with a Queens Twist Comes to Jackson Heights
BY J. A. STRUB | DECEMBER 31, 2018

For years, a humble triumvirate of Jackson Heights residents dreamed of opening a bistro. In 2015, they embarked on a long and winding road that eventually lead to the Queensboro, a new restaurant on Northern Boulevard that puts a local twist on the popular New American format.

“Everybody talks about the nightmarish process of opening a restaurant in New York, but we had no idea it would be this intense,” recalls cofounder Dudley Stewart. “Conflicts over permitting, issues with contractors, losing money every day. We put our house on the line, used pretty much everything we owned as collateral.”

“Our goal was to open the kind of place that the three of us have always wanted in the neighborhood,” says fellow co-founder Michael Fuquay. “There are so many fantastic food options in Jackson Heights, so we wanted to add something new and needed. To emphasize fresh and local ingredients, a convivial atmosphere, and an elevated beverage program – that was always our aim.”

When searching for an executive chef, Stewart and Fuquay brought on their neighbor Tony Liu as a third partner. Instilled with a love of seafood and pan-Pacific flavors from his childhood in Hawaii, Liu has directed kitchens in some of Manhattan’s most acclaimed destination restaurants. After cutting his teeth at famed French restaurants Lespinasse (now shuttered) and Daniel, Liu has since worked at Mario Batali’s Babbo and the late, storied pizzeria Pulino’s. 

The Queensboro opened the first week of June. The restaurant is spacious yet comfortable, with an all-wood interior and a long, inviting bar. One’s eyes are naturally drawn to the open kitchen and its centerpiece: a massive brick pizza oven.

“We bought this used from a guy in Pennsylvania,” says Stewart. “We’ve gotten it to an internal temperature of close to 900 degrees. It’s the real deal.”

A little more about Steffen:

Steffen's journey into the world of cheese began 20 years ago, as a student in Philadelphia. A part-time job at Downtown Cheese in the Reading Terminal Market turned into a full-time passion. Steffen was drawn to the diversity of cheeses from around the world and the stories behind them. He enjoyed how discussions about cheese often transformed into conversations about life.

After moving to New York, Steffen work at local institutions Ideal Cheese and Artisanal Fromagerie and Bistro. He also guided food and beer tours throughout the city. Steffen dreamed of opening a shop in Queens to provide quality cheese and specialty products to an underserved part of the city.

When his friends at Table Wine (https://tablewine-ny.com/)  encouraged him to check out 37th Avenue, Steffen knew that he had found just the place: an amazing community hungry for cheese, and on June 1, 2019, he opened Stand Alone Cheese in Jackson Heights.[/size]
