Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

since i've already been tagged as 'obstreperous'  for disagreeing with our urban planner, i'll weigh in on this.

Rikers opened 85 years ago - and NYC has changed a lot since then, both in terms of number of people incarcerated (for better or worse) and in terms of number of non-wealthy neighborhoods (shrinking yearly).

I don't think they're likely to reopen the huge prison that once stood on Roosevelt Island, re-convert Jefferson Market Library at 6th and 10th in the village, or return a prison to the waterfront of Staten Island. the small facilities we have now would have to expand by leaps and bounds - and that's not likely in Downtown Brooklyn or the wildly gentrifying part of Lower Manhattan hear our courthouses.

and, while this is off topic, i do think we'd have a bit less noise. more planes using the facility, but that location would seem to push planes over the bronx, more than over us (i think).
