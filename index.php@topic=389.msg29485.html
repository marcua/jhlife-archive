I, along with a few friends, tried Hornado Ecuatoriano on Roosevelt (between 76th & 77th) again on Saturday night.  What a huge disappointment my second time around.  The Hornado (pork) was dry and salty.  The Llapingachos we ordered were small, lacked flavor and were served sans traditional peanut sauce.  Instead of morcilla, they were accompanied by two salty hot dogs.  The Guata (tripe) was also lacking in flavor, and the peanut sauce's texture was much too thick from an over abundance of potatoes.  IMHO, a few leaves of cilantro would have helped boost the flavor.  The bistec encobollado arrived burned along the edges and the aji sauce was a thick paste severely lacking in the heat department.  It's too bad the entrees were not up to par.  That said, the jugo de naranjilla I ordered was good.  I won't be returning.

You guys are so multicultural its a beautiful thang. My mama is Ecuadorian and I have to say there are a few spots but I would say the following are my go to spots.

Picada Azuaya on 37th and 85th I think - very good hole in the wall place (good food, although late night music is loud)
Pequeno Coffee Shop - This is on I think 86th and Roosevelt Ave is very very good. Its literally an Ecuadorian Diner.  I think that's why it might have not been mentioned but this might be the best place all around.  Interior design and wait staff are good.  Good for like a Sunday Lunch.
Barzola  on 92nd and 37th I think- Its a more uppity casual experience but also has good food.  Nice for a date or weekend thing.
