The End of An Era - Grey Nuns of the Sacred Heart (GNSH)
« on: March 06, 2011, 09:49:29 PM »
I am very saddened, but I knew it was coming.

No matter a persons faith, the historical aspects of the following are important.

The Grey Nuns of the Sacred Heart will leave their last place of ministry in the NYC Area, this summer.

As far as I can count historically, the Grey Nuns have been a presence in the Jackson Heights. East Elmhurst, Elmhurst, and Corona areas for about 85 years.

Their last remaining convent, located at Our Lady of Fatima Church will close this coming summer, at one time there was a convent of Grey Nuns at most of our local Catholic Schools.  i.e.: The Catherine Sheridan Center, was previously a Convent, as the cornerstone indicates.

While I am still rather young, I have had interaction with many Grey Nuns in the past, when I was a student at St. Joan of Arc from 1993-1996, there were still 3 Grey Nuns on staff, and there were 2 on staff at Blessed Sacrament.

It is important to note, that the Grey Nuns of the Sacred Heart were responsible for establishing a thriving Food Pantry, named Marguerite's Pantry, after the founder of their religious order, St. Marguerite D'Youville.

Let me also note, in terms of notable individuals in the area, one of Assemblymember Michael DenDekker's teachers was Sr. James Maureen, GNSH, due to health and age reasons, this week she moved back to the Motherhouse in Yardley, PA. as the remaining Grey Nuns are likely to do as well, there are only a few that remain.

This year brought Sr. Alma Regina back to St. Joan of Arc, where hopefully she will remain, along with 2 Sisters of St. Francis Assisi, but still a mystery where they will reside.

I have written so long because when a person thought of the Grey Nuns of the Sacred Heart, our area was always the place that was associated with these great women of service. 

These women have dedicated their lives to serving our community, in my lifetime I have met many nuns who have passed or are currently quite aged, Sr. James Maureen is likely 90 years old, and taught until she was in her early eighties.  Sr. Mary Alice who has since passed, taught well into her 90's, which was until the time of her death.  Sr. Margaret Mary was my Assistant Principal, who became interim Principal, was over 80 years old at the time, and rode the bus daily to and from her convent at Blessed Sacrament.

Let us all try to remember the importance of service to our community as we pay homage to great women associated with this sad ending of an era, during this Women's History Month.

I went to St Mary's Help of Christians grammar school in Woodside, the oldest Catholic church in Queens.  We were taught by the Dominican sisters from Amityville.  The school became a parking lot even though the parishioners came  up with a viable plan to save the school.  Holy mother church in all of its infinite wisdom saw fit to sell the church and the school to a garage ... the faithfl be damned.

The sisters were kind and dedicated to all of us.  Their lives were all about us ... their students.  The church was small and country like.  There was virtue in its plainess.  We were a working class parish with all of the hopes and promise of the American dream.  We received a solid eduation and none of us would dare embarrass our parents or even ourselves with bahavior that was either reprehensible or unseemingly.  We weren't angels but we tried to do our best.  We respected those whose achievements were greater than ours and we respected those who tried their best even if they were not at the top of the class.

I miss the good sisters of Amityville ... especially Sister Vincent Marie.  She was a remarkable teacher whose knowledge of world history and her demands of excellence from all of us changed my life.  I miss that little country church with its marvelous choir.  We learned the mass in Latin. We grew up in this rich atmosphere ... were taught to love the Church, our country and our family.  As took pride in our work and sought to make ourselves good and decent adults.

Holy Mother Church paid off its crushing debt to those victims of molestation by selling its very valuable property which housed those needed churches and schools.  In its infinite wisdom the Church exonerated the sinful clergy by shielding them from the law opening the church to lawsuits from which it could not insure themselves so they sold church property to pay the lawsuits they lost.  The clergy paid nothing.  The innocent faithful lost their parishes and their schools.  God may forgive the clergy but I won't.
