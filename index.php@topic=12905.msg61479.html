Could I ask what you mean by a gastropub?

It's a phenomenon that started in the U.K., where it basically means a drinking establishment that sells premium beers, decent but not great wines and hard liquor, as well as mostly (although not exclusively) traditional food that is prepared to a higher standard than in a basic pub, all of which is priced accordingly. The business model requires high turnover, meaning get them in and get them out unless they keep buying alcohol. TV sets in at least part of the establishment are pretty much a given (sports attract pub clientele), and a lively level of noise is common, certainly as the night wears on, if not encouraged.

As far as I know, U.S. versions of gastropubs don't stray from this model. They are basically drinking establishments with a more interesting selection of beers, etc. and better food than the average drinking establishment, therefore commanding higher prices. And they are based on high turnover and selling a lot of alcohol. It's hard for me to imagine anybody starting a gastropub in Jackson Heights that did not offer clients the ability, at least in the bar area, to watch sporting events on TV.

Is this what you mean, or something else?
