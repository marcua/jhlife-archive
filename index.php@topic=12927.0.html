Tax Deductions you may have missed courtesy of TaxTerminal.com and the Onsite CPA Will in Jackson Heights

No you cant deduct your pet - Unless it is for health purposes like a seeing eye dog !!! :-)

1. Student loan interest.

2. Medical transportation including tolls, parking, and standard mileage deduction of 19 cents per mile for visiting doctors or dentists and picking up medicine.

3. Medical aids such as crutches, canes, and orthopedic shoes.

4. Hearing aids, eye glasses, and contact lenses.

5. Hospital fees for services such as nursing, physical therapy, lab tests, and x-rays.

6. Equipment for disabled or handicapped individuals.

7. Part of life-care fee paid to retirement home designated for medical care.

8. The cost of alcohol and drug abuse treatment and certain stop-smoking treatments.

9. Special school costs for mentally or physically handicapped individuals.

10. Wages for nursing service.

No your yearly vacation to the Bahamas is not a business trip...Sorry cant deduct that :-)

11. State income taxes owed from a prior year and paid in the tax year.

12. Payment by December 31 of your last quarter of estimated state taxes.

13. Personal property taxes on cars, boats, etc..

14. Taxes to a foreign government.

15. Mandatory contributions to state disability funds.

16. Points paid on mortgage or refinancing.

17. Property donated to a recognized charity.

18. Cash contributions to a recognized charity.

19. Mileage incurred in relation to charitable activities at 14 cents per mile.

20. Casualty and theft losses in excess of $100 and totaling more than 10% of adjusted gross income.

21. Education expenses paid to maintain or improve job skills.

22. A handicapped individual's work-related expenses.

23. Professional journals, magazines, and newspapers that are job-related.

24. Seeing eye dog or guard dog for business.

25. Required uniforms and work clothes not suitable for street wear.

26. Union dues.

27. Employment agency fees or commissions.

28. Home office expenses, if primary place of business.

29. Job seeking expenses within present field of employment.

30. New business start-up costs.

31. Dues to professional organizations.

32. Business gifts up to $25 per customer or client.

33. An employee's moving expenses.

34. Business expenses including travel, meals, lodging, and entertainment not reimbursed by employer.

35. Cleaning and laundering services for business travel.

36. Tools bought for use at your job.

37. Cellular phones required for business.

38. Worthless stock or securities.

39. Commission to brokers or agents for sale of property or property management.

40. Fees for tax preparation or advice.

41. Legal fees to collect taxable income.

42. Support of a non-dependent exchange student living with you.

43. Services of a housekeeper, maid, or cook needed to run your home for the benefit of a qualifying dependent while you work.

44. Gambling losses to the extent of winnings.

45. Birth Control if Purchased with a subscription
