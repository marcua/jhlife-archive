Neighborhood Chat / JHBG's Halloween Parade for All - Special Year
« on: October 30, 2014, 08:00:17 PM »
 This is a great year to come to the JH HALLOWEEN parade and show solidarity for our neighborhood, especially the
folks that were burnt out of their businesses last year. There are three Grand Marshals for the parade all from the
Bruson Building fire and we can show them, and our children, how much we support our neighbors when they are
in need.
 Queens Pride, Armondos and Franks are the honorees (don't have the names at hand). Brought to you by the
Jackson Heights Beautification Group (JHBG) and local business as sponsors.The parade has a 5:00pm start time
on 37th Avenue all the way from 89th street to the Halloween bag give-away in front of P.S.69 all on 37th Avenue.
Two local school bands, Scouts, Dance troops and lot's more. It's a celebration and the whole neighborhood is
very welcome.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Queens Day, Aug20th, US Open- free concerts, kids activities,Mary Poppins@night
« on: August 19, 2014, 04:17:08 AM »
  The US Open will host its inaugural “Queens Day” on Wednesday, August 20, featuring Queens-based performers, exhibitions and family activities throughout the grounds of the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center in Flushing, N.Y. Local Queens-based musicians, from jazz, to rap, to traditional Indian, will perform throughout the second day of US Open Qualifying.  Also, in conjunction with the Queens Economic Development Corporation and Chamber of Commerce, information and other offers for Queens businesses will be provided to fans and visitors.
 Doors open at 10:00am. Play begins at 11:oo am.
 Queens Day will include performances from: Mind Open, instrumental and jazz band, Bethany Wild, vocalist and songwriter
Uruza Performing Arts, N’YCE, Derrick Anderson, rap artist, Mestizo, Latin dance,Miss Reign, percussion, poetry and hip-hop
Roopa Mahadevan, Indian Fusion American soul singer, City University of New York, jazz band,Ayazamana Ecuadorian Cultural
Center. The Queens Museum and New York Hall of Science will each have exhibits during Queens Day.

 Also, a free movie screening of “Mary Poppins” will take place in Flushing Meadows-Corona Park, beginning at dusk, at
111th street, near the carousel.
 

                                Doors open at 10 a.m. Admission is free. Play begins at 11 a.m.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Help this Sunday with JH Green Agenda tree care group, 84th St & 34th Ave
« on: August 11, 2011, 07:18:20 PM »
 This Sunday, a group of volunteers is meeting at 34th Ave and 84th Street to focus on street trees in that area. This means helping in whatever
way the trees need. Mulch, staking, widening the tree pits by taking out bricks. Anything that will help the continued growth of the street trees.
Come join this roving band of helpers, even if you have only an hour to spare, as we need all the help we can muster. Keep Jackson Heights
as green as possible with caring for our street trees in need. We are doing this on monthly basis all around Jackson Heights.
  Starting at 9:00 and ending at 12:30. All ages and experience levels welcome. Tree pruners especially welcome. Need someone to work the
table and give information at the site too. Bring gloves and trowels if you have them.
  Come out, meet your neighbors, help the community and get dirty!
