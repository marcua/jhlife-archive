I don't have a proposed expansion handy now, but it narrows as it stretches down toward 70th and up to 90th. JHBG's understanding of the limited initial landmarking is that it probably had to do with avoiding biting off too big an area, and that it would be easier to designate half the area first and then, if successful, come back for a second phase.
I think most people pretty feel landmarking has been a success - both in limiting out of scale development and renovations that would destroy the quality architecture here, without being too heavy handed for most.
When JH was first considered for landmarking, the NYT was very skeptical that immigrant merchants would be at all interested. Some of the immigrant merchants, however, have done the best renovations.
