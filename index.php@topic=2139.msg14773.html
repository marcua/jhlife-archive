Greetings,

I have been living in the same Apt. (a co-op) in J heights for over 6 years.  My downstairs neighbor is an elderly lady whose hearing is getting progressively worse  (She wears a hearing aide).  Unfortunately because of this, She plays her television so LOUD that I can hear every word of the show and quite clearly.  It seems to be coming from every wall in my apartment.   In the past she has only kept her tv on until 6:30 or 7:00 but lately she's going much later with the programs sometimes till ten.

Needless to say it is incredibly disruptive and now getting unbearable.

I have left her nice note asking her to turn it down.  I have spoken to the Co-op president who promises she will speak to my neighbor and also asked her in person to please turn it down; her response "My tv is not loud".

does anyone have other suggestions, advice3  or experiences they could share with me?  I'd be grateful, thanks.

Lex.

It sounds like the Co-Op President needs to pursue this.  There must be something in your lease regarding not bothering the neighbors.  My neighbors had their tv on loud and wouldn't answer their door, so I looked at my rental agreement, and there is a clause in there "not to do anything that is annoying to neighbors".  A loud tv is extremely annoying if you're trying to sleep, read or just have some quiet time.

Keep complaining to the Co-Op President and demand that something be done.  Maybe call her while your neighbors tv can clearly be heard in the background.  As far as your neighbor goes, I think the headset the previous poster mentioned is a great idea for her to wear.

Perhaps the coop could contact the lady's family for assistance. It is possible that she is not wearing her hearing aid when home alone. Lot's of people feel there's nothing to hear when alone. Also, there's a question that the aid is not functioning properly of the tv is that loud. Perhaps, the battery is dead or the ear has too much ear wax.

The lady's family could get her to the audiologist for a hearing eval and hearing aid checkup. Of there is not family, perhaps a social or religious organization could be of assistance.

If you are having this problem, the lady is having problems also. The problem is she just doesn't realize it.  She might just be hurt from finding a note as previously suggested. Remember it is nicer to treat her like you would want your own mom treated. Kindness works better with elderly.  :angel:

Good luck.
