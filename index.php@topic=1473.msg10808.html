US Mail Delivery Problem

I've registered three 'misdirected mail' complaints with the Post Office in the past few weeks. I got a personal response each time, something like this: "We've got several regular carriers on vacation. Hopefully, when they come back, things will improve." Not so reassuring.

Mostly my complaints are about receiving other people's mail - the folks next door, some fella in Corona, a gal on 78th Street (I'm on 93rd) - but I've also had  mail go missing . . . Netflix, specifically. And two boxes of blank checks. That's scary.

Anyhoo . . . I'm sure this happens to lots of people in Jackson Heights, not just me. If you've had similar troubles please register a complaint with the USPS. Their website makes it easy. Maybe the right person will get the message - maybe.

Thanks.

I lived in Brooklyn before I moved to JH.  I have to say that the Postal Service here is a joy in comparison.  If I had a priority package that I KNEW was being delivered a certain date, I would leave a note on the door saying that I was home and to ring the bell.  Once, I actually saw the postman walk up the stoop and then leave without ringing the bell.  I ran down and there was one of those notes saying "we tried to deliver and you weren't home".  I chased him down the street and he just laughed, handed me my package and wouldn't explain why he didn't ring the bell.  I'm talking about priority mail with a tracking number, not just regular mail.  Also,  I would only get mail  three or four  times a week; when I called to complain I was told that the postman didn't want to walk up everyone's stoop every day because it was too tiring!  WTF?  And it was a rare day that I didn't get someone else's mail. 

I think the problem is more with individual employees - if you have a lazy person working your route, you're out of luck.  Or if the manager at a specific post office is a jerk, it only makes sense that the employees won't go out of their way to do a good job for someone they hate working for.  Of course, I am generalizing.  My uncle was a postman in Brooklyn and he loved his job.
