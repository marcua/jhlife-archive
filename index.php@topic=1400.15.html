The remarks made here are not meant in any way to minimize the latest incident in the building on 75th St. (or violence against women in general), to excuse such behavior in any way, or to question how serious and frightening it was for the woman who was targeted. This is simply an effort to supply more information about what is known about the incident. I am a tenant in said building. I did not personally witness any of the following, but have spoken to various tenants who were involved.

The individual who was arrested entered the building around 7 or so in the evening, apparently by waiting for someone to unlock the front door and then following in after them (our building has 100+ units and quite a lot of in-out traffic at this time of day). At some point a little later he entered the elevator, where a tenant, by herself, was going up to her floor. He remarked that she had very pretty feet. Taken aback, she simply said thank you and ignored him. He then asked if he could kiss them (her feet), but at this moment the door opened at her floor and she passed him and got out. As she headed away from the elevator she turned around to glance back and saw him exposing himself. She began to scream and ran toward her apartment. Hearing her screams, almost immediately several tenants came out into the halls, on her floor and others. A chase ensued down the stairs and several residents cornered and held the intruder in the lobby. Someone called 911 to report the incident and call for assistance. It apparently took 25-30 minutes for the police from 115 to respond.

Several details:

--the perpetrator appears to be Hispanic, probably in his 20s.
--he has been arrested before for exposing himself to women and I've been told that he is registered as a sexual offender.
--he has not served time previously for offenses, but is now, with this latest offense, being held until a trial date sometime in October. This latest incident apparently violates conditions allowing him to avoid jail time previously, so there appears a better chance that he will now be given time.
--neither in this latest incident nor in early ones involving this individual was there any physical contact or "assault"--as far as I've been able to ascertain (other than the obviously serious emotional/psychological assault). As I said, this is in no way meant to minimize the seriousness of this behavior!

In general and perhaps more helpfully:

This is the second time this individual was behaving like this in our building. In the first instance (at least that we're aware of), 5 or 6 weeks ago, the super happened to pass him in the basement and, not recognizing him, asked him what he was doing. (Our building has outside entrances into the basement, so people are frequently passing through this level.) The young man left the building at that point, so the super went to our video security cameras and discovered that he had been hanging around the basement for half an hour or so, watching when women came down to enter the laundry room. This was approximately 6 PM in the evening, with a lot of activity in the building, in general. During this period he exposed himself several times without any of the women being aware of it (to the best of my knowledge). (They were in the laundry room, he was in the hallway outside, peering around the doorframe at them). At one point he waited out of sight until a young woman left the laundry and got in the elevator, then tried to run to the elevator and get in with her. Fortunately, the elevator door had already closed. A few minutes later the super encountered him and he left the building.

In this previous case we were able to find video of him entering the main lobby door when someone from the building was leaving. We posted pictures of him (very sharp, easily identifiable images) in several locations besides the lobby, together with an alert to tenants to call 911 if he was seen. We also stressed the need to not let anyone enter the building that the tenant does not know. We left these photos and warnings up for a week. Subsequent to this latest incident we have again posted the photograph and the warnings and plea for tenant vigilance--along with a thank you to the many people who responded so quickly and effectively!

The video camera system has been very helpful in this situation, allowing the latest incident to be taken in the context of a prior one, which may well make the difference in this man having to serve time and/or get additional help. The bottom line continues to be tenant awareness of those coming in and out of the building and being willing to "be rude" and not let people enter on the heels of someone else. A building's security measures are ultimately only as effective as the residents make them.
