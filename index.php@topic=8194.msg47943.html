Travers Park Wish List
« on: March 18, 2013, 11:33:13 PM »
Hi everyone,
After reading several posts on the subject it seems that many of you are as concerned as I am that the new additions of 78th Street and the Garden School property to Travers Park may not be designed in a way which considers a wider range of activities and uses than the park currently encompasses. I thought it could be useful to start a wish list for the design of these additions to Travers Park. I can’t promise that anyone involved in the design will look at this list and take our suggestions into consideration but it is definitely worth a try.

I will start with my wish list for Travers Park:

Space for a greater variety of activities – Currently, Travers Park is mostly geared towards athletic activities and children. It would be nice to see space for activities for a broader range of adults such as sitting and reading and relaxing. The redesign should create a desirable place for all to gather.

Free Wi-Fi.

More seating.

More green space and less pavement.

Forward-thinking, eco-friendly design that incorporates ideas such as: permeable pavement, native plantings, low needs for fertilizer and watering (no, this does not mean more pavement), solar-powered lighting.

This is a rare opportunity and I think it is important that it is well thought out and takes into consideration the needs and desires of our community. It would be great to have our park become an innovative model for all parks in the city.

I’m sure you all have some great ideas to add here and I’m sure I’ll have more to add later.

Thank you for starting this up Matt,

There certainly should be some hearings before hand to determine the contents of the new expanded park. What actually goes into the new expanded park will depend a lot on whether the Garden School rec field can accommodate a youth-sized softball field.

If so, it would be quite reasonable to take much of the athletic field and turn it into a green space - as in lawn, trees, shrubs and flowers, something that the many JHBG/Friends of Travers Park have sought for a couple of decades. It would also be great if we could have permeable surfaces, as someone mentioned, with a rain garden so that the park can absorb all its storm water runoff, and a butterfly garden - both of which they have in Astoria Park.

One thing to remember, though, there is a huge demand for active recreation in JH. As many of us have learned, toddlers grow up at a ridiculously fast pace and they will quickly need places to play ball.

Though it has a new corps of leaders, Friends of Travers Park still believes in “Creating the Best Little Park in NYC.”  Check out there FB page and get involved. Also we are looking to continue our greening of 78th Street this year.
