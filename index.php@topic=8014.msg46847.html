First of all congratulations!  I just had my first child in November and it's an amazing experience.  I too had questions about the daycare options in Jackson Heights and I spent the summer researching, visiting, calling, etc.  It's surprisingly hard to find any information about daycare in NYC in general.  I tried to join the JH yahoo parent group, but I think it's defunct, so I was on my own.  I researched options in JH, UES (where my husband works), and Bayside (where I work). 

Here's what I found:  There are 3 types- Daycare centers, Home daycare, Nanny/babysitter.  Daycare centers get certified to care for children of certain ages, infant care (under 2 years old) being the most rare and expensive.  There are a lot more options for children 2+ in the neighborhood.   I found only one center in JH that has infant care.  It's called Little Elms and it's located in Elmhurst Hospital.  They mostly serve the children of doctors and other staff at EHC but have a few spots for the community.  I visited Little Elms and was very pleased to see a high quality option in the neighborhood with convenient pick up/drop off.  The only drawback was the price.  I think it was something like $450/week which was just too high for my family.  If we had the money, I would have sent our baby there for sure. 

Another place I really liked was Kiddie Academy in Whitestone.  If you have a car, it's only 15 mins away.  It's close to my job.  It was similar to Little Elms in that it's very high quality of care.  They have a lot more space and are located in a much nicer residential area than Little Elms.  It was less expensive:  $350/week. 

I also checked out Tutor Time in College Point, but didn't like that it was really big.  I think they had something like 100 children there.  There is also a Tutor Time in Bayside.  Pricing was somewhere between Little Elms and Kiddie Academy. 

UES was completely out of the question-  way too expensive.  Some places were 3X the price and had waiting lists of up to 2 years.

Okay, now for home daycare.  The NYC dept of health has lists of licensed and inspected home daycares.  You can find it on their website.  There are tons in JH, like over 50.  I think they can have up to 6 children in their care, but there are rules about what ages.  You can also see if the provider has any violations, but don't be freaked out, some violations are really minor, like not having a paper on hand for an ointment a child needed.  They usually charge around $200/week.  I had no idea how to get started, so I just randomly called a few of them close to my building and asked to visit.  In every case, the provider was either full or did not want me to visit (not a good sign).  After calling around 10 of them, I gave up.  I had no one to refer me because the moms in my building seem to be mostly stay-at-home or have older children or relatives taking care of their kids.  I think it's much easier if you can get someone to recommend a provider.  I ended up finding one that a co-worker sends her daughter to in Fresh Meadows close to our job.  She cares for 4 children and had an opening for one infant.  This is where we will ultimately send our daughter when I go back to work in a month. 

Nanny/Babysitter-  we didn't research this option.  Too expensive.

Good luck.  Let me know if you find anything else.  Again, it was shockingly hard to find any information.

Hi Gina,
Congrats on your upcoming little one!
Your process of finding a daycare will also be influenced by when you expect to need the care. If you are going back to work 6-12 weeks after birth, I would recommend that you look for specialized infant care programs. There are many home day cares in the area and I'm sure many are solid, but your baby might not get the close attention she needs if she is mixed in a program with boisterous toddlers. I've noticed that many families that need infant care work out nanny share arrangements, and you could make inquiries about this on the jhfamilies Yahoo group.
My son has been in daycare since he was 10 months old, working up from a two-day a week schedule to full time now. He is turning three this month, and we are overall very happy with his daycare, Little Hands, which is on 73rd St. near 30th Ave. Please feel free to DM me if you want to talk more.
