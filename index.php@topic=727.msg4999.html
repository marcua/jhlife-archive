Last night, as I was coming home at close to midnight, I passed a man on the sidewalk on the southeast corner of 78th St. and 35th Avenue, who appeared to be going through and reading the mail out of someone's trash. He had a suitcase with him. I couldn't see him well, it was dark, but he appeared to be fairly well dressed.

I was concerned, but did not feel comfortable stopping and asking him what he was doing. I thought about calling the police, but also felt that might have been overkill, given that nothing violent was going on.

What should I have done in this situation? I am worried that this person was trying to find out information to be used in fraud/identity theft. I really felt at a loss.

Thank you,

Deborah

My sister just had a trash incident in Massachusetts.  She did not have her identity stolen.  However, she did receive a $1,000 ticket in the mail from the city of Boston for a trash dumping violation.  

She was told that a piece of trash bearing her husband's name and their address had been found in a trash bag that had been dumped somewhere on another property.  Neither she nor her husband had any incentive to dump trash anywhere besides in the trash cans outside their house on trash day, as it's a service they did not have to pay extra for and they were not running a business or anything that would generate excessive trash.

So neither one of them could figure this out, though it didn't seem too hard to figure out that the city was trying to generate revenue from poor unsuspecting apartment dwellers who thought they were minding their own business and disposing of their trash properly.

But at some point some city employee must have looked exactly like the well-dressed man on 35th Ave., rooting through trash at night to see if he could find an intact envelope with a name and address so that he could then issue a $1000 fine.

For all enquiring minds...my sister was planning to appear at the court hearing to fight the ticket but she made some well-placed phone calls and sent a registered letter advising the city that it was illegal for them not to be shown the "evidence" in advance.  And magically the matter was cleared up and the case dropped and they even received an apologetic phone call.
