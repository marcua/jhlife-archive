FYI, price gouging usually refers to taking advantage of emergencies to charge folks exorbitant prices for necessities for living. You might recall after Sandy there were calls to report businesses that engaged in price gouging (quadrupling the price of gas, milk, etc.)

My own take is the ice cream lady should have just sold the small ice cream at $1.50 since the customer knew how much someone else paid, but at the same I can't find a lot of reason for moral outrage that someone hustling to support themselves and their family might quote higher prices to someone who looks like they might be able to afford it. Especially since we're talking about a difference of a dollar and ice cream.
