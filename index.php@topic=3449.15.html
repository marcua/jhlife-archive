Some of the South American Wines are great.  For pairing with Pasta it depends on your sauce.  For a robust sauce ( Puttanesca, Amatriciana, etc.) you need something like a cabernet sauvignon, Chilean Casillero del Diablo is good and under $10 a good American Cab is Gallo Sonoma Cabernet Sauvignon  abut $11-12 ( not the old Gallo you heard about). More robust wines are Zinfandels.  A bit lighter are the pinot noirs and the Syrah.  If you want to go cheap but still quite drinkable try the Rawson Retreat Shiraz Blend - Australia (about $7).

If you are going with seafood pasta or a vegetable pasta then whites go great.  I can't tell you a thing about Chardonnay as I don't like it or drink it, but for very aromatic, citrus wines- the sauvignon blancs from the Marlborough region of New Zealand are excellent.  They go for about $12 - 18.  Otherwise there are some good Chilean Sauvignon Blancs and Italian Pinot Grigio..

Don't forget the Spanish Tempranillo ( red medium body) and the South African Whites ( there is a brand that is good and cheap  I think it is KWV.

Enjoy.  For local wine stores the only one I like is on 74th and Bway/Roosevelt. The manis very nice and accommodating.  If yo ahve a car go to the Costco in LIC and the wine store next door is great.  If you feel like a bit of a s..plurge there is an incredible wine  Montes Alpha Cabernet - it is about $16 here  just great and it changes the more you let it breathe.

My two favorites (and they are very cheap) are:

Montepulciano D'Abruzzo
Readily available at nearly all wine/liquor stores. I've paid as little as $5/bottle before. But certain vineyards can be near $20/bottle.

Lambrusco (bubbly red)
This is more difficult to find. I've found it at bottlerocket on 19th and 5th Avenue in the Flatiron District. They have two types; a red and a rose. You can also find it at Via Amelia, on 21st? between Park and Broadway. It's a restaurant, but they sell wine as well and they have a larger selection. Lambrusco is particular to the Amelia Romagna region of Italy (northern Italy, Parma region). This is usually priced in the mid-teens. There are cheaper ones (by Riunite, ick...), but I don't recommend them.


They have a lambrusco at Table Wine. And another sparkling red from somewhere else in Italy. They are both under $20.
