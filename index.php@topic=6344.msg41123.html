News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I (and probably many others on this board) emailed Danny Dromm about the play street when lmaniace and Dudley posted the bad news.  Here is his response...not much to say right now, but at least it's on his radar (emails and calls usually only matter if the office hears from a lot of people!):

Quote
Dear Constituents,
 
Thank you for your correspondence regarding the Play Street. We are working closely with the Garden School and the Department of Transportation to come up with a solution that is acceptable to all. While it might not make everyone 100% happy, we hope that we can come up with a solution that everyone can live with. Our immediate concern is the safety of the families and children on the Play Street and at the Garden School. We will continue to work with DOT, the Garden School, The Jackson Heights Beautification Group, and the Jackson Heights Green Alliance to resolve this issue. We will be back in contact with you shortly.  
 
Sincerely,
 
Daniel Dromm
NYC Council Member, 25th District
