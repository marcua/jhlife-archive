From Today's New York Daily News: http://www.nydailynews.com/blogs/dailypolitics/2008/05/queens-dems-vs-john-sabini-tak.html

May 22, 2008
Queens Dems vs. John Sabini (Take 2) by Elizabeth Benjamin


Democratic leaders in the 13th SD will reconvene tomorrow to finish what they started Monday - a vote on whether to switch their support from incumbent Sen. John Sabini to his primary oppponent and former party insurgent, Councilman Hiram Monserrate.

The district leaders adjourned without a vote to give Sabini time to "evaluate the situation" and consider his options, said party Executive Secretary Mike Reich (basically, in hopes that he would walk away quietly and perhaps decide to do something else, like maybe pursue Councilwoman Helen Sears' seat in 2009).

But a Sabini source said the senator hasn't changed his mind about seeking re-election, and he's got Senate Minority Leader Malcolm Smith backing him up.

The party hasn't changed its mind, either, according to Reich, who said he fully expects the vote will not go Sabini's way tomorrow.

    "There's no question about it," Reich said. "(Sabini) knew Monday he didn't have the votes. I don't think anyone has changed their mind."


    "We put an awful lot into John last time," continued Reich, who stressed that Sabini is a "personal friend" and said the decision to jettison him has been a difficult one. "All the unions were there. We even had Hillary Clinton endorse him. And what happens? He barely wins. He wins by 200 votes."

(Actually, it was 247 votes).

This issue has to be settled soon, as petitioning starts June 3 and petitions are traditionally printed over Memorial Day by many political organizations.

Reich rejected the idea that the party is making this move in an effort to protect its chairman, Rep. Joe Crowley, from a potential future challenge by Monserrate, adding: "The statement we made in 2004 holds true today: It would be our pleasure to have Hiram run. He would never come close to beating Joe Crowley."

Reich acknowledged, however, that the abandonment of Sabini by the party is being done in part to protect the district leaders from potential challenges by Monserrate supporters, which, if they were successful, would provide him with a toehold of control in the party organization.

Other issues: The ongoing demographic shift in the 13th SD, with an ever-increasing number of Hispanic voters; and Sabini's drunk driving arrest in Albany last September (he pleaded guilty to DWAI on Super Duper Tuesday, a canny communications-management move given that reporters' attention was elsewhere).

    "In the end, Hiram's going to beat John anyway, so let's start as a community working with Hiram," Reich said. "We haven't always seen eye-to-eye with him, but he works hard for the community. He is a force to be reckoned with, and you ignore him at your own peril."


On the question of what might happen if Monserrate gets ensared by city and federal investigations into the ongoing Council slush fund investigation, Reich said:

    "There's no allegation at the moment that I know of, other than that there have been questions about funding he has supported to certain groups. I have yet to see anything that indicates Hiram has anything to fear."


Sabini, of course, was a Councilman before he became a senator and has directed his share of discretionary funding during his time in both elected posts.

As for other incumbent lawmakers who are upset by the party's decision to turn its back on Sabini - largely out of a "there but for the grace of God go we" kind of concern - Reich had this to say:

    "If you look at the history of the Democratic organization, it's very rare that you go against an incumbent unless the incumbent has done something either to embarrass you or unless they're surely going to lose. John has done both."

New York Daily News "Queens Dems Dump Sabini, Officially" by Elizabeth Benjamin
http://www.nydailynews.com/blogs/dailypolitics/2008/05/queens-dems-dump-sabini-offici.html

After delaying the vote for a week, Queens Democratic district leaders in the 13th SD reconvened this morning and, as expected, officially voted to support Councilman Hiram Monserrate over incumbent Sen. John Sabini in the primary this fall.

There were 14 leaders in the room, 12 of whom voted for Monserrate. Two abstained out of respect for Sabini, according to party Executive Secretary Mike Reich. Both candidates spoke.

    "It was a tough thing, I think, for most people," Reich said. "They have a warm spot in their hearts for John. This was not done lightly, and it's not done often."


    "I don't think it reflects on the work John has done over the years," Reich added. "I don't think it reflects on John as a person. I think it reflects on the political reality of the district. We saw the handwriting on the wall that John was extremely vulnerable."

Reich noted that while it's unusual for the party to turn its back on an incumbent, it is not unheard of.

In fact, he said, Sabini himself came to his first elected post in much the same way when Councilman Joseph Lisa lost party support (he was rated among the seven least effective Council members in a 1987 Daily News poll) and faced a potential challenge from Sabini in the early 1990s. Lisa was appointed to a judgeship in 1991 to avoid a primary.

So what does this mean for Sabini and Monserrate, practically speaking?

First and foremost, Reich said, Monserrate is not only the designee of the party, but his name will be on the petitions that the party troops carry when petitioning starts June 3. Sabini will have to pay to print his own petitions and find his own volunteers to carry them.

The candidates need to collect 900 valid signatures to get on the ballot. The rule of thumb is you need to collect three times that number to provide a cushion to prevent getting tossed off the ballot.

Now that the Democrats have made their decision, it's like the Working Families Party will follow suit. The Queens chapter of the WFP already recommended Monserrate over Sabini when the party met last weekend to review candidates.

UPDATE: Sabini, does not appear to have any immediate intention of dropping out of the race. He released the following statement:

    "It is ultimately up to the Districtâ€™s voters to decide whom they want as their state senator. I will fight for the privilege to continue to serve them."
