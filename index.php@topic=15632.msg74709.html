Quote from: queensplantboy on November 17, 2016, 06:28:00 PM
As someone who drives through the city every day for work, i can attest that bicyclists are absolutely the worst offenders of traffic laws......

Actually, pedestrians are objectively the worst. Did you know it's illegal to walk across the street except at a crosswalk? Did you know it's illegal even at a crosswalk, even with no cars in sight, unless there is a walk signal? It's true! That's what jaywalking is!

Yet these rules are ignored by 100% of pedestrians, with good reason. It is not dangerous to walk across a completely empty street, and walking all the way to the end of the block and waiting for a signal would be pointlessly inconvenient. Technically any police officer could give you a ticket for it, but they don't, because everyone walks sometimes and therefore everyone knows how stupid it would be.

Well, what we're talking about here is similar. This is not about bicyclists going through red lights at full speed, cutting through traffic. That does happen pretty often, and they can and should be ticketed. This is about bicyclists coming to a stop at red lights, checking to make sure nobody is coming, then proceeding through when safe. This practice is almost as common as common as jaywalking, for the same reason: it's completely harmless. The only reason the cops can get away with ticketing it is because there are fewer bicyclists than pedestrians.

Finally, as an aside, if you find you keep having close calls with bicyclists, maybe you should look before stepping into the street? I'm not saying it's your fault, but that is still basic safety, no?
