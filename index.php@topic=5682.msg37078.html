Himalayan Hut
« on: December 18, 2010, 02:57:11 PM »
The former Family Restaurant is now Himalayan Hut located at 75-18 37th Ave opposite Trade Fair.  It's a very attractive sit down restaurant serving both Chinese and Desi Chinese.  I ordered lunch take out of Fish Chilli with hot & sour soup which is tonight's dinner for just $6.95.  I just sampled it and it's very good.  The $6.95 lunch has chicken, fish, beef and vegetable dishes.  I quickly looked at the Desi Chinese eat in menu and it's very reasonable (shrimp dishes $10.95).  It's owned by a very pleasant couple.  They deliver.  The take out menu just has Chinese dishes similar to the neighborhood take out places.  I took home a lunch menu which has on one side Chinese and the other side Desi Chinese.  They need a take out menu for Desi Chinese that's similar to the dine in menu.  I mentioned this website to the owners.

Hi Everyone - I tried Himalayan Hut  yesterday with a group of 4.  We had the most fabulous meals -- not typical fast food chinese but quality desi and chinese food.  Finally! We had chicken momos, veggie spring rolls, and their version of buffalo wings called Drums of Himalayan Hut.  All we fabulous but the drums were a brilliant surprise.  A little kick to them, nice presentation, wonderful flavor.  The entrees were also good: manchurin chicken and a ginger basil chicken (our fave), and beef lo mein.  We will all be going back.  Oh, and to make this a no brainer favorite for us - the bill was super low considering how much we all ate.  Love it!

sol33, we were there the same night, and I suspect you were the group of 4 that was looking over at our table and then asking us how our dishes were before leaving??  My foodie radar went off.  Ha ha.  We found our meal pretty tasty as well.  We had chicken momos and spicy lemon coriander soup to start - the soup was delicious, very much like egg drop but with a kick.  The momos didn't do it for me - I find the momos in the neighborhood all seem like a much rougher version of Chinese dumplings.  For entrees, we tried the curry fishball noodle soup, shrimp coriander garlic, and fish chili (it's advertised on their window).  The shrimp dish was my favorite, but the other dishes were good enough that I would order them again.  The fish is breaded and fried, for those of you who are interested.  It came to us looking like chicken and even our somewhat distracted waitress thought so until we reminded her what we ordered.  Interestingly, the fishball soup had a Thai curry base.  Not what you would expect in this type of restaurant, but I'm not complaining - it fit the bill on a cold night.
