News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Wednesday, February 28: The German-Jewish Cookbook
7:30pm
Jackson Heights' own Sonya Gropman will be here with her mother Gabrielle Rossmer Gropman for the neighborhood premiere of their fabulous book, The German-Jewish Cookbook, a fascinating collection of delicious recipes, history, and personal anecdotes.
“With a preservationist zeal and a modern day appetite, Sonya and Gaby pay a long-overdue tribute to the fascinating German-Jewish culinary traditions. Through charming stories and mouthwatering recipes they bring that almost-vanished gastronomic culture to life—and the results are delectable.”—Anya von Bremzen

LITERARY: First Tuesdays at Espresso 77
Tuesday, February 6: Sasha Kamini Parmasad
Open mic sign up: Starts at 6:30
Open mic reading: Begins at 7pm (3 minute limit per reader)
Featured reader: Begins between 7:45 and 8pm

Sasha Parmasad, educator, visual artist, and award-winning writer, is the daughter of a Trinidadian historian-poet-songwriter and a lawyer who devoted many years of their lives to the struggle for social justice. Her novel, Ink and Sugar, placed third in the national First Words Literary Contest for South Asian American Writers (2003) and her poetry placed first in the annual Poetry International Competition (2008). Her collection of poems titled No Poem: A Divine Rising was published in 2015.

Restaurants & Food / ACLU fundraiser this weekend at Espresso 77
« on: February 02, 2017, 07:50:52 PM »
Just a quick word to let everyone know that starting tomorrow, February 3, Espresso 77 is participating with over 500 independent coffee shops in a nationwide fundraiser for the American Civil Liberties Union.  We will donate a percentage of our profits from every sale of brewed coffee that we make over the weekend.

The ACLU is a defender of our inalienable right to free speech, and they are also currently working overtime to defend immigrant and refugee families caught in the current confusing web of executive orders and court decisions.  As a proud member of the vibrant, artistic and diverse neighborhood of Jackson Heights, Espresso 77 is committed to supporting immigrant, refugee and civil rights.

You can learn more about the fundraiser here:
http://sprudge.com/a-nationwide-coffee-fundraiser-for-the-aclu-115163.html

Thanks!

Business Listings and Services Directory / Meeting / studio space in Jackson Heights available for hourly or daily rental
« on: December 13, 2016, 10:56:42 AM »
Need a space in Jackson Heights to:
 
Have a meeting?
Make some art?
Hold a class?
Have a photo shoot?
Make a film?
Get some work done?

A new work rental space is available in Jackson Heights!

The 500 square foot space, located on the 3rd floor of a commercial building on 74th Street just off of 37th Avenue, is available for micro-term rental by the hour or by the day.

This space is ideal for freelance desk work, meetings with clients, project teams, neighborhood organizations, photo shoots, small yoga or pilates classes, music lessons, instrument or band practice.

Square-shaped 500sf room, with wood floors and many windows and skylights
for great natural light. There is a restroom accessible directly from the space, coat closet, air-conditioning and heating, and free wi-fi. Tables and seating are available, and can be folded away if you would rather use the open space. (The building is not served by an elevator - this space is accessible by stair only).

Hours Available:
Monday through Friday, from 9am to 9pm
Saturday and Sunday, from 4pm to 10pm
 
Rental Rates for the entire space:
$45 per hour, 2-hour minimum
$150 per half-day on weekdays (9am-1pm or 2pm-6pm)
$250 for a full-day rental
($60 cleaning deposit required,will be refunded if the space is left clean)

Interested?
Please contact Afzal Hossain at Espresso 77
Phone: 212-671-2937
Email: info@espresso77.com
