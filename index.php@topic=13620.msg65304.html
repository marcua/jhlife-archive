It does look like the inventory for that category is really low.  I thought there was one at Colonial Court on JH Listings, but I checked it on Streeteasy and it says it's in contract.  Make sure you are checking all the realtor sites including the ones that don't place their listings on MLSLI, like MCP Properties.

I guess if you feel like you want to try something different, you could do a postcard mailing to all residents in the buildings you're interested in to see if anyone is interested in selling.  I get postcards frequently addressed to my unit number (no name) from realtors advertising their services by highlighting recent sales nearby.  Also I once got a letter from a realtor saying they had a buyer specifically interested in apartments in my line in my coop, since I have a one bedroom that's the largest 1 bdr layout in the complex.  Again, that did not come addressed to me, just my unit number, but I still received it no problem.

Not really sure, but I have noticed listing prices have gone way up in 2015.  If a unit sells for a low(er) price, it could mean it's an estate sale, not marketed properly or has a much smaller floor plan than some other units (even one bedrooms can vary by 100 square feet or more in the same building).  The one bedrooms up the street at the Carlton House are being listed for $350K to $399K, whereas a few years ago they were going for mid 200s.  I think this price increase is a product of marketing and overall neighborhood increases, but then again, coops are complicated so I could be wrong. 

I don't think a $350K to $399K listing price is out of line for a large 950 sq foot apartment with a garden in the historic district, provided financials are good.
