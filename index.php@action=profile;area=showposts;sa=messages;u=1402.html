Neighborhood Chat / Any recommendations for a good bike for recreational riding in Queens
« on: May 26, 2009, 09:20:35 AM »
I'm thinking of getting a bicycle. I moved to Astoria right across from the park in 2001. I thought WOW. Finally this Manhattan transplant can get a bike again. I fixed up my sister's racing bike. What a nightmare. It couldn't handle the streets and I got flats constantly. Also, Queens drivers are insane. B/w the flats and the driver hostility, I gave up riding and left my bike in Astoria Park as an offering.

I live in JH now and the driving appears even worse. But recently I've been canvassing in East Elmhurst so it's taken me north of the madness and there's hardly any traffic at all. The roads mostly are in good shape. I could ride a bike for exercise around here and not get killed. And of course, there's the bike path on 34th Avenue.

Old posts suggest there are some avid cyclists on this site. I of course am not one yet, but any ideas on where to start--that is, what kind of bike would you suggest? I'm sure the possibilities are endless so this is a tough question. Racing bike? Fuggedaboutit.

Thanks!

Restaurants & Food / Taste Good in Elmhurst. Gone?
« on: May 26, 2009, 09:02:37 AM »
This was (I think it's gone, otherwise I'd say IS) my favorite restaurant in Queens. It's next to NY Supermarket...41st Avenue? I dunno for sure, but it's a small strip of restaurants on a kinda smelly little block. It's a hole in the wall that serves the best Malaysian food! Just wonderful. But we were going to take relatives there last month and it was closed. It was a Thursday night. That's not a good sign. Haven't been over there since. Anyone else know?

We went over to Chou Thai instead--an absolutely wonderful place, on Whitney Avenue off of Broadway. If Taste Good is truly gone, Chou Thai takes over as my favorite. But be careful re the spice. Medium spicy is intense. I just can't do it anymore--I love it but it wrecks my insides.
