I haven't found anyplace other than the "Green Market" that has fresh fish.

Most of the stuff in the tanks at the Asian markets come from a warehouse on John Street in Dumbo (I think it was the original Brillo factory) where they have huge holding tanks with pumped in oxygen.  I have no idea from where these fish originated  It's across the street from a ConEd power station, if that helps.

Some fish buying tips:

Look at the eyes:  are they clear?
Touch the flesh if you can:  is it clammy?   Is the flesh firm?

Remember:  If it smells like fish, it's old.  It should smell like sea water.

Most shrimp sold locally is farmed in Asia and frozen.  Farmed shrimp destroy the environment.  Asian farmed shrimp usually have unacceptable levels of antibiotics.  Wild shrimp are relatively expensive (but you don't need a lot).  If they're local (Gulf Coast) you're supported a way of life that might perish.
