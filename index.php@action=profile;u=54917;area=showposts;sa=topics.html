Pets and Animals / Unleashed dogs: Did I overreact?
« on: March 16, 2019, 03:42:50 PM »
Recently I saw an unleashed dog—small, white, fluffy, older—standing outside a corner store at 73rd and 37th. I watched it for a minute as I waited to cross the street. When I knelt and talked to it, it was friendly but looked a little forlorn. I opened the door to the store and asked if the owner was in there. He came out, and I said he should have tied up his dog—that it could have run off or been snatched by someone. He was (to say the least) not very receptive: He called me a b--tch and told me to shut up.

I followed him and his still-unleashed dog a couple of blocks. The dog was well behaved—did not run into the street or anything. I didn't feel like the dog was in imminent danger when it was walking with the man, though it got pretty far ahead of him at times. That seemed risky. I've heard stories about dogs being snatched and used for training fighting dogs, like pit bulls.

In the end, I felt like maybe I was being a stick-in-the mud who should have kept her mouth shut. I tried to make it clear I was worried about the dog's safety, not about him following some arbitrary rule. I really was mostly concerned when the dog was outside the shop alone.   

So why am I posting about it now? I guess maybe I want someone to tell me I was right. Or to say, "Oh, that's just Nick and Buster, no need to worry!"

Neighborhood Chat / What was that??
« on: January 20, 2019, 12:47:31 PM »
I often wonder about stuff I see and hear in the neighborhood. I've finally decided to post about one of them.

About a half hour ago I was in my bedroom and heard, on the far side of the room, a chirpy ringing sound, almost like a telephone. It was kind of loud. It rang three or four times. The only suspects near the source of the sound are my computer and my Spectrum cable modem. My computer was asleep, so I woke it up and looked around to see if it could possibly have been something there. Couldn't find anything. So I think it may have been the cable modem.

It hasn't recurred but I am still wondering what the hell it was. And I figure posting here to ask if anyone else had a ringing sound from their modem would be a lot easier than trying to find someone at Spectrum who might know anything!

P.S. I realize I must sound pretty crazy posting this.

Neighborhood Chat / "Artwashing" article mentions Jackson Heights, local artist Ro Garrido
« on: August 15, 2016, 01:53:37 AM »
"This neighborhood has changed" has become a clichéd "thing New Yorkers say" in 2016. The phrase echoes from Bushwick's graffiti-covered walls to the gallery-lined streets of Chinatown. But when we check out exhibitions and street art in these "up-and-coming" neighborhoods, we often fail to recognize an important part of the problem — "art washing." Coined by The Atlantic in 2014, the term refers to the superficial cultural narrative that new art spaces and non-native artists bring to gentrifying communities.
