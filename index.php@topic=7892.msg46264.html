Relatively speaking, given that Jackson Heights is a far more reasonably priced to live in then the other trendier, culture-filled neighborhoods, the amount of money you save will allow you to afford visiting those cultural institutions and drinking their beer.  If you lived there and paid those rents, it would be harder to afford.  The access to public transportation here is great, so getting there is not as much of an issue.  Plus, there is less attitude here than in other trendier 'hoods, and that is worth something.

I just wanted to weigh in as someone who never goes to Brooklyn.

First, I'm surprised nobody mentioned Legends. It's our neighborhood's best down-to-earth pub, with fine beer, a solid crowd and absolutely spectacular barbecue being sold in the back.

As for "restaurants that aren't Latin or Indian," we're walking distance from Sripraphai, the best Thai in the city, along with having a pretty decent selection of Thai that deliver here including Arunee, Spicy Shallot and Ploy. "Latin" is also a big tent as it includes the fancy Mexican at Coyote, the fancy Peruvian at Pio Pio, the down-to-earth Mexican at Coatzingo, and the Argentinian steakhouse La Portena, which don't serve any of the same things. Let's not forget Cannelle in the shopping center on 31st Avenue for pastries and Plaka, a pretty good Greek restaurant up there which delivers to most of our neighborhood.

As Jackson Heightsers we tend to go to Astoria and Flushing a lot. In Flushing, it's a lot of fun to eat in the food courts, spend a warm day at the Botanical Garden and shop at Target (hey, OK?) We also go to the science museum and Nixtamal in Corona with our daughter. In Astoria, there's the movie theater and a lot of young unmarried hipster stuff I'm incapable of perceiving any more.
