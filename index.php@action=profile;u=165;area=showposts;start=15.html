Neighborhood Chat / Re: Random Tip Question
« on: June 04, 2009, 11:33:14 AM »
I agree with your coworker, $100 is too high.  You should definitely give the super something, in appreciation for his efforts.  I think $50 maximum is more appropriate.  (I'm assuming that the task took no more than an hour)

I firmly believe in what Benjamin Franklin once said: ""He that has once done you a Kindness will be more ready to do you another, than he whom you yourself have obliged."

Others have restated that quote to something like, "The best way to get someone to like you, is to have them do you a favor." 

If you give someone a tip that's disproportionate to the task performed, they may expect that same high level in the future.  Plus, people may resent you, if they feel that you're trying to "lord over" them with a large tip.  A large tip may imply that you feel sorry for them, and possibly could be construed as patronizing, rather than simply showing appreciation for the task performed.

I tend towards over tipping cab drivers and baggage handlers, but I don't believe they like me any more than someone who gives them a standard or even meager tip.
