Queens Paideia School
Our Focus: Your Child

Queens Paideia is known for its sophisticated learning modalities and emphasis on our students' emotional well-being. We provide resources, activities, and opportunities that meet our students where they are and that promote their next steps, and we help them stretch in how they see themselves and their place in the world.

*6:1 student-to-teacher ratio
*Academic excellence combined with a whole-child orientation
*Enriched curriculum with independent learning, hands-on projects, and group work that ensures engagement and responsibility.
*Affordable tuition--less than half of what it costs in Manhattan.
*Emphasis on the arts, global studies, applied math and sciences, healthful living, and social-emotional growth
*Inspired location in an arts center in the heart of Long Island City
*15-20 minute commute from many parts of Manhattan and Brooklyn

QPS's low student-teacher ratio means we know each student’s strengths, challenge areas, and interests, and can therefore guide them through learning the skills and content they need to be well educated and prepared for life beyond school. Our approach challenges students in the core subject areas and helps them acquire the important skills of critical thinking, problem solving, and learning how to learn. They step solidly onto the path of becoming motivated learners who are driven by curiosity and strong analytical thinking; and empathetic, helpful people who enjoy working independently and in collaboration with others.

QPS is located in an arts center where artists, dancers, musicians, and entrepreneurs open their doors to us. Our community of professional educators and thoughtful parents believe deeply in providing children with an educational experience that is both academically rigorous and socially enriching. Come meet us and them at our next open house!

QPS is accessible from Manhattan, and Brooklyn by the E/M/R/7/G trains. For more information, see our Web site (queenspaideiaschool.org) or send us an e-mail at info@queenspaideiaschool.org

Queens Paideia Announces $150,000 Scholarship Program

Long Island City, NY, December 4, 2013 Queens Paideia School, known for its 6:1 student-teacher ratio and fully individualized education that emphasizes strong academics, thinking skills, and self-management skills, announces a Merit Scholarship program worth a total of $150,000. Students 13 and up are eligible to apply for the 2014-15/16 school year. The school is holding an open house on the morning of December 18 for interested candidates and their parents/guardians.

Dr. Francis Mechner, director of QPS and president of the Mechner Foundation, founded the school “to provide the best possible--and most complete--education for children and to demonstrate the amazing results that an individualized education can deliver. We are funding the scholarship program to add seven promising students to our secondary school.” Selected applicants will be interviewed and evaluated on their abilities and personal qualities.
Queens Paideia School is an independent K-12 school located in Long Island City. Its highly regarded team of educators focuses detailed attention on every student. “We spotlight every student’s unique potentials and talents,” explains Mechner, “preparing them for college but also for a successful, fulfilling life.” Adds Dr. Tim Fredrick, QPS’s ELA instructor: “Our students learn to be critical thinkers and to ask questions of the world and themselves. That is what makes them stand out.”

Three $30,000 scholarships will cover the 2014-16 school years, and four $15,000 scholarships will cover the 2014-15 school year.

The Mechner Foundation has been supporting educational, scientific, and cultural activities since 1996. Further information and the application form can be found at http://queenspaideiaschool.org/process/. For questions, and to attend the December 18 open house, please email info@queenspaideiaschool.org.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Call for Submissions--Newtown Literary Magazine
« on: July 08, 2012, 06:10:20 PM »
Queens' First Ever Literary Journal Seeks Poetry and Prose from Queens Writers of all ages, all backgrounds.

Newtown Literary is a new semi-annual literary journal published in electronic and paper formats. We look for fiction, creative nonfiction, essays, and poetry that go beyond entertainment and storytelling.  The pieces we include for publication have struck us on a deep level.  Pieces that juxtapose humor with grief, tell untold love stories, and replace clichÃ© with innovation are examples of works that will find a home in Newtown Literary.

Newtown, the journal's namesake, was one of the original towns incorporated into the New York City borough of Queens in 1898. Newtown Literary is dedicated to writers from and writing about Queens, NY and allocates half the journal to these writers and their work.  But this is not our exclusive focus, as we recognize that Queens is just as much a state of mind as it is a geographic boundary.  The diversity of Queens is our inspiration and we hope to fill the journal with diverse stories, experiences, and voices. 

The inaugural issue of Newtown Literary arrives late Fall 2012.  Submission deadline for this issue is August 4th.  For submissions guidelines and to submit, visit http://www.newtownliterary.org/ or email editor@newtownliterary.org.

Creative Expressions

This 8-week after-school guides student-writers--enthusiastic and reluctant alike--toward the skill, and joy, of using language creatively to express ideas, feelings, and experiences. Instructor Tim Fredrick brings humor and pinpoint precision to assessing and teaching a small group of students, who come together weekly for a series of exercises that will hone their ability to play with language and discover new ways to use words and sentences. Creative Expressions

This 8-week after-school guides student-writers--enthusiastic and reluctant alike--toward the skill, and joy, of using language creatively to express ideas, feelings, and experiences. Instructor Tim Fredrick brings humor and pinpoint precision to assessing and teaching a small group of students, who come together weekly for a series of exercises that will hone their ability to play with language and discover new ways to use words and sentences. The ongoing result is greater mindfulness of word choice, expressive connections, and the confidence that comes from improvement in these areas.

At the end of the course, students will present works from their final portfolios and walk away with the awareness that they can apply their growing skills to all types of writing and presentation formats.

There are 2 sections:

1. Monday afternoons, 3:45-4:45 (this section is almost full)
2. Tuesday afternoons, 3:45-4:45

All classes held at the LIC Art Center, 44-02 23rd Street, 2nd floor. Accessible by the E/M/F/R subways. 1st stop in Queens.

Cost: $160 for 8 sessions, STARTS WEEK OF APRIL 16.

For more information, contact info@queenspaideiaschool.org.
