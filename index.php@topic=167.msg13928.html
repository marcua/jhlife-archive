I live in a building with 90 units.  We have one Super (and the entire family helps run the building from their older children mowing the lawn, removing snow, waxing floors -- to accepting and holding packages from UPS etc.  Our Super is on call and available and always there 24/7.  Depending on the year and how much I need them to accept packages (not part of their job description) etc. I give them at least $100.  Some years it's more because they also have keys to my apartment and on those rare times I think I left something on in the apartment, they will go and check for me.  I sometimes lock myself out and go get my own keys from them to get back into my apartment.  I love them and trust them and think they do more than they have to.

In the past, when we had a porter, I gave him $50 a year.  I give my mail carrier $20.  And I don't care if he has "a thousand people on his route and if they each give him $20 he's made a gazillion tax free dollars.... blah blah blah".  He also does more than he has to.  He always makes sure I get my mail that's over sized by coming to my door to see if I'm home and if not, giving the package to the Super. 

These people, when they're good and kind and generous with their time, imo, deserve their Christmas "bonus".

If they're just average at what they do, I'd give the Super $50 and the porter $20 and the mail carrier $10.  Our building also gives Christmas bonus to the Sanitation men.  They take large items on days they don't have to etc.  If these figures seem too high, you can bake them something or give them a bottle of wine.  I think it's important to acknowledge them, especially if they give good service year round.
