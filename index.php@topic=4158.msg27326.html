Anyway, the jist of it was that theholidays could be reversed in order as tongue in cheek humor, but let me hijack my own thread topic a bit to explain before I get branded as a sexist.

I work in school, that usually has about 50 female employees and in our luckiest years, 2 or 3 guys.  The Friday conversations before Mother's Day?  Kisses, hugs, oh what are you doing, treat yourself girl, did I wish you a happy mother's day yet, etc. etc.  The Friday conversations to the guys before Father's Day?  ::cricket chirps::

Two years ago, Luc was in pre-K.  His teacher was a very cool lady whom I got to know very well over the course of the year, really into him, called weekly, worked with in-house early invention team, etc., so I felt comfortable joking with her on this. 

See, on Mother's Day, the kids came home with a huge ceramic frame, they made it class, fired or sculpted somewhere after creation I think, with a nice 4 x 6 photo on heavy stock photo paper of the student posing.  Nice right?

Father's Day?  The same photo, printed on thick typing paper, laminated, about 1 1/2 by 2 inches, with a ring pushed through a hole punched in the corner, for a keychain.  Now I loved his teacher, but when she called that week I couldn't resist a friendly jab about that.  Even though my school does something similar too.


(Truthfully, with all the different family combinations, in my class we just have a "Parent's Project" in middle-May, but that's way off the issue.)
