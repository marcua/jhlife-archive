Mobile Food Trucks & Locations
« on: August 07, 2011, 03:12:05 PM »
I noticed a new mobile food truck parked at the corner by HSBC at 37th Avenue and 77th Street earlier today.  A few people walking by seemed to express surprise and disappointment as the vendor was setting up for business.  Someone from the nearby pizzeria was trying to convince the vendor to move to another location, but was unsuccessful.  A few others said they would complain to Danny Dromm's office.  One person was disappointed in that the food being offered was "the same" as the nearby vendors in the low 70s.

From looking at the Jackson Heights Historic District map, I can't really tell if the northwest corner of 37th and 77th falls within its boundaries.  I believe it doesn't, and I assume vendors are not permitted within the Historic District anyway.  That said, are food vendors permitted to set up shop anywhere there's a spot?  This particular vendor was very close to HSBC's entrance, so I'm not sure if that's an issue.  Hmmm... probably not.  Doesn't Sammy's Halal park right outside Astoria Federal?  In any event, I'd say there were some mixed reactions to the truck, but the negative seemed to outweigh the positive today.

that's more of a cart than a truck, and different regulations apply to both: trucks can only park at metered spaces for three hours at a time -- a big sticking point in midtown manhattan in recent months, with some esteemed trucks serving awesome food forced to leave by 99 cent pizza joints selling slop. not sure if there's a historic district prohibition on food carts, but somehow i doubt it.

fwiw, i noticed it too... it's on my corner, so it'd be hard not to. i had already eaten so didn't try, but i'd be thrilled to have it there if it served good food. and, yes, customers might drop refuse on the ground. but that hasn't forced mcdonald's or met food out of business yet......
