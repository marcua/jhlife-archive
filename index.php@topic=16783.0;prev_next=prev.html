 My experience is 95% safe but the few instances of criminality were up there. Night is an issue as it is most anywhere in the
city for single women. Any kind of perceived vulnerability- age (older or younger), being alone, dark quiet side streets, displaying
valuable items, etc- are the same kinds of problems here. But we could do more to eliminate pockets of crime in this otherwise
safe neighborhood. Our parks and Roosevelt Ave are particularly badly lit at night and with the new LED's this could be corrected.

 Some co-ops have done a better job than others in making their whole perimeter well lit and not just the entrance. We almost
never see police here patrolling the neighborhood except for parades and giving tickets and just an occasional presence would
be welcome especially at the subway exits at night. Most of the break-ins that I hear about sound like inside jobs as the people
are usually not home much and the thieves seem to know of the valuable items like unusual coin collections, for example.

Hi there! I'm a woman, in my early twenties, and have lived in JH for 4 years now. I'm extra-wary when it comes to safety because I'm also a lifelong NYer and have had some very bad experiences. However, JH feels safe to me. There have been many, many times in these past few years where I've come home very late, like 4 am, and had to walk home from the subway station (living on 35th Ave) or have walked down Roosevelt very late to get home from a friend's place or bar, etc.

I've gotten catcalled a lot (even got one this afternoon, gross), but for the most part they've just been harmless creeps. I've been followed, but those situations have always been resolved by letting the guy know that I know what he's doing and giving him a look of death. As other people mentioned, I'd avoid walking on Roosevelt late at night, but that's something I do often and never have a serious problem with.

I genuinely feel safe here and have had so many good experiences living in this neighborhood. I'd definitely consider it a comforting place to live where you don't have to worry about crime. Although, of course, stay alert despite that.

I agree with the above post. It is always wise to stay vigilant at night walking alone, especially if you've had a few drinks. But I moved here nine years ago from a Brooklyn neighborhood where we could sometimes here gun shots at night, even after years of gentrification. Admittedly I am older than the previous poster, and don't attract many cat calls these days. But overall JH strikes me as a safe neighborhood, especially when you factor in the widely varying income levels/language differences and so on. If you are concerned, you can always go to your local precinct and ask the community affairs officer for a breakdown on crime. There are also maps online that show these. You can see where the most crimes are committed and make an effort to avoid those areas. Engagement and awareness --and a little luck- are the key to street safety, no matter where you live.
