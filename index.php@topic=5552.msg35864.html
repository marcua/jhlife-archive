I have been in Jackson Heights for 25 years, which is most of my life.

The surge of crime that happened is very small scale compared to the past.

In the 80's and 90's, there were a few violent crimes every week, the murder rate was high, rapes were up, robberies were up, and muggings were up.

Let me tell you, in an average year my apartment was burgularized at least 3 times, this was in the late 80's, early 90's.

I have experienced what I call a lock down, when a Police Officer was shot on 75th Street, and there were officers with machine guns roaming the streets, and vehicles resembling tanks driving around searching all the yards.

These two major crimes that occurred over the past month could have happened anywhere.

And on the note of Colombia and gang members, neither group are likely to bother anyone unless prevoked.

Be aware, and try to be less paranoid.

On average, these days Jackson Heights is a pretty safe place, yet not the safest in Queens.

I have no plans to leave and doubt that I ever will move from here.

I live in the 70s near 35th.  I guess what's feeding my fear is:

a) The nature of the recent crimes.  They're violent, generally senseless and indicate that there are people in this neighborhood who are seriously psychologically messed up.  I am a criminal psychologist and can tell you that burglaries and car break-ins are scary but crimes where people are being beaten with baseball bats (to death at times) indicate that seriously disturbed people are roaming around on our streets.  It's one thing to mug someone for money (such crimes usually do go up with a receding economy) but it's another thing to beat someone to death or pose a fake food order and then crack a baseball bat across their skull.  These recent crimes were not committed by isolated figures looking for cash.  They were, in general, committed by groups of two or more.  It indicates a culture of violence--these criminals are socializing with each other and making their behavior acceptable.

Anyway, I won't get into the research of it but it shows that there are some very bad characters living amongst us and around us.

b) The quiet, isolated, dimly-lit streets.  I won't get into this one as I had already mentioned what I thought of this in my earlier posts.


I know every neighborhood has crime but it's unfortunate that this would be happening in Jackson Heights.  When I first moved here, my husband and I went out for dinner on Valentine's Day.  We decided to check out a restaurant on 37th Avenue.  The street was crowded with people and it was fairly early (around 7:30pm).  Pretty soon, we noticed two young men following us.  They would stop whenever we stopped to look at a menu and wait for us.  We even did a little experiment to see if they would cross streets with us, etc.  When we started taking obvious notice of what they were doing, one of them grinned at us.  Finally, we went into Jahn's because we felt so uneasy and they waited outside for two minutes before taking off.  It was the first time something like that happened to me in the city in a long time (I grew up in Queens) and, after living in JH for a few months, it wasn't the last time...
