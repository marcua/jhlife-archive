News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

If you fast foward to the 4:00 min mark you see a picture of 74th Street and see the very same buildings that are present now. Except for new storefronts of different businesses you see how little 74th Street has changed since the 1960’s. No boom in construction here and you wonder how long these one story buildings next to a major subway stop can last. It could be that almost the entire block being owned by Midwood who has blocked a merchants association and doesn’t want to invest in the neighborhood has a lot to do with it. No new housing here folks.
