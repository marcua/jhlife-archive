Carnegie Hall looking for local musical venue
« on: January 13, 2009, 07:25:26 PM »
The Carnegie Hall's Weill Music Institute is looking for spaces in
Jackson Heights to present music performances as part of their
Neighborhood Concert Series, which brings free concerts to
neighborhoods of New York as a service to the community.

Does anyone have suggestions of local venues that would fit the
following criteria?

The venue should have:

1. a performance area that is designed for musical performances
2. experience in presenting musical performances
3. a sound system or a space with good acoustics for acoustic performances
4. seating capacity between 100-300
5. some means of marketing (can be simple like posting flyers, but
some mechanism in place is helpful)
6. accessible via public transportation

Thanks so much,
Joy

Natives theatre is closed. And the JH Library doesn't have a performance space that matches the tech specs they require. I'm not sure about Elmhurst Hosp. either.

The only thing in JH that comes close is The Lexington School For The Deaf. Info on their Performing Arts Center can be found here: http://www.lexnyc.com/laurencenter/index.html however the school is not really accessible as it's far from the subway, though a bus ride from the train station will get you there pretty quick.

The main room of the Jewish Community center could probably do it, as could the auditorium in the Renaissance Charter School, but both are relatively small.

St. Joan of Arc has Ward Meehan Hall which is a gymatorium, has the size and accessibility but I doubt the acoustics and technical capabilities are near what they'd require. 

IS 230 on 34th ave. and 75th st. has a small auditorium that looks halfway decent from what I can tell through the windows.

The elementary school on 82nd St. between 35th and 34th Aves. has a decent auditorium as well.

The Garden School may also be an option, but I have no idea what's there in the way of a performance venue.
