I've noticed this and been annoyed by it as well.  I don't think it has anything to do with people anticipating a bus not coming through the depot; I think it is a line motivated only by people who want to guarantee themselves a seat on an empty bus.

The problem is just as described above -- the people waiting in the real line for the bus sometimes cannot get on when the bus is filled.  So the first person that can't board the full bus should therefore be the first person in the line for the new, empty bus.  However, all these people who have formed the rogue line push their way over and it's chaotic and not fair to the people who have been patiently waiting in the real line.

I don't know what can be done about it either.  Perhaps a standing sign could be placed in the vicinity that just says "Form one line only, please."  However, I kind of doubt that would be effective. 

Also, there could in fact be a reason why some people want or need a seat on what could be a long bus ride (if they are going to the end of the line).  I do recall when I had an injured foot, that even while wearing an orthopedic shoe, no one was particularly nice or generous with offering a seat, possibly because I looked rather healthy besides the foot issue.  But... I still never joined the rogue line.  I just took my chances.
