Apparently, not everyone thinks JH is that great. That's very interesting. Perhaps we should start being more objective in our opinion of JH. And I think many have been as we listed our own "worst of JH or what needs to be improved"
Some may ask:"Why is Bayside much better to live in? Why is Bell Blvd so much cooler than 37 Ave?" What makes Metropolitan Ave in Middle Village so attractive? What makes Bay Terrace coops and condos and their shopping center so unique? Why is living in LeHarve coop in Beechurst so attractive? I know there are folks in Jackson Heights who are not familiar or who have never heard of these places? Additionally many folks like their neighborhoods not at the focus of attention since they don't want their neighborhood to change. Sometimes change is NOT good, as the McMansion trend in LittleNeck, Douglaston and Bayside has angered residents.
Cool bars, Starbucks and ethnic diversity may take a back seat to peace & quiet suburban setting (but knowing you're in NYC and paying NYC R/E taxes!!), excellent school districts, not being near the city, and a place to park your car, namely your own driveway.

I did not move to JH because of ethnic diversity, great restaurants or as some folks on the other link mentioned "trannies". Nevertheless it is the best location for my wife and me and we have no immediate plans to move.
However I can't see myself retiring here, as I'm some of us don't see as well.

Hi Dawnie!

Re: Cubano -- The closest 2 Cuban restaurants in the area are Rincon Criollo, right off Roosevelt Avenue on Junction Blvd. and El Sitio, on Roosevelt Avenue, near the corner of 69th Street.  I've heard that El Sitio also has an Astoria location.  I wouldn't recommend either one of these places.  They've seen their best days come and go a long, long time ago.  There's a sort of decent takeout place on Austin Street in Forest Hills called Latin Cabana.  It's small with 3 or 4 stools by the window.  Again, o.k., but not great.

I would also love to have a great Cuban restaurant in the neighborhood, but truth be told, the best food we've had has always been in Florida.  I'm not a huge fan of all the fusion preps that are commonly found in Manhattan...typical dishes plated up traditionally are best in my opinion.
