I've been having similar issues with 300/20 since February.  I'm near 81st as well.  It gets fixed for a while, then it starts again within a month or two. 

Have you tried twc.cotp@twcable.com?  (Office of the President, Executive Customer Care Team.) The level 3 support people are good (direct number 866-506-0218), but once these issues are referred to their network group they end up in limbo with no response.

I wish we could get FIOS, but there's no fiber passing most of our streets.  When Verizon ripped up 37th Avenue near 81st I saw they were burying some fiber there, but when I asked they said it wasn't for FIOS, only phone service. 

Last but not least, complain, complain, complain!   The NY State Attorney General's office was interested in whether or not New Yorkers were actually getting the bandwidth we're paying for.

Title: Re: TWC Maxx 300/20 Service is Crippled to 5-15 Mpbs at Night
Post by: wlirfan on August 25, 2016, 10:09:52 AM
Actually, Verizon has the absolute legal right, under State and Federal statutes, to enter into any multiple dwelling and install FIOS.  There is literally nothing that an owner of a multiple dwelling can do to stop them.  They will enter into an agreement with the owner regarding damages and liability for their installations, but that's it.  I forget how many units a building has to have for this to apply.  It may sound weird, but think of it this way - no owner of a multiple dwelling can prevent the phone company from coming in and installing phone service either, and since Internet has really replaced the phone in most instances, the State is just assuring that renters are not denied an essential service by their landlord.

Verizon has sued owners for access, and has always won.
