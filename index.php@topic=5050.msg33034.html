Hello JH!

First, I want to say how cool it is to have a neighborhood forum like this - I just moved to JH in June and love it, and part of what I loved was that I found this board before i even moved so I could check out what the local issues, hot spots, gripes were.

Unfortunately, my first post is related to the gripe column. it is August, it is beautiful out and I can't find a single outdoor place to study.  I'm a PhD student with a major test in 3 weeks, and all I'm doing is studying from now till then.  I moved from the UWS where I could float from one park, to an outdoor cafe, to an outdoor bar, to a roofdeck when it was nice.  Now, I'm locked in my apartment and can't find a single spot of grass to lie down on or a cafe table in the neighborhood.

Can anyone please help - I'm afraid I'll go crazy.  In particular, any info on the nearest park with grass (i live across from the one with basketball courts which is no help) would be super helpful.  I'm also very willing to barter/rent/sell my first born for access to any of the gardens in the area just for this month.

Thanks in advance for any & all help!

Unfortunately your choices are limited.  I moved from UWS 5 years ago, and still miss the lack of outdoor space to sit.  Fortunately my building now has a pleasant garden area.  When I want park I usually drive (or you could bike or take the train) to either Astoria Park or Gantry Park in LIC.  Here in JH, there a few quiet benches on the grounds of the Episcopal Church on 34th Avenue...sometimes gate is open.  There's a bigger playground with more benches over on Bway in Elmhurst.  Gets noisy, but may be okay at certain times.   You could sit at one of the tables on the side of Restaurant La Nueva on 85th Street/37th Ave.-- usually they are empty.
I think benches could be added around perimeter of Travers Park and could be away from the noisy courts and playground...we need to push for this.  Good luck with your studies and finding some space!

While it's true that there are very few places right here in the immediate neighborhood, a bicycle might be just the thing - NW and central Queens is a fantastic place to ride a bike around, and there are lots of places that are easy to get to on two wheels (Flushing Meadows/Queens Botanical Gardens, Socrates Sculpture Park, LIC waterfront and Forest Park are some of the first few to come to mind). With the exception of a few of the major blvds (which are easy to avoid), most streets are quiet and bike friendly.

I'll also second the church gardens recommendations - they're wonderful little tranquil oases that are open to all.

I don't know where you live in JH, but if you're not up too high, there is a park on Woodside Avenue in the 50s (I believe around 58th Street is where it begins).  You would walk down Roosevelt Avenue, and just past the 61st Street 7 train stop, Roosevelt Ave and Woodside Avenue intersect and the park is a few blocks down, between Roosevelt Ave and Woodside Ave.  Or, you could take the 7 train 2 stops down to the 61st Street--Woodside stop.

I've never been there, but I drive by all the time and it always seems quiet and not crowded. 

http://www.bridgeandtunnelclub.com/bigmap/queens/woodside/doughboypark/index.htm

Also, Astoria is full of places with outdoor seating.  If you don't work during the weekdays, it will probably be pretty quiet and you can get some studying done.  At night and on the weekend, those places get VERY crowded, though, especially in the nice weather.
