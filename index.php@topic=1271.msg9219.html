I worked in Flushing Meadows Park for a while, and we had a family of Raccoons living in the building next to us (I named them Inky, Blinky, Pinky, Clyde, and Sue, and I think Uncle Norbit came to visit every now and then) I did a great deal of research in to them as we had workers going in and out of the building they lived in.

It's illegal to kill them.

Animal Care and Control will not remove them from any locations in NYC unless they are dead or injured.

They are as smart if not smarter than the average dog.

There are more rabid dogs in this country then there are rabid Raccoons.

Coyote Urine powder doest not work to keep them away.

Apparently, they hate tomatoes.

The fact that they are becoming more common in residential areas is a cause for concern. The less they fear people the more dangerous it becomes for everyone involved. We took to barking and yelling at them, and that seemed to help until they got used to that too. Best way to deal with them I'd say s give them a wide berth and keep on going.

Of Course the other way to reduce the Raccoon population is to reduce or eliminate their food source, this is where keeping the streets clean becomes more than just aesthetics it's a matter of public safety.
