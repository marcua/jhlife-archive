I agree this is a situation that needs to be addressed right away.  We got a little bit of a reprieve when the bus route was mercifully moved from 73rd St., but things seem to be getting worse again.  And the new construction just north of The Birchwood House does not bode well for our congestion and noise concerns. 

A cop friend of mine advised that it might help if we all consistently attend the Community Council meetings and make our voices heard.  We need to get in their faces and be the squeaky wheel.  The 115 gets an allotment of "Operation Impact" officers and they generally put all of them on Roosevelt Ave, busting drunks and prostitutes.  If we could just get a couple of those Impact cops posted on 73rd, we might see a decrease in these quality of life issues and the city will make a mint on traffic violations.  Here is info on the 115th Pct. Community Council meetings:

Community Council

President: Janice Scurry
Vice President: Alice Thorpe
Treasurer: Yvonne Scott
Recording Secretary: Myrna R. Jackson
Seargent at Arms: Vacant
Corresponding Secretary: Vacant
Assistant Secretary: Vacant

Meetings: The Community Council meetings are held the third Tuesday of the month (except July and August) at 7:00 P.M. at the 115th Precinct, 92-15 Northern Boulevard.

And just a note about honking violations.  The reason why cops rarely write citations for honking is because these tickets get challenged and thrown out all the time.  The judge will ask the officer, "Did you actually see the driver hit the horn?"  Just hearing the horn is not enough; the burden of proof hurdle is pretty high.

The city has told us that it will no longer enforce honking laws, issue tickets, etc.  Is that really a surprise, given how pervasive honking is, how unenforceable the rules/laws?  Now, Erica, you point to the myriad of problems here -- blocking the box, delivery hours, jaywalking, double parking.  yes, all problems.  And hopefully each issue can be addressed, problems minimized.  But this works best if added to another approach -- just learn to better tolerate such disturbances.  They are -- fundamentally -- an unchangeable thread of the fabric of urban life.  The problems cited are not unique to J.H. They are city wide, they are national, international.  Sure, we may want our neighborhoods to be quiet pedestrian zones, locomotion limited to feet and bicycles.  But that ain't going to happen, so best to try to accept and adjust.  Also, for pure pedestrians, it may be useful to try to imagine what it all this looks like from a driver's point of view.  Car and truck and bus drivers are, and will remain, part of the mix, after all.

I have lived in JH since the mid '50s - so I am a really long time resident. Yes, the noise has definitely increased along with a major parking issue, double parking, etc. Remember the fact that cars were not as abundant in the 50's, 60's, 70's, etc. Therefore, buildings were built without garages, parking spots were eliminated because of construction and urban planning (i.e. 82nd Street) and more people with cars (and even 2 cars) have moved to the area.

Then there are more merchants with large followings - many outside the general area (esp. 74th Street Indian/Palestinian stores). All are wonderful but bring in more cars and traffic. There are also many medical offices with patients from outside the area. More schools built for a ever growing population - with curtailed parking and school buses. All good things for a vibrant neighborhood.

JH is a great area and is definitely crowded. More high rises were built in place of single family homes - a good reason for the historic area to curtail that. We need to encourage thinking and planning to address these issues so that quality of life can be more relaxed and quiet.

It will be a hard issue to tackle but we need to start working together. We need to be united and bring in all aspect of the community - residents, merchants,  professional and elected officials. I would call upon the JHBG to be the force that brings the greater JH community together for a think tank sort of process. Only then will all aspects be heard and addressed. JHBG has brought positive actions before and can do it again in the future.
