Checkpoints on Roosevelt Ave
« on: July 06, 2015, 05:39:32 PM »
Last night while driving on Roosevelt Ave there was a NYPD checkpoint set up for cars driving westbound on Roosevelt at around 93rd. St. I waited in a long line of cars who were also being stopped, and when I finally got up to the cop who was stopping people I was asked for my license. I handed over my license and asked what I was being stopped for. He looked over my license and said that they were just stopping people to check licenses. After looking over my license he sent me on my way. I've got a valid license, car is registered and insured, no reason to worry, etc., and I was pretty shaken up. I've never encountered this before. How frequently does this happen here in JH? Is this something new? Why are they doing this? Anyone have any insight into this checkpoint practice? Is it even legal to be stopped while driving and asked for ID without cause (or, not to be told what the reason for the checkpoint is)?

You know what - my husband is a person of color who's been stopped and frisked his whole adult life, and he would gladly sit in a checkpoint for this purpose because it is worth it.
