It was a great meeting tonight.  I didn't get a head count, but the JCJH was packed to capacity.

The coalition of aggrieved 37th Road businesses and 75th Street residents was there and was quite vocal.  They made their presence felt with some sharp questioning and a few outbursts, but they were significantly outnumbered by supporters of the plaza and other transportation improvements.  When Dromm made his pitch for the types of transportation and livability improvements he was trying to bring to the neighborhood, the crowd broke out into loud and sustained applause.  

In general, Dromm and Crowley divided up the questions so that Dromm answered the questions about local issues and Crowley answered the questions about federal issues.  But I was quite pleased that when Crowley was pressed on the plaza controversy, he didn't distance himself from it, as he easily could have.  Instead, he reminded the audience that he was the one who secured the federal grant for the study, and that he had done so because the neighborhood had been looking for solutions to its chronic traffic problems.  I thought this was a lot more helpful on this issue than I expected him to be.

At the end of the meeting, a representative from the JH Green Alliance read a very nicely written statement in support of the plaza.  He said that while the Green Alliance wasn't involved in asking for the plaza and the transportation changes, they recognized that the city had made these changes after an extensive public process -- and that opponents' claims to the contrary were damaging efforts to build civic engagement and ensure that public agencies are accountable to the demands of the community.   It made a number of excellent points.  Hopefully somebody from the Green Alliance can post the statement here.
