Restaurants & Food / Re: Best Pizza
« on: October 12, 2008, 05:28:03 PM »
Walk into a room full of 100 native new yorkers and ask where to get the best pizza or bagels or burger, and you are sure to get 100 unique answers to each questions. So I humbly give my opinion. Gustosa's is the best new york pizza in Jackson Heights.
Since moving to Jackson Heights I have been to 3 pizzerias:
Famous Pizza: you can have whatever you want as a topping on these custom-made pizzas, but the crust has the consistency of wet cardboard. Plus, be prepared to wait 15 or 20 minutes for your pie. I don't know what this is, but it's not NYC pizza.
Peppino's: the crust is ok but the cheese is too greasy. If you're eating a Peppinos slice you better have some napkins nearby because there is going to be some grease running out the back of the slice and onto your hands or your lap. Beware the large inventory of pizza with different toppings they have sitting on the counter. The only way they can have that kind of inventory is by putting it in the refrigerator at night. They don't have much pizza with vegetables and seem to have focused on pizzas meat toppings. They don't even display their plain slice. They have it under the counter and you can ask for it. Is it so much to ask for a simple pizza with broccoli or spinach?
Gustosa's: a crispy doughy crust, an even distribution of sauce and cheese, and the cheese is not very greasy. Every time I have been there, I have been served by the old italian guy who is clearly the owner. They have very little inventory because they clearly make their pizza fresh as it is needed, and I think their strategy is more focused on vegetable slices, and if you want meat they start with a plain slice and add whatever you want. At $2 for a plain slice, i think Gustosas is a great value and probably the best in Jackson Heights.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: summer night noise
« on: April 24, 2008, 08:04:43 AM »
I am not aware of any traffic ordinances requiring motorists to not play loud music. If there were such an ordinance, I doubt it would be effective. On my block, there are multiple signs posted with $350 fines for honking, and yet everyone honks incessantly. Every once in a blue moon a cop writes some tickets, but the next day the cars are out there honking again.
To combat the noise, a first step would be to install sound-proof windows. Also, I have found that air conditioners installed in windows will let in a lot more noise than closed windows that do not have air conditioners mounted in them. But living without AC in the summer could be worse than the noise.
