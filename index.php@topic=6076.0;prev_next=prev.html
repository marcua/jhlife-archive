welllll....

my ex and i bought a house on 75th street in 1995 (and moved in in 96). it had been owned by the same lady for 50 ish years and had almost all of the original 1920s details. that's why we fell in love with it and bought it. we maintained all of the original stuff in the duplex that we occupied, which was pricey to fix up, and we went half and half with the rental unit, replacing with old subway tile, maintaining wood floors etc etc

i think the classic look/feel is really cool, and it fits the vibe you get walking down streets here: seeing classic facades, gardens, etc, and walking in to cold, sleek modernity is kind of a disconnect to me. "open" can be good, depending on the feel when you walk in. it can also be bleak and cavernous. on the fence about that, but marble floors would be a deal breaker. those always make me feel like i am in a public space, too cold, too soulless.

i also like your idea of different feels for the different bathrooms. i'd really like a whirlpool
