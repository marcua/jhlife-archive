The main reason this building looks contextual is because it is in the Jackson Heights Historic District.  It also helped that a number of people, including myself, testifed at the Landmarks Preservation Commission's public hearings three or so years ago.  For example, had it not been for our commentary, the east-facing facade would have had a wall of brick without one window.  Other aesthetic improvements were also the result of community input.

According to the final plan, there will be a small garden, of similar depth to the adjacent residential building, on the 84 street elevation... although it will be interrupted by the entrance to the building itself and to the garage.

Again, my advice is get involved and not simply react.  If you want something better for your community, you have to stick your neck out and have your voice heard when it matters.
