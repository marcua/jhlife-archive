Title: Re: where was jackson diner located?
Post by: toddg on December 04, 2009, 09:35:29 PM
I think it may have moved more than once.  My first-ever visit to Jackson Heights was circa 1990, and when a friend who grew up nearby took me to the Jackson Diner.  I remember it being on the second floor of a building on 74th Street... perhaps near Al-Naimat, or perhaps not quite so far up the block. It was a genuine dive.  I remember the restaurant as being hot and even steamy because it had an open kitchen.  It had plastic checkered tablecloths and other diner-like touches, but served miraculously delicious and spicy Indian food.  I think the contrast between the excellent food and dive-y atmosphere of the place is what led to its citywide fame around that time. 

Little did I know at the time that this would be my future neighborhood.  And little did I know at the time that another friend who was along for the meal would become my future wife!

The original Jackson Dinner was a small silver bullet dinner - free standing - on 74th street next to Pizza Boy. The building was built around it. It was the basic dinner - burgers, eggs, sandwiches, etc.
