Some questions to ask yourself:

-how far are you willing to travel to go to the gym?
-does it need to be near a train or have parking?
-how late/early do you need it to be open?
-how clean do you need the locker rooms to be?
-would a more heavy weightlifting atmosphere be bothersome?
-what kind of class or group are you looking for?
-how up-to-date do you need the machines to be?
-would you need the gym to provide towels or anything in the locker room like shampoo?

A general word of advice about gyms: in my experience, memberships are negotiable.  Many will tell you they have a special going on "today only" and that if you walk out the door, the price will surely rise in the time it takes you to make up your mind.  High pressure sales tactics.  Don't fall for it.

If you need to find a gym right here in JH, the options are severely limited.  You could check out EFN Zone at 78th and Roosevelt.  

If you are open to gyms in different areas, then I suggest you find out if the ones you are interested in will offer a free trial workout.  This is a good way to see if you like the atmosphere. For gyms in Woodside, Astoria, etc. read the thread on local gyms, health clubs and exercise spots .

Yeah, you can spend as much or as little as you want. $50- $100/month seems about standard. I know Ballys is cheaper. But you get what you pay for. The one in Elmhurst is inconvenient and not very nice. You can even spend nothing and get a great workout in your apartment or the park. It's all about the atmosphere and extras your want.

Keep in mind too, that gyms make their money from people with good intentions who end up not using the gym but still paying the monthly fee. I recommend trying out different gyms to see what you like. Most any gym will give you a free day or week to try them out. Just don't give in to the hard sell right away. This approach will help you see what's really important to you and make an educated choice. It will also help you see if your heart is really in it. If after 2 weeks of workouts you find you're not going anymore, at least you won't be stuck with a monthly gym bill.

Lucielle Roberts is around $325 with a 2 year contract.  If you want to do monthly its around $10-12 per month and every 6 months a maintenance fee of I think 10-15 dollars.

I use to go to the one on Steinway hopping on the G,V or R train to Steinway.  I paid $40 bucks more to upgrade to gold, meaning I can use other gyms.  So now I frequent the one on 71 Continental, a one express stop towards Jamaica.  Both take me 10 minutes to get to by train.

First use a free 5 day pass and see if you like the gym. 

I use to be a NY Sports Club member, but that can cost to more than $100 a month.  Over there, I would get a new towel, sometimes shampoo, (key locker-some locations) and massages.  Big difference in machines.  I remember their running machines had headphone pug-in to listen to their own tv network. 

Some gyms are 24/7.  Try the out before paying.  Also just for canceling my NY Sports Club membership within 2 years, I got charged around $175 and an extra month since their policy is to tell them one month in advance. 

If you need any further information, feel free to message me.

Also, taking classes with your friends is fun.  The classes at 71 Continental get very very packed.  Perhaps 100+.  Its not as crowded at Steinway. Both have waiting lists for the Elliptical during rush hours.  Ask the reps when both get busy.
