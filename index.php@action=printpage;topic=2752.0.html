Title: Re: plumber hunt
Post by: sulvndnr on June 22, 2009, 12:26:43 PM
I don't want to say who not to use, but i had someone who was recommended on this board check out a leaky toilet that was leaking from the second floor bathroom.  I wasn't to happy. He said the kitchen ceiling had to be broken to fix the leaky pipe. Uh Oh, does that mean breaking parts of the 100 year old tiled wall as well? Yup, but he said "you could find the same 100 year old tiles on e bay or craigslist."
  HA Ha. Ha
 i asked how do you know  where the leak is coming from? His answer was the pipes are 100 years old, and that's what it was. And he told me he has been doing plumbing for the past 30 years. Yup, he said "that's where its coming from."  No Doubt.       Hmmmmm.
   And i asked "how much would i get to pay you to do  this?"  $1800. Nice.... And that's not including repairing the damaged walls and ceilings.  I had serious doubts. I spoke to a friend who is a super.  "Wax Ring." I said what? "Wax Ring."  He came over, we lifted the toilet, and  yes, the 100 year old wax ring was indeed decomposed. We went to that great little hardware store, on 88th and 37th, A $10 wax ring, and a little epoxy later, my ceiling has been dry for the past 2 months. So everyone, get multiple estimates BEFORE committing to the first one that comes buy. And dont take recommendations as fool proof. They help, but buyer beware.
