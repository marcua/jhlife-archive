A Weird Thing that Happened: The Thomas Pizza Edition
« on: April 15, 2010, 08:29:47 PM »
So the boyfriend and I ordered a mini pizza and a salad from Thomas tonight.  When It arrived, the delivery guy said "$20."  $20?  For a mini and a salad?  "No," he replied.  "MEDIUM."  But we ordered a MINI.  TEN INCH.  "No.  Medium."  Well we're not going to pay for something we didn't order.  Should we call the restaurant?  "Ok."  So the boyfriend rang them up, explained the situation, the restaurant asked to speak with the delivery guy, got back on the phone with boyfriend, yadda yadda yadda.  The long and short of it was that the guy at the restaurant asked us to KEEP FOUR SLICES AND SEND THE REST BACK.  Which we did.  Because we were hungry.  But what are they doing with the rest of that pizza?  I can only imagine that they're going to sell it as slices.  Which is gross.  I mean call me a little hygiene-obsessive, but I wouldn't want to eat a slice of pizza that had already been in another customer's kitchen.  Ew.   

...And I mean maybe they were just going to eat it themselves?

...Or it was just the principle of the thing?

I think I'll be looking for a new pizza place now.  After I eat this one last slice.

I really doubt they re-sold the slices, they probably dumped them or just ate them.  It is common practice for pizza places to make you return the pizza if you call and say they got the order wrong.  They will bring you a new one, but they always take the old one.  Otherwise they'd be dealing with scammers always calling because their order was "wrong".  It's a little odd because they asked for half of the pizza back, but I guess it's policy.  But if you are regulars, they should have just let it go.

I have to say - they have my least favorite pizza in NY. It's just awful (to me).  Least favorite in the world: Lido's Pizza in the DC-area - it's like matzoh with catsup. And people love it! Does.not.compute.
