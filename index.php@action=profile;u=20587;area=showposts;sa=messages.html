Neighborhood Chat / Re: Is this the end of the Jackson Heights Historic District??????
« on: March 02, 2016, 12:18:58 AM »
It's not the end of the historic district.  It's a homeowner who didn't follow the rules of a landmark district.  Landmarks can be a pain, but Jackson Heights has been landmarked for more than 20 years now, and this homeowner bought AFTER the landmarking, so they just seem to be throwing good money after bad at this point. (My advice is, quit tilting at windmills)

I'll note, that the reason the LPC was showing the 1980's photos is because they care about landmarked neighborhoods!  They want them restored, not degraded further by bad renovation.  If you try and replace the siding, they will probably steer you toward a more Tudor style than is now there, to return the building to its ORIGINAL CONDITION!  That's the GOAL, one that a lot of owners in landmarked neighborhoods share.   

And if you think that LPC is over reaching by telling you to remove your new fence,  think again -  in 2005 they had a building in TRIBECA remove an entire 2 story addition, that was finished.  And I think those developers had a decent legal team as well.

From the article about 145 Hudson -
http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/05/06/big-deal-hudson-st-penthouse-back-on-the-market/
The original design and construction ran into trouble after the Landmarks Preservation Commission found that it was not appropriate for the neighborhood.
For years, enraged residents were unable to move into their apartments because the commission’s decision kept the building — known as Skylofts — from getting its certificate of occupancy. In 2005, the developers were forced to tear down the original penthouse.
