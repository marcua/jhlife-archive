I'll defend them somewhat.  I have a home in upstate NY that is on the corner of a block near Main Street.  Quite often, campaigns place their signs on that corner, in the grass, in a part of the corner that is public property, but that is directly in front of my home - the only home visible from the street.  Over the years, I have taken down every single sign put up by Republican politicians as I view this as a campaign sending a message that my home is voting for, and supporting, their candidate.  One time, I caught a campaign staffer attempting to place a sign and told him not to place it there because I'll throw it away.  He obliged, got in his car, and went two homes down the road.  That homeowner also removed the sign.

If I recall correctly, someone actually sued for this right, and the Court agreed that placing a sign in front of a home violates the First Amendment as it has the net effect of impugning political speech on you.  So maybe the people complaining were doing so because they were not Nixon supporters, and did not wish to have an advertisement in front of their building giving people the impression that they are.
