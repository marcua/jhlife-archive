I thought I would start a thread for the newcomers in the neighborhood with a few of the places that I didn't see right away when I moved here.  Most of these stores are not exactly "hidden" but newcomers may not know they are nearby. Please feel free to add on your non-food finds.
 
Home goods
Home Depot in East Elmhurst, corner of 73rd St. and 25th Ave. about 100x more pleasant than the one on Northern
Bed Bath and Beyond (same location as Home Depot)
Corningware/Revere/Corell factory store on Northern Blvd one block
west of the BQE on the north side of the street (haven't been here yet)
 
Grocery Store with parking lot
Waldbaum's in East Elmhurst - same location as Cannelle Patisserie
 
Target
Target in Elmhurst is easy to get to via subway; walking it's about 30 min.
Target in Whitestone is very easy to drive to (10-15 min) and has a huge parking lot.  Plus lots of other big box stores nearby including a BJ's, Waldbaums, Marshalls or maybe it's a TJMax, Circuit City, Staples
 
books
Barnes and Noble
Forest Hills on Austin St. - take the E or F train one stop
 
Odds and Ends
Mexican cowboy boots and sombreros: Zapateria Mexico 8807 Roosevelt Ave
Work and moving equipment such as safety vests, flat movers' dollys, ladders, work gloves: Northern Blvd somewhere in the low 100's on north side of street (haven't been inside)
Pattycakes Cake Decorating Supplies and Candy Making Equipment
34-55 Junction Blvd. (read about it on another thread here)
Sewing Machine and Vacuum Store: 82nd St. about one block into Elmhurst from 82nd St. 7 station (they didn't have my vacuum bags in stock but said they would order them)

PaintBenjamin Moore Paint on 37th Ave and 90th St.  Also another location at 65th and Roosevelt in Woodside

For those with cars:

I like going to the shops at Bay Terrace in nearby Bayside.  There's a large Barnes & Noble there with a nice children's section in the back.  The staff is friendly, and I've gotten great recommendations on gifts for my nephews.  Bay Terrace also offers free parking in its large parking lot.  There's also a Sony movie theater, diner, good pizza, a nice and clean Waldbaum's, Duane Reade, Kinkos and a post office that's conveniently low key.   All this in addition to a host of other shops.  We'll usually catch a movie, kill some time browsing the bookstore, have a snack and pick-up some groceries before returning home.

http://www.bayterrace.com/

For rugs I needed two different types - one large authentic oriental rug for my bedroom, which I found via a rug dealer who was advertising at the time on craiglist.  He no longer seems to have ads up, which may mean he is out of the country or something, but there is some contact info on this blog http://www.herebeoldthings.com/alternative_hunts/index.html

I also needed something very inexpensive and kind of industrial for another area of my apartment.  I thought about remnants from ABC Carpets but nothing really fit the bill.  I ended up going to JAG Carpet and Rug distributors in Brooklyn, as that's where I was moving from and it was convenient for me.  I found them easy to work with and thought the prices were very reasonable.  They deal quite a bit with institutional rugs (for schools, etc.) so they were definitely right for my needs, but may not be for yours. http://www.jagcptrugs.com/  There are lots of photos on their website.  I'm not sure if they deliver.

There are several carpet and rug stores within walking distance on Northern Blvd.  I can think of one that may be in the '80s on the south side of the street, another on the north side just by the BQE around 70th St. and another past the BQE in Woodside possibly a few blocks from the BQE.

Mainstream Movies - take the R/V/G to Steinway and walk to UA Kaufman Astoria theatres for first run movies.
Artsy Movies - Kew Gardens Cineman on Lefferts Blvd. is a good local'ish place to see artsy movies - better than going to manhattan. And you can cross the street and have great pub food at Austin Ale House.

Chain Stores: Take the R/V/G to 46th and walk a long block south to Marshall's/Old Navy/Chucky Cheese/Home Depot. 
Mall: Take the R/V/G to Woodhaven and you'll be at a Queens Center Mall - pretty conventional mall where you can find Macy's and JC Penney.
Atlas Park: Take the 29 bus from 82nd Street to Atlas Park - a strange european like mall with a few decent restaurants and nice open space with a fun kid friendly water fountain. The shopping sucks though - they are still working on filling vacancies. But they also have a movie theatre that is decent, and an Amish Marketplace. Oh, I shouldn't forget the large Barnes & Nobles. Driving there is a better idea....

Bowling Alley/Ping Pong/Pool/Fooseball - there is a decent and fairly nice place to do all of these things. BQE Billiards and the bowling alley are at 34th Avenue and 71st or so...about a ten minute walk from the historic district. They have a nice bar too - sort of sportish.

Jewelery/Framing: European Gallery/Franco's Corner on 35th around 84th. Franco has some nice antique jewelery. I have used the Gallery for framing quite a bit, but I think they are overpriced and a bit rude.

Newman Jewelers on 35th Between 78th and 79th has beautiful china (Kate Spade, Vera Wang, ect) at very good discount prices.

Clothing
Famous Brands - Roosevelt and 78th. Cheap, cheap, cheap clothing from relatively well known brand name companies like NY&Co, Liz Claiborne.

EFN Zone - a gym right above Famous Brands. The only real full service gym in the 'hood. They are VERY inexpensive too - but I found too crowded after work and the aerobics instructors were poorly trained. They do very little warm up.

Corona Ice King in Corona around 103 and 55th Avenue or so - an EXCELLENT thing to do on a weekend night in the summer is go to Corona Ice King, get a lemon ice (or a gourmet flavor if you choose) and sit and watch the men play boci (sp?) in Spaghetti Park (that's a nickname...but it sticks....) Watch out for loose bocci balls....

I used to live in Manhattan - and now I barely ever leave Queens! My good friends in Brooklyn who are still renting are jealous!
