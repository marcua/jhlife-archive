News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I have been following the construction of Diversity Plaza from the consultation meeting at PS 69 some long while ago to the tree plantings over the last week. Although I can't imagine any circumstances that might make me a regular user of this space some of the original concerns expressed by other attendees at the PS 69 event still seem relevant. The main concern was that the space is safe for women and children and that means enforcement of a no drinking,no hassling policy and that means some kind of oversight.
In short it is a Bryant Park solution tailored to the needs of JH. It doesn't require constant policing just someone who keeps an idea on things and can summon help if necessary. if we don't have something like this it will just be a men's club for guys with too much time to kill.
