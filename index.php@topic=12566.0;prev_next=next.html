Hi, Neighbors!

This will be our first Halloween in Jackson Heights... so what's the trick-or-treating situation here?

I live in a decently sized apartment building (65-ish units, some families with kids) I'd like to know if I should expect kids knocking on my door. If so, I want to make sure I've got something for them.

When we lived in Astoria, kids trick-or-treated at local businesses on the main thoroughfairs, not at apartments. I learned that the hard way when I first moved here and ended up with a gigantic bowl of candy to myself. Oops.

I guess I can always bring any extra candy into work, but I am concerned about the prospect of being left alone all weekend with a bowl of KitKats. 

Thanks in advance for any info!

If I were you I would ask the Board to allow the creation of a Holiday planing committee.
While in one of the Boards we set up a Holiday committee several years ago.  We had the beautification committee handle it at first but as time past it started to fizzled out because the committee had so many projects going on and Christmas was such a huge engagement .  In one of the Board meetings I suggested a Holiday committee and as of today they have amazing decorations in the large lobby of the building for every major Holiday as well as Halloween. 

I was just there today to help them out with some issues and I was amazed on how haunted house-like the lobby was.  It was wonderful to see it since I haven't stepped into the Lobby during Halloween in almost 10 years and I had completely forgotten about it.  I had to congratulate the VP (she is still a dear friend) and the super for fomenting the participation of the Holiday's committee.  Its important to invite and allow the participation of the shareholders in as many committees as possible. 

Admittedly,  since tomorrow is Friday this will be the 1st time I bring my youngest son (He is now 6) to the parade.  He is super excited about the parade and the thought of his first Trick-or-treating ever.
