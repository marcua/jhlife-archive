Community Groups, Activities & Events / HOLIDAY CAROLING - Sat, Dec 17
« on: November 26, 2011, 08:28:20 PM »
Saturday, December 17, 2011
7:00pm until 10:00pm
* * * FREE EVENT * * *

Meet near the Dante's Shopping Center, 31st Ave at 76th St

3rd Annual Jackson Heights Winter Holiday Sing-A-Long
An Outdoor Carroling Event -- Snow or Shine !!!


Meet on 31st Avenue, between 76th Street and 77th Street, near the bus stop on the Modell's side of the avenue. (It's okay to park in the Dante's lot for this event.)

Dress warmly and according to the weather!
Wear comfortable / appropriate shoes or boots.

Bring a flashlight or well-protected candle for each person in your group.

School Groups welcome and encouraged to join us.
All children under age 16 must be accompanied by an adult!
If possible, children under age 12 should wear reflective clothing instead of carrying open-flame candles.... for obvious safety reasons.

(Service animals welcome; all other pets should remain at home.)

We will sing winter and holiday songs from all faiths, languages & cultures as a group, while walking thru the local Jackson Heights area. Please be respectful and courteous to others, even if you don't know the particular song we're singing at the moment. We will sing in every and any language the members of the group know, including American Sign Language from students and faculty at the Lexington School.

Small, accoustic, portable instruments welcome and encouraged.

We will begin by crossing 31st Ave and walking up 76th Street, while singing. The group will then determine the direction and how long we stay out there singing. A good suggestion is to look for houses with holiday lights!


Santa Hats encouraged, but not required.
Holiday outfits, costumes, or dress-up welcome.


SPREAD THE WORD & PLEASE INVITE ALL YOUR FRIENDS TO JOIN US

Neighborhood Chat / Tractor Trailers and double parking on 31st Ave
« on: December 17, 2009, 08:50:45 PM »
How do we get the Transportation Committee to address the ongoing hazzard of LARGE TRACTOR TRAILER TRUCKS that are using local streets (off their designated truck routes) when leaving the Dante's Shopping Center on 31st Ave?

The streets are caving in some places, full of holes in other places.... the trucks literally rattle the houses and knock items off shelves.

It was NEVER like that before !!!!!!!!

And while we're at it.... I love having a 24 hr store on 31st Ave BUT..... the endless double-parking by customer of the Super Bazaar is really out of hand. It is dangerous because when you're headed up 31st Ave, you can't see around all their cars & SUV's. Someone is going to end up having a head-on collision with another driver turning right out of the Dante's parking lot.

Local residents have tried 311, NYPD, the tv news, local papers, CB3, and even the Mayor's office and other elected officials with no luck. Any suggestions, before the upcoming snow/ice season makes this problem even more dangerous?

Neighborhood Chat / JACKSON HEIGHTS MILLION DOLLAR LOTTERY WINNER !!!
« on: December 17, 2009, 08:45:41 PM »
From the Daily News:

A Queens construction worker who just won $1 million in the New York State lottery plans to use the cash to bring his wife and eight children here from Ecuador.

"This is the best Christmas present," said Florencio Guaman, 64, of Jackson Heights, who hasn't seen his family in 15 years.

"I just remember their faces vaguely," he said during a ceremony at Trump Wollman Rink in Central Park Monday. "I even began forgetting about their birthdays. I left them behind when they were children and now they are grown men and women.

"Some of them have children of their own," he said.

As soon as Guaman won the scratchoff New York Black on Nov. 23 during a stop at the K&L; Dairy Farm store on 37th Ave. in Queens, he knew right away what he would do with his winnings.

"I scratched the ticket and showed it to my friend, the clerk and we both started jumping around," he said. "I began dreaming. Maybe I don't have to just speak on the phone anymore."

Guaman left his native Azogues in Ecuador when his oldest children were teenagers. Now, they are in their early 30s.

"It has been very difficult for me, to be so far away," he said.

Guaman's immigration documents do not allow him to leave the country, so he said he will bring his family here soon for a visit on tourist visas.
