I live on 88th & 35th. I am affiliated with a performing arts studio on 89th. I catch the train at 90th. This is my backyard. There are a number of apartments on these corners that have tenants that gather outside the entrances and connect. There is a barber shop on 37th that is central of the apartments. Last time I was stopped by the cops (I am always getting stopped) the woman cop let me go with a compassionate warning. But oddly, she hardly looked at me and never looked at my insurance paper. She kept her eye on the barber shop, and she made eye contact with someone at the barber shop and even made some eye/head gestures to him. My opinion is there is gang activity centered around the shop--mind you not saying it IS the shop--and undercover is positioned there. My son went there once years ago, and refused to ever go back, even to walk past it. They would approach him. He was afraid of them.

Maravillosa is a lousy establishment too. I think what it is by day--a restaurant--is not what it is by night, with completely different ppl running it. These places, like the cabs, rent out and are open for business 24/7. 

Every young man's life that is snuffed out for this BS/nonsense makes me very sad. We always focus on the criminal, but there is something very wrong at the core of our society that fosters this violence.
