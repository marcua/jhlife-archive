"Rembrandt: The Master as Printmaker"

Who:  Dr. Martha Hollander

When:  March 9, 2008
           Sunday
           3:45 PM

            Club Gallery
            33-50 82nd Street
            St. Mark's Church (lower level)
            Jackson Height, NY  11372

More info:  718.899.0065, or
             AlexisADaniels@aol.com


Dr. Martha Hollander, who will give a presentation entitled "Rembrandt: The Master as Printmaker" at the General Membership meeting of the
Jackson Heights Art Club.  Dr. Hollander received a B.A. from Yale University and a PhD. from the University of California at Berkeley, both in art history, and she has taught at Hofstra since 1995.  She has also taught at UCLA, SUNY Albany, School of Visual Arts, Pratt Institute, and Parsons School of Design.

Her specialization is seventeenth-century Dutch art, but she also teaches surveys of western art, courses in visual culture and art historical method, women's studies, and courses based on sites in and around New York City.  She is also interested in the history of costume and the history of film, film adaptations of literature, and the history of production design and art direction. 

We all know Rembrandt as one of the greatest painters of the western tradition, but you may not be aware that he was also an innovative and
pioneering printmaker.  He was one of the first artists of his age to do a brisk business in the new medium of etching.  In this talk, Dr.
Hollander will explore his etched portraits, self-portraits, and narratives.  Plus, she will discuss his restless experiments with the complex etching technique, and his entrepreneurial spirit as a marketer of his own prints.
 

Dr. Hollander is also our neighbor.  She moved to the area with her family on the historic New Year's Eve of 1999.  Don't miss this opportunity to not only hear an outstanding presentation but also to meet a local resident of Queens.

The event is free and open to the public.

Jackson Heights Art Club

Club Gallery
33-50 82nd Street
St. Mark's Church (lower level)
Jackson Heights, NY

ABOUT US[/b]
Since its beginning in 1948, the Club has been committed to furthering the knowledge and enjoyment of visual arts among the people of Jackson Heights and adjacent communities in western Queens.  We accomplish these objectives by maintaining an art studio and teaching facility which offers classes in all media and by presenting a year-round calendar of visual arts events including indoor and outdoor art exhibits and painting demonstrations.



EVENTS

1.  OUTDOOR ART SHOW

In the Spring and Fall, The Jackson Heights Art Club holds its Outdoor Art Show called "on the fence" off PS69 located on 37th Avenue between 77th and 78th Streets in Jackson Heights.  For two weekends each season from 10:00 AM until 5:00 PM, members and nonmembers are invited to exhibit their works for a small fee.  The exhibit features both framed and unframed paintings in oil, watercolor, acrylic, pastel, mixed media and photography.  The event is open to the public free of charge.
 

2. ARTIST DEMONSTRATIONS

About 4 times a year, the Jackson Heights Art Club presents a demonstration by a guest artist at the Club Gallery located at 33-50 82nd Street, Jackson Heights.  The demonstration is open to the public free of charge.



3.  ART INSTRUCTION

The Jackson Heights Art Club offers watercolor, oil, acrylic, pastel and drawing classes for both adults and children's at the Club Gallery located at 33-50 82nd Street in Jackson Heights.

Adult Classes

There are classes throughout the weekdays and are day, or evening, depending on the medium.  The classes are 8 week sessions and cost $80 for members and $100 for nonmembers.  The Club also offers some specialty 4 weeks classes.

Kids Classes

Multiple children's classes are offered on Saturday and Sunday at various times.  An 8 week class is only $55.



The Club currently has more than 100 members and meets at St. Mark's Church located at 33-50 82nd Street in Jackson Heights, NY.

Membership in our Club is open to artists and non-artists at an annual fee of $60.  Whether you are a working artist, an aspiring artist or someone who enjoys the art of others.  The Jackson Heights Art Club is the place where you can satisfy your urge to create, meet others in the artistic community, and learn and grow.  Watch on the Jackson Heights Life calendar for more information on our full schedule of classes, demonstrations, exhibits and other upcoming events!

The Jackson Heights Art Club Presents

53rd Annual Members Show
 

The Jackson Heights Art Club is proud to announce the opening of its 53rd Annual Members Exhibit and Sale on Friday, April 4, 2008, at 7:00 PM in the Parish House of St. Mark's Church, 33-50 82nd Street, Jackson Heights.  The exhibit will also be open from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM on Saturday, April 5, and Sunday, April 6.  The public is invited to attend the reception and the exhibition free of charge.

 

This year, The Jackson Heights Art Club celebrates 60 years of  bringing art to the Jackson Heights community.  The Club was founded in 1948 when a local artist, Natalie Norris, saw an exhibit by Nassau County artists at the Jackson Theater.  Outraged that the theater displayed work from Nassau County when there were so many talented artists in Jackson Heights, she contacted other local artists and arranged a show at the theater.  This group of 17 formed the nucleus of the organization which was originally called the Twentieth Century Art Association but was soon renamed The Jackson Heights Art Club.

 

Since its beginning, the Club has been committed to furthering the knowledge and enjoyment of visual arts among the people of Jackson Heights and adjacent communities in western Queens.  The Club operates an art studio and teaching facility which offers classes in all media and presents a year-round calendar of visual arts events including indoor and outdoor art exhibits and painting demonstrations.

 

The Club's first annual show was held in 1952 in an empty storefront just a few blocks away from where it is located today.  Since then, the Club has had an annual show every year except for a few years early on when exhibit space was not available. 

 

The current members of The Jackson Heights Art Club are pleased to continue the rich tradition, begun by the founding members many years ago, of bringing professional quality art to their neighbors in Jackson Heights. The works of art on display this year represent the finest efforts of the Club's Members and include watercolors, oils, acrylics and pastel. 

 

Activities at The Jackson Heights Art Club are supported in part by grants from Senator John D. Sabini and the New York State Education Department, Assemblyman Ivan C. Lafayette and the New York State Department of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation, Mayor Michael Bloomberg and the New York City Department of Cultural Affairs and Councilmember Helen Sears .
