A small group of us (some from JHL and some not) have been doing a little research in to the property on 82nd St. As far as the Jackson is concerned, there is interest among theater operators to lease or possibly purchase the space. I can tell you it isn't cheap.

Outreach to press etc. won't be much help if there isn't an organized group with the money to assume control of one of these spaces. Contacting electeds and getting them involved won't do much to help in the short term. We can all agree that money coming from the city or the state takes a long time to come if it comes at all.

We also have to consider that this is ultimately a Real Estate deal, and as such the more attention generated surrounding either one of the two spaces the higher the price of the property will go.

I hate to sound like a Nay sayer, and believe me if I had multiple millions of dollars to buy one of the spaces I would.  But it seems the best bet at the moment is to wait and see who wins out in the bidding process and work with them to create a space that we will all find useful and enjoyable.

Of course if any one out there has several million they aren't doing anything with, please feel free to let us know

Does anybody know how exactly the Jackson closed?

I'm originally from this area but have been living and working the past 20 years out in Long Island.  I always found myself going back to the Jackson to watch the latest movies.  The movies always seemed more special and magical in that building than the multiplexes out here.

I was in the Jackson on Sunday October 18th, 2009 and assume this was their last day of operation.  The theater's telephone schedule never changed from that weekend timetable.  The staff must not have been allowed back in on the following Monday.

Even though I live out here, I still have many emotional ties to your community and would like to include myself in any effort to help save the theater.  The Jackson was an integral part of my life growing up in the Jackson Heights community and if possible I would like to help.
