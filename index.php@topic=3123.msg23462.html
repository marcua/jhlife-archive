The photograph says it all! It's really gross and for a neighborhood that prides itself as a garden community---well---I rest my case.  One of the frustrating things about this situation is that our sanitation dept. appears to be making minimal efforts to resolve the problem, and in some cases, sad to say, contributes to it. While they are not responsible for changing peoples behavior,  they fall short with regard to enforcement and appear complacent in their own service.

How many times have you walked down Roosevelt or 37th Avenues and have seen the fronts of stores littered with trash. Same to be said for some of the private homes that line the side streets. It's so noticeable who makes an attempt to clean their property and who does not.  If owners were warned first and subsequently ticketed if appropriate, the streets would surely be a lot cleaner.

On many occasions I have witnessed sanitation trucks fly by. If they dump garbage into the truck and half of it falls on the street, don't expect them to pick it up.

Now I'm certainly not trying to hurt small business owners, especially in hard economic times or knock hard working city employees. I own my own business and my father worked for the sanitation dept. I do think that when you open a business or live and work  in a community you assume a respect for that place.
