from what I can see in the photo, he is not eartipped so he either is not a stray, a stray that has not be TNR'd or owned by someone.
Has anyone tried to pet him?  If anyone is thinking of taking him in I would strongly suggest that he go to the vet first.
They can check him for a microchip which would indicate that he has an identifiable owner. Vet will determine if he has been neutered, is FIV/FELV negative and doesn't have parasites, earmites etc.  Dr Reilly is close by.  All vets will check for a microchip at no charge.
