Lucky Bag operations have been going on for more than 10 years at least in Manhattan where I work.  They are not usually carried out by precinct cops, but by specialized units (who work in plain cloths) in response to a spike of "unattended purse snatches".  Often a business will claim that a person is hanging around and snatching unattended items, but they have no proof, so a "Lucky Bag" operation is set up.  Unattended purse snatches are often problems in bars and coffee shops.  The culprit often hangs out and once someone goes to add sugar to their coffee, the thief picks up the bag and walks out, making catching them in the act difficult.  But quickly store employees catch on that the purses always go missing at the same time as the same customer was in the store or store video shows the thief walking by the table and then leaving.  The video usually is not enough to prosecute the person as the stolen property is long gone and unless the thief is dumb enough to use the credit card from the purse, there is little to base an arrest.  A store employee can call the police and state the the suspected thief is hanging out in the store and the cops will rush over to set up a "luck bag".

The originals Lucky Bag operations that I knew about were in Port Authority with a camera bag left in a bathroom.  They were set up as travelers would constantly complain that their luggage was being stolen as they tried to use the restroom.  The general rule is that a person is not arrested unless they walk by a uniform police officer or marked police car.  There should be an obvious way for a good Samaritan to return the items.   The cases are only prosecuted if the person is seen taking and hidding items (such as opening the wallet and taking the cash and secreting it in a pocket) and then walking past a uniform police officer without attempting to return the item.

Taking the unattended items is usually just a misdemeanor, but if the police are smart, they put multiple credit cards in the luck bag.  Criminal Possession of a Stolen Credit card is a felony.  Often the police are targeting a particular person, so they are not pleased to see a good Samaritan foil their plans.  I have worked on cases where after the person is arrested we have executed search warrants on their apartments to find hundreds of purses.

I hope that helps explain the use of "Luck Bags".
