More ATM skimming in our area
« Reply #5 on: November 19, 2015, 11:40:36 PM »
http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/ATM-Skimmers-Thefts-Quadruple-Queens-NYPD-351902801.html

Excerpt:

"Identity thieves stealing information through ATM skimming devices are increasingly preying on customers in Queens, with reports of skimming incidents quadrupling in a 10-week time period this year compared to last, police say.
The NYPD is posting warning fliers on ATMs in East Elmhurst, Jackson Heights and North Corona, showing customers how to spot a skimming device. Skimmers have been found in ATMs at gas stations and convenience stores, and the high-tech devices resemble real cardreaders. "

Skyrocketing Use of ATM Skimmers
« Reply #6 on: November 20, 2015, 07:03:28 AM »
Apologies if someone's already posted a link to this story, but I thought this was disturbing:
https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20151118/east-elmhurst/yank-on-your-atm-before-you-use-it-prevent-fraud-115th-precinct-says

From the story it's not clear what part of the ATM they want you to yank on: they mean the card-reading part where you insert your card. But you should also cover your hand when you enter your PIN, since most skimmers record that by video and need that part to gain access to your account.

Here's another story on the same topic:
http://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/ATM-Skimmers-Thefts-Quadruple-Queens-NYPD-351902801.html

And here is a link to a detailed NYPD PowerPoint on the topic:
http://www.nyc.gov/html/nypd/downloads/pdf/crime_prevention/ATMskimmingpowerpoint.pdf

Looking elsewhere on the web it's clear that skimmers are becoming increasingly sophisticated and that some are compromised on the inside, so there's effectively nothing you can do to detect them. Here's a good set of posts on the topic:
http://consumerist.com/tag/atm-skimmers/
