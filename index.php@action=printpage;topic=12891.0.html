Title: Re: Can people in JH get FIOS?
Post by: mchafkin on February 01, 2015, 10:33:10 AM
I had fios for a couple of years in my old place in Brooklyn. Basically the pricing -- after all the add ons -- works out to be a couple of bucks cheaper than TWC. (I'd guess after all's said and done you can expect to save $5 a month.) You get better upload speeds, and maybe marginally better customer service. (Though for all of you expecting a huge improvement over TWC's service, don't hold your breath.) There is one significant downside: The fios box, which nobody tells you about until they show up at your door, is freaking huge. Like 5 times the size of a normal TWC modem. It's a large piece of equipment that, because of the nature of fibre optic cables, needs to be close to where the fios line comes into your house. We ended up hiding it behind a bookshelf, but its something to think about.

All in all it seems like people who don't have access to fios imagine a promised land of cheap internet. But the truth is, it's really basically the same as TWC. I'd probably order fios if it came to my building, but I don't miss it hugely
