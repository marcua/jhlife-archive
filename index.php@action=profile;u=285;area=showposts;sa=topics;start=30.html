Neighborhood Chat / New Vulnerable Street Trees Covered in Tiny Lights on 73rd St & 37th Ave.
« on: August 02, 2012, 05:03:01 PM »
  Three newly planted street trees (this year by Million Trees.org) on 73rd Street and the corner of 37th
Avenue have been chosen for decoration for the celebration of EID. They have been wrapped with strings
of hundreds of tiny blue lights and this is dangerous for their survival. These trees were already replaced
once and a few people have been caring for them as they are clinging to life on this very busy street. It's
a concern that the community organization has chosen this method of using vulnerable street trees on
public property to celebrate. The leaves are already browning and the tape for the lights is all over the
branches.
  This has been reported to the Million Trees group but if you can think of another way to tell the owners
on 73rd St. that the celebration and the lights are lovely - but just not on these young fragile trees, please
help! Thanks.

Neighborhood Chat / If any more of the local public high schools close...?
« on: April 30, 2012, 06:47:55 AM »
 I find it alarming that the situation for students who are in or headed to local high schools  from JH is turbulent. Four high schools
in a five mile radius of Jackson Heights are on the failing list out of 24 schools citywide! and are slated for closure now. Those are
Bryant and Newtown which are most popular in JH, then Long Island City and Flushing. A lot of people in the neighborhood do look
for better alternatives and we have local private schools which are great like Garden School, Renaissance HS and now McClancey
which will be co-ed in the fall. Specialized high schools and most of the charters mean long commutes and schedules which inhibit
other activities, family time and jobs, things I personally think are as important as grades. I think this is a real hole in the
neighborhood and hope it is on someone's radar as a high school would take awhile to build but think it's a truly great asset to a
community.
  Anyone?
