Bars? More than a Bunch of Dudes?
« on: June 14, 2014, 10:03:44 PM »
There are a fair amount of bars in the vicinity of Jackson Heights and Elmhurst.  Some that are halfway decent.  Just had a couple of margaritas at Cafe 75 while watching some World Cup and listening to U2, Gorillaz, Radiohead--not too bad.  And the ReadyPenny has it's moments--cheap, strong drinks, sometimes, decent tunes, sometimes abhorrent dated speed metal and bad post-grunge nonsense.  Fine and dandy.  We do the best we can in a bar-challenged neighborhood.  But what particularly sticks in my craw is that we are that rare neighborhood in which there is no place for a woman to drink in a bar--with friends, with her S.O., whatever.  Every bar, be it of the dime-a-dance, gay disco, working man's sports bar is primarily there for dudes and dudes alone.  Maybe a few exceptions along Northern?  Great!  You might be right.  But it would be amazing to see a few new places that could show up to correct this mono-gender trend.  It's boring.  It's unimaginative. It's predictable.  On that note, the most obvious exception is the amazing Terraza 7 but I went by last night and saw it was closed down for a "work order."   Anyone know anything about that?
