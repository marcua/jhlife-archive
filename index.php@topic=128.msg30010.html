It is much easier for our kids who are growing up in this neighborhood to take advantage of the diverse cultures. 

They will make friends with all kinds of different kids who speak different languages and come from different cultures.  It will be become part of their landscape of what home is.

It is not something you can really create for adults just moving into the neighborhood. 

I think if you talk to people who grew up in Jackson Heights or most parts of NYC where the neighborhoods were diverse, in the last 20 years they are pretty attuned to other cultures and peoples and have friends that are very different from them.

Yes, they do talk to each other.

Looking for cool people from all over the world who definitely want to meet their neighbors?  Here are a few suggestions from my own experience.

1.  New Visions Democratic Club - If politics is your thing, this is the activity for you.  This club really reflects the diversity of the neighborhood.

2.  Queens LGBT Pride - The first Sunday in June every year, the LGBT folks put on the production, and the whole neighborhood comes out to enjoy.

3.  Make the Road, NICE, and many others - There are so many opportunities to meet recent immigrants who are involved in the community.  Attend a march or rally.  Volunteer to help teach English.  Get involved!  :coolsmiley:
