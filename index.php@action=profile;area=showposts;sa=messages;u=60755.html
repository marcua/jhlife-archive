Community Groups, Activities & Events / NYC Neighborhood Civic Engagement Pilot - Jackson Heights Canvassing Day
« on: November 04, 2016, 11:15:48 AM »
Are you interested in community outreach?

Do you want to meet, socialize and serve with other New Yorkers passionate about volunteering and community engagement?

Do you want to bring greater opportunities for civic engagement to your community?

On Saturday, November 12th and Saturday, November 19th, NYC Service will be hosting two large-scale canvassing events in Western Queens as part of the NYC Neighborhood Civic Engagement Pilot. These events are specifically designed for New Yorkers interested in civic engagement and volunteering, and will help the City bring resources, programming and tools to outer-borough neighborhoods so they can better connect with each other and the City.


Interested in Signing Up? Volunteer Canvassers will:

  • Have a chance to meet, socialize and serve with other New Yorkers passionate about volunteering and community engagement
  • Gain experience in community outreach, an important skill for individuals interested in careers in government and working on campaigns
  • Receive free NYC Service gear!
  • Gain experience using the “VAN system” – a state of the art canvassing tool used in campaigns across the country
  • Help make a real difference in outer-borough communities in Western Queens!

Looking to get involved? To sign up for a shift, visit the NYC Neighborhood Civic Engagement Pilot Registration Page (https://a002-irm.nyc.gov/EventRegistration/RegForm.aspx?eventGuid=e2861950-8bd0-4968-a362-0bb1756519c7) or contact Evan Karl at ekarl@cityhall.nyc.gov. We are looking for energetic volunteers of all ages to come out this fall to help canvass Queens and ask residents about their experiences engaging with the city.

Don’t have time to canvass but still want to play a role? If you’re a resident of Jackson Heights, Sunnyside or Woodside, keep an eye out for canvassers wearing orange t-shirts in your neighborhood during the time periods listed below and fill out a survey! Surveys only take about 5-10 minutes to complete, but each one will play an instrumental role in determining how the city can best support civic engagement in your community.

TIMES TO VOLUNTEER

Canvassing Day #1: Jackson Heights, Queens
P.S. Q222: 86-15 37th Ave, Jackson Heights, NY 11372
11/12/2016: 11:00am – 5:00pm

Canvassing Day #2: Sunnyside/Woodside, Queens
Sunnyside Community Services: 43-31 39th St, Sunnyside, NY 11104
11/19/2016: 11:00am – 5:00pm

Community Groups, Activities & Events / NYC Neighborhood Civic Engagement Pilot - Day 4: Jackson Heights Canvassing Day
« on: September 27, 2016, 12:47:04 PM »
Do you want to play a role in empowering your neighborhood?
Do you want to bring greater opportunities for civic engagement to your community?

This fall, NYC Service, the Mayor’s Community Affairs Unit and the City University of New York will be conducting a pilot study in Western Queens to learn more about how residents engage with their communities and determine how the city can better support civic engagement and neighborhood empowerment. We are currently recruiting volunteers who will spend a day canvassing a neighborhood, administering surveys, and speaking with members of the Queens community to better understand their experiences volunteering and locate areas in which the city can provide support to organizations dedicated to volunteerism and community engagement.

Looking to get involved? To sign-up for a shift, visit the NYC Neighborhood Civic Engagement Pilot website at http://www.nycservice.org/pages/pages/101 and click the orange “Registration” link on the right-hand side of the page! We are looking for energetic volunteers of all ages to come out this fall to help canvass Queens and ask residents about their experiences engaging with the city.

Don’t have time to canvass but still want to play a role? If you’re a resident of Jackson Heights, keep an eye out for canvassers wearing orange t-shirts in your neighborhood during the time periods listed below and fill out a survey! Surveys only take about 10-15 minutes to complete, but each one will play an instrumental role in determining how the city can best support civic engagement in your community.

TIMES TO VOLUNTEER
October 8, 2016: Jackson Heights (P.S. Q222)
12pm-3pm | 4pm-7pm

*Canvassers will arrive 1 hour prior to their assigned timeslots for check-in and a short training.
