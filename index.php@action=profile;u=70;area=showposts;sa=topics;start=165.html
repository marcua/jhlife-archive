Is your stuff crowding you out of your home? We've got an answer - Stop & Swap at The Renaissance Charter School in Jackson Heights, noon to 3 p.m., Saturday, March 10, 2012. FREE

Bring your *stuff to the Stop & Swap, and while you're there, take away other people's cool stuff. Even if you don't have anything to give away, come and take away cool stuff. FREE

Neighborhood Chat / Community Composting Center Coming to Jackson Heights
« on: February 03, 2012, 08:46:17 PM »
BIG NEWS - We've received our permit to operate a community composting center under the BQE at 69th Street and 35th Avenue. We have been working on this for nearly two years.

Initially the center will take fruit and veggie scraps dropped off at the Greenmarket, and possibly from elsewhere later. That should produce a big increase in the amount of material kept out of the waste stream and will be a  big step in our quest to make Jackson Heights the greenest neighborhood in NYC (And an example for what other places can do.)

We will be posting info on compost center's opening and operation here when that info is available. For the most complete info please go to Green Agenda for Jackson Heights Facebook page and "like it."

Thank you to Lenny Olsson, Melissa Zavala, Julian McNamara, Roy Sirengo, Ed Westley and to the NYS Department of Transportation for your work on this project.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Sunday's 37th Road event postponed
« on: January 20, 2012, 07:05:00 PM »
We are postponing Sunday's "South Asia Store Crawl" at the 37th Road pedestrian plaza. A plaza opponent suddenly called a protest against the plaza for the same time as our previously schedule event.

After discussions we decided to postpone our event for at least one week. While our event was to demonstrate to merchants that the pedestrian plaza could be good for business, there's no point in risking a confrontation, as slim as that chance seems.

There is opposition to some of the traffic changes made by the city in that area, but not all the criticized changes are directly related to the pedestrian plaza.
For instance:
*The bus stop was moved off 37th Road - a major complaint of some merchants - to speed bus service. That's been accomplished, according to Councilman Dromm's office.
*The elimination of several left turns near Roosevelt and Broadway was designed to make a dangerous intersection safer. And that's also been accomplished, Dromm's office said.

It's not clear how much business is off. We saw no empty storefronts along 37th Road today (except for the long-shuttered Eagle Theater). Also, the Councilman's office says it's been rebuffed in its request for merchants to open their books to document the losses. In any case, not all business problems can be tied to traffic changes.These are tough economic times and many storefronts have become empty along 37th Avenue and 82nd Streets in the last few months. Also, plaza supporters (including a large restaurant on that street) believe a landscaped permanent plaza will eventually boost business in the area.

Finally feel free to dine and shop along 37th Road (and 73rd and 74th Streets, too) on your own and let merchants know that you think a permanent plaza will help business.

Len Maniace
