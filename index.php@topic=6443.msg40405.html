You mean American Cockroach right?  (Landlords always seem to call them waterbugs so they sound better)
I hate them too but I console myself with the fact that they reproduce slower than the little german roaches so you don't get as big of an infestation even though they are larger.

I seal everything, around floorboards, around pipes, anywhere there is a crack.  Don't leave standing water anywhere, if you go on a trip, pour ammonia down the drains before you leave so there isn't standing water.  Vacuum often.  If you notice a particular area that they always seem to come to, put out poison. 

Also if I notice bugs of any type always showing up in the same areas in addition to the above I wipe it all down really well with rubbing alcohol to try to erase any residue or markers they may be leaving.  I hate hate hate having to make contact to kill them so I keep rubbing alcohol in a spray bottle to kill them, I don't have to squish them and I don't have to breathe raid, simply sweep up a perfectly preserved body with no guts to wipe up.

They come up from the basement or from outside (You see them outside in hot and humid weather and they can FLY!!!)  Check under sinks and stoves, where pipes come up, and fill in any space.  Check base boards to see if there is enough room for them to come from behind a wall.  They can crawl from the incinerator room down the hall and under your door.  Make sure there is not enough space under the door or, alternately, put a rug over the crack during sumemr months.

It is possible that they are coming in through the opening surrounding the radiator pipes. They may also be coming up from inside the drain pipes in your kitchen sink, bathtub, and bathroom sink. The drainpipe in the kitchen sink is a big culprit for roaches entering since 1) it connects to other apartments providing a nice highway for roaches to travel between your apartment and your pigsty neighbors 2) it often has food particles decaying in it from the dirty dishes, food preparation, etc. 3) it has plenty of H2O for roaches to drink. Get a stainless steel kitchen sink drain stopper that allows water to drain but won't allow food to go down the drain (it also won't let roaches up). I would get rubber stoppers for the bathroom sink and tub as well although they are less popular with roaches than kitchen drains. Any water drain pipe is a roach party zone so I would put stops in them all when you are not using them.

Secondly,  Go to the hardware store and get acrylic painters caulk and a caulking gun. These items are not expensive. Then, go back to your apartment - caulk, caulk, and caulk some more. Roaches love cracks, crevices, nooks and crannies. They can fit through 1/4 inch spaces so go around the edge of the rooms where the floor meets the walls (or molding). Go around where the windows meet the walls. Check for any holes in the walls, ceiling, or floors; even if they are only the size of a dime, they are big enough for a roach to fit through. Fix and holes with spackel .

Lastly, get spray foam and spray around any pipes that are entering your apartment, including your radiator pipe. Make sure the foam can be used on hot pipes.Spray around the plumbing pipes, drain pipes, radiator pipes - any pipe that has a gap between itself and sheetrock/plaster surrounding it will let roaches in.
