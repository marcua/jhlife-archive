Section 508 only addresses technology being ADA accessible (e.g. websites, work equipment) and mainly applies to the realm of employment discrimination.

Despite being enacted by the Federal Government, the ADA act is expected to be enforced on a state level, and the Federal Government is exempt from complying to law since they assume it will be taken are of at a state level.  The Post Office is a Federal Entity, so they are not required to add a ramp, nor can the state force the Federal Govt to do so.

It's a big Catch-22, but this is why there is no braille on our paper currency, despite braille on drive through ATMs, or different sized currency like with the Euro, and there is still a long series of steps to get to the supreme court.  (Not sure if they have an elevator like the Lincoln Memorial)

Does your coop have published house rules regarding parties/noise complaints, or rules on subletting?  Have you spoken to the members of your board?

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Planes taking off At 5:30
« on: November 24, 2014, 03:25:16 PM »
I heard one about 5:45 which was pretty loud.  I found this on Boeing's site.

http://www.boeing.com/commercial/noise/laguardia.html


*************
AIRPORT CURFEWS
Aircraft operators are requested to voluntarily not schedule flights between the hours of 2400 and 0700.
PREFERENTIAL RUNWAYS
0600-2200 Takeoff - R/W 13 Landing R/W 22
2200-0600 Takeoff - R/W 31 landing R/W 22
****************

Now it says "voluntarily not schedule flights between" Midnight and 7am.  That's not an explicit ban on takeoffs, just departures. Potentially there was a midnight flight which got delayed for 5 hours due to last night's rain, and when they had a clearing around 5:00/5:30 they took advantage of the opportunity.

The site also says the preferred takeoff runway for that hour is 13.  Not sure if that points our way or not, but that might explain this morning.  ***Edit/Addition:  13 faces Northeast not our direction, again 'Preferred Runway' not 'Mandated Runway'  Weather factors may have prevented 13 from being utilized***

Not necessarily defending LaGuardia here.  I get just as upset when I hear one every 3 minutes about one weekend a month, but when I lived in Woodside, I remember it being far worse, and someone will get upset no matter what direction they take off and land in.

One of the inconveniences of living in an area where you're able to walk to the airport (or at least get a real cheap cab) is going to be noise.
