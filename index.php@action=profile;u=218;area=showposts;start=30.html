News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Graffiti
« on: February 19, 2008, 05:49:39 PM »
There have always been impoverished kids in America, but they didn't always feel free to vandalize public and private property. Graffiti was once practically unknown in our cities; to suggest otherwise is simply not correct. The cultural upheavals of the '60s and '70s, which encouraged the abandonment of all behavioral standards, resulted in a huge proliferation of vandalism and even the perverse glorification of it as "art". The term "graffiti artists" is especially telling in that connection.
