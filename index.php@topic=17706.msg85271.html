I think that depending on where you live, your mileage will vary.  Time Warner Cable, and now Spectrum, are great in terms of service as long as you are not having any problems that aren't caused by your modem or the cable in your house, apartment and/or building.  If it's another network or infrastructure issue it can take months to resolve.  I fought with them for half a year to get slowness and constant drop outs resolved in the 81st Street/37th Avenue vicinity.  I lived there 20+ years, and had that happen 3-4 times.  They also don't have a lot of extra capacity at their nodes, so when they do their subscriber drives they don't tend to increase unless there are enough complaints.

FIOS has a better network with a lot of extra capacity.  I manage the technical side of an IT department and have at least half a dozen FIOS circuits at work sites.  Performance issues and downtime have been extremely rare.
