Neighborhood Chat / Neighborhood Library
« on: September 04, 2010, 03:16:53 PM »

First, to save people the walk, as I saw many confused people as I walked up 81st just now, the library is typically closed Saturday during the Labor Day weekend.

Also, on that note, we are lucky that, among many library Saturday closings effective this upcomning week, our is one of I think 17 that will remain open on Saturdays.  (In fact, a disproportionate number of 6-day-open libraries are in our area of Queens, no doubt to address the greater population issue.)

The sad thing is, just two or three years ago, almost all libraries were open Saturdays and a select few (including ours) were open Sundays except the summer.

I would hope that the closings are temporary recession measures, but I feel as a parent and teacher that this is an issue that our local political leaders may want to get involved with at some point.

Neighborhood Chat / Travers Park
« on: August 28, 2010, 04:01:16 AM »
Who exactly is Travers Park named for?

Insomnia led me to this (source:  http://www.nycgovparks.org/parks/Q303/)

This park is named in memory of Thomas J. Travers (1897-1958), a prominent Queens Democrat and Jackson Heights community leader. Born and raised in Manhattan, he attended St. Agnes Church and Parochial School and MacDowell Lyceum. He served in the U.S. Navy during World War I and returned to New York to marry his childhood sweetheart, Ann Desmond. They settled in Jackson Heights, where Travers took an active part in the social and political life of the community. He served on many committees for St. Joan of Arc Church, devoted countless hours to the Catholic Youth Organization, and helped to organize the Jackson Heights Sandlot Baseball League.

The website goes on to talk about the history of the park briefly ...

Restaurants & Food / Yan Yan
« on: August 25, 2010, 06:41:12 PM »
It's a Asian desert treat, sticks and cream.

They have a few places to buy it in the neighborhood, like the fruit market across from PS 69, or the market on 79th and 37th.  The cheapest I've seen it is 99 cents each at the large Asian supermarket on Broadway and 75th.

The problem here is that they have the sticks with chocolate cream, and strawberry cream, and vanilla cream, plus the double dip versions.

Apparently though, there are mango and hazelnut versions, plus chocolate stick varieties.

One of my son's classmates got him into Yan Yan, and we are the hunt for places that sell the more eccletic flavors. 

Any Yan Yan fans out there?
