I agree that commercial rents seem to be way too high; however, let me add just a bit of good news. When Uncle Zhou left, another restaurant immediately took the space. When Ploy Thai closed, another Thai restaurant,Lamoon, took it's place. Khao Kang on Woodside Ave. recently opened Khao Nom just down the block. Thailand Center Point in Woodside is expanding into the space next door. I don't know what we can do about the high rents, but we can support the restaurants that we love. I remember when Zabb Elee opened a restaurant in Manhattan. It wasn't nearly as good as the one here on Roosevelt because Manhattan doesn't have the ethnic community to keep it real as this part of Queens does. I'm hopeful that Swim Two Birds, The Queensboro and Romeo's  will all open soon.
