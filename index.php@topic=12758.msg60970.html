Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Not sure if we really need a Food Court in JH even if its well managed and more creative then what we currently have.  There are plenty places now that I can grab momos, empanadas, kebabs.  Bourdain concept wouldn't likely fly here since it would likely have to charge higher price for the same street food that's available throughout Queens.

The main reasons I leave the neighborhood and spend money elsewhere is to:
1) go to a good food market with fresh selection of foods (produce, fish, meats)
2) get a meal outside of the ethnic Asian/Latin cuisines available here (quality brick oven pizza, upscale burger, brunch, mediterranean/falafel, quality sushi are all on the list)
3) go to a full-service gym such as NYSC. They seem to be opening all around, but on in JH probably would do well an be a better alternative to Blink over the 7 line

Also a larger cafe would be nice where you can work on a laptop/read a book. Can't take the Starbucks here--always too crowded--or Espresso 77--too small.  Caffe Bene that opened in Sunnyside is a nice alternative to Starbucks there.
Would love if the new business address 1 or 2 of the reasons I frequently leave the neighborhood and spend money elsewhere.

Does anyone know if there has been any follow-on to this meeting from Peralta's office or from Danny Dromm's office?  Lisa on jhfamilies proposed putting together a brochure designed to attract business to the neighborhood.  I think Lisa's idea is a great one.  If we put together a brochure, not only with the historic district housing prices as our metric, but also listing local successful businesses which fit a similar profile.  I would also list all of the business that we are hoping to attract.

I think that it would take a group of us meeting (a coalition, if you will) to do the following:

- Come up with information for the brochure
(I know a lot of info is already out there)
- Come up with a list of stores to target
- Perhaps distribute an online survey
- Come up with a strategy to target those businesses
- Act as a liaison to Danny Dromm's office (or maybe someone from his office would participate)
- Act as a liaison to the Bruson building's real estate agent
- Possibly work with our own commercial real estate agent to approach our targeted list of businesses
- Design and distribute the brochure.

I understand that there are things that are out of our control, in particular the willingness of the building owners to negotiate on price, but I believe it is possible for a concerted, coordinated effort with a large group of people behind it to make a difference.

If there is already a group doing this, please let the list know. I want to help!

If this is not already in process, I volunteer to host the meetings and jump-start the group.  If you are interested in participating and/or have a skill to offer the group (e.g. design experience, commercial real estate experience, have connections to Danny Dromm, the Bruson trust, their real estate agent) please e-mail me off-list at kcummings at nyc dot rr dot com and let me know what you would be interested in doing.  I don't always have time to read the list.  Please feel free to forward to anyone that you think would be interested.
