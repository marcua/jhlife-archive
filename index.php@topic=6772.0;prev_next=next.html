ART for a START is now registering for the Fall Semester!
« on: September 16, 2011, 06:31:05 PM »
Hello folks,

ART for a START, the visual arts program in Jackson Heights, is  now registering for the fall semester. We serve kids, young adults and adults, ages 3-20. 
 
Last year we had more than 45 students attending our program on a weekly basis. We offer high quality art classes while encouraging: Creativity, expression, imagination and skill building. Classes are divided into age groups and take place at P.S. 69 and in student's homes.  We also offer private classes on weekends, at Mondo Mundo Studios in Woodside.   

Check out some of our student's work from last year's Queensboro Bridge Art Contest (http://www.licartsopen.org/?page_id=262).  We have one child winning or receiving an honorable mention in each and every age group. 
More images of student's work can be found on our web site.

Please take a minute to read the info below, or check out our web site
for the fall schedule at: www.artforastartny.com.

If you choose to register, contact us directly at: debwass@earthlink.net
or send us an email via ART for a START web site.

Looking forward to another successful year!

 
Fall Semester:

Fall Semester will start in the beginning of October and will until the end of December.  Spring Semester will start in January and end in June. 
Classes are one hour long (except for the Saturday teen class). 
We send weekly emails with the plan/curriculum for the week. 

Registration (Please read. We have made some changes): 

1. Everyone can take up to two trial classes before making a full commitment. Trial classes are paid classes.

2. This year we will require a deposit for each semester and that deposit will cover the last month of each semester.  If you choose to leave the program before the end of the semester, you will lose your deposit. We are open to make exceptions to this rule in the case of an emergency or any unplanned circumstances.

3. In this up and coming Fall Semester we will limit the classes to no more than 10 kids.  This way we will give more personal attention to each student.

4. This year we are also offering a special class for students who show an exceptional talent in the arts. We invite students ages 5-8 to join this class, based on their performance in last year's class and this year. 

5. We run the Tuesdays and Wednesday classes from P.S. 69. The Friday toddler's class from one of the student's home. Private classes run on Saturdays at our Woodside Studio.

6. Make up classes: Tuesdays and Wednesdays classes are very similar in age and content.  You are welcome to bring your child to any of these classes if he or she missed her regular class.  At this moment we can't offer a make up class for the toddler's class.

7.  This year the P.S. 69 classes will start on the hour: 4:00PM-5:00PM and 5:00PM - 6:00PM. Thursday toddler's class will start at 3:15PM.

6.  Schedule is still subject to change, depending on enrollment.


Fall Schedule:
 
 1.     Now offering two classes on Tuesdays:
 4:00PM-5:00PM, Ages 7-8 Location: P.S. 69. Still open for registration.
 5:00PM-6:00PM, Ages 5-8 Gifted art students. Location: P.S. 69. Still open for  registration.

2.    Now offering one class on Wednesdays, 4:00PM-5:00PM, ages 5-6 Location: P.S. 69. Still open for registration.

3.  Toddlers Afternoon: Now offering one class on Fridays, 3:15PM-4:15PM, ages 3-5.  Still open for registration.

4.  Saturday teen class, (an exceptional, small class with fantastic art projects). Ages 8-10, 11:00AM-12:30PM. Location: Mondo Mundo Studios in Woodside.  Still open for registration. Length of class: 1.5 hours.   
 

Charge: We charge is $15 for all one-hour classes. The monthly fee is $60.
Materials included.

Subjects to explore throughout the year:

1. Techniques in drawings
2. Techniques in painting
3. Sumi drawing
4. Collage techniques
5. Sculpture and 3-d
 
PRIVATE CLASSES: Are available throughout the week but mainly on weekends.   Please contact us if interested in arranging a meeting to discuss the possibility of a private art class.

Portfolio preparation: Now offering a portfolio preparation class for teenagers and young adults who are applying for art schools and are seeking to build a strong presentation. Please contact us if interested in this class.

About ART for a START:


With school budgets being drastically cut, one of the first subjects to be eliminated is the art class. Children are magnificently creative.  They want to make art, explore, touch and experiment with materials. They need to make art as much as they need to learn how to read, write, do math exercises etc... To fill this critical void, we will be conducting art classes for children to explore the exciting and enjoyable process of art making.  Working out of a centrally located space (P.S. 69) in Jackson Heights, this convenient location allows parents to catch up with some local errands or shopping while your child is in class.

All classes will be conducted under the supervision of Deborah Wasserman.  Deborah has been teaching art to children and adults for over 15 years.  She has worked in the Art Education Departments of many major museums in NYC (the Guggenheim Museum, The New Museum of contemporary Art, The Bronx Museum, the Jewish Museum, Rotunda Gallery, The College of New Jersey) and has developed an â€˜inquiry basedâ€™ approach to teaching art. Deborah Wasserman's biography:

Born in Sao Paulo, Brazil and raised in Israel, Deborah Wasserman is currently living and working in Queens, NY.  She is a graduate of the California Institute of the Arts and the Whitney Museum Independent Study Program with a Masters and a Post Masters degree.  Deborah Wasserman has received grants from the Experimental Television Center, Skowhegan School of Painting and Sculpture, Aljira Center for the Arts and the America-Israel cultural foundation. Her work has been exhibited in the USA, Brazil, the Netherlands, Germany and Israel, in such venues as The Bronx Museum of the Arts, The Tel Aviv Museum of the Arts, The World Wide Video Festival, White Columns, Gale Gates Gallery, GenArt and A.I.R gallery.
