Hi, I work for the sponsor of the building and have a 3 bedroom available. I wouldn't be concerned about the bikes, I've never heard any complaints about a situation involving them. If I may, I wish to point out that many bikers in NYC are actually well to do folks and not gangsters (they may be badasses  :rockon: ).  Many of them are actually just regular folks and many are actually "well to do" people that simply want to take a few weeks per year or a couple of hours per week and feel the rumble and wind.  Riding a Motorcycle is a very American thing to do.  I used to ride bikes, slumming it several years ago and I was never a gangster - I actually wear a suit 3 or 4 times weekly (I did then as well). I used to ride with 4 other guys and all of them were better educated than me - 2 lawyers, a politico and a queens business owner (happens to be investor in the Magic Jack thingy)

@ bethanygrrl, I understand your frustrations with management companies - but to be fair, you mentioned that you had gotten permition to remove items on Sunday.   Only the management office or the Board would have the authority to allow moving furniture or large items.  In fact, not even the superintendent can authorize moving furniture during the weekends unless he receives an OK from others in a position to do so.  Obviously if the superintendent told you it was OK i would argue that the primary contact with the residents is the superintendent of the building and you simply assumed what any other person would have assumed  - "that the super cleared the activities on Sunday".  as per Saturday, if you simply made one trip in the elevator with carry on items I would definitely argue that point - now if in fact you made several trips with large items then i would side with management since most residents in buildings don't want to be inconvenienced during their day off.

Hola,

Our house rules are clear with the move-in and move-out policy.  It has to be during the week, you must stop moving at 5 p.m. and you are not allowed to move in or out during the weekends.  There is a fee collected up front and if you do not comply with the rules you will be fined.  With 200 plus units in the building and all the traffic, it works best when everyone simply complies with the rules.  Cameras are all over the building and if you are indeed interested in breaking those rules, you will be penalized for doing so.  

In addition, any and all deliveries have to be done during the moving-in and moving out period.  That would include the delivery of furniture, refrigerators, etc. and exclude food, and courier deliveries of course.

Please note that our building requires that all deliveries be made through the basement and not through the lobby.

Security is a huge problem during the moving in or out period.  Unless you have a guard standing at the doors at all times, or someone keeping an eye on the security cameras, anything can happen.  In addition, $500 for a move-in, move-out deposit is nominal.
