That's exactly what I mean.  The plaza in Manhattan looks GREAT.


It's worth recognizing that not all NYC plazas are the same. Several plazas in Manhattan, for instance, are managed and financed by the local Business Improvement District. That means that if your plaza's BID includes Macy's or the Flatiron Building and the surrounding real estate, your plazas are going to have a massive infusion of corporate capital aimed at beautifying that area to serve their business. It's not fair to compare Diversity Plaza to those. That does not mean that it cannot be an attractive place. It's just important to recognize that in a neighborhood like Jackson Heights, the capital improvements are going to be fairly standard fare -- how the plaza is programmed or maintained is up to you and me and any partners we choose to bring in.
