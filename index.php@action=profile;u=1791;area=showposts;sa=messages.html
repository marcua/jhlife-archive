Neighborhood Chat / Will Jackson Heights undergo the same transformation?
« on: January 28, 2011, 04:57:14 PM »
Hi all,

Just something I was thinking about recently:

I lived in Greenpoint and Williamsburg before and noticed that, over the years, changes happened (I think most of you have heard).  Organic food stores started popping up, innovative restaurants opened their doors and art galleries sprung up.  I am hesitant to call it true gentrification (although Williamsburg now may be undergoing that) because, at least in Greenpoint, the old character of the neighborhood was somewhat preserved.

The changes were a function of hip people looking for better rents and more space moving out of Manhattan and setting up in these areas.

I wonder if Jackson Heights would undergo the same sort of transformation?  For those of you who have lived here for a while, have you noticed any changes?  Do you think what happened in Greenpoint might happen in Jackson Heights one day?

I'm not saying it's a good or bad thing (although I would welcome an Organic Food restaurant and more restaurant varieties)--I'm just curious...

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Queens Library stops book buying due to budget squeeze
« on: January 28, 2011, 04:48:53 PM »
Just my two cents: I grew up near the Central Branch of the library in Jamaica and I have to say that it was one of the most important parts of my childhood.  I spent hours at the library, as did others I grew up with because it was an oasis of calm, escape, etc. in a sometimes-not-so-pleasant urban jungle.  For us kids, it was a place to get and learn to love books when we couldn't afford to go to a Barnes and Nobles.  I loved/love that library from the bottom of my heart.

The Queensborough Public library system has some budget issues right now but over the years, I've found them to offer a ton of literacy and enrichment programs.  I still go to the free concerts at the Central Branch.  Their online book search and reservation system is amazing!

I haven't been to the Jackson Heights branch but maybe their particular management isn't good...Also, I think libraries in general aren't doing so well in our digital age...
