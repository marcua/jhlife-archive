Neighborhood Chat / Re: Piggy backing off farmers market
« on: August 06, 2012, 10:38:58 AM »
I think that because these vendors are operating on the Playstreet, which is technically an extension of the park, they actually could just get a "restricted area" mobile food permit, which I understand does not have a waitlist, but which also doesn't allow them to operate outside of the park. I believe this is managed through the Parks Department, although the vendors would still have to complete the Food Protection Course and verify that their equipment is in compliance with the applicable rules of the Department of Health.

This situation is not dissimilar to that of the Red Hook "Soccer Tacos" vendors, albeit the scale is much smaller here in JH, with only a handful of these types of vendors at most (speaking strictly about those set up at the Sunday market). I believe the solution in Red Hook was the issuance of this type of "restricted area" permit, although the vendors had to upgrade their equipment, and those that could not afford to do so were weeded out, I suppose. Still, it offers a little bit of hope that our local food vendors can actually sustain their businesses legally if they go through the proper channels.

Personally, I do think the vendors add a unique quality to the Sunday market, and one that is worth protecting. I had been eyeing their food for weeks - I usually go first thing in the morning, so I hadn't tried the food yet because tostadas and elote aren't exactly breakfast in my mind - and thinking that it was really cool that there was food on offer at the market that reflected the cultural diversity of the neighborhood. Yesterday my husband and I went early in the afternoon, and enjoyed some flautas and chicken mole tamales while watching the soccer game (on asphalt!) in the Garden School yard. The vendors were very pleasant, the food was tasty, and I think the environment at the market is better for it. I hope that they do ultimately go through the permit process, and that they're able to continue to sell there for a long time.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Citi Bike share program - let's bring it to JH!
« on: June 30, 2012, 08:10:16 PM »
Hi Neighbors,

As I'm sure many of you know, the DOT is launching a new bike share program beginning in July, which will place bike rental stations in locations all over Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens. The idea is to encourage bicycling for short trips, especially to work, to relieve congestion on mass transit. For anyone who has ever considered biking to work, but hasn't wanted to make the commitment of investing in a bike for fear they won't like it, this is a great opportunity to try out cycling before making that big purchase.

Sadly, Jackson Heights is not on the map of proposed bike station locations. However, the DOT has a page where people can submit requests for additional locations, and I think Jackson Heights residents could greatly benefit from getting a bike station or two. Here is a link to the form:

http://a841-tfpweb.nyc.gov/bikeshare/contact/

Please pass this along to anyone you know in the neighborhood who might be interested in affordable cycling, and have them submit requests as well.

To learn more about the bikes, rates, and to see the current station map, go here: http://citibikenyc.com/

Thanks!
Erica
