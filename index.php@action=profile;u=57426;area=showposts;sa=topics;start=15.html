Neighborhood Chat / Commercial Sign Wars
« on: May 22, 2015, 08:45:02 PM »
Last night, in the Jackson Heights of Yesteryear section, toddg posted that the New York Public Library has put its collection of photographs of old New York on line coded to location.

His post is #5 here: http://www.jacksonheightslife.com/community/index.php?topic=5808.0

I spent some time today looking at the Jackson Heights photos. One was taken in 1938 of a building on 82nd Street. When I pulled up Google Street View of the same building, I was struck by how much the facades had changed. Not the physical facade, but the signage.

It's like there's been a war to see who can put up the most prominent, garish sign.

You can see this by looking at the posts I made right after toddg's.

Probably unrealistic, but it would be really nice if the stores on 82nd would roll back their war to the original design and start over.

P.S.  You can see the original 1938 photo by going to the site referenced in toddg's post, and there is a tool that will let one enlarge the photo. However, note that the NYPL site positions the photo at 82nd and Roosevelt, whereas it was in fact taken on 82nd close to 37th. Also, I had to compress the photo to post it here. The version on the NYPL site shows more detail.

If anyone knows the history of the Citadel Cinema, shown in the photo, I'd love details.

Restaurants & Food / Announcing a Little Jackson Heights Restaurant
« on: April 23, 2015, 09:13:53 PM »
Hi,

My name is Stew. I have been a resident of Jackson Heights for 14 years and I am opening a restaurant in the neighborhood.

We'll be offering contemporary American cooking. The current plan is for the restaurant to be open nightly for dinner, Thursday and Friday for lunch, and Saturday and Sunday for brunch. The interior will be modern, but not formal, and the restaurant will accommodate about 50 guests. Despite not wanting the atmosphere to be formal, service will be highly professional and unobtrusive.

The restaurant will be at 76-05 37th Avenue. I'll be taking possession of the space on Monday. While the address is currently a restaurant, I've been working with a designer, architect and contractor on renovations. These will will be extensive and take some months to complete. We hope to open by mid-September.

This is a gamble based on the belief that Jackson Heights will support a restaurant that is unlike anything that currently exists in the neighborhood, and on the belief that it is possible to create a restaurant here that will attract New Yorkers from other parts of the city.

We will be working very hard to prove that those beliefs are well-founded.

Thanks for reading this,

Stew
