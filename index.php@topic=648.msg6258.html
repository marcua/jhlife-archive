Apologies to all. Yes, the Louis Armstrong house. I loved the kitchen and all the wallpaper.

Sump pump - well, many years ago, shortly after Thanksgiving, my son and I were awoken by a geyser of water shooting up out of the kitchen sink! It was raining out and whatever the problem with the building's aging water lines and downspouts had caused a mini Old Faithful.

After hours of bailing, disinfecting, etc. We blocked the drain, put a heavy kettle with rocks in it over the drain and had the plumber come.

The son is a very cautious person and went out and purchased a sump pump in case it happened again. It hasn't, but there are very strange sounds from that sink still.

It was an awful experience and we still had to go to work in the morning. Ugh.

My Mom and (Aunt) Lucy, (Uncle) Satchmo's wife, had a weekly poker game that rotated from house to house. Although I was only around 7 and my sister around 10, I vividly remember the night my Dad was out of town and Mom took us to the game at their house. (Uncle) Satchmo played games with us and then he and 2 of his friends took us out for dinner to a restaurant on either Northern or Queens Boulevard for fried chicken and French fries. (I wish I could remember the name of the place.) I didn't realize the significance of this until many, many years later when I heard him retell the story at my wedding about how 3 black men took 2 little, red-haired, white girls out for fried chicken at a family-style restaurant and how they were at first unwilling to seat us until they realized who he was. Whenever that poker game was at our house, Aunt Lucy always brought my sister and me a board game to keep us busy so the cussing-related game could be played without interruption.

The Armstrongs will always have a very special place in my heart for the kindnesses and love they shared with us. My darling Aunt Lucy especially, for all those forbidden words we learned on Tuesday nights.
