News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Hart Playground in Woodside will be closing one of its entry gates in an effort to discourage the use of the area by adults unaccompanied by children.  This affects many of the area's day laborers that gather by the playground to sit for a bit and use its restrooms.  This action comes after complaints by neighboring residents to local leaders about the men gathering at the playground.

I only recently became aware that there's some kind of law prohibiting adults unaccompanied by children from hanging out at city playgrounds.    Also, this article makes mention of new signs stating park regulations in various languages.  I haven't been to Hart Playground in years, but am wondering if we have similar signs at Travers Park in Jackson Heights.  Many of my visits to Travers have been unaccompanied by kids, so does this mean I'm breaking the law?

City will close gate at Hart Playground

There is a Parks Department Rule that adults can not be in playground areas unless they are in the company of a child. This does not apply to the entirety of Hart Playground, only the areas where there is play equipment. The day laborers will still have access to the Park's bathrooms and areas outside of where the play equipment is. As I understand it they are closing the gate by the 65th St. subway entrance, so they can better control access to the park and prevent adults without children from walking in to theplayground area.
