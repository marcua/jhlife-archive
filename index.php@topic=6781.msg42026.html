Kids will paint the most offensive thing they can think of because they want to get a reaction. I grew up in a small town with not even one Jewish family and we still had a couple of situations where swastikas were spray painted.  Another common vandalism tactic is knocking over gravestones - something else that causes people to get emotional.  There are two types of young vandals:  ones that are self-centered and do it for egotistical reasons (tagging their name or Class year or sports team, etc) and the ones that want people to react and be horrified by their actions (swastikas, gravestones, defacing people's homes). 

I think it's terrible that it happened, especially on the Temple, but I just don't think it had any true meaning and I'd be utterly astonished if it was done by a white supremacist group.
