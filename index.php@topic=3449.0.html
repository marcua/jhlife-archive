A part of my life is devoted to conducting private wine tastings for small groups so I have been drinking and collecting wines for a number of years.  I'm not sure whether you are looking for whites or reds, but assuming it's red wine I would suggest the following: Any Salice Salentino ;Primitivo or Nero d'Avola by just about any producer.  The first two are from Southern Italy and the Nero d'Avola is a Sicilian grape.  Also, any nice Montepulciano goes well with pasta.  I am also fond of Corvo white and red wines.  They are from Sicily and a great bargain (The Parkside has them on its wine list for around $18 for the white and $20 for the red so the retail price is under $10).

I'm not sure which stores in the area carry these.  Grand Wine and Liquors in Astoria, 30-05 31st Street, has a good selection of wines, but you can't walk there.  There is also a rather large wine store around 61st Street and I believe Woodside Avenue, just south of Roosevelt.  In the neighborhood, the store on 84th(?) and 37th has a better selection than most locals, but it's not great.

chuckster, i am a great fan of rioja, a spanish red wine of tempranillo grapes and torres is my choice vineyard. i also love ravenswood's red zinfadel, but they tend to be about $12/bottle. i usually buy a case of torres' sangre de toro at colony wine & liquor (37th ave & 84th st) and get 20% off. i believe their price per bottle is $8 or $9, so if you buy a case of 12, you'd save about $1.60 a bottle.

on a side note, i did find a wonderful wine and liquor store in sunnyside called lowery liquor (off the lowery st stop, 7 train). they have the most thorough selection of wine and liquor. plus, their price of freixenet (cava) is $2 or $3 cheaper per bottle than at colony. they also give 10% off for a purchase of a case. i have found some liquor brands i've not seen around, such as patron's citronage (orange liqueur) and green jack (as in daniels).

My two favorites (and they are very cheap) are:

Montepulciano D'Abruzzo
Readily available at nearly all wine/liquor stores. I've paid as little as $5/bottle before. But certain vineyards can be near $20/bottle.

Lambrusco (bubbly red)
This is more difficult to find. I've found it at bottlerocket on 19th and 5th Avenue in the Flatiron District. They have two types; a red and a rose. You can also find it at Via Amelia, on 21st? between Park and Broadway. It's a restaurant, but they sell wine as well and they have a larger selection. Lambrusco is particular to the Amelia Romagna region of Italy (northern Italy, Parma region). This is usually priced in the mid-teens. There are cheaper ones (by Riunite, ick...), but I don't recommend them.
