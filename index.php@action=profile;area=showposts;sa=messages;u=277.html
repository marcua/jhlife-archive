Neighborhood Chat / Re: Will Jackson Heights undergo the same transformation?
« on: April 10, 2011, 07:16:40 AM »
Are all white people the same? I think those that move out here may be a little more complex than the ones you might encounter in Williamsburg or the Village. The ones that move out here are going to tend to be the ones that appreciate the diversity of the neighborhood.

Are Jewish people with white skin just "more white people invading the hood"? In NYC, Jews are numerous, but in the rest of the States, they are a very small minority. In that context, I would assume that an influx of Jews does indeed add diversity. What about Russians, Ukrainians, Poles, etc.? Are they being mistaken for "more white people invading the hood" when in fact they are immigrants with different cultures and languages and cuisines?  What about the many South Americans who happen to have white skin?  Many of the white people that I know in this neighborhood fall into one of these categories, and the rest are most certainly the type who appreciate all the diversity of the neighborhood.

If you look back through the history of the neighborhood, there have been many waves of immigrants to this neighborhood, and Indians and South Americans were not the first. The character of the neighborhood will forever be changing slightly.

Apart from the introduction of Starbucks and the proliferation of banks, I haven't seen a whole lot of changes for the worse hitting the neighborhood since I moved here 3 years ago. And there is a lot of great new stuff happening here.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: RCN Cable and Internet Service--Opinions??
« on: January 30, 2011, 07:19:35 PM »
In apartment buildings, time warner will give you basic cable for free. You don't need a box just plug the cable wire directly into your TV.
Instead of a DVR, I use on-demand services through the internet to my computer (hulu, netflix, etc.), plus I sometimes get TV shows on DVDs from netflix. So I don't bother with the pay channels.
You might also just try calling TimeWarner and saying you can't afford the plan you are currently on. They will probably find some new discounts available to you that they never told you about before.
Another possibility is some buildings have a negotiated package rate that you can ask for. Mine has a discount package that saves you about $10 off of the standard package, but you have to know to ask for it.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Overheated Apartment?
« on: November 14, 2010, 09:56:29 PM »
Two possibilities:

1. Get a thermostatic radiator valve. They are costly ($75-$100 per radiator) but they can solve your problem. I just installed one on my radiator and now I am enjoying comfortable temperatures in my apartment. The top manufacturer of these thermostatic valves is Danfoss: http://www.danfoss.com/North_America/EnVisioneering/EnVisioneering/ProductGroups/TRVradiatorvalves.htm

2. Does your radiator have a little canister on the side of it that makes a loud hissing noise? If so, you might have a one-pipe steam system with old air vent valves that need to be replaced. My building went through and replaced all of the old air vent valves and it stopped a lot of apartments from getting overheated. As steam fills up the radiator, the air vent valve is supposed to stay open to let the cold air out. Once steam reaches the air vent valve, the valve is supposed to shut, keeping the hot steam in. But as they age, they get stuck in the open position, allowing lots and lots of steam to come out into your room. The air vent valves are relatively cheap to replace (less than $15)
