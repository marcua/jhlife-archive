Quote from: Bel84 on June 04, 2017, 12:02:35 PM
I think some of you are missing the point. It's a garden not a playground. We have playgrounds in the areas. The backyards of our homes are a respite from everyday noise. Take your kids to a park if you want them to be free to be kids. That's what they are there for.

Well, this is the genuine debate over the use of the gardens that's been going on for decades, and it isn't going to stop any time soon. Sadly, Jackson Heights doesn't really have parks. We have Travers, one little patch for way too many people, and it has to serve all of the thousands and thousands of us who aren't lucky enough to have our own beautiful internal parks. The green space we have, predominantly, is the co-op gardens.

Families picnicked in the gardens decades ago, when families lived there. Then, over time, the buildings NORCed, and so the perception of the gardens became the perception of a NORC community, and thus much quieter. For the past ten years, the generations have been turning over again, and there's been some grinding of the gears between their expectations of what to do with the garden. But it's really just a generational difference, not some essential element of what the garden is or was designed for.

One thing that always helps is a strong community where people know and care for their neighbors. When my daughter liked to run through the halls of our building, I'd tell her that she had to keep it down for Mrs. N down the hall. But Mrs. N also understood that sometimes a child's enthusiasm would be her overriding quality. They still get along.
