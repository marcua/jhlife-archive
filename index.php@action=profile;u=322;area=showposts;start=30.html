As referred to in Aronan's post yesterday, Roosevelt Ave. in our neighborhood is divided between two police precincts and it seems they have never really worked out a comprehensive plan between the two to curb illegal activity.  Because of that, it's kind of a "no man's land" for all sorts of nefarious doings. 

About three weeks ago, I was walking to the subway at 90th Street and I saw this guy grab three buckets full of flowers from the florist and take off running down the street.  About 20 seconds later I saw a police officer and told her.  She looked at me and told me to "call it in."  She didn't give a #@%*.  I was thinking, wow, you can basically do whatever you want here and the cops don't care.  Reporting crimes would be more paperwork for them.

It's not just our neighborhood though.  In 2001 a drunk driver hit my parked car in Ridgewood on Cypress Avenue.  My car was parked in Brooklyn but got pushed into Queens.  I sat there for 2 hours while the cops argued with one another about which one had to file the paperwork and deal with the situation.  Sigh.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Thinking of Moving to Jackson Heights-- Questions
« on: February 12, 2010, 10:58:17 AM »
I don't know how old your children are but there seems to be a few "extra" child friendly buildings in the neighborhood.

Roosevelt Terrace has an awesome private playground.

My building, The Griswold, The Saybrook and I believe also one of the Berkeley Buildings have indoor children's playrooms which is great for little ones during the cold months.

Also, there is a listserve "Jackson Heights Families." I don't belong to it but many of my friends with children do.  

In our building alone, there was a baby boom last year and we have a dozen little ones.  Many, many young families moving to the neighborhood.

I hear wonderful things about the Renaissance Charter School as well as the Garden School.

Good luck with your search.
