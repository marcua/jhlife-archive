rhydewithdis & Contessa are right...what amenities does the building have?  One of the most expensive amenities in a building would be full time doormen.  While many coops carry an all inclusive maintenance fee that covers even the electricity it should be noted that most may have charges of $25 to $30 per month for each AC unit you have in your coop.  That ads up to be anywhere from $300 to almost $400 for the year. In addition, original Coops (vs conversions) tend to have minimal overhead since many donnot have underlying mortgages.  On the other hand, they are very restrictive and most won't even allow subletting.  This is fine for someone that wants a 100% owner occupied edifice.  I have dealt with coops that have relatively low maintenance charges but they also tend to have very low underlying mortgages and / or have regular assessments. 

One of the ways I calculate the true maintenance fee is to deduct the tax burden of your monthly payment.  If you are paying $800 per month and your tax deductible is 65% then compare that to a similar unit paying $720 per month and a 30% tax deductible.   I have been in apartments that are 1000 sq ft but they have so many halls and walls that a large percentage is not usable.  I have also been in 2 bedroom units that are just 800 sq feet but have a wide open layout with no halls and they look and feel bigger (oops, I'm off the subject a bit).  Anyway...I'll take a look at the per sq ft / maintenance later on...On my way to pay the employees...
