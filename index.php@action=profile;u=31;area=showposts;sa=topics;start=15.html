Neighborhood Chat / Lead Paint Poisoning
« on: August 27, 2009, 04:24:52 PM »



According the most recent Dept of Health study, more children test positive for
lead paint poisoning in the Jackson Heights area than any other area in Queens.
Lead-based paint is a major source of lead poisoning for children and can also
affect adults. In children, lead poisoning can cause irreversible brain damage and
can impair mental functioning. It can retard mental and physical development and
reduce attention span. It can also retard fetal development even at extremely low
levels of lead. In adults, it can cause irritability, poor muscle coordination, and nerve
damage to the sense organs and nerves controlling the body. Lead poisoning may
also cause problems with reproduction (such as a decreased sperm count). It may
also increase blood pressure. Thus, young children, fetuses, infants, and adults
with high blood pressure are the most vulnerable to the effects of lead.

Learn more at the Dept of Health website:

http://www.nyc.gov/html/doh/html/lead/lead.shtml

Also here is a link to a Lead Test Kit for Households.

Neighborhood Chat / Julissa Ferreras Wins City Council Seat
« on: February 25, 2009, 12:21:24 AM »

From Daily News:
http://www.nydailynews.com/blogs/dailypolitics/2009/02/ferreras-declares-early-victor.html

Ferreras Declares Early Victory, Peralta Sees 'New Three Amigos' by Liz Benjamin
February 24, 2009

Less than half an hour after the polls in the 21st CD closed, Julissa Ferreras' campaign blasted out a statement declaring victory.

    "Julissa says, United we did. Unidos pudimos!'" the statement read, referring to the Barack Obama-inspired name of the line on which Ferreras had decided to run in the nonpartisan special election.

According to Alex Navarro-McKay, a former operative for the Working Families Party (a big Ferreras backer) who now works for BerlinRosen, which handled press for the campaign, Ferreras led her nearest competitor, Francisco Moya, by 1,034 votes with 92 percent of precincts reporting.

Ferreras' (unofficial) victory is historic. She's the first Latina to represent the borough on the Council. But it also marks the rise of what her ally, Assemblyman Jose Peralta, called "the new Three Amigos."

The victory of Ferreras - Monserrate's former chief of staff - with an assist from Peralta, a former ally of ex-Sen. John Sabini, the man Monserrate forced out of last year's Senate Democratic primary, is sure to send a frisson up the spine of the Queens Democratic establishment.

    "This is an opportunity for the three of us to really send a message," Peralta said. "It's a big victory not only for Latinos, but for all of Northern Queens; it's very positive for out community, which has been underrepresented for many years."

    "The new Three Amigos are coming together and setting a new agenda for Northern Queens," the assemblyman continued. "...It's not a threat to anyone. Now we have more of a voice at the table, that's all we wanted."

Peralta did suggest, however, that "county" should sit up and take notice.

Officially speaking, the party organization remained neutral in this race. But, in Peralta's view, there was a tactic understanding that it was OK for people to back Moya, a former aide to Gov. David Paterson (in his Senate minority leader days).

    "There was a lot of passion in this race; there was a lot of mudslinging and fear that didn't have to take place," Peralta said.

During a brief phone interview from Ferreras' victory party, Monserrate, who has a long history of making trouble for the party and its chairman, rep. Joe Crowley, refused to discuss whether the organization should see this new threesome as a threat.

    "This is a very good thing for the borough of Queens, and we did it at the grassroots level," said Monserrate. "It's a beautiful day...Jose Peralta is my brother, and he is my ally, and he is part of what we did and what we put together to make history in the borough. The Latino leadership in this borough is unified."

When pressed on whether the Queens Democratic Party should consider him worrisome, Monserrate replied: "That's a question I guess you should ask them; we want to work with everybody."

Then Ferreras got on the phone and praised the Working Families Party for doing an "incredible job," and adding: "I have to work with those who stood with me; I'm honored to do that. I can't really tell you what else it means right now."

Neighborhood Chat / Jackson Heights Has a Dog Poop Problem
« on: February 21, 2009, 08:41:23 PM »

From the New York Post: http://www.nypost.com/seven/02212009/news/regionalnews/nys_crappiest_neighborhoods_156296.htm

 Step lightly in New York's crappiest neighborhood - the Upper West Side.

The tony area is ground zero for doggy doo, packed with inconsiderate people who don't pick up after their pups, according to a list compiled for The Post by the Department of Sanitation.

Manhattan saw an 88 percent spike in fines for unattended droppings last year, with Morningside Drive, Amsterdam Avenue, West End Avenue, Riverside Drive and Central Park West leading the complaints, Sanitation enforcement officers said.

"It's a minefield over here," said Max Moyet, 34, a dog walker who lives on Central Park West. "It upsets the hell out of me."

Moyet says he takes pains to "stare down" people who do not clean up after their pooches, a practice that's become a daily activity.

"It's their self-absorbed, all-about-me attitude," Moyet complained. "They're like, 'I'm too busy going to my Pilates class. I don't have the time to keep the neighborhood clean.' "

Suparna Chakrabortti, who lives in Queens but visits her family on West End Avenue almost every day, says the Upper West Side has gone to the dogs.

"It's on every block every day," she said. "In the past couple months, it's gotten much worse. I don't remember it being this bad."

While those Upper West Side streets have the highest concentration of complaints in Manhattan, the prize for the crappiest borough goes to The Bronx, which with 354 violations accounts for over a third of the summonses issued citywide, the Sanitation Department said. The two worst locations are on Mosholu Parkway and Hunts Point Avenue.

Brooklyn pet owners, who live in the second-worst borough for doggy doo, have been slapped with 224 fines in 2008, up 47 percent from the year before. Sunset Park, Park Slope and Brownsville are the three top Fido-fouled areas.

Watch your step in Jackson Heights - it topped the list twice in the top three worst dog-dung locations in Queens, which was nipped with 168 violations in 2008. Trendy Queens neighborhood Astoria also topped the list.

Staten Island residents were up to their neck in doggy doo - hit with a 120 percent increase in the number of fines issued, from 24 in 2007 to 53 last year.

The Department of Sanitation hopes to combat inconsiderate pet owners after raising the fine from $50 to $250 this past November.

Sanitation officers issued 909 pooper-scooper violations citywide from July 2007 to June 2008, up 16 percent from 783 for the same period the previous year.
