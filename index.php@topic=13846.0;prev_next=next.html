As a driver, I've actually had cars pull around me to go through the crosswalk as I've been waiting patiently for pedestrians to cross.  This happens frequently on Roosevelt as I am turning right onto my numbered street.  As I'm waiting to make the right turn, pedestrians are crossing, and invariably there's a car coming the opposite direction on Roosevelt wanting to make a left turn onto the same numbered street, and the driver gets so impatient that my car and the pedestrians are blocking where they want to go that they basically swerve in front of me to make the left turn, even while pedestrians are still coming (and the pedestrians have the walk light).

So, I guess I have made posts against drivers and against pedestrians in this thread.  Basically, everyone should learn to follow the rules, whether they are a pedestrian or a driver.  There's definitely bad behavior on both sides.
