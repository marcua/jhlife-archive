Bellabella,

There is Google advice on what to do with wild birds but, normally, they're better left to their parents and the parents will feed them even if they have fallen from the nest.  If it is really young, try to put the box you now have it in near to where you found the bird and in a place where cats/raccoons, etc, can't get it.  It's not easy to find such a place probably but see of you can.

I believe there are also raptor rescue groups in the area and I'll do some research.  Before you put it out, you might want to try to feed it some scraps of meat with  tweezers or chopsticks.  Young birds are ravenous eaters and they need to be fed often.

Good luck and thanks for taking care of  our local wildlife!

Madalyn

You probably got the bird after it's first flight.  They often make crash landings until they get used to the flight-thing.  I saw a young red-tailed hawk crash-land into a shrub once and it wasn't pretty!  Took him (or her) some minutes to extricate himself but he was fine.
