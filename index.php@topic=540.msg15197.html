The End of Analog TV: Act Soon!
« on: March 01, 2008, 09:29:40 AM »
On Feb. 17, 2009, under federal law, all television stations will cease broadcasting traditional analog signals.  That means that if you rely on an antenna and have a TV that is over a couple of years old, you will need to purchase a converter box or lose your television service.

Here's an article on the implications of the for New York.

Here's the federal website that explains what you need to do.

And here's an article explaining that the federal coupons expire after three months

I ordered two coupons on behalf of my parents (who flat-out refuse to get cable) towards the end of 2008. They arrived about three weeks later and we used them to purchase two Zenith converter boxes at P.C. Richard & Son on Queens Boulevard and 51st Street (final cost, $50). I have to say that they work well, giving my parents about 20 channels to chose from now in both the living room and bedroom with just a pair of rabbit ears as antennae. The screen aspect ratio can be adjusted from channel to channel or show to show, there's even a rudimentary on-screen program guide, and the remote control is easily programmed to work on both the converter box and television set.

One difficulty is recording a program to your VCR, which as best as I can figure (and I think I'm fairly tech-savvy) now requires that you leave the digital converter box on and set to the channel you want to record at all times. (However, my parent's VCR is old and maybe newer models have digital tuners much as newer TV set are supposed to.)

In any case, Congress pushed the date for the transition back until June 12th now.
