Neighborhood Chat / Pregnant woman with baby in carriage is followed to her home by stranger
« on: September 24, 2008, 10:34:50 PM »
Reposted from Yahoo JH Families Listserv

[Moderator Note:  Edited to remove personal information]

A Disturbing Neighborhood Experience
Wednesday, September 24, 2008 9:38 PM

Hi Folks,
 
A few days ago I had someone follow me for six blocks (including a two-block detour) and rather aggressively try to follow me into our building.  It was broad daylight, but I was pushing a stroller (and my two-year-old daughter had said rather loudly "I want to go HOME") so we might have looked like easy targets.
 
We had stopped for lunch at Pepino's on 37th and 76th.  It was full and I was looking for a place to sit.  I noticed a guy sitting alone at a table.  There was something about him that struck me as "off," so we waited for another table to open up.  He was probably in his mid-50's, Caucasian or Latino, 6' or so, and very burly with a shaved head--looked like he worked out a lot.
 
After a few minutes, my daughter said "I want to go HOME," so we set off.  I was pushing her in the stroller down 37th when she started to fall asleep, so I walked her up to 81st, then turned around and backtracked to our street.  I then walked up the steps to our building, and as I turned around to pull the stroller up, there was the creepy guy, grabbing the bottom of [edited to remove child's name] stroller to lift her.
 
When we got to the door, I just waited.  He reached for his pocket like he was going to get keys (which I knew he didn't have--our building is small and I know all our neighbors) then waited for me to open the door.  I just looked at him, so he buzzed an apartment and then he gestured for me to open the door.  I just waited, hoping he would leave.  Then he buzzed another apartment, and someone answered.  He first kept saying he was there to see "Eddie" and when the person inside insisted there was no Eddie there and asked his name, he then said HE was "Eddie."
 
At that point, I left.  I called the police who said they would come take a report, but of course never did.
 
The thing that disturbed me most was that he really seemed skilled at this--he had picked us out (pregnant woman pushing a stroller and going "HOME!"), and his "technique" of pretending he had keys and looking at me like I was being rude not to let him in was really effective--he definitely made me feel guilty and uncomfortable about being so rude.
 
So, be safe and keep your eyes open, everyone....
