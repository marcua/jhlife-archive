Need for a Safer 111th Street Along Flushing Meadows-Corona Park
« on: April 08, 2015, 11:52:06 AM »

The Dept of Transportation is planning safety improvements on 111th Street along the western edge of Flushing Meadows Corona Park. This is the wide street that includes the entrance to the Hall of Science, Queens Zoo, and the Playground for All Children.

As many JH families know - this is a very dangerous street which is extremely difficult to cross as a pedestrian due to speeding cars and lack of crosswalks.

City Councilwoman Julissa Ferreras has allocated money for the Dept of Transportation to fix this problem and provide safety solutions including crosswalks, more pedestrian islands, additional street parking, and bike lanes.

However, a few Community Board members do not support these improvements. I urge people to email the Community Board and tell them we need a safer 111th Street. There will be a public hearing on April 14th at 7pm at 51-11 108th Street​ ​Corona, NY 11368

EMAIL:
Christian Cassagnol, Community Board 4 District Manager: qn04@cb.nyc.gov

To learn more about this, read these articles:
http://www.streetsblog.org/2015/04/06/cb-4-members-pan-fix-for-coronas-111th-st-not-enough-traffic-lanes/
and this article from last year: http://www.streetsblog.org/2014/11/10/111th-street/

Here's an article where Moya explains his point of view.  I'm not impressed.

Personally, I find Moya's characterizations offensive and frustrating.  The city is not removing traffic lanes to make room for a bike lane.  It is removing traffic lanes because in their engineering judgement, they are not needed and result in an over-wide, dangerous street.  There has been an extraordinary amount of outreach on this issue, including, right now, at the Community Board, which is the appropriate channel for this.  This is a plan that arose from grassroots efforts in the community, gained support from the elected City Councilmember, and eventually from the City Department of Transportation, and is now coming to the Community Board for discussion... Now to have a state legislator whine and slander the proposal for lack of consultation and phantom "fatal flaws" has a terribly chilling effect on the whole effort to get the city to be more innovative and more open to public input.
