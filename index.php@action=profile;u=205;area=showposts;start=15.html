Neighborhood Chat / Re: Business Rehabilitation/Construction after the 37th Avenue Fire
« on: February 14, 2010, 06:44:53 PM »
Quote from: jhlifer on February 14, 2010, 06:36:14 PM
The rules cited above were promulgated for new storefronts and renovations done to existing commercial structures.  Because the site of the fire is in the the Jackson Heights Historic District, any new construction must be approved by the Landmarks Preservation Commission.  New construction can, however, be built within the existing zoning envelope for that site, but the exterior of any proposed structure must be approved by the LPC. 

The building that rose out of the ruins of the arson fire that took place on 37th Avenue between 82nd and 83rd Streets over 20 years ago was NOT subject to LPC approval because a foundation for the new building had been poured prior to Jackson Heights being landmarked in October 1993.  The only reason that this building blends in well was because of the efforts of the Jackson Heights Beautification Group. In fact this fire was a preciptating event for the creation of the JHBG.  It is up to us to make sure our concerns and expectations are heard by our elected officials and the LPC.  This will be a long process, and our community needs people who are committed to volunteering some of their time to get this done.  Please contact the JHBG www.jhbg.org to get involved.
With this devastating fire, and the emptying of half of a 37th Avenue blockfront one block away (to ready it for a sale - the site of the Cavalier), it is highly likely that our historic district will be subject to some "new development."  If we are truly concerned about what potential new construction means for Jackson Heights, over the next year we will all need to do more than just talk - we need to get involved and effect positive change.

Thanks , that really cleared up a lot . Although , sometimes I am fearful of the Landmarks Commission since so many rules are broken on a constant basis in JH especially when it comes to commercial and retail spaces . But, we all really have to come together to make sure this change is going to be a positive one indeed in the shadow of so much tragedy.
