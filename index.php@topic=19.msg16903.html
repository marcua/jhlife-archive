After living here with a car for 6 years, parking on the street the entire time (not without incident and hassle, admittedly), I got my first ticket for an "alternate side parking" (street sweeping) time violation about a month ago.  The fee was $45.    At that rate you're looking at $180/four weeks if you just leave your car on the street.  And if you manage to move it to the opposite side on schedule, it's $0/week.  Of course, you should factor in gas for finding a space, and it may take a few months to uncover the hidden science of space tracking.  But if I had been paying $300/month for that time I would be $21,600 poorer.  Or by the Co-op parking I'd be $10,000 poorer.  That certainly dwarfs the cost of the occasional mirror-replacements I've had to deal with (the driver's side mirror has been knocked off two or three times).  Just don't keep anything valuable in the car.  And if you can't get home to move it on schedule, think of what you're saving and have another beer.
