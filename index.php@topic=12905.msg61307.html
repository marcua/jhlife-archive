Between the Post Office and about 85th, it depends on a lot of things, and some of the factors seem pretty murky. Let me suggest that Novo was paying about $3,000 per month and that Arunee Thai, which took over from Novo, is paying $11,000 to $12,000. The landlord who owns the health food store wants a great deal more per square foot. The trust that owns the Prima Donna space (the same trust that owns the Brunson Building) wants $85 per square foot for about 1200 square feet, or $132,000 per year plus an annual increase of 3% per year, which exceeds current inflation and expected inflation. They want to do a five year lease.

Outside that area, it looks to me that rents are much more in line with what makes sense for the area. On the other hand, it's pretty obvious that going outside that strip presents the tenant with a problem attracting customers.
