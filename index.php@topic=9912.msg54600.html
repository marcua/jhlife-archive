JACKSON HEIGHTS — A sprawling new organic food store opened last week on 37th Avenue, an offshoot of a collection of family-run health stores that were inspired by the owner's Hindu guru and designed to cater to residents who move to the area from Manhattan.

Downtown Natural Market opened Friday afternoon in an 8,000-square-foot space between 84th and 85th streets.

The owners Mike Patel, 62, and his son Amit, 36, own the Jackson Heights Health Food store at 83-06 37th Ave., as well as two other Downtown Natural Markets in Downtown Brooklyn and Flatbush.

The family's path to operating a chain of health food stores started with a bit of divine inspiration.

I've been shopping at this store every couple of days since it opened.

Observations:

The people serving are trying very hard to help/please customers.

The store is very spacious and very clean.

It appears that what they want to do is carry, and sell, dry goods and fast-moving perishable goods.

Prices are high. Just about everything that they sell is cheaper elsewhere.

It's really unclear where they are going with things like meat, vegetables, fruit, cheese. Currently, they are selling organic chicken (with no packaging date) at $5.19/lb. Vegetables and fruit are of good quality, but there is nothing unusual being offered. Two days ago, I couldn't buy Thyme, which is pretty basic. It is just core produce at expensive prices. The cheese section is quite large, but there is not a singe cheese for sale that is not mass produced.

So far, I see it as a place to pick up stuff on short notice.

I don't see it as a serious store.

Living a block away, I've also noticed that it isn't very busy. I'm not surprised. It just isn't a very attractive place to shop, despite the spiffy space and eager staff.

After several visits, my basic reaction is that I'd rather buy at the Jackson Heights Greenmarket and that it's unclear to me who their target clientele is.

As it currently stands, I'm now using it to buy milk and Liberté yogurt and that's about it.
