I know Community Church's beautiful garden is available free, if you go through the main (not church) entrance and ask. Or, if you pay a fee to become a member and receive a key, you can enter through the gate on 82nd St.

Upkeep is the problem. Why wouldn't you want to keep these looking good?

The Griswold has a garden in the back that we restored last year with a lot of donations and a lot of hard work. We use the space for building sponsored barbeques and we encourage renters and shareholders to use the space.  We want people to enjoy the beautiful shrubs and trees.  What we don't want is for children to run amok and damage the garden. I welcome any parent who teaches their child that gardens are beautiful and that they connect you with nature, but also that they need to be respected and cared for.  It's a garden.  Not a playground.  We just ask that you respect it and those who work hard to keep it beautiful.

I think this thread should be revived.  So here it goes.  After looking at the Chateau and a few other buildings where the gardens were very nice, we ended up in the Greystones (West Side of the street) -in our case the shared garden is with other buildings -some co-op and some rental.  I was saddened to learn after I moved in that these were basically off limits.  You can bring a folding chair outside and, you can have a picnic, but there are no benches at all.  Also if a toddler wants to run around with a large rubber ball -I personally don't see the harm.  With the horrible lack of park space, the gardens should be a respite from the noise of the traffic -instead they are basically people free zones.  I don't believe that was the original intent.  I'm not suggesting daily lamb barbecues or parties, but a few permanent benches would enable neighbors to meet and talk, and build a community.
