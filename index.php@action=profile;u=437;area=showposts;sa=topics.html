Neighborhood Chat / Washington Plaza going coop?
« on: April 23, 2014, 10:05:52 AM »
I know there are a couple of other people who post here and live in Washington Plaza (73rd/35th) - we got a red herring preliminary prospectus plan left outside our doors last night. 

At first I panicked because I got pushed out of my beloved Financial District apartment a few years ago when it went condo (I couldn't afford the $1.3 million purchase price for 650 square feet!).  But the more I read, it seems like it's a non-eviction plan - meaning non-purchasing tenants won't be evicted.  It still scares me a bit because they can easily find petty reasons not to renew a lease if they really want us out.

Anyway, my main reason for writing here is to see if any other tenants looked at what they plan to charge. It seems really high to me considering there's no parking or private garden and not in the historic district.  They plan to charge $270K for my one-bedrooom, and that is the preferential price for existing tenants. Maintenance would be $780. Nearby coops in that price range either have a much lower maintenance or a garage (or a private garden). 

Any opionions on that price?  It's a good size apartment but has a lot of street noise (faces 73rd).  Is it possible to negotiate price like if I were just buying a regular coop instead of a conversion?
