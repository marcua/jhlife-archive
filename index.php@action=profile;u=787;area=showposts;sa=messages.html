Neighborhood Chat / Re: Laptop repair places in Jackson Heights or Elmhurst.
« on: January 06, 2009, 09:43:59 PM »
a laptop that is put to the use such as being a desktop replacement, travel, carried around from room to room, constantly plugged in, etc... has an average lifespan of 3-4 years. 

in my opinion, it is cheaper and more cost effective to replace the entire unit. i just picked up a HP pavilion dv4 model line for about $500 (black friday).  laptops go on sale all the time due to the constant model changes, they normally have a market lifespan of 2-3 weeks.  with that said i hardly go after the expensive laptops since its pointless. here is the reason:

-a larger hard drive is the main reason for the extra pennies. i solve this issue with a portable external drive (500gig, which is more that any laptop can offer)

-screen size. i have a 14 inch screen since it is the perfect size for me since i travel.  i also have a desktop.

as mentioned i dont really see a point in spending 250-350 on laptop repairs since a new model is not that much more considering all the new technology. 

here are some tips so your laptop can have a longer lifespan:
-if using as a desktop replacement, leave the screen opened. flipping the screen up and down will cause screen failure
-store files on an external harddrive. this keeps your unit running free and fast plus it is less likely to lose files
-buy and use a laptop fan ($40 for a really good one). this keeps your laptop from running too hot
-avoid smothering the vents

also you can donate your laptop for a tax break...

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Clothing Store on Northern Blvd. in JH
« on: October 10, 2008, 09:01:35 AM »
these are very similair to the "hot houses" in corona.  these are mainly goods that "fell off the truck" and conveniently ended up for resale.  this operation really hurts retailers in many ways.  we all had shopping experiences with clothing stores like the gap, banana republic, j crew, etc... and i am sure we noticed the stiffer return policy that they offer.  this is all due to large returns/exchanges without a purchase receipt. 

a location that sells name brand clothing at a cheaper price seems like a bargain that we can't overlook. but keep in mind that these locations will frequently change and move without any notice.  also, it may seem exciting and all but understand that these "business'" are frequently raided by the authorities and any "consumer"  and spectator are considered a "buyer of stolen goods".

some may think: "how can a small shop selling Gap clothing hurt an empire like Gap Inc.?"

the answer is simple, if you recall there was a Gap location in Flushing. that location closed due to the heavy exchanges and returns of "stolen goods" and high volume of stolen merchandise.  being that it is so close to corona and jh where majority of these "hot houses" are located it greatly affected the everyday business of that particular Gap location.

I am happy that Jackson Theater still have it's loyal movie goers.  I do admit (from my last J. Theather experience) that the film quality may not be up to par but it has tradition.
