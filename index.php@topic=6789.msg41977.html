Indian Community
« on: November 07, 2011, 10:05:46 AM »
I lived in Manhattan for 32 years before arriving in JH's in June 2001.  In Manhattan we had a very large Indian population who lived in the very best Buildings.  They're all highly educated and speak with a  British accent.  I came here and they were good quality but not on Manhattan level.  I came here to invest in Real Estate and know many Indian investors who became friends.  They own many homes in JH's and rent mostly to Indians.  They informed me that the Indians who are coming are poor, no education and completely illiterate.  They are coming for the Food Stamps and Health Care.  This investor is purchasing many more homes to accomodate all that are coming.  An entire family lives in the Livingroom and many others in the bedrooms and basements.  Indian investor follow Indian Law.  The poor have no health care in the villages so now they have Elmhurst General Hospital.  I have many South American friends who are leaving JH's for other Queens communities and are selling to Indians.  This has all just beginning and thousands will be arriving.  In Staten Island where family lives up until now they were Christian Indians.  No more.  They now have Hindu Temples but not the Southern Part of SI where the Mafia lives.  JH's will be radically changing according to these Indian Investors who are bringing them here and paying the air fare.

I'm moving back to Manhattan early next year.  I can't sell so I'm using my apartment to run a business.  It will be an office. 

I know many Realtors in JH's.  Business is way off.  Lots of listing but not a lot of buying.  Some are moving to Brooklyn where you have buyers.  The professionals in Astoria where I have a young nephew are moving to Manhattan where rents have dropped significantly.  My nephews is moving back to Lincoln Towers.  He sold his apartment in 2006 for $650,000 and is purchasing it back for $300,000.  The cooperative Market on the upper East Side is down 40% from the peak 2006 and going much lower.  I began real estate in Manhattan in 1980 so I know the Market. 

I'm passing on the info that they're giving me.  I don't own anything other than this one bedroom and I'm now in a different business. 

I'm hoping its not as bad as they predict.  The Indians are not criminal in any way.  Many will be employees in restaurants and other businesses all over the City but they like Queens.  They will work for $5 and hour.  The minimum wage is $10.50 but they don't follow our Law. 

I like JH's so I will be here 5 days a week.  I've met an incredible amount of wonderful people so I can't leave entirely. 
I like JH's people better than Manhattan residents.  This has been an incredible 10 years.

This is America, land of immigrants. When my ancestors arrived from Italy, they were not formally educated, could not speak the language (but, learned), had no health care, etc. Very similar to what you've described. I'm certain this is the same story for many, many immigrants. I can recall hearing similar complaints being made about the increasing Colombian population back in the 70s and 80s. I've lived in Jackson Heights on and off for over 3 decades. I remember when the Indians first began arriving, in the 1970s. When the Jackson Diner was, in fact, an American diner called -- The Jackson Diner, which, when it was going out of business, was purchased by a wonderful man, a small business, chef-entrepeneur from India. In its present location, it's now the most popular restaurant in J.H., by reputation. I welcome the Indian people; I always have. I find them to be a gracious, soft-spoken and peaceable people. I love the vibrancy of their culture, their exotic way of dress, their delicious food. In my opinion, the more of them the merrier. I also welcome the newly arrived Serbs and Croatians, Russians, French, Asians, Africans -- and the newest arrivals: Yuppies from Brooklyn -- many of which live in my building. This is the reason I've always loved Jackson Heights and why I've always moved back here after I'd lived elsewhere. It's so diversified. Some people have complained that Jackson Heights should be like Manhattan. Are you willing to pay twice as much rent? Everything comes with a price. If you can afford Manhattan, well, more power to you.

i posted this some 3 years ago, still has its merits:
I'm 2nd generation IrishAmerican and am quite aware that not all of my family came here legally.  Like many others they came here in search of a better life, they settled in even though many thought they should be sent back.  They supported schools and churches.  Sent sons and daughters into the school system first as students later as teachers; sent others into the police and fire departments; joined unions to better their lives and saw to it that those that came after them had better employment opportunities and faced less discrimination.  I hope these recent arrivals do not lose the hope that led them here, turn out to be as lucky as my forebears were; i hope they ignore the xenophobic cat calls hurled their way and persist in going after the American dream."
