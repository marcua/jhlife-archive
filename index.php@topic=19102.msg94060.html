Ocasio-Cortez 'Stalker' Arrested Again For Visiting Her Office

JACKSON HEIGHTS, QUEENS — A Queens man was arrested Wednesday for visiting Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez's office in Jackson Heights, violating a restraining order.

South Jamaica resident Douala Hashi, 31, was charged with stalking and criminal contempt for flouting an order of protection by visiting Ocasio-Cortez's district office on Tuesday, according to news reports.

Hashi rang the bell and said, "I want a meeting with the congresswoman," and was asked to leave, according to ABC7.

Hashi first showed up at the congresswoman's office on June 2, according to ABC7.

He was arrested nearly two weeks later for trespassing in the building where Ocasio-Cortez has her office.

Judge Jerry Iannece on Thursday warned Hashi to stay away from Ocasio-Cortez and two aides, according to the New York Daily News. The judge also ordered a psychiatric evaluation.
