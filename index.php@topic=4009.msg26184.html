Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

We must not confuse Jackson Heights with Park Slope, Brooklyn Heights, Forest Hills or Astoria.  What makes Jackson Heights different is that we have a Latin community which makes up the majority of the population here and the restaurants in the area reflect this.  The steakhouses here are Argentinean not French and a $25.00 steak that's enough for two and let's face it most people dine out in restaurants where they feel comfortable and where they serve food that they enjoy eating.  Maybe I'm wrong but I cannot see many Latinos flocking to a french restaurant to pay $9.50 for a grilled ham and cheese with a tiny salad, $10.00 for a omelet or $9.50 for a slice of quiche with a salad.  Yes we do have a small population that would enjoy this type of food but will they frequent it enough to pay the crazy rent!  Who knows we may have people from other communites making the trip to JH to dine at Le Gamin.  I'd love to see a nice cafe/coffee shop but not just french but more a combination of many cuisines....Greek/Mexican/middle eastern/American.  That's my opinion but regardless I do wish them success.
