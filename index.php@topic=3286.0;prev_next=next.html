Another vote here for Bide-A-Wee's spay/neuter clinic.  Their charges are generally very reasonable & they took good care of my poodle.  She was back home with me that night, groggy & hiding in her favorite den spot for a day, & then racing around & trying to jump up onto the bed the day after that (I had slept with her on a futon on the floor the first night, but she didn't think much of that arrangement).  I did let her go through one heat cycle (some think that this reduces the risk of incontinence later on in life, & the cancer risk is almost as low as spaying before the first heat), so she was several months older than your Shih Tzu, but I can't think why that would make any difference in price.

I took my pupster to the Humane Society right over the 59th Street bridge (took a livery for $20) and they only charged me $50.  They were fabulous ... very caring and so reasonable.  There is also a pet mobile that is very reasonable for transport or, if your pupster is small take him by public transport.

Also you can call the ASPCA that has a  Mobile unit that parks itself, when it comes to Jackson Heights, on the corner of 89th Street and 34th Avenue.  They spay for free or for whatever you can afford to pay.  Find out their schedule and make an appointment.

Vets should spay for less than cost because it is in the public interest to neuter our animals. 

Good luck.
