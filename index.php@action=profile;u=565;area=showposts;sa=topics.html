Neighborhood Chat / A widow and her dog......help write a happy ending.
« on: February 10, 2011, 03:26:36 AM »
A 79 year old neighbor lost her husband right before this past Christmas. For many years they maintained separate residences--he in the bronx and she in JH.  5 years ago they purchased a co-op in  a no dog building (cats are allowed) they accepted  and understood the rules.  the dog lived in the bronx, when the husband and dog came to JH several times a week the dog remained in the car until the woman came downstairs.  Over the 5 years the dog never entered the apartment.

When her husband died she took charge of the dog and brought him to her JH co-op.  The dog is a miniature, 10 pounds, 13 years old and very sick.  The dog uses a wee wee pad box and doesn't leave the apartment.

So this fragile and grief stricken woman has companionship with the dog and in her thinking, responsibility of taking care of him till he dies.  Sweet Hallmark story.  Except...

The co-op wants the dog out.  She made a plea for an accommodation.  Plea denied.  Yesterday, she received a letter from a lawyer informing her that unless she removes the dog by March 8 they will begin proceedings to oust her from her apartments. To add salt to the wound the letter informed her that she would be charged for all expenses.  The woman is devastated and frightened.

Does anyone know of a win/win strategy that would let the co-op make a one time  good will exception without destroying their ability to enforce this and other rules?  A win/win strategy that would enable this grieving widow to keep her dog without losing her house?

Given her condition could the dog be considered a "service animal" and would that exempt her from the rule?  Anyone know of a legal precedent in this area? Is the designation "service animal" only reserved for physical disabilities? Can it be extended for emotional, psychological conditions as well?

I can only imagine her pain.  The thought of being separated from my dog scares me.  I want to help her but am at wits end.  Any ideas?

As dog owners and dog lovers is there a way for us to push for legislation that would prevent this from happening?  Efforts to allow "replacement" animals proved successful, why not this?

Any ideas, thoughts or a plan of action would be appreciated.

Thanks.
