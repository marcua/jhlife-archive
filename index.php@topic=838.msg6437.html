KC...Don't pass a great deal because of a Mgmt firm.  Most problems with Bldgs is not the Mgmt but that the Bd failed to check up on references just as Buddy stated before.  The 2nd problem is employees.  It is almost impossible to rid a bldg of union workers unless they truly resolve to do so.  Keeping all records of any mishaps with them and even when you have everything you would ever need...it is still cheaper to $ettle and save lawyer fees.  32Bj will on occasion turn their back on a really problematic employee but that's rare.  Sometimes the Bldg are just in really bad shape and only the Bd have the authority to release large funds.  Remember, Mgmt firms are there to do what the Bd wants...but, sometimes they are just wacky !...lol

Remember, Mgmt companies can be changed fairly easily.  Boards are sometimes to blame whenever there is no unity within.  Sometimes the powers that be disagree in everything and at the end of the day nothing gets done and the bldg and occupants suffer.  Serving in Boards is a thankless job and it can be overwhelming if the members do not see eye to eye.  More often than not occupants can make it really difficult, especially those that never had to go through Bd approval or have been there for a zillion years and may feel owed something.  The Bd's are an absolute necessity and I respect those that stick to the position since I have seen many that will not serve the full term.

So, if you find the unit you love, get it and then work with your Bd members if you feel dissatisfied with the Mgmt firm in the property.  As buddy said before, they rarely last more than a year
