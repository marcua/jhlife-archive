News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

It's funny AmazingJason, when I first moved to Astoria in the mid 90s, it was quite different than it was today. There is a degree of change in the air in Jackson Heights, for better or for worse.  My thoughts are, rather than it becoming a young, hip, neighborhood,  I get the feeling it's going to head in the direction of Park slope stroller set rather than Williamsburgh hipster.  The reason largely being some of the factors you mentioned above.  I think 37th Ave can maintain some degree of uniqueness while maintaining an active nightlife.  It doesn't have to be loud 20 somethings on a bar crawl, as many may be concerned about.
