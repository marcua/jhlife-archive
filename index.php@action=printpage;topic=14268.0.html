We have just released the “Elliman Report: Manhattan, Brooklyn & Queens Rentals” for December 2015, the leading resource on the state of the rental market.  As always, our market reports are produced in conjunction with Miller Samuel to provide you and your clients with the most comprehensive and neutral market insight available.
 
The growth in Manhattan rents continued although the rate has slowed since the end of the summer. Despite a strong local economy, landlords provided more concessions as affordability remained an ongoing challenge for renters.
 
Brooklyn rents remained strong for smaller apartments and weak for larger ones as overall rents did not rise for the second consecutive month. Like Manhattan, the amount of concessions provided by landlords increased.
 
Overall prices for the northwest Queens rental market slipped. Like most of the region, rents for smaller apartments outperformed larger apartments. Marketing time slowed as negotiability expanded.
