It's been about a year since Henry Boimel was killed on 37th ave by an Uber Driver.
So, it's been about a year since our elected officials held a Pedestrian Safety Town Hall, which was well attended.

I just did a bit of data gathering on NYC Open Data. There have been 948 crashes since January 2017.
The data link here is interactive. You can click on a dot to see injuries and also what the NYPD determines are contributing factors. https://data.cityofnewyork.us/d/qasi-3vq8
I've noticed that the mentioned fatality and another on 86th/34th are not listed as such here.
My group is Make Queens Safer on twitter and Facebook. We also have another great safe streets neighbor, @streetfilms if you want to follow.

Has anyone EVER seen NYPD enforcement on 37th? Roosevelt? 35? Northern? 34th? (except bikes?)
We need to let precinct and elected know that we will not tolerate another death or injury due to the wanton entitlement drivers in the neighborhood feel empowered by.

This is a terribly dangerous time of year, and traffic crashes spike with daylight savings and the holidays. Road rage and impatience will put us all at risk. Be safe. Drivers: Knock it off.

To answer your first post, I have seen enforcement on both Roosevelt Ave - 75th Street and 37th Ave intersections.  This usually involves giving tickets for failure to yield and registration/inspection violations.
To answer your second post, if you raise an issue about car safety, you cannot ignore pedestrian, bicycle and illegal delivery vehicle issues.  These are all interrelated.
Traffic is ruled by laws, but it is the observance of the laws that determine whether they are effective.  Too many drivers, walkers and bicyclists act as if the laws do not apply to them.  Each person that flouts the law renders the social contract underlying the law null and void.
When the don't walk is flashing, don't cross the street.  Allow cars to turn.
When there is a pedestrian in your lane, wait for the pedestrian to complete their passage before continuing.
If you are riding one of these electric bikes or scooters, get rid of it, get a legal scooter or motorcycle or bicycle, and stop for red lights and yield to pedestrians.
The DOT recently installed a delay between car green lights on 37th Avenue and other areas. This makes it much safer for people to cross.  However, rather than adding the 7 seconds to the red light green light process, they deducted the 7 seconds from the amount of time drivers have to pass through the light legally.  This makes it more likely that frustrated drivers will go through an amber light (or even a red light) and puts different pedestrians at risk.
Drivers, get a bluetooth already.  What's wrong with you? Put your phone away.
Pedestrians, stop texting long enough to look where you're going, especially when in an intersection.
Finally, stop pushing your infant's stroller off the curb to wait for the light to change or for traffic to clear.  Is the extra second you gain by this stupid practice really worth risking your child's life?
