Sorry for the redundancy here, I know that this topic has been brought up many times before.  Whenever I mention to a friend that I live in JH their first reaction is "Awesome! Food! Tell me where to go!"  I've been here for a little over 2 years and have tried a few places (Dosa Delight, Spicy Shallot, Melhfi, Chili Chicken / Himalayan Hut, SriPraiThai [sp?], Jax Inn, Cannelle, Filipino and Issan Thai restaurants on Roosevelt, the taco carts, the South Asian sweet shops, and a handful of others that I can't think of right now).  Unfortunately I don't get to eat out as much as I'd like.  I'd love to explore some new restaurants - so where would you suggest taking a friend for their first foray into the food of Jackson Heights? I'd prefer somewhere a sit down place that's relatively cheap (dinner for 2 w/o drinks under $30) and within a 10 minute walk of the 74th St station.  My go-to place to take people is Dosa Delight, and I love it, but I want to try something new and don't want to take a friend to a so-so place.  So what would you suggest?  I'm open to any kind of cuisine. Thanks in advance!

(And as a side note, if anyone has recs for a bar in the area that's not Ready Penny or Texas BBQ please share! I know that E77 serves alcohol but it's tiny and often too crowded.)

Agree with Ayada Thai.  I have taken people to Phayul for Tibetan on 37th Road/74th (2nd floor) and they loved it.  Pio Pio on Northern for Peruvian Chicken in a large, noisy restaurant atmosphere.  Chivito d'Oro or La Portena for steaks, etc. on 37th Avenue.  Taco Coatzingo for a more relaxed Mexican cantina.  All of these are good choices to take visitors.  Indian buffets at lunch can be fun intro to neighborhood, but have been hit or miss--so hard to recommend which is good.

My friends (20s bk and manhattanites) loveeeeee Mustang Thakali. It's the perfect place - spacious, clean, close to the train and with cool decor. The Thali plates and chicken lollis are yum. La portena has great atmosphere and is good if you like steak. I've had mixed experiences there though- I went once with my boyfriend and the food was subpar. Then I went with a group of friends with three argentinians in the mix and voila - five star service and food.

Bar wise, all of my hot Spanish speaking hipster friends go to Terraza 7. I don't speak the language so I'm usually along for the ride but it's fun! There is a music and act schedule. It's the only non dive bar in the area. The vibe is something like a bar in Condesa DF in Mexico city.
