Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Queens Library stops book buying due to budget squeeze
« on: January 25, 2011, 04:28:46 PM »
"The Queens Library, one of the city's public libraries experiencing the budget squeeze, is taking an unprecedented step in its 104-year history and has stopped buying books."

http://www.wnyc.org/articles/wnyc-news/2011/jan/20/new-books-we-dont-got-em-queens-library-halts-book-purchases/

Of course this is a shame... at the same time, when I visit the Jackson Heights library branch I get the sense that this is not an organization that's innovative and deeply engaged with the community. When I take my kid to story hour I get the sense that the librarian is just doing the bare minimum to clock in her hours and collect her check. It's not the kind of service that inspires you to break out your checkbook.

What do other members of this board think about our library branch? About the Queens Library system in general?

Seems to be some varied opinions on the local library.  A lot of this comes down to personal preference and how you use the library.  I love the proximity of the branch (obviously that depends on where you live) and I really like the on-line services.  I've gotten probably 20 books from other branches.  Put the request in on-line, get an email a few days later saying the item is in and then pick it up at my convenience.  You can keep track of what you have checked out and can renew on-line as well.  It's great.

I'm a bit surprised to hear someone had difficulty getting a library card.  When I got mine it took about two minutes.  Really.   I'd also say that I've always found the staff to be helpful.

The only negative thing I would say is I don't find the environment all that inviting.  It's not really a place I'd care to hang out.  I prefer to be in and out.  But that is just me.

On balance I'd say the library does a great job.  It is disheartening to know that their finances are so abysmal that they have suspended buying books.  Public libraries are an incredibly important part of our community and need to be supported.
