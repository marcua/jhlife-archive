Think of tipping as insurance for good service next year.

$75 for the super seems reasonable but if you call on him regularly then maybe more. The super is essential to tip. When you have a flood in your apartment, or no heat, or some other issue and you have to call him at 1 a.m., you want him to remember that you were the very nice person who generously tipped. He will likely respond in a much more timely manner. Cynical, yes, but true.

Maybe $50 if you have a doorman.

If there's a big staff the other rule of thumb would be to only give to those you really know and have interacted with or those who have assisted you in some particular way.

Most supers live rent free and get a salary. You still ought to tip them before anyobne as they're your go-to when problems arise.

I can't tell you how many times I've heard folks say: `I called my super five times about this issue and I can't get him to come.' Next question I ask: `Did you tip him at Christmas and do you ever tip him when he stops by to do something?' The answer is usually no.

I know that sounds harsh but the reality is the super is a service employee and no one gets rich doing the job. It's often dirty and thankless and not that all well paying. You're expected to be on call 24/7 and deal with all manner of people.

Giving the guy $75 or whatever is reasonable.
