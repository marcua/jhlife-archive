Neighborhood Chat / Re: What is considered unreasonable noise (Coops)
« on: April 19, 2018, 04:18:31 PM »
Quote from: am315 on April 19, 2018, 12:00:01 PM
Carrefour makes an essential point and that's worth focusing on: Write a letter asking for specifics about what noise and what times. The board needs to be able to say it's your TV or your walking or whatever. Ask when there have been complaints.

Keep your communication positive and reasonable. You might also consider attaching pictures that show you have rugs throughout your apartment, etc. or anything you have in place that abates noise.

These things are always ugly but do ask what you can do to abate the issue and open a discussion with the managing agent first, if there is one.

IMHO, a building shouldn't impose a fine without an earlier warning and I would make that case to the managing agent and the board.

Btw, I had a similar situation with my 80-year-old father in a co-op he lived in. They claimed he was making overnight noise even though he went to bed at 8:30pm each night. The thing they were unable to detail: What noise specifically? Pretty idiotic.

Thank you for your advice, my husband had had numerous conversations with the building manager, tenant and super about this issue. We received 3 notices about "things being dropped on the floors at all times of the day including very early mornings (5:30am)" - It could be my husband opening the bathroom door and closing it to use it since our cat lives in the bathroom during evening and early morning hours. Management knows we have padded carpet since we had to provide insurance letters and have the super available when we installed wall to wall carpeting in the apartment. The super will be requesting someone from management to come in and take pictures of the carpeting.

We have discussed this issue with a lawyer when we received a 3rd and final notice of things being dropped during random hours and we may retain him if needed.

I wrote lengthy letter to the building manager asking if we can discuss what constitutes as "disturbing or unreasonable noises", if they can show us the noise log and what remedies they can suggest to us.
