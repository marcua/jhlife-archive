Currently reading "The Mapmakers", by John Noble Wilford, a history of map making.  Pretty good so far.

Last book I really enjoyed was "The Black Swan" by Nassim Nicholas Taleb.  It gets thrown in the "business books" category, but touches on a lot of other topics - logic, history, etc.  Black swan is used as a metaphor for unusual, unpredictable events - they are hard to predict, have an extreme impact, and we concoct explanations after the fact to explain their significance, in an attempt to make these events explainable and predictable.  His previous book "Fooled By Randomness" is good also. 

Don't read as much fiction as I should.  Interesting that you mention "The Glass Palace".  I thought that title sounded familiar, and I looked on Amazon and remember the cover picture - my wife has that book floating around the house somewhere.  I'll have to read that one!

I enjoy reading the works of Hispanic authors such as Sandra Cisneros, Isabel Allende, Oscar Hijuelos and the like.  My brother gave me a copy of Death in Andes by Mario Vargas Llosa, so I'll be starting that one next.

I recently finished Cabeza de Vaca's Adventures in the Unknown Interior of America, a translation of the original writing by Spanish explorer Alvar Nunez Cabeza de Vaca, entitled La Relacion.  It's written in diary/log format, and is the account of Cabeza de Vaca's explorations of the New World.  This is a truly fascinating read and the narrative gets even more interesting due to the fact that Cabeza de Vaca's exploration party loses contact with their ships and nonetheless set off on foot to explore the interiors of North America.  They start off as a large group in the hundreds and dwindle to practically nothing as a result of their adventures.

By the way, I have an extra and brand new copy of Traveling with Che Guevarra, The Making of a Revolutionary by Alberto Granado.  If anyone would like it, please PM me.
