I think there's definitely a demographic that has to do with the farmers market that can be served more along 37th Ave. Not sure what that would be.


It wouldn't necessarily be bad if Triskell was here at all, it's locally run and I'd love for it to take the place of our Starbucks or dunkin donuts. However, and I'm still developing theories on this so don't get all up in arms it's just a local forum, I think that for every non-enclave business that opens in the area of the enclave, it erodes the culture of that area. Maybe it starts a new enclave, which would be cool, but for the moment, it would erode the place. You need the population of regulars to create this sorta-supermarket of a neighborhood. When you reach such high levels, it creates cliques and niches which I think is what makes this area so awesome. I'm not looking for just a singular restaurant to satisfy a temporary desire, I'm looking for what will add to the landscape.


So, would I welcome M Wells here? That's one of my favorite places, I want them to succeed, and I have a personal connection to them, but I'm going to press myself to answer this....


I guess if they opened up outside of an ethnic enclave area, then I'd be into that. It'd inspire people to leave the neighborhood less and keep the money in our neighborhood and our borough (the owners live in LIC at least).


SO, just don't impede the growth of ThaiTown, Himalayan Heights, Little Manila, Cozy Quito, La Roosie, Peru part deux, or Calle Colombia, and I'm cool with that. I think that's my issue.
