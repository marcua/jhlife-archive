Neighborhood Chat / Vigil to Mark 20th Anniversary of Julio Rivera's Murder in Jackson Heights
« on: June 30, 2010, 08:11:20 AM »
Dear Friends & Neighbors,

Please join the family of Julio Rivera, Leonor Garzon (the mother of Edgar Garzon) and me on Friday night, July 2nd to mark the 20th Anniversary of the anti-gay murder of Julio Rivera.  The vigil will take place from 8 PM - 9 PM on the corner of 37 Avenue and 78 Street in Jackson Heights in front of PS 69 where Julio died.   

Julio Rivera was murdered by three self-described white supremacist skinheads out "hunting for a homo to kill" on July 2, 1990.  We will gather to honor Julio's life and to also call attention to the fact that these terrible hate crimes still occur not only to LGBT people but to many others including immigrants and Latinos (among others) as witnessed by a string of recent attacks in Brooklyn and Staten Island.

I hope you can join us as we stand to say Julio did not die in vain and that, in fact, his murder led to the creation of the current LGBT rights movement in Queens helping thousands upon thousands of people.

Please try to come.  Thanks for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
Daniel Dromm
NYC Council Member
www.DanielDromm.com

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Con Ed Cable Fire on 80th Street
« on: February 11, 2009, 09:40:10 PM »

Daniel Dromm's statement on the Con Ed fire on 80th Street in Jackson Heights on the morning of February 11, 2009.

"It has been reported to me that an underground Con Ed feeder cable caught fire last night causing an explosion
to occur from beneath the sidewalk in front of 34-47 80th Street in Jackson Heights, Queens. One resident's car
was completely destroyed. In addition, many neighbors were awoken by the explosion and by the noise from the
fire trucks that responded. The FDNY contacted Con Edison and the fire was brought under control a short while
later. Con Ed worked on the problem for most of the day.

Upon hearing of the fire, I went to the site and took the above photographs.
I was shocked by what I saw. Con Ed left the transformer covers ajar with wires clearly visible.
They told residents that they would return on Tuesday to fix the wires.

This is clearly unacceptable.
I contacted Council Member Eric Gioia's office.
He will be holding a press conference to immediately demand action from Con Ed at 10 AM on Thursday morning on the corner of 80th Street and 35th Avenue.

Once again, Con Edison has let the people down.
This is at least the fourth time a feeder cable has caught on fire on 80th Street in the last few years.

What does it take for Con Edison to pay attention?
Does someone have to die?

While I cannot be at the press conference tomorrow because I am a NYC public school teacher,
my sentiments go out to the residents who have to deal with the fear that Con Ed will do absolutely nothing to improve the situation.

I agree with the residents that Con Ed must inspect all the surrounding cables and replace the old wires immediately.
Additionally, Con Ed must fully pay to replace the resident's car which was lost in the fire.

Together we must demand that Con Edison protect the residents of our neighborhood from this ever happening again."

To learn more, please email me at danieldromm@yahoo.com or visit my website: www.danieldromm.com

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Parking Innovations: Muni Meters, Permits, etc.
« on: January 27, 2009, 05:22:28 PM »
Daniel Dromm is running for New York City Council to improve the quality of life in our community. Residential parking permits are a major part of Dromm's platform to improve transporation in our area.

Take a look at the issues and solutions on the campaign's website:
http://danieldromm.com/site/issues/transportation.html



Transportation
Creating Parking Permits for Residents & the Installation of Muni-meters for Parking in Commercial District

A major contributor to traffic congestion is simply motorists driving around looking for parking. By establishing a residential parking permit, the city can reduce wasteful driving by guaranteeing drivers a spot in their local area. As your city council representative, Daniel Dromm will ensure that the Department of Transportation installs muni-meters in the commercial districts of our neighborhood. Muni-meters are much more effective devices for improving parking turn-over and preventing meter abuse.

Improving Pedestrian Safety

Our city's streets and intersections expose pedestrians to unacceptable risks. In particular, many senior citizens are often the victims of crashes with motor vehicles in our area. As your city council representative, Daniel Dromm will introduce legislation to give pedestrians priority over motorists at all intersections. He will direct the Department of Transportation to get people across the street in a safer fashion by retiming traffic signals to allow for more time to cross the street, installing pedestrian ramps flush with the street to make ascending and descending the curb possible for all users, extending curbs at intersections to make pedestrians more visible, and placing bollards at these curb extensions to prevent trucks and buses from driving into pedestrians while turning.

Reducing Traffic Congestion

Our area experiences severe traffic congestion because of intense commercial and retail activity that caters to motorists within a densely populated residential area. Residents have regularly identified traffic congestion is the most frustrating problem in our district. As your city council representative, Daniel Dromm is committed to solving this problem. Studies have identified three areas which can successfully reduce traffic congestion: education, engineering and enforcement. He will work the Department of Transportation to look into engineering solutions as such traffic calming and rebalancing the street grid. Dromm will ask the local merchants to educate their customers about local traffic laws. He will also co-ordinate increased traffic enforcement with the local precinct.

Neighborhood Chat / Dromm is the "Education Candidate"
« on: January 24, 2009, 02:10:14 PM »


From Daily Gotham:http://dailygotham.com/blog/mole333/educators_running_for_city_council
Daniel Dromm: Running for City Council in Queens' Council District 25

Daniel Dromm is an educator, community organizer, union leader, and political activist. His experience and dedication to the community has led to Dromm being overwhelming elected Democratic District Leader in the 39th Assembly District, Part A (Jackson Heights, Elmhurst, Corona and Woodside in Queens) in 2002 and re-elected in 2004, 2006 and 2008.

Daniel Dromm is an award-winning New York City public school teacher. Since 1984, he has instructed and inspired thousands of students at PS 199Q in Queens. In 2006, Dromm was chosen as Outstanding Teacher of the Year by his school's principal...Dromm took a lead role in implementing several successful projects such as the "Philosophy for Children" project and the Integrated Language Arts project. He was hand-picked by the New York City Opera to give a workshop at Lincoln Center on bringing opera into the classroom. In 1997, along with colleague Barbara Baruch, Dromm won the 'UFT Kid's Day on Broadway' grant for his school. He has received awards from both the District and from the PTA for his dedication to students...

In 1992, Dromm courageously came out as an openly gay teacher. The story became front-page news in daily newspapers throughout the city. The ensuing battle tested Dromm's resolve as a fighter who is unafraid to take on the powers that be. Dromm defeated several unsuccessful attempts to remove him from his job and became a role model for equality in the workplace. This episode in Dromm's life illustrated to him how important tenure is as a protection for teachers.
