That's awesome, such a ringing endorsement!
How do the different towers work, are they each a standalone coop or is it all the same complex?

They are all individual coops. Separate financials, staff, Board, facilities, etc. I don't think they (at least my building) have any connection to each other.

Personally, if I were looking for a unit now, I would only consider the building I am in now (3965 52nd) or the building located on 54th Street http://streeteasy.com/building/39_60-54-street-queens
To get to the Berkeley buildings located between these two buildings, you have to walk down 52nd St and around my building - there is no access from Skillman Ave so that adds another 2-3 minutes to your walk. Doesn't seem like much (for me at least), it would be an annoyance that would irritate the heck out of me.

But you can't go wrong with any of these buildings - they are all super close to the subway, across the street from the park and withing easy walking distance to all of the local bars, restaurants, library, coffee shops, bakery, etc.

Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: Berkeley Towers - different sections?
« on: February 07, 2017, 07:27:40 AM »
Quote from: kellydaniel on February 06, 2017, 03:16:09 PM
Quote from: rhydewithdis on February 06, 2017, 02:54:01 PM
I live in Berkeley Towers and can answer any questions you have.
I live in the building which has the gym, which is on 52nd btwn Skillman Ave and 39th Dr.
Not all units have terraces, but yes the building is dog friendly.
Other amenities include: night watchman from 8p-5a, attended office to receive packages, each unit comes with a basement storage unit, bike room, gym, parking (with a very long wait-list, took me 13 years for an outdoor spot), nice outdoor seating in the rear of the building and you are a resident within the primary zone to join Sunnyside Gardens Park (one of only two private parks in NYC).

Let me know what else you would like to know.

Awesome, thank you! So are the different towers different coops, technically?  Like you can't use the gym unless you live directly in that tower?

Is it a nice community vibe?  I've gotten that sense walking by.  Also, have you kept tabs on your property values over the years?  I've lived basically across the park for 3 years and can see the neighborhood getting trendier, more fitness options like Blink & yoga studios, little restaurants... I like the upswing, but not being able to sublet if I bought there, I have to think about future options in terms of resale rather than rental...  What about turnover?  Clearly you've been happy there for a long time!  How often do apartments go on the market?

I've LOVE living here.  When I moved in at 25yo, I was one of the younger(est?) shareholders. Because the location is so convenient to literally everything and the building accessible-friendly (no stairs), the turnover of units (220 total) has been well below average. As shareholders age, they don't have much reason to move anywhere else, so unless they are moving out of state for warmer weather and golf (like Florida) - they stay until through the end. When units do come on the market, they are usually estate sales. There was so much excitement when a few shareholders including myself recently had children (the first time in a LONG while), we set up a kiddie playgroup hours twice a week down in the community room.

I paid $232k for my 3bed/2bath (1300 sqft) in 2002 and could easily get $600k (likely closer to $700k) for it now. My line (T line) has the largest units - I don't think another apartment in my line (11 of them, 11 floors in bldg), has come up for sale in over a decade). There is a flip tax of 25% of profits which you need to take into account. Subletting is really not allowed. So it's a GREAT place to live but from an investment standpoint, you will always be better off buying a condo, house or coop with looser rules.

Lastly, I can say without hesitation there isn't a better managed building out there. We are self-managed and haven't had a maintenance increase in 11 years and still managed to pay off our underlying mortgage a few years back.
