Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

A good friend of mine is with sanitation - even he is at a loss for how bad this cleanup has been. But even though the entire force was called in, he was so snowed under he couldn't get out to get to work. Said half the force was out either as "sick" or car-down since they are as stuck as us and honestly a lot of the garages aren't that accessible by trains so they need their cars.

Since so many are out a lot of the guys out driving have never driven a plow which is probably why we're seeing videos on youtube of cars being smashed :-)

I don't know why everyone dwells on the inches. I think wind, drifting and ice conditions make more of a difference than several inches here or there... Once you go over a foot its all the same until about 2.5 feet :-). We should just have a grading system using ranges of accumulation as well as wind and temperature conditions to compare storms

I think I'm developing a deep hatred for snow after a few years here. Whereas I used to love it as I'm from the south... But there we knew to just shut down at the prediction of snow: everyone buys out the grocery store, the nurses camp at the hospitals, no dares to drive and no one has to try to carry on as usual.  Just drink hot chocolate with all the milk you bought at the store because you don't normally buy milk, its just survival instinct...:-)
