Community Groups, Activities & Events / Re: Physical Activity/Recess at local schools
« on: June 19, 2011, 02:04:37 PM »
Please sign your son up for St. Joan of Arc's Youth Council.  They offer a variety of activities from soccer, basketball, baseball, softball and track through art, chess, needlework and theatre.  It's a great and inexpensive way to supplement the limited public school physical and afterschool activity programs, and is open to all Jackson Heights school-aged children.  Check their website at www.sjayouthcouncil.com for details.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: anyone have Metro PCS?
« on: June 19, 2011, 01:59:00 PM »
I had Metro PCS, but dropped it recently.  The voice quality on my Samsung Freeform was horrible.  Everyone said I sounded like the MetroPCS "Hello, Hello, Hello" robot voice.  Coverage in the NYC metro area was very good and I don't recall any dropped calls.

   The price is good, and they do offer a reduction in price to $35 each if you add a second line to your account.  The Metro PCS main store in Jackson Heights engages in what I consider deceptive business practices and you may do better off going to Best Buy.  The JH store will try to tell you that the advertised price requires a $20 accessory bundle package, which is nonsense.  Previously a representative told me the $40 plan was no longer available and that I would have to purchase a $50 plan, more nonsense.
   The customer service is very hard to wade through, as you will never get through to a native English speaker.  If your native language is Tagalog or Malay, this may not be an issue for you.  When I finally got through to someone and got her to understand what I was complaining about, the support rep was very helpful and made an adjustment to my bill to compensate for the sales people's setup error. There is no 3G coverage, so if you want a smartphone, you'll have to spring for the 4G coverage which begins at $50.
   I recently switched to Boost, which has unlimited for $50 a month, which includes 3G internet.  Virgin Mobile has a very good plan for $40 a month that includes 1200 minutes and unlimited text and 3G internet.  Both use the Sprint Network.  I chose Boost because of the phone I wanted, but others using Virgin say the service and quality is similar to Boost and Sprint.  Good Luck.
