Hi Captain Flannel,

The position is not be confused with the federal work study program, just in case that is not clear. As someone else mentioned, it's an old school barter, as in "work in exchange for study". The interested person would show up 20 minutes ahead of time to open the studio and sign in the class. That person can take classes at no cost.

I held a work study position at a neighborhood studio when I first started practicing yoga. I was able to take as many classes as I wanted for about 40 minutes of work per week. 2nd Story has the same arrangement with our work study people.


It's not for everyone, but the people who participate tend to stick around and keep their shifts. It's a friendly, low stress situation that I hope is mutually beneficial.

I hope this calms any looming concerns you may have about my posts, or about the sign in/work study position. I'm happy to answer any questions directly, so feel free to swing by the studio, or reach me via email at info@secondstorymovement.com.

Kindly,
Elaine O'Brien

She was very sweet and friendly and not aggressive toward us, but she had an injured right ear and traces of blood on her jaw, as she appeared to have been in a fight. Animal control couldn't help at that hour so some neighbors took her to the 115th police precinct on 93rd and Northern. She looked young, with white fur and light brown spots, probably 25 lbs, and looked like she was nursing.

If anyone has any leads on the dogs owner she should still be here http://www1.nyc.gov/site/nypd/bureaus/patrol/precincts/115th-precinct.page
