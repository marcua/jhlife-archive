The  study from which the article was gleaned does not measure average wages of county residents against housing prices.  Rather, it measures the wages physically generated in the county itself against housing prices.  That is how New York County is "more affordable" than Queens, for every job located there, worked by people from across the metropolitan area and even farther (including wages pulled in from supernational headquarters and the United Nations) gets included in that statistic.  It's odd piece of data to use, especially when county economies are so integrated that most wouldn't even think of them as discrete economic entities. 

RealtyTrac probably wanted to make some stat pop for its own ends and therefore produced the study.  Ms. Canal has to churn out an article every 5 days on topics ranging from Taylor Swift to badminton rackets, so one wouldn't expect that sort of nuance from her, but she should atleast get the meat of the original article right.
