Talking form experience, CIGI's is a ripoff, and the Mexicans on 93rd and Roosevelt is okay but they don't have all the right tools depending on what you need to be done, the one in woodside is okay usually you can try to bargain with the guy. Now if your having serious work done to your bike like changing the brake fluid for your disc brake, or getting the gears changed, I suggest two bike shops from the area:

Close to PC Richards right by the movie theater near Kaufman Astoria studios

Bicycle Repairman
40-21a 35th Ave
Astoria, NY 11101
(718) 706-0450

The bike mechanic that works there is great, he use to be the head mechanic at metro bicycles for 20 years and later on opened up his little shop in Astoria. He knows what he is doing and has all the right tools.he even does old school lacing for wheels which is rarely seen.  The only thing is that he wont work on your bike that day depending on how much work you need done, it usually takes 3 days to get your bike back since he has other jobs. Prices are reasonable, and he will order anything that you need if he doesn't have it.



Spokesman
49-04 Vernon Boulevard
Long Island City, NY 11101
Open Daily 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM
(718) 433-0450

The owner of this places has several bikes shops around the city one at Atlas Park, and another on 17th and 3rd Ave called Manhattan Velo there mechanics are good but some prices at times are a bit too expensive.

As for paint jobs, you can do it on your own with some paint stripper, sand paper, primer, spray paint and clear coat. If your patient the results come out great.
I would never pay a professional to paint my bike for normal day use, unless your building some sort of fixed gear show bike.

I hope this was helpful, and if you have any questions let me know.
