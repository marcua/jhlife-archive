Happy revive this thread after several months with great news to share:

Community Board 3 approved its section of the bike lane on 31st ave this yesterday. This bike route will connect Jackson Heights and East Elmhurst to North Corona and the Flushing Promenade. Pretty soon, you will be able to hop on your bike in Jackson Heights and reach Fisher Pool, Flushing Promenade, Bulova Park and St. Michaels Park more safely.

This bike lane is a direct result of the campaign that started right here on this thread.
It was requested to the DOT by thousands of members of our community and we're deeply thankful that the board attended our request and approved it. We are also deeply thankful to Council Member Daniel Dromm, who embraced this vision from the very beginning and has joined us full force to make it happen.

How safe is it?
The project has some sharred lanes, actual bike lanes, but no protected lanes. It is not suitable for unaccompanied children.

Our hope is that later this year the DOT will promote a workshop in our community to get broad community input and design a more complete and safer grid. We would like to have at least one bike route connecting Astoria to Corona, via East Elmhurst and Jackson Heights, that will be friendly to people of all skill levels from 8 to 88 years old. We're not there yet, but we'd love to keep counting on the amazing support we've been receiving from this community to make this happen.

While not perfect, the current project is a small step in the right direction to making our streets safer for all of us, including pedestrians and drivers. (Studies have proved that bike lanes make the street safer for all road users.) We have nearly 3,000 signatures for a petition requesting safer bike routes here. If you haven't signed it yet, you can sign the online version here: http://www.qns.bike/petition

You can read details of this bike route approved yesterday here:
http://www.nyc.gov/html/dot/downloads/pdf/31st-32nd-aves-bike-lane-apr-2016.pdf

Paint will hit the ground this summer.

Next stop: Astoria
Tuesday there will be a meeting in Astoria to vote on the section of this bike route that runs through Community Board 1. If you live have friends in Astoria area, it would be wonderful if you can ask them to attend and tell the board why this new bike route is good for you and kindly ask that they approve it.

If that section is approved, we will be the safer bike route will connect Socrates Park to Flushing Promenade, via Woodside, and connect to the section approved yesterday. These are the details of that section:
http://www.nyc.gov/html/dot/downloads/pdf/31st-ave-bike-lane-mar2016.pdf

Tuesday, June 21st, 7PM
Community Board 1 (Astoria area)
Location: Astoria World Manor, 25-22 Astoria Boulevard
Time: 7 P.M.

You can get frequent updates of our work on facebook.com/qnsbike and join our listserve trying to work to make this happen.

Again, THANK YOU ALL SO MUCH FOR YOUR SUPPORT!
