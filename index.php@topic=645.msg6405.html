You hit it right on the nose...You really have to try different ones.  Tapas are not your typical "bar finger foods" joint,  its more like a miniature version of an entree.  Often some foods may be too "greasy" or spicy for an individual.  I realize that now  most ethnic restaurants will cook sudo-traditional or traditional.  In the old days I used to see lots of bland foods because it was believed that the US consumer found ethnic foods spicy, too greasy or too salty.  These believes, for the most part, are no longer true, well at least in large cities like NYC.  While I have gone to Jardi 4 or 5 times I do not go there during the colder season. 

In fact, I have never planned to go to "jardi".  The times that I did stop was because I was passing by and a parking spot was by the entrance or near it and the openness was attractive to me.  I tried maybe 7 or 8 different tapas, like you, I found them to be OK but a couple of tapas were really tasty.  I wish they would add cocktails, this would bring more business and a happier crowd :rockon:.  I understand that the owner wants to keep a certain cache to the place by leaving Jardi as a wine war but my opinion differs.  Next time I go I will list the few items that I did like.  There's quite a few I didn't particularly loved but I'll stay away from "not recomending" because my taste may differ from others.  I am more of a cold tapas individual.

Well, Happy Mothers Day to all you wonderful Ladies that keep our society from falling apart.  Hope you have a fascinating and fullfilling day.    :smitten:
