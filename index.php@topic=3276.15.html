Helen Sears wanting to ban all vendors in our neighborhood reminds me of what Obama said during the election about John McCain "using a hatchet where you need a scalpel."

While I agree there are some negative side effects from some vendors in some places, we shouldn't be banning all vendors.

I think that 73rd Street, 74th Street, 82nd Street, 37th Avenue and 37th Road should be prioritized for pedestrians. I think that these streets are so active with pedestrians and so critical to mass transit access that blocking them with vendors is counterproductive.

However, I think that areas of Broadway and Roosevelt Avenue lend themselves to vendors because of the extra wide sidewalks. Also, there is a benefit of having vendors being 'eyes-on-the-street' especially on Roosevelt Avenue.

I think that the smart move is to get more sanitation services (additional garbage cans, additional trash pick ups) and increase code enforcement for all these areas.

Sears hopes to finish what she started

Sears Hopes to Finish What She Started
From the Daily News, August 4, 2009

Even in the most diverse county in the United States, Queens' 25th Council District is a melting pot in a league of its own.

Three candidates are striving to carve out niches for themselves before the Sept. 15 primary election in this district - encompassing stretches of Jackson Heights, Elmhurst, East Elmhurst, Rego Park, Woodside and Corona.

Incumbent Helen Sears, 80, is battling fellow Democrats Daniel Dromm, 53, and Stanley Kalathara, 64, for a third term in office. The winner will go up against Mujib Rahman, 51, on the Republican ticket, in the Nov. 3 general election.

Sears, who is backed by the Queens Democratic Party, said she is running again to finish what she started.

In her almost eight years on the Council, she said she is most proud of opening a cancer clinic at Elmhurst Hospital Center and securing funding for a women's health clinic there.

If reelected, she wants to work on opening new primary care centers and come up with a plan to expand Travers Park, she said.

But critics have accused the longtime Jackson Heights resident of ignoring the needs of immigrant constituents.

Sears rejected the criticism.

"The people that use the health services at Elmhurst Hospital are all immigrants," she said.

Leading the pack of challengers is Dromm.
