I am leaving the country, and must sell my two bedroom co-op. It's been a perfect place to live with my wife and child, while making a daily commute to lower Manhattan for work.

I've lived here for two years and still haven't tried all the restaurants close-by. As a foodie especially, there's a lot I'll miss.

The apartment is spacious, and is broken up nicely. It's also a corner apartment, which means lots of light and unobstructed views out of every window. My 4 yr old daughter has a good sized room, and great friends in the building. She attends pre-school at the Red Apple school in Flushing, which I can't say enough good things about.

The apartment is approx 3 blocks away from three large grocery stores. Since the neighborhood is largely middle-class Hispanic, there's some great imported groceries. Closeby on Northern Blvd is DespaÃ±a Foods, which is one of the best importers of gourmet Spanish meats and cheeses in NY. Pio Pio and the Y Gallery round out a few more great neighborhood assets.

Many days on the way home I'll get off the train at 74th street walk from there, just to stroll down 37th St and by all the shops -- perhaps to grab a chocolate cannoli at Leti's Bakery, or stop at Italian farms, or the organic food store (Jackson Heights Health Food).

Late nights on the weekend I'll miss strolling down Roosevelt Ave for an arepa from the famed Arepa Lady, or getting chicken over rice at Sammy's trophied vendor cart. Jackson Heights is home to an incredibly diverse and seemingly endless number of places to eat.

It's just also just short ways away from Woodside, Sunnyside, and Elmhurst, which also have a good food selection.
