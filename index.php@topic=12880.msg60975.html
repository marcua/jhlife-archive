From talking to friends, it seems that most addresses in Jackson Heights are zoned for at least 2 public schools. I think that you might mean you live in District 30, and are zoned for PS 69 and PS 212 (District 30 is big and includes many neighborhoods in western Queens).

When you register your kid for kindergarten, you submit a ranked list of your school choices. The order of priority is usually the following:

Priority 1: Kids that live in that school's zone and have a sibling at the school
Priority 2: Kids that live in that school's zone without a sibling at the school
Priority 3: Everyone else

PS 69 is big, crowded and popular (because it is a great school!). It is possible that you will be put on the waiting list for PS69 if you don't have kids there already.  That happened to some friends of ours, but I think they eventually did get a spot (although by that point they had already committed to somewhere else).

The school you are zoned for is set by the city for each individual block. It doesn't really matter if PS69 is equally close if you are zoned for PS212 and not PS69. You can list PS69 on your kindergarten registration, but will be hard to get a spot there if not in the zone (and FYI, the zones are strange shapes to alleviate overcrowding at individual schools).

I just checked our address: we're zoned for PS69 and PS222, but maybe most addresses in the neighborhood aren't zoned for two elementary schools as I wrote earlier...
