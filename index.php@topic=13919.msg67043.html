Car Break in
« on: October 29, 2015, 12:14:24 PM »
I encountered the strangest thing this morning, I had parked my car last night in front of 78th/35th and noticed this morning that my owners manual and some papers that were in my glove box were now on the front windshield of my car. I opened the door to find that my entire glove box contents and storage areas were all dumped on the floor. The strange part is/was that nothing was taken that i can recall. I had an expensive bike in the back, tennis/golf clubs, ez pass, another set of keys but luckily nothing was taken. There was no forced entry to my car nor was it damaged, I might of left it unlocked by accident...there is a camera from the building pointing in my cars direction or maybe someone scared them off? Who knows, I did not call the police because I am not sure what they could do if nothing was taken...I imagine this happens often if the opportunity presents itself? Baffled to say the least..

Sorry to hear about the break-in to your car, HAMjacksonheights, and I'm glad nothing was stolen.  I think it's a good idea to inform the police of the event.  While nothing was taken, what occurred is an act of vandalism in the neighborhood.  The precinct needs to be alerted to these types of happenings.  You mentioned that a nearby building has a security camera.  If that camera caught footage of the event as it happened, it can help substantiate the police report.  Of course, the building's residents have to be willing to cooperate by sharing the footage.  In any event, I think it should be reported.
