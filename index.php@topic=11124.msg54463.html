Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Agree with a few of these, although unfortunately conversations on this board rarely extend outside Jackson Heights (despite the stated broader focus).

Many of those places on Woodside Ave have been there for a while (maybe 2009 or before), and others listed above are simply places that moved location and spruced up a bit. Hardly seems like wholesale Williamsburg-level gentrification. As for whether "ethnic" or any other places count as gentrifiers, I think it depends...did the Thai bar or other nearby places open to attract customers that grew up in Woodside / Elmhurst, are now of drinking age, and want a cool place to hang out? That doesn't seem the same as gentrification that forces out pre-existing residents.

And I'm happy about that! My point about posting the old thread is that we have gone through a few cycles of doom and gloom predictions on this board about widespread gentrification, and they just aren't coming through. There are still only a few storefronts out of well more than 1,000 that might even be included as "gentrifying".  That hardly qualifies as even a slow creep of gentrification...seems more like the natural business cycle.  Many restaurants today don't look like they did 25 years ago; doesn't = gentrification.
