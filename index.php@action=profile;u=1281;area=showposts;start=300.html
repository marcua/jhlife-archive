Neighborhood Chat / Re: Honking
« on: May 15, 2016, 08:25:56 AM »
Several factors at work contribute to the problem.  Inadequate parking near shopping leads to double parking.  Trucks using non-truck routes lead to slow traffic flow as they attempt to negotiate the double parked cars. Pedestrians crossing despite the flashing "Don't Walk" signs prevent cars from turning. Lack of turn lanes mean that traffic backs up behind the cars waiting to turn.  Poor visibility due to the double parked cars leaves drivers unaware that there are pedestrians present in the crosswalk. Taxis and Ubers stop in random locations to pick up and discharge passengers, rather than at the curb as required by law. Nitwits on their cell phones in the car don't see the light change.

Solutions: Ticket blitz on cars double parked and not yielding to pedestrians. Jaywalking tickets to pedestrians who interfere with traffic.  Pull licences for cars for hire that obstruct traffic or honk to flag down possible passengers.  Build municipal parking lot.  Don't allow new construction without adequate parking. More turn lanes and turn signals in congested areas. Ticket blitz for horn honking. Make it illegal for pedestrians to cross streets while looking at a cell phone.

What would also help is for pedestrians and drivers to have consideration for each other and for the neighborhood. But since everyone is self-absorbed, this will be the hardest to implement.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Travers Park - Fitness Area?
« on: April 11, 2014, 05:08:28 AM »
Travers Park may be the most overused piece of parkland in the city. At any given time you may have volleyball,  tennis,  cricket, softball, soccer and basketball games at the same time on the same field.  There's a small workout area adjacent to the fullcourt basketball at the north end of the park.  Nothing to get excited about. If you workout early enough you will have room but any other time of the day you'll be short on space. As for running, it seems most runners jog along the bike paths on 34th Ave.  Tai Chi people and a personal trainer giving group lessons in the morning at Travers if you're into that.

Jackson Heights is critically short of greenspace so for a scenic run you might have to take a trip to Kissena Park or the Botanical Gardens in Flushing.
