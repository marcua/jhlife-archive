Title: Walking Tours in Jackson Heights
Post by: toddg on November 20, 2007, 02:21:53 PM
Here are some walking tours of Jackson Heights and surrounding areas... great ways to learn about the neighborhood's food, architecture, and history.  

Jackson Heights Beautification Group's Historic Weekend
Held every June, the JHBG Historic Weekend includes many ways to explore Jackson Heights, including a self-guided tour of the neighborhood's private interior gardens, and escorted walking tours of the historic district.  See the JHBG website (http://www.jhbg.org/index.html) for more information.

Sunnyside to Jackson Heights
Urban geographer Jack Eichenbaum (http://www.geognyc.com) leads two walking tours in the Sunnyside-Jackson Heights area.  "The first walk concentrates on inter-war residential and industrial developments with open space. The second walk follows Roosevelt Ave, the #7 elevated train built on its right-of-way, and late twentieth century immigrant commerce."  Schedule information can be found on the Municipal Arts Society (http://www.mas.org/viewcategory.php?category=19) website or through the Brooklyn Center for the Urban Environment (http://www.bcue.org/?go=cmn.49).

Noshwalks: South Asian Jackson Heights and Latin American Jackson Heights
Myra Alperson's Noshwalks provide a great introduction to the delicacies of many NYC neighborhoods.  Schedule information can be found on the Noshwalks (http://www.noshwalks.com) website.

The Queens Jazz Trail
The Queens Jazz Trail map (http://www.ephemerapress.com/queens.html) provides a self-guided walking tour of the neighborhoods and residences of many great jazz musicians.

Are there any others?
