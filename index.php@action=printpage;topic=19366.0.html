Aged Salice Salentino!

This week we are featuring a dynamic red from Puglia (the heel of Italy’s boot). Cosimo Taurino’s family has been making wines using the ancient grapes of southern Italy for seven generations. His Salice Salentino is made from a blend of Negroamaro and Malvasia, which is aged at the winery until it is ready to drink.

The “current” vintage is from 2010 and shows a wonderful balance of earthy, spicy and fruit flavors. It's a wonderful opportunity to savor a wine that has been allowed to properly age, a luxury which has disappeared for most wine drinkers in our modern age.

The Queensboro will be featuring the 2010 Salice Salentino by the glass starting Wednesday night. Because of its balance, it matches a range of food - pizza, burgers, pastas- but we especially like it with Chef Tony’s Double-Cut Pork Chop with grilled peaches… delish! Come on down and try and glass- we love it and think you will too!

Not only are we offering this wine by the glass today, but if you get a bottle we'll knock 25% off the price, and as the Italians from Puglia say, "that's a heel of a deal!"
