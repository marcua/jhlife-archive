NEW TO MARKET TOWERS APT W/ CITY VIEWS, 1400 SQF 4 BR/2.5 BA , OPEN HOUSE SAT
« on: January 06, 2010, 02:23:56 PM »
Trophy apartments only appear once in a blue moon and this top floor, 1400+ square foot 4 BR/2.5 BA represents the cream of the crop. The living room with wood burning fireplace and the formal dining room offer sweeping views of the Manhattan skyline. The large bedrooms all face the elegant interior courtyard. The whole apartment has been carefully renovated. The windowed kitchen has a dishwasher, w/d, and an adjacent butler's pantry with original cabinetry. All plumbing has been replaced in the main bathrooms and the pre-war tiling and tubs have been preserved. The hardwood floors are refinished, all of the walls are replaced, and new electrical has been installed throughout. This home is located in The Towers, the most exclusive, landmark buildings in Jackson Heights, a historic district consistently praised for its stately pre-war buildings, green streets, and multicultural cuisines. The building has a children's playground and is a short walk to the E/F/V/R/7 trains. And Manhattan is only a 15 minute ride away! If you're looking for all the pre-war beauty of Brooklyn and Manhattan for half the price, then this home will do your dreams justice. OPEN HOUSES SAT 1/9 AND SAT 1/16 FROM 12 PM TO 2 PM. For pics and floor plan, go to: http://www.prudentialelliman.com/1199692

There are two ways of looking at this listing and please note that this is the asking price, everything is negotiable.

1.  There may be someone that appreciates the space, doesn't want the hassle of living in a house, and loves having gardens, a children's playground, the option of a 10% downpayment, the acceptance of dogs, the no subletting policy, the grand living and dining room, the fireplace, and four exposures.

2.  There may be many that are not comfortable with the price as they wouldn't be interested in spending that amount of money, because they can't afford it, although they want to comment prior to viewing the property.  In addition to other personal reasons.

And yes there is the reality of the comps/recent sales.  At this point that hasn't been disclosed and unless you
have done your due diligence, no one can say what the last sale has been other than the agent representing the property, the seller and the management office for the Towers.

For the moment, I think that everyone should back off and let the real estate agent and the seller do their thing.  I don't believe that many that are attempting to sell their property would appreciate reading these negative posts relating to the price prior to an Open House.  Give the seller and the agent a break, again everything is negotiable.

Therefore, if the asking price is too high and a buyer makes a realistic offer, so be it.  They'll negotiate.

Sincerely, a property listed for this amount has no real effect on anyone on this listserve.  It doesn't mean that it will sell for that amount, and ultimately, the only person that will be affected will be the seller.  That's it!  Unless I'm missing something.
