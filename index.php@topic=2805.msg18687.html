The concept that you need a "tough bike for the city" is questionable.  All bike frames are strong, the wheels however are subject to bending.
The key to riding in the city is tire pressure, you must check the tires before you ride and fill to the max. 
I use a Black & Decker air station that is quick and has a built in gauge.

Well, I don't believe all bike frames are strong, and I do believe that a well made bike (if that phrasing suits you better) is important.
When I say that the Cross-Check is a tough bike, it's also in regards to how well it is built. It will last a long time. The City can be hard on a bike, so it is more enjoyable to ride something that is well made.
The comment about the wheels bending is a good one. If you ride your bike in the city, you will frequently need to have the wheels trued.

However, I'd be weary of filling your bike tires up to the max. Just look on the tire and fill it up to the recommended PSI. It is possible to over inflate, and in the summer the air will expand in the heat.

Another good alternative to the Cross-Check is the Bianchi Volpe:
https://www.bianchiusa.com/05-bicycles/05-specialty/05-volpe.html

I tried them both, but felt the Cross-Check was a smoother ride.

Hi Muppet,

Unless you're riding to work every day, I think $300 is enough to spend on a hybrid. That's how much I spent on my own bike and it holds up great.

East Elmhurst is nice but I think it's too small to enjoy for more than 30 minutes. I recommend taking 34th Ave. to Flushing Meadows Corona Park (at around 114 St.). The park is HUGE, mostly car-free, and circles a body of water (which you can ride around). It also has perks like a skating rink, pool and zoo. What other park has all of that?

Have fun!
