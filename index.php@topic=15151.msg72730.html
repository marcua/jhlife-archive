One of the problems is JH has been overbuilt over the past 40 years or so, hence we have no sizable public park space, congested traffic, crowded sidewalks among other things.  The idea is to re-vision our neighborhood.  We are very lucky to have so many mass transit options e.g. many subway lines/bus routes in JH.  Whatever we can do to cut down on traffic and open up space for people I think would be a step forward.  This is not an anti-car idea.  Parking solutions are possible.  Perhaps a neighborhood parking garage on Northern (?)  We don't need to think in terms of the car being the center of our community that we have to work around.  This is about the quality of life on the street.  It would be nice if we could actually begin a dialogue on how we would like to see how our neighborhood should evolve/improve.  In its creation JH was a planned community.  Now it has become a hodge podge of buildings with little planning.
