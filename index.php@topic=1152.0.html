Aronan, I think that whatever information exists regarding possible health implications is inconclusive at best.  That uncertainty can be a bit frightening to some residents, particularly because contracts entered into with telecommunications companies can last up to 25 years.  Because of differing views on the subject, many buildings can experience disagreement among its residents.  People worry about health and possible liability issues as well as lowering property values.  Here's an article on a Brooklyn co-op that decided to have towers installed:

Cell Rage Roils a Co-op on a Historic Block

Like you mentioned, I'm wondering if cell phone towers fall within the boundaries of what the Landmarks Preservation Commission deems permissible from an aesthetics point of view.  I remember reading an article about how some buildings in historic areas circumvent this by installing smaller, inconspicuous towers.  I assume the telecommunications company pays all fees.
