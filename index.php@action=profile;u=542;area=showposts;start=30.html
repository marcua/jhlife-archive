Neighborhood Chat / Re: Apartment Robbed on Wednesday in Jackson Heights
« on: December 07, 2008, 04:45:25 AM »
I lived on 82nd and 35th about 3 years ago and our building experienced at least 3 similar incidents over a year or two.  Keeping your windows locked and shades down when you aren't home definitely helps.

Also, take a look around the outside of your building and see if there's a way your landlord/super/board can better secure any entry points to the fire escapes.

A lot of the buildings are about the same height and very close to each other around here too, which means if the thief (or thieves) have access to one adjacent rooftop, they can stroll up and down the block from above until they find an open window.

Experienced a similar robbery years ago in a different neighborhood.  Fortunately, all that was taken from me were about 200 CD's and when the perp was caught I recovered about half of them.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: musical instrument stores in JH or nearby
« on: October 02, 2008, 12:18:16 AM »
Yes, while crafting this post, I deliberately avoided using the term "busted nut" but in retrospect "cracked nut" doesn't sound much better.  It is always nice to keep an old guitar in working order though.

Unfortunately, I don't remember the exact price.  As I said, it was much earlier this year, so the price slips my mind, and the receipt was in the wastebasket once I proofed the service.  Reasonable, but not a bargain.  The nut replacement and setup/intonation including strings was probably just a bit over $100 or so.  Reasonable considering that the bulk of the cost is for labor for something I'm unskilled to do, and the quality was good.

Could be a different repair guy over there by now too so take my testimonial for what it's worth.  I always end up dealing with a different 19 year old kid behind the counter every time I go in.

I generally like dealing with the smaller guys too for things like repairs, but I've only been in JH for about 3 years now and haven't discovered anybody like that.

I wouldn't worry about the intimidation factor at guitar center.  If you go in there with an instrument for a repair, they're generally not going to push a lot of crap on you.  Most of these big chains offer repairs as a value added service to lure in customers for future business.  Generally the repair guys at these big shops are local private contractors they hire who control their own rates and when they work, and they usually split their time between a few different shops.  I suspect that if you went to the Sam Ash, you might even get the same guy servicing your axe.  Bottom line is that the repairman sets the rates, and the clerks just take the orders hoping you'll come back one day to browse.

Rincon is a chain too, albeit a smaller one.  Don't know much about them.  Bought a Capo from them once, and on my second visit, I found out they don't stock midi cables.  In general I see it as having a much smaller and less diverse selection of products and services.

Most of my repairs I do myself.  I can do maintenance level setups and intonations, and I know how to solder which helps with most electrical issues, but once you're dealing with the body/wood or physically trimming and filing something like a fret or a nut, I'm lost.  Soldering is a science, woodworking is an art.
