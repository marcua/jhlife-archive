I'm a broker in Jackson Heights who works primarily for a sponsor. We have a 3 bedroom/2 bath at 35-20 Leverich at the moment if you're interested. It's $399K and fully renovated.
In terms of location, neither this nor 85-11 are in the historic district, and both are post war buildings, with Leverich having more amenities (doorman, pool, laundry on every floor), although  both have garages. Real Estate & Home Improvement / Re: The Colton - Thoughts?
« on: April 29, 2010, 08:19:08 PM »
You're right, the Colton is a no pets building, that does include cats. The elevator was replaced last year, and after a couple of weeks of adjustment issues, both of them have been working perfectly. I'm not sure why someone would find it sterile, it's got all the pre-war charm one could hope to find - large apartments with graceful arches and built-in bookcases, just to name a few features. The roof deck is spectacular, and brings the residents together in a relaxed environment you don't often find in any building. It is a condo, well over 90% sold, with the vast majority of apartments lived in by owners. There is a very low percentage of rentals from owners in the building. Yes, I live there, and find it a beautiful building. I also have a 2 bedroom sponsor unit for sale, which will up the percentage of owner occupied units!
