Not to add fuel to the fire but ...

... whether or not we want to walk home safe at 3 am, or expect to walk home safe at 3 am, stuff is more likely to happen at night.  Stuff is more likely to happen on less crowded streets.  Stuff happens.

Do we need to live in fear?  No.  Do we need to live a little more street smart?  Yes.  Do we need to raise public awareness of safety issues?  Yes.  Do we need to involve/contact our police precinct with concerns?  Absolutely.  Do we need to pack heat?  Probably not.

The best way to survive a fight is not to be in one, but sometimes it is unavoidable.

Personally, I agree with the elements of the post saying beware and be forewarned. I don't think this area is unsafe but
ANYWHERE you are at 3:00am if you are a person who is not the strongest looking, would mark you as vulnerable. I was
first jumped at age 11 and bit the guy who I left crying on the ground with my teeth marks drawing blood on his arm.
From then on I became hyperconscious of these possibilities and I have heard of attacks in all economic areas. Usually
at night, usually single people alone though our super was brutally beaten for a chain in the East 50's not long ago and
this was at 4:00am. I live near the attack area and when I go out alone for a late night, I save a portion of the money for
a cab which is usually $5. or $6. -less than a drink in many places. A few more swings by of a patrol car now and
then might be helpful as I don't see them often. Also agree that African Americans are not a big population here and
three young men together is pretty unusual in this area now. This is something my black friends would immediately
point out.
