Not to get off the topic of legalization, but Queens was the first county in New York State to have a Human Trafficking Intervention Court.  Many of the women are picked up on Roosevelt.  They are screened and if trafficking is an issue the court works with service providers to "rescue" these women from the "life."  Anyone can go to the court and observe.  Sometimes the pimps sit in the audience waiting for their "property."

The point is, people are finally beginning to look at these cases differently with the goal of treating many of these women as victims as opposed to criminals.  The organizations that provide these services--from addiction, to immigration, to safe houses--need help.  These new courts, which are now found around the city and in places outside NYC as well, have put a strain on the service providers who have reached their limit. 

If you want to learn more, Google NY Times and Queens Human Trafficking Court.  A long article was published last year.  We can do something to stop this modern day slavery which is happening right in our community.
