Walking home tonight. I was reminded of Danny Dromm's comments about parking in the area and this thread.

It seems like they are taking steps towards migrating to Muni meters in Jackson Heights.  There are two new bases, one on 37th ave between 84th and 85th (right by the fire) and one just around the corner on 85th between 37th and 35th.

My first hunch was phone booths, but given that there are existing phone booths in a very close proximity to these new bases, and the one on 85th is very close to a new signpost put up where the metered parking ends (no sign on it yet though) it would strike me that they may be working on the early phasing in of muni meters to replace the coin-op meters.

Just curious as to what anyone on the boards might know or may have heard (aside from Danny Dromm's seemingly off the cuff comments in the Daily News which mentioned nothing about currently enacted initiatives) about this transition.

Considering the typical pace of government, I highly doubt that this was a 'speedy' reaction to Dromm's message, but having missed any other mentions in the media or council meetings, wanted to know if I am correct in my assumption or if anyone else had gotten any headwind about this, and why it didn't get more fanfare.

Regardless of where I stand on this change, I always cringe a little when the government does something without proper forewarning and forum for the public to voice their opinions (not that it makes a heck of a lot of difference anyway when they do)
