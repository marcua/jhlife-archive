I've been reading more about this project, and it looks like very good news for Jackson Heights.

One component of this project is the construction of new switches that allow trains to move between the local and express tracks before and after Jackson Heights.  Currently, when one of the local tracks is closed due to a stalled train or routine maintenance, trains run express between Woodside and Junction Blvd.  This means if you want to use the 74th Street or 82nd Street stations, you often need to get on trains traveling in the opposite direction, and transfer at an express stop to a train going the way to want to go.  Or, returning home, you need to bypass your Jackson Heights station and then backtrack to it from the other direction.

If I'm reading MTA's website right, when this project is complete, trains using the express track will be able to switch to the local track to serve the 74th Street Station, where large numbers of people want to transfer to other subway lines or buses (and, of course, where many of us just want to get on or off!).  It's very unlikely that this means express trains will begin stopping at 74th Street during regular service, but it does mean that trains running on the express track due to many types of service interruptions will be able to stop at 74th Street.  This would certainly be a big improvement!
