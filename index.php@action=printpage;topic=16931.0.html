Title: Re: tingling fruit (mango)
Post by: mewmewlia on September 03, 2017, 11:03:27 PM
A few theories:

If the fruit was overripe, they may be starting to ferment and that could cause a tingling sensation.

Sliced fruit is sometimes dusted with ascorbic acid to prevent browning. That's probably the preservative you were thinking of. The reaction of acid and water can cause fizzing. My guess is that you got a double-whammy with sliced fermenting fruit.

Allergies are also possible, depending on what you mean by tingling. I'm mildly allergic to mangoes, melons, papaya, and kiwi--- I can still eat them, but they make my mouth and throat feel itchy and numb.

Mango skin is related to poison ivy. http://web.extension.illinois.edu/cfiv/homeowners/120726.html

If the mango still has little specks of skin on it, as in not 100% peeled, and you have this allergy, you will get the tingling and swelling sensation.
