Re: Where Did You Eat Last & What Did You Eat? - LA GLORIA! JH
« Reply #149 on: April 10, 2011, 10:26:32 PM »
"la Gloria" on Saturday.  Cute and cozy with a daring decor.  I think they did a good job incorporating and hidding the kitchen.  The small Menu makes it easy to chose your dishes but some patrons may not like that fact.  Looks like the bar can get really friendly later in the evenings.  The Ceviche mixto was good, the croquetas were extremely dry but it was served with some greens in a really delicious vinaigrette.  The ropa vieja with polenta could have done without the bean soup but they had the right idea.  I would get rid of the bean soup it's immersed in and just put the ropa vieja on a creamier polenta - I think that would be memorable.  The snapper had too much breading and way too crispy and dry for my personal taste.  Maybe I was expecting a traditional pargo frito with no breading, but if the fillet weren't so over done I think it would have been a nicer entree.

The pork dish was very tender and well seasoned so it might be borderline salty for some...again, one of those dishes I wish they would skip heavy sauces. In my opinion, Roasted pork needs very little messing with (outside of a mojito isleno or similar).  The mariquitas were served with guacamole that tasted fresh. The sangria was "spritzy' or maybe had some champagne or something like that in it.  It was OK but I don't like my red sangria to taste "white sangria" like.  Sangria is one of those things that have too many variations and establishments like to get creative with.  The Waiter was a very nice and attentive young man, unfortunately he knew nothing of the dishes, every time I asked about how the dish is made, he had to get the bartender  .  I would go again but maybe to the bar and try out some of the other appetizers.  It's an interesting spot and has the potential to improve if they wouldn't try so hard to over-manipulate what is otherwise simple traditional Latin dishes.
