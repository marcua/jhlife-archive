Title: Eminent domain to expand Travers Park, i.e., the car dealership
Post by: JDinJH on July 13, 2016, 09:42:42 AM
I think that it makes sense for the government to exercise its eminent domain powers to seize the car dealership on Northern Boulevard to expand Travers' Park. I focus on the car dealership as opposed to the Garden School because the school serves a greater public good in that it provides parents in the neighborhood with greater educational options whereas the car dealership has no comparable intrinsic public good - this argument only works if one assumes that the promotion of education is a public good.  It would be much harder to make a case for the government seizing a school in a neighborhood like JH as opposed to a car dealership, which there are already plenty of. 

Another reason it makes sense for the government to seize the car dealership is because there are renovations planned for Travers Park and seizing that one lot is administratively simple to the extent that there will only be one proceeding involving one lot.  The community would reap tremendous benefits that will dwarf whatever negative consequences the landowner will suffer, i.e., the landlord will be compensated with money and will be able to buy another piece of property, whereas the public will have a larger public space intended to promote community cohesiveness and provide residents with opportunities to participate in recreational and cultural activities and/or events. 

Has there been any serious discussion about this issue?  Is this the kind of community that can band together and force the government to invest more than is already planned?

Title: Re: Eminent domain to expand Travers Park, i.e., the car dealership
Post by: abu benuska on July 19, 2016, 11:58:37 AM
It's very easy to imagine what that space could be used for. For example, an extra playground (have you noticed how packed existing playgrounds get on weekend afternoons?), or more green space, or a senior gym, or a two story recreation center, with a garden at the top... I think it's worth looking into what it would take.

We do have a couple of strong groups, JHBG and JHGA. You could reach out to them and propose. If you are willing to do much of the legwork (like spending time emailing and meeting with politicians and other groups), there's a good chance one of these groups would support you (like telling you who to meet, going with you, sparing you time on on routes they may have tried in the past).

Just put a call over here if you want to lead the effort, a few more people might want to help with legwork. What these groups probably can not do is to run the idea for you. They are all volunteer-based and they already do what they do (JHBG does a lot of the public gardening and JHGA is responsible for the play street).

But they have many of the connections that you'd need and some experience in doing this (remember, the play street and Rory Staunton field were not there until very recently).
