Well, I'm either one of the most oblivious people ever, or I don't walk on Roosevelt after midnight.  I lived through Times Square in the early 80's, so I remember walking around and being approached by ladies offering their services (and cabbies wishing to purchase my services, which were not for sale), and dealers offering me an array of drugs.  I've never been approached for any of this on Roosevelt.

Not a complaint, because human trafficking is real, and those people who set these women up in these situations should be arrested, tried, convicted and put in jail for many years.  But two thoughts -- first, I'm one of the people who thinks that prostitution should be legal, and taxed.  Other states permit it, and I personally find nothing wrong with making a living through sex work.  Second, while headlines like this are always good politics, both for the police department and local officials, who can point to doing something, I would rather their efforts be focused on actually stopping the sex trafficking operators than arresting some guys (or ladies) that want to patronize a sex worker.  By doing the former, you are actually stopping a wrong by focusing on the roots.  By doing the latter, you are stigmatizing someone, permanently attaching registered sex offender status to them for the rest of their lives, without causing any disturbance to the organization that you should be focusing on.  It's like arresting the guy who buys some coke, instead of busting up the drug ring that imports it from abroad, and the distribution ring that pushes it on the streets.  Sure, you're doing something, but you're focusing on the wrong thing.

I hope you will all email and tell him that you want him to focus on the issues that matter to us: traffic calming, less residential street noise, less airplane noise, and trees NOT trying to turn Roosevelt Ave into Times Square. jperalta@nysenate.gov



Dear Friend,

Cleaning up Roosevelt Avenue has been at the top of my priority list for years. It was terrific to learn this week that eight people were arrested for soliciting sex for money.

The undercover prostitution sting was conducted at a spot under the elevated tracks of the 7 train which I have complained about for many years. Unfortunately, the commercial corridor has transformed itself into the old Times Square. As part of my holistic proposal to modernize the avenue, I called for a moratorium on liquor licenses for new clubs, and proposed the creation of a Commission on Roosevelt Avenue, an entity that would release a study with an action plan to modernize the busy commercial thoroughfare. I would like to thank the local authorities, including the NYPD and the Queens District Attorney's office for taking a step in the right direction.

What we need here is political will, the same political will that converted Times Square into what it is today. I will not give up until the Roosevelt Avenue is transformed into a destination for families and visitors.

If you have any questions or need help, do not hesitate to stop by my office or to call us at (718) 205-3881.

Sincerely, 

Jose Peralta
