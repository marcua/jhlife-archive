Neighborhood Chat / Making signs for the hood. Any Urdu/Hindi/Spanish speakers?
« on: July 07, 2014, 04:47:46 PM »
I've lived all over NYC and consider myself pretty resilient, but damn, Little India on a summer day is pretty freaking intense.  It's like a hot garbage BBQ out there.  That's just the lawful garbage on the street, never mind the huge amount of litter, which is what I want to address.

I think a nice reminder that litter is not welcome on our block could be enough to reduce litter by a significant percentage.  A little education, a little public shaming could go a long way to clearing the street of kulfi wrappers, dunkin donut cups, and takeout containers. 

So! I'm planning on putting up laminate signs up on the street signs in the many languages of the many cultures repped in JH.  Would Urdu, Hindi, Spanish, English, and Chinese  cover a fair % of our good residents and neighbors?  If anyone's familiar with those languages, pls chime in. I'd hate to have Google Translate make a mockery of these signs.
