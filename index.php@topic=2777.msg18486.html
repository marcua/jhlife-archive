Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I find it humorously ironic that an elected official is holding an emergency town hall meeting to discuss the swine flu problem.  Having the public get together and meet in a closed room seems the opposite of what you'd do in the midst of a contagious disease spread precisely by close contact.  I hope they have Purell and face masks available!

Reminds me of when there was an outbreak of measles during my college days.  For a couple weeks, the school was in "lockdown".  You had to produce an ID and a card showing proof that you were inoculated against measles to enter the campus.  After a couple weeks, they declared the emergency over.  So what did they do?  They placed plates of jellybeans out in the open for everyone to take, as appreciation for our patience!  Someone wrote a letter to the school newspaper saying how silly it was to leave food out in the open for everyone to touch, and just a few days ago we had to carry measles cards!
