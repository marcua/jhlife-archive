I think the Night Market might have had excellent promotion. Friends of mine in Brooklyn were marking it on FB as something they were interested in. That might account for the high attendance on the 2016 launch. It's bound to get better.

One of the things I love about living in Queens is our culinary options. But, you can't get it all everywhere. JH has a lot of great food, but there are definite gaps (bangers & mash? French bistro? Greek?). Last year at the night market there was a vendor selling spam wrapped in rice and seaweed. I know spam is definitely a thing in Pacific countries with a U.S. military presence post-WWII, and for me it's the canned food I keep in my emergency box. So I gave it a try and it was alright! Experiences like that are the fun of events like the Night Market.

I went for closing night. It was fantastic. Not crowded at all, with slightly fewer vendors than there were in the summer. By the looks of it, this time it was mostly families, probably less of the explorers from BK and more of a local crowd. The $5 price cap also makes this a relatively affordable excursion, with $15-20 being enough to have a nice, outdoor evening out with friends and leave full. The stalls here are also less gimmicky than those at similar food fairs in Brooklyn and Manhattan.
