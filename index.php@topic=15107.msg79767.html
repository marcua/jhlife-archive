I'm sorry if police are drawing the conclusion that cyclists are the cause of accidents, and targeting cyclists via fines at red lights and stop signs. A bit like stop and frisk a particular race, just that it's stop and fine a particular kind of vehicle. If statistics are being used which reveal a rapid increase in cyclist fatalities and other involvement in accidents, I hope the analysis is controlling for (per eyeball observation over the past several years) a very rapid increase in cyclists using NYC roads. No doubt number of automobiles on NYC roads has been pretty constant and stable over the past few years, especially compared to growth in cyclists.

Thanks to the CitiBike share program, ex-Mayor Bloomberg's good decision to paint more bike lanes (and isn't de Blasio generally continuing this trend?), and initiatives of organisations such as Transport Alternative to promote cyclists' interests.

For info, I got slapped with a fine by a police officer at 34th Av and 59th St (really nasty spot around 34 Av, 58 St, 59 St, 60 St and Broadway). Officer was sitting in his police car on 59th St. I failed to completely draw to a halt at a stop sign. The officer informed me a cyclist had been killed at that intersection (which has since had a bike lane about 10 feet long painted especially for cyclists turning left from 34 Av onto 59 St) the weekend beforehand.
