Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I agree.  I wish more information were available about the plans for the two sites.  All I know is that one site (I think on the east side of 74th) will be a school, and the other site (I think on the west side of 74th) will be a "school yard".   I welcome the school -- our neighborhood certainly needs more classroom space.  But I'm skeptical about the school yard.  We don't need another fenced-off stretch of asphalt -- I'm afraid that this will eventually become a teachers' parking lot like so many other "school yards" across the city.

I'd like to see several things happen.  First, I'd like the see the sites swapped.  I know the western site is smaller, but perhaps they can build the school there anyway by building it a bit taller.  That would reduce the pressure to tear down the remaining house east of 74th Street while we figure out a course of action that benefits the community.  Some options:

1. Save the house for some type of educational or community facility (it would make a great site for that long-promised daycare facility).  The students could use other nearby locations for recreation - Travers Park is just a couple of blocks away, and IS 145 isn't much further.  Who knows -- perhaps the Garden School would even be willing to let the city lease access to its school yards for a few hours each day.

2. If the house must be torn down, then the "school yard" that replaces it should be designed to meet community needs beyond the limited needs of the school day.  Let's see something designed creatively, and not just some asphalt surrounded by a chain link fence.
