Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

 I know for the Halloween parade that the middle school puts together a marching band and does songs like the Adams family theme, etc.  if I remember correctly, I think sometimes the gay pride parade has a marching band as well. I do know that there is often a marching band at the St. Patrick's for all parade in Sunnyside.

Your point is valid DSSJH, marching bands can be loud, but there's something about the timbre of an acoustic band versus overdriven speakers that often can't handle the bass being pumped through them that just is more appealing to the ear.  It also gives kids a chance to perform on an instrument in front of an audience, which is a wonderful and inspiring thing for them.

There is a lack of marching bands in the New York City public school system. This goes all the way up to the Collegiate level in the fact that with all the music teacher education programs in the city, there isn't one that teaches a class in marching bands.  Part of the reason I guess is the lack of space vs. the suburbs, but then again a lot of  middle and high schools do have football fields and some form a band program.

Maybe it's a simple matter of knowing which schools have the  ability of putting together a marching band, and then for the parade organizers to reach out to them.  It also would mean that the respective music teacher would have to sacrifice their Sundays, something that not all music teachers are willing to do!
