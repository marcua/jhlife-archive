This week's roundup by Sweeten Blog includes a 2 BR in Linden Court for $399k:

http://blog.sweeten.com/on-the-market/top-five-fixer-uppers-on-the-market-in-nyc-8/

Says Sweeten Blog:

"What we love: Historic, landmarked building, over ten feet of south-facing windows in living/dining, generously-sized rooms, great character.

What we’d do: $20k is a good starting point for a basic kitchen renovation in New York City. A gut kitchen renovation would add significant value to this home. Some owners might be tempted to update the bathroom but we love the West Coast vibe with those mod teal tiles!"

Listing at NY Times Real Estate:
http://www.nytimes.com/real-estate/usa/ny/queens/jackson-heights/homes-for-sale/37-18-85th-street/857-55D8512F1FF93

I have to agree about the renovations — kitchen is a must but those bathroom tiles look fine to me and I like the unusual color.

I've always admired the windows in Linden Court. This is a lovely home.

The apartment for sale in 2008 Shelby referenced is the same one featured by the OP, so talk about a perfect example.

$780 a month maintenance back then was pretty high, so perhaps there is a large, underlying mortgage the building had to take out and we are still seeing the effects of it?

Of the 10 Linden Court co-ops, the middle 6 each have an apartment that they can rent out to help keep the maintenance down.  The corner 4, however, do not, so that can explain some of it (the said co-op is in a corner).  That the land is already owned helps keep it down as well.  Also, the corner co-ops only have 12 units, as opposed to the middle 6, which have 16.  This puts renovations more heavily on the fewer units.
