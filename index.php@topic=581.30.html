I think the community is changing rapidly.  A good example of this is how the co-ops on 73rd street are being marketed.  About 20 years ago, I remember accompanying my sister and fiance to many of the neighborhood's open houses, including a few at the apartments on 73rd Street.  In my opinion, that whole area had more of a family appeal to it.  You saw many senior citizens living there as well as younger families.  On my way to the subway, I remember seeing people seated on lawn chairs outside the buildings and they would greet you as you walked by.  I look at the website for these buildings now, and it's very evident to me that they're being marketed to the gentrifying set.

"Jackson Heights was basically an unknown entity"....I disagree this this statement.  Jackson Heights has always been known for its garden communities.  As a child, I remember my father making the trek from the Bronx all the way to Roosevelt Avenue because that's where he liked to shop.  On the darker side, Jackson Heights was also well known for its drug trafficking history.

As an urban planner who grew up in JH in the 50's and 60's with family that had roots here from the '20's, it's interesting to follow this discussion.  JH was gentrified from the '20's thru the mid '60's when it began its downward slide, and as a poster said, it's swinging upward now. 

Maids and housekeepers were the norm until the early '60s.  No one shopped on 82nd Street without being "dressed" complete with gloves and hats.  JH was mentioned in many movies as a desirable place to live.... as in  Ingrid Bergman's home in "Cactus Flower".  Even today, living half a continent away, when I mention growing up in Jackson Heights, I always get a favorable response, particularly from the generation ahead of me.

The current trend is not taking away from JH, it's returning it to its original roots, as it was for much of  the last century. 

I see the same issues across the country in neighborhoods that have experienced a downturn and then begin to move upward again.  The people who moved there during the down spiral resent change more strongly then those who were there in the  first upswing.
