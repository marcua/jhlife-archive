Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I don't see anyone here saying they don't accept the election results. And I can tell you that the people I know who marched (myself included) don't deny that Trump won enough electoral college votes to win the election.

The point of the protests is that there are people who have watched Trump throughout the campaign (some for decades longer) and seen his behavior and heard what he plans to do and watched as he's nominated candidates they consider extremely poor choices for cabinet and administration positions. They are worried about the direction the country is headed in. And they're concerned about Trump's conflicts of interest and how his reckless tweeting and his imprudent dealings with leaders of other countries will put us all at risk.

Now, you may think everything will be fine under Trump. But I don't understand your attitude that people who don't agree should sit down and shut up. Are you confusing democracy with fascism? Even if Trump is the president, people who have concerns have a right to express them, to make their voices heard, if not by Trump then by their representatives, by the press, and by one another. They have a right to try and make a difference.

Have you never protested anything? Marched, or at least written letters, made calls, worn an armband, boycotted a business or company?
