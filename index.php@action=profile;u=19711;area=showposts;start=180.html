Hi rhydewithdis

The short answer is yes. We do have statistics on individual Coops. Sales and active listings are on public record, so they are easy to access at any point in time on the web. We have a roster of 25 buildings which we try to keep up to date. Look at it this way, if most buildings in the neighborhood have between complexes have approximately 120-160 units 4% turnover isn't that big of a number, we are talking 5-7 units per year. There are buildings like The Manchester that are just under 100 units, that are more effected by the general statistic - Manchester had 7 sales last year, that already puts them at over 7% turnover, but they have no units available for sale now. So once a unit comes to market there, it will most likely command a healthy sale price.

I've had to do statistical analysis for a client recently who brought multiple charts from Trulia.com, Red Fin and Property Shark, all of which had conflicting data. Why? it's because the web statistics do not break down Studios, One Bedrooms, 2 Bedrooms or Renovated or Unrenovated, low monthlies or high monthlies and based on square footage estimates in listings give a number per square foot that real estate can sell for in Jackson Heights. As a real estate professional I know first hand when there is a shortage of certain inventory. 2 bed + apartments and anything larger are in short supply, therefore there is a great price difference between at 950 sf one bedroom and a 1080sf 2 bedroom (100-125k to be exact). If they were priced on per square foot basis like the statistics suggest this would not be the case, do you agree?

In my opinion as 2012 slowly comes to a close, and I say this is because most transactions going into contract now will close and register their sales in 2013, the inventory in Jackson Heights has diminished as the new unit owners truly believe in the neighborhood as we do. So inevitably we are going to see an improvement in all areas, prices included.
