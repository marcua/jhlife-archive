Title: Re: Southridge Sued Over Accessibility
Post by: Ms. Jackson on March 15, 2016, 01:55:37 PM
The definition of compliance changes depending on when a building was built. Most of the buildings in Jackson Heights, having been built before these laws were in place, will only have to make "reasonable accommodations" for residents with disabilities. Sometimes, what's reasonable, especially with such a vague collection of laws determining accessibility, is best decided by courts.

Title: Re: Southridge Sued Over Accessibility
Post by: wlirfan on March 17, 2016, 12:11:00 PM
State and City law goes well beyond the ADA requirements.  Slope is always an issue, and even if you're an old building, you typically must show that it is architecturally (e.g., making a walk-up accessible) or financially (e.g. if you are a small low income co-op) impossible to avoid compliance with city law.  As it should be.
