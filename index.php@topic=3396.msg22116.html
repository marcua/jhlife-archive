I think the Eagle represents a great opportunity for the area. What if it expanded beyond simply being a movie house in to a community arts center? Film would still be a major part of the programming, but there is already a small stage in the Eagle, with a a little bit of lighting and curtains there then exists the potential for a theater space that could showcase the talents of our local actors and playwrights. Perhaps a new home for Jackson Rep ?

The space could function as a destination for seniors and students during the week days with all sorts of relevant programming. evenings could have different film series', play readings, speakers, community events, weekend mornings could have children's / family movies and shows. Throughout the year  the space could host community events and gatherings for which there is currently no suitable place in the neighborhood.

A revitalized Eagle Theater may just help to revitalize 37th Rd. and clean it up a bit too.

We lost the Polk, let's not lose the Eagle.
