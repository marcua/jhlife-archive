Garden School Head Master's Last School
« on: February 01, 2011, 02:54:51 PM »
I was doing some research about the Garden School and I came across this article in the New York Times. According to this story, Richard Marotta (the current Head Master at the Garden School) was involved with a school (Walden Lincoln) in the 1990s which sold its property to solve its financial problems. However, after two years the school was still losing money and was forced to close its doors. I'm afraid that the Garden School will meet the same fate as Marotta's last school if it sells its field to a real estate developer. 

http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9D0CE7DF173DF93BA25751C0A967958260&scp=2&sq=richard%20marotta&st=cse

Slump Not Exclusive, Private Schools Find
By ANDREW L. YARROW
Published: February 18, 1991

One school recently confronted by these brutal realities has been Walden Lincoln. Three years ago, the Walden and New Lincoln Schools, both early-20th-century Progressive schools, merged after a decade of declining enrollments. New Lincoln's East 77th Street building was sold to the Birch Wathen School for about $7 million, said Richard Marotta, Walden Lincoln's director. In 1987, Walden sold property next to its school on Central Park West and 88th Street to a developer, Sutton East Associates, for $5 million, with another $2 million dependent on condominium sales by Sutton East.

Despite this huge cash infusion, in December Walden Lincoln's board voted the school out of existence after a two-year, $3.6 million loss, and it was taken over by the Day School, an East Side elementary school.
