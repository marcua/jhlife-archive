Citi Field
126th St. & Roosevelt Ave.
Flushing, New York 11368

Date:  Tuesday, May 18, 2010

From Discover Queens:

"Hailed as Queens' largest celebration of culinary diversity, Queensâ€¦A Taste of the World will feature over 40 restaurants and fine eateries from the borough which represent the vast variety of cuisine ranging from traditional Italian dishes to new-age Asian fare.  This fundraising event will support the Queens Economic Development Corporation, a not-for-profit organization providing vital services to business communities throughout the borough.

So here's my favorite bakery: Nita's in Sunnyside (pronounced Nitza's).
It's on Greenpoint Avenue between 40th & 41st Streets
They specialize in Eastern European sweets, primarily Romanian.  I'll even give you my 3 favorite pastries in order of my personal preference
1. Amandine cake (also in individual mini pastry)
This is a chocolate sponge cake with chocolate creme filing and chocolate glaze. The kicker is the sponge cake....it's saturated in rum! (extract of course). Sooo good
2.  Mascot cake  (also in individual mini pastry)
This one is a very dense chocolate mouse with candied fruit pieces. Not everyones favorite but certainly one of mine. This cake is very heavy.
Both of the above pastries go best with milk as they are wayyyy chocolately!
3. Cozanac
This is a sweet bread....you could call it the Romanian version of pound cake. I don't know if they sell it by the slice since I usually buy it by the loaf.  This also comes in 2 other varieties: with a chocolate swirl in the center or with raisins mixed throughout the loaf. Both also good.

I hope this will be truly be a find for you and not a "repeat"

Now if I may, I'd like to rave about the Austin Street area of Forest Hills:

Five Burro's- 72-05 Austin Street. It looks just like a dive bar from the outside and you could easily just pass it by but they have a full Mexican menu and ridiculous portion sizes.  Good food and fun atmosphere.

Piu Bello's Gelato- 70-09 Austin Street.  Yummy gelato, plenty of other desserts and gourmet coffee and outdoor seating.  Perfect on a summer evening.

Sargeant Garcia's Mexican cuisine- 70-09 Austin Street, 2nd Fl. Casual atmosphere, yummy tortilla soup, super friendly staff.  Save room for dessert and ask for the fried ice cream....mmmm

Narita Japanese Cuisine- 107-08 70th Rd.  Hibachi is awesome, sushi is always fresh and they have the best fried rice I've ever had.  Also, they have a great variety of seating.  You have the hibachi grill seating with mini show similar to Benihana, you can sit at the sushi bar and watch the chefs roll it up, you can sit at traditional tables or (my favorite) you can take off you shoes and sit on the floor!  Love it!

Cabana, Cuban cuisine-10710 70th Road. There are 3 in NY now but this one is the original.  This is my only go-to spot for paella and they have a remarkable sangria too.

I just made myself hungry!
