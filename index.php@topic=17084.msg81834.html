Quote from: lmaniace on October 10, 2017, 10:10:25 AM
Supposedly the police were going to accommodate this problem, but it seems the police just are not aware of what's going on or, if beat cops are aware, the info is not effectively communicated or acted upon.

AFAIK one of the big problems with Rosie is that it's the border between the 110 and 115 precincts, and they don't really coordinate. So the north side of the street and the south side are entirely different policing units.

But mentioning the BID also brings up the fact that I'm sure many of the small business people on Rosie don't want it to get -too- nice, because then they would be gentrified out of their livelihoods. That's why the BID was killed: existing business owners were concerned that it was a stalking horse to force them out and replace them with national chains, as has happened on a lot of 82nd. The businesses along Rosie tend to be busy - they're all rented, not abandoned - and I bet that they fear multiplying their rent a lot more than they fear Senorita Puta leaning against the 7 train pillars. In fact, I'm pretty sure she buys drinks and is part of their community.

JH happens to be a bunch of non-overlapping communities who coexist and don't talk to each other much. But let's respect that and not try to force the other ones into becoming us. (While advocating for a reduction in things that hurt everyone, like gang violence.)
