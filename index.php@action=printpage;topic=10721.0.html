We switched to Green Mountain about 18 months ago and are very happy with them.  In fact, I can't really comment on customer service or anything else because I've never had a reason to call them or deal with them.  Everything has been exactly as they said it would be.

Title: Re: Green Mountain Energy at the Greenmarket
Post by: knatchez on February 13, 2014, 11:57:53 AM
Just a quick explainer: NY is a deregulated utility market, which means people can choose who *supplies* their energy. This can mean generation, but more often it means reselling from someone else. In NYC, ConEd still *distributes* your electricity (that is, sends it through the wires) but the electrons themselves come from someone else. Since ConEd owns the wires and meters they are also handling billing but that could change in a few years as customers become more familiar with Retail Electric Providers (REP). The idea is to create competition in the market. You can make up your own mind if this is a good thing. You can learn more about retail electricity at ConEd's website http://www.poweryourway.com/ -- in fact, if you move and call up ConEd to transfer service, they are legally required to offer you the chance to use an REP like Green Mountain Energy. I don't use GME personally, instead I buy 100% renewable power from EnergyPlus because they give me Frequent Flyer miles for every dollar I spend on the *supply* portion of my bill. They also have hotel and retail rewards programs if that's what you're interested in. The REP you select is totally up to you. The quality of the service should remain exactly as you have it now, no matter whose service you choose, because ConEd still owns and manages the wires that get the electricity to your home. You should shop around for the best rates for you. Most REPs offer the option to buy 100% renewable power (including ConEd) so you should consider shopping around for other perks as well.
