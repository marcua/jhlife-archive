Been meaning to make it to Juanita's since they opened about a month ago.  Finally did yesterday and--damn!  Great sandwiches--we tried the lomo saltado and chicharron--perfectly marinated meats, just enough extras to bring out, but not drown the flavor.  The sweet potato fries were perfectly prepared--delicate but substantial.  We ate in the covered patio out back, a great hang.  And the staff were friendly, glad to explain the menu (we were intrigued by the quinoa and apple drink) and gave us some free beverages while we waited.  All in all, a winner.  Was a little dismayed to see it was pretty empty on a Saturday afternoon so I hope you will all go and check it out--it's really a winner.

The person who designed Juanita's had some fun. The facade is clean with some classic New York elements. The floor is concrete/industrial. The South American murals add colour and personality. For Jackson Heights, the terrace is an aesthetic and practical delight, and the statue - pure kitsch, inspired by Rodin - adds humour.

I'm pretty sure that Pio Pio intends to make Juanita's into more than a sandwich shop, and will be disappointed if they don't.

Nice addition to the neighborhood.  Back patio is brilliant.

Had the saltado.  Nice sandwich, perhaps a little too much on the soy for me.  Not sure how soy plays into the Peruvian theme. 

Also tried the pollos a la brasa sandwich.  This is more like a chicken salad sandwich made with pio pio pulled chicken (which I love in and of itself).  To me, the rotisserie chicken felt a little too intense to be turned into a chicken salad.  The short lived Roosevelt Diner made a pollos a la brasa panini, which I felt was creative and more successful.

I asked the gentleman what can they do for a vegetarian, he said they are planning on redoing the menu, to add a fish sandwich and possibly more burger options.  I hope they come up with a vegetarian option.

He also mentioned they plan on adding beer and wine.  That will make that back patio very appealing.

As others have mentioned, we were the only ones in the joint.  I hope that changes.
