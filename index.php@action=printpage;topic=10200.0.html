Title: Need some help to open a restaurant/bar in JH
Post by: Dudley on December 18, 2013, 10:54:55 AM
Dear friends and neighbors,

Some of you may have heard that I am part of a small group that is trying to open a new restaurant to serve our neighborhood. We've been searching for an appropriate space over the past year, but as many of you know the market for commercial real estate in our neighborhood is limited and very expensive, making our search a challenge.

We're turning to the JH community to enlist your help!  Try as we might, it is simply not possible to be aware of everything that goes on in our neighborhood. Timing is crucial in real estate deals and getting an early tip can be the difference between making a deal and being on the outside looking in. This is why we need your eyes and ears. If you have any leads on a potential space- a business owner looking to sell, a store that is closing, or just the latest for rent sign- please let me know. Your information could be the difference between seeing another dollar store and having the convivial neighborhood institution we all want to see flourish here.

In a perfect world, the space would have an existing kitchen, but since the world still isn't perfect (we’re working on that!), we're actively considering all opportunities.
 
To give you an idea of what we want to do, what we have in mind is a place that will offer great beers, excellent wines, killer cocktails and delicious food. We've been fortunate to line up a highly regarded chef to helm our kitchen and are excited for the opportunity to bring their food to Jackson Heights. This is the kind of place that will keep you local on date night; the kind of place where you can get a quick dinner with the kids without compromising on quality (where is that chicken from exactly?); the kind of place where you can enjoy a quality beverage in an inviting atmosphere, and is above all affordable and welcoming to all. That's what we want and trust that it’s what you want as well.

So please let us know if you hear of anything that we may have missed, thanks!

Dudley

Yes, but it's sometimes useless. Here's what you do.

Enter an address into the BBL finder here: http://webapps.nyc.gov:8084/CICS/fin1/find001I
For more details, then enter the block and lot into ACRIS: http://a836-acris.nyc.gov/DS/DocumentSearch/BBL

If you're lucky, you'll get the financial documents transferring the property into the name of an individual or a recognizable corporation. But sometimes, you'll get documents showing that the ownership is a synthetic LLC like "73-01 37TH AVENUE ASSOCIATES LLC" which exists only to own the property and shield the actual owners' names. You would then have to go to the Corporations Department search at http://www.dos.ny.gov/corps/bus_entity_search.html and try to dig up information on that corporation.

Now as regards my sample address (the Duane Reade on 73rd), we do get lucky, as we find it is owned by "WALGREEN CO," as you'd expect it to be. Have fun punching in other addresses and seeing what you find.
