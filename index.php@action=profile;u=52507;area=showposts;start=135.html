Restaurants & Food / Re: El Rico Tinto
« on: April 25, 2014, 05:38:33 PM »
Stopped by Rico Tinto today for breakfast.  They've long since regained their A rating and I truly believe this is one of the best restaurants in the neighborhood.  It gets most of its attention for its bakery items and coffee and empanadas--all great, by the way--but its shining stars are the items from the full menu.  Try the fish tacos.  Incredible.  Or the guacamole.  Fantastic.  Or, as I did today, the huevos rancheros.  Amazing.  Great ingredients, beautifully prepared and presented, tasty and even somewhat lighter than other Mexican breakfasts in the area.  I've been going on and on about this place since they opened but I find that they're better and better with each visit.  Along with Phayul, Ayada, Cositas Ricas and La Fusta, I think this is the best spot around.  Also, nice to see Rico Tinto and Arunee paying attention to their decor.   Seems to be paying off in both cases.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Jackson Heights - Corona BID
« on: October 31, 2013, 09:14:35 PM »
Great article by Senator Peralta.  And reading the related article about the resistance to improving our neighborhood is absolutely baffling.  Terraza 7 is actually one of the greatest things about our neighborhood--a great bar with great music and unlike any other bar in the city.  But to hear the bar's owner complain that improving the neighborhood will rid Jackson Heights and Elmhurst of its diversity is so wrong.  There's no need to protect sex trafficking and gang wars and making an effort to shift away from that paradigm will not turn us into Williamsburg overnight.  And the caption below a picture of the fabulous Cositas Ricas, bemoaning that a BID expansion would cause the restaurant to disappear is crazy.  Cositas Ricas is packed almost 24 hours a day.  Terraza 7 is bustling most nights.  I just came back from Unidentified Flying Chickens, a great place that has seriously upped their game and they're doing great business.  These places won't go away.  Neither will Ayada, Phayul, Table Wine, Ricos Tintos and other great local establishments that do what they do really well and are rewarded with loyal customers.  But a little less of hanging on to the pride of being (as Senator Peralta suggests) the heir apparent to the 80's version of Times Square?  That is a loss I would hardly bemoan.

Restaurants & Food / Phayul
« on: October 19, 2013, 09:45:39 PM »
With both cold weather and cold season approaching, it's hard to say enough good things about Phayul, especially the perfectly spicy and most likely perfectly medicinal Thenthuk.  The hand drawn noodles are so tender and so light--and it's a delight to watch them being pulled and formed right before your eyes and only seconds before they end up in your very own soup.  I was still fighting off a lingering cold when I walked in and only hours later felt completely healthy.  Final score:  Thayul 1, Head Cold 0.

Restaurants & Food / Re: El Rico Tinto
« on: October 16, 2013, 04:24:26 PM »
Never mind the empanadas (which are mighty good).  I just came back from a great lunch at El Rico Tinto and everything was great.  Starting with a really well-made guacamole (neither as easy nor as common as one would think) followed by a carnitas taco, two chicken chalupas and one of the Columbian breakfast platters with scrambled eggs, sliced chiles, rice, beans an a mini arepa (what can I say--the wife and I were hungry!) What's more, the place is attractive, comfortable, always has great music and the staff is friendly and willing to recommend favorite dishes from the menu.  The bakery items looked great as well but we were much too full at that point.  A really great addition to the neighborhood and one which I'll be visiting often.
