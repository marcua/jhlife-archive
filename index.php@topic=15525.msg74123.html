A big flip tax has its benefits. it attracts shareholders who aren't likely to move in just two years, so if you seek stability in your neighbors that's a plus, but it's less appealing if you see yourself needing more space in the near future and/or want to climb the property ladder using equity.

Flip taxes can keep maintenance down and help shareholders avoid assessments. If you are good at setting your money aside and not spending it, that money can be invested and hopefully make more for you in the long run. Flip taxes also help keep expenses down for long time residents on fixed incomes who bought when prices were much lower so they can continue to live in the community they helped build and not be priced out by rising maintenance costs.
