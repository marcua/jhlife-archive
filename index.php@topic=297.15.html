I like Berry Farms in Astoria but I have to disagree with your assessment about their oranges and apples.  I have looked many times at the citrus fruits and apples there and have always found them to look past their prime.

In JH, I have found nice apples only on a handful of occasions.  Once I stumbled upon a huge crate of local NY grown apples that had just been delivered to Super Compare Foods, and those were great.  A few weeks ago I bought a nice one at the Growing Farm across from Met Foods (but when I passed by a week later thinking about getting more, they didn't look so great anymore).  And a few times I've gotten nice ones at Met Foods and Mango Rico.  I bought several on one trip to the JH farmers market this past fall and ended up making applesauce instead of eating them raw because not one was crisp.

Oranges don't seem to be as much of a problem, but I would agree that even those can be hit or miss.  I just don't happen to think that Astoria's markets have better produce, and over the past few years I have been to several markets there that were supposed to have good produce according to chowhound or yelp or astorians, or whatever I was reading at the time.

It seems like good fruit is hard to find.  I think going to places with a very high turnover helps some.
