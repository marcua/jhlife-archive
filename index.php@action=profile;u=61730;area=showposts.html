Neighborhood Chat / Re: Why Did You *Actually* Move Here?
« on: July 09, 2018, 05:56:59 PM »
We lived in Gramercy Park for 23 years in a shoebox of an apartment, but an under market rent. When the building got sold they raised the rent and after searching in Manhattan and eventually Long Island City; the latter is where we landed for 3+ years. We got so much more for our money there, a city view, doorman, gym. Alas, it was time to buy and after looking at several places in the Long Island City area and Astoria, where we placed a back up offer on a 1 bed new construction condo, which fell through, then told that they'd accept it after the 1st offer fell through, we said no, after reconsidering price versus size. In general, in both LIC and Astoria, apartments were very expensive for what you didn't get, closet space and square footage. Enter Jackson Heights. We ended up buying a 1000 sq ft co op a year ago; with a walk in closet and a second large closet in the master bedroom, plus a second bedroom, for much less then where we had been looking. The transportation options are great, also a consideration for us. I knew little about Jackson Heights before looking to buy here, but I love the diversity, markets and restaurants. I also enjoy walking the neighborhood and those surrounding us to see what I can see.
