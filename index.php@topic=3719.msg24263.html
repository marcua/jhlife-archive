« on: November 27, 2009, 03:12:46 PM »
Hey readers,

A recent survey from all Community Boards in Queens showed that Jackson Heights (and districts to the west) ranked among the dirtiest streets in Queens, while communities east of Flushing Meadows Park are the cleanest. There is also more crime here on the west side, it's much more ethnically diverse, and the population is younger on average. But on the east, suicide is a TOP 5 cause of death (according to NYC department of health).

This is the trend since, at least, this year.

Any thoughts on why "suicide" might be more prevalent on the cleaner streets east of Jackson Heights?

Thanks,
