Warning: BEDBUGS at 70-35 Broadway - infestation is massive and neglected
« on: August 22, 2008, 06:30:18 AM »
Hi folks,

I'm in the market for a studio or 1 br apartment.   Yesterday, I went to check out a co-op apartment at 70-35 Broadway.   As tenants leave/are evicted, the sponsor is renovating the apts and offering them for sale.

I saw a studio apartment that had yet to be renovated.  According to the super, the tenant had been evicted three weeks ago.  Soon after I stepped in, I was horrified to see a boxspring covered in bedbug fecal matter. Then, I noticed the walls & ceilings were covered in big splotches of  black-peppery smears - more bedbug waste matter.  There were also clusters of spindly fiber-like things all over the walls. Nests?  All signs indicated to this having been a a massive infestation.   With an infestation this size, no way was this infestation confined to this one unit.  This monster has spread to other rooms, other apartments.

How do I know this?  I survived a battle with bedbugs back in 2004.  While I'd never seen signs of such a huge infestation in person before, I've read info from public health guides, blogs and sought out pictures on the internet so I can reduce the risk of ever having to go through such an ordeal again.

I immediately said, "Bedbugs."  The super, who was showing me the space, was sheepish at first.  But after I identified the huge markings on the boxspring and the walls, he admitted it and said the bedbug problem had been really bad.   I bolted out of the apartment.   As I made my escape through the building, I got the following info out of the super:

1/  there would be no exterminator hired.  The super says he knows what he's doing.  Ha!  He had applied kerosene.  Kerosene!  Is that even legal??  Or effective or safe?  The apartment's been "treated" by this super and there are still meadows of bedbug fecal matter and bedbug evidence in there.  This shoddy approach means bedbugs are probably never eliminated once they get into an apartment.

2/  "Most apartments don't have bedbugs."  Um ... that implies that a sizable minority of units like 49% could be infested.  That's assuming he's telling the truth in the first place.

If you're looking for an apartment, watch out for 70-35 Broadway.  They're advertising a studio, a 2 bedroom and a 1 bedroom on craigslist these days.  Watch out for contact name Andrew and a (516) area code number.  The ad describes the apt as being on Broadway between 74th St and 69th St.

Like their ads say, they are renovating these apartments.  A cosmetic facelift does nothing though to get rid of bedbugs, and it doesn't keep bedbugs from other parts of the building entering your space.  Bedbugs can inhabit the nicest "cleanest" apartments. 

In general, the building's common areas are in disrepair and extremely unpleasant.  I should've trusted my first instinct in the lobby to flee, but I thought I'd keep an open mind and follow through with the appointment.   I'm pissed that they put me at risk of having a bedbug hitch a ride on me back home.

Sorry for the long post.  I want to make sure that people understand I'm coming from a sincere place and credible place.  I don't want someone starting out in home ownership (just like me) to invest their hard-earned dollars in such a foul place.

I just wanted to note that because the bedbug problem is spreading, there are plenty of hacks that have companies who will supposedly get rid of the pests.  Like any other home issue, do your research and get a referral before hiring anyone. 

Also note that there are a few companies that have an advertising tactic of joining forums like ours under different usernames.  They have that "person" (username) pretend to be a satisfied client of the company in order to persuade people on a grassroots level to use their services.

So watch out for that tactic too.

There's a website  http://bedbugregistry.com/  that lets people report bedbug sightings & infestations.  The site is run by a private individual - not the city.  Anyone can search to see whether an address has been reported. 

It's very helpful but you can't rely on it 100%.  If you see an address reported as having bedbugs, I'd believe it. But even if an address has zero reports on the site, that doesn't guarantee 100% that it's bedbug-free. :-(

Most people with bedbugs don't file reports on that site. Even if you report an address, the site admin might not accept the report. For instance, twice, I tried to report the bedbug infestation that I saw at 70-35 Bway on the registry. Neither of those reports were published on that site, and the address is still listed as having no bedbug encounters reported.
