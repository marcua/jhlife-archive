You are welcome. When I was buying, I didn't have a choice to have my own broker (see the discussion at http://www.jacksonheightslife.com/community/index.php?topic=8054.0).  As there is one major broker in jackson heights who does not allow co-brokering  and tends to price the home higher than the other brokers (when you are looking for the apartment for a while, you will know who I am talking about), but this broker gets the inventory. I ended up buying a coop with this broker. In general, my dealing with him seems relatively smoothly. This broker is professional and you are his customer after all.  But don't expect him to get a good price for you as he is exclusively working for the seller.

I personally don't expect that a buyer's agent is going to help me to get a good price as his primary purpose to have a deal. If the buyer's agent deems it too difficult to negotiate a good price for the buyer, he will loose the interest to work for you.
Here is another thread where a broker asks the seller to consider co-brokering (http://www.jacksonheightslife.com/community/index.php?topic=8051.0). I don't think this broker is successful to convince the seller to do this as evidenced by the fact many good apartments were sold by the above mentioned no co-brokering agency. I sort of understand from the seller's point of view (The person who can get me a better price and sells faster will get to sell my apartment, co-broking or no co-broking is none of my business).

Anyway, after you are looking for the apartment for a while, you will have a good feeling how to price the apartment correctly. acris is good source to check the price history.

Apartments, Sublets & Roommates / Re: Birchwood House

As I just bought a coop recently, my experience told me that you can offer your price you are comfortable with and then during the contract signing time, get your time to do the due diligence. Like reading the financial documents and the board meeting minutes, if you find something you are uncomfortable with, you are free to back out. You probably have to pay several hundred dollars for the attorney. Some attorneys will not charge you unless you have signed the contract.

Of course, if the broker/seller is helpful, ask them to give you the building' financials for the past five years. A lot of them are unwilling to give those unless you give an offer price. The financial statement gives you full picture of what is going on.
