 Clear glass can help to visually enlarge small bathrooms, and if you have a nice tile design in it can to be viewed from elsewhere in the room.  The cost depends...are you redoing the entire shower area or just replacing the door.  Is the opening plum or do you have to repair it.

If you are simply replacing the door and the area is square than you have to figure about $300.  If your area is not square and more work is to be done than you should ask your local contractor take a look at it or even Home Depot may be able to supply a list of qualified contractors.

I like classic timeless bathrooms.  Most especially in pre-war apartments.  The sponsor who I purchased my apartment from unfortunately gutted the entire bathroom and installed a new bathroom in a beige / taupe color scheme.  While this is nice, neutral and in style, I can't help but think it will be out of style in another ten years (I am already tired of it by the way).  I wish that when he renovated the bathroom they would have done something more in keeping with the pre-war feel i.e. white subway tile, maybe with black accents, marble, brushed nickel, octagonal tile, pedestal sink etc....  I love my apartment but if I had the extra money, I would re-do the bathroom.  You can't go wrong with simple, classic and elegant.  You can always change the look slightly with accessories, towels, candles etc... to suit ones changing tastes.

@cl4t: the previous owner of my apartment installed a faucet on the wall in the middle of the bathtub, in addition to the tub faucet. It seems to me that in my building at least, in terms of plumbing, it was doable because the pipes run exactly where the new faucet was installed. But at a certain point they disconnected this faucet and went back to the old tub faucet.

Pros: there is a standing shower (which I don't particularly care about but many prefer this option, like my SO)

Cons: 1. currently, the faucet is not recessed into the wall, so I constantly hit myself on it; 2. currently, the shower is simply sticking out of the wall and would splatter directly onto the shower curtain. We plan to get a shower that extends out a little bit so that water falls down, rather than out toward the shower curtain. 

Hope this helps.
