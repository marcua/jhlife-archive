Community Groups, Activities & Events / How much hotter would JH be without trees?
« on: August 03, 2012, 10:26:17 PM »
Hello all,

Think how much hotter Jackson Heights would be without trees. So hot that you'd be barely be able to drag yourself home from work. So hot that you'd need to run your AC longer to cool down your home. And let's not even talk about more polluted, noisier and uglier. That's why we need your help at two events in August.

JHBG's Tree LC program keeps our existing trees healthy and is getting new street trees planted. It'spart of JHBG's Green Agenda, a plan to make Jackson Heights more environmentally sustainable by iinviting Mother Nature back to the city.

We have a couple of events where we need your help:

Saturday, Aug. 11, 10 a.m. - 37th Avenue between 73rd and 74th Streets. A full tree-pit overhaul for  five or six compacted tree pits - that means removing bricks, adding compost, mulch and then watering.

Sunday, Aug. 26, 10 a.m. -  Site to be determined. Similar deal as Aug. 11.

So what have we done so far? We've created a pilot project garden along the 78th Street Plaza. We are continuing to add drought-resistant plants.

Our tree-pit to tree-garden project, Tree LC, most recently did major work on five tree pits on 78th Street north of 37th Avenue. We will return to these tree to prune them before summer is over.
We did another six trees in June on 37th Avenue from 76th Street to 75th Street.
