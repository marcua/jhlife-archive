 Not much green grass here for a number of reasons. We have lot's of shade from our mature trees in JH which is not
conducive to thick lush lawns. Sustainability is another factor. Green lawns are costly!! One co-op now has the lawn
roped off for weeks so it can be reseeded which has to be done regularly and annoys people who don't know about
lawn care. Long Island is full of green lawns but also on any given day, crews with mowers and blowers and
various chemicals used to manicure those patches of green are present. Public spaces that have money in NYC usually
have green carpet looking lawns with Central Park being the star. Flushing Meadows lawns are actually well-maintained
only one time of year from what I see and that is for the US Open in August. Otherwise, they are pretty only from afar.

 The upshot is that a lush green lawn in a dense public area of NYC requires substantial maintenance, lots of rules and
serious funding. NYC parks is going the other direction in new plantings with really pretty, easy care perennials that
come back each year on their own. While these are beautiful, they are not the rolling lawn experience you ask about
for kids.
