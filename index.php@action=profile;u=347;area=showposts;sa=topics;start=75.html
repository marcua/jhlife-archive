I am a Jackson Heights Lifer:

1)  I lived on 81st Street, next to the library, from birth until age 3 1/2 ...

2)  Then I lived on 76th Street (in Hawthrone Court), from 3 1/2 until I was 20 ... (although I left for college (Stony Brook, GO PATRIOTS!) at 15, and did summers at a camp I worked at upstate, it was still my residence during college)

3)  Then I moved in with my childhood friend until I was 23, in the rental apartment on 35th Avenue (Between 75th and 76th Streets) he grew up in  ...

4)  Until I bought my own co-op (80th Street, the Greystones) just before I turned 24 (back then, you could get one so cheap I wish now I'd bought 5!).  Stayed there after I got married, until a few years ago ...

5)  When we moved to Dunolly Gardens (79th Street) a year before Luc was born (also at a price that now folks can only dream of!) until now, present day (I just turned 39!).

So I'm a Lifer I guess ... 5 apartments, 1 neighborhood.  Anyone else

Neighborhood Chat / Halloween for Kids in Jackson Heights
« on: October 25, 2008, 11:09:17 AM »
    It's come a long way since the '70s when we just rang all the bells to get into a building and trick or treated!     But teh world has chanmged, our kids need safe fun on Halloween!

    Last night, we joined a large contigent of kids and families at P.S. 69's Halloween party.  Outside of too long a wait on the pop corn line, the event was great, but there were only like 150 kids there.  My son had a blast, as did his pre-K alumni friends (he "dated" a girl from his old class, as they danced all night) and his new kinder friends, but there were maybe only 15 kindergartreners there.  In a school with 9 kindergartens alone, it got me thinking that these great events need some hype here.

    I figured we could share the public parties info on here (in case parents don't know).  Obviously, only the public ones would work (for example, our co-op has one Sunday for just the kids in the co-op, so no point hyping that), but for instance I know that St Joan of Arc has one tonight (Saturday), as we have tickets.

   Of course, no one needs to hype the big parade Friday night (we have a pack we seem to be travelling with for that!), but maybe there are other schools and/or churches/mosques/synagogues with events?  Anyone?

Neighborhood Chat / Parent Coordinator Workshops
« on: October 23, 2008, 01:25:20 AM »

     The other night I checked out the first of this year's batch of Parent Coordinator run workshops at PS 69.  (As a teacher, I've been to many in my school, even presented at a few, but it was fun to go as 'just a parent'.)

     I must say I was very impressed with the topic (Multiple Intelligences) and turnout (about 80 parents).  I even thought of this bulletin board when they talked about the famous PS 69 alumni (Lucy Liu).

     Today my own school's tireless parent coordinator ran a workshop (though only 10 parents showed ... special ed schools tend to have travel issues community schools don't:  my own class has three kids whose loacls chool is 69, but are bussed to Floral park for their specific school needs).

    I logged on tonight with the hope of increasing parent awareness.  I recommend to any parents to check out these parent coordinator workshops the public schools offer.  It really is one of the few totally positive changes in the school system the past few years.
