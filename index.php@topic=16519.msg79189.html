A few months back, a young lady had her hand on the car horn for so long that I was able to wait for my elevator, go down 5 floors, walk outside past two stores and see who was making all the noise. She said she was trying to reach her husband and that his phone wasn't working. Had she not thought of walking up to his place of work and knocking on the door?

I think the honking issue has a lot to do with respect for others. If I am driving at night, I always assume people are sleeping so I don't honk unless it's vital. People these days must think it's the NYC thing to do, to honk whenever they get stuck behind a car or when they want to grab someone's attention. I don't understand this! But what REALLY bugs me are the ignorant drivers who sit behind others honking and honking and honking when clearly the cars in front of them can't move (due to an emergency vehicle in the way, a delivery truck unloading, a person crossing the road etc.). Drivers (and everyone else) need to understand that the world does not revolve around them. Have some goddamn patience!
