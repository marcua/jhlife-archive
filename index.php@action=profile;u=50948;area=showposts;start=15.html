Neighborhood Chat / Re: LaGuardia Central Terminal Overhaul
« on: June 15, 2016, 07:03:47 PM »
Unsure about the future. In the past few years, about 12 bad Saturdays per year seems right. How bad? Like what you heard during that day of the garden tour is about as bad as it gets. On the very worst day I saw, it started around 6am, woke me up. Went on all Saturday long. (Don't think it ever happens on Sundays.)

One issue that bothers me the most is that the runway work tends to happen in the warm months of the year, exactly when you want to be outside and enjoy that nice garden that's luring you here.

Last year those 12 bad days happened from April to October, more or less. So it sounds hellish just when you want to enjoy life the most. Then, when it gets cold, you get no noise for a long time, nearly forget about it.

I find the noise deeply annoying on the bad days. I can't overstate my upset. But I also find Jackson Heights the most awesome place that I have ever lived, despite that. An incredible mix of people, nice vibe, close to the city, decent schools. I got a good price when I came a few years ago and I am happy about the decision. But boy that noise is a bitch.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Riding your bike on the street 101
« on: May 22, 2016, 01:18:12 PM »
I think about the same for pedestrians, wouldn't you agree? For example, if you are walking and stop at a red light, no cars or bikes coming, do you wait for a green light to cross? I don't. And, when/if you drive, do you sometimes go above the speed limit, even if slightly? I do.

The problem is not that the a cyclist crosses a red light in a situation similar to what a pedestrian would. (Truth is: very rarely you have a cyclist hurting anyone.) The problem is if a cyclist (or a pedestrian or a driver) don't respect others on the road...

I am totally with you here: bikes can go pretty fast and make very little noise, so they can startle. Then, even if they don't hurt, they can scare and upset you. This is probably a big reason why some people don't like cyclists.

But this is no reason to single out bikers more than you would the other crazy people who make transit in this city sometimes dangerous or upsetting (including pedestrians)... In the end, they are pretty harmless. We would all benefit for more education and common sense on the streets.
