Who said eating every two weeks at the Arepa Lady will kill you? For most people, it won't.

But guess what? Some people actually do have health conditions that might make eating fatty/carby arepas however often a very bad choice. And why would it be such a bad idea for a restaurant like that to add some options that feature more vegetables and fruit and no cheese?

And why would it be such a bad idea for people from cultures who, yes, prefer the heavy, fatty, carby food they grew up with---and who do suffer from health consequences and obesity, just look around you or check out a local medical office--to branch out to, yes, "healthier" food?

You seem to suggest that the notion of some food being "healthy" while other food is not is "utterly ridiculous." Again--you are simply wrong. I see no reason why local restaurants can't get with the program and offer healthier options--as so many chain restaurants are now doing, eg, Sweetgreens, Digg Inn, and many others, including those based on various world cuisines who have a healthier take on those foods. And no I don't expect rice paper wrapped kale, etc. at the Arepa Lady--just a nod to different food needs in the form of lighter and yes, I still maintain, "healthier" options. News flash: some foods really are healthier than others--suggest you keep up with modern medical research.

This to me is the same as people moving into a neighborhood and wanting it to change to accommodate them and their tastes.

If you want healthy then don’t eat at arepa lady when she opens again. Same reason you don’t go to digg inn for a greasy cheeseburger. The reason you go there is for an unbelievable arepa, not an arepa salad.
