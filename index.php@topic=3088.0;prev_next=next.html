It is almost jersey fried tomato season!  I'll fry up some jersey tomatoes 3 times a week for the next couple of months.  These are my two favorite preparations:

Slice thickly, and chop up the little ends.  Coat the slices in white flour.  Fry in corn oil until slightly brown.  Put a little salt and pepper on each slice.  Take the slices out, and toss in the chopped up little pieces of tomato.  Stir them around, and add a little more oil if needed.  Then add milk, turn down the heat, and smoosh up the little pieces until the sauce turns pink.  Pour the sauce over the fried slices.  Yummmy!

Or

Slice thickly.  Coat in egg, flour and Italian bread crumbs.  Fry in olive oil.  Transfer to a baking pan.  Put a fresh basil leaf and a slice of mozzerella on each slice, and bake in the oven until the cheese melts.  Enjoy!!!!

A note on jersey tomatoes.  They are ugly.  The are shaped funny, and are often uneven.  They have yellow and sometimes even greenish spots on them.  They sometimes have odd lines that look like seams on the side.  If you didn't know, you might walk away from them.  But trust me.  There is no other frying tomato that comes close.  And the season is short.  I look forward to it all year.

I made a lovely dish with the sugar snap peas that have been in season. It's a sugar snap pea pesto on pasta. It was delicious.

I got it from this website: http://www.madisonfoodie.com/recipes/sugar-snap-peas-and-pasta/ and they got it from Gourmet magazine

But the gist is...

Cook a pint of the (ends trimmed) peas (pods and all) in salted boiling water for 2 minutes. Remove about a cup of the peas and plunge into ice water to stop cooking. Cook the remaining peas until well cooked and soft- about 5 more minutes. Reserve pea water for cooking pasta. Move the well cooked peas into a food processor. Reserve a 1/3 a cup of water for pesto.

Bring the water back to a boil and add pasta.

While pasta is cooking, and add herbs (I used mint and basil), garlic, olive oil (and if necessary a little bit of the reserved water) to the peas in the food processor.  Process until vaguely pesto consistency, maybe a little thinner.

When the pasta is done, mix the pesto, blanched peas and pasta together. It's light and flavorful and I was happy with a new way to use sugar snap peas.
