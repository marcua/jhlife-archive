« Reply #61 on: March 09, 2010, 11:03:25 PM »
Got this email today. The three candidates will be attending a forum tomorrow at the Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual and Trans Community Center in Queens. As per the email, people are asked to be respectful. To which I say...I'll be as respectful as Monseratte has been to our community.

--------
New York State 13th Senate District Special Elections Forum on LGBT Equality
La comunidad LGBT alza la voz

March 10, 2010
7:00pm - 8:30pm
Location: Queens Pride House
76-11 37th Ave., Suite 206, Jackson Heights, Queens (Map)

What kind of change do you want to see for the LGBT community of Western Queens? What pressing issues affect you? Come bring these ideas to a forum with political candidates and local activists!

 In connection with the upcoming special election for the New York State Senate 13th District Seat, the LGBT Equality Coalition of Queens is organizing a forum to discuss the status of the LGBT community, particularly of Western Queens.

The forum will focus on the following four major issues: responses to hate crimes against LGBT Queens residents, police-community relations, funding for health and social services in the district, and LGBT-related legislative initiatives.

Candidates attending will be Jose Peralta, Robert Beltrani and Hiram Monserrate.
Please show respect when attending the forum.

Hello everyone,

Just got back from Special Elections Forum on LGBT Equality at the Diversity Center in Jackson Heights were Jose Peralta joined the small group in attendance.

I was there from 7:15 until 8:15 p.m.  I don't know if the other two candidates arrived after my departure.

It was a very well organized and informative forum, although there were few in attendance.

Jose was well received and the questions were direct, and his responses were detailed.

I do have to mention that at one point I felt like the responses from Jose were geared towards heterosexual families. The moderator had to redirect Jose and question him on what his position would be in the case of a gay or lesbian family.  The topic at that point included immigration and the possibility of separation of family, which is a very real and painful discussion within the community.

Jose indicated he has been working and will continue to work very hard for the rights of all.
