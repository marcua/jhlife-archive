Freddie Mac buys mortgages on the secondary market, pools them together and finally sells them as mortgage-backed securities to investors on the open market - this is old news.

Then the tax laws changed in 1986 creating basically today's sub-prime market.  If regulators had been empowered to oversee the rapid increase of properties prices and these mortgage companies and how they were doing business.  If the big name journalists had paid more attention and reported on the increase of defaults.  Maybe just maybe the pain wouldn't have been felt so deep.  This of course is part of it and it cannot explain the Commercial loan defaults.  In fact, when you look closely at the Commercial mortgages it would literally put to question the generally accepted belief that predatory lenders went after minorities and unsophisticated costumers.   

Commercial mortgages are also in big trouble but no one seems to be talking much about the 55% of loans that might default within the next 5 years.  Well, back to Fannie and Freddie in a few but first...You have the critique that blames the homeowners and you have the other side largely placing the blame on Wall Street.  Well, what essentially developed was a mass social mania (not too far from mass hysteria if you ask me after a few drinks...hehe). The homeowners are also partially to blame.  Everyone is to blame, there are no innocent parties and the Biggest to blame is probably the Federal Reserve.  There is a law in the books known as the Homeowner or equity Protection Act (HOEPA).  Congress basically requested the FR to regulate the sub-prime market since only the FR had the power to do so.  They never did and therefore you had Wall street run buck wild with the secondary market.  Clinton tried even to the point to go after the worst offenders but he was more interested in his 1996 reelection campaigning on home ownership.  (That's a whole different thread).  You see, our own government is the real cause or the toxin that allowed the decease to take hold (Wall St).
