News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I'm sorry to see that the large store with all the fun mannequins outside it is going out of business.  There are two stores next to it, that are also vacant as well as a few across the street.  They have for months now.  I have a sinking feeling we'll only get more chain outlet stores to replace them because they're the only ones who can afford the high rents.  The interesting thing to note is the tacit acknowledgement of these chains that the area cannot afford these high prices so they only put outlets out here, but since they are chains they can afford the rent.   The mannequin store brought 82nd Street character and I'm sad to see it go.  But I have watched it happen all over Manhattan, and then Astoria, and now I'll watch it happen here.  Vanishing NY indeed.  Oh well.

I agree that there's a certain loss if 82nd Street becomes all national chains, but it's not something that I personally will cry over.

This is just my little opinion, but many of the stores that have left 82nd Street, and some that are still there, just don't sell anything that I would be interested in purchasing.  The store that the original poster speaks of is one of them.  I personally like the fact that BR opened, even if it's an outlet.  I know that if I need a shirt at the last minute, I can duck in there and get something of good quality for a good price in a store that is well organized and designed.  Call me picky, but part of the shopping experience is having a welcoming environment and many stores in our neighborhood are poorly planned, have little space to move around in and generally speaking, don't eliminate inventory that just does not sell - making a crowded store even more crowded.

I grew up in Astoria.  There were mom and pop stores all over, and there still are a few left (and some that open all the time).  Some of the stores that closed are missed, but that's because they actually were well stocked, nicely planned out, and had sales people that knew what they were doing.  If these little stores were the same, I would agree that I would miss them. 

I'd like to say that some of the stuff carried is generally cheap, but as anyone that walks into UNIQLO can attest to, cheap clothes can be sold in a location that actually looks good and inviting.
