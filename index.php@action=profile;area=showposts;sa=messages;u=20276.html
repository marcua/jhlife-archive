Saw Horizon III  (69-14 41st Avenue) in Woodside and it definitely has more of the "luxury" feel than Plaza 75 and Bravo. The lobby is very nice, outdoor common area is great (with fountains, nice seating areas and grills for bbq), the elevator is made of glass, and it comes with a gym on the top floor. All units are sold in the past year except 2 two bedrooms on the 2nd floor now. Even with a large bbq area and rooftop gym, the monthly maintenance is still cheaper than Plaza 75. In addition, Horizon's price/ sq. foot is about the same compared to Plaza 75 even though Horizon is better built and includes storage for all units, so I'm surprised that Plaza 75 is not discounting. (Josh.Lin, when you talk about "comparables", could you be more specific as to which buildings you are referring to other than Plaza 75 and Bravo?). On the other hand, this may explain why Plaza 75 is not even 50% sold after 2 years. The only thing missing in Horizon iii is a doorman but given the small building (only 27 units), it's probably not worth it.

Another question for those familiar with the neighborhood, do you see "luxury" mid-size to large condo/co-op buildings popping up more frequently nowadays? Lastly, did you see real estate price dropping drastically back in 2008-2009 in this neighborhood? Has the price come back up to the 2006-2007 levels here? I recently learned that people paid almost $600/foot in Elmhurst area for condos back in 2007 and took a huge hit in 2009. Thank you!!

Thank you all in advance. i understand that these questions can be tough, whatever insight you have will be much appreciated.
