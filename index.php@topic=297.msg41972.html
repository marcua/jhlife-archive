New York Daily News
Fairway to heaven: Queens residents anxiously await opening of 55,000 square-foot food oasis
BY Lisa L. Colangelo
DAILY NEWS STAFF WRITER
Tuesday, October 4th 2011, 4:00 AM

In a few short weeks, the gutted former Waldbaum's in Douglaston will be transformed into a food oasis.

Fairway is set to open its first Queens store on Nov. 16 and, at 55,000-square feet, will be its largest in the five boroughs.

Owner Howie Glickberg said shoppers will be greeted with rows of fresh produce when they walk through the doors of the new store, currently under construction at the Douglaston Plaza shopping center off the Long Island Expressway.

"We are recreating what people had 30 or 40 years ago when they had their own neighborhood butcher, fish and produce stores," Glickberg told the Daily News during a tour of the construction site yesterday. "We even roast our own coffee. We're doing it the old-fashioned way."

Community Board 11 Chairman Jerry Iannece said the community has been "anxiously awaiting" the opening of the store.

He pointed out that residents who live in the area, especially seniors and people without cars, have had to travel long distances to buy basic groceries.

"This was unacceptable," he said.

The store will offer a large selection of organic food as well as Kosher meat and poultry, Glickberg said. Bread, pastry and other items also will be baked on site, he said.

Shoppers can also take a break in the 60-seat cafe.

Glickberg said Fairway, which has eight stores in the metro area - including Manhattan, Brooklyn and Long Island - has staked its reputation on providing high quality at low prices.

"We have 60 different kinds of olives, over 600 different kinds of cheese," he said, walking through the future deli section, as construction workers buzzed around him.

Plans for the borough's first Fairway have been in the works for over two years. Transfer of the property was slow and there were some minor zoning issues to contend with.

Fairway also did a top-to-bottom renovation of the former Waldbaum's building, which included new infrastructure and a 15,000-foot expansion.

"We have tremendous support from the community," said Glickberg. "People are telling us there are no good food stores around here. They just want a Fairway."
