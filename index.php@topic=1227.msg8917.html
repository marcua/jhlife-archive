Hey all,

My friend Brian and I live in the Heights and run a NY Times Crossword blog at http://crosswords.ryanfacestheworld.com/.  We are planning to hold our first ever puzzlehead event in the neighborhood on August 23rd.  We're calling it Lollapuzzoola 08.  Does anybody have any suggestions on where we could hold this event?  We figure it will be at the most 30 people.  And we'd need a place where we could bring in food.  Nothing major.  Just chips, oreos, drink, etc.  We'd probably need it for 4 or 5 hours.  We're open to church basements, community centers and similar venues.  Any suggestions would be greatly appreciated.

Once we nail down the details I'll post it here as we'd love to have some people from this board attend.

Thanks so much,
ryan

This should be great fun.  Possible venues to contact: the Garden School, St. Mark's Church, the Community Church, and the Renaissance School.

In August, I certainly understand if you feel an indoor location would be preferable.  But if you're open to the idea of an outdoor location, and are willing to shift your event to a Sunday, you may want to consider holding it at the 78th Street Play Street.  The organizers are open to proposals from people who want to hold public events and activities on the street.  Crosswords could be the next spectator sport!

I haven't seen all of the rooms shown there, but the Community Church can be quite a hub of activity in the evenings, as many groups hold meetings there.  It's at 81-10 35th Avenue.  It's a good match for your event because it is the church where Scrabble originated.  Your best bet may be just to visit and inquire at the front desk.

The Renaissance School also seems to be open to hosting events.  There's a big folk music festival held there every year.
