News:

Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

I can't say that I'm opposed.  As the poster above noted, that's not the historic district, and there's nothing to prevent a high rise from going in there. 

I sympathize with those people who live in or around 74th Street, I really do.  I've lived here for over 13 years and I can count on one hand (maybe one finger) the number of times that I have driven on 73rd, 74th or 75th streets.  I avoid them like the plague, because I see how I can walk so much faster than any car can drive there.  But that was a known factor even when I was buying my apartment, which is why when a broker offered to show us something on those streets, I immediately said I'm not interested.

The fact is that area permits this kind of development.  The parking garage may actually help things as some people will opt to pay for parking rather than circle the area for 1/2 hour.  And frankly, the building is better than what's there now.
