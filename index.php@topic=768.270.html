At Monday night's meeting the powers that be (Port Authority and FAA) indicated that the takeoffs over Jackson Heights occur when the main runway is down for construction or repairs, or perhaps weather issues, as it might have been this morning.  The runway used for takeoffs over Jackson Heights is typically used for landings, the ones that descend over Woodside and then East Elmhurst.  The main runway mostly flies over Flushing, and they will likely bear the brunt of the NexGen "improvements."   

In "normal" years, the takeoffs over JH were usually limited to about 6 weekends.  This year - like about five years ago or so - the number of weekends seems to have doubled.  Hopefully the frequency will return to the "normal" level in 2014.  Although I left before the meeting ended, I don't think they plan to permanently re-route takeoffs over us, but we certainly need to stay vigilant.  The good news is that it seems as if the Port Authority and FAA realize that they can no longer proceed without input from the surrounding communities.

I live in East Flushing, and have been dealing with the same issue (NextGen) over the past two years. It is heartbreaking to see this destructive airline practice suck the life out of my neighborhood. People have started wearing those big ATC headphones in the park. I live 6.5 miles from the LGA runway axis (by flushing cemetery)and it is still awful. Kids can't grow up normally in this environment now.

Folks, this all about increasing airport capacity. LGA is at maximum output when JH gets arrivals on R-4/22 while Flushing/Bayside gets departures on R-13/31. This formation alleviates congestion at the runway axis and can be implemented anytime winds are below a certain threshold (which is just about every summer day). When winds pick up out of the northwest in the fall, we start getting a few breaks. Flushing gets the low impact arrivals over FMCP, and departures go out over the river, thus avoiding JH. The airlines hate this, as it reduces capacity and causes delays.

Check out PASSUR.com to verify. It tracks flights in real time on a map. West, Northwest and Southwest winds above 10 knots are our friend.

Also, there is a new airport advocacy group called the Global Gateway Alliance. It is funded by Jared Kushner and Joe Sitt, real estate tycoons. They have already lobbied the FAA to avoid studying NextGen and have no trouble swaying reporters and journalists exactly what they wish to have written. They cite "NextGen" advocacy as there primary mission. They are very slick, and could care less about Queens.
