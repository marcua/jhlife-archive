You're also misreading the Delta release.

Delta increased its flights by 60% because it took over most of US Airways' operations at the airport. The increase in Delta flights was accompanied by a reduction in US Airways flights. Delta in exchange gave a bunch of DCA slots to US Airways. Delta and US Airways TRADED slots, they didn't ADD slots.

The increased noise we're hearing over JH is because of rerouted flight paths, not many more aggregate  flights.

I have my suspicious about this too.  It doesn't make sense that Bayside residents started complaining last year (2012) about the new system, and only now in late 2013 are the residents of Jackson Heights, Woodside, Elmhurst, Maspeth, etc. beginning to feel the effects of the changes and starting to complain to their elected officials.

Here is an article about Bayside in 2012 http://www.timesledger.com/stories/2012/41/airlinepushback_ne_2012_10_11_q.html

and Elmhurst/Maspeth  just recently in 2013 http://www.timesledger.com/stories/2012/41/airlinepushback_ne_2012_10_11_q.html

However, it seems that the group Queens Quiet Skies, which I believe is based in Bayside, is committed to quieting ALL of Queens skies, and they according to the Maspeth article above, they are advising all communities on how to address this.

It's not just Queens that's bearing the brunt of the new system.  Some large parts of Brooklyn are also getting the increased noise.  There is a sister group, Prospect Park Quiet Skies , that is also addressing these issues.  I have seen complaints from Park Slope all the way to Bath Beach.

Hopefully if all communities band together with their elected officials, everyone stands a higher chance at fighting this.
