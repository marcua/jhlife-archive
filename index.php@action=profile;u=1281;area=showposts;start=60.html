Neighborhood Chat / Re: Finally, a Community Center!!!
« on: June 14, 2021, 05:03:55 PM »
Quote from: itsit on May 27, 2021, 12:35:31 PM
Other communities in Queens have enviable places for year long sports, classes, all ages meetups and general community meetings. Here are just a few of the facilities that are some miles away and have full season use of indoor (and some outdoor) spaces that Jackson Heights does not. The question is are we in JH undeserving?? Who has the momentum to change this??

 - Sunnyside Community Services  43-31 39th Street Sunnyside, New York sysny.org

 - Maspeth Town Hall 53-37 72nd Street Maspeth, NY 11378 maspethtownhall.org/

 - Long Island City Y 32-23 Queens Blvd. LIC ymcanyc.org/locations/long-island-city-ymca

 - Ridgewood Y 69-02 64th Street Ridgewood, NY ymcanyc.org/locations/ridgewood-ymca

 - Cross Island Y  238-10 Hillside Ave. Bellerose, NY  ymcanyc.org/locations/cross-island-ymca

YMCAs are religious organizations with expensive dues, hardly community centers.  You could get involved with the St Joan of Arc Youth Council, Men's Club, Ladies Club, etc. or any of the organizations sponsored by Community Church, like Scouting, if you're looking to get involved with religious organizations in JH.  You don't mention the Y in Forest Hills, so I assume you were just listing a few.

The Parks Department has Lost Battalion Hall in Rego Park and the pool in Flushing Meadow Park, which have lower membership fees, particularly for seniors.

A real community center would be expensive, probably in line with what a small school costs these days.  Maybe if it was combined with a senior center and public health facilities, federal funding could be incorporated.  I can't imagine where it could be built, though.  When I saw the subject, I thought maybe this had already been announced.  We can dream, though.

Neighborhood Chat / Petition started for "compromise" on 34th Ave Open Streets
« on: May 03, 2021, 02:46:13 PM »
This past week, the City Council approved making the Open Streets program permanent. Since the mayor suggested this originally, I think it's safe to predict he will sign this. Streetsblog is reporting on a petition to compromise on creating a permanent green space on 34th Ave. The "compromise" is long on promise but short on details. Everyone will get everything, but it doesn't appear obvious to me who will make these things happen. The article is at https://nyc.streetsblog.org/2021/05/03/the-compromise-new-open-streets-group-seeks-linear-park-for-jackson-heights and the petition is at  https://www.34avelinearpark.com/
The article quotes a supporter who says that tens of thousands of people use the open street each day. If that is not an exaggeration, then by all means support the petition! I haven't seen any real count of how many different people use 34th Ave, but if there is one please post a link so I can educate myself.

Whatever happens, someone has to prevent delivery scooters and electric bicycles from using this street when other vehicular traffic is prohibited. They rarely follow posted speed limits, often ignore traffic signals, and don't seem to understand that this is supposed to be a pedestrian pathway, not a short cut set up for their convenience.
