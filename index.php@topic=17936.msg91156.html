In an aggressive expansion plan, there will soon be a total of four Taco Mixes, three recently descended from the legendary closet taqueria in East Harlem on 116th Street that specializes in al pastor — intertwined pork fragments turning on a vertical spit marinated in pineapple juice. Re: New “trendy” Taco Joint in the works on 37th
« Reply #9 on: November 04, 2018, 09:58:56 PM »
When I lived in Manhattan 5+ years ago I was a huge fan of the original/only location in East Harlem.

Tried the new JH outpost of it tonight and was not disappointed!  Totally an authentic taco joint, don't be fooled by the modern/minimalist decor.

The menu has a great selection (tacos, tortas, cemitas, sopas, nachos) and they prepared everything fresh while I waited.  We got mostly al pastor tacos since that's their specialty and they were definitely the best, but we also tried the bistec (very good), carnitas (not crispy enough for my taste), and chorizo (not dry enough for my taste).  Lots of toppings available but kept it simple with onions/cilantro, limes, salsa verde, and pickled veggies (onions, peppers, carrots, broccoli, and cauliflower).

Just be warned there's no seating.  There's an L-shaped counter to stand and eat at but not conducive to eating in with kids, we did take out.
