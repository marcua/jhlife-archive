Neighborhood Chat / Re: Food Stamps in JH Supermarkets
« on: May 17, 2008, 07:29:51 PM »
Quote from: eddiestjohns on May 08, 2008, 09:11:13 PM
Does it irk anyone else when waiting to pay on a cashier line that the person in front of you who is using food stamps is eating better than you are?  Today while waiting on line the couple in front of me had some premium steaks and then paid separately with cash for a 12 pack of Heineken and a 12 pack of Corona.  Last week I was trying to get an estimate on how much the jewelry of the woman in front of me was worth.  Next time I go I think I am going to check out what vehicles if any the people are driving.  I don't mind my taxes paying for the elderly or truly needy but there seems to be a lot of abuse of the program.

Seems to be two seperate issues here.

The latter one is easier to discuss.  The woman with the jewelry?  Could be heirlooms, could be fake, could be sentimental ... I don't think being in hard times means one should hock the legacy locket she got from Nana, right?

The former though, I see your point about the beer (which you say was bought seperately, as per the existing legislation), but not the steaks.  Maybe it was for a special ocassion, or maybe that meal was going to last them for more than serving. Heck, we live a neighborhood we some of us eat out a lot (as we have a lot of great choices).  Maybe they cook at home every night as eating out is beyond their means, so why not entitle them to the treat.

Who are we to judge how people should live by the fact they use food stamps, and may be poorer than us.  It's as bad as judging by race, religion, or gender, because its done on face value observations.

Truth be told, its probably embarassing for those on assistance to have to use the food stamps in public because of just this type of scrutiny.  After all, the nosy person on line behind them is more apt to notice them paying with them than cash or plastic.

That all said though, I agree that the beer being purchased right after is disturbing, only because as I don't drink, I don't see the necessity of it, but I also admit that's my own value judgment creeping in.
