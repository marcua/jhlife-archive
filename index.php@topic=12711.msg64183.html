Third time today, so now a better review. Disclosure: I'm Brazilian.

The place is legit: it's a Brazilian-run operation, for better and for worse. It's what you'd get in a similar establishment in Brazil.

Meaning: food tastes like it should. Staff can be absent-minded, but hyper friendly. Some of the dishes served are well-known national dishes. Others are unknown to the world, because the cook has invented that day and you will never see again (same could happen in Brazil).

Main cook is from Minas Gerais (one of the best regional foods), meat guy is from the south (best Brazilian meat).

Tips:

- The buffet might be at it's best at lunch time. You gotta weigh your food before you eat.

- Get the meat. Brazilians are not vegetarian. (Yes, I generalized it, but we like generalizing it.) The meat the only thing there not seating in a buffet. Ask for picanha (a bovine cut) and eat the half-inch layer of fat. Come on, yolo.

- From the buffet: Rice, beans, farofa (that yucca flour stuff that you eat with rice and beans), beets and collard greens if they have it. This list is also your best vegetarian option. Just enjoy the little pieces of bacon or sausage that might come in  â€” you had your heart in the right place.

I'm already a loyal costumer.

I went by yesterday with the wife and we thought it was okay. Our view point is not comprehensive since we don't eat beef or pork, and only tried the chicken. But at 530p on a Sunday they only had chicken wings and sausage available at the BBQ section. The wings were tasty. The sausage was hard and bland.

The buffet options were okay, decent enough variety with their version of a chicken lasagna, roasted cauliflower, cod stew (lacking any stew), another fish dish as well other options.

As others have mentioned, you weigh your food and pay before you eat. Personally, I think they should instead have folks pay after they've completed their entire meal to encourage multiple visits to the food options. Once you've paid, you become almost psychologically resigned to being satisfied with your meal and therefore unlikely to go for seconds (or even thirds).

I didn't like that we had to eat our food on plastic plates.

They need a beer (liquor) license.
