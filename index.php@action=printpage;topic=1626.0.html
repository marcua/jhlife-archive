Post by: NYC Native on October 09, 2008, 09:23:36 AM
Shelby2, you are right on the money.  If anyone is selling a Coop you should know that no FHA loans are available at this time for Coops and no bank will finance a coop without 2007 financials (Chase is the exception with a letter and....)

If the financials are not available I wouldn't even put it on the market until you do because it will kill the deal.  Another thing to look for is to see if 10% or more of shareholders are late or defaulting on their maintenance, this will also worry the lenders.   I deal in all levels of Coops from renovation to sponsor sales and I have seeing 1 out of 3 deals die in the last few months.  If you are a seller and have not signed an agreement with a broker and don't wish to you can do an open listing and give it to a few people in the neighborhood, but they will need this info in this market so they can answer all questions that may arise.  If you don't know any of these answers it will delay your sale.  If you get a savvy buyer than I recommend you know every answer plus know the underlying mortgage and comparables as well.  Shoot me an email if you need help.

Be well and Good Luck!
