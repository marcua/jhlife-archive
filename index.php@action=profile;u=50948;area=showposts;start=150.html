Erica, I wouldn't worry about gentrification, as what happens in Brooklyn, for the reasons previously said here: established neighborhood, not really poor, high levels of ownership.

Today Jackson Heights is 60% foreign-born. But I think that we are about to see a wave of American-born migrants to the area. Maybe that's why some might call it "gentrification" -- when white-Americans move to an area where they were previously a minority.

For this reason, we might see some changes, but it's hard to say how fast it will happen.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Diversity Plaza Upgrade?
« on: July 17, 2013, 11:54:23 AM »
I support your idea, but would not hold my breath for when CitiBike might arrive in JH. But, yes, it would be great to have CitiBike at the plaza.

One of the problems is that the plaza does not have a "function" yet. The first step was to close the area. The second is to make it become something.

For example, some more landscaping and a few more tables could turn it into a sitting place for those who get food at food carts nearby. (It could be easy to make it better than eating standing up under Roosevelt Ave!)

A flower stand in the plaza could help make it look good and could become the place where you get flowers once in a while. A coffee cart or news stand could be useful on your way to work.

Not to mention that these stands would create revenue for maintenance and would help to keep bums away.
