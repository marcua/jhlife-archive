Neighborhood Chat / Brooklyn refugees
« on: September 05, 2018, 10:02:44 AM »

I moved to JH eight years ago from Brooklyn in search of a better deal. I got one! I love my apartment and my neighborhood. Lately I've seen an influx of young families in particular, looking for that extra bedroom for a child. Occasionally I look at the site Brownstoner to keep up with developments in the former borough. This morning I was struck by this entry. I haven't seen JH featured here before. Yes, there will be more refugees from that lovely but now quite pricey borough of Kings. You can count on it. I hope the newcomers come to appreciate the mix of people here and work to make JH even more wonderful than it already is.

Restaurants & Food / Re: New report calls Jackson Heights a "food swamp"
« on: April 12, 2018, 09:42:18 AM »
Take note of the steady business at places like Made Just 4 U. The same Dominican family owns Emoji Burger, which is never as full. The population here is increasingly well educated about good food choices. It's just that so many cost-conscious families cook at home.

Personally,I am thrilled that so many of the little Colombian bakeries, and places like Rico Tinto, make home-made soup, my healthy takeout of choice for a quick and cheap lunch. The sopa de pollo at the little bakery on 82nd right across from the fruit vendor's stand, is really delicious. I think you can find a few more healthy options here is you are willing to try some Spanglish. These are not nice sit-down spots, true. But JH has many more options than other places that I've lived in the city. And they are family run, not chains. That's not a small thing.
