The two-story theater building on 82nd Street that's already been demolished would be replaced by a 13-story tower, with 128-space garage that is likely to worsen already difficult traffic.

A public hearing on the plan is set for Tuesday, March 13 at 7 p.m. at Elmhurst Hospital, Room A122. The developer is seeking a zone change that would permit construction of 13 floors, four more than now permitted.

I haven't seen an article covering the meeting either, but I received this from Catalina Cruz. It's not really a detailed summary but it does include some additional info on the decision. Perhaps Len can confirm its accuracy and fill us in on other details? Either way, I'm relieved to hear the news.

***************

Dear Friends and Neighbors,

Our community prevails! Last night, I stood with many members of our community to urge Queens Community Board 4 to vote down a rezoning proposal that would allow the construction of a 13-story luxury condo and Target Shopping Center at 40-31 82nd Street. The proposal was defeated and a recommendation was made for downzoning the site.

I commend the members of Queens Community Board 4 for recognizing the very serious concerns from local residents and small business owners over the proposed development from Sun Equity Partners and Heskel Group. The board’s decision to vote down this rezoning proposal is an enormous victory for a community that vehemently expressed objections over the detrimental impact the development would have on the surrounding neighborhoods and the absence of a clear community benefit. It is a reckless and unsafe proposal. I want to especially commend Queens Neighborhoods United for their efforts organizing and educating the community about this problematic development. I’m proud of our community for making their voices heard, and fighting to protect the interests of our family, friends and neighbors.

The board’s vote was taken after a public hearing at Elmhurst Hospital. And, came on the heels of a rally we held yesterday morning to denounce the developers’ plan. A plan which we illustrated would likely drive small businesses away and most importantly endanger lives by generating an unprecedented amount of traffic congestion that would obstruct access to Elmhurst Hospital, which is only one block away from the site.

The rezoning was being pursued to gain a 50% increase in residential floor space, which would make it the tallest residential building in the surrounding area - more than twice the height of any other residential building in Elmhurst or Jackson Heights.

I am eager to work with our entire community to address challenges like this one. Last night was an important moment for our neighborhoods of Elmhurst, Jackson Heights, and Corona because we showed that united - the people will never be defeated.

Sincerely,

Catalina Cruz

Elmhurst Community Board Says No to 82nd Street Rezoning for 13-Story Development
