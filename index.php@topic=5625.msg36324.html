Re: has Vantage gotten their act together?
« Reply #6 on: December 06, 2010, 04:39:33 PM »
gingata, I think the only way you can avoid paying a broker's fee is to sublet a condo in one of the well-known buildings directly from the owner.

There's no way I could have rung a super's bell in my current building - there is no bell and he doesn't deal with rentals.  It's very difficult to find a nice place without paying a fee - it's a win-win for the landlords because the tenant pays the fee and the broker does the work.  There's no incentive for them to do it themselves.

Although it was painful, I paid a fee for my rental and it was well worth it - the building is spotless, well-managed and responsive to any issues.  And the fact that I was so well-vetted before they'd let me move in gives me some comfort that any new people moving in have to go through the same process.  About 8 years ago, the former super used to do the rentals himself and he didn't care if 10 people wanted to shove into a 1-bedroom.  It's nice knowing that it's not going to happen now.

If an individual or body have an agenda / vendetta against a landlord, then there is no way I can beat some sense into them.  My advice, don't cheat yourself out of renting a good apartment because someone had a bad experience or todays flavor is to beat on that landlord.  Is Vantage a good Landlord...LOL, maybe not in your building but maybe in another they are a Gr8 one.  The only good landlords out there are those that are getting market rents in most of their buildings...PERIOD...
