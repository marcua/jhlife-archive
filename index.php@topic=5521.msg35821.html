Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

The need for permits was mentioned to a person working in the store.

Storefronts that are in NYC Historic Districts have this fact included in their commercial leases (thanks to leglislation from our former Councilman John Sabini), plus tenants typically have an attorney review the lease.  I also believe the property owners informed the tenant of the need for Landmarks compliance since the owners themselves have spent a great deal of money maintaining their property according to the Landmarks Law - note the lower fence and new shutters installed last month. Those shutters must have cost thousands and thousands of dollars.  The owners also posted a Landmarks permit for their recent work.
