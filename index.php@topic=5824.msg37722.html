Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

 Dr. Marotta, the Headmaster of Garden School, is in a class of educators that puts to shame some of what currently goes
on under the guise of instruction, particularly through NYC's Board of Education. He is revered by colleagues, students, alums
and parents in the school and throughout the indie school system. I, for one, am thrilled that my family has the opportunity
to work with someone of his caliber and that he is here in the Jackson Heights community. He is a candid man and has spoken
a number of times about his previous employment. This is not news to the community but thanks, Jessica Ramos, as we did
learn something about you.
 The recent appointment of Cathy Black as Chancellor of the NYC's school system points to a sea change in leadership in
education which see it as a business. If I would give Dr. Marotta any bad marks on his tenure at Garden School, it would be
to say that he has been so focused on students and how they learn that the business side of the school has been somewhat
neglected, though it has had up and down cycles for awhile. The perfect storm of the recession, a necessary tuition increase,
investments tanking and rampant unemployment hit the school hard. That said, Garden School (and by this I mean the whole
Garden School community), is in my opinion looking very much forward to the sale of the land to the city and to re-structuring
it's financials. In many business, schools, governments and even families, tough times make you re-evaluate the essentials
and Garden School greatly looks forward to streamlining it's operations so that the celebration of it's 100th year in Jackson
Heights will be truly an achievement. We also look forward to continuing to make the school as green as possible with some
of the funds. I wish for all of the parents here that you will be so lucky with whatever school it is you choose, that it will
be run by someone with the character of Dr. Marotta.

The Ramos story could be seen as necessary background providing a fuller discussion of the issue, as it is germane to know as much as we can about all parties involved in the Garden property sale.  The catch lies in the line that says Ms Ramos is afraid the school (Garden) will meet the same fate (closing) if, and I paraphrase, "they sell the yard to a real estate developer."  It simply doesn't matter to whom the school sells the property.  Money is money.  It could sink or swim with five million from the city as well as five million from a developer.  I am a parent of a Garden student and Dr. Marotta's history is certainly no secret at school.  He has never evaded a question about anything like that, and speaks frankly and openly regarding any personal professional question.  The situation to which the article refers was, like the one today at Garden, much more of an institutional dysfunction and to simply hang the entire affair on Dr. Marotta is misguided, at least.  Dr. Marotta is one member of an administration in whose hands Garden's affairs are placed.  He is an outstanding Headmaster and very much wants the best for his students and the greater Garden community in Jackson Heights.  I feel that the mention of the Times article was pitched to misinform and influence the discussion unfairly.
