Here's more information about the Subway Film Series

The Subway Film Series is a project conceived in 2011 by New York City poet
George Wallace and Jack Feldstein, a transplanted neon animation filmmaker from
Australia, now living in Jackson Heights, after Wallace saw Feldstein's `How To
Be Romantic In New York' at the Bowery Poetry Club. Their subsequent
collaboration on Walt Whitman`s `Manahatta,' which was shown at the Angelika...
for two weeks, led to the duo deciding to pair some of New York's freshest
filmmaking talent with poems from Token Entry: Poems of the NYC Subway (Ed.
Gerry LaFemina, Smalls Books, NY, 2012).

Filmmakers in the series include Heather Spilkin (Claude MacKay/Subway Wind),
Richard Shpuntoff (George Guida/Manhattan Bound F), Don Cato (Cornelius
Eady/Dead Man Rides Subway), Jack Feldstein (Philip Dacey/Subway Services),
Peter Haas (George Wallace/Men & Women on the Subway Line) and Gabriel Rodriguez
(Cheryl Dumesnil/Q to the 6 train). Rodriguez' film debuted Sept 7th, 2011 at
New Filmmakers, Anthology Film Archives.

A series of events is planned through 2012 to show the films in conjunction with
presentation from the anthology. The world premiere for The Subway Film Series
is a free screening at the QWFF 2012, March 4th, 4pm in Jackson Heights.

Three of the 6 filmmakers in this series are from Queens...and some of their
films are set in Queens and Jackson Heights.

These filmmakers are Gabe Rodriguez (Woodside)
Richard Shpuntoff (Elmhurst) and Jack Feldstein (Jackson Heights)

More information about the Queens World Film Festival 2012...based in Jackson
Heights is here...

http://queensworldfilmfestival.com/

Looking forward to seeing you there.

Jack Feldstein

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Woman chooses Honor over Man's Law. And Endless Tragedy Ensues.
« on: July 30, 2011, 11:29:39 AM »
The remarkable Shela Xoregos, who last year presented MEDEA... has again brought great theater into our lives.

This time, ANTIGONE by Sophocles.

The tragedy of a woman choosing to honor her dead brother's corpse by proper burial against the wishes of a stubborn king...in inimitable Greek tragic style...brings sorrow and death to one and all.

It's one of the great morality plays... which asks should a person choose the right thing to do?  Or what they are told to do?

Again, AMANDA ELIZABETH SAWYER plays the lead...once more with astonishing power and emotion. And AMY BOHAKER,  ANDREW R. COOKSEY,  BONG DIZON, MICHAEL LAWRENCE EISENSTEIN,  DAVID ALLEN GREEN, KATHERINE MYLENKI, REAGAN PORTER, BRENNAN-PIERSON WANG all help bring this incredible drama to life.

The costume design by Regina Cate with its hues of lilac and purple...and musical compositions by EDWARD ROSENBERG 111 are impressive.

It's free and playing all over New York (including the Jackson Heights Library Aug 6th Sat 3pm.)

Whatever you do, don't miss it.  

And let's be appreciative that Ms Xoregos continues to bring us fine and worthwhile theatrical experiences.

Jack Feldstein

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Queens Library Theater Performances: Free and Brilliant...HARLEM ON MY MIND
« on: January 31, 2011, 07:43:12 PM »

HARLEM ON MY MIND

Shela Xoregos is a phenomenon.  

Last year Ms Xoregos directed an excellent production of MEDEA which toured various Queens libraries...this year she brings us the eclectic HARLEM ON YOUR MIND which has recently completed a successful season at the Harlem Renaissance Festival in downtown Manhattan.

We are lucky that HARLEM ON MY MIND, a trip into 20's and 30's Harlem, which includes dance, short plays, terrific songs and music, plus exquisite Langston Hughes and others' poetry, will be presented FREE by the Queens Library for everyone to appreciate.

So note these dates and venues

Saturday, Feb 5 at 2:00 PM   Peninsula Library   92-25  Rockaway Beach Blvd. Queens
Saturday Feb 12 at 3:00 PM  Long Island City Branch Library  37-44   21st  Street  Queens

And you'll be in for a treat.

With a remarkable cast of six, mesmerizing  Sydney Allyson Francis, cute and precocious Emani Spence, commanding Kedrick Crowder, appealing Paule Aboite, and the hilarious Andrew R Cooksey and Z Louis Finney...  HARLEM ON MY MIND, a variety show featuring  literature, song, music, poetry and dance from 1920's and 1930's Harlem...is a winner.

The authentic songs from the period, selected by Eugene Abrams, the musical director, add to the perfomance's charm and include, amongst others,  HARLEM ON MY MIND by Irving Berlin plus HARLEM SERENADE by the Gershwins.

Highlights of the show are the poetry and "Grimke's Shadow" a playlet by Kimberly Shelby-Szyszko which has a lot of fun with Harlem slang.

And of course, the songs.

But it is the perfect re-evocation of an era in long gone Harlem... that Ms Xoregos, Mr Abrams and the fine cast bring to us that is the true joy.

HARLEM ON MY MIND makes a person wish they were there at that time.

Don't miss it.

Personally, I thank Queens Library and Ms Xoregos and company, for allowing us a glimpse into a remarkable cultural period that burst into bloom... 80 and more years ago.

JACK FELDSTEIN
78th street
Jackson Heights

Neighborhood Chat / Witchcraft! Poisoning! Infanticide!...In Jackson Heights!
« on: July 24, 2010, 07:26:16 PM »
If you believed such happenings only occurred, perhaps, in Manhattan...then you weren't at the brilliant performance of MEDEA in the Jackson Heights Library basement...2-30pm Saturday July 24th.

This great classical Greek tragedy by Euripides...was brought to life by the Xerogas Performing Company...replete with masks, singing, chorus and costumes.  

They call it "The Subway Medea" because the troupe arrives from all over NYC by subway to the various performance venues.

And with passionate and electrifying acting... for 70 minutes or so...we were transported 2500 years into the past...to a time of Zeus and Fate and the most powerful woman in antiquity...MEDEA.

For those who don't know the tale...Medea, who has murdered her brother to help her husband Jason retrieve the Golden Fleece...is spurned by Jason for a younger model.  A princess.  And Medea plots to take revenge by bringing about Jason's complete downfall. She poisons the princess with a treacherous gift of deadly gown and crown...which leads to the demise of the king... who expires when faced with the sight of his daughter frothing.

And then to top it off...Medea kills Jason's children...who also happen to be her kids.  Before escaping on a golden chariot.  A gift from Helios, the sun, who's some distant relative apparently.  

As they say in the play, that's one woman with a heart of iron.

..."There is no fury like a woman scorned"...Well, this woman brings down the whole kingdom.

Beautifully directed and choreographed by Shela Xoregos...and acted with truth, drama and committment by a troupe of actors...Each remarkably believable in their roles...

Now, remember, this is no mean feat...considering the basement of the Jackson Heights library in 2010...is not exactly the amphitheatre at the Acropolis in 431 BC.

The fact that we are offered a production of this calibre gratis...is something for which we must thank the Gods.  Or at least the Queens Library...which may or may not be the same thing.

It's astounding and admirable that the Queens Library... has the vision to support performances like Medea...so that we, mere mortals, get to experience the genius of classical Greek theatre.

...And so the men of Jackson Heights get to be reminded that leaving your wife for a princess... may not end as delightfully as you might imagine.

An awesome afternoon.  For FREE.

Kudos to Queens Library, Xoregos Performing Company and Euripides.

MORE PLEASE.

Jack Feldstein

PS... If you missed Medea in Jackson Heights...it's already been performed in Flushing, Forest Hills, and the Bronx.  However, it'll be on in various locations in Manhattan and Queens this coming week.

See : www.xoregos.com (and look under current productions)
