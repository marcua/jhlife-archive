Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

What do people think about the Mi Tierra at 86th & Roosevelt? It's not a regular stop for me, but I've been in there a couple times and it seemed clean and well-stocked—though it's probably not the place to buy things like cashew milk. (And it's little far for people in the 70s.)

Green grocers like Fruitadonna and Growing Farm can fill in some blanks, and for occasional things I want but don't need (like fancy ice cream), I will splurge at Downtown Natural (though the prices there really are outrageous).

Most of the food I buy actually comes from the farmer's market though: vegetables, fruit, bread, cheese, etc. We are lucky to have such a great and plentiful farmer's market despite our less-than-excellent grocery options. (Not a replacement, of course, especially since it's only once a week.)

While it wouldn't work as an everyday "on my way home from the subway" supermarket, I've found the Food Bazaar on Northern Boulevard across from 42nd Street to be physically large, have a varied selection of goods (including organic produce) and pretty reasonable prices.  I like it better than our local supermarkets (better than the Food Bazaar on Junction Boulevard) and would probably make it my default supermarket if it were within walking distance.

There's a Q66 bus stop less than a block away.  The Q66 bus line runs on Northern Boulevard from one end of Jackson Heights to the other.  Sometimes you end up waiting a while for it though...
