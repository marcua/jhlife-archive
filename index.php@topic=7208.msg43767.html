Hi, I just discovered this site.  I'm the farmer who is raising chickens.  I just put the first flock out to pasture yesterday morning, they all weight about 2.5 to 3 pounds right now and they should be ready for market in 3 weeks.

We are raising 120 birds every four weeks through late fall and then adding a few more at the end of the season to help carry through the winter.  We'll have fresh whole and half chickens every two weeks and frozen every other week.

So the first chickens should be at market April 29th with the caveat that a really cold April will affect the birds' growth rate.  Our poultry provides the majority of the fertility for our pastures and vegetable garden so we only raise them when we can put them outside.

We raise a Label Rouge heritage breed of chicken called a "Freedom Ranger" (I didn't name the breed) that forages well and has a really complex flavor that is superior to any supermarket chicken (in my opinion, at least).

Feel free to ask me any questions about our farm,

Michael
Sawkill Farm
Red Hook, NY
