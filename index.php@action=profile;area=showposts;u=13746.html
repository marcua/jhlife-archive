Neighborhood Chat / Pattern Burglary in Jackson Heights
« on: March 11, 2014, 10:52:20 AM »
I just want to alert everyone about this if you don't already know:

There is an ongoing pattern of burglaries in our area.  They hit 4 apartments in a building at 82nd and 37th on Saturday night March 8th and the night before they hit a building at 83nd and 37th. They pry open fire escape windows or make entry through open windows. 

These tend to be occurring in the evening hours just after dark.  The police think the scumbags are accessing roofs and making their way down the fire escapes to look for empty apartments and open windows or windows not reinforced for security. 
Basically everyone needs to reinforce their fire escape windows. Just get one of those security bars that extends between the frames on the inside.  They'll probably just see it and move on.  Also, leave lights on and maybe a radio so it appears someone is inside.  They're looking for empty apartments so if someone appears to be home they'll probably just move on.
Hoping they get busted soon.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: Foreclosure implications
« on: January 25, 2012, 10:09:58 PM »
I can shed some light on this as I work for an auction company that does business like this.  Generally we handle bankruptcy and public administrator auctions so a straight foreclosure might be slightly different. Here's how for example a bankruptcy property auction would work...The debtor either vacates the unit immediately or is given until the date of the auction to vacate.  We, the auction company would generally take photos of the unit, post them online along with the terms of the auction, bylaws of the co-op and other pertinent documents. We'd also usually schedule at least two times where people could come and look at the place like an open house.  Next the unit goes to auction.  In the case of bankruptcy auctions the high bid is subject to the approval of the trustee....for a foreclosure I'd imagine it's at the discretion of the bank. (So this should cover the "if the bid is too low")  Also, any time we've ever auctioned a co-op the high bidder is subject to the approval of the co-op board before the sale can go through.  (and this should cover "if the buyer is a corporation")     I honestly wouldn't be too concerned with this situation...sadly it happens all the time whether it be foreclosures, bankruptcies, public administrator properties, guardian ordered etc etc.
