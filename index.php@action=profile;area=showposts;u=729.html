Neighborhood Chat / Re: Jackson Heights getting slammed on Queenscrap.blogspot.com
« on: February 17, 2010, 05:54:35 PM »
When I lived in London, I was shocked at how often people littered on the street--everybody: kids, rich professionals, hipsters, working class... And I think it was because the city paid for a huge number of street sweepers to constantly patrol the streets and clean up after people. It wasn't a dirty city, but the people had the mentality that they could get away with it, that someone else would deal with it. I don't know if their behavior would change if all those street sweepers disappeared.

Not that this applies to Roosevelt, where there are no street sweepers and people are blatant about littering. I've seen moms tell their kids to drop trash on the ground when there's a can just a few feet away. I don't get it.

Neighborhood Chat / gas-powered leaf blowers
« on: November 25, 2009, 02:48:30 PM »
I've noticed a lot of our neighborhood co-ops have their supers using gas-powered blowers to get rid of leaves--and I'm not sure these supers are collecting and bagging, or if they're just blowing them into the street. Since the city isn't doing leaf collection this year, maybe a the JH Beautification Group or our local CSA or someone could start a leaf drop-off and composting program. It would cut down on stinky, noisy blowers, and keep the leaves out of the landfill. Surely some of our gorgeous garden apartment buildings could use some mulch! Let's keep it in the neighborhood.
