This won't necessarily help you get places, but a very cool tool that google maps  http://maps.google.com/maps has now is something called "street view".  If you go to google maps, type in any address in NYC, and then click "street view" -- any street nearby that is outlined in blue means you can click and see a 360 degree photo of that exact spot.

In JH, you can see most of Roosevelt Ave and most of Northern Blvd this way, but not much in between.

Some other cities have this function as well.

I haven't tried that service by Google, but do you know that if you're looking for a place and do not know the address, you can send a text to "GOOGLE" (466453) and in less than a minute, the address of the place will be texted back at you?  It's free (just standard text-messenging rates) and I use it all the time! I try to be as specific as possible (like including the name of the city/town and state, along with the place name), but it will give you several options if you're not as specific.

There is also another free service called "Cha Cha"  In this case, you can text any question you have to "Cha Cha" (242242) and a real person will answer you.  You can ask anything you want, but I've used it for directions before, or to see where the nearest subway was from where I was.
