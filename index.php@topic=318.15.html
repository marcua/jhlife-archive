Went looking for the Mayan Cafe for brunch this morning... only to find that it had closed.  Instead we ended up at a Peruvian place on Northern and 87th Ave called El Sol. 

At 11 AM on a Sunday, the place was MOBBED.  We got the second to last table in the house, and after we sat down, there were at least three parties standing at the door waiting for a table. 

I thought the food was ok.  Not lots of options for non-meat eaters, and the beans which I ordered for my daughter were pinto beans, not the really yummy yellow beans that everyone loved at Inti Raymi.  I tasted a non-chicken part of someone's tamal, and it was very tastey and a bit bitter.  The mango batitdas made a hit all around the table.  Service was very attentive.  VERY CHILD friendly, with lots of kids of all ages, including babies at various tables, and they brought a plastic spoon for my two-year old.  We seemed to be the only non-Peruvians there, and it was a good thing we all spoke Spanish because they hadn't even bothered to translate the breakfast menu, and we were in brunch mode.

You sure wouldn't have any clue that the economy was in trouble watching the crowds lining up out onto the street for a table at 11:30 AM on a Sunday!  Maybe folks who've lived in triple digit inflation think that bad financial situations mean it is time to spend the money that you've got while you've still got it.  Who knows.

I used to love Inti Raymi but always wished the decor was a little spiffier.  I brought visiting friends to this new place last week after we found Inti Raymi locked.  Not only does this spot have the handsomest dining room in JH (and the best lighting) the food (same menu as Inti Raymi) was as good or better than its days down the block.  The service was excellent as well.  My friends, foodies who live in San Francisco, thought it was one of th best meals they had in long time.  We had an entire meal of fish dishes and it was spectacular.

I look forward to going here often.

    Yes I must admit that Inti Raymi has/had the best ceviche in the neighborhood but as far as the other dishes such as lomo saltado, tacu-tacu, aquadito and tallarin you must head over to Las Delicias Peruanas Restaurant on 43-07 104th Street in Corona.  This place is not much to look at but the food is sublime!  But for the BEST ceviche and for that matter the best overall Peruvian food one must head over to Pio Pio Riko restaurant at 45-23 Greenpoint Ave. in  Sunnyside.  The ceviche is perfectly fresh and tangy.  One of my favorite appetizers is antichos which is skewers of grilled marinated cows heart served with potato and a wonderful spicy green sauce.
