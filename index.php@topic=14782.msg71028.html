Just bumping this up.  I went with my husband this weekend - and we loved it.

You absolutely have to work for your food.  You are cracking the lobster and peeling the shrimp and crawfish.  The fish was cooked excellently - some places overcook the lobster and it becomes rubbery.  Not the case here.  The Oceanic Boil sauce was spicy without being too hot for my taste.  They had an oysters appetizer that he loved, and the lobster roll appetizer was meaty and tasty.

All in all, very glad we went, and we'll be back.  It wasn't that busy, so spread the word.

Never heard of this place until last night. Driving down 37th Ave. and see a sign for Cajun food in Jackson Heights! How could this have opened without my notice? Had no idea what to expect. No, folks, an Oceanic Boil is not some kind of skin affliction. It is a delightful little addition to the JH culinary mix. In case you haven't had a New Orleans style seafood boil, it's sort of the seafood equivalent of a chopped salad. The server puts your choice of boiled seafood into a plastic bag in a large wooden salad bowl with potatoes and a half ear of corn and your choice of sauce and shakes it up until everything is imbued with the sauce. We had the 1 lb of mussels and the 1 lb of shrimp with the spicy garlic butter and Bay seasoning (the traditional New Orleans seafood spice mix) and it was delicious. You'll have to work for your supper, as the shrimp are served with the shell and head still on. The waitress will offer gloves, and if you are digging into the shrimp or crawfish, you'll appreciate them.
Most of the main courses are boiled, most of the appetizers are fried. We spilt the Cajun Seafood Mac 'n' cheese appetizer and it was plenty for both of us and very tasty. Well worth a visit, and I wish someone had mentioned this place recently, so I hope this bump helps someone else looking for something different. Oh, the bill came to about $40 before tax and tip. Two $13 entrees, an appetizer, one glass of wine.
