Neighborhood Chat / Re: Useful but hidden stores nearby (non-food items)
« on: March 19, 2008, 08:52:47 PM »
Mainstream Movies - take the R/V/G to Steinway and walk to UA Kaufman Astoria theatres for first run movies.
Artsy Movies - Kew Gardens Cineman on Lefferts Blvd. is a good local'ish place to see artsy movies - better than going to manhattan. And you can cross the street and have great pub food at Austin Ale House.

Chain Stores: Take the R/V/G to 46th and walk a long block south to Marshall's/Old Navy/Chucky Cheese/Home Depot. 
Mall: Take the R/V/G to Woodhaven and you'll be at a Queens Center Mall - pretty conventional mall where you can find Macy's and JC Penney.
Atlas Park: Take the 29 bus from 82nd Street to Atlas Park - a strange european like mall with a few decent restaurants and nice open space with a fun kid friendly water fountain. The shopping sucks though - they are still working on filling vacancies. But they also have a movie theatre that is decent, and an Amish Marketplace. Oh, I shouldn't forget the large Barnes & Nobles. Driving there is a better idea....

Bowling Alley/Ping Pong/Pool/Fooseball - there is a decent and fairly nice place to do all of these things. BQE Billiards and the bowling alley are at 34th Avenue and 71st or so...about a ten minute walk from the historic district. They have a nice bar too - sort of sportish.

Jewelery/Framing: European Gallery/Franco's Corner on 35th around 84th. Franco has some nice antique jewelery. I have used the Gallery for framing quite a bit, but I think they are overpriced and a bit rude.

Newman Jewelers on 35th Between 78th and 79th has beautiful china (Kate Spade, Vera Wang, ect) at very good discount prices.

Clothing
Famous Brands - Roosevelt and 78th. Cheap, cheap, cheap clothing from relatively well known brand name companies like NY&Co, Liz Claiborne.

EFN Zone - a gym right above Famous Brands. The only real full service gym in the 'hood. They are VERY inexpensive too - but I found too crowded after work and the aerobics instructors were poorly trained. They do very little warm up.

Corona Ice King in Corona around 103 and 55th Avenue or so - an EXCELLENT thing to do on a weekend night in the summer is go to Corona Ice King, get a lemon ice (or a gourmet flavor if you choose) and sit and watch the men play boci (sp?) in Spaghetti Park (that's a nickname...but it sticks....) Watch out for loose bocci balls....

I used to live in Manhattan - and now I barely ever leave Queens! My good friends in Brooklyn who are still renting are jealous!

There are some creative ways to use space that work to discourage loitering of the kind folks are talking about. Consider Bryant Park - it used to be a poorly designed space that encouraged vagrancy, but is now a place that encourages use by regular folks (which in turn serves to discourage use by drunks). Partnership for Public Spaces is a good NYC-based non-profit that helps communities brainstorm about design solutions that work for spaces like this. It IS possible.

Raw rent numbers don't tell us a thing. We cannot form an opinion about rents on 37th avenue without doing a comparative analysis. According to the previous post, the former Greenpoint space on 37th Avenue is 300,000k for 3,400sf - which is about $88/sf a year (I'm not sure given the information whether this includes basement space. If it does, the rent is less than $88/sf)

Consider this - on some major commercial strips in Manhattan rents are almost $300/sf a year on average. The average for all of Manhattan, both major and minor corridors, was about $100/sf a year. See the attached article: http://www.huliq.com/2530/manhattan-retail-rents-continue-to-surge

I also read another article where asking rents on Austin Street in Forest Hills (which supports a healthy mix of national and local retailers) is about $150/sf. Frankly, for some businesses $88/sf might be a good deal. The only thing that keeps a retailer from paying these rents is whether they make enough profit from customers to do so.
