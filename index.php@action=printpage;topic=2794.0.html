Title: Re: Where's the Ambiance?
Post by: Shelby2 on May 24, 2009, 10:42:02 PM
The restaurants in JH are short on ambiance but what the area lacks in ambience is made up for in low prices for good ethnic cuisine.  If you are looking to go out locally to the kind of cute, cozy places that are plentiful in the LES, then JH probably isn't the right place for you.  But if you are up for local culinary adventures and saving some money (and going into Manhattan when you need a seriously cozy place full of ambience) then maybe JH will work out for you.

I just had dinner tonight at La Cabana Argentinian on Northern Blvd-- my first time.  I thought it was very cozy and sweet, with the doors opened to the sidewalk.  We had a half pitcher of sangria, the brick oven roasted pizza and some pasta.  however, it's still not the LES and you have to be willing to embrace the difference.

Title: Re: Where's the Ambiance?
Post by: will4food on May 25, 2009, 09:48:45 AM
I moved here from the UWS a year ago and faced the same issues as you (the restaurants are lacking ambiance and most are mediocre at best).  HOWEVER, there are a few very fine places here to recommend--Uncle Peter's Continental and Trieste are lovely spots with great food.  Second La Cabana.  There's also La Pequina (a pretty spot for Columbian food).  And a short walk will take you to the best Thai place on the East Coast--Sriphapai and walk a bit farther where you'll discover Saphori D'Ishia for fine Italian food in a grocery store setting. And there's Park Side in Cornona...When it comes to fine dining, Jackson Heights is not Manhattan but you'll find a handful of worthy restaurants to keep you satisfied.

Post by: abee on May 27, 2009, 09:35:20 PM
I'm a big fan of brunch, and slightly crushed that no one in the neighborhood has one. However, brunch is one thing that I'm willing to travel- near and far- for. Dim Sum in Flushing, Sparrow or Bistro 33 in Astoria, or Dillinger's in Woodside- off the top of my head- are all very good and not a far trip (though, admittedly, Astoria is easier if you have a car). Lots of my friends live in Astoria or Brooklyn, and when I meet them for brunch, we usually meet in Manhattan because it's more central (though I have yet to meet anyone who isn't willing to come to Flushing for Dim Sum at the hint we might be going out, though, of course, harder- impossible?- to navigate if you're a vegetarian who doesn't speak Chinese). Cassidy's, a bar on 31st Avenue (near Canelle- delicious, and cute, though not intimate) used to have a small brunch, but I'm not sure if they still do. It's a bit of a hike, but it's not a horrible walk, and there's a bus that heads up that way.

The ambiance of most places doesn't bother me- when I go to a hole in the wall, I don't expect much, and the "nicer" restaurants I think are generally cute- Himalayan Yak just got a nice makeover (is that the Nepalese place you mentioned in your post?), but most of the places are, for lack of a better word, "ethnically" decorated rather than trendy and sleek, but I don't think that makes them any less cute, and most certainly doesn't hurt the food!

Post by: normelrod on May 28, 2009, 12:31:47 AM
The restaurants on Roosevelt are essentially what you'd expect to find underneath subway tracks. There are some great places (Tacqueria Coatzingo for Mexican, El Sitio for Cuban, Rice Ave. for Thai). But with the exception of Rice Ave. the decor is lacking. There's a great place on 83rd St. just on the Elmhurst side of the tracks called El Meson Colombiano. It's a quaint little restaurant with an owner who might just sit down at your table and order for you. I've had some fabulous meals there. Armondo's on 37th Ave. at 75th St. has good Italian food, a good vibe and really attentive service. Urubamba on 37th Ave. at 86th St. looks like a really cozy place for Peruvian food, though I've never been. I can't speak to the vegetarian options, but there's a nice brunch spot in Astoria called Cafe Bar. It's located at 34th Ave. and 36th St. not too far from the Steinway stop. There are no brunch spots in Jackson Heights as far as I know. But that will change if the neighborhood continues to change. Happy eating.

Title: Re: Where's the Ambiance?
Post by: spanishfish on June 23, 2009, 06:08:37 PM
We went to Urubamba for Father's Day dinner on Sunday. It was excellent. For those who don't know, it's located at the corner of 37th Avenue and 86th (or 87th?) Street. It's Peruvian, the atmosphere was very nice. My wife thought the dishes were too salty, but I disagreed. The only down side was that it took a long time to get our food, granted they looked extremely busy for a Sunday evening. The appetizer sampler plate was excellent. It had fried calamari, ceviche, a chicken tamale and beef/olive/cilantro stuffed potatoes. My wife had the paella, which she said was very good. I had the Skirt Steak (enormous) with rice and Peruvian beans. Also very good.

We also had a pitcher of the Peruvian spiced punch. Also excellent.

The ambiance was the best I've experienced, next to Jardi, in the neighborhood so far.

Highly recommended. I hope the slow service was just a fluke. I'll be back here for sure.
