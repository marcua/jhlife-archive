Great topic... been forming my mental list all day.

Besides momos, here are other yummy foods that I most likely would never have encountered if I didn't live in Jackson Heights:

Cholados
Indian ice cream: kulfi (available from street vendors) and faloodas (a sundae of kulfi + rose syrup + noodles + basil seeds, which I used to get at Dimple)
Espresso 77's fabulous Honey Mint Limonata
Pio Pio's amazing hot sauce
Salchipapas (a simple but brilliant merger of hot dogs and french fries)
Indian-Chinese cuisine (Gobi Manchurian, Chilli Chicken, etc.)
Gujarati cuisine (patra, khandvi, etc.) at Rajbhog

  IMO  Elephant jasmine rice is the most fragrant and tasty! You can pick up a 20 lb bag at Patel Bros. on 74th St. for  $17.99.  This is my everyday rice but when I am preparing Biriyani or Indian curry I buy Swad Deharaduni basmati rice a 10 lb  bag @ Patel's will cost you $13.99.  I will be preparing a spicy fish curry tonight made with many spices, coconut milk and chunks of the stripe bass I  recently caught on the Hudson.  To cool the palate I will serve along my curry a salad called pachadi which consists of tomato, cucumber, red onion, roasted (mustard seed, urad dal and curry leaves), black pepper, salt to taste all tossed together with creamy whole milk yogurt!
