Green Market Time For Shopping
« on: September 01, 2013, 07:25:31 PM »
As I tried to maneuver my way through the Market this morning I wondered if it was possible to have an hour just for shoppers.  No animals, bikes, scooters, "SUV" strollers or other impediments ("circled wagons"). The pavement on 34th Ave is app 3' wide before the banked cobblestones:  this is usually blocked by persons or animals having social interactions, completely oblivious to their surroundings.  On 78th St, the "circled wagons" just expand to fill the space.  Just have some sense and give others some space.

My husband and I go together with our dog. My husband takes care of the dog; I shop. I can't take the dog without my husband so either he comes with me or I don't go.

Yes, it's a little crowded but really it's not that bad. People stop to look at my dog (he's very beautiful) and we meet people through the process.

the one thing I would not do is tie my dog to the fence. It's dangerous (dog can be stolen) and you really should supervise your dog around people, especially with children running around.

Union Square is a zoo in terms of crowds. I would never think to complain about JH market in those terms.

I don't expect the market to be perfect for me, but's very very nice. I'm grateful. So if I have to do a little work (manuever around people, take my husband so he can manage my big dog) it's worth it.
