My husband and I bought an apartment here right after we got married in P-Town over 11 years ago.  In the 11 years, I've walked hand in hand with him, shown affection in public and acted, in every way, as a straight couple would.  I've never once experienced any negative reaction.

That said, as everyone else made clear, even though we live in a very accepting neighborhood, things happen, especially around Roosevelt, and especially if you flash something expensive.  This is not unlike what would happen in any neighborhood in NYC, or in any state, as I'm sure you probably have read on your Facebook account or on any of the gay news sites.  I would not make out with my husband at 2 a.m. on a dark street.  I don't think I'd do that even if I were straight.

Generally speaking, this is a safe neighborhood and I have never experienced any negative reaction to being gay.  In fact, our state senator a few years back had a great deal of political fallout for many reasons -- one of which was his position that NY should not permit gay marriage.  He was voted out of office in a landslide. 

As for Board requirements, it depends on the Board.  If you buy in certain buildings (Towers, Chateau, etc.), then the Boards tend to be very strict and they want to make sure that you have cash available after you purchase to afford to carry the apartment and your mortgage if you both find yourselves unemployed.  Don't think of it as a barrier so much as a protective zone -- you want to make sure that your neighbors don't stop paying their maintenance or go into bankruptcy, and this is one way to assure that.  All boards (including mine) look very carefully at finances, which is why during the last market crash, JH coops were not impacted the way that the housing market in other areas was.  The Boards look to solvency to assure everyone that their neighbors would be better able to weather such a storm.  Take comfort in that, and welcome to the neighborhood!

Hi, maccrategarcia. You might want to look up that documentary "in Jackson Heights", launched last year. It will give you a good idea.

While not perfect, Jackson Heights is great. I already liked it a lot before I moved here. Now that I live here, I like it way more than I thought I would.

People of all kinds from all places. I could be statistically wrong, but my impression is that "white males" are just another minority here.

JH also has a variety of environments. Under the elevated subway, you have some grittiness. While the historic district sometimes reminds me of cute parts of London. Old New York dicey and some fanciness at the same place.

We just don't have a great, large public park. But Danny, our very progressive council man, has bought more land to the little park, Travers. The park is scheduled to be renovated next year.

Sometimes JH a melting pot, other times it's patchwork. JH is generally safe and a pretty good place to live. You will be just another one in the mix.
