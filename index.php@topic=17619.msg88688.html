Election for NY State Assembly District 39 (2018)
« on: March 19, 2018, 11:43:22 AM »
https://www.thecut.com/2018/03/meet-catalina-cruz-queens-dreamer-running-for-office.html

Excerpt:

“I’m a punk when it comes to pain,” admits Catalina Cruz. “I’ll cry from a paper cut.” These are not sentiments one would expect from someone entering the pummeling world of American politics, but Cruz is currently standing in front of the Queens establishment where she got her ears pierced 24 years ago. She peers through the window at the baubles on display before continuing on, her rain boots making a muffled clomp on the sidewalks of Jackson Heights — one of several neighborhoods that comprise District 39, the vacant Assembly seat of which Cruz hopes to fill after the primary election this September.

Cruz, who became a U.S. citizen in 2009, is currently the first Dreamer to run for office in New York state; if she wins, she will be the third Dreamer to ever take office in this country (after Wendy Carrillo of California and Ruben Kihuen of Nevada). In 1992, at the age of 9, she boarded a plane in Medellin, where she could see Pablo Escobar’s jail from the sidewalk of her childhood home. She arrived at JFK accompanied by her single mother, wearing her favorite outfit — a “pant situation with a shirt that had too many ruffles; it was not cute” — and carrying a tourist visa set to expire in six months. Growing up, this stretch of 82nd Street is where she came to tap into the Colombian community vibe. First stop: Las Americas Bakery, established in 1976 but since closed, never again to grace the world with their buñuelos.

Last night I got my secondvisit from Espinal rep.

Now I can definitely say that they are indeed nervous.

While it's true Espinal wasn't actually appointed by the Queens machine, she might as well have been. She ran unopposed during a low-turnout special election in April. For a special election to the state Legislature, there are no party primaries, instead the party county committee selects its candidate for what is a singular public vote. In a heavily Democratic district like most in New York City, the party’s nominee is virtually assured victory in a special election, and therefore gains at least some of the benefits of incumbency ahead of any subsequent intra-party challenges.

But the reality is...she was ``elected'' in April and now has a real challenger, something she didn't have before.

 I can give a little bit here as I wasn't able to concentrate on the whole thing for other reasons. But Ari Espinal
did not show and there wasn't a reason specified and it seemed to have been canceled on her part that day.
So Catalina Cruz and Yonel Lettelier Sosa answered questions from the moderator, Katie Honan, who was
terrific. They were polite to one another which was appreciated by the audience and the CHHAYA staff.

 The two primary sponsors along with a number of other local community organizations are all non profits who
have concerns that they wanted to hear answers on and it was a disappointment not to hear Espinal have a
voice here- but it was her choice. That said, Catalina, was electric and seemed passionate, informed and
ready to take on this job. She had a number of concerns with immigrant issues like rent controls, recognizing
basements apts, school issues all the way through college affordability and housing for new grads who stay
and work here. Also allowing drivers licenses to be issued without a ss#. And concerns with health care and
seniors who use the ER for their doctors visits. She supports the bike lanes and wants transit reform with
some refinancing and stopping the use of transit funds for other projects. I am sure she said much more
but this is what I remember. She seemed very interested in listening to the constituents and the nail salon
workers who she had helped before were there and showed support. She's an attorney. Also the audience
seemed to respond well to her.

 Yonel was more laid back and repeatedly said that the problem with Catalina was her lack of long term residency
here in Jackson Heights, not Queens, but specifically in this area she would represent. She did not dispute this
so I guess it is true. He had some good points to make but on others he wanted to defer to have more time
to analyze issues like commercial rent control and the one test specialized HS exam. It sounds like he has had
experience representing this area over a number of years with police issues and labor disputes. But I sense he
is not thinking of changing the system but working with it as is. This may not be a bad approach but Cruz wanted
to course correct on a number of issues. Yonel's work experience definitely qualified him and I especially liked
the work he said he does with his wife with victims of domestic violence.

 Both of these candidates were credible and the event was well run by CHHAYA and JHBG and the other
community groups. It was well attended even though the auditorium was hot. Great to have this forum to see
what's out there. Feel much more informed as a result.
