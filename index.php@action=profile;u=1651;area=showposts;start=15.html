Neighborhood Chat / Re: Eagle Theater
« on: October 27, 2009, 09:45:42 AM »
Regardless of how things turn out, the organizers should make sure that the entire community is represented in determining the possible conversion of the Eagle Theater. Their voices should also be heard, in addition to the internet savvy blogger types (myself included) that are active on this message board.

I personally would love to see a more multi-use community space or varied community theatre, showing art films as well as Bollywood flicks and live local performances. The Angelika Film Center is a good idea, but let's play devil's advocate for a second. The Angelika Film Center has its own place in SoHo, with its art films, gourmet food, etc -- do we really want Jackson Heights, with all its diversity and frenetic energy -- to become another SoHo? Think about it.

I don't want this to turn into a debate on gentrification, but I do think we have a responsibility to influence change for the benefit of the ENTIRE community. Everyone on this blog seems to have good intentions, so I am not at all being critical of the initiative that people are taking. I just think that we should remember that whatever we do and push for, we should make it unique to and for the benefit of all of Jackson Heights.

Neighborhood Chat / Re: A neighborhood with no green space - will I be happy?
« on: September 16, 2009, 01:42:38 PM »
Well, if you're moving into a garden building, you would definitely have greenspace right in your courtyard. This is one of the best parts of living in JH! The tranquility of the garden buildings contrasted with the chaos and life of 37th Ave and the grit of Roosevelt.

Don't underestimate Travers Park. It gets a lot of use -- everyone from children to adults playing basketball and elderly doing tai-chi. On the weekends during the farmer's market, there are even more people hanging out in the Park.

As for neighborhood get-togethers, I would highlight the number of festivals in JH. 37th Road by the Roosevelt-74th stop is often made into a pedestrian zone and the entire neighborhood congregates. See the Calendar on this forum for a sampling.

Further afield, you are in somewhat proximity to Flushing Meadows Corona Park. When I am feeling ambitious, I go for a long run to Flushing Meadows and back. It's a shame that more people don't use the park. It's actually quite spacious, and the area around the Queens Museum has some nice architectural relics from the days of the World Fair.
