Quote
You expect they when they step off the plane and take a 20 minute ride to JH they want to be clean and it is our fault that we do not have enough trash cans?

I've been to India several times.  My husband is Indian-American.  I lived there for 3 months as a volunteer during the last recession.  My second family is from there.

I love how you can point to a couple of videos that you found on Youtube and generalize an entire sub-continent.  I assume that you are well aware that public garbage cans are infrequent in many cities because of fear from bombings/terrorist attacks, which leaves most people without a place to drop off their trash.  I assume you've personally witnessed public employees cleaning the sidewalks and streets in the mornings, only to have them back in the same condition the next morning because -- in large part - of the lack of trash cans.  And I assume that you've been to the less crowded portions of the continent - where terrorism is less of a threat and, as a result, the streets are cleaner and, in some parts, downright clean.

There are, of course, slums, including the heavily populated slums of Bangalore.  Pointing to those and generalizing that Indians live like that is a great oversimplification.  It's like saying that all Americans are fat, orange-tinted racists.  As an example, of course.

The truth is that the areas around 73rd and 74th are the most high traffic areas in our little hood.  Every single time I pass by, the few trash cans that exist are full - which means that people try to dispose of their trash.  It would not hurt to have more of them, like we do around the 82nd Street area, which is much cleaner because the trash cans are plentiful.

The solar "Big Belly" trash cans are an attractive idea, but their implementation in Manhattan recently as part of a big rodent control program has been disappointing. They cost $3,000 for the can, and $4,000 extra for the service contract that includes an app that alerts whoever is responsible to empty the can when it is full. The can actually locks itself once full.

In Manhattan, many of these Big Bellies have already broke down, been hit by cars, etc. The larger problem is that if they aren't emptied fast enough, people just dump the garbage around and on top of the cans. I have personally seen several of these cans rendered unusable by people stuffing pizza boxes or other trash into the slot.  See some pics here:

https://voicesofny.org/2017/12/bigbelly-garbage-cans-bring-new-trouble-to-chinatown/
