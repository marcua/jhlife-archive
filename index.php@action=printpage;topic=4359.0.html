Title: New Letter Grading System at Restaurants -- Will it work?
Post by: Chuckster on March 17, 2010, 12:21:01 AM
I just came across the following article from the Associated Press stating that a new letter grading system for NYC restaurants has been approved by the City Board of Health.  This new system will indicate cleanliness levels at all eateries based on grade scores "A" through "C" which must be prominently displayed near entrances.

From the article:

"The plan approved Tuesday gives restaurants that receive grades lower than an A time to improve their sanitary conditions before they have to post anything.

For those eateries, the health department will return within a month to conduct a second inspection, and the second grade will be posted unless the restaurant operator contests it. Restaurants appealing their grades will be allowed to post a "grade pending" sign."

NYC restaurants must now show their report cards (http://www.google.com/hostednews/ap/article/ALeqM5iiKZkxwIBeY3492JyGpuSZC_qkpgD9EG22Q00)

Food carts will not be affected by this new regulation.
