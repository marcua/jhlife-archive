Community Groups, Activities & Events / Summer Youth Employment Program
« on: February 10, 2017, 10:49:04 AM »
Hi neighbors,

Applications for the 2017 Summer Youth Employment Program (SYEP) are open!

SYEP is the nation’s largest summer youth employment initiative, and since 1963 has provided New York City young people between the ages of 14 and 24 with up to six weeks of entry-level experience at worksites in all five boroughs.

Participants are compensated for their work at diverse worksites that include government agencies, hospitals, summer camps, nonprofits, small businesses, law firms, museums, sports enterprises and retail.

Specialized programming for disabled, foster care, runaway/homeless and court-involved young people are also available.

SYEP offers workshops on job readiness, career exploration and financial literacy, and opportunities to continue education and social growth. Ladders for Leaders is an employer-paid internship component of SYEP for youth aged 16-22.

The deadline to apply is Friday, March 17.

Click to learn more and apply: https://application.nycsyep.com/

And please share with your networks!

Stay warm,

Community Groups, Activities & Events / Council Speaker Candidates in Jackson Heights on 11/14
« on: November 08, 2013, 02:27:56 PM »
At the next meeting of the New Visions Democratic Club, candidates for City Council Speaker will come to speak to us and share their vision for the future of the city. The meeting will be Thursday, November 14 at 6pm in the Jackson Heights Jewish Center, 37-06 77 Street.

As you know, the Speaker is elected by Council members and is primarily responsible for obtaining a consensus on major issues, calling bills for a vote and doling out funding among council districts. He or she will play a critical role in shaping the next four years of New York City in the fourth most important post.

The election to replace Christine Quinn, the current Speaker, will not take place until January, after those elected in November take office, but campaigns for the post have been under way for months.

While New York City residents cannot vote for Speaker, it is important we are all educated on the role and voice our thoughts on how our city’s legislative body operates.

Please join us on Thursday, November 14 at 6pm in the Jackson Heights Jewish Center, 37-06 77 Street.

Feel free to email me offline with any questions at jesoram@gmail.com.

Community Groups, Activities & Events / When you vote tomorrow, flip the ballot!
« on: November 04, 2013, 01:20:11 PM »
Hi neighbors,

Tomorrow is Election Day! Please don't forget to go out and vote for your candidates of choice, but there are also six proposed amendments to the State's constitution for your consideration. They will be on the back side of your ballot, so don't forget to flip it!

Little has been said about most of these ballot initiatives, but it is important to understand what's being suggested, so here are the proposed amendments for you to think about before heading to the polls tomorrow:

Proposal One: Authorizing Casino Gaming

This is really the only proposal that has received at least some press coverage. The proposal is to allow the legislature to authorize and regulate up to seven casinos across the state.

Proposal Two: Additional Civil Service Credit for Veterans with Disabilities Certified Post-Appointment

This proposed amendment would permit veterans who are certified disabled after having already received credit at one appointment or promotion to receive additional civil service credit a second time after certification of their disability.

Proposal Three: Exclusion of Indebtedness Contracted for Sewage Facilities

The purpose for this one is to continue to allow counties, cities, towns, and villages to exclude from their constitutional debt limits indebtedness incurred for the construction or reconstruction of sewage facilities.

Proposal Four: Settling Disputed Title in the Forest Preserve

This amendment would allow the legislature to settle 100-year old disputes between the state and owners of land in the forest preserve by giving up the State's claim to disputed parcels. In exchange, the State would receive land for incorporation into the forest preserve.

Proposal Five: In Relation to a Land Exchange in the State Forest Preserve with NYCO Minerals, Inc.

This amendment would allow the State to convey approximately 200 forest preserve acres to NYCO Minerals for mining. In exchange, NYCO Minerals would give the State as much or more land of at least the same value, with a minimum assessed value of $1 million. This land would be added to the forest preserve.

Proposal Six: Increasing Age Until Which Certain State Judges Can Serve

This measure increases the maximum age until which Justices of the Supreme Court and Judges of the Court of Appeals may serve to 80. This is upon certification of need and competence.

Don't know where to vote? Click here and enter your address: http://nyc.pollsitelocator.com/Search.aspx
