Neighborhood Chat / Re: Police Trap Last Night on 82nd
« on: May 17, 2009, 10:58:05 AM »
  To the original poster, and to everyone else interested.  There are plenty of things to look for in determining if the person is indeed a Police Officer.  Most obviously, look for other police equipment;  a HOLSTERED gun, handcuffs, pepper spray, a police radio etc.  Often most or all of this equipment is covered, but you can usually see it bulging through their clothing, or maybe sticking out a bit under a shire etc.  Listen for actual radio chatter, see if you can make out a bullet resistant vest etc.  Most, if not all plainclothes cops, especially at the precinct level, have at least one partner working with them, and at least one supervisor.  You can ask to speak to the supervisor, or have the plainclothes cop have a uniformed unit respond to confirm their identity.
