Title: Re: An end of a Pizza Era?
Post by: QueensLand on March 15, 2015, 12:31:18 PM
As a resident of JH for several decades, & a pizza lover who has had authentic brick oven pizza throughout Italy and from Manhattan's best places like Patsy's, Lombardi's, Grimaldi's (originally Brooklyn now one on 6th Av), John's, and Joe's, here's a list of joints in the neighborhood I adore:

--Of course Romeo's which started this thread - same owner for decades...I used to love getting Italian ices there (not as many options as Lemon Ice King of Corona, but hey, it's walkable) It's been so empty lately though; how do they stay in business?

--Louie's in Elmhurst (near La Fusta Steakhouse)... I daresay the best tasting pizza in Queens. The grandma pie is incredible; they also make a small yummy margarita pie (for half the price of the grandma). And the pizza guy is SO welcoming (Louie himself?) Plus they deliver throughout the area.

--Pizza Boy (74th St at 37th Ave) - fresh, cheesy individual pies ... Plus a fabulous thin slice pizza option.

--Thomas Pizza on 37th Ave (across from Downtown Market).  I remember when Thomas from Italy owned it.  He was a sweet, special man. He watched me grow up.  Now it is owned by a new family (as of about 10 years ago), and for a while they were making delicious whole wheat pies.  They discontinued those when they weren't selling as much.  Their regular slices are still Thomas' old recipe, a nice thin yet soft crust (like Pizza Boy's) They also make a delicious fresh mozzarella & basil pie, if you get it delivered.

--Gianni's off 82nd St (near the firehouse)

--Due Fratelli (diagonally across from PS69 on 37th Ave) has modern decor, plus a lot of unique pizza options that are made fresh throughout the day (I like the mushroom  pesto).

--Mr Bruno's
(near Cannelle bakery) in East Elmhurst.  It's spacious with big windows and they have some interesting combinations there too.
