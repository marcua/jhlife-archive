Greetings, JH Lifers.  Just in case you missed it, this website will be closing down on December 31st.  It's been a great journey, but it is time for this site to walk off into the sunset (or whatever lies on the other side of the BQE).   Please see our recent announcement for more information.

Hopefully this is just the beginning of a new chapter in efforts to build community online and in real life.  Come join your neighbors at r/jacksonheights or r/queens on Reddit, Jackson Heights 11372 on Facebook, and the venerable JHfamilies group on Google.

Here comes Solomon.

Dssjh, of course you are right that it's unreasonable to expect all airplane noise over Jackson Heights to cease, and that it would not be equitable to force other neighborhoods to take on the entire noise burden. But I assume you do agree it's gotten a bit worse lately? It sounds like you do. And without the ability to be in every neighborhood at once, it's impossible for any of us to know whether the change is an appropriate rebalancing or an unfair burden placed on Jackson Heights. So people complain and write letters to the Port Authority, Danny Dromm, etc. I think the complaining is understandable, and the letter writing is potentially effective.

That said, I would note that this thread may itself be as big a threat to property values as the airplane noise. People who consider buying property here look at Jackson Heights Life all the time, and this thread could, I think, easily scare some of them away. That doesn't mean the thread shouldn't exist. But, as someone else said above, let's ease up on the over the top comments, try to be as realistic as possible about how bad it really is, and maybe consider writing letters to people in power as a more effective response than venting emotions on JH Life. My perspective: I'd say the noise is worse than it was, and worse than in neighborhoods that are nowhere near airports. But there are certainly neighborhoods with a lot more noise than we get, and I think there's a decent chance political action can help roll back some of the increase in plane traffic over our neighborhood.

solomonic reasoning is quite welcome!

I know people in college point, and one person in a part of the Bronx that gets a lot of takeoff noise from LGA. the former group are relieved to have their noisy-day quota lowered (from about 95 percent of days to about 80 percent). according to info I've read, they have not added flights to the airport -- although there has been talk about adding another flight or so every three minutes, which would make things worse. it's just been something of a rebalancing of the hefty number of flights that currently come in/go out of the airport. is it bad for some of us -- including me? yep. should we just flip the bird to other new Yorkers, as newbies like lifeofpi suggest? nope.

I was deeply involved in protesting late '90s plans to increase air traffic at LGA and would do the same again. it's overused, overcrowded, and, frankly, dangerous. but I do believe that mindless NIMBY bleating -- as opposed to rational discussion and legitimate protest-- should be ridiculed.
