Community Groups, Activities & Events / Fall writing classes for residents of Queens
« on: September 02, 2013, 10:25:50 PM »
Newtown Literary presents E-books 101: A DIY Introduction and Queens Short Fiction Writing Workshop this fall at the Greater Astoria Historical Society.

E-books 101: A DIY Introduction
When: Saturday, September 28, 2013 2:00 p.m. – 3:30 p.m
Where: Greater Astoria Historical Society
              Quinn Building, 35-20 Broadway, 4th Floor
              Long Island City, NY, 11106
Cost: $20

Production Editor for Newtown Literary, Katherine Garrigan (visit katherinegarrigan.com for information on her work) will instruct the one-time session that will discuss the resources and programs you will need to be familiar with in order to create e-books usable across many platforms. We will be discussing Calibre, Sigil, and other (free!) programs used in making e-books, as well as what computer languages you may want to learn or brush up on. You will leave this session with all the information needed to craft your own e-book and publish it.

Queens Short Fiction Writing Workshop
When: 10 Tuesdays 6 – 7:45 P.M. (October 8th – December 17th)
Where: Greater Astoria Historical Society
              Quinn Building, 35-20 Broadway, 4th Floor
              Long Island City, NY, 11106
Cost: $200

Founding editor of Newtown Literary, Tim Frederick (More information on Tim can be found at  www.timfredrick.com ) will facilitate the workshop where participants will:
•   Engage in weekly, short writing exercises to explore aspects of
short  fiction writing, such as mood, character, setting, word
choice, revision, and dialogue.
•   Workshop two short stories (of approximately 5,000 words
each) or one longer short story, in two parts (of approximately
10,000 words total).
•   Prepare at least one manuscript for submission to literary
journals and set a submission plan for that story.

This workshop is for short fiction writers living in Queens, NY who aim to have their work published in literary journals and want a kind, informal environment to improve their writing. Due to limited space in the workshop, interested writers will be required to submit their first short story for workshopping, as well as proof of Queens residency, as part of an application process. The deadline for application is September 15, 2013, and invitations to participate will be sent out by September 25, 2013. There are eight available seats in this workshop.


For more information or to sign up for either classes, visit: www.newtownliterary.org/classes ; email events@newtownliterary.org
